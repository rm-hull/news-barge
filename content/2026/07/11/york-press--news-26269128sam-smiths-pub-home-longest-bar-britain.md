## Historic Sam Smith's pub home to the longest bar in Britain that serves pub classics

The pub is known for having the longest bar in Britain *(Image: Tripadvisor)i>*

You do not have to stay in Yorkshire to enjoy a Sam Smith’s pub, with many found in other parts of the UK.

London is packed with pubs owned by the brewery giant, including the historic Cittie of Yorke, which is home to the Henneky’s long bar, the longest in Britain.

The site has been used as a pub since 1430 but was around a quarter of the size it is today until it was rebuilt in 1695 and renamed the Queen’s Head.

The pub might be the perfect place to escape from the busy London streets and step back in time for a beer or two.

  The pub has a varied food menu featuring pub classic dishes. 

*(Image: Tripadvisor)*

## What is the Cittie of Yorke in London like?

The pub’s website says: “The present owner, Samuel Smith Old Brewery, purchased the pub in 1975.

“The pub was renamed the ‘Cittie of Yorke’, after a 16th Century pub that had stood in the Staple Inn’s – the timber framed building on the other side of the road.

“Next to the pub is the gatehouse to Gray’s Inn, where Charles Dickens describes David Copperfield having temporary residence.

"Dickens himself worked for some time as a clerk at Gray’s Inn.

"Dickens also wrote about the connection between this pubs cellar and the Gordon Riots in his book Barnaby Rudge.

“The cellar bar of today, is the old wine and beer cellarage of the 17th century Coffee House.”

Alongside being referenced by Charles Dickens, the pub has been visited by the famous models Mandy Rice-Davies and Christine Keeler, as well as the poet Dylan Thomas.


Like with all Sam Smith’s pubs, all of its beers are brewed by the Old Brewery in Tadcaster.

As well as its beers, you can also find wines and a selection of spirits to enjoy.

The pub also offers a range of bar snacks such as flatbread, fish finger bites, and crispy squid to have alongside your drink, as well as pub classic dishes like steak, sausage and mash, and a range of fish options.

There are lots of historic places to sit in the pub, including Victorian-style cubicles, a grand hall back room, a quirky cellar bar, and a hidden beer garden.
