The Met Office has forecast a mix of mist, sunshine, and cloud across Malton this weekend.

Saturday will start cool and calm, with temperatures around 15–16°C.

Clear skies will give way to patches of mist between 9am and 10am, potentially reducing visibility in low-lying areas.

The mist will lift by late morning, leaving mostly cloudy skies before brighter conditions develop.

Winds will be very light at 2–3 mph, and the chance of rain is less than 5 per cent.

In the afternoon, the cloud will break, revealing sunny intervals for the rest of the day.

Temperatures will rise to 21°C, with a light breeze developing, and rain remains very unlikely.

The evening will be warm, with temperatures peaking around 22–23°C.

Cloud will gradually increase later, becoming mostly overcast after sunset, although it will stay dry with only a slight 10 per cent chance of a shower.

Winds will be a gentle breeze at 9–11 mph.

Sunday will start fresh but pleasant, with temperatures around 15°C, quickly warming under sunny skies.

After a few early sunny intervals, conditions will become fully sunny through the morning, with the chance of rain less than 5 per cent.

The afternoon will see uninterrupted sunshine, with temperatures reaching around 22°C.

A light breeze will keep conditions comfortable.

The evening will continue with sunshine before clear skies return after sunset.

Temperatures will gradually ease from 20°C to around 15°C, making for a pleasant and comfortable night, and winds will ease from 11 mph to 5 mph.

Overall, the weekend promises a mix of mist, sunshine, and cloud, with temperatures reaching the low 20s.

The chance of rain remains low throughout the weekend.
