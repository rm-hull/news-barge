## City of York Council's housing development wins top design honours

The City of York Council’s first Passivhaus development, Duncombe Square in Clifton, was recognised at the York Design Awards *(Image: City of York Council)*

A sustainable housing development has won three architecture awards.

The City of York Council’s first Passivhaus development, Duncombe Square in Clifton, was recognised at the York Design Awards for its high standards of environmental performance and design.

The scheme, made up of 34 homes designed in collaboration with residents and architect Mikhail Riches, won three awards at the York Design Awards.

Councillor Michael Pavlovic, City of York Council’s executive member for housing, said: "Our Housing Delivery Programme has been ambitious from the get-go, and these awards recognise the vision and quality of the housing being created in York to meet residents’ needs and budgets.

  The scheme, made up of 34 homes designed in collaboration with residents and architect Mikhail Riches

"I’ve watched Duncombe be built and have seen how modern building techniques can produce exceptional homes and transform lives."

The development won the Sustainability Award, the Multi-Residential Award, and the Lord Mayor’s Award at the York Design Awards on Tuesday, July 7.

Lowfield Green, another council scheme in Acomb, earned a commendation for Best Homes and Communities Scheme.

Sophie Coles, architect at Mikhail Riches, said: "City of York Council's Housing Delivery Programme has delivered some of the UK's best and most sustainable homes.

"Duncombe Square is a net-zero, child-friendly, green and biodiverse neighbourhood that sets a benchmark for sustainable development."
