YOUNG people have been putting their lives at risk by swimming in dangerous open water in North Yorkshire.

North Yorkshire Police said officers had received reports of youths jumping into and swimming in Selby Canal and Brayton Canal.

The force has launched an appeal, in response, urging parents to speak to their children about the dangers of entering open water.

  


A spokesperson said: "Hidden hazards beneath the surface, including submerged objects, sharp debris, sudden changes in depth, strong currents, entangling weeds and cold-water shock can quickly turn a fun activity into a life-threatening situation.

"Even strong swimmers can find themselves in difficulty.

"Canal water can contain harmful bacteria, viruses and other contaminants that may cause illness if swallowed or if it comes into contact with cuts, grazes or open wounds.

"If your child is currently out in Selby, please make contact with them and check on their safety."

Residents are also encouraged to look out for friends and call 999 immediately if someone gets into difficulty.

The appeal added: "As temperatures rise, we ask the whole community to help spread this important safety message.

"By working together, we can help prevent serious injury and keep our young people safe."
