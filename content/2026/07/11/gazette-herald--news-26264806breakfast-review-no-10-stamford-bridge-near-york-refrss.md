**Breakfast review of No 10 in Stamford Bridge, near York**

STAMFORD Bridge will forever be in the history books as the site of a legendary battle between the English and Viking invaders in 1066.

  But my favourite Stamford Bridge story dates back to Easter Day in 2008, when a taxi driver drove a customer all the way from their Northamptonshire home to the handsome village ten miles outside York rather than the *other* Stamford Bridge, home of London premiership football giants, Chelsea. Needless to say, they missed the match.

No taxi was required for my trip to Stamford Bridge last Saturday morning; and the only battle I faced was with the 20mph headwind as I took my e-bike out for the ten-mile ride to the picturesque village perched by the River Derwent.

Ten miles – which takes me about an hour – is the optimum distance for my level of cycling.

And there has to be a proviso too – the promise of a coffee or decent snack at the end of the 60-minute ride.

And so my destination was clear: we were making tracks for No 10 – the well-established eating and drinking spot in The Square, directly on the left as you cross the bridge and enter Stamford Bridge.

  No 10 cafe and bistro at The Square in Stamford Bridge


Prime Minister-in-waiting Andy Burnham has been making big noises about 'No 10 North'; but we already have one close to York!

No 10 is a real asset to Stamford Bridge. With cafe tables roadside and a large patio garden to the rear to complement its generous indoor seating, there is ample space for locals and visitors.

The cafe has been a favourite on my radar for a number of years and I have enjoyed everything from a pasta lunch, to home-made soups and paninis as well as savoured several of its excellent home-made cakes during my many visits.

It advertises itself as a 'cafe, bar and bistro' as well as a 'wine bar' and has menus to match, covering everything from breakfast and lunches to main meals and specials options. An ambitious list of drinks is on offer too: all in all, No 10 is an easy place to find something to your liking and budget.

  Rear garden/patio area of No 10 at Stamford Bridge


We arrived at 11.30am; lunch wasn't being served until noon, so we picked our refuelling dishes from the breakfast menu.

Settled at a table in the patio garden, the sun came out and it felt almost like being on holiday as we relaxed under its warmth and enjoyed our drinks: an apple juice (£3) for my husband and a berry smoothie (£4.95) for me.

The smoothie was impressive and one of the best I'd tasted. The mix of cranberry juice and crushed strawberries, raspberries and blueberries made for a tangy but not overly sweet drink. It was cold and refreshing; served in a tall glass, it proved to be a generous serving and good value at the price.

  Berry smoothie at No 10 cafe


All the usual suspects were on the breakfast menu, from bacon, egg and sausage rolls and pancakes, to avocado on toast, with veggie options available too.

We went for two classics: eggs Benedict (£10.95) and a full English (£12.95).

Service was really excellent; although not too busy, there were plenty of staff on duty, and we were served by a succession of waitresses who all seemed to be on top of our order and could not do enough to make sure everything was to our liking and ensure we had just what we needed.

One even returned to the kitchen to see if any Marmite could be found to accompany the toast with the English breakfast.

And our food arrived promptly too.

It all looked appetising – always a plus, especially when you are hungry and really looking forward to your meal.

My husband often orders eggs Benedict and sang the praises of this one. "It's really, really good," he said as he cut into the perfectly poached eggs which sat upon squares of salty bacon and a toasted muffin, all smothered in a creamy Hollandaise. They came as a duo and he devoured each one, not leaving a scrap on the plate.

  Tucking into a very good eggs Benedict at No 10 cafe and bistro at Stamford Bridge


My English breakfast was hard to fault. Nicely presented with small ramekins filled with warmed tinned tomatoes and baked beans, the large oval plate was brimming with all the favourites: sausages, bacon, poached egg, sauteed mushrooms, black pudding, hash brown and fried bread. Two pats of wrapped butter and a dish of brown sauce were provided too.

Everything was decently cooked and I savoured every mouthful (although my husband hoovered up most of the mushrooms, which he particularly liked).

  Full English breakfast at No 10 cafe and bistro in Stamford Bridge


To finish, we ordered coffees – a flat white for me and even though it wasn't on the menu, our waitress made my husband a cortado (basically a less milky flat white).

Our bill came to a nudge short of £40, so twenty pounds each for what was a really tasty and filling breakfast, in a pleasant setting, with excellent service.

Whether you get here by car, bus, bike, on foot – or taxi, No 10 is worth a visit. Just remember to set your sat nav for the correct Stamford Bridge!
