Defendants aged 30 to 81 were among those sentenced recently at Scarborough Magistrates Court.

Zowie Hannah, 51, of Elm Court, Helmsley, pleaded guilty to driving on the A170 Sutton Bank with a cannabis breakdown product in her body.

She was given a 12-month community order with 10 days’ rehabilitative activities, banned from driving for 12 months and ordered to pay an £80 fine, £85 prosecution costs and a £114 statutory surcharge.

Dylan Norman, 30, of Wreyfield Drive, Newby, Scarborough, pleaded guilty to assaulting a police officer and failure to attend court.

He was given an 18-month community order with 26 days’ rehabilitative activities and 120 hours’ unpaid work and ordered to pay £85 prosecution costs.

David Roberts, 81, of Fieldside, Scarborough, pleaded guilty to six charges of breaching a restraining order, assault and criminal damage to a gate lock.

He was given a 12-month prison sentence suspended for 12 months on condition he does 20 days’ rehabilitative activities and £85 prosecution costs.

Stacey Ludford, 36, of Queens Terrace, Scarborough, pleaded guilty to assaulting a man in Whitby and criminal damage of a police vehicle.

He was given a 12-month community order with 24 days’ rehabilitative activities and must wear an alcohol abstinence tag for 90 days. He must pay £85 prosecution costs, a £114 statutory surcharge and £74.49 compensation to the police.
