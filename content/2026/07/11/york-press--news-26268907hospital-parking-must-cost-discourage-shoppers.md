**Hospital parking must cost more to discourage shoppers**

RE: York Central MP Rachael Maskell's call for the end to hospital parking fees.

Opened on July 28 in 1977 by Princess Alexandra the carpark was free, and with it being free and near to central York, literally everyone parked there.

Shop workers, office workers, shoppers and guess what – there no parking left for hospital visitors.

So they introduced a parking fee.

Then the 'free parking' became the streets near to the city centre, so residents-only parking was introduced.

And like a cancer, residents parking was (and still is) expanding further afield.

And now Rachael Maskell thinks free hospital parking is the way forward.

I don't know whether to laugh or cry.

Those who cannot remember the past are condemned to repeat it – hospital parking has to be more expensive to discourage the wrong type of casual parking.

  *D M Deamer,*

  *Penleys Grove Street,*

  *Monkgate,*

  *York*

... RE: the article about the MP for York Central, Rachael Maskel, requesting the removal of car parking fees from York hospital.

Many years ago hospital parking was free, however those attending hospital appointments or visits couldn’t park.

The car park was a magnet for city workers and those who went shopping.

The parking fees were brought in to stop the all-day parking.

The parking problem will become an even bigger nightmare.

The honourable MP needs to research the problems and not come out with silly statements.

I take the bus and no doubt many others could too.

  *L Fraser,*

  *Flaxton*

  **It's a Mystery - no more**

I ALWAYS like to see coverage of the Mystery Plays in the York Press, and the wagons have been fantastic this year.

But your Nostalgia page (Wednesday, July 8) makes a mistake about the 1966 plays, giving a caption “on the streets” to a photo showing Jesus (John Westbrook) being pulled along to Calvary by soldiers.

The Plays were performed in the Museum Gardens that year as usual then.

When you look carefully, the photo is posed with the audience stands in the background. They look like scaffolding on streets – because the 1966 programme in the Mystery Plays archive records that the stands were built by Mills Scaffolding Limited! (And scenery by the Shepherd Building Group Ltd and lighting by the Strand Electric and Engineering Company.)

There was a wagon in 1966, with boys from Archbishop Holgate’s school, as famously seen in a photo of Noah’s Ark manoeuvring rather precariously in Stonegate.

  *Margaret Scott,*

  *Volunteer Mystery Play archivist,*

  *National Centre for Early Music,*

  *St Margaret’s Church, Walmgate*
