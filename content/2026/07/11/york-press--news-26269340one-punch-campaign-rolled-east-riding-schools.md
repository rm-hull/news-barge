More than 1,000 pupils have heard a talk about the potential consequences of violence.

The sessions, part of the ‘One Punch Ruins Lives’ campaign, are led by brain injury survivor Paul Spence in partnership with East Riding of Yorkshire Council.

Mr Spence, founder of Hull-based charity P.A.U.L For Brain Recovery, has already delivered talks at schools in Hornsea, South Hunsley and Snaith.

  The ‘One Punch Ruins Lives’ campaign is led by brain injury survivor Paul Spence 

*(Image: East Riding of Yorkshire Council)*

Paul Spence said: "It's a privilege to be able to share our life-saving message in every school across the region.

"We’ve talked about the impact of violence and the importance of emotional intelligence.

"If just one young person walks away and makes a better decision in a moment of anger, then the message has made a difference."

  The presentations aim to educate students about the potential consequences of violence and offer practical, age-appropriate advice 

*(Image: East Riding of Yorkshire Council)*

The presentations aim to educate students about the potential consequences of violence and offer practical, age-appropriate advice on conflict resolution, emotional regulation and brain health.

Each school involved in the campaign also receives copies of Mr Spence’s 'One Punch Ruins Lives' books.

Councillor Victoria Aitken, cabinet member for children, families and education, said: "This message is so important, and I’m pleased that so many of East Riding’s young people are having the opportunity to hear about Paul’s lived experience and will benefit from the awareness and practical tips he provides."
