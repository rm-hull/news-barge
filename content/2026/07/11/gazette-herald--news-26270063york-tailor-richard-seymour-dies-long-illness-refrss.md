'EVERYONE in York knew him'. Those are the words of a loving son about York tailor Richard Seymour who has died.

Richard ran his popular family tailor’s business for decades – dressing hundreds of customers, from Rowntree's workers to MPs and jockeys.

His son Philip told The Press: "Everyone in York knew him. He was awarded the Seal of the City by the Lord Mayor when he retired."

  2015: The Lord Mayor of York, Cllr Sonja Crisp, presents a commemorative Seal of the City of York to Richard Seymour, watched by members of Richard's family


Besides making suits for professional politicians and sportsmen, he also tailored for factory workers.

Philip said his dad was working in an era when the wearing of suits was more popular.

He said: "Men would get paid by Rowntree's on a Friday then go to Dad's to get a suit."

Having taken over CE Seymour Tailors from his father in 1969, Richard ran the company until he retired in 2015, moving the business from its shop in Church Street where it was established in 1950, to Bootham in 1990, where it remained until he closed the doors for the final time.

As well as serving the York and North Yorkshire community for over 65 years, the business had customers all over the world, as far afield as New Zealand, Canada and America.

After going to school at St Peters in York, Richard moved to London and then the Midlands to further his career, before moving back to York to work in the family business.

Philip said: "Richard was a popular member of the York business community for many years."

On his retirement in 2015, the Lord Mayor of York presented Richard with the Ceremonial Seal of the City of York, to mark the great customer service he had delivered to local people for so many years.

In an interview with The Press on his retirement at the age of 69 back in 2015, Richard explained his business ethos.

  Richard Seymour - well known York tailor who has died


He said it had thrived through several recessions by priding itself on traditional values and excellent customer service.

"Many people have come to York on holiday, visited the shop, and then stayed as customers for years, sometimes decades. Many customers over the years have added York into their holiday itinerary just to visit the shop.”

But he felt that despite its popularity with foreign visitors, it was the residents of York and North Yorkshire who had proved to be the mainstay of the company.

“It has been a real pleasure to serve the people of North Yorkshire for all these years. As a business we have never really moved with the times. Instead we believe that if we look after our customers they will look after us, and I think it is this that has kept people coming back to us year after year.”

Richard passed away on Thursday, June 25 at Meadowbeck Care Home, Osbaldwick, after a long battle with illness.

  2015: The Lord Mayor of York, Cllr Sonja Crisp, presents a commemorative Seal of the City of York to Richard Seymour to mark the service that he and his business, C E Seymour Tailors, have given the city


He leaves behind his wife, Celia, three children, Anthony, Philip and Helen, as well as seven grandchildren.

Richard's funeral will take place at York Crematorium at 12.20pm on Friday July 24, with everyone who knew him welcome to attend.

The family have requested family flowers only, and there will be a collection for St Leonard's Hospice on the day.

The wake afterwards will be held at Burnholme Working Mens Club, where Richard was a life member.

Mourners are asked to wear bright coloured ties, or bright clothing, no black ties, as was Richard's request.
