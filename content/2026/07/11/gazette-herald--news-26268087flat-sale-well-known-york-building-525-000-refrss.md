A “magnificent” flat has hit the market in a well-known York building – for £525,000.

The two-bedroom flat is for sale in Walmgate’s Fossbridge House, the former home of the FR Stubbs ironmongers.

Stubbs moved from the site in 2001 after 86 years but its branding remains on the Merchantgate side of the street – and has become something of a York landmark.

The Grade II-listed building was converted into homes in 2007, with this being the first time the apartment has been listed for sale, according to the estate agent, Linley and Simpson.


Linley and Simpson, which is marketing the top floor apartment, said it has “unmatched views” across York towards the Minster.

“The spacious home is in the heart of the city centre and a large living area and primary bedroom suite giving the best views across the city,” a Linley and Simpson spokesperson said.

Inside, a private entrance hallway leads to the centrepiece of the home – the large lounge/diner which is bathed in light from large windows.

A door to the rear leads to the roof terrace offering views towards Piccadilly.

There is a kitchen off the lounge area which features built-in appliances.

The main bedroom comes with a modern en-suite shower room.

Another double bedroom also comes with an additional shower room.

The flat is a leasehold property, with the lease starting in 2007 and ending in 3006.

According to the listing, the ground rent is £300 per year and the service charge is £1035.52 per quarter.
