Search the Public Notice PortalHere are the latest planning applications submitted to City of York Council for York and the surrounding area.

York Pullman Bus Company has submitted an application for a new fuelling and coach-washing building at its Rufforth Airfield depot.

The plans include associated plant equipment and infrastructure, aimed at improving operational efficiency.

The application was validated on July 1 and is currently awaiting a decision.

Ref. No: 26/01208/FUL | Validated: Wed 01 Jul 2026 | Status: Awaiting decision

An application has been submitted for the construction of a new home following the demolition of an agricultural building at Spring Wood Stables on New Road in Deighton.

The plans propose a single dwelling on the site, replacing the existing structure.

The application was validated on July 3.

Ref. No: 26/01149/FUL | Validated: Fri 03 Jul 2026 | Status: Awaiting decision

Plans have been lodged to convert two separate dwellings into a single residence on Ousecliffe Gardens in York.

Additionally, two flats in the same vicinity are proposed for conversion into a single home.

The application forms part of ongoing redevelopment in the area.

Ref. No: 26/01081/FUL | Validated: Tue 23 Jun 2026 | Status: Awaiting decision

A screening opinion has been requested for a temporary solar farm development at Heeling Bottoms, Wheldrake Lane in Elvington.

The proposal includes a battery energy storage system, substation, grid connection cable route, and related infrastructure.

Plans also feature security fencing, landscaping, and biodiversity enhancements.

Ref. No: 26/01192/EIASN | Validated: Tue 23 Jun 2026 | Status: Awaiting decision

Applications have been submitted for tree works at two locations.

At 34 Shipton Road in Clifton, a Prunus and a conifer are proposed for removal.

Ref. No: 26/01141/TCA | Validated: Mon 22 Jun 2026 | Status: Awaiting decision

At Dick Turpin House, 30 Main Street in Fulford, plans include felling one walnut, six laburnum, one sycamore, and one holly tree.

Ref. No: 26/01227/TCA | Validated: Mon 22 Jun 2026 | Status: Awaiting decision

Both sites are within designated conservation areas.

A planning application has been submitted for new signage at the Boots store at 86 Clifton in York.

The proposal includes a hanging sign, a new fascia sign, and a window graphic.

Ref. No: 26/01071/ADV | Validated: Wed 01 Jul 2026 | Status: Awaiting decision
