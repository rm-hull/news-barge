The Met Office has forecast a warm and sunny weekend for York.

Saturday will start off cool and fresh, with temperatures easing from 18C to 15C before rising again.

Clear skies will dominate the early morning, with sunny intervals developing by late morning.

Light winds of 3–4 mph will make it feel calm and pleasant, and the chance of rain is less than 5 per cent all morning.

The afternoon will see plenty of sunshine, with temperatures rising steadily from 16C at noon to 23C by 5pm.

UV levels are likely to be high, so sunscreen is recommended for those outside for long periods.

The evening will be the warmest part of the day, with temperatures reaching 24–25C between 6pm and 9pm.

Conditions will stay dry and pleasant, although cloud will gradually increase later in the evening, becoming overcast by around 10pm.

The chance of rain remains low at 10 per cent, and temperatures will slowly fall to 18C by 11pm, with a gentle 7–10 mph breeze.

Sunday will start mild at 16C, warming quickly under sunny skies.

Sunny intervals early on will become widespread sunshine by mid-morning.

The chance of rain remains less than 5 per cent throughout.

The afternoon will see wall-to-wall sunshine, with temperatures peaking at around 24C.

A gentle breeze of 10–11 mph will help keep it from feeling too hot.

The evening will continue to be sunny, with clear skies developing after sunset, and temperatures will ease from 22C at 6pm to around 15C by 11pm.

Winds will gradually ease from around 10 mph to 5 mph, making for a calm night.
