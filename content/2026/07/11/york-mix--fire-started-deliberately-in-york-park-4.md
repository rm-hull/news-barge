A fire was started deliberately in a York park this afternoon (Saturday).

Acomb firefighters went to the incident in the park off **Bellhouse Way** at**1.50pm**.

A North Yorkshire Fire & Rescue Service spokesperson said: “On arrival the crews found a fire to approx 5m by 2m of grassland.

#### Incident location

“They used buckets and knapsacks to extinguish the fire which was believed to have been caused deliberately.”

Later, at **4.07pm**, Acomb firefighters went to another report of grassland on fire, this time in the** Rawcliffe** area.

“On arrival the fire measured approx 20 x 20 metres. The crews extinguished fully using 45 jet and beaters. Police were informed of the incident,” the fire service spokesperson said.
