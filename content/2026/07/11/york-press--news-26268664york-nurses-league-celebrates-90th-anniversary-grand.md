## York Nurses League marks 90th anniversary with special celebration

A group of nurses in York marked 90 years of shared history and professional legacy at The Grand *(Image: Supplied)*

A group of nurses in York marked 90 years of shared history and professional legacy.

Members of the York Nurses League gathered for their annual general meeting and afternoon tea at The Grand in York on July 4.

  The event brought together retired and practising nurses to celebrate 

*(Image: Supplied)*

The event brought together retired and practising nurses to celebrate nine decades of friendship and dedication to the profession.

Kate Hodge, president of the York Nurses League, said: " This afternoon is not only a celebration of our remarkable history, but a heartfelt 'Thank you' to the incredible nurses who have provided care and comfort to the York community for generations, while remaining loyal members and supporting the League over an amazing 90 years."

  The York Nurses League was founded in June 1936 by the matron of the County Hospital 

*(Image: Supplied)*

The York Nurses League was founded in June 1936 by the matron of the County Hospital with the aim of bringing together past and present nurses to maintain social connections after leaving the hospital.

The event included a tribute speech honouring the commitment and compassion of York’s nurses, both past and present.

  The event included a tribute speech honouring the commitment and compassion of York’s nurses 

*(Image: Supplied)*

A toast was raised to the league’s future, and two of its longest-serving members, who joined in 1959, cut a specially decorated anniversary cake.

We will continue to look forward to friendships and traditions that have defined the organisation for almost a century.
