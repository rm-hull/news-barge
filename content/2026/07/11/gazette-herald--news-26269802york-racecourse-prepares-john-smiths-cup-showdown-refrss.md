The John Smith's Cup aims to create another historical sporting moment this weekend.

This flat-racing event, which is the longest-running commercial sponsorship in British flat racing, began in 1960.

It has become known as one of the most fiercely contested betting races.

Rich in history and prize money, this handicap race is a £200,000 showpiece and is highly sought-after by jockeys.

It is an event people always try to win, with only one jockey, Andrea Atzeni, having managed to win it twice in the current century.

The race features some notable horses, including Warrant Holder, who was part of a remarkable double win for HM the King and HM the Queen at York’s Dante Festival and recently finished second in the Duke of Edinburgh Handicap at Royal Ascot.

Saturday's three trainers with the best recent records in the race also have strong contenders for the win this year.

William Haggas, who has three previous wins under his belt, seems to be pinning his hopes on Yabher, set to be ridden by Jason Hart.

Yabher previously won his only outing this season at Goodwood in May.

Andrew Balding, having clinched three wins as well, is fielding Respond, who recently finished as the Epsom runner-up.

Dual-winning handler Roger Varian presents Raammee, the Sandown runner-up.

Yorkshire's last victory came in 2009 when the David Barron-trained Sirvino won the race.

This year, the region’s best hope appears to be Ed Bethell’s trained Danger Baby, the Zetland Gold Cup winner at Redcar.

The 2005 race saw Aidan O'Brien's Mullins Bay win as the last outright favourite and the most recent Irish-trained winner.

This year, Jessica Harrington trains the sole contender from the Emerald Isle, Iron Fist, who finished third in a big field Premier Handicap at The Curragh during his last race.

The racing day isn't all about the John Smith's Cup, as there is a robust supporting card headed by the £100,000 John Smith’s Silver Cup and the big Sprint with a £70,000 purse.

The John Smith’s City Walls Stakes features last year’s winner, Washington Heights, and Group 1 winner American Affair, as well as JM Jungle, trained by John and Sean Quinn, a Group 2 winner at Goodwood last season.

In response to the weather, the dress code has been relaxed - jackets are not required in the County Stand enclosure.

The York racecourse offers usual facilities including fresh tap water at all main bars.

The action begins at 2.02pm, with ample free parking and tickets for all enclosures available at the gate.

A free shuttle bus will be operating as usual for easy access to the venue.
