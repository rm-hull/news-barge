The Met Office has forecast a mix of sunshine and cloud around Malton this weekend.

Saturday will start off mild, with temperatures between 12–14C.

Patchy cloud will give way to sunny intervals throughout the morning, with bright spells becoming more frequent towards lunchtime.

There is a slight chance of a shower around midday, while light winds will gradually strengthen from 4–5 mph.

In the afternoon, a mix of sunshine and cloud is expected, with a cloudier spell during the early afternoon.

The highest chance of a shower comes between 1–3pm, peaking at around 30 per cent, although many places are likely to stay dry.

Temperatures will rise steadily from 14C to 18C, and a gentle breeze will increase to 9–11 mph.

In the evening, sunny intervals will continue, with temperatures holding around 18–19C before cooling after sunset.

Skies will become partly cloudy overnight, with temperatures falling to around 13C.

Dry conditions will return, with very little chance of rain, with light to moderate winds easing from 11 mph to 5 mph.

Sunday will start off bright and fresh, with temperatures rising from 11C to 17C.

There will be plenty of sunny intervals with just a little patchy cloud, and it will stay dry throughout the morning.

In the afternoon, long spells of sunshine are expected, with temperatures reaching 19C.

It will stay dry all afternoon, and gentle breezes of 9–11 mph will keep the air feeling fresh.

In the evening, sunshine will gradually give way to clear and partly cloudy skies overnight.

Temperatures will ease from 19C to around 14C, while continuing to stay dry throughout the evening.
