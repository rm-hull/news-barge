Children will bring York’s history to life through drama this summer thanks to a new community partnership.

The York Dungeon, Shared York CIC, and St Sampson’s Centre have joined forces to deliver free, interactive workshops for families at St Sampson’s Centre in Church Street, offering a hands-on way to explore the city’s past.

Mark Mattinson, general manager of The York Dungeon, said: "The York Dungeon is proud to take our storytelling beyond our walls and into the community.

"These workshops let children experience history in a hands-on, exciting way."

Led by performance manager Olivia Mortimer and members of The York Dungeon’s team, the sessions will encourage children to step into the shoes of historic characters.

Dr Rama Isaiah, deputy manager of St Sampson’s Centre, said: "We are delighted to work alongside Shared York CIC and The York Dungeon to bring families and local residents together through this unique initiative.

"We hope the event will encourage people to connect, share experiences, and strengthen the sense of community that St Sampson's Centre has proudly supported for many years."

Dr Will Zhuang, project director of Shared York CIC, added: "At Shared York, we believe that culture and heritage should be accessible to everyone."
