## York retail sales down 8.2% but top councillor says it's not just 'parking charges'

Executive Member for Economy, Cllr Pete Kilbane *(Image: Pic supplied)*

A leading York councillor admits retail is going through ‘challenging times’ but says there are more factors than just parking charges.

Cllr Pete Kilbane made the comments in response to business criticism of City of York parking charges introduced last year, which they blame for fuelling a drop in city centre sales and footfall.

As previously reported, York BID has reported an 8.2 per cent drop in sales at city centre businesses in May, compared with the same month last year. The national decrease over the same period was 4.5 per cent.

For the year to date, sales are down 4.7 per cent, compared to 4.1 per cent nationally.

York BID’s May Insight Report says footfall is down 8.8 per cent compared to last May, with the year to date down 8.2 per cent in the first five months of 2025.

The figures come amid efforts from central and local government to boost footfall in the high street.

Initiatives include extended hours for park and ride introduced last summer, with upgrades, including overnight parking due this year.

City of York Council is spending millions on the Station Gateway project and it continues to support Make it York and its events and festivals.

There is also the Mayoral High Street Fund, which also aims to boost footfall by supporting local projects.

In addition, central government has announced free bus fares for five-15-year-olds over the summer, a £1 young person’s fare and reduced VAT for attractions and children’s meals over the summer.

When asked for comment, City of York Council provided ‘provisional’ figures for June, which have yet to be published, some of which show York performing better than the national average, even if the figures are still down.

The footfall figures for the year to date for June dropped 8.6 per cent, compared with 9.5 per cent regionally and down 4.3 per cent nationally.

Sales in the year to date for June dropped 3.4 per cent in York, compared with a four per cent decrease nationally.

And sales in the fashion sector dropped 5.3 per cent in the year to date for June, compared with 5.9 per cent nationally, the ‘provisional’ figures also said.

Council executive member for economy, Cllr Pete Kilbane, said: “Retail is going through a challenging period, with shaky consumer confidence seeing people being more cautious about discretionary spending.

“Year to date retail sales to June were down in York, but not down as much as nationally, showing there are significant factors beyond parking charges influencing consumer spending.

“Fashion sales also show York outperforming the national average, but the current fall has to be seen in the context of a 17.5 per cent increase the year before, highlighting the volatility of this sector.”

Cllr Kilbane added: “While we have seen a small drop in people driving and parking in York city centre, we’ve also seen an increase in people travelling to York by bus and cycling into the city centre.

“And while it’s easy to form quick judgments based on a month or two of data, a more thorough assessment would consider other factors that influence consumer spending like the expansion of shopping online, all forms of travel and as we’ve seen recently, the weather”.
