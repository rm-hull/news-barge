# Grandparents jailed after South Elmsall village drugs raid

## Three generations of the same family have been jailed for drugs offences after a police raid at a Yorkshire mining village uncovered a stash of crack cocaine.

Grandparents Pamela and Philip Spencer, of South Elmsall, have been jailed for possession of crack cocaine with intent to supply. Their grandson Blake Cowell was also sentenced for drugs offences and owning a knuckle duster.

Leeds Crown Court heard police acted on community intelligence after the suspected drug-related death of a man nearby.

Speaking after sentencing, Det Insp Sarah Degnan of Wakefield CID said: “The Spencers have been a blight on the local community for some time, so I am pleased that justice has been served and they have been deprived of their liberty for a significant time.”

![Philip and Pamela Spencer](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOmE2ZDZlNDhjLWE4ZmUtNGMxOS1hNjQ1LTc1MDYyYTE1ZjE3Yjo1NjA1OWVlYy1hZTg0LTQ4Y2YtOWJjZi1jZWY5ZTA5YzNlMGY=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



Officers had raided the grandparents' address on Harrow Street in January, also seizing a crossbow and weapons.

Philip Spencer, aged 64, who admitted possession with intent to supply, was sentenced to five years and seven months’ custody. Pamela Spencer, aged 66, admitting the same, will serve three years. And Blake Cowell, aged 21 and of Beech Street, has been given a two-year suspended sentence after admitting possession of cannabis with intent to supply and possession of an offensive weapon in a private place.
