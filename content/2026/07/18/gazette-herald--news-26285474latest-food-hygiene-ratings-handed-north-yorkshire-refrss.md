The scores are in — the latest batch of food hygiene ratings for North Yorkshire restaurants, cafes, takeaways and businesses has been revealed.

Environmental health officers have been visiting businesses across the region to see if they are meeting hygiene requirements.

Based on guidelines set by the Food Standards Agency, inspectors look to see how clean the facilities are; what food safety management procedures are in place and how hygienic food handling is.

Ratings are then dished out from five to zero — with the higher score the better the result.

Five — Very Good

Copperclay Mews at Copperclay Mews Care Home, Copperclay Walk, Easingwold, rated 5 on July 3, 2026.

Plum and Partridge at Low Street, Husthwaite, rated 5 on July 8, 2026.

The Bakehouse Yorkshire Ltd at Mill Green Farm, Crayke Road to Mill Green, Crayke, rated 5 on July 8, 2026.

The Oak Tree Inn at Raskelf Road, Helperby, rated 5 on July 8, 2026.

The Olive Branch at Market Place, Easingwold, rated 5 on July 3, 2026.

The Penny Bank Cafe at 19 Market Place, Kirkbymoorside, rated 5 on July 7, 2026.

Businesses may have been reinspected since, and this will be reflected in our next round-up.
