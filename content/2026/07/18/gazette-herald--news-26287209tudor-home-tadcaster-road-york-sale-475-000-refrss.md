A Tudor home in York is back on the market – with a new price.

The three-bedroom home, dating back to around 1525, is for sale in Tadcaster Road for £475,000 – down from £525,000.

Ashtons, which is marketing the property, said it is “rich in character and history”.

“This unique semi-detached property offers a rare opportunity to own a remarkable piece of York’s past,” a spokesperson for the estate agent said.

  Inside the Tudor home for sale in Tadcaster Road, York, for £475,000 

*(Image: Ashtons/Zoopla)*

The home originally formed part of the Chapel of Ease linked to the Holy Trinity Church in Micklegate.

Inside, the property retains period features such as exposed beams and timber latch doors.

The ground floor has a spacious lounge centred around the feature fireplace, alongside a separate dining area.

To the rear is the extended kitchen diner, which, along with the ground floor bathroom was upgraded in 2020.

Three bedrooms are on the first floor – “each continuing the home’s characterful feel”, according to Ashtons.

Outside, the home has a rear garden and a parking area.
