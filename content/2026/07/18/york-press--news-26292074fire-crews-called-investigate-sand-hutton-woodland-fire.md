FIRE fighters were called out to investigate a blaze that was extinguished by the public in a village close to York today.

North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service said its York crew went out to Sand Hutton to investigate at 10.39am today (July 18) after reports of a fire in the woodland earlier that day.

“On arrival action had already been taken to extinguish by members of the public. Crews used a thermal imaging camera to ensure there was no hotspots due to peat in the area,” said a spokesperson for the service.

They added that advice was given to ring emergency services if there is a fire in a woodland area.
