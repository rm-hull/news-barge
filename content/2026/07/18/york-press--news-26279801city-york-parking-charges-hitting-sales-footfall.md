## York business leaders blame parking charges for city centre sales and footfall slump

Phil Pinder of the York High Street Forum *(Image: NQ)*

BUSINESS leaders have hit out at York parking charges which they say are hitting sales and footfall in the city centre.

The comments follow data from York BID, which reveals an 8.2 per cent drop in sales at city centre businesses in May, compared with the same month last year. The national decrease over the same period was 4.5 per cent.

For the year to date, sales are down 4.7 per cent, compared to 4.1 per cent nationally.

York BID’s May Insight Report says footfall is down 8.8 per cent compared to last May, with the year to date down 8.2 per cent in the first five months of 2025.

Phil Pinder, chairman of York High Street Forum said of the figures: “Truly shocking data, you'd like to think councillors would be concerned.”

Phil continued: “In reality, 30 per cent down in fashion is devastating, a sector that relies on locals - car parking is clearly the biggest factor.

“You don't buy clothes on a day trip. The council policy on car parking is just encouraging people to drive to Monks Cross - as they'd say in Private Eye, trebles all round.”

Nick Brown, managing director of Browns department store in York, says the store has seen a drop in footfall, but he says those that have come out, “have come out with a mission, that they are going to buy something” which has been “helpful.”

Nick said: “The serious problem we have with the new parking charges, they are a real deterrent to those living on the edge of York.

“They can just park around the ring road and park free at four shopping centres.”

Nick also says the recent hot weather has also reduced footfall to the city centre.

City of York Council, he continues, needs to consider rates such as two hours for £3.50.

  Nick Brown of Brown's York department store 

*(Image: NQ)*

The current parking charges of up to £5.50 an hour makes York more expensive than Leeds and Newcastle, Nick said.

Whilst York’s Park and Ride is ‘good’, Nick added: “That’s not a panacea in itself.”

Chris Pegg, head of regional commercial, sales and marketing at City Cruises, said: “The data provided by York BID is excellent, provides so much context to businesses and broadly matches our performance at City Cruises.”

Chris reported a double digit revenue drop from a record 2025.

He continued: “As a country, we're seeing slow economic growth, for an outdoor attraction we had a wet start to the year and we're deeper into a cost-of-living crisis due to the conflict in the Middle East.

  Charlotte Watson of Indie York 

*(Image: NQ)*

“A double hit for our boats as our fuel price has increased by 80 per cent at the same time!

“Beyond that, and highlighting York BID's report that shows that York has it worse-off, local pressures such as steep parking hikes risk sending a message that York is too expensive and by closing the Christmas market on Tuesdays it seems as a city, we're actively asking visitors to spend their money elsewhere.”

Charlotte Watson, chair of Indie York, stressed that her group wants to remain positive to support the city’s independent businesses.

She said: “We have such a wide variety of businesses in our community that we all feel the peaks and troughs differently. A decline in footfall to the city is never something that we want to see and it highlights that now more than ever we need to support our favourite independent businesses.”

Responding to our earlier story on the May figures, City of York Council said the high street faced many challenges with people more cautious about discretionary spending and the June figures showed York was performing better than the national average.

City of York Council executive member for economy, Cllr Pete Kilbane, said: “There are significant factors beyond parking charges influencing consumer spending.

Mr Kilbane added: “While we have seen a small drop in people driving and parking in York city centre, we’ve also seen an increase in people travelling to York by bus and cycling into the city centre.

“And while it’s easy to form quick judgements based on a month or two of data, a more thorough assessment would consider other factors that influence consumer spending like the expansion of shopping online, all forms of travel and as we’ve seen recently, the weather."
