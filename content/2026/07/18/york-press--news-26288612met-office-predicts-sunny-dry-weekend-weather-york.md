## The weekend weather forecast for York

A person enjoying the warm weather outdoors. *(Image: PA)*

The Met Office has issued the weekend weather forecast for York, predicting a mix of sunshine and cloud.

On Saturday, the morning will start cool at 13C with partly cloudy skies, but sunny intervals are expected from around 11am.

Rain is highly unlikely, with a less than 5 per cent chance, and a light northerly breeze of 6–7 mph is expected.

In the afternoon, temperatures will gradually climb from 14C to 19C, with a slight chance of a passing shower.

The breeze will increase slightly to 8–10 mph.

The evening will be sunny with temperatures holding around 20C, and skies will become clear after sunset.

The breeze will ease from 11 mph to around 6 mph.

On Sunday, a bright, sunny start is expected after a cool night, beginning at 11C.

Temperatures will rise steadily to 19C by lunchtime.

The afternoon will see plenty of sunshine with highs reaching 21C, making it the warmest part of the weekend.

The evening will be warm and sunny with temperatures slowly easing from 21C to around 15C overnight.

Clear skies are expected for most of the evening, with a little patchy cloud developing late at night.

Very light winds are expected, dropping to just 3–5 mph by late evening.

Overall, the weekend is expected to be dry with excellent visibility, making it ideal for spending time outdoors.
