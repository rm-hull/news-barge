# Tuchel hits back at Trump criticism before England v France bronze showdown

- England are preparing to face France in Miami for the World Cup 2026 bronze final, following their devastating semi-final defeat to Argentina.
- Manager Thomas Tuchel has faced widespread criticism for his tactical decisions during the semi-final, including remarks from Donald Trump regarding his strategy.
- Tuchel defended his in-game choices, asserting he has no regrets over his decisions despite the team's failure to secure a victory.
- The bronze final is widely considered “the game that nobody wants to play,” with both sides aiming to conclude their World Cup campaigns, and France marking the end of Didier Deschamps' tenure.
- Speculation is mounting over Tuchel's future, with reports indicating Pep Guardiola previously had a “verbal agreement” to manage England and may still be interested in the role.
