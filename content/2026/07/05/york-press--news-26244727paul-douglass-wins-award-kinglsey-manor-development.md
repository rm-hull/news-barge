A site manager from York has been named among the best in the country.

Paul Douglass, 54, received a Pride in the Job award from the National House Building Council (NHBC) for his work at Redrow’s Kinglsey Manor development in Harrogate.

The awards, known as the ‘Oscars’ of the housebuilding industry, recognise the very best site managers across the country.

Mr Douglass said: "This is an award for all the team; I have a brilliant team around me.

"Kingsley Manor is now nearing its end and is almost complete.

"As a team, we are absolutely delighted to have received this award."

It is the second consecutive year he has received the award.

Mr Douglass began his career in the building industry at 15 and has worked his way up from labourer to site manager over a 38-year career.

He lives with his wife Debbie and stepchildren Leah and Josh.

John Handley, managing director at Redrow Yorkshire, said: "These awards demonstrate our long-term commitment to looking after our customers and building high-quality homes.

"Anyone who buys a home from a site with a Pride in the Job award winner knows their home has been built to the highest standard."
