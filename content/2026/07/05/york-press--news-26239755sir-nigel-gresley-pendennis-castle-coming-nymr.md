## Two legendary steam locomotives to star on North Yorkshire Moors Railway

No. 4079 Pendennis Castle *(Image: Frank Dumbleton)*

Two legendary locomotives will return to the rails for a series of special journeys.

The North Yorkshire Moors Railway (NYMR) has announced that No. 60007 Sir Nigel Gresley and No. 4079 Pendennis Castle will join its summer and autumn Rockstar Engine services.

Charlotte Hawes, events and guest experience manager at NYMR, said: "We’re absolutely thrilled to welcome Sir Nigel Gresley and Pendennis Castle to the North Yorkshire Moors Railway.

"They both truly are icons of the steam age with remarkable stories, and the chance to travel behind them through our spectacular moorland scenery is something really special."

Sir Nigel Gresley will operate from Friday, September 18, to Sunday, September 20, and again from Saturday, October 17, to Monday, October 20, courtesy of the Sir Nigel Gresley Locomotive Trust Ltd.

  No. 60007 Sir Nigel Gresley 

*(Image: John Hunt)*

Pendennis Castle, built in 1924, will haul services on October 3 and 4, and again on October 10 and 11, courtesy of Didcot Railway Centre.

All journeys are pre-booked and seat-allocated.

Tickets are priced at £35 for adults, £17.50 for children, £30 for members, and £15 for carers.

Travel upgrades are available, including a fish and chips service on October 19 and 20.

Guest packages and souvenirs are also on offer, with bookings available at nymr.co.uk/rockstars.
