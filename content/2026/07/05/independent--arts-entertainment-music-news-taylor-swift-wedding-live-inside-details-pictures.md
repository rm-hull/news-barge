Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce are married!

After months of speculation over how they’d celebrate their nuptials, the mysterious Madison Square Garden rumors became a reality when paparazzi and countless chauffeured cars began pulling up outside the New York City arena Thursday and Friday.

In a statement shared with *The Independent*, Swift’s representative revealed that the ceremony was officiated by Adam Sandler and that Swift chose not to have bridesmaids.

Instead, her brother Austin Swift served as her Man of Honor and Jason Kelce was the groom’s Best Man.

Around 7.30 p.m. Friday, LED signs outside the venue read “JUST&T MARRIED!” The “T&T” is a nod to their shared first initial, which has been featured on guest merchandise for the event.

The couple’s bleary-eyed celebrity guests began pouring out of the venue in the early hours, helping to confirm who was lucky enough to receive an invite... and who might have been left off the guestlist.

## Kirk Schwabe, Taylor Swift’s favourite teacher, dies on her wedding day

Taylor Swift’s favourite teacher and one-time security guard died on the day of her wedding, it has emerged.

Kirk Schwabe, 69, died of cancer on 3 July, the same day the pop star married Travis Kelce at Madison Square Garden in New York City.

His family confirmed the news to *The Telegraph*, not long after he gave his first and only interview to the publication ahead of the ceremony. 

Schwabe’s wife, Jane, said he treated Swift “like he did his daughters”, while his daughter Jane said: “My dad had a remarkable way of making people feel seen, valued and protected.

“Whether you were family, one of his students, or someone like Taylor whose path crossed his, he cared deeply about people. That’s the legacy he leaves behind.”

## Taylor and Travis's wedding favours

While Taylor and Travis’s wedding was shrouded in secrecy, amid rumours guests were asked to sign NDAs before attending, some of the couple’s celebrity friends couldn’t resist sharing a few snippets from the big day.

Country star Maren Morris shared a number of shots of herself and fellow singer Kelsea Ballerini in their dresses, before one picture at the end of an Instagram carousel revealed a delicate lace handkerchief with Swift and Kelce’s initials, “T” and “T”, intertwined with a love-heart design, the date of their wedding and the location, “New York City”.

The handkerchief also featured a lyric from Swift’s hit song “Blank Space”, specifically the line: “So it’s gonna be forever...”

## Lena Dunham leaves Taylor Swift-Travis Kelce guests ‘gasping’ with outrageous wedding speech

Comedy was clearly at the forefront of Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce’s star-studded wedding on Friday.

The Madison Square Garden ceremony was officiated by Adam Sandler and included a dinner speech from *Girls* creator Lena Dunham that had attendees “gasping.”

Dunham, 40, and Swift, 36, have been close friends since the early 2010s when Dunham was dating Swift’s musical collaborator Jack Antonoff. Swift later served as a bridesmaid at Dunham’s wedding to musician Luis Felber in 2021.

## The glaring absence at Taylor Swift’s wedding to Travis Kelce

As Taylor Swift celebrated her union with NFL star Travis Kelce alongside around 1,000 of their closest friends and family, there was one (seemingly) notable absence.

On Thursday, as workers carried props, food and pink carpet into Madison Square Garden, the pop star’s longtime friend Blake Lively, 38, was spotted with husband Ryan Reynolds, 49, hundreds of miles away in Lake Placid, New York, at their daughter’s horse show.

This was the same day that Swift and Kelce were said to be hosting their rehearsal dinner, with Swift’s close friends Selena Gomez, Lena Dunham and Jack Antonoff among the guests spotted arriving at the venue.

Then, on Friday, hundreds more celebrities were seen making their way into the stadium and then leaving in the early hours of Saturday morning, among them Hugh Grant, Gigi Hadid and Bradley Cooper, Gracie Abrams, Ed Sheeran and Cherry Seaborn, Ellie Goulding, the Haim sisters and Graham Norton.

Yet Lively and Reynolds weren’t spotted by photographers, social media, fans or the hundreds of news outlets that have been reporting on the celebrations in the heart of New York City.
