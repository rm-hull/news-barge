France edged past Paraguay in the last-16 of the World Cup 2026 on Saturday, after Kylian Mbappe’s penalty secured a 1-0 win.

Didier Deschamps’ men have looked like the best team left in the tournament but the South Americans left them frustrated for more than an hour in Philadelphia.

However, Desire Doue’s trickery was enough to force Diego Gomez to dangle a leg and, after a VAR intervention, Ilgiz Tantashev awarded Les Bleus a spot kick, which Mbappe converted to move back level with Lionel Messi in the race for the golden boot on seven goals.

The feisty contest saw Paraguay use rough tactics on the French superstars, including Juan Caceres’s late challenge on Mbappe, and there were numerous moments when arguments between players threatened to boil over into physical altercations – even after the final whistle.

## French FA chief condemns ‘disgraceful’ ex-Paraguay goalkeeper Jose Luis Chilavert for racist remark

Paraguay legend Jose Luis Chilavert has been labelled a “disgrace” by French Football Association (FFF) chief Philippe Diallo for “racist” comments made towards France before their World Cup 2026 last 16 clash.

The former Paraguayan goalkeeper said that his national team would face a "squad from Africa" in the World Cup tie in Philadelphia.

The 60-year-old has shared a screenshot on social media of comments he attributed to former French forward Christophe Dugarry, which stated that Paraguay would get hammered by Les Bleus.

"Christophe, you're right. In 1998 we faced the French and now PY will face a squad from Africa," Chilavert said.
