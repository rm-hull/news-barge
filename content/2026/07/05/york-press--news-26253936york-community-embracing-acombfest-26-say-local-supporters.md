## 'An upward cycle of vibrant people' - York community group on art festival excitement

Peachzz's creation What Walks Among Us - one of the large murals capturing the imagination in Front Street during AcombFest *(Image: Kevin Glenton)*

OFFICIALS from a York community group have added their backing to a three-day festival which has brought hundreds of new visitors to the area and said it's got locals "fired-up" about what's next.

Charlotte Davies, treasurer of Greater Acomb Community Forum, a group which creates opportunities for the local community and is a voice for engaging with local businesses and representatives, has been watching the crowds stroll into AcombFest 26 since Friday (July 3).

The three-day arts festival celebrating a theme of "return to nature" has been brought together over more than 20 festival venues across Acomb and nearly 100 activities and events have come to life.

  


The centrepieces are four Front Street area murals which have been spraypainted over the three days and captured Charlotte's attention.

She said: "We've been absolutely delighted with the impact of AcombFest.

"The murals have made a huge difference, you see them, people are tripping over themselves in front of them because they're so distracted.

"And it's getting people fired up to say what's next, where can I go next?"

She added that attendance at AcombFest was also fantastic from a public health point of view, with people walking and moving around the community, talking to each other and "just being delighted and excited."

Local businesses which Charlotte said were initially a bit hesitant were now on board and some were sending out their own flyers and promotion.

She said: "The murals started going up and the shop owners realised the scale and saw that people were coming in and they wanted leaflets and maps.

"We've started some momentum going and next year will be even bigger.

"This is very much a springboard to start making Acomb a real centre of vibrant art."

The forum official paid tribute to curators The Art of Protest, who were speaking to the community across the weekend as well as bringing workshops, installation art trails across the festival site and spray paint exhibitions.

Charlotte Davies said: "They've done some open events but now we need to make this a more regular, because there's such a large number of artists in Acomb, they want to be doing things, there's also been more interest in the Open Gardens which ran for the first time in May.

"Businesses are realising they need to service these new communities, the whole thing is an upward cycle of vibrant excited people.

"They're not interested in profit maximisation, they're interested in serving each other, having a lot of fun, really enjoying being in community, being nice to one another and sharing joyous moments."
