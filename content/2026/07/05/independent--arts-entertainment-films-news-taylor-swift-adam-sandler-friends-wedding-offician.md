Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce tied the knot in New York City Friday evening in a ceremony officiated by... Adam Sandler.

Yes, really. The news was confirmed by Swift’s representatives, who told *The Independent* in a statement: “Taylor & Travis did not have bridesmaids or groomsmen. Instead, her brother Austin Swift served as Taylor’s Man of Honor and Jason Kelce was Travis’ Best Man. The ceremony joined both families together and was officiated by friend Adam Sandler.”

For those curious about the comedy star’s integral role in the ceremony, Kelce met Sandler after the actor cast him in his 2025 film, *Happy Gilmore 2*, in which the Kansas City Chiefs star made a brief cameo.

“Man, this life is crazy!! Still so surreal I got this opportunity,” Kelce wrote on social media at the time, under a photo of himself with Sandler on set.

Meanwhile, Sandler has known Swift for years through his two daughters, Sunny and Sadie, who are longtime fans of the “Lover” singer.

![Sandler appeared on Kelce’s ‘New Heights’ podcast in 2025 after casting the NFL athlete in ‘Happy Gilmore 2’](https://static.the-independent.com/2024/08/28/19/G0P9xmvq-720.jpg)

Sandler spoke about Swift and Kelce last year, describing Swift in an interview with *Entertainment Tonight* as “incredible” and praising her kindness to his family.

“My kids have met Taylor a bunch of times over the years, and she’s ridiculously nice to them and warm,” said Sandler. The actor called Kelce “such a gentle, nice guy, and funny as hell.”

Appearing on Kelce’s *New Heights* podcast last year, Sandler told the host that Swift “means so much to our house” and championed their relationship: “When you guys first started dating, my family was like, ‘Yes. Look how good they are together. He’s a gentleman and she’s having so much fun.’”

After the pair were announced husband and wife on Friday, jumbotrons outside the arena shared the news with crowds of fans: "JUST&T MARRIED!"

A who’s-who of A-list stars were spotted arriving across New York City for the celebration, including Gigi Hadid and Bradley Cooper, Ed Sheeran and Cherry Seaborn, Dakota Johnson, Zoë Kravitz, Hugh Grant, Karlie Kloss and Joshua Kushner, Mariska Hargitay and Peter Hermann, Ethan Hawke, Jason Sudeikis, Jimmy Fallon, Tommy Hilfiger, the Haim sisters, Ellie Goulding, Benson Boone, Maren Morris, Kelsea Ballerini, Miranda Lambert, The Chicks, and Brad Paisley with Kimberly Williams-Paisley.

![A ‘just married’ sign appeared on Madison Square Garden after the pair said ‘I do’](https://static.the-independent.com/2026/07/04/09/04-d4de8ef14f5c4ed3b472ea431b5af8e8.jpg)

However, one notable absence was Swift’s former best friend, Blake Lively.

Swift is godmother to Lively and her husband Ryan Reynolds’s children, and, up until last year, they were often photographed spending time together.

Their friendship was put under strain, though, in 2025 when the pop star’s name was dragged into Lively’s highly publicized legal battle with Justin Baldoni.

Amid legal proceedings in January, texts between Lively and Swift were unsealed – among them two long notes from December 2024 that suggested their friendship dynamic had shifted.

In May 2025, a representative for Swift told*The Independent* that her only connection to the film was that her song “My Tears Ricochet” was used on the soundtrack and criticised Baldoni for subpoenaing the star in the ongoing legal battle.

Baldoni’s attempt to depose Swift in the case was rejected by a judge in September last year. He and Lively then settled their lawsuit in early May 2026.

*The Independent* has contacted Lively’s representatives for comment regarding her absence from the wedding.
