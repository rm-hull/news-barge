# Khamenei’s funeral marked by notable absence

![](https://cdn.jwplayer.com/v2/media/mS9Dq707/poster.jpg?width=720)

- Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, the slain Iranian leader, and several family members were killed in a US and Israeli airstrike on February 28.
- His funeral on Sunday in Tehran saw his sons Mostafa, Meysam and Masoud Khamenei praying beside his coffin, but his successor, Mojtaba Khamenei, was absent.
- Mojtaba Khamenei is believed to have been injured in the same attack that killed his father, suffering facial disfigurement and leg injuries.
- Iran is holding a week of mass funeral processions for Khamenei, with his remains to be taken to Shi'ite religious sites in Iraq and other Iranian cities.
- A ceasefire has been agreed, suspending the four-month conflict, which Iran's authorities claim will bring significant economic benefits and is seen as a victory.
