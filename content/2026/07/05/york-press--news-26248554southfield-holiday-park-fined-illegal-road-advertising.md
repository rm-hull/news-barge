A COUNCIL is reminding businesses that they can issue fines for illegal road signs.

It comes after a business has been prosecuted for putting up illegal road signs without permission.

Southfield Holiday Park Ltd, in Wilberfoss, pleaded guilty at Beverley Magistrates’ Court to illegally placing two advertising signs on the A1079.

The offences took place between August 1, 2025, and January 7, 2026, on public verges without approval from East Riding of Yorkshire Council.

The company was fined £1,000 and ordered to pay £817.46 in costs and a £400 victim surcharge, totalling £2,217.46.

Carl Skelton, director of streetscene services at East Riding of Yorkshire Council, said: "Our officers will investigate and remove any signs put up illegally on the highway, and we will prosecute wherever we can.

"Signs like these could be a major hazard to motorists."

The council has issued a reminder that placing signs on highway land without the council’s permission is a criminal offence under the Highways Act 1980.

Unauthorised roadside advertising can distract drivers and increase the risk of accidents.

East Riding of Yorkshire Council can issue a £500 fixed penalty notice for each illegal sign.

If a case goes to court, those found guilty could be fined up to £2,500.

For more information, or to report an offence, visit the council’s website eryc.link/fly-posting
