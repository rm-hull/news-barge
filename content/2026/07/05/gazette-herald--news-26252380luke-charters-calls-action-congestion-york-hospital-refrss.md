A YORK MP has shared his frustrating experience with congestion along a main road leading to York Hospital, saying it caused him to miss his wife's maternity appointment.

Luke Charters is calling for a bespoke hospital Park & Ride system to help the thousands of patients, visitors and staff at York Hospital who get stuck in traffic and miss appointments each year.

It comes after the York Outer MP shared his personal experience with congestion along Wigginton Road, which forced him to miss a crucial hospital appointment with his wife.

  


He said: “I had to drop my wife off to attend her maternity appointment alone after we got stuck in traffic in Wigginton Road.

“She was heavily pregnant and had to walk the long road to the hospital to make it in time.

“It was absolutely heart-wrenching to have missed this.”

Mr Charters said he spent “hundreds of pounds” at York Hospital’s car park facilities after visiting family members, who of whom was receiving end-of-life care.

He said: “The congestion and parking add so much stress to those already facing difficult circumstances.

“I meet regularly with York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust’s chief executive and have brought these issues up with her.

“The congestion causes people miss appointments, through no fault of their own, which ultimately costs the NHS and prolongs treatment and diagnosis.

“Reducing this should be a top priority for all of the city’s leaders.”

The MP said he was considering a proposal to introduce a Park & Ride service for the hospital, which would include electric shuttlebuses.

He said: “Having this would be a game changer for so many people.

“I welcome feedback on this from my constituents and users of York Hospital.”
