Kaleb Cooper, the breakout star of *Clarkson’s Farm,* has tied the knot with his partner Taya at an intimate ceremony in the Cotswolds. 

The farm manager, 27, shares three children with Taya, whom he has described as his “best friend as well as the love of my life”.

Cooper proposed in 2022, sharing photos to his Instagram confirming: “She said yes! 2022 has been an amazing year. This definitely tops it off.”

Taya commented: “I love you, can’t wait to marry you!”

The couple welcomed their third child together, Ashton, last August, and also share son Oscar, five, and daughter Willa Grace, three.

*The Sun* reports that Cooper arrived at the 500-acre wedding venue Merriscourt in Chipping Norton, Oxfordshire – just a stone’s throw from Jeremy Clarkson’s Diddly Squat Farm – in a £250,000 McLaren supercar, with his son Oscar by his side. 

His boss and co-star Clarkson – who recently disclosed he has been diagnosed with cancer – was pictured arriving with his girlfriend, actor and sculptor Lisa Hogan, along with other castmembers of the hit Amazon Prime show.

Clarkson reportedly played an important role in the wedding by providing an “unlimited” supply of beer and cider from his Hawkstone brewery.

Posting about the nuptials to his three million Instagram followers, Cooper said it was “an amazing day”, also sharing a photo of him with his new bride in front of a tractor.

“Such an amazing day yesterday celebrating our wedding!” he wrote. “We both had the most amazing day! Mr and Mrs Cooper (more pics to come don’t worry).”

![Cooper with his new wife, Taya, pictured after proposing in 2022](https://static.the-independent.com/2025/08/18/10/55/Kaleb-Cooper-and-his-fiance-Taya.png)

Country music star Luke Combs was one of the many people posting messages of congratulations in the comments section.

Last month, Cooper provided an update to fans on Clarkson’s health after learning about his prostate cancer diagnosis on camera.

Cooper was shown being told the news in the final episode of the Prime Video show’s fifth season, with Clarkson, 66, telling him the cancer was aggressive, but had been caught at a “really early” stage.

He later informed Cooper he’d undergone a procedure to remove part of it, but later in the episode, Clarkson said from his hospital bed that some of the treatment had “gone awry”, stating: “I don’t know what’s going to happen.”

![Cooper said he’s been supporting Clarkson after his cancer diagnosis](https://static.the-independent.com/2026/06/18/7/35/Jeremy-Clarkson-Farm.jpg)

Cooper reassured fans that Clarkson’s “spirits are high” and that he was supporting his co-star every step of the way.

He told *MailOnline* that he hoped people were encouraged to get checked if they felt anything was amiss and not put it off.

“The message about catching anything early is quite important,” he said. “That's the main thing. You've gotta keep checking, you know what I mean? It's all these things.

“People can sit there and say I'm busy, busy, busy. But actually sometimes you've gotta go, ‘I'm gonna just have to check and see if I'm OK.'”
