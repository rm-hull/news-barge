Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce are married!

After months of speculation over how they’d celebrate their nuptials, the mysterious Madison Square Garden rumors became a reality when paparazzi and countless chauffeured cars began pulling up outside the New York City arena Thursday and Friday.

In a statement shared with *The Independent*, Swift’s representative revealed that the ceremony was officiated by Adam Sandler and that Swift chose not to have bridesmaids.

Instead, her brother Austin Swift served as her Man of Honor and Jason Kelce was the groom’s Best Man.

Around 7.30 p.m. Friday, LED signs outside the venue read “JUST&T MARRIED!” The “T&T” is a nod to their shared first initial, which has been featured on guest merchandise for the event.

The couple’s bleary-eyed celebrity guests began pouring out of the venue in the early hours, helping to confirm who was lucky enough to receive an invite... and who might have been left off the guestlist.

Dior confirmed its status as the creator behind Swift and Kelce’s wedding looks, and revealed that both designs were crafted at the house’s historic ateliers at 30 Avenue Montaigne in Paris.

## Dave Chappelle mocks Travis Kelce's bachelor party on CNN

Divisive comedian Dave Chappelle joined CNN’s Anderson Cooper and Andy Cohen on Thursday evening as part of the network’s coverage of America’s 250th birthday.

At one point, Cohen asked Chappelle about the details of Kelce’s bachelor party, during which the groom and his friends were seen at one of Chappelle’s comedy shows in San Diego.

“I was shocked. I’d never seen anything like that. A whoreless bachelor party. Whatever makes you happy, Travis!” Chappelle joked.

Chappelle also noted that he did not receive an invite to the wedding.

“Now you know, I heard he was getting married at Madison Square Garden. You would think, you know what I mean, I could have got an invite. I didn’t make the 15,000 closest friends,” he said.
