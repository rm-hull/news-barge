Search the Public Notice PortalA developer has admitted the firm “just didn’t have the cash” to turn a closed York care home into affordable housing – but said it is “actively looking” at how the work can be carried out.

James Neward, chief operating officer at YorSpace, told The Press it is estimated that between £2.5 million and £3 million is needed to redevelop Morrell House, off Burton Stone Lane in Clifton.

It comes after plans were approved in 2023 to convert the former council-run care home into 12 affordable flats and a six-person house of multiple occupation (HMO).

It also follows new figures revealing that Morrell House has cost City of York Council £145,217.66 in security and related expenses since it closed eight years ago.

Nearby residents told The Press Morrell House has become a hotspot for anti-social behaviour which they felt would stop if the site was redeveloped.

YorSpace, the not-for-profit organisation behind the Lowfield Green development in Acomb, withdrew its application to redevelop Morrell House in 2024.

Mr Neward said he could “absolutely understand why residents are upset about it – because it’s been empty for so many years”.

  An artist's impression of the plans develop Morrell House into affordable flats run as a co-op 

*(Image: Supplied)*

He said YorSpace looked at the Morrell House site some years ago and secured funding in 2021 to develop plans and create a community group that would run the building as a housing cooperative.

But he said the planning permission in 2023 was subject to section 106 agreements – a legally binding planning obligation between a local authority and a developer – which YorSpace could not commit to at the time.

“We just didn’t have the cash at the time to commit to buying the land,” Mr Neward said. “We’ve been around for a long time but we’re essentially a start-up.”

YorSpace’s model is to lock buildings into community ownership – protecting them from speculative sale – then create permanently affordable sustainable homes for communities.

  Morrell House which has stood empty since it closed in 2018 

*(Image: Dylan Connell)*

It has successfully delivered the first phase of the Lowfield Green development under this model, creating 14 homes with a community-focused approach.

Mr Neward said YorSpace could not afford to progress with the plans for Morrell House when it withdrew its applications and was also “distracted with the delivery of Lowfield Green”.

“There were lots of reasons we couldn’t progress it,” he said. “And so, it was a shame – but we had to step back from the project.”

Mr Neward said it is understood that City of York Council has been working to find an alternative plan to develop Morrell House and has “put calls out for housing associations to provide 100 per cent affordable housing on the scheme”.

However, he said: “[The council] just can’t make that stack up, as I understand it.”

  Morrell House which has stood empty since it closed in 2018

The council’s director of city development, Garry Taylor, confirmed it is “considering options for the site as no bids were received from registered providers for affordable housing when it was out to tender”.

“We are working with partners, including YorSpace, to bring forward proposals that deliver genuinely affordable housing and a long-term future for the site,” Mr Taylor said.

## 'We know it can be done' - developer

Mr Neward said YorSpace remains optimistic that it could one day redevelop Morrell House, adding: “We revisited the numbers again recently. We’re trying to find a route to make this stack up financially for us that supports the long-term vision of YorSpace to deliver more of these cooperative communities in the city.”

He continued: “We have an aligned vision with the council – we want to see 100 per cent affordable housing … We know it can be done. We’ve done it at Lowfield Green so we’d love to come to Clifton and do it there too.”

YorSpace, Mr Neward said, is currently exploring grant funding options for Morrell House and whether it can raise the money from the community. This is something YorSpace did successfully at the Lowfield Green development with the community investing more than £422,000 to take the project forward.

“We are in dialogue with City of York Council because we understand the building is still available and we’d love to see affordable housing happen there,” Mr Neward said. “We’re just trying to find a way to make that happen in a very challenging landscape with an organisation that doesn’t have deep pockets.”

He said YorSpace could also explore borrowing money to fund redeveloping Morrell House but added: “We can’t borrow too much money because then it becomes unaffordable.

“It’s all about securing the grant funding and [how] we can find ways to borrow money hopefully from the local community at a much lower rate than we can from a lender – that’s how we made Lowfield Green stack up.”

He ruled out demolishing Morrell House to build new homes on the site, saying the former care home is “structurally sound” so “it makes no environmental sense to knock that building down”.
