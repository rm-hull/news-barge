Brew+ at Goole Market Hall has announced its exciting line up of events for the coming months.

The venue, which is run by York-based Brew York under their ‘Brew+’ brand, was relaunched in early April this year after a £4m redevelopment project as part of the Goole Town Deal regeneration programme.

As well as live World Cup football, a rotating number of local bands such as The Numbers, Soul Revolver and Northern Velvet, Brew+ has also booked leading tribute acts paying homage to the Arctic Monkeys, Fleetwood Mac, The Killers and Oasis.

Additionally, the venue also has made plans for a regular quiz night, “painting in the pub”, business networking and the return of the very popular Northern Soul events.

Sean Hill, general manager at Brew+ Goole Market Hall, said: “The people of Goole have really welcomed Brew+ and have given plenty of suggestions and requests for what we could do – and we’ve listened.

![](https://wp.theyorkmix.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/GooleMarketHall-1.webp)

“Our events calendar is really shaping up and we’re so pleased that so many organisations want to use this fantastic space. This is just an indication of things to come.”

A notable inclusion in the confirmed events is *Helios*, a touring art installation by renowned artist Luke Jerram, which will be at Goole Market Hall from 25 September to 5 October.

East Riding of Yorkshire Council is bringing *Helios* to Goole as part of the busy, yearlong programme of events and celebrations taking place to mark the town’s 200th birthday. 

![](https://wp.theyorkmix.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Helios-pictured-here-at-Fountains-Abbey-will-be-coming-to-Goole-in-September-Photo-c-National-Trust-Images-Anthony-Chappell-Ross-1000x667.jpg)

*Helios*(pictured here at Fountains Abbey) will be coming to Goole in September. Photograph: National Trust Images, Anthony Chappell-Ross

Measuring six metres in diameter, the artwork features detailed imagery of the sun’s surface. At an approximate scale of 1:230 million, each centimetre of the internallylit spherical sculpture represents 2300 kilometres of the sun’s surface.

Ryan Madin, programme coordinator for Goole 2026, said: “We’re delighted to be bringing *Helios* to Goole as part of the town’s bicentenary celebrations. Located in the heart of the town centre, Goole Market Hall is the ideal venue for this installation, which has drawn huge crowds as it has toured the UK.

“Working in partnership with Brew York, we’re planning a series of fun community events at Goole Market Hall during *Helios*’ visit to the town to encourage as many local people as possible to get up close to this highly acclaimed piece of art.”

The planned events include;

- 26 July & August 12th – Boomchikkaboom (family-friendly rave)
- 30th July – Pub Painting (art classes)
- 2 nd August – The Bat Cave (Comic Con)
- August 7th – Antic Monkeys (Arctic Monkeys tribute)
- August 16 th – Fakermaker (Oasis tribute)
- August 18th – North Ska
- August 22 – Goole Pride (plans tbc)
- August 23 – Northern Soul Ball
- August 28 – The Killers Experience (The Killers tribute)
- September 18 – Gypsy (Fleetwood Mac tribute)
- September 25th to October 5th – Helios touring art installation
- October (tbc) Oktoberfest Beer Festival
