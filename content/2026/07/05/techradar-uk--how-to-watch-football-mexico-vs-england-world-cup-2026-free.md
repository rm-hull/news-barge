England will hope to take their FIFA World Cup 2026 campaign to new heights when they face co-hosts Mexico at the Estadio Azteca in the last 16 — and you can live stream the last-16 clash around the world for free.

At 2,200m above sea level, the iconic Mexico City venue presents challenges unlike any the Three Lions have faced in the tournament so far. The high altitude means England's stars are likely to fatigue quicker than their opponents, who are used to playing in these conditions. Throw in the hostile home support at the 87,000-capacity stadium, plus the ghosts of Diego Maradona's 1986 Hand of God goal here, and you get a sense of the task facing Thomas Tuchel's men. They will have to play much better than against DR Congo in the last 32, when a late double from captain Harry Kane avoided a humiliating exit.

Mexico will be raring to go after winning their opening four tournament games to nil. Three of those victories came at the Azteca, where El Tri have an astonishing record – they have lost just two of their 89 competitive matches at the stadium, while they are unbeaten in 10 World Cup games there, winning seven. They beat Ecuador 2-0 here in the last 32 to end a 40-year wait for a knockout win, so Javier Aguirre's side will be full of belief as they look to emulate Mexico's run to the quarter-finals in 1970 and 1986, the two other times they hosted the finals.
