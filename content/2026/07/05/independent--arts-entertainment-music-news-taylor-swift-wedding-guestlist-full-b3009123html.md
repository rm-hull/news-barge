Taylor Swift’s wedding to Travis Kelce has taken place in the heart of New York City, with hundreds of celebrity guests showing up to celebrate the happy couple.

In the hours before the bride and groom kicked off their cocktail hour Friday at Madison Square Garden, hordes of fans swarmed the area near the venue to get a glimpse of the stars arriving in a steady stream of blacked-out SUVs amid 100F weather.

A-list actors, football players and high-level executives alike were made to flash QR codes from the backseats of their rides to security guards, allowing entry onto 33rd Street.

At the same time, singer Benson Boone and British icon Hugh Grant walked into the massive arena through another entrance, while other stars like Gigi Hadid and Bradley Cooper were seen leaving their New York apartments en route to arrive later at the festivities.

Then, hordes of stars were snapped leaving MSG in the early hours of Saturday morning, presumably after dancing the night away. Among them were Milly Bobby Brown, whose husband Jake Bongiovi is the son of Jon Bon Jovi, whose band were believed to be performing at the event.

Beatles legend Paul McCartney was also spotted leaving with Hollywood star Tom Hanks, with McCartney rumoured to have performed at the ceremony with Swift and Stevie Nicks.

Also spotted: the rapper Ice Spice, Ed Sheeran and his wife Cherry Seaborn, pop singer Gracie Abrams, Dakota Johnson, Jennifer Lawrence and Andrew Scott.

Some celebrities were guaranteed spots on the thousand-person invite list, like Swift’s close friends Zoë Kravitz and Selena Gomez, as well as Kelce’s Kansas City Chiefs teammates. However, other attendees have surprised fans — like Swift’s former best friend Karlie Kloss, who she was last seen with in 2017, and her husband Joshua Kushner.

However, Swift previously warned that she was not planning on keeping the guest list short. She joked on the *Graham Norton Show* in October 2025 that “anyone I’ve ever talked to” would be invited to the big day (Norton was invited along with his husband, Jono McLeod).
