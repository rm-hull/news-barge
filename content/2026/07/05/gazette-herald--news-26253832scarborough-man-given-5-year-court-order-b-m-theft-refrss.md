A man from Scarborough has been given a five-year Criminal Behaviour Order following a theft from a B&M store, North Yorkshire Police said.

Graham Coggins, 47, of North Marine Road, appeared at Scarborough Magistrates’ Court and pleaded guilty to the offence, which took place on Tuesday, June 2.

He received a 12-month conditional discharge, was ordered to pay £18.49 in compensation, and was handed a Criminal Behaviour Order lasting until July 1, 2031, the force said.

  


PCSO Sasha Twyford from Scarborough’s Neighbourhood Policing Team said: "Coggins’ persistent offending has caused misery for retailers across Scarborough.

"I am thankful that the court placed this order upon him."

Coggins is also banned from entering several retailers and supermarkets in a defined area of the town, police said.

In addition, he is not allowed to enter Alma Square or any properties on Alma Square.

He must leave any retail premises immediately if asked by a member of staff, a police officer, or a Police Community Support Officer.

Breaching the order could result in a prison sentence of up to five years, police said.
