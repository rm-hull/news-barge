East Midlands Railway (EMR) will significantly reduce its Intercity timetable from 20 July due to ongoing reliability issues with Hitachi Rail's new Class 810 fleet.

The cancellations will affect hundreds of services on the Midland mainline, including about 20 fast trains daily connecting London, Sheffield, and Nottingham.

EMR said that the temporary timetable aims to provide a more consistent service while they work with Hitachi Rail to resolve the performance problems.

Customers affected by the changes can request a full refund or travel on alternative services immediately before or after their booked journey.

Separately, LNER train drivers, members of the Aslef union, are set to vote on strike action in the coming weeks over a pay dispute, potentially impacting East Coast main line services.
