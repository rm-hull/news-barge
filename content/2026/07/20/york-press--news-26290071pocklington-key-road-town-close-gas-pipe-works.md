## Key road in town near York set to close for gas pipe replacement works

George Street in Pocklington will be closed from Monday, July 20 between Chapmangate and Scaife Garth and from Monday, August 3, Percy Road will be closed between The Avenue and Burnby Lane *(Image: Kevin Glenton)*

A PROJECT to replace ageing gas pipes is set to begin in a town near York and a key road is set to close.

Northern Gas Networks (NGN) is replacing old metal pipes with durable plastic ones in Pocklington.

The project, planned with East Riding of Yorkshire Council, is expected to finish at the end of August.

  


To ensure safety, George Street, one of the key routes from Pocklington towards Stamford Bridge and the A166 will close for three weeks between Chapmangate and Scaife Garth from Monday, July 20.

  Road signs in Pocklington at the junction of George Street and Chapmangate indicate the extent of the closure 

*(Image: Kevin Glenton)*

Percy Road will close between The Avenue and Burnby Lane from Monday, August 5, close to Pocklington RUFC.

Temporary traffic lights will also be on Percy Road/New Street.

Mark Mawhood, Operations Manager at Northern Gas Networks, apologised for the disruption but stressed the importance of maintaining a reliable gas supply.

The company said the work is being done to keep homes and businesses safe and connected.

NGN is the gas distributor for the North of England, overseeing the pipeline network that distributes gas to homes and businesses.
