ORGANISERS of a community day in a North Yorkshire town have celebrated its biggest event yet.

SKATEFEST 2026, held in Norton on Saturday, saw the most successful event in its history at the weekend, attracting hundreds of people from across the UK for a day celebrating skateboarding, arts, music and community.

Organised by Ryan and Sam Swain from Ryedale Skate School, with support from Mayor Of Norton Di Keal and Norton Town Council, the free community event transformed Norton and Malton Skatepark into a vibrant hub of creativity and inclusion.

Throughout the day, visitors enjoyed high-level skateboarding competitions, BMX, scooter and inline riding, live music, graffiti art demonstrations, shuffle dance performances and activities designed to bring people of all ages and abilities together.

The event showcased everything from first-time riders stepping onto a skateboard for the very first time to experienced competitors pushing the standard of action sports in North Yorkshire.

Ryan Swain, organiser of SKATEFEST and founder of Ryedale Skate School, said: "We would like to thank everyone who made the event possible, including Sam Swain, Dinah Keal, Ryedale Skate School, every volunteer who generously gave their time, the Malton and Norton Lions, all of the skaters, BMX riders, scooter riders and inline skaters who travelled from across the country, and everyone who came along to support the event.

"Special thanks also go to the day’s performers and entertainers, including Callum Russell, Strawbs, Jamesy & The Roofraisers, Charlie Boy Sings, DJ SWAG.

"Also thanks to the talented live graffiti artists Moni Graffiti Art (Ben Walgate) and Evoke One, who both created and commemorated artwork to Victoria Collins Smith from Malton to raise awareness for York Against Breast-cancer and the incredible dancers from Shuffle With Sam, whose performances helped create an unforgettable atmosphere throughout the day.

"The event would not have been possible without the generous support of sponsors including Etnies, Shiner Distribution, Koncrete Killerz, Ookkie Skateboards, Skatelite, Surface Matter, Mongo Skates UK and Santa Cruz Skateboards Europe, Live Like Ralph, alongside many local businesses that donated prizes and supported the festival."

The organisers also thanked David Skaith, Mayor of York and North Yorkshire, together with the Mayors of Norton and Malton, for taking the time to visit the event and show their continued support for community-led projects across the region.

Ryan added: “Seeing hundreds of people come together to celebrate skateboarding, art, music and community was incredible. This was our biggest SKATEFEST yet, and that’s entirely down to the amazing people who gave their time, supported us, sponsored the event, volunteered and attended.

I’m incredibly grateful to every single person who played a part. Events like this don’t happen because of one person, Watching people of all ages encouraging one another, making new friends and enjoying action sports in such a positive environment is exactly why we do this. That is what community spirit looks like.”
