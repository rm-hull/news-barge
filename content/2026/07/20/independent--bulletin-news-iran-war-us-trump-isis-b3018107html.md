# Iran compares US to ISIS after Trump strikes bridges and power plants

- Iran accused the United States of behaving like Islamic State, citing threats of strikes against its civilian infrastructure, including bridges and power plants, and a reported attack on a watchtower in the southeastern city of Chabahar.
- Iranian foreign minister Esmail Baghaei condemned these actions as "completely incompatible with international law and the principles of civilised humanitarian conduct," likening them to the conduct of "terrorist groups like ISIS."
- Conversely, Iran has also been accused of war crimes in recent days for allegedly targeting civilian infrastructure in Bahrain, Kuwait, and Jordan, specifically mentioning a vital desalination plant in Kuwait.
- Both nations continued to exchange strikes for a ninth consecutive day, with reports indicating that the US is preparing for a greater escalation of the conflict.
- An anonymous American official revealed that the US is "planning for a wider war" by rushing military aircraft to the region, though they warned of potential limitations due to low stockpiles of air defences and long-range munitions.
