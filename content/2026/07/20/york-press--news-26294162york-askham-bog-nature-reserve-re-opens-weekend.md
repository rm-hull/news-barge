## One of York's best-loved nature reserves reopens after five-month hiatus

Roe Deer have been spotted in the reserve, which reopens after a five-month closure, on Saturday, July 25 *(Image: Andy Whale)*

Yorkshire Wildlife Trust’s oldest nature reserve - based on the edge of York - is reopening after a five-month closure.

Askham Bog, in Tadcaster Road, has undergone major improvements, including a new boardwalk and upgraded visitor facilities, and will welcome the public again from this Saturday, July 25.

The reopening event is part of a community celebration weekend marking both the reserve’s return and the Trust’s 80th anniversary.

  


David Craven, Yorkshire Wildlife Trust’s head of operations in East Yorkshire, said: "Caring for Askham Bog has always been an honour, but it has been particularly lovely to see and hear just how much it means to York’s local community whilst the reserve has been closed."

The celebration weekend will feature guided walks, theatre performances, craft workshops, and pond dipping.

  A new boardwalk is a feature of the reopened Askham Bog 

*(Image: Dee Lewis)*

Askham Bog was gifted in 1946 and became the organisation’s Yorkshire Wildlife Trust's first reserve.

The site is designated a Site of Special Scientific Interest and has been described by Sir David Attenborough as a “cathedral of nature conservation” because of its exceptional diversity of plants, invertebrates and birds.

The reserve is a mosaic of fen, woodland, meadow and open water, and the thread of boardwalks give easy access for visitors.

It supports spectacular royal ferns, the UK’s largest colony of rare gingerbread sedge, and displays of marsh orchids and water violets.

Wildlife ranges from water voles and roe deer to breeding warblers, woodcock and dragonflies, making Askham Bog a notable wild refuge a short journey from York city centre.

Yorkshire poet Ian McMillan has written a special poem in honour of the occasion.

The second half of the poem is buried in a time capsule at the reserve, along with other memorabilia, to be reopened on the Trust’s 150th anniversary in 70 years’ time.

He said: "I wanted this poem to be my birdsong, my splash of a fish in a river, my breeze whispering through the trees."

Yorkshire Wildlife Trust said it was grateful to contractors Bison Bridges for supplying the new boardwalk, Elms Solutions Limited for management of its removal and reinstallation, and Piers Edsall of Environmental Art for supplying the time capsule.
