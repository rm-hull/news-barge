## New care home to bring 75 jobs to York suburb

Acomb Manor Care Home *(Image: Provided)*

A NEW care home is set to bring 75 jobs to a York suburb.

Acomb Manor Care Home, located in Acomb Road, has announced the roles as it enters its final stages of construction - with the property expected to begin welcoming residents in early winter of this year.

The 66-bed care home will offer residential, dementia, and respite care for families across York and North Yorkshire, addressing what developers say is a "rising demand for specialist elderly care in the area".

Kate Desmond, managing director of Acomb Manor Care Home, said: "We are looking forward to welcoming residents, their families and the wider community to Acomb Manor Care Home later this year.

"Our priority is to create a warm, welcoming home where people receive compassionate, person-centred care and are supported to live fulfilling lives.

"Every aspect of Acomb Manor has been designed with residents in mind, from the comfortable living spaces and engaging activities to the dedicated team who will provide personalised support every day.

"We want Acomb Manor to become a valued part of the local community – somewhere residents remain connected to the people and places that matter to them, and where families always feel welcome."

Recruitment for the 75 roles - covering care, hospitality, wellbeing, and support -will begin later this year; the opening is expected to boost employment in the area and will offer long-term roles.

  An artists recreation of how the home will look 

*(Image: Provided)*

Acomb Manor Care Home owned by St Mary’s Care Homes and is one of their most environmentally friendly developments; the building is fully electric and powered by a mix of air source heat pumps and solar PV panels, achieving an EPC rating of A.

The home will span 34,643 square feet and include en-suite bedrooms across three floors, communal lounges, an on-site pub, a hair salon, landscaped gardens, and a schedule of resident-led activities.

Steven Davis, CEO of St Mary’s Care Homes, said: "The opening of Acomb Manor Care Home marks another important milestone in the continued growth of St Mary’s Care Homes, reflecting our commitment to investing in high-quality care homes where they are needed most.

"This represents a significant investment in York, creating around 75 new jobs while delivering a modern care home that will serve local families for many years to come."

Demand for specialist care in North Yorkshire is high, with 57 per cent of all new permanent care home admissions now for people living with dementia, with a quarter of the county’s population is aged 65 or over, well above the England average of 18.4 per cent.
