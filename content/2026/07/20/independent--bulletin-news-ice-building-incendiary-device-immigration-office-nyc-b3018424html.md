# Suspect in custody for lighting ‘an incendiary device’ outside ICE field office

- A man was taken into custody after allegedly igniting an "incendiary device" outside 26 Federal Plaza in Manhattan on Monday morning.
- The incident, which occurred around 8:30 a.m., involved fireworks and flammable liquid in a cart that reportedly had a sign reading "ICE off our streets."
- The suspect was apprehended by police while allegedly wielding multiple airsoft rifles, a helmet, and carbon dioxide cartridges.
- The suspect allegedly wheeled a cart with fireworks and flammable liquid in front of the building to ignite the fire, sources told the *New York Daily News.*
- Three people, including two firefighters, suffered minor injuries, sources told the *New York Post*. Only one person was taken to a nearby hospital, the New York Fire Department told the* New York Times*.
