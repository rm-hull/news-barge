## Gardens are being destroyed in this part of York

I fully agree with Colin Moore. The way gardens are being destroyed in Poppleton is nothing short of disgusting.

Residents used to take care of their gardens making sure they were green and attractive.

Now we see the atrocities of paving and gravel. For some reason, they are always grey coloured. Even worse some lay tacky AstroTurf. In heaven's name, why?

It's a fact that mature gardens help keep areas cooler in summer.

We're all suffering in this heat because of the uncaring stupidity of those who want to concrete everything.

I suggest that those who like barren gardens should go to live in multi-storey car parks.

That would spare the rest of us the damage they're doing to what was an attractive area.

  **Marion A. Hartley
  Beech Way
  Upper Poppleton
  York**

## Nothing 'Marxist' about cycling

I fear James Hennigan gets his underwear in a twist with his denunciation of City of York Council's transport policies as "Marxist".

He'd probably have difficulty spotting a Marxist if he met one in a bar. There's nothing Marxist about promoting walking and cycling.

When I lived in London, a keen cyclist, at least before he became Mayor, was Boris Johnson.

Boris went on to be the only recent Prime Minister of any party to show any interest in public transport but certainly no Marxist!

Traffic problems on Wigginton Road are caused by too many cars accessing the hospital when visiting time starts.

Staggered visiting times for different wards might help but little will solve the traffic issue unless more use public transport, walk, or cycle.

The alternative is massive road construction and property demolition - all very expensive.

Still, I hope Mr Hennigan will write again. We all deserve a laugh in the humourless times.
