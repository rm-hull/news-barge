## Britpop icon rocks North Yorkshire - bringing to life Oasis, Blur and Pulp classics

Fans descended on Scarborough Open Air Theatre for Blur bassist Alex James' acclaimed Britpop Classical show. *(Image: Cuffe & Taylor)*

A BRITPOP icon turned back the clock on the North Yorkshire coast as his Britpop Classical show delivered a rousing tribute to the 90s.

Blur bassist Alex James, backed by the Manchester Concert Orchestra, reworked a string of era-defining hits into sweeping, cinematic arrangements, without losing the punch and swagger that made them famous.

  Blur's Alex James brings Britpop classics to Scarborough. 

*(Image: Cuffe & Taylor)*

  From the opening bars, the high-energy set struck a balance between nostalgia and reinvention, with crowd-pleasing staples including Supergrass’s *Alright*, Pulp’s* Common People,*The Charlatans The Only One I Know, and* Disco 2000*, and The Verve’s* Bitter Sweet Symphony*.

  Fans descended on Scarborough Open Air Theatre for Blur bassist Alex James' acclaimed Britpop Classical show. 

*(Image: Cuffe & Taylor)*

  **Recommended reads:**

  Oasis anthems drew some of the night’s loudest reactions, with *Don’t Look Back in Anger* and*Wonderwall* prompting mass singalongs across the open-air venue. Blur’s own catalogue featured prominently, with*Parklife*,* Girls and Boys*and a punchy orchestral take on* Song 2* standing out.

  Fans descended on Scarborough Open Air Theatre for Blur bassist Alex James' acclaimed Britpop Classical show. 

*(Image: Cuffe & Taylor)*

  Guest appearances added further weight and star power to the bill. Ocean Colour Scene’s Simon Fowler opened with *The Riverboat Song*, Republica’s Saffron brought high-octane energy to* Ready to Go*, and Reef’s Gary Stringer led a crowd-rousing* Place Your Hands*.

  The evening closed with Blur's *The Universal*, as all performers returned to the stage for a celebratory finale that capped a night built on collective memory and enduring appeal.

  Fans at the Scarborough Open Air Theatre waiting for Blur bassist Alex James' Britpop Classical show. 

*(Image: Cuffe & Taylor)*

The show forms part of a record 28-date run at TK Maxx presents Scarborough Open Air Theatre, which continued with Irish pop star CMAT on Saturday, July 18.

The venue has been hosting a series of concerts this summer, including Rick Astley, Madness, Paul Weller, The Kooks, Skunk Anansie, Garbage, Anastacia, Heather Small, Pete Tong, Richard Ashcroft, Billy Ocean, Bowling for Soup, James Arthur, David Gray, Alanis Morissette, Michael Bublé, Deacon Blue, and Bastille.

Upcoming shows include James Taylor, Tom Jones, The Streets, the Sex Pistols, the Scissor Sisters, and Nile Rodgers & Chic.
