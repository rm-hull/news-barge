Noah Kahan issued a heartfelt apology to fans following the last-minute cancellation of his Saturday night concert at New York's Citi Field.

The show was called off due to severe weather and storms sweeping through the area.

In a video shared by E! News, Kahan can be seen telling the crowd during his Sunday night concert: “I know you guys are all here, but I don’t expect any sympathy at all. I’m so f***ing sorry for the folks that sat all day out in the rain and traveled. I’ve been thinking about you guys, and I’m really sorry.”

He highlighted that he has only cancelled six shows in his nine-year touring career, explaining how “they’ve only ever been [cancelled] when we cannot do anything else.”

He added: “I promise you, I would play in a f***ing volcano, the hole of a volcano for y’all. And I, I’m so sorry.”
