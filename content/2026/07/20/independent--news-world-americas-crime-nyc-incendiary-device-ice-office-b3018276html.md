A man is in custody after he allegedly ignited “an incendiary device” outside of a federal building that houses an ICE field office and immigration services Monday morning.

The New York Joint Terrorism Task Force is investigating the incident which took place at 26 Federal Plaza in Manhattan around 8:30 a.m, according to a video posted on X by Freedom News TV. Smoke could be seen billowing from an area directly in front of the building.

Preliminary information indicated that fireworks exploded inside a garbage can, sources told CBS News.

Photos showed officers tackling the man, who has not been publicly identified. The suspect also had airsoft rifles, which deploy pellets, along with a helmet and carbon dioxide cartridges, according to authorities.

“This morning an individual deployed an incendiary device outside of 26 Federal Plaza,” the FBI’s New York field office said in a statement. “The individual has been taken into custody and the FBI New York Joint Terrorism Task Force is investigating the incident.”

Three people, including two firefighters, suffered minor injuries, sources told the *New York Post*. Only one person was taken to a nearby hospital, the New York Fire Department told the* New York Times*.

The suspect allegedly wheeled a cart with fireworks and flammable liquid in front of the building to ignite the fire, sources told the *New York Daily News.*

The cart allegedly had a sign attached that read, “ICE off our streets.”

A video of the suspect, obtained by ABC7, showed the suspect dumping fluid and what appeared to be canisters onto the ground in front of 26 Federal Plaza before setting it on fire.

Flames quickly erupted, and nearby law enforcement approached the scene, apprehending the suspect.

New York City Mayor Zohran Mamdani called the incident “deeply disturbing” in a statement on X.

“I’m relieved no one was seriously injured and that a suspect is in custody. My team is in touch with the NYPD and we will support the federal investigation. Our administration will continue to ensure that every New Yorker is safe in their city — and hold accountable any who threaten that,” Mamdani wrote.

The joint task force combines resources from the FBI with the New York Police Department.

Investigators have not released the suspect’s motive in starting the fire.

26 Federal Plaza is located in downtown Manhattan and houses the New York field office of ICE, immigration courts, U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services and other agencies.

There has been much attention on the building over the last year as the Trump administration has cracked down on illegal immigration and conducted ICE operations in New York City. Protesters have taken to the streets surrounding the building in opposition to the administration’s harsh enforcement.

The administration has faced intense scrutiny for deploying federal agents inside 26 Federal Plaza to arrest immigrants as they arrive for court hearings or check-ins.
