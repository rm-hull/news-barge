POLICE are investigating a ‘suspected arson’ that took place in a popular nature reserve in a York suburb over the weekend.

North Yorkshire Police say that an investigation has been launched after an incident which saw the Hob Moor nature reserve, close to Thanet Road in Acomb, catch alight – causing damage to hundreds of meters of grassland.

As The Press reported at the time, the blaze that was first spotted around 3.30pm on Sunday afternoon (July 19) with emergency services called to the scene quickly after the grass caught alight.

Eyewitnesses at the scene reported seeing smoke in the area and smelling the burning before three units from across the county got to work extinguishing the blaze.

In an update published at around 4.50pm, North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service confirmed crews from York and Acomb responded to multiple calls regarding the fire and that, on arrival, they requested a further appliance from Selby to assist them.

"The fire measured approx. 200 x 300 metres in size and consisted of grassland. Crews used beaters, knapsack sprayers and hose reel jets to fight the fire," said a spokesperson for the service.

   

*(Image: Beth Ebdon)*

Crews continued to fight the fire before leaving the scene at around 5.30pm; they continued to attend the site throughout the evening to check that the blaze had not reignited.

At the time of the incident, the area was closed off by North Yorkshire Police who advised the public to avoid the area and thanked passersby for flagging down their officers to report the incident.

A spokesperson later referred to the incident as ‘suspected arson’, with officers stationed at the site throughout the evening who were spotted investigating in the area at around 8pm that day.

   

*(Image: Max Downing)*

They added: “We are asking anyone who witnessed suspicious activity in the area prior to the fire starting, or who may have captured footage on mobile phones, dashcams, CCTV or doorbell cameras, to come forward quoting reference NYP-19072026-0354.

“Deliberately starting fires is against the law, and even small fires can put lives, homes and communities at risk. It also diverts fire crews away from other incidents. “

This follows several field fires around York – some of which were attributed to the hot weather, but others which were deemed arson and caused damage to fields in the area.

One of these was on Sunday, July 12 when four teenagers were arrested in connection with an alleged arson attack on a farmer’s field in York, which caused £30,000 worth of damage.

The incident saw much of Russell Wagstaff’s fields close to Askham Lane go up in flames at around 3.30pm, with police adding that the incident was thought to occur from a lighter.
