# Five things Andy Burnham said in his first speech as prime minister

Andy Burnham is the UK’s new prime minister – here’s what he said in his first speech

![](https://cdn.jwplayer.com/v2/media/LG2RVpk4/poster.jpg?width=720)

- New UK prime minister Andy Burnham delivered his inaugural speech from Downing Street, pledging to win over voters disillusioned with politics and restore the UK's global stability.
- He promised to decentralise power from Westminster, granting the public greater control over 'life's essentials' and addressing the ongoing cost-of-living crisis.
- Burnham's first specific commitment as prime minister was to eradicate rough sleeping across the country.
- Acknowledging the frequent changes in leadership, he noted he is the sixth prime minister in a decade and seventh since 2016, calling for politicians to 'raise our game'.
- He concluded by urging the public to unite with him to foster a new national sense of unity, common purpose, and positivity.
