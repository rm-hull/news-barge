Powerball, the world's biggest rolling jackpot game, is launching in the UK this week, allowing British players to compete for shared jackpots potentially worth billions ahead of Thursday’s opening draw.

Operated by Allwyn, tickets will be available from tomorrow, with draws taking place on Tuesdays, Thursdays and Sundays at 4am, following sales until 11.55pm the night before.

Players must select five white numbers from 1 to 69 and one red 'Powerball' number from 1 to 26, with tickets costing £4 per line.

The game offers uncapped jackpots starting at £12 million, with the largest ever Powerball win being $2.04 billion (£1.5 billion) in 2022.

UK players will have exclusive prize tiers, including £8 for the 'match two main numbers'.
