A soap star has spoken about whether she would ever return to the Cobbles.

Charlotte Jordan played the popular Daisy Midgeley on ITV’s Coronation Street for half a decade, from 2020 to 2025.

It was a role that saw the actress earn a British Soap Award for Best Dramatic Performance.

Now, Ms Jordan has weighed in on a potential return to Weatherfield, speaking to Radio Times.

She said: "Oh, well, nobody's asked, but for now I'm happy to sort of just keep exploring and keep seeing what... I mean, I only left a year ago, it's very early days, so now I'm happy to keep, keep giving it a good go."

Since leaving the soap, she has taken on new roles in television, first in the radio soap opera The Archers.

Her latest role is in the new BBC period drama series The Hairdresser Mysteries, where she stars as Clary Coombs, alongside Sally Phillips, who plays the main character, Lily Petal.

It tells the story of upmarket hairstylist Lily, who leaves the city to run a hairdressers in a small town, and becomes the centre of gossip and secrets.

Ms Jordan revealed that the transition from Coronation Street to her latest project was relatively smooth.

She said: "I mean, speed-wise, it wasn't an adjustment.

"Corrie was a fantastic training ground in that sense, for this particular job, but I did really like playing a character that couldn't be more different from who I played, which was great.


"I was hoping for that.

"I wanted to play someone next who was the polar opposite, so I'm grateful for that.

"But no, it wasn't super different technically, it was all sort of the same ingredients, but it was a very different world, and it's a very different tone, and it's obviously very different, it's a different genre, different vibes.

"So that was great."

## Coronation Street star's new career after exit

Charlotte Jordan may be enjoying more acting roles since leaving Corrie, but another star is trying out a new creative venture since their exit.

Luca Toolan, known for his role as school bully Mason Radcliffe, was killed off in a January storyline last year.

Now, the actor is making strides in his post-Corrie career with the launch of his own production company.

Speaking in a recent TikTok interview with Phil Roberts on Cheshire’s Mix 56, the 22-year-old said: "Life after Corrie, I’m not actually dead.


"Life continues, I’m making my own stuff now, I’ve got my own production company so that's my passion is making projects."

He continued: "It’s a dream come true, that’s the stuff that really excites me, having that creative licence and writing, directing, making, hearing stuff.

"Honestly, that's the most fun stuff."
