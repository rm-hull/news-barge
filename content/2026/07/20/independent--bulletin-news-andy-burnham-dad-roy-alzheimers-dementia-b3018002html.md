Andy Burnham becomes Labour leader ready to take over as PM on Monday

Andy Burnham's father, Roy, who has Alzheimer's, is unaware that his son will become the next prime minister, which Mr Burnham described as a sadness.

Mr Burnham views his upcoming premiership as a 'family achievement' and noted his father's past passion for proportional representation, a stance he has now adopted.

The incoming prime minister has vowed to 'grasp the nettle' of reforming the adult social care system, drawing on his personal experience with his father's condition.

He described England's social care system as 'the worst of all worlds' and a 'massive' public priority, pledging to expend political capital on its reform during his premiership.

Mr Burnham criticised the current system where vulnerable people pay the most, likening it to the American healthcare system, and aims to create a universal national care service.
