Temperatures to hit 28C as UK continues to bake in hot and dry weather

The UK is set for another week of warm and dry weather, with the Met Office forecasting "plenty of sunshine" across the country, particularly in the south.

London is expected to experience the highest temperatures, reaching 28C on Wednesday, while other parts of southern England and Wales will see highs of 27C for much of the week.

Monday will be mostly dry, though light rain is anticipated in Northern Ireland and parts of Scotland before clearing, with the best sunshine in mainland Scotland, Wales, and western/southwestern England, where temperatures will climb to the mid-20s.

High levels of UV are expected throughout the week due to strong July sunshine, and the settled, warm conditions are predicted to continue into the beginning of next week.

Temperatures are forecast to drop to the mid-teens by next Wednesday in Scotland and northern England, signaling the arrival of cooler weather for the remainder of the week.
