Ladies, gentlemen, and children (those over the age of 12, anyway), this is not a drill: the official trailer for *Avengers: Doomsday* is finally here!

![Avengers: Doomsday | Official Trailer | In Theaters December 18 - YouTube](https://img.youtube.com/vi/irVNGjRFZGk/maxresdefault.jpg) 

Released today (July 20), the new Marvel movie's latest teaser packs a *lot* into its two-and-a-half minute runtime — so much so, in fact, that you probably missed quite a few details that it contains.

Well, worry not your heads, fellow Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) nerds, because I'm here to break down the Marvel Phase 6 film's fifth teaser in real time. So, grab a drink and some snacks, and let's analyze this beast into an inch of its life.

## On your left

 ![Chris Evans' Steve Rogers smiling as he looks down at Mjolnir in Avengers: Doomsday](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/kWCcuekgma6GpuQebTjdS3.jpg) 


Thor isn't the only big-name actor who'll be back as his universally adored Marvel hero, because Chris Evans is also returning to play Steve Rogers.

Admittedly, we've known about Evans' involvement in *Avengers: Doomsday* since rumors first emerged in December 2024. Confirmation that Evans would be back as Rogers came last December in*Doomsday*'s first teaser, too, so only those fans who didn't see or read about his appearance will be shocked to see the first Captain America in this movie.

There are questions about his role in the story, mind you — chief among them being what universe did he end up in at the end of *Avengers: Endgame*. If it's the dimension that The Fantastic Four (and, by proxy, Doom) hail from, is Rogers' decision to stay in this universe the root cause of Doom's family dying?

Moreover, how and why does Rogers return to the proverbial trenches to battle Doom? Sure, he'll always do the right thing and protect those he cares for — which, based on the aforementioned Rogers-starring teaser, now includes his baby son.

Nevertheless, how does he learn about whatever nefarious plan Doom has cooked up? Does he travel back to the MCU's primary universe at The Fantastic Four's behest? If so, how and when do they find out that he's secretly living life in a dimension that he's not supposed to be a part of?

I actually think there's a very specific individual who can help to answer those questions — and you might now know him as the God of Time and/or Stories...

## A godly presence

 ![Thor looking taken aback as he looks at an off-camera Steve Rogers in Avengers: Doomsday](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/2Bi3ECmBRBVnwSHX9J9Y9A.jpg) 


I'll come back to Doom later, so let's focus on some of the other returning — not to mention popular — heroes we'll see in *Avengers 5*, starting with the God of Thunder himself.

Indeed, it's a curious move on Marvel's part to put Thor front and center of this latest trailer. After all, Thor received his own mini *Doomsday* teaser last December, so you might've thought that MCU heroes who weren't spotlighted in said teasers, such as The Fantastic Four, would've featured more heavily here. Clearly, that's not the case.

Why is Thor, then, seemingly being positioned as Doomsday's co-lead? Well, Chris Hemsworth is not only still one of Marvel's most bankable stars, but one of the few who remains from the very start of Marvel's cinematic juggernaut. Placing an instantly recognizable face at the heart of this film, then, is a way to get more butts on seats.

Of course, there's more to it than that. Thor appears to be taking on more of a leadership role in*Doomsday* than past Avengers films, which is evident by the fact he plays the brutally honest negotiator-in-chief in trying to get the MCU's various factions to work together to combat this incredibly powerful new threat in Downey Jr's Doom. Add in the fact that his brother Loki is sure to have an integral role in proceedings (more on this shortly), Thor's clear concern that Doom is someone that Earth's Mightiest Heroes should fear even more so than Thanos, and his desire to protect adopted daughter Love, and Thor's position as a prominent figure in*Doomsday* isn't as outlandish as it first appears.

## Down(ey) for a new MCU role

 ![A close up of Robert Downey Jr's Doctor Doom with his hood up and wearing his metal mask in Avengers: Doomsday](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/aK9ZgazwF5HpXYhNqyBeMj.jpg) 


Alright, let's start with what's obviously the biggest reveal of the entire trailer: Robert Downey Jr's Doctor Victor von Doom.

The Multiverse Saga's new primary villain, the grand unveiling of Downey Jr's second MCU character — the first, of course, being his iconic portrayal of Tony Stark/Iron Man — has been a long time coming.

Based on the brief glimpses we get of him in this latest round of footage, though, it was worth the wait because, hoo boy, does he look not only look very menacing, but also incredibly powerful. Hey, you don't stop an attack from Thor, aka the so-called strongest Avenger, and Stormbreaker with two fingers if you aren't!

So, what's his deal? Based on what we can read into the teaser poster in this blog's previous post, the prevailing theory is that Doom seeks revenge on something — or someone — that led to his loved ones' deaths. And, given that Marvel's latest saga has centered on the multiverse, it's possible that their demise was the result of some universe-hopping or time-traveling shenanigans.

Are the events of *Avengers: Endgame*, and/or other MCU multiverse projects, such as* Loki*,* Doctor Strange 2*, and* Deadpool and Wolverine*, to blame? We'll have to wait until* Avengers: Doomsday* is released in theaters worldwide on December 18 to find out. Regardless, expect Doom's crusade to be based around the loss of his family — and, if other rumors are to be believed, him taking advantage of the multiverse's potential collapse due to Incursions and potentially bringing said family back from the dead.

## Oh my god, it's happening!

Well, who expected this? Nobody, that's who!

Many, if not all, of us though Marvel would withhold the release of *Avengers: Doomsday*'s first official trailer until its forthcoming Hall H panel at San Diego Comic-Con (SDCC) 2026, which takes place on Saturday, July 25. But, lo and behold, the comic book titan has taken us all by surprise and unveiled it today (Monday, July 20).

As I said up top, there's a lot to unpack from the film's latest teaser. Join me, then, over the next few hours as I comb over it with a fine toothed comb and pick out all of the Easter eggs, references, and other bits and pieces you might have missed on first viewing. Excelsior!
