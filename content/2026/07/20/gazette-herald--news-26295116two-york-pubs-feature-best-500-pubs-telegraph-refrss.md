A selection of York and North Yorkshire’s best, award-winning pubs have made a Top 500 of ‘Best Pubs in England.’

The Telegraph first launched its listing last year, to support and raise awareness of the importance of pubs in society and to the wider economy.

This year, the newspaper has extended its horizons to include 75 in Scotland, 50 in Wales and 25 in Northern Ireland.

The Telegraph commented: “The criteria for inclusion – charming character, welcoming staff, rich history and, of course, award-winning beer .”

The newspaper continued: “Every one of the 650 listed establishments has been tried and tested by our panel of six experts. Together they represent the very best the country has to offer.”

  Jon Pybus behind the bar of The Blue Bell in Fossgate 

*(Image: NQ)*

York has two pubs listed, the world-famous Blue Bell in Fossgate, which was established in 1798 and last decorated in 1903, retaining its Edwardian charm.

Run by John Pybus, the Bluebell was York Camra’s Pub of the Year for 2025. The pub, said to be York’s smallest, also featured in a list of York’s best pubs from Dale Harvey of the Great British Pub Crawl when he visited York recently.

Telegraph reviewer Will Hawkes said of the historic pub: “Buoyant York classic that takes a dim view of mobile phones and big groups.”

He continued: “You know where you are at the Blue Bell. All the beer comes from Yorkshire, and there’s a (not very long) list of rules to abide by, the most important of which is not to turn up in a big group, because the pub is quite small (oh, and no mobile phone noise).

“If this sounds a bit miserable, it’s really not: this is a very lively, enjoyable pub. There are two rooms, the rear of which struck me as slightly calmer on my most recent visit. Most pints going over the bar appeared to be Rudgate Ruby Mild, a genuine modern classic (and one of my favourite beers).”

York’s other entry is The Swan in Bishopgate, which Will praises for its “Historic interior and lively chat in this York classic.”

  Blind Jack's in Knaresborough was also Beer Pub of the Year 2025 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

  He continued: *“* The Swan doesn’t look like much from the outside, but inside it’s magic. The preserved 1930s interior consists of two rooms – a Public Bar and a (slightly nicer) Smoke Room – and a connecting lobby in which you’ll find the bar itself.

“Some of the apparently humble decorative detail, such as the terrazzo flooring in the lobby, is marvellous. This layout offers both space and intimacy, and hence conversation flows. Located outside the city walls, the Swan also has a small garden.”

The other North and East Yorkshire pubs listed in the Telegraph 500 include the White Horse Inn, Beverley.

Well-known as ‘Nellie’s’ this Samuel Smith’s brewery owned pub is noted for its characterful interior with gas lighting and a reputation gained from its ownership by the Collinson family, until the long-serving Nellie Collinson sold it to the Tadcaster-based brewery in 1976.

The Old Ship Inn at Bridlington is also listed, as is the Old Star at Kilham, along with the Birch Hall in at Beck Hole, near Whitby, and the Goodmanham Arms, Goodmanham, near Driffield.

  The Tan Hill Inn is often blocked by snow, leading to lock-ins. 

*(Image: Tan Hill Inn)*

The world-famous Tan Hill Inn, near Richmond, known for people getting snowed-in, as at 1,732ft above sea level, it is the highest pub in Britain. A lock-in there was immortalised in a Christmas tv campaign in 2017 by supermarket chain Waitrose.

The Hales Bar in Harrogate, said to be the town’s oldest and most historic bar, noted for its gas lighting, also makes the list.

Finally, we have Blind Jack’s in Knaresborough, which is set in a historic 1741 building in Market Place, though it only opened in 1991. Named after an English road builder, the Grade II-listed pub is noted for its extensive range of real and craft ales. Last year, Blind Jack’s was named Best Beer Pub in the UK at the 2025 Great British Pub Awards.
