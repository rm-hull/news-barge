The White House also touted the poll Monday, framing the findings on social media as “TRUST IN TRUMP” and “Voters Overwhelmingly Support Iran Peace Deal.”

Critics pointed out that the poll was outdated, as the agreement it referenced has since collapsed, and hostilities between the U.S. and Iran have resumed, leading to American casualties.

Recent events include renewed bombings, retaliatory strikes, the deaths of three American service members, and the closure of the Strait of Hormuz.

More recent polls, such as a Washington Post/Ipsos survey from July 8-13, show Trump's overall approval rating at 37%, with only 29% approving of his handling of the Iran conflict.
