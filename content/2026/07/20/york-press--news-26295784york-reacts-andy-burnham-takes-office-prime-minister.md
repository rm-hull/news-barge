YORK politicians have shared their thoughts on Andy Burnham becoming prime minister.

Cllr Claire Douglas, City of York Council’s Labour leader, congratulated the new prime minister but said it was vitally important Mr Burnham’s government recognises the challenges facing the city.

Opposition Liberal Democrat leader Cllr Nigel Ayre said Mr Burnham should reverse planned funding cuts which threaten the loss of tens of millions in the coming years.

Conservative group leader Cllr Chris Steward said it was hard to see what Mr Burnham stood for and he would need to do more to win over York residents.

It comes as Mr Burnham said he wanted Britain to start believing again when he took office as the country’s seventh prime minister in a decade on Monday.

The former Manchester mayor’s entry into 10, Downing Street followed the formal resignation of his predecessor Keir Starmer earlier in the day.

The outgoing prime minister said he was confident he had left the country stronger and fairer than it was two years ago.

Cllr Douglas said she would invite the new prime minister to visit the city as a matter of priority.

Speaking following Mr Burnham’s arrival in Downing Street, the authority’s leader said: “My priority remains securing funding for the city and moving forward with a number of important projects that are already ongoing.

“It’s vitally important the Government understands both our challenges and opportunities and works constructively alongside me and other city partners to allow us to improve the lives of every man, woman and child who call York home.”

Cllr Ayre said York would face choosing between savage cuts to services and huge council tax rises if Fair Funding reforms go ahead under Mr Burnham.

Officials warned in January York faced losing £20 million under proposals to change the way national funding is allocated to councils.

The Liberal Democrats have said York looks set to lose £38.4 million by 2029 according to Government figures for the authority’s forecast core spending power.

Cllr Ayre said: “Residents shouldn’t have to pay the price for a funding system that treats York so unfairly.

“If the new prime minister wants to show he is serious about supporting communities like York, he should start by giving the city the fair funding settlement it deserves.”

Conservative group leader Cllr Steward said Burnham had only come to power because Labour was badly-divided and his predecessor Sir Keir had been disgraced.

The Conservative said: “Here in York we have seen a huge amount about the likes of York Central, the dualling of the A1237 and Haxby Station but delivery has been generally lacking.

“We have Labour politicians at every level of our government, there really are no excuses now but that has been the case for two years already and instead they are just doing damage.”

Labour York Central MP Rachael Maskell said the change marked a new story, not just a new chapter.

The MP said: “We can all be hopeful when a prime minister understands the challenges people face and is giving his all to resolve them, inviting us all to solve the injustices we see around us and to rebuild our country.

“I am so looking forward to being the voice of York Central as part of this new administration and want every person in our community to know that they are part of this too.”

Speaking outside 10, Downing Street on Monday, Mr Burnham said many people felt they were living through decline and they were unable to turn it around.

The new prime minister said: “That’s why we will change politics, to make it more collaborative, more about problem solving than point scoring.

“We will take power out of here and carry it into every postcode in the land so that they can do more.”
