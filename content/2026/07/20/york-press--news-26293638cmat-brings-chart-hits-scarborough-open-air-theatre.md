## International pop powerhouse wows thousands at North Yorkshire open-air gig

Thousands flocked to Scarborough Open Air Theatre to watch international pop powerhouse CMAT. *(Image: Cuffe & Taylor)*

AN international pop powerhouse delivered a standout set in North Yorkshire packed with hits.

CMAT, whose real name is Ciara Mary-Alice Thompson, delighted fans at Scarborough Open Air Theatre with with two hours of tracks from her debut album If My Wife New I’d Be Dead, as well as follow-up Crazymad, For Me, and the Ivor Novello-winning Euro-Country.

  CMAT brings chart hits to Scarborough Open Air Theatre. 

*(Image: Cuffe & Taylor)*

The show opened with Jamie Oliver Petrol Station, with the chart-topping singer-songwriter declaring: "Scarbados, I said Scarbados! My name is CMAT, this is the very sexy CMAT and we have a great show for you tonight."

The Irish star, and self-proclaimed Dunboyne Diana in celebration of her hometown, then wasted no time performing fan favourites When A Good Man Cries, Running/Planning, and Have Fun.

  Thousands flocked to Scarborough Open Air Theatre to watch international pop powerhouse CMAT. 

*(Image: Cuffe & Taylor)*

The audience then united for the viral hit "Take A Sexy Picture Of Me."

CMAT also had a special moment with a young fan from Hartlepool named Monty, inviting him on stage to play guitar during Euro-Country.

*(Image: Cuffe & Taylor)*

The night ended with "I Wanna Be A Cowboy, Baby," and "Stay For Something," where CMAT jumped into the crowd to create a Scarborough catwalk, finishing the show with a flourish.

Singer-songwriter Katy J Pearson opened the night with a set.

  Singer songwriter Katy J Pearson opened the night with a superb set ahead of CMAT's set at Scarborough Open Air Theatre. 

*(Image: Cuffe & Taylor)*

The concert was part of the TK Maxx Presents Scarborough Open Air Theatre series.

The venue has been hosting a series of concerts this summer, including Rick Astley, Madness, Paul Weller, The Kooks, Skunk Anansie, Garbage, Anastacia, Heather Small, Pete Tong, Richard Ashcroft, Billy Ocean, Bowling for Soup, James Arthur, David Gray, Alanis Morissette, Michael Bublé, Deacon Blue, and Bastille.

Upcoming shows include James Taylor, Tom Jones, The Streets, the Sex Pistols, the Scissor Sisters, and Nile Rodgers & Chic.
