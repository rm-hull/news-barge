Search the Public Notice PortalA burst water main left the basement of a former York pub flooded resulting in thousands of pounds worth of damage, a developer at the site has said.

Stuart Stagg, of Tri-Core which is turning the former Jubilee pub in Leeman Road into six flats, said he heard a “big bang” when the pipe burst outside the pub on Sunday evening (July 19).

Yorkshire Water said the leak was “stopped promptly” and its team is working to fix the “burst pipe and repair significant damage to the road surface”.

A large hole has been left in the road on Monday with Yorkshire Water on the scene working to fix the issue. Salisbury Terrace remains closed due to the work.

Mr Stagg said he was on site at the Jubilee pub when the pipe burst on Sunday evening.

“I heard a big bang and I came out,” he said. “We were already flooded … I thought the bridge had gone over on the far side and the river was coming down here – until I got to the gate and saw that the actual road had blown up.

“Within 10 minutes I had 15 kids up on the scaffolding, I had people all around [the site], and then the police turned up and started clearing everybody and putting tape out.

“Everything happened so fast – it was flooding in so quickly.”

Mr Stagg said there was “a lot of water and a lot of damage in 20 minutes”, adding: “My car’s gone – that was up 2ft in water … Hopefully they’ll have [the water main] repaired in a couple of days.”

He said the emergency services finished pumping water out of the basement of the Jubilee pub at 12.30am on Monday before a digger started digging the road up.

The developer said the damage is a setback for the project, which aims to be complete by Christmas and has been “mostly fine” since work started eight months ago.

“We’re looking at thousands [of loss] straight away – maybe £7,000, £8,000 just with materials,” he said. “And then whatever damage has happened downstairs into the basement.”

Mr Stagg said Yorkshire Water would be paying for the damage.

He added that the closure of Salisbury Terrace due to the burst water main means the site cannot have deliveries dropped outside.

Mr Stagg said it is hoped that the road will have reopened before the next delivery is due at the site.

## 'Repairs to the main are underway,' says Yorkshire Water

A Yorkshire Water spokesperson said: "Our teams responded very quickly to the significant burst on a 21-inch water main in York yesterday.

"Due to the size of the main there has been some water ingress at three properties, including the former pub and some residential properties, and damage to some vehicles in the area.

"Our customers teams were on site last night speaking directly with customers affected and they have returned today to continue speaking with anyone affected and will work with customers to understand any damage caused and how we can assist them in the short and longer-term.


“A case manager has been assigned to this as a single point of contact for affected customers to assist them with any claims or ongoing issues related to the burst.

“Repairs to the main are underway and to allow this to be done safely, a road closure and signed diversion is in place.

"Unfortunately, the burst caused significant damage to the road surface, which will take longer than usual to repair and reinstate.

"Our teams will be working as quickly as possible to carry out the reinstatement and will open the road as soon as it is safe to do so.

"We apologise for any inconvenience and thank customers for their patience while we complete the necessary repairs.”
