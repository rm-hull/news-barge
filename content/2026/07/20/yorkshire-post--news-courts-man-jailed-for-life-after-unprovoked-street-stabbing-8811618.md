# Man jailed for life after unprovoked street stabbing

## A man has been jailed for life after carrying out an unprovoked attack in which he stabbed an acquaintance in the neck outside a takeaway.

On September 18, 2025, police responded to reports of an unprovoked attack in Shipley.

Asad Abbas, 41 from Shipley, was outside a takeaway shop on Crag Street, as was his victim and acquaintance, a 52-year-old man.

A court heard how Abbas punched the victim, then used a weapon to stab him in the neck.

![Asad Abbas has been sentenced to life](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/jpim-static/image/2026/07/20/15/50/asad_abbas.jpeg?trim=168,0,169,0&width=640&quality=65)



The man suffered serious but not life-threatening injuries.

Officers swiftly arrested Abbas and he was interviewed, charged and remanded and put before the courts.

The 41-year-old admitted to grievous bodily harm and on Monday (Jul 20) was sentenced to life imprisonment with a minimum term of four years and 250 days.

It was made clear that he will only be released at a time that the Parole Board is satisfied he is no longer a danger to the public.

DC Arman Hussain of Bradford CID said: “Abbas’s offending was violent and unprovoked. He used a weapon which caused a stab wound to the victim's neck.

“The victim was very lucky he wasn’t more seriously hurt.

“Violence on our streets is never the answer. I hope this sentence shows how seriously we take assaults of this nature."
