## Star chef of Michelin Guide-listed restaurant heads to Swinton Park

Jamie Keeble will be Head Chef of the refreshed Chef’s Table *(Image: Pic supplied)*

A chef once named among the UK’s rising stars is set to take over an acclaimed Michelin Guide-listed restaurant in North Yorkshire.

Jamie Keeble, who was part of the opening team at the Michelin-starred Mýse in Hovingham and appeared on series 21 of Great British Menu, has been appointed head chef at the Chef’s Table at Swinton Park near Masham.

He will begin his role in September 2026 ahead of the restaurant’s reopening on October 1.

Mr Keeble said: "Joining Swinton Estate is an incredible opportunity.

"The connection between the estate, the walled garden and the kitchen provides an outstanding platform to create a dining experience that is personal, memorable and rooted in the seasons.

"My cooking celebrates modern British food, inspired by memories, tradition and the exceptional produce we have here in Yorkshire – whether that’s using produce from our kitchen garden, tenant farmers or local Yorkshire suppliers.

"I’m looking forward to joining the team in September and putting my mark on Chef’s Table – I’m excited to see what we can achieve; along with welcoming our first guests from October."

The Chef’s Table, located in the historic Coach House at Swinton Park is known for its immersive dining experience for just 12 guests.

  The Swinton Estate between Masham and Ripon 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

It embodies an 'estate-to-plate' philosophy by using ingredients sourced from the estate and surrounding Yorkshire suppliers.

Executive head chef at Swinton Estate, Steve Darou, said: "Jamie is one of the most talented young chefs working in Britain today.

"His experience at some of the country’s most respected kitchens, including Michelin-starred Mýse, together with his creativity, technical ability and genuine passion for seasonal British produce, make him the perfect person to lead the next chapter of the Chef’s Table.

"We are thrilled to welcome him to Swinton and excited to see his vision come to life from October."

Mr Keeble succeeds former head chef Josh Barnes and joins Swinton Park following the recent appointment of Mr Darou, who previously served as group head chef at London’s renowned St. John restaurants.

Swinton Park said the duo will help strengthen its reputation as one of the UK’s top estate-to-plate dining destinations.

The Chef’s Table will reopen from October 1 for dinner between Wednesday and Saturday from 7pm, and for a five-course lunch on Saturdays from 12.30pm.
