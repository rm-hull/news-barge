A MOTORIST has been banned from driving after being caught at 130mph on the A64.

Robert Cummings, 44, from Burley-in-Wharfedale, has been banned from driving for three months after his case was heard at York Magistrates’ Court on July 14.

Due to the severity of the offence, he was not eligible for a speed awareness course or the option of a penalty fine - instead being fined £666 by the court, as well as being ordered to pay a £266 surcharge and £130 in Crown Prosecution Service costs.

Cummings was driving a white Volkswagen Golf R when he was caught speeding by a mobile safety camera at Whitwell Hill on May 16, with operators alerting police due to the excessive speed.

Officers stopped the vehicle near Tadcaster where the driver admitted that he had "no excuse" for driving at such high speed.

He was reported for the offence and later summoned to court.

Richard Fletcher, deployment manager, said: "Mr Cummings drove at almost twice the legal speed limit with complete disregard for other road users, putting multiple people at risk of serious injury or worse.

"The quick thinking and subsequent actions of the safety camera officer and responding police officers ensured that he was stopped at the earliest opportunity and brought to Court to face the consequences of his actions."
