Good morning. The US launched a ninth night of strikes targeting Iran early Monday, after announcing the death of another American service member – the third in recent days. Donald Trump said the new attacks were “in honor” of the US dead. The Iranian supreme leader, Mojtaba Khamenei, warned of “unforgettable lessons” if the US kept attacking.

Trump has threatened to target Iran’s power stations and bridges to try to compel Tehran to loosen its hold on the strait of Hormuz. Last week the US reimposed a naval blockade on Iranian ports. On Saturday, the military said it had redirected six ships and disabled one since then. Oil prices rose at the opening of trading in Asia on Monday, with the price of Brent crude climbing 2.72% to $90.50 a barrel.

- 
  **Where have US service personnel been killed?** The service member was killed on Saturday in northern Iraq during a controlled detonation of what was called unexploded ordnance on a downed Iranian attack drone. It was the third US death in recent days, after two military service personnel were killed in an Iranian attack on a Jordanian airbase on Friday, with a third missing in action. Since the war began, 17 US service members have been killed.
- 
  **What is the US mission against Iran?** The war was started in February when the former Iranian supreme leader was killed in joint US-Israeli strikes. Trump has indicated several motives for the war, including preventing Tehran from gaining nuclear weapons and trying to force regime change. On Sunday, the US energy secretary, Chris Wright, claimed the mission had shifted toward ensuring oil shipments could move through Hormuz, after the US-Iran ceasefire faltered.

**Mamdani exploring whether he can arrest Netanyahu if he visits New York**

![Mamdani shakes hands with a prisoner.](https://i.guim.co.uk/img/media/9a54c1bc65d485274adf8bef2468d88b3754f8c1/781_0_4868_3894/master/4868.jpg?width=445&dpr=1&s=none&crop=none)

New York City’s mayor, Zohran Mamdani, has said he is reviewing whether his administration could arrest Benjamin Netanyahu if, as expected, he visits the city for the UN general assembly in September. The mayor said he believed Israel’s prime minister “belongs in The Hague”, referring to the Dutch city home of the international criminal court (ICC), which issued an arrest warrant for Netanyahu in November 2024.

Marco Rubio has announced a US policy of trying to “dismantle” the court. Early in his presidency, Trump ordered economic sanctions against the ICC, maintaining it lacked jurisdiction over the US or Israel. Netanyahu brushed off Mamdani’s arrest threats in a radio appearance, saying he was not concerned, and that New York’s mayor was “with the terror actors”.

- 
  **What did Mamdani say?**“I believe that Netanyahu belongs in The Hague. He’s a war criminal who has been charged by the ICC, and what you will find is that is an opinion that is held by many, purely because of what his actions have wrought over these past many years. Whatever the law allows me to do in New York City, that’s what we will do.”
- 
  **What is Netanyahu accused of by the ICC?** The warrant which the ICC issued for the arrest of Netanyahu and other Israeli leaders accused them of war crimes and crimes against humanity in Gaza, including “starvation as a method of warfare, and … murder, persecution and other inhumane acts”.

**Spain win World Cup after Ferran Torres breaks 10-man Argentina’s resistance**

![Spain, wearing their winners’ medals, celebrate as captain Rodri lifts the World Cup](https://i.guim.co.uk/img/media/b50e5ac2b6a5a5846fb8bf53729cf03168a36c85/401_0_5000_4000/master/5000.jpg?width=445&dpr=1&s=none&crop=none)

Spain have won their second Fifa men’s World Cup. The reigning European champions enjoyed a 1-0 victory over Lionel Messi’s Argentina in extra-time at New York’s New Jersey Stadium on Sunday. Spain previously won the men’s World Cup in 2010. Spain’s women’s team are also the current world champions.

- 
  **How did Argentina react to their defeat?** In his match report, David Hytner described their response as “shameful”. Argentina instigated a brawl at the full-time whistle. Enzo Fernández was sent off during the game, while Leandro Paredes received a red card after the match for his part in the fighting. The team then turned their backs on the Spain players as the trophy was presented.
- 
  **What happened to Donald Trump at the final?** The US president, accompanied by Fifa president Gianni Infantino, was booed as they both took to the field for the presentation ceremony before Trump attempted to gatecrash Spain’s victory party picture. Trump’s appearance was the first time he has attended a match at this summer’s World Cup, which theUS co-hosted with Canada and Mexico.
