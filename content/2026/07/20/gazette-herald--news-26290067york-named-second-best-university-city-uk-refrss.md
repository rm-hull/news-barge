A NEW study has named York as the second best student city in the UK - beating both Oxford and Cambridge.

The research, carried out by Canvas Student, has ranked York second in the UK for overall student experience.

It recognised the city for its affordability and student-friendly atmosphere, with estimated monthly living costs of £799 (excluding rent); other factors included in the ranking were the cost of a pint (£5) and a coffee (£3.71), as well as access to two universities and 11 libraries.

Herman Barros, UK director of student operations at Canvas Student, said: "Deciding where to go to university can be one of the biggest choices someone can make.

"From the consideration of costs, social life, accessibility and education, there’s much to keep in mind when deciding where to spend years of your life studying and living."

The study considered living costs, coffee and pint prices, number of universities, average graduate salaries and job availability, safety levels, and access to study spaces such as libraries.

Aberdeen topped the list for its balance of affordability, nightlife, and career opportunities with the estimated cost of living there is £910 per month (excluding rent).

Cambridge, in third place, offers high graduate job availability and an average starting salary of £30,000, along with extensive study resources.

It is followed by Dundee, Oxford, Cardiff, Coventry, Nottingham, Norwich, and Reading.
