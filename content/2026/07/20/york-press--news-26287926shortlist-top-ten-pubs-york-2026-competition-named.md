READERS of The York Press have been marking their ballots to crown their favourite pub of 2026.

The latest in this year's "Best Of" competitions looks sure to be as fierce as ever, with last year's winner re-nominated and some new faces in the mix.

We've counted your personal choices of favourite places and whittled them down to a shortlist of the ten most popular venues sent to us.

From tomorrow (Tuesday, July 21) we'll feature one of them each day in a short profile online and in the newspaper.

A voting slip to choose an overall winner - with all ten names listed and where to post it - is in today's newspaper and will also feature next to each pub profile we publish in the paper every day until Saturday, August 1.

The last day we can accept these postal votes is Wednesday August 5.

This competition has always been popular and shines a spotlight on the city’s vibrant pub scene and celebrates the premises - old and new - that help make York a top destination.

Ben Hardy, editor of The Press and Gazette and Herald, said: "York and the surrounding area have an excellent selection of pubs which are cherished by locals and tourists alike.

"Not only do they bring in footfall and boost the local economy, they also create a sense of identity.

"Pubs act as a community hub for people to meet and our area has plenty of watering holes which are thriving with unique character."

He said: "We were lucky to see four North Yorkshire pubs crowned among the best in Britain by The Good Food Guide for 2025, namely The Abbey Inn at Byland, The Star Inn at Harome, The General Tarleton at Ferrensby and The Owl at Hawnby.

"Pubs in York are also excellent of course.

"Don't miss out on the opportunity to vote for your favourite pub and keep an eye out for our coverage in print and online to see who emerges as the winner."

The shortlist of the top ten pubs in York for 2026, as voted by Press readers, in alphabetical order is:

- The Burns Hotel – Market Street
- The Crooked Tap – Greenside House, The Green, Acomb
- The Knapton Lion – Main Street, Knapton
- The Lord Nelson – Main Street, Nether Poppleton
- The Nags Head – Heworth Road, Heworth
- The Rook and Gaskill – Lawrence Street
- The Ship Inn – Hauling Lane, Acaster Malbis
- The Stone Roses Bar – King Street
- Walnut Tree – Heworth Village, Heworth
- Ye Old Starre Inne – Stonegate
