# US releases identities of 19- and 25-year-old soldiers killed by Iran

- Two U.S. soldiers, 1st Lt. Tyler James Feehan, 25, and Pvt. Isabella Gonzales, 19, were killed in Jordan by Iranian ballistic missile and drone attacks.
- These fatalities represent the first U.S. troop deaths from direct Iranian fire in the ongoing conflict, raising the total U.S. service member casualties to 17.
- Both service members were deployed to support a U.S. mission targeting the Islamic State group, underscoring the significant risks faced by American forces in the region.
- President Donald Trump asserts the war is essential to prevent Iran from developing nuclear weapons and is under political pressure to bring the conflict to a close.
- The escalating conflict has also resulted in the death of an American service member in Iraq from an Iranian drone's unexploded ordnance, while Iranian authorities claim U.S. strikes have caused at least 50 deaths and over 500 injuries.
