Search the Public Notice PortalA FORMER pub in a small East Yorkshire village could be set to become a one-bedroom home.

An application from Richmond Properties (UK) Limited for planning permission to change the use of 31 Main Street in Tickton - formerly The Micro Pig Bar - back to a house has been submitted to East Riding of Yorkshire Council this month.

According to the Campaign For Real Ale, the vacant pub closed at the end of September 2025 and The Micro Pig Bar Facebook page said it had open for eight years.

  



The site measures 116 metres squared and would come with space for two parked cars and a cycle store would be built.

"Given the successful operation of a well-established Crown and Anchor pub in the village, the change of use of the Micro Pig will not affect the local provision of community facilities, documents included with the application said, adding, "Its re-use as a home provides an opportunity to bring a redundant building back into productive use.

"The village had a recorded population of 1,387 in the 2021 Census and has experienced a gradual decline since 2011, indicating a limited a reducing customer base.

"In this context, the settlement is not of a scale that can realistically support two pubs in the long term."

Plans also state that the proposed development requires no external alterations and that the internal layout lends itself to provide an attractive one-bedroom home.
