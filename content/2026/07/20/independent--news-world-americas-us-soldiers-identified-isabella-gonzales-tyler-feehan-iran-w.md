Two U.S. soldiers killed in Jordan by Iranian ballistic missile and drone attacks were identified on Monday, marking the first troop deaths from direct Iranian fire since the conflict began.

The Defense Department confirmed the fatalities as 1st Lt. Tyler James Feehan, 25, from Ewa Beach, Hawaii, and Pvt. Isabella Gonzales, 19, from Carrollton, Texas.

Both service members were deployed to support a U.S. mission targeting the Islamic State group.

These deaths bring the total number of U.S. service members killed in the ongoing conflict with Iran to 17.

The casualties underscore the complex reality that American forces, positioned across the Middle East, face lethal risks from drones, missiles, and aircraft, even when not directly on Iranian soil.

This situation has made other nations targets for Iran and its allies as the fighting has intensified following a breakdown in peace talks.

President Donald Trump has asserted the war is essential to prevent Iran from developing nuclear weapons.

Trump, a Republican, is now under political pressure to bring the conflict to a close and avoid the type of prolonged Middle East engagement he had previously campaigned against.

The escalating conflict continues to claim lives on both sides.

An American service member was also killed in northern Iraq on Saturday during a "controlled detonation" of unexploded ordnance from a downed Iranian drone, U.S. Central Command reported.

A second service member sustained a minor injury and is receiving medical treatment. The military stated it would withhold further information until families had been notified.

Iranian authorities claim that U.S. strikes over the past three weeks have resulted in at least 50 deaths and more than 500 injuries, including eight fatalities from a strike on a bridge last Friday.

Both sides have targeted civilian infrastructure vital to millions. Workers on ships, along with foreign workers and others in Gulf nations, Israel, and Lebanon, have also perished in the violence.

The initial U.S. military fatalities occurred shortly after the U.S. and Israel initiated the war on February 28, when an Iranian drone strike at a civilian port in Kuwait killed six American soldiers.

These soldiers, part of a supply and logistics unit from Iowa, were working in a shipping container-style building that lacked defenses.

A seventh soldier died more than a week after being wounded during a March 1 attack by Iran on the Prince Sultan Air Base in Saudi Arabia. Later in March, six service members were killed when a KC-135 refueling aircraft supporting U.S. military operations against Iran crashed in Iraq.

The aircraft was in "friendly" airspace when an unspecified incident involving another aircraft took place, according to U.S. Central Command.

Most recently, on Monday, the U.S. military announced a Navy pilot died in a helicopter crash in the Arabian Sea.

The Navy initially described the July 1 crash as an emergency landing, stating there was "no indication the emergency was caused by hostile action." The three other sailors aboard the helicopter were rescued.
