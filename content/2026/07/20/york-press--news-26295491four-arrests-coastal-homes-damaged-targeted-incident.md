Two properties in separate North Yorkshire towns were damaged by a car in a 'targeted' incident last night.

The two criminal damage incidents took place yesterday evening (Sunday 19 July) in the Scarborough area.

The first took place at 11.18pm at a property on Grange Avenue in Filey. Criminal damage was done to the property and a car rammed into the house. An engineer and assessments are needed to determine the level of damage.

The second took place at West Street in Scarborough, where another property was damaged.

  


A spokesperson for North Yorkshire Police said: "Thankfully no-one was physically harmed in either incident.

"A man aged 27, a boy aged 17, and two women aged 44 and 51 have been arrested in connection with these incidents and currently remain in custody.

"It's believed these are linked and targeted events, no wider risk to the community has been identified."
