TV presenter Holly Willoughby is back with an "exciting new project".

The 45-year-old former ITV host has launched a YouTube channel, Holly Willoughby Together, taking her signature charm and conversation to a new digital platform.

It marks her first major project since stepping down from the daytime show This Morning in October 2023.

## Holly Willoughby returns with new project after This Morning


Willoughby teased her channel in a post to social media last week, which also revealed its pink and red branding.

She said: "I'm so proud of the work all the team who make Together have put into this exciting new project.

"We've had a lot of fun developing it and look forward to seeing what you think."

Filmed in a conversation-focused studio, the channel will feature a lifestyle series exploring practical advice, fashion, beauty, and health, alongside a mix of celebrity and expert guests.

The line-up includes actor Stanley Tucci, Spice Girl Emma Bunton, All Saints singer Nicole Appleton, and comedians Joanne McNally and Katherine Ryan.

The show will also feature candid conversations with a variety of guests, including a tech safety campaigner, chefs, a florist and a make-up artist.

Episodes will be released twice a week, premiering on Tuesday, July 28.

Holly Willoughby Together will be available on YouTube, digital streaming platforms, and across social media.

## Why did Holly Willoughby leave This Morning?

The channel marks Holly Willoughby’s return to broadcasting following her departure from This Morning in 2023 after 14 years on the sofa.

In a statement at the time, she said: "I have let ITV know today that after 14 years, I will not be returning to This Morning.

She added: "It's been an honour to just be part of its story and I know this story has many chapters left to go.

"Sadly, however, I now feel I have to make this decision for me and my family."

"I will miss you all so much."

Her exit came months after co-host Phillip Schofield left ITV after admitting to an "unwise, but not illegal" affair with a young male colleague on the show.

The star was also part of a kidnap and murder plot, which saw Gavin Plumb, 36, from Harlow, convicted.

Plumb told what he believed to be a US-based co-conspirator, who was actually an undercover police officer, that he had prepared a "kidnap and restraint kit" with the intention of abducting Ms Willoughby.


He was convicted of soliciting murder, encouraging or assisting the commission of kidnap, and encouraging or assisting the commission of rape, and was sentenced to life in prison, with a minimum term of 16 years.

Holly Willoughby chose to waive her right to anonymity, typically granted to alleged victims of sexual offences in the UK.

She did not attend any of the court hearings, and her victim impact statement was not made public, although prosecutors described its contents as "catastrophic."
