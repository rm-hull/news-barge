# Fifa says it will investigate violent clash between Spain and Argentina after World Cup

- FIFA has launched an investigation into the "ugly scenes" that marred Spain's World Cup final victory over Argentina, following clashes between players on the pitch.
- Incidents included Argentina midfielder Leandro Paredes shoving Spain's Eric Garcia and Gavi, and Argentina defender Nahuel Molina appearing to strike Spain captain Rodri.
- Footage also showed Argentina assistant coach Daniel Ayala seemingly striking Spain midfielder Dani Olmo during the post-match altercations.
- FIFA's disciplinary committee has appointed a Disciplinary and Ethics Prosecutor to investigate potential breaches, but no specific players were named, and no timetable was provided for the inquiry.
- Further tension arose when Argentina's squad turned their backs to the podium as Spain lifted the trophy, an act of disrespect criticized by pundits like Gary Neville and Alan Shearer.
