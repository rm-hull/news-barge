                                    King Charles has confirmed his continued support for the Great Yorkshire Show following another successful event.

The Royal Household has announced that His Majesty will remain Patron of the Yorkshire Agricultural Society following a review of Royal Patronages, ensuring the King's continued support for the organisation behind the Great Yorkshire Show.

The announcement came as the 167th Great Yorkshire Show drew to a close on Friday after four days at the Great Yorkshire Showground in Harrogate.

The final day also saw the annual President's Handover, with Nick Lane Fox handing over the presidency of the Yorkshire Agricultural Society to Rodrica Straker for 2026-27.

Show Director Rachel Coates said:

"It has been an incredible four days at the Great Yorkshire Show. We've had fine weather, the highest standards of competition and some really thrilling moments.

"I hope everyone who visited the Show has taken away a little bit of extra appreciation for British food and how it is produced, and we look forward to welcoming you back in 2027.

“I would like to thank our outgoing President, Nick Lane Fox for his invaluable contributions over the last 12 months.

"The Show's success really is the sum of all of the contributions made by our volunteers, staff and stewards, and I'm hugely grateful to everyone, including our visitors, who have made this record-breaking Great Yorkshire Show such a wonderful occasion in its 167th year.”


Friday's programme featured a host of competitions, entertainment and special guests.

Clarkson's Farm star Harriet Cowan attracted large crowds on the Ariat GYS Stage before presenting rosettes in the Beef Young Handlers competition and meeting the Field Nurse team, which provides free health checks and wellbeing support for farmers.

Elsewhere, Emily Fretwell, 22, from Penistone was crowned Yorkshire's Best Young Farmer, while British Saanen goat Dalestone Britney was named Best Goat in Show for the second time at the age of nine.

Michael Longton, from Lancaster, won the prestigious sheepdog trials with his dog Max, while Dan Whelan successfully defended his Great Yorkshire Pole Climbing title.

Entertainment on the final day also included performances from Knaresborough's Knot Another Choir and line dancing from the Boot Scootin' Cow Girls.

The next Great Yorkshire Show will take place from Tuesday 13th July to Friday 16th July 2027.
