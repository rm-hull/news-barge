# Instagram accused of creating addictive algorithms for teens in latest trial

- Meta Platforms is facing a trial in Tennessee, accused of intentionally designing Instagram to be addictive and contribute to a youth mental health crisis.
- Tennessee's Attorney General Jonathan Skrmetti alleges Meta violated consumer protection law by misleading the public and concealing internal research showing Instagram's harm to teenagers.
- The state seeks financial penalties and a court order to modify Instagram features like autoplay, Reels, and notifications, which are deemed detrimental to teen mental health.
- Meta denies the claims, asserting it has implemented safety controls for teens and is protected by federal law (Section 230) from liability for user-generated content.
- This trial is part of a broader legal challenge against Meta, with similar lawsuits across nearly every U.S. state, including a New Mexico case that resulted in a $375 million award against the company.
