## How to get a free bowl of home-made pasta in York city centre this weekend

Nduja paccheri. *(Image: Pic supplied)*

Fresh pasta will be served up for free in a mouthwatering giveaway in York city centre.

Pranzo Italian is marking its first anniversary on Church Street by treating the public to a taste of its signature dishes on Saturday, July 25.

The offer runs from 11am to 12pm and includes their beef shin mafalde and nduja paccheri.

Marco Greco, owner of Pranzo, said: "We want to say thank you to the York community.

"Whether you're a regular customer or will be trying our pasta for the first time, we'd love to see you.

"Opening a restaurant in York was always on the cards for Pranzo.

"The people of York have been so welcoming in our first year here."

The restaurant is currently ranked 15th out of 797 in York, based on 508 reviews.

Mr Greco, a Yorkshire-based chef, was inspired by his Calabrian roots and the cooking of his grandmother.

Pranzo’s team makes fresh pasta every morning, offering menus that reflect Calabrese tradition.

The York venue follows the same format as its other locations, serving Roman-style rectangular pizza, seasonal specials, Sunday roasts, and a set menu.

It also features wines imported from family-run vineyards in Italy.

Located at 15 – 17 Church Street, the restaurant is close to York Minster, the Jorvik Viking Centre and the Theatre Royal.

The giveaway is first-come, first-served and guests are encouraged to register in advance.

Pranzo’s first location opened in Ilkley in 2018.

To claim a free pasta ticket, visitors can register at pranzoitalian.co.uk/york-restaurant/pasta-giveaway.

The event is free to attend.

Pranzo’s menus feature dishes that capture the bold and spicy flavours of Calabria.
