## Have your say on York café's bid to sell alcohol in Coney Street

Coney Street in York *(Image: Newsquest)*

### What is the Public Notice Portal?

The Public Notice Portal carries statutory public notices published in local newspapers and is the fastest and most effective way of finding out what is happening in YOUR neighbourhood.

Search the Public Notice PortalA York café has applied for a licence to sell alcohol.

Federal Café Limited submitted the application to the City of York Council on July 6, requesting permission to serve alcohol at their premises in 5 Coney Street from 8am to 11pm, Monday to Sunday.

The café, located in the heart of York, is known for its breakfast and brunch offerings.

The application is open for public comment until August 3.

The City of York Council's register and the application record can be inspected by prior arrangement with the licensing team.

Anyone wishing to object or comment must submit their views in writing to the City of York Council Licensing Team at Hazel Court, EcoDepot, James Street, York, YO10 3DS, or by email to [licensing@york.gov.uk.](mailto:licensing@york.gov.uk.)

The council has urged that any representation made should clearly state the grounds for the objection or support.

The council also warned that it is an offence to knowingly or recklessly make a false statement in connection with an application, with an unlimited fine upon conviction.
