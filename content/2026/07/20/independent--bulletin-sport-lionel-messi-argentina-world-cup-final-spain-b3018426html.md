# What Lionel Messi said after Argentina’s World Cup final defeat to Spain

- Lionel Messi has spoken out about the "immense pain" he is experiencing after Argentina's World Cup final defeat to Spain.
- The 1-0 loss to Spain, secured by Ferran Torres's extra-time goal, is widely believed to be Messi's final World Cup appearance as he approaches his 39th birthday.
- The match concluded with a mass brawl on the pitch, reportedly involving Argentina's Leandro Paredes throwing punches at celebrating Spanish players.
- Messi posted an emotional statement on Instagram, saying: “The pain is immense, and it will take time for this wound to heal. But I also hold on to all the good.” He also thanked fans for their support, acknowledged Argentina's achievement of reaching two consecutive finals, and congratulated Spain.
- Argentina manager Lionel Scaloni expressed uncertainty about Messi's international future, praising him as the greatest footballer ever and hoping the country remains proud of him.
