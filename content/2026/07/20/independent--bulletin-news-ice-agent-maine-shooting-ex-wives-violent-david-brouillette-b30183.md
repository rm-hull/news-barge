ICE agent in Maine shooting has history of violent behavior, relatives say

ICE agent David Brouillette is accused of fatally shooting Colombian immigrant Johan Sebastián Durán Guerrero during a traffic stop in Biddeford, Maine.

Brouillette's ex-wives, Ashley and Lucinda Brouillette, claim ICE failed to contact them during his background check, despite his documented history of violent behavior and mental health issues.

Ashley Brouillette stated she had warned people for years that her ex-husband would hurt someone and that he continued to leave threatening messages after their divorce.

Lucinda Brouillette, his second wife, sought a protective order against him, citing a history of anger, aggression, and abusive behavior, and had previously reported concerns to law enforcement.

The incident raises questions about ICE's hiring practices, which were accelerated under the Trump administration, leading to lowered standards and a rapid increase in personnel.
