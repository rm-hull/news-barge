![The headline on the front page of the Daily Telegraph reads: “Burnham heralds new era of big state”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/1040/cpsprodpb/020a/live/58e6b370-83cb-11f1-b976-0b9c15b0ccfc.jpg)

The majority of the papers have split their focus between two stories: Andy Burnham, who will become UK prime minister on Monday, and Spain's victory over Argentina in the World Cup final. The Daily Telegraph features a photograph of Spain's team adorned in medals and holding the World Cup aloft, alongside a story that highlights the incoming prime minister's plan for a "new era of big state". It says that Burnham is "eyeing public ownership of key industries", including the National Lottery, Thames Water and local bus services.

![The headline on the front page of the i Paper reads: “Burnham pledges 'breathing room' to help UK on cost of living as he moves into No 10”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/830/cpsprodpb/3f48/live/434cdcb0-83c6-11f1-bee8-53ce494e1abc.jpg)

A number of the papers went to print before the result of the World Cup final was known, but the i Paper captures Spain's 1-0 extra-time win with the headline "Viva España!". Spanish footballer Ferran Torres scored the winning goal in the 106th minute. The paper leads with a preview of Burnham's first speech as prime minister on Monday, which it says will include a pledge to ease the cost of living.

![The headline on the front page of the Guardian reads: “Time for UK to face up to its challenges - Burnham”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/782/cpsprodpb/f619/live/02517860-83bc-11f1-bee8-53ce494e1abc.jpg)

The Guardian writes that Burnham is set to begin appointing his Cabinet on Monday afternoon, shortly after he formally accepts the premiership from the King at Buckingham Palace. The paper reports that ministers remain in the dark about whether they will be offered roles in Burnham's team, but adds there is "widespread belief" that Shabana Mahmood will be made chancellor and Ed Miliband the foreign secretary.

![The headline on the front page of the Times reads: “Burnham: My 10-year plan”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/809/cpsprodpb/126a/live/a61b6910-83cb-11f1-b976-0b9c15b0ccfc.jpg)

The 2026 World Cup champions are also splashed across the Times, which put out a fresh edition on Sunday night to include Spain's triumph. The paper notes that US President Donald Trump was present at the final, where it says he was booed by a portion of the 80,663-strong crowd. The front page also includes an interview with Burnham, in which he says he wants to "draw a line" and show it is possible to "do things in a different way". He tells the paper that he will offer Britons a series of "tangible" improvements to help with the cost of living, as the first step in his 10-year plan to "rewire the economy".

![The headline on the front page of the Mirror reads: “Burning ambition”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/802/cpsprodpb/de36/live/9a266b30-83be-11f1-926f-c90d1bcfbc84.jpg)

"Burning ambition," declares the Daily Mirror, which notes Burnham will be the UK's seventh prime minister in 10 years. Much like other papers, the Mirror points to his first speech as prime minister, where it says he will promise to "give Britain more breathing space" on the cost of living.

![The headline on the front page of the Daily Express reads: “New PM has no mandate to raise taxes again”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/802/cpsprodpb/740b/live/8f81be40-83b5-11f1-bee8-53ce494e1abc.jpg)

Conservative leader Kemi Badenoch tells the Express that Burnham is entering Downing Street on Monday without "either a plan for government or a mandate for raising taxes". The paper says Badenoch has "torn into the incoming prime minister", after he made a suggestion that he could "ask for a little more".

![The headline on the front page of the Financial Times reads: “Burnham seeks tight grip on spending with beefed-up control centre at No 10”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/1056/cpsprodpb/b9d9/live/6ce0c310-83b3-11f1-b976-0b9c15b0ccfc.jpg)

The incoming prime minister's approach to spending also leads the Financial Times, which says Burnham will enter Downing Street seeking "greater sway" over important public spending decisions. The paper reports he has already held talks on a "significant restructure" at No 10, so he has more direct oversight over the execution of his government's priorities.

![The headline on the front page of the Daily Mail reads: “Burnham doesn't speak for us, say voters in South”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/810/cpsprodpb/1b24/live/e1a78ae0-83bc-11f1-b976-0b9c15b0ccfc.jpg)

The Daily Mail says Burnham faces an "extraordinary crisis of legitimacy" upon taking office on Monday, writing that the "self-styled King of the North" faces a "battle" to win over voters outside of his "Manchester stronghold". The paper has done its own polling, which it says suggests that only 24% of the people it surveyed in south-east England believe Burnham's rise to power has been "legitimate".

![The headline on the front page of the Sun reads: “Turmoil”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/3840/cpsprodpb/6b50/live/d2e3f0c0-83bc-11f1-bee8-53ce494e1abc.jpg)

Burnham is taking the reins amid global "turmoil", or so says the Sun, which writes that chaos is "already engulfing the crucial issue of oil and gas drilling".

![The headline on the front page of the Metro reads: “Welcome home... there've been a few changes”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/828/cpsprodpb/962e/live/6536f080-83b3-11f1-b976-0b9c15b0ccfc.jpg)

The Metro says England's football team will arrive home from the US on Monday to "a few changes", characterising their World Cup campaign as a seven-week "rollercoaster political ride".

![The headline on the front page of the Daily Star reads: “Rhian: Truth about Vernon 'sexts'”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/802/cpsprodpb/0a92/live/a5b5f280-83b5-11f1-bee8-53ce494e1abc.jpg)

And finally, former model Rhian Sugden is set to release a new "tell-all" autobiography, according to the front page of the Daily Star.
