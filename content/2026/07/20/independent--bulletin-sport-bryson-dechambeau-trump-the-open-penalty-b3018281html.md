The penalty was issued late on Friday night for improving the area of his backswing by flattening long grass on Royal Birkdale’s fifth hole.

DeChambeau, a friend of Trump, allegedly requested Trump's involvement, drawing parallels to Trump's past intervention in a Fifa decision regarding Folarin Balogun's ban.

R&A chief executive Mark Darbon confirmed he had not received a call from Trump, stating the penalty decision was 'clear cut' and based on rules.

DeChambeau finished 14th, six shots behind champion Ryan Fox, and later had his request to retrieve his previous day's scorecard declined by the R&A.
