A VOID has opened in the road in a suburb of York close to the site of a large burst water main which caused flooding, road closures, and disruption overnight.

North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service said crews from Acomb were called to Balfour Street in York around 7.40pm yesterday (Sunday, July 19) to multiple reports of the surface water flooding from the affected main which measures 21 inches.

A service spokesperson said crews pumped out the basement of a public house and blocked water access from an affected property.

Last night, North Yorkshire Police said it was at the scene assisting residents and that some roads were impassible and was advising the public to find alternate routes for their journeys.

  


Diversions are in place and local bus services may be affected, City of York Council said.

Yorkshire Water is reporting a temporary road closure between Salisbury Terrace and Jubilee Terrace.

The utility company said Swinnerton Avenue bollards have been removed which will take vehicles onto Lincoln Street, whilst it carries out essential repairs.

It added that properties in YO24 and YO26 may have no water or low water pressure this morning.

A company spokesperson said it will be getting water in the area back to normal as soon as possible.

Customers are advised that water may be discoloured for a while when it comes back, but running the tap for a few minutes will see this clear.

The Press has a reporter at the scene and will be updating the story here.

Yorkshire Water has also been contacted for an update.
