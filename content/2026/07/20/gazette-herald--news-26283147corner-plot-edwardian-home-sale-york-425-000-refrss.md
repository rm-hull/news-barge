A three-bedroom Edwardian house in York has hit the market for £425,000.

The semi-detached property in Ouseacres is said to "boast period charm, with stained glass windows and original fireplaces" according to Price Independent Estate Agents.

The house is on a corner plot, and has a gravel driveway with a single garage, while inside, there is an entrance hall with tiled flooring.

  Reception room with stained glass windows and original fireplace 

*(Image: Price Independent Estate Agents)*

The open-plan reception rooms are said to be ideal for families, and there is an extra reception room which could be used as a playroom, study, or dining room.

The modern dining kitchen has French doors leading to the enclosed rear garden, which is home to a lawn and patio area.

  Dining kitchen with French doors to the rear garden 

*(Image: Price Independent Estate Agents)*

The estate agents said the property has easy access to York city centre, York Station, and York Central.

The home is tucked away on a generous corner plot, where period charm meets modern family living.

There is tiled flooring, high ceilings, and original character that create a lovely first impression, and throughout the house are stained glass windows and fireplaces.

  Enclosed rear garden with lawn and patio area 

*(Image: Price Independent Estate Agents)*

The two reception rooms flow beautifully together, creating a fantastic family living space.

The kitchen has been thoughtfully opened up to create a lovely sociable dining space.
