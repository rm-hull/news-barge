FDA retracts Taylor Farms parasite alert after confirming false positive on iceberg lettuce

The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) initially linked a nationwide cyclosporiasis outbreak to Taylor Farms iceberg lettuce but later retracted the claim, stating it was a "false positive."

The FDA apologized to Taylor Farms after its initial detection of the cyclospora parasite on lettuce from Mexico was found to be incorrect, with no confirmed positive sample results as of July 19, 2026.

Thousands of cases of cyclosporiasis, a gastrointestinal illness causing "explosive" diarrhea, have been reported across the U.S., with nearly 7,000 confirmed and suspected cases by July 13.

Health officials are struggling to identify a definitive source for the widespread outbreak, with some experts suggesting that staffing and funding cuts at the CDC have hampered the response.

Cyclosporiasis is spread through food or water contaminated with fecal matter, and public health experts recommend thorough washing of produce and hands to reduce the risk of infection.
