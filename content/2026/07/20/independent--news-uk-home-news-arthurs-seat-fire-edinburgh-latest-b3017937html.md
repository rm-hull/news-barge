Fire crews are continuing to battle a fire that broke out at Arthur’s Seat in Edinburgh on Sunday evening.

The Scottish Fire and Rescue Service (SFRS) had six appliances at the scene at 6.30am on Monday after first responding to reports of the fire at 6.38pm on Sunday.

It came as SFRS continued to battle a wildfire in the Cairngorms, which has been burning since Wednesday.

![SFRS first responded to reports of the fire at 6.38pm on Sunday](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/20/08/01KXZ60EPH04FEKR4Q4K4ASCGX.jpg)

A spokesperson said: “We were alerted at 6.38pm on Sunday, 19 July to reports of a fire in the open at Arthur’s Seat in Edinburgh. Operations Control mobilised four fire appliances and crews are still at the scene. There are no reported casualties.”

Earlier on Sunday, Cairngorms park officials said the large-scale wildfire affecting the region is now “largely surrounded”, and expect evacuated residents to be able to return home “later today”.

On Sunday afternoon, park bosses said the fire, which burnt around 4sqkm at its peak, is largely under control.

The park authority announced that the incident remains ongoing, nevertheless, with fire crews and other partners tackling the blaze together.

Park bosses said resources, including helicopter support, have been integral in battling the fire in tougher-to-reach areas.

![The wildfire burning near Nethy Bridge in the Cairngorms National Park in northeast Scotland](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/19/16/6f70130686b26970d20045e636742fd4Y29udGVudHNlYXJjaGFwaSwxNzg0NTYwNDI5-2.85332997.jpg)

Gavin Miles, director of planning and place at the park authority, said: “News from Glenmore this morning is very encouraging, with colleagues from SFRS indicating that the fire is now largely surrounded. This is, though, still a live situation and teams are still very active on the ground, so we are taking nothing for granted at this stage.

“We’re really grateful for the continued efforts of everyone helping fight the fire in Glenmore, and for the cooperation of local residents, visitors and businesses at what I know is a very difficult time.

“The Ski Road and surrounding areas are likely to be off-limits for some time to come, given the sheer number of people and machinery on-site, but we’re hopeful that residents and campers directly impacted by the exclusion zone will be able to gain access later today.”
