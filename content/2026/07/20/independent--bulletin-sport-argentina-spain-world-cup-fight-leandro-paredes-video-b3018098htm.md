Argentina spark shocking brawl with Spain at full time in World Cup final

Argentine players sparked a mass brawl with Spain following their 1-0 World Cup final defeat in New Jersey.

The altercation began after the final whistle when Spanish substitutes ran onto the pitch to celebrate.

Argentina defender Nahuel Molina reportedly threw a punch at Spain's captain Rodri, and midfielder Leandro Paredes had confrontations with Eric Garcia and Gavi.

Argentina's head coach, Lionel Scaloni, and other coaching staff intervened to try and stop the brawl.

The players' behaviour has been widely condemned, with Alan Shearer calling it 'terrible' and Gary Neville branding it 'a disgrace'.
