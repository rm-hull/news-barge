# Nigel Farage’s donation drama is a question of transparency and disclosure - Yorkshire Post Letters

## From: Dilys Morgan, Braddons Hill Road East, Torquay.

Nigel Farage wants us to believe that his decision to stand in the 2024 General Election, having first ruled it out on May 23 before reversing course on June 3, had nothing to do with the £5m he received around the same time from crypto billionaire Christopher Harborne. On the BBC, he dismissed it as simply a gift from a friend, not a donation.

That misses the point. Whether Harborne is a friend is irrelevant. He is Reform’s biggest political donor, with substantial financial interests that could be affected by government policy. The real issues are conflict of interest, transparency and disclosure. Is it really credible that a £5m personal gift played no part in Farage’s decision?

We are also asked to believe it is pure coincidence that Harborne has donated more than £20m to Reform UK while Farage now campaigns to relax cryptocurrency regulation - changes that could see significant financial benefits for Harborne.

![Reform UK leader Nigel Farage makes a statement to the media. PIC: Gareth Fuller/PA Wire](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOmUxMWQ3MmFjLTY3MGUtNDVjOS1iZTY0LTQ1NjI4NDZhMDc3YjpkYjllMWY2Yi0zODQxLTQxMWMtOTMwYS1mNTVkODVlMjA5ZWM=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



Farage insists he cannot be bought. Isn’t this a signal he has already been? He also once recorded a Cameo video where he was paid under £100 to say “Up the RA (IRA)”. What won’t he say or do for money?

And of course Farage claims the ‘Establishment’ is out to get him. In reality, a multi-millionaire party leader bankrolled by billionaire donors is very much ‘the Establishment’. What’s actually happening is right and proper scrutiny of very dodgy ‘Establishment’ dealings.

So now Farage has resigned and said he will stand in the by-election. Let the voters of Clacton decide, he says. The other parties won’t stand because Farage is obviously trying to avoid the scrutiny of the Standards Committee.

It’s a circus. At the centre is a dodgy wealthy politician trying to avoid consequences and deceive voters.
