York residents have described how their street turned into a “river” after a water main burst flooding a home and a former pub.

Valerie Turbot said she was watching television in home in Salisbury Terrace on Sunday evening (July 19) when her neighbour asked if she had “seen all the water outside”.

“I walked through to the front door and I was paddling in water,” she said, adding: “It was just like a river. It was like the floods in '78 when I got flooded.”


Ms Turbot said firefighters and police were quickly on the scene outside the former Jubilee pub, which had its basement flooded after the pipe burst.

A large hole has been left in the road on Monday with Yorkshire Water on the scene working to fix the issue. Salisbury Terrace remains closed due to the work.

Yorkshire Water said the leak was “stopped promptly” and its team is working to fix the “burst pipe and repair significant damage to the road surface”.

The company said it has rerouted water around the network so all its customers have their water supply restored while the work is carried out.

“We're working to get everything back to normal as soon as possible,” a Yorkshire Water spokesperson said.

For Ms Turbot, the water from the burst pipe entered her home and left her wooden floor badly damaged – meaning it must be replaced.

But she praised the “brilliant” response from the emergency services.

“I had firemen in my house – it was lovely!” she said. “[The firemen] were helping to stop the water from coming in and everything.”

Ms Turbot said she can still live in her home, “but whether I can still live in it when I have to get the floor [replaced], I don’t know”.

The former school crossing lady, who has lived in Salisbury Terrace for 60 years, added that the road closure means the area is “quiet now”.

Meanwhile, Salisbury Terrace resident Marta Berezvai said she and her 10-year-old son Connor saw the water “bursting” out of the ground at around 6pm on Sunday.

“One of the pipes underground was bursting up and it lifted all the road up and it ended up bursting out water,” Ms Berezvai said. “[The water] was pretty high – it almost came into the doors of the house.”

The water did not enter Ms Berezvai house, but she said it left the playground behind her home underwater.

“In the little park it was like a swimming pool,” she said. “It was looking quite scary.”

## 'It was a scary situation'

Members of the community came together as the emergency services brought the burst main under control, Ms Berezvai said. “Lots of people were asking if we were OK and offered to help, but we didn’t need any help because we were alright.

“It was a scary situation but I don’t think anyone was panicking. Everyone was keeping calm and making sure everyone was OK … Neighbours were calling each other making sure everyone was OK.”

Ms Berezvai said some of her neighbours had lost water but “our water was coming” after the pipe burst. “But I will still buy some water for a few more days,” she said, adding: “I’ve lived here for 14 years and nothing has happened like this [before].”

Residents said the crews have worked through the night but there is yet to be an indication of when the work will be complete.

“They’ve been very professional and we’re very grateful,” Ms Berezvai said.

The Yorkshire Water spokesperson said: "Our teams responded very quickly to the significant burst on a 21-inch water main in York yesterday.

"Due to the size of the main there has been some water ingress at three properties, including the former pub and some residential properties, and damage to some vehicles in the area.

"Our customers teams were on site last night speaking directly with customers affected and they have returned today to continue speaking with anyone affected and will work with customers to understand any damage caused and how we can assist them in the short and longer-term.

“A case manager has been assigned to this as a single point of contact for affected customers to assist them with any claims or ongoing issues related to the burst.

“Repairs to the main are underway and to allow this to be done safely, a road closure and signed diversion is in place.

"Unfortunately, the burst caused significant damage to the road surface, which will take longer than usual to repair and reinstate.

"Our teams will be working as quickly as possible to carry out the reinstatement and will open the road as soon as it is safe to do so.

"We apologise for any inconvenience and thank customers for their patience while we complete the necessary repairs.”
