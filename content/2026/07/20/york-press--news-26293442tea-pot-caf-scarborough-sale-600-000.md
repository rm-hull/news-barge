A long-established family-owned café in Scarborough is now on the market for £600,000.

The sea front business, known as The Tea Pot, is located on the East Pier next to the Luna Park Fun fair and at the start of Marine Drive.

The business, which has operated for over 50 years, is currently run by Julie Smith, who took it over from her parents 20 years ago.

Sales details from Scarborough-based Colin Ellis Property Services describes the sale as a “unique opportunity.”

The particulars continued: “The business comprises of 5 brick built kiosks and is prominently referred to as the Teapot.

  “The Tea Pot and East Pier has operated as a popular cafe / food / gift stall for a number of years and has been within the same family hands for over 50 years. It benefits from all year round trade.


  “It is only coming onto the market due to retirement and is a rare opportunity for a keen operator."


The agents say the business can be scaled up or down, depending on the season. It serves a range of hot and cold food, teas and coffees to sweets and ice cream.

  One of the kiosks is set out to sells gifts and novelty goods but could be used for a variety of businesses. The business also has an alcohol license.

  

  There is seating for approximately 80 covers with an extra seating area above the pier that can accommodate a further 40 covers.

As the Press recently reported, The Tea Pot Café has also submitted a planning application to North Yorkshire Council to build a first-floor bar serving alcoholic drinks, teas, coffees, and cold platters at its East Pier harbourside location.

A terrace with a seating area would also be created, allowing customers to enjoy Sandside’s vibrant atmosphere. This would provide 75 extra covers.

If the scheme goes ahead, the business would increase the number of staff from 15 to 23, according to applicants Marvin and Julie Smith.

  Colin Ellis Property Services added offers are invited based a guide price of £600,000 for the business goodwill and Fixtures and Fittings. Stock will be at valuation.


  The tenure is leasehold with a current 15-year rolling lease due for renewal in June 2027 with the potential for a new 25 year lease from North Yorkshire Council, with terms to be agreed.

  

  The current passing rent is £9672 per annum per unit and paid quarterly / monthly in advance.
