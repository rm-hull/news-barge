**A NEW York city centre bar hopes to create a sense of community for country music lovers in the city.**

Ruby Rae’s – a country and western bar in Newcastle – has revealed that it will be opening its doors on a second venue in what was Jalou nightclub in Bridge Street, York.

This move brings the var into an area of the city with two other country venues – Silver Spur in Bridge Street and FortyFive Vinyl Café in Micklegate.

In a post, founder Dean Saunders said: “There were a few perfect places we could have opened, the right city, the right vibe, the right venue, the right time but all the stars have to align and somehow we’ve ended up with our own little Ryman in York!

“Follow along as we give it a sprinkle of Nashville and make a few changes to turn it into a live music focused country lovers venue with community in mind while keeping the unique & historic parts of the church alive.

“Our second city is perfect for it but we wouldn’t move without the right sort of building to make sure we don’t lose the Ruby Rae’s magic.


“We want that Nashville inspiration to come through, we want live music to be the focus, we still want it to be intimate and local and central so it becomes a go-to part of any live music or country music lover’s night out.

“We hope this will become a second home to any York country music fans and also remain accessible and fun for anyone who loves live music in general. We’re live music venue & country community space first, bar second “

He added that they were keen to attract the local country music community and support local performers.
