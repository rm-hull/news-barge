A new aparthotel has opened in York marking the end of almost five years of work to create a modern medieval-inspired building in the city.

Supercity Aparthotels, which has venues across the UK, has opened its ninth premises in Micklegate, in what was formerly Minster Van hire and the Jinnah restaurant.

Work has seen the new wood-fronted building, designed by Blossom Street-based architects brown + company, built to house the 46-suite hotel.

Roger Walters, chairman of Supercity Aparthotels, said it has “long admired” York’s “unique blend of history, culture and vibrancy”.

“The Micklegate is our way of bringing the Supercity experience to this remarkable city – offering travellers a stylish, flexible place to stay right in the heart of it all,” he said.

*(Image: Supercity Aparthotels)*

Mr Walters previously told The Press the aparthotel’s self-contained apartment units are aimed at people having longer stays in York, such as those visiting the city for work, rather than short breaks.

He said SuperCity had been attracted to York due to the city’s high visitor footfall and felt there was a gap in the market for people staying in the city while working.

*(Image: Supercity Aparthotels)*

Work on the Micklegate aparthotel first started in late 2021 and involved demolishing the former Jinnah restaurant and Minster Van hire buildings.

  It has seen a **new building created** with its front modelled on the timber-framed medieval buildings further down Micklegate.

The project faced delays including a year-long excavation at the site after Roman artefacts were unearthed beneath what is now the aparthotel

Part of York’s original city walls were found at the site as well as around 36,000 artefacts, some of which dated back to the Roman period, Mr Walters said.

  Lead architect Neil Brown, from brown + company, in front of the new medieval-style SuperCity Aparthotel in Micklegate, York 

*(Image: Dylan Connell)*

Neil Brown, director of brown + company, said the aparthotel’s historic surroundings were a key factor for its medieval-inspired design.

“The idea was always that it was a 21st century contemporary building that fits within its overall context – it doesn’t harm the conservation area and the adjacent listed buildings,” Mr Brown said.

Mr Walters said the aparthotel is going to “make a massive difference to that part of Micklegate” and hopes it could “encourage” more businesses to open in the area.

He also said the aparthotel aimed to support the community in York.

The aparthotel’s reception area has been filled with art by five York artists – Donna Maria Taylor, Kareem Baqai, Rae George, Timothy Marvell and Ufy – who collaborated on the project with curators PickArt and designers of the space Kinetic AI.
