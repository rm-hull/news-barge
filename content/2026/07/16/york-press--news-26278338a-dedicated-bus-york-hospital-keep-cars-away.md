## 'A dedicated bus to York Hospital would ensure a large number of cars stay away'

Traffic queuing to get into York Hospital (Image: Newsquest)

## Dedicated hospital bus could ease congestion

Although I agree with recent correspondents that free parking at York Hospital is not the answer to the horrendous congestion around York Hospital, I take issue with L Fraser’s recent comment that he or she takes the bus, implying this as a solution for all.

A large percentage of hospital users and visitors are sick or elderly, some with mobility problems.

The current No 5 bus on Wigginton Road runs at irregular intervals and its bus stop is too far to walk for many hospital users.

  A dedicated bus to York Hospital would keep cars away, says our letter writer. 

*(Image: Newsquest)*

I applaud Luke Charters’ suggestion of a dedicated bus to the hospital. This should be every 10 minutes, 8am to 6pm, from York station with a stop in Exhibition Square. This would therefore link in to almost all the park and ride and other bus routes in the city.

It should have plenty of space for wheelchairs and mobility aids and stop outside the main entrance.

Crucially, unlike the previous bus from Rawcliffe, it should be heavily promoted, especially on hospital appointment letters and texts.

These measures would ensure that a large number of cars stay out of the area.

## We're creating more heat by trying to beat it

With increasingly hot summers, year on year, instead of trying to fight the near impossible heat with fans and air-conditioning, in itself burning more energy and thus creating more heat, it's like trying to put out a fire by throwing petrol on it.

I realise older members of society, young children and informed people need cooler conditions to survive and food shops need a cooler environment.

But do other shops need near Arctic conditions (and that with the doors wide open)?

On short journeys or in a parked vehicle, do you really have to have air-conditioning on, burning fuel and creating pollution?

In trying to beat hot conditions, we are actually creating more heat.

Top tips: keep the heat out by closing the curtains and blind, exercise dogs early and late in the day, avoid the midday sun (mad dogs and English men go out in the midday sun); wear light clothes and cover your head (a hat or umbrella will do).

I personally slacken my tie and undue my top button on my shirt for comfort. And most important, drink plenty of water, pray for rain and just get used to the hot weather.
