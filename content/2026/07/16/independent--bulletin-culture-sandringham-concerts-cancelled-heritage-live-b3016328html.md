# Fans furious as Heritage Live concerts at Sandringham and other venues cancelled

- The Heritage Live festival series, featuring major artists such as Janet Jackson and Christina Aguilera, has been cancelled for the summer due to significantly lower than average ticket sales and the failure to secure a crucial investment package.
- The cancellation, announced on Monday, 13 July, left thousands of ticket holders, who had paid up to £200, in disarray as they attempted to secure refunds for the events planned across Englefield, Audley End, and Sandringham Estates.
- Initial confusion and frustration mounted among ticket holders as the festival organisers directed them to their ticket providers for refunds, while SeeTickets, a major provider, reportedly told customers to contact the festival directly, with many struggling to reach either party.
- Heritage Live, managed by GCE Live, expressed disappointment with SeeTickets' initial position, noting that other ticket agents had already processed refunds and advised SeeTickets customers to contact them directly with their order numbers.
- Despite maintaining that the event promoter was responsible for refunds, SeeTickets ultimately decided to fund customer refunds themselves to ensure ticket holders were not left out of pocket, promising to contact them shortly with instructions on how to claim their money back.
