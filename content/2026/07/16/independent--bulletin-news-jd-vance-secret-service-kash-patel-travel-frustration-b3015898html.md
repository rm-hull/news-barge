# Secret Service agents reportedly ‘fed up’ with JD Vance over last-minute travel demands

- Vice President JD Vance's frequent last-minute travel requests, including an attempt to use a military helicopter for his son's golf lesson, have reportedly caused frustration and low morale among his Secret Service detail.
- Sources indicate Secret Service agents are 'fed up' with the lack of notice for Vance's movements, though his spokesperson and an administration official defend the travel as necessary for security and family needs.
- Separately, FBI Director Kash Patel is under investigation by House and Senate Democrats for allegedly misusing taxpayer funds for personal travel perks, such as VIP snorkeling and helicopter use, which he denies.
- Patel reportedly canceled a trip to Chicago for his girlfriend's concert after White House officials, reportedly 'disturbed' by his actions amid the Iran war re-escalation, summoned him back to Washington.
- These incidents occur as the Secret Service faces significant challenges, including understaffing by over 20% in 2024, highlighted by an inspector general report following an attempt on Donald Trump's life during a campaign rally.
