# One Yorkshire police force slips in new performance ratings

## Performance levels for all forces across England and Wales will be decided by HMICFRS and published online.

West Yorkshire Police is the only one of Yorkshire’s four forces not to be given the standard rating in a new public performance level system, designed to increase public confidence and accountability.

Performance levels for all forces across England and Wales have been decided by His Majesty’s Inspectorate of Constabulary and Fire & Rescue Services (HMICFRS) and will be published online.

These range from level one – the default level – to level four (special measures) for the most significant performance issues.

North Yorkshire, South Yorkshire and Humberside were all granted level one, in the new rating system which has come in today.

However, West Yorkshire Police was one of a dozen forces across the country to be rated level two.

Lincolnshire Police was the only force to receive anything below this - and was handed level three.

HMICFRS said West Yorkshire Police required "continued support to address its performance issues".

The new ranking system has been brought in to provide support to struggling forces faster.

The policing inspectorate added: "In our 2025-27 PEEL inspection, we grade five areas as requiring improvement.

“These areas included leadership and force management, developing a diverse and inclusive workforce, investigating crime, safeguarding children and adults at risk of harm, and managing fraud.

“While we are pleased at the force's quite response to the feedback we have already given it still has much work to do".

Tracy Brabin, Mayor of West Yorkshire, said: “I welcome nationwide efforts to drive up the performance of our country’s police forces.

“While there are positives for West Yorkshire Police to take from the report, I’m disappointed the force has not achieved a higher rating.

“I continue to challenge the leadership to improve in the areas identified, ensuring better outcomes for communities.

“A high-performing police force is essential to ensuring a safer, fairer West Yorkshire.”

Speaking of the wider reforms, His Majesty’s Chief Inspector of Constabulary Michelle Skeer said: “This strengthened system-wide approach will support all police forces in England and Wales.

“By using a range of data, evidence and insight to identify concerns earlier, the Policing Performance System will provide the correct level of support at the right time.

“This will assist forces in addressing any performance issues before they get worse and help make our communities safer.”

Crime and Policing Minister Sarah Jones added: "For too long, failings in policing have been identified too late, allowing poor performance to go unchecked.

“Our new system will catch problems earlier, ensuring forces are challenged, supported and held accountable for improving.

"We are strengthening our powers to ensure we drive up standards, to catch more criminals and cut crime.”

West Yorkshire Police has been contacted for comment.
