A historic beauty spot is opening to the public, offering visitors a new way to explore North Yorkshire’s heritage.

English Heritage has announced that Rievaulx Terrace, an 18th-century landscaped promenade overlooking Rievaulx Abbey, will now be accessible through a joint ticket with the nearby abbey.

  English Heritage Curator of Collections Susan Harrison adjusts a table setting in the temple 

*(Image: Dominic Lipinski/English Heritage)*

The Grade I listed parkland, completed in 1757, features two neoclassical temples and was designed to frame dramatic views of the abbey ruins below.

Tuukka Laakso, Rievaulx Abbey cluster manager at English Heritage, said: "We are delighted to now offer visitors access to both the Abbey and the Terraces with a single ticket, providing excellent value and the opportunity to enjoy a memorable day out in one of North Yorkshire’s most beautiful locations."

  Aerial view of the Temple at Rievaulx Terrace 

*(Image: Dominic Lipinski/English Heritage)*

Rievaulx Terrace was commissioned by Thomas Duncombe II of Duncombe Park and is considered a prime example of the ‘Picturesque’ landscape style popularised in the 18th century.

The terrace and its temples, inspired by Duncombe’s Grand Tour travels, were designed for both aesthetic enjoyment and entertaining guests.

  English Heritage Curator of Collections Susan Harrison uncovers a sofa in the temple 

*(Image: Dominic Lipinski/English Heritage)*

The larger temple includes original furnishings and a painted ceiling influenced by Renaissance Rome.

A joint ticket costs from £33.46 for a family of two adults and up to three children.

Members of English Heritage can visit for free.
