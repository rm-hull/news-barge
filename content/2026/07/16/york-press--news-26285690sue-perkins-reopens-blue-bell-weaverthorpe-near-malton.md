## Much loved North Yorkshire village pub re-opens today after major upgrade

Mark Langton, Sue Perkins and Lauren Tranmer, who will be working together at The Blue Bell Inn *(Image: Pic supplied)*

A much-loved village pub in North Yorkshire reopened today (Thursday) after several months closure.

The Blue Bell Inn at Weaverthorpe, near Malton, opened its doors at noon following a major refurbishment.

Sue Perkins, who owns pubs in Hull and Beverley, took over the pub after seeing it marketed for auction last November by Allsop with a guide price of £280,000+, but it was taken off the market before the hammer fell.

Sue, who has a lifetime in hospitality, recently told The Press: “I’m really looking forward to bringing this lovely pub back to life and becoming part of the village community.”

Sue added later: “What started as a few upgrades and improvements has turned into a full blown renovation project, breathing life back into a tired country pub.”

The pub has been updating details of its renovations on social media, along with giving sneak previews of the food it plans to offer.

  The exterior of the refurbished Blue Bell Inn at Weaverthorpe, near Malton, which re-opened earlier today. 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

As part of the move to offer top class pub food, Sue has ‘teamed up’ with couple Lauren Tranmer and Mark Langton, who previously ran the Mill and Vine in Driffield until it closed in May after ten months.

The pair confirmed: “We couldn't be more excited about this new chapter!

“We’re looking forward to bringing our passion for great food, excellent service, and warm hospitality to this fantastic local pub.”

  The interior of The Blue Bell 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

Earlier today (THUR) Sue’s daughter Lydia Mainprize told The Press that she and others were busy doing the finishing touches to get everything ready for the re-opening at noon.

Lydia said: “The beers are in, the pub is nice and clean. Everybody is working hard getting it ready. We are good to go.”
