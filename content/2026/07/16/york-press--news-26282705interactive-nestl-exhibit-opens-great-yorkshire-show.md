## York-based food manufacturer unveils interactive exhibit at Great Yorkshire Show

Nestlé has unveiled plans for an interactive public experience, Common Ground, at the Great Yorkshire Show *(Image: Supplied)*

A popular York-based food manufacturer has unveiled plans for an interactive public experience at the Great Yorkshire Show.

Nestlé will launch the project, titled Common Ground, at the 167th Great Yorkshire Show in Harrogate from July 14 to 17, from 8am to 6pm.

Located in the Picnic Area next to the Foodhall, the exhibit, aimed at educating visitors about food, farming, and sustainability, will showcase Nestlé’s commitment to regenerative agriculture.

  Nestlé’s interactive space highlighting the role of healthy soil, British farming and regenerative practices in food production 

*(Image: PA)*

Visitors will be invited to explore the connection between soil health, British farming, and the food on their plates.

Dr Emma Keller, head of sustainability at Nestlé, said: "As a food manufacturer with a heritage spanning more than 160 years, working with local farmers through regenerative agriculture isn't just the right thing to do, it's how we futureproof our supply chains.

  Show visitors choose what matters to them when considering the food on their plate at Nestlé's Common Ground activation 

*(Image: Supplied)*

  The exhibit showcases Nestlé’s commitment to regenerative agriculture 

*(Image: Supplied)*

"By supporting regenerative farming methods that enhance soil health, improve biodiversity on the farms and landscapes we depend on, it helps to ensure we can keep making the products people love for generations to come.

"We’re excited to bring regeneration to life at the Great Yorkshire Show."

  Emma Keller, Nestlé Head of Sustainability, and Mike Warmington, Nestlé Regeneration Lead at Nestlé's Common Ground activation 

*(Image: PA)*

Nestlé employs 2,000 people at its York campus and has produced KitKat there for more than 90 years, making up to four million bars each day using 100 per cent British milk.

The company has worked with First Milk for more than two decades to help farmers in Ayrshire and Cumbria adopt regenerative practices.

  Show visitors Jayne Barnard, her daughter, and Sue Meikle testing themselves at a memory game at Nestlé's Common Ground activation 

*(Image: Supplied)*

It has also recently partnered with Wildfarmed, which will see 51 per cent regenerative wheat used in the 1.5 billion KitKat bars made at its York factory each year.

The Common Ground activation will include interactive games, a large-scale KitKat model, and a seating area overlooking the showground.

Visitors will be able to engage with the latest farming practices and explore how changes on the farm can influence the foods they enjoy every day.
