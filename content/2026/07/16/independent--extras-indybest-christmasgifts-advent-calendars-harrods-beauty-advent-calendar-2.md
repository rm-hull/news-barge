The Harrods beauty advent calendar for 2026 has been unveiled, and we are in for a treat this December. Comprising 34 products (two more than last year) and 21 full-size items (a whopping seven more than 2025), the department store is delivering something considerably bigger and better than we’ve seen previously.

This year’s advent haul is a true representation of the retailer’s expansion into beauty (it launched H Beauty – its dedicated beauty locations outside of London – in 2020) and its luxury heritage. You can expect to unbox formulas from the likes of La Prairie, Augustinus Bader and Sisley.

Whether you’re swatting up on all of 2026’s calendars so far in a bid to find the very best, or you’re a true Harrods devotee, here’s everything you need to know before the retailer’s Christmas countdown launches next month. Plus, browse details on the separate hair and body, and fragrance advent calendars, which, in 2025, sold out in record time.

True to form, the Harrods beauty advent calendar for 2026 is a luxury offering.

While the calendar still leans heavily into the high-end skincare synonymous with the department store's beauty halls, newer additions from Skin Cycles, U Beauty and Sync Beauty sit comfortably alongside heavyweights such as Augustinus Bader, Sisley and Shiseido.

For anyone who buys a beauty advent calendar to sample products they'd never usually justify splurging on, Harrods remains one of the strongest contenders. There's a 15ml pot of Augustinus Bader's the rich eye cream, Shiseido's future solution LX cream and a Montale fragrance – all of which, if bought outright in the featured sizes, would cost more (£273) than the entire advent calendar.

The category split also feels expertly curated. Make-up products from the likes of Charlotte Tilbury, Gucci Beauty and Nars feature alongside haircare from Philip Kingsley and Moncho Moreno.

The numbers are more impressive this year, too. The price has increased by £20 to £270, but Harrods has added two extra products overall and boosted the full-size count from 14 to 21. As always, I'd treat that figure with a little caution until every product size is confirmed, as luxury retailers have a habit of stretching the definition of “full-size”. Even so, on paper, this is comfortably Harrods' most generous beauty advent calendar to date. Watch this space for my full tried and tested review.

Harrods’s 12 days of fragrance advent calendar sold out at lightning speed last year, so I’m pleased to see it’s back for 2026. The line-up is set to leave olfactory obsessives in awe. The edit brings together some of the biggest names in niche fragrance, including Maison Francis Kurkdjian, Initio and Parfums de Marly, with seven full-size bottles and four scents exclusive to Harrods.

Compared with last year’s offering, which featured the likes of Creed and Roja, the 2026 calendar continues the retailer’s focus on hard-to-find luxury fragrances – except a Jo Malone English pear and freesia cologne because, well, everyone loves a spritz of JM.

Spanning everything from rich ouds and gourmands to more floral, wearable compositions, your fragrance library will be set for 2027 with this selection in your arsenal.

For 2026, Harrods’ 12 days of hair and body advent calendar has held firm at the same £99 price point, bringing together a mix of haircare, body care, fragrance and a few token beauty treats (for instance, spot patches). The edit includes nine full-size products, with this year’s roster featuring sought-after names including Sol de Janeiro, Kérastase and Color Wow.

Last year’s calendar combined cult favourites such as Ouai, Gisou and The Ordinary with newer discoveries, so the 2026 edition looks set to continue Harrods’ balance of recognisable beauty names and new ones – in this case, Starpowa – worth trying.
