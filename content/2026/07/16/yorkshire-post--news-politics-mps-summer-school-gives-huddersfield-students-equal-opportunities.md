# MP's summer school gives Huddersfield students ‘equal opportunities’ - including meeting the kings weavers

## Huddersfield youth won praise from their MP after a politics crash-course – including a trip to the King’s weavers.

Last week (July 6), Huddersfield MP Harpreet Uppal welcomed a cohort of young people aged 18-24 from across the local area to her second annual ‘Political Summer School’, with the aim of immersing them in local and national politics.

The goal of the summer school was to give local students the “same opportunities” as “anyone else” in the country, according to Ms Uppal.

Ms Uppal said: “As someone born and raised in Huddersfield, I know first-hand how vital it is that young people in our local area are given the same opportunities to learn, grow and experience new things…

![Harpreet Uppal's summer school](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOjJiNGM0OTc0LWJiMGItNDUwNi1iMjhmLWEyMmJiZDQzMzVhODo5MDU1YTcxOS0wZDU2LTRhMmEtYjM3OS05OWE4MGNiZGU1ZWE=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



“That is why it is so important to me to run this Summer School every year, to reach as many young people as possible, and allow them to have experiences they may not have otherwise had – learning about politics, public service, business, and community engagement – and giving them the skills they need as they move into further education or the world of work…”

The week had a busy itinerary, with a trip to Parliament, visits to businesses across the town, a surprise visit from Andy Burnham and an afternoon at BBC Yorkshire’s studios.

One trip also involved a group visit to C&J Antich and Sons; a world-famous textiles company whose fabrics have been worn by celebrities, Olympians and even King Charles.

Managing Director of the Textile Centre of Excellence, Shahban Aziz, explained that the centre had trained over 6,000 apprentices in fashion and textiles.

Mr Aziz said: “We inspire people to work in both traditional textiles, so in suiting fabric for the king, for example, all the way through to automotive fabrics, carbon fibre for aerospace, technical textiles, medical textiles – you can’t do that without inspiring the next generation in skills.”

When asked about the success of the summer school, Ms Uppal said: “I am so proud of this group. They have been so engaged throughout the programme, and brought ideas, curiosity, and thoughtfulness to every activity they have taken part in.”
