A COUNCIL flat has been issued with a closure order following reports of serious criminal activity, drug use and anti-social behaviour.

East Riding of Yorkshire Council secured the closure order on a flat in Morley Street, Goole, at Beverley Magistrates' Court on July 15.

It came after numerous complaints were made to Humberside Police by nearby residents, including reports of disorder, criminal activity, violence, verbal abuse and drug use, with problems reportedly beginning in March 2025.

  


Many individuals believed to be under the influence of drugs or alcohol were seen to visit the flats daily and are thought to be involved in drug dealing, a council spokesperson said.

  They added: "In recent months, the seriousness of the incidents escalated, despite interventions by both the police and council.

  

  "Nuisance caused to people living nearby was described as very serious, with residents fearing for the safety of themselves and their homes.

  

  "Accordingly, the council served the closure order to protect nearby residents and enable their quality of life to return to a good standard."

The order forbids entry to anyone except the tenant, officers from the council, police, other emergency services or utility providers for three months, and remains in place until Thursday (October 15).

Cllr Lyn Healing, cabinet member for communities and public protection at East Riding of Yorkshire Council, said: "We’re dedicated to taking firm action against anti-social behaviour wherever it occurs.

"This closure order will play a key role in supporting the local community and ensuring a good quality of life for local residents."

Anti-social behaviour can be reported to East Riding of Yorkshire Council by calling 01482 396380 or visiting here.
