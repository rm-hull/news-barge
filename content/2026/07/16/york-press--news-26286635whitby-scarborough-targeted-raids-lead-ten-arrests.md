## Nine arrests made in targeted police operation in North Yorkshire

North Yorkshire Police made ten arrests made in Whitby and Scarborough as part of Operation Tornado *(Image: North Yorkshire Police)*

NINE arrests were made as part of a police operation targeting drugs, anti-social behaviour, and retail crime took place across North Yorkshire’s coastal region on July 15 as part of a wider crackdown.

The county's police force launched its latest Operation Tornado "intensification day" in Whitby, Scarborough, and surrounding communities.

The force said the day focused on boosting neighbourhood policing and tackling priority issues using extra resources and specialist support.

  


In Whitby, a drugs raid was carried out in St Mary’s Crescent.

A 48-year-old man and a 48-year-old woman were arrested for allegedly possessing cocaine and cannabis with intent to supply, as well as alleged possession of criminal property, and a large amount of cash was seized by police.

Officers remained at the property throughout the day.

The two were later released under investigation.

A second drugs bust was carried out on Longwestgate in Scarborough during the evening and into the early hours of this morning.

Five people were arrested for allegedly possessing cannabis with intent to supply, including a 34-year-old woman, a 16-year-old boy, and three 15-year-old boys.

The woman was also arrested for allegedly possessing a Class A drug with intent to supply and remains in police custody.

The teenage boys have been released under investigation, and police said appropriate safeguarding measures are in place.

Operation Tornado also targeted retail crime in both town centres, combining high-visibility patrols with plain-clothes operations supported by North Yorkshire Council’s CCTV teams.

In Scarborough town centre, this led to the arrest of one man who it is alleged was involved in conspiracy to supply drugs, and another who was wanted on a court warrant.

Neighbourhood policing teams also conducted engagement events in rural areas around Whitby.

Officers shared crime prevention advice with farmers and gathered intelligence on suspicious activity.

  Chief Constable Tim Forber of North Yorkshire Police 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Police)*

Chief Constable Tim Forber said: "It’s always a pleasure to bring Operation Tornado back to Whitby and Scarborough and show that North Yorkshire Police is at the very heart of the community.

"Our officers and staff do an incredible job day in, day out, in keeping people safe and secure.

"We are relentless in our efforts to deter, disrupt, and detect criminality and anti-social behaviour."

Elsewhere, high-visibility patrols continued throughout the day and night in areas with known anti-social behaviour problems.

This included gathering information about nuisance off-road bikers and e-bike riders.

The patrols continued into the night as football fans gathered to watch the World Cup semi-final between England and Argentina.

Roads Policing Units also launched a drink and drug driving operation and patrolled areas close to the Cleveland Police border north of Whitby.

Chief Constable Forber warned criminals that North Yorkshire Police will not tolerate illegal activity.

He said: "My stark warning to the offenders is this: if you come to North Yorkshire, you will be arrested, and we will do everything in our power to bring you to justice."
