# Clean Power Hydrogen: Yorkshire firm raises £7.3m to aid turnaround

## Yorkshire firm Clean Power Hydrogen has raised £7.3m as the company looks to turn around its fortunes following a major incident at its Doncaster site earlier this year.

![Clean Power Hydrogen announced to the London Stock Exchange that it had now raised £7.3m to aid its turnaround. (Photo by James Manning/PA Wire)](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOmI2OWM5YTRkLWUxZGMtNGUwMi1hM2Q4LTEwYTdmYmZmYzJjODo3MmU2MGU1NS0zODJmLTQzMWMtYTY1NS00NjMzYjMxNzc5M2M=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=70&enable=upscale)



Following an incident in May which led to equipment at its site suffering “significant damage”, Clean Power Hydrogen suspended its operations and announced that it was switching from manufacturing to focus on research and development and the licensing of intellectual property.

At the start of this month, the firm said it would attempt to raise £7.5m to support the change in operating model.

In an announcement to the London Stock Exchange yesterday, Clean Power Hydrogen confirmed that it had now raised the money through a multi-stage fundraise.

CEO Richard Scott said: “The confidence shown by our investors in a new capital light, global licensing model is humbling.

“We recognise that the innovation journey is always challenging, but our recent setbacks have visible solutions, and our new partners enable international reach. The new leadership team is focused more than ever on delivering for all our stakeholders".

Mr Scott recently took over the top role at the firm after previous boss Jon Duffy stood down following the incident at the firm’s site.

Clean Power Hydrogen also suspended its shares from trading on AIM following May’s incident, but commenced trading again earlier this month.

The incident occurred after the firm’s MFE220 electrolyser unit – a piece of technology used for the production of hydrogen – suffered what the firm described as a “loss of containment”. This came after a hydrogen-oxygen mixture "ignited" during an automated depressurisation of the system.

The incident took place as the unit was completing the final stages of its factory acceptance testing.

Clean Power Hydrogen said last month that its investigations had established that key parts of its technology – its proprietary membrane-free stack and associated separators – were not the cause of the issue.

News of the latest fundraise comes after last month, Clean Power Hydrogen announced that it had entered into an agreement which could see the firm sell its manufacturing rights to Irish renewable energy company, Hidrigin.

The agreement could see Hidrigin, through its engineering partner Jones Engineering, serve as the “exclusive manufacturing partner” for Clean Power Hydrogen in the United Kingdom, Ireland, the United States, Canada and Mexico.

According to last month’s announcement, Hidrigin was also set to offer a £750,000 loan to Clean Power Hydrogen, contingent on the Yorkshire firm being able to raise £3m in share value.

The firm’s latest fundraise was completed through a firm placing, which raised approximately £2.54m, a conditional placing, which raised around £0.61m, a conditional subscription, which raised around ££.65m, a directors subscription, which raised around £10,000, and a retail offer, which raised approximately £0.5m
