## York MP says 'new era' under Burnham could bring significant change to city

York MP Rachael Maskell says Andy Burnham brings new era *(Image: Submitted)i>*

A York MP has said a "new era" in British politics under Andy Burnham could bring significant change to York.

The former mayor of Greater Manchester returned to Westminster just three weeks ago following his by-election win in Makerfield, and is scheduled to become Prime Minister on Monday (July 20, 2026) after securing the Labour leadership with the backing of 349 MPs.

  York MP Rachael Maskell says Andy Burnham brings new era. Andy Burnham, pictured, is expected to become Prime Minister on Monday, July 20, 2026. 

*(Image: Peter Byrne)*

York Central MP Rachael Maskell has described the moment as "not just a fresh start for Labour, it’s a completely new approach for everyone", with a greater emphasis on listening to residents and responding to local priorities.

Writing in her latest column, printed in today's Press (Thursday), Ms Maskell said she believed the change would shift power away from Westminster and towards local communities.

Ms Maskell, who has previously lost the Labour whip for rebelling on party policy, said she has consistently put York residents first - citing her opposition to Winter Fuel Payment cuts, action on child poverty, and resistance to proposed changes to Personal Independence Payments.

She claimed those interventions helped force government reversals.

She contrasted what she called the "privileged politics of the elite" with a more community-led model, arguing that MPs should follow constituents rather than party lines.

Looking ahead, Ms Maskell said Andy Burnham’s leadership would "turn the tables" on Westminster politics by empowering regions, towns, and cities.

She suggested this approach could give York residents a stronger voice in shaping policy and tackling local challenges.

Ms Maskell said the shift could help address both "the little things that trip us up" and larger issues such as climate change, public services, and economic growth.

She highlighted the need for stronger accountability across health, education, and local government, alongside support for businesses and high streets.

She added that she would continue engaging with residents over the summer to shape policy, urging people in York to play an active role.

Ms Maskell said: "Here in York, we have the opportunity for everyone to have a real say.

"It is now our chance to get things working and get things right."

Sir Keir Starmer faced his last Cabinet and Prime Minister’s Questions on Wednesday, while Andy Burnham neared the Labour leadership finish line as nominations closed.
