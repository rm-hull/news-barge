- **Dragon's Dogma 2**-** producer Naoto Oyama says Performance Mode will reach 60FPS on console ahead of the**-** Dark Arisen**-** release**
- **Oyama says the Switch 2 version will hit 30FPS and "even higher"**
- **He adds that Capcom's RE Engine has been "a really great fit" for Switch 2**

*Dragon's Dogma 2* is coming to Nintendo Switch 2 later this year alongside the launch of its*Dark Arisen* expansion, and Capcom has been making some major improvements to the base game in preparation.

Speaking in an interview with Eurogamer about the process of porting the fantasy role-playing game (RPG) to Switch 2, producer Naoto Oyama said that Capcom's RE Engine "has actually been a really great fit for Nintendo Switch 2."

He explained that the team has been able to use knowledge of previous Switch 2 ports, like *Pragmata* and*Resident Evil Requiem*, and the improvements made to the RE engine to create a solid port for* Dragon's Dogma*.

"Because this is an internal [Capcom-owned] engine, we've been able to accumulate knowledge and know-how from other teams," Oyama said. "And that means that the improvements that are made to the engine with each title, and the different features that are added with new games, get to stay with us - and we get to benefit from them on future titles. It's a really positive cycle for us."

The producer confirmed that the team has also been working to improve the game's overall performance, not just on Switch 2, but also on the launch PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X and Series S platforms.

"In particular, we've been trying to find as many small tweaks as we can make so we can build up and have a big performance improvement on consoles when it comes to Performance Mode itself," he said, adding that this will be a feature of the August title update, allowing the game to reach 60FPS in Performance Mode on console.

Oyama continued, confirming that the game can reach 30FPS on Switch 2 and "even higher" during many scenes.

"That means that, commensurately, all that effort we've put into the PS5 and Xbox Series X/S versions of the game also pays off in the Switch 2 version," he said, "and we're currently seeing a minimum 30 frames per second rate performance on that hardware (and, in many scenes, it's even higher!).

"So it's a little while to go until the game releases, but it's exceeding our expectations on Switch 2, and I hope the players agree that we've done a great job again with bringing this game to that platform."

*Dragon's Dogma 2: Dark Arisen*, which introduces a new region, Norgan, new armor, weapons, dungeons, and more, launches on October 9 for all platforms.
