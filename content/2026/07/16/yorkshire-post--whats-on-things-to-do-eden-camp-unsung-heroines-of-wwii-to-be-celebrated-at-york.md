# Eden Camp: Unsung heroines of WWII to be celebrated at Yorkshire museum event ‘Women at War’

## Eden Camp will celebrate the unsung heroines of the Second World War with the Women at War programme during the school summer holidays.

This summer, families are invited to uncover the extraordinary stories of the women who helped Britain during the Second World War with Eden Camp’s Women at War programme which will take place from July 17 to August 31, 2026.

The immersive programme brings to life the courage, determination, and resilience of the women who stepped into important roles across Britain during wartime.

From the fields and factories to hospitals and military bases, visitors will discover how women helped keep the nation running through interactive activities, workshops, and live experiences.

![Women at War event. (Pic credit: Eden Camp)](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOjVlY2ZjZjVhLTI3NTgtNGI2Ny05MWVjLTFhMzAyZGExOTgwMjplMDVhNGM5OS1iMTU1LTRhZTItOWVhZC0wZmYwYzY1NTgxNzg=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



Alongside the host of new workshops, events and activities, Eden Camp is also taking part in the Great British Summer Savings VAT reduction scheme, implementing the reduction to help make family trips more affordable this summer.

Families can purchase advance tickets from £14.75 for adults and concessions, £13 for children, with family tickets available from £26.25.

History enthusiasts and curious minds can take part in a range of themed activities designed to educate, entertain and inspire.

**Women at War: Poo Clues Trail**

![Dig for Victory Garden. (Pic credit: Eden Camp)](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOjY3OTBlYWYxLTYyYTktNDgwMS1hMjFhLTZjNmNkNjJmYzY0YjozNmM5OWJjZi05NDY3LTRmZGItYTBkOS1iZTBkYjQ2ZTQ0Y2U=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



When? Every day

Families can follow an educational and interactive trail inspired by the work of the Women’s Land Army and identify animal droppings hidden around the museum, collect letters, and uncover a secret wartime word before claiming a sweet treat from the shop.

**Little Evacuees Workshop**

When? Monday to Friday from 12pm to 1pm and 2pm to 3pm

This free bookable workshop offers children a chance to experience life on the Home Front with hands-on activities including, creating a cardboard gas mask, learning about evacuation and rationing, and discovering the stories of wartime women.

**Cow milking experience**

When? On selected dates from 11am

Families can meet Connie from the Women’s Land Army and learn about the role women played in wartime farming, and try their hand at milking a model cow.

**1940s Summer Street Games**

When? Every day

Traditional wartime games will be set up for families to challenge each other at sack races, three-legged races, hopscotch, skipping, marbles, and other activities that entertained children during the 1940s.

**Living History Weekend**

When? From August 29 to 30

The popular Living History Weekend returns in August, welcoming reenactors as they bring wartime Britain to life across the museum. History fans can meet medics, explore a Sherman Tank, sample authentic wartime ration recipes, and enjoy live music. Visitors are encouraged to dress up in 1940s attire to become part of the immersive experience which is included in the Summer Saver Scheme.

Museum manager at Eden Camp, Nick Hill, said: “We’re so pleased to be implementing the Government’s VAT reduction scheme in full and passing those savings directly on to visitors, helping more families enjoy a day out.

“Our Women at War programme is full of hands-on and interactive activities that bring history to life for all ages and we can’t wait to welcome families and history enthusiasts alike to experience it.”

After exploring the immersive Second World War exhibits and taking part in the summer events, children can enjoy a Children’s Meal for £6 during the summer holidays.
