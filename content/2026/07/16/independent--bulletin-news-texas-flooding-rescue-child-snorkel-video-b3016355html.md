Child wearing snorkel is rescued from catastrophic flooding close to Camp Mystic

A child wearing a snorkel was rescued from catastrophic flooding in the Texas Hill Country, near Camp Mystic.

Heavy downpours have transformed rivers into dangerous surges, resulting in over 100 roads being washed out.

Emergency services are urging residents to move to higher ground as Texas Game Wardens continue rescue operations.

More than 40 people have been saved by state police officers from homes and vehicles submerged or swept away by floodwaters.

No immediate reports of deaths or injuries have emerged from the current flooding, though the area is close to where 28 people died in floods in July 2025.
