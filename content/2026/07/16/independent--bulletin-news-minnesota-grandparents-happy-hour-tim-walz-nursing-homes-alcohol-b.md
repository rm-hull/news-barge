# Your grandma’s nursing home could soon be serving wine at happy hour

![](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/06/18/15/32/The-Whiskey-Exchange.png)

- Minnesota has enacted a new law, dubbed 'Grandparents' Happy Hour', permitting nursing homes and assisted living facilities to serve alcohol to residents during social events.
- Governor Tim Walz signed the measure into law on Tuesday, with its provisions scheduled to take effect on 1 August.
- The legislation allows facilities to offer alcohol without requiring a full liquor license, provided they notify the state and adhere to safety standards.
- The law explicitly states that alcohol can be served but not sold, ensuring that these senior care facilities do not operate as bars.
- Supporters argue the law promotes dignity, independence, and social interaction for seniors, allowing them to maintain traditions and enjoy community life.
