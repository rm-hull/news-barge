As someone who loves to travel, having the right equipment is important, so it’s essential to find the perfect weekend bag or suitcase. While I always rely on Nere and Away when it comes to suitcases, I’m yet to find the right overnight bag that’ll come with me on trips back home to see my family, spontaneous UK staycations and European city breaks, so I went on the search to find the ones worth taking. Turns out, there are so many incredible styles to choose from, each with different people and plans in mind. I’m here to show you the ones that I, a self-confessed globetrotter, found to be not only chic, but also practical.

If you’re extra organised and even like your toothbrush to have its own place in your bag, Antler’s overnight bag is one to trust with your belongings thanks to the multi-zips and endless compartments. If you travel a lot for work purposes and prefer to splurge on luxury luggage that’ll have promised longevity, Carl Friedrik’s or July’s weekender bags will be strong contenders. For a personalised touch, Katie Loxton’s initial-adorned style may be for you, and if you love a statement “look-at-me” bag then look no further than Damson Madder’s compact polka dot style or the Nobody’s Child gingham iteration you’ll end up taking for a mini break then also to work with your laptop in.

I put lots of weekend travel bags to the test by using them for different trips, and while all very different, I consider these 12 to be the best ones around for 2026, and beyond – because these are the keepers that’ll stick around for years to come.
