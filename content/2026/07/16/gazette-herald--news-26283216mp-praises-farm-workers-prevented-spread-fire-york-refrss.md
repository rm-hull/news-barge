A YORK MP has praised two farm workers who put their lives and machinery at risk to prevent the spread of a fire close to homes in York.

Luke Charters, MP for York Outer, has invited Jay Loft, 22, and Cameron Bartram, 19, down to Parliament after their quick thinking helped extinguish a blaze which had spread over 700 metres across wheat fields in Woodthorpe on Sunday (July 12).

As The Press reported, the pair were five minutes away from the fields at the time.

  


Without hesitation, they jumped in tractors and began discing the fields to create a firebreak before the arrival of North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service.

  The fire covered a vast area of field, owned by Russell Wagstaff, and threatened to spread to nearby homes 

*(Image: Rob Loft)*

The fire had begun to sweep dangerously close to nearby homes.

Speaking about this, the MP said: “People are often far too quick to write off the younger generation, but Cameron and Jay have shown the exact opposite.

“They’re two Yorkshire lads who didn’t stand around waiting for someone else to sort it.

“They rolled up their sleeves and stepped up when their community needed them the most.

“Their quick thinking helped stop the fire spreading towards people’s homes, and our city owes them a huge thank you.”

  Luke Charters has praised Jay and Cameron for their quick thinking, saving multiple homes in Woodthorpe 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Mr Charters posted a letter of thanks to Jay and Cameron, along with “a couple of beers”, as a “small token of thanks”.

He said: “I’ve also invited them both down to parliament because I think their bravery deserves to be recognised and their story deserves to be heard.”

Landowner Russell Wagstaff has also thanked the pair, along with local farmers Jonathan Sykes and Steven Beckett, who brought a water bowser to the fields – helping extinguishing the blaze.

As The Press reported, damage to Mr Wagstaff’s fields is estimated to cost around £30,000.

  The pair were hailed as heroes after their efforts on Sunday (July 12) 

*(Image: Jay Loft)*

Speaking about Jay and Cameron’s efforts, Mr Charters added: “They’re a real credit to themselves and their families.

“Their parents should be incredibly proud, and I hope other young people across York see Cameron and Jay for what they are: proper role models.”

The duo have since appeared on Good Morning Britain, ITV Calendar, BBC News and Look North after a number of videos of them racing to disc the fields received hundreds of thousands of views, comments and likes.
