A chef who turned tragedy into a culinary movement will headline this year’s Scarborough Seafest.

Ryan Riley BEM, an award-winning food writer and founder of Life Kitchen, will deliver chef demonstrations at the popular annual festival, taking place at Scarborough Spa from July 17 to 19.

Mr Riley launched Life Kitchen, which offers free cookery classes for people experiencing taste loss due to cancer treatment after losing his mother Krista to lung cancer when he was just 20 years old.

Chemotherapy had dulled her sense of taste and made eating unenjoyable, prompting Mr Riley to work with sensory experts and develop umami-rich recipes to help others in similar situations reclaim the joy of food.

His work quickly gained national attention after culinary icon Nigella Lawson championed his cause on social media.

Ms Lawson, who lost her first husband to cancer, became an early and vocal supporter and helped raise public awareness of his mission.

Other celebrities soon followed. Hugh Fearnely-Whittingstall hosted Mr Riley’s first class at River Cottage; Sue Perkins co-hosted sessions and became a patron and trustee; and Jamie Oliver opened his own cookery school for Life Kitchen events.

Mr Riley’s expertise and passion led him to become Britain’s leading taste and flavour expert.

During the Covid-19 pandemic, when millions reported losing their sense of taste, he released a free cookbook that gained global attention.

In the 2026 New Year Honours, he was awarded a British Empire Medal by the King.

"Ryan continues to prove that profound comfort and healing can be served on a plate and we’re delighted to welcome him to the Yorkshire Coast for Seafest at the Spa."

Mr Riley will appear at Scarborough Seafest on both Saturday and Sunday, July 18 and 19.

Other chefs will join him on stage throughout the weekend in the Promenade Lounge.

The full chef line-up for Saturday includes Scarborough chef and restaurateur Dan Hargreaves of Embers Restaurant at 10.30am, followed by Michael Burgoyne, executive chef at Wolds Restaurant, Orchard Lodge, at 11.30am.

Mr Riley will take the stage at 12.30pm.

He will be followed by Martyn Hyde, chef and owner of Eat Me Café at the Stephen Joseph Theatre, at 1.30pm, and Ryan Osborne, executive chef at The Hotel Victoria in Robin Hoods Bay, at 2.30pm.

Chris Hobson, sous chef at Wrea Head Hall Hotel in Scalby, will close the day’s demos at 3.30pm.

Sunday’s line-up features many returning chefs including Mr Hargreaves at 10.30am, Kath Breckon at 11.30am, Mr Riley again at 12.30pm, Mr Hyde at 1.30pm, Luke Daniels at 2.30pm, and Mr Hargreaves rounding off the programme at 3.30pm.

With free entry and a full schedule of events, organisers are encouraging both residents and visitors to enjoy one of the region’s most anticipated summer celebrations.

Scarborough Seafest will take place from Friday to Sunday, July 17 to 19, at Scarborough Spa in South Bay.
