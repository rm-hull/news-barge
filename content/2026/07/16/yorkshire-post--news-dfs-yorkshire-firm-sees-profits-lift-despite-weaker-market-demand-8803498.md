# DFS: Yorkshire firm sees profits lift despite 'weaker' market demand

## Yorkshire-based furniture retailer DFS has said it expects to post a lift in profits despite what it described as “weaker market demand”.

The firm said it expects profit before tax for the year ending June 28 to land around £45m, within its previously upgraded guidance of £43 to £50m.

Speaking on the results, Tim Stacey, group chief executive of DFS, said: "I would like to express my gratitude to all our talented and dedicated colleagues, whose continued commitment strengthens the DFS Group and ensures outstanding products and services for our customers."

"Through the year we have made important strategic progress across the business while also delivering a strong financial performance.

![DFS's Sheffield store. Photo: Google Street View](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOmEwYzEwNjhiLTBhNmItNDJjMC1hNGUyLTlhYTZmZWVmMmRmZTphZDM1YmZlOS0zM2EzLTQ3NTYtOTUwZC01Y2ZmODMwZTJiNTA=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



“We have navigated the complex and changing market environment focusing on our customer propositions combined with disciplined cost management ensuring that we delivered our upgraded profit expectations despite the market softening in the second half.”

The firm said elsewhere in the announcement that following some market recovery in the first half of the year, it had witnessed a “notable softening” in market demand in the second half.

DFS said this was driven by a decline in consumer confidence and housing transactions, in part related to the Iran War.

The comapny said the softening market was reflected in its order intake, with orders up 2.3 per cent year-on-year in the first half but down 4.4 per cent year-on-year in the second.

The firm added that its full-year order intake of one per cent drop was “broadly in line with the market”.

During the period, DFS also lowered its net bank debt to around £69m at June 28, down from from £107m in the 2025 financial year.

Mr Stacey added: "Importantly, a strong profit performance and capital rigour has enabled us to further reduce our net bank debt and improve our leverage position, providing the Group with a solid financial foundation to navigate any further market volatility.

"We remain firmly committed to our medium term ambitions of £1.4bn revenue and an eight per cent profit before tax margin, and I am confident that our strategy will drive strong shareholder returns as market conditions improve."

The Group is set to announce its full year results for the period ending June 28 on September 24.

The firm added in a statement: “Our position as the clear market leader, combined with the decisive cost actions over the past three years and the strengthening of our balance sheet has improved the business's resilience and we remain positive about the medium-term ambitions of the group.”
