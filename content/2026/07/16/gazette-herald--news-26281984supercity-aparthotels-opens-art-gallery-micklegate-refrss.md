A city centre lobby has been transformed into a gallery and opened to the public.

Supercity Aparthotels, located in Micklegate, officially opened its doors with a celebration of York’s creative community and a new collection of artwork on display in its lobby.

The launch on Friday, July 10, also marked the company's first property in York.

The event was a collaboration between PickArt, who curated the exhibition, and Kinetic AI, who designed the gallery-inspired space.

Five York-based artists — Donna Maria Taylor, Kareem Baqai, Rae George, Timothy Marvell, and Ufy — were present on the night to speak with guests and showcase their work.

Joe Allen, composer and launch event coordinator, said: "It’s been a pleasure to work with Tim and Olivia from PickArt, on behalf of York Creatives, to bring this launch event to life.

"Our aim was to create an evening that celebrated this great collaboration — one of companies from outside the city working alongside the companies, creatives, and communities from within.

"From locally sourced catering to a guest list that included everyone from neighbouring residents and the local church warden, to major York business leaders and Superсity guests, we achieved exactly that.

"It was an evening showcasing York at its finest: communities and businesses coming together and the arts playing a central role."

  The event was a collaboration between PickArt, who curated the exhibition, and Kinetic AI 

*(Image: PickArt)*

Guests ranged from City of York Council officials and hospitality professionals to members of the Micklegate business community and representatives from York’s arts sector.

Supercity also welcomed passers-by and hotel guests, keeping the event open to the public in line with PickArt’s philosophy of making art accessible to all, not just a select few.

Conversations throughout the evening focused on the positive outcome following a long and, for some nearby, frustrating construction period on Micklegate.

  Supercity also welcomed passers-by and hotel guests, keeping the event open to the public 

*(Image: PickArt)*

Many praised Supercity for inviting the community inside to enjoy the finished space free of charge and for dedicating it to the display of local art.

Guests were said to have "left the evening in good spirits", many remarking on how the collection will become one of the more interesting new spots in the city centre.

Although the event marked the official opening, the lobby and its art collection remain open daily.

Each piece is available for purchase through QR code labels displayed beside the artwork.

Further events are already in the works, including opportunities for members of the public to meet the artists in person.

PickArt and York Creatives have said further events bringing artists into the space are already being planned.
