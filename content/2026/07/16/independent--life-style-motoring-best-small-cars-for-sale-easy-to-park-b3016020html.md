For urban residents seeking a compact vehicle that simplifies parking, the market offers numerous compelling options.

Small cars are particularly well-suited for navigating built-up areas where space is often at a premium and parking can be a challenge.

These following models combine practicality with an easy driving experience, proving both easy to manoeuvre and often possessing a distinctive, fun character.

So, for anyone considering a new small city car, what are the top contenders available today?

We’ve compiled a list of the best small cars that are the easiest to park.

## 1. BYD Dolphin Surf

BYD has become one of the most recognisable car brands from China in just a few years, and the Dolphin Surf is a great option.

![BYD has become one of the most recognisable car brands from China](https://static.the-independent.com/2026/07/16/10/01KXMRE72CA5RK928G5R439SYD.jpg)

With its cheeky looks, smart cabin layout and compact dimensions, it’s an easy car to live with and makes parking in tight spots a doddle.

There’s a choice of two battery packs with the 30kWh model featuring a single electric motor that has a claimed range of up to 137 miles, while the larger 43.2kWh car can manage up to a claimed 200 miles between trips to the plug.

## 2. Dacia Spring

The Dacia Spring is now Britain’s cheapest new car with a starting price of just £11,990.

As it’s a small car, it feels most at home around the city and its short overhangs make it very easy to park.

It’s not the most dynamic car to drive in its class and a lot of materials in the cabin feel cheap, but its budget credentials make it represent excellent value for money.

The Spring comes as standard with an electric powertrain and is fitted with a 26.8kWh battery that can do up to a claimed 140 miles on a single charge.

## 3. Honda Super-N

The Honda Super-N is the city car of the moment thanks to its retro styling and impressive interior space.

In terms of the design, the Super-N takes a lot of inspiration from the Honda City Turbo II from the 1980s with its flared wheel arches and circular headlights.

![The Honda Super-N is the city car of the moment thanks to its retro styling and impressive interior space](https://static.the-independent.com/2026/07/16/10/01KXMRE546AHSSKPTZYSN1SJBH.jpg)

Its narrow body and tiny dimensions make it one of the easiest cars to park and its ultra-tight, 10-metre turning circle makes it perfect for nipping about town.

All Super-Ns come fitted with a 29.6kWh battery pack that can take the car a claimed 128 miles.

Inside, the cabin boasts bucket seats and Honda’s ‘Magic Seats’ where the rear-seat bases fold up to allow taller items to be carried in the back.

## 4. Hyundai Inster

The Hyundai Inster is a tough act to follow if you’re looking for a funky, fresh and versatile electric city car.

Just like the Honda Super-N, the Inster has very small exterior dimensions which makes it effortless to park, while the Tardis-like interior makes it feel like a car from two classes above.

If you go for a top-spec 02 model, it comes with sliding rear seats and fold-flat front seats to turn the Inster into a bed.

Underneath, the Inster is fitted with either a 42kWh battery or a larger 49kWh unit that can deliver claimed ranges of 203 miles and 229 miles, respectively.

## 5. Kia Picanto

The Kia Picanto is one of the last new combustion-powered city cars on the market today, but it’s still got plenty of things going for it.

![The Kia Picanto is one of the last new combustion-powered city cars on the market today, but it’s still got plenty of things going for it](https://static.the-independent.com/2026/07/16/10/01KXMRE3PS08XSGANQG9F0XVS4.jpg)

It’s great to drive and the 1.0-litre three-cylinder naturally-aspirated petrol engine can return up to a claimed 50mpg.

Its natural habitat is around town with its feather-like clutch, gear change and steering, while the large windows make it easy to see out of when parking in tight gaps.

## 6. Leapmotor T03

Leapmotor is a Chinese firm that has part ownership with the European giant, Stellantis.

The T03 is a rival to the Dacia Spring and is the brand’s smallest car. It comes as standard with an electric powertrain; using a 37.3kWh unit with an electric motor to deliver a claimed range of up to 165 miles.

Due to the car’s flat back end, that makes it very easy to park, while all cars come with lots of standard kit including a glass panoramic sunroof, adaptive cruise control and a 10.1-inch infotainment system.

## 7. Suzuki Swift

The Suzuki Swift has built up a reputation for being a fun and cheap-to-run small car.

![The Suzuki Swift has built up a reputation for being a fun and cheap-to-run small car](https://static.the-independent.com/2026/07/16/10/01KXMRE2V16ARFNYMTWXZYV7NJ.jpg)

Now in its sixth generation, this latest model is fitted with a 1.0-litre three-cylinder turbocharged petrol engine that boasts mild-hybrid technology.

It’s still a relatively small car which means parking in spaces is simple, while all models are engaging to drive and you can even get the car with Suzuki’s ‘Allgrip’ four-wheel drive system.
