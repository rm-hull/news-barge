## Helios and other events to shine at Brew York's East Yorkshire venue

Helios (pictured at Fountains Abbey) (c) National Trust Images, Anthony Chappell-Ross. *(Image: National Trust Images, Anthony Chappell-Ross.)*

A major series of innovative events has been announced to showcase the flexibility of a relaunched venue, which is run by the Brew York brewery.

The events take place at Goole Market Hall, which the York-based brewery runs, following its April re-opening after a £4million redevelopment as part of the Goole Town Deal regeneration programme.

The events aim to showcase the hall as a place for the local community and to make it a destination for visitors.

  As well as live World Cup football, a rotating number of local bands such as *The Numbers*, *Soul Revolver* and *Northern Velvet*, Brew+ has also booked leading tribute acts paying homage to the Arctic Monkeys, Fleetwood Mac, The Killers and Oasis.

Additionally, the venue, which operates under Brew York’s Brew+ brand, has also made plans for a regular quiz night, “painting in the pub”, business networking and the return of the very popular Northern Soul events.

Sean Hill, General Manager at Brew+ Goole Market Hall, said: “The people of Goole have really welcomed Brew+ and have given plenty of suggestions and requests for what we could do – and we’ve listened. Our events calendar is really shaping up and we’re so pleased that so many organisations want to use this fantastic space. This is just an indication of things to come.”

  A notable inclusion in the confirmed events is *Helios*, a touring art installation by renowned artist Luke Jerram, which will be at Goole Market Hall from September 25 to October 5.

  East Riding of Yorkshire Council is bringing *Helios* to Goole as part of the busy, year-long programme of events and celebrations taking place to mark the town’s 200th birthday. Measuring six metres in diameter, the artwork features detailed imagery of the sun’s surface. At an approximate scale of 1:230 million, each centimetre of the internally-lit spherical sculpture represents 2300 kilometres of the sun’s surface.

Ryan Madin, Programme Coordinator for Goole 2026, said: “We’re delighted to be bringing Helios to Goole as part of the town’s bicentenary celebrations. Located in the heart of the town centre, Goole Market Hall is the ideal venue for this installation, which has drawn huge crowds as it has toured the UK.

“Working in partnership with Brew York, we’re planning a series of fun community events at Goole Market Hall during Helios’ visit to the town to encourage as many local people as possible to get up close to this highly acclaimed piece of art. We’ll share more details about these events in due course.”

The planned events include;

• July 26th & August 12th - Boomchikkaboom (family-friendly rave)

• 30th July - Pub Painting (art classes)

• 2nd August - The Bat Cave (Comic Con)

• August 7th - Antic Monkeys (Arctic Monkeys tribute)

• August 16th - Fakermaker (Oasis tribute)

• August 18th - North Ska

• August 22nd - Goole Pride (plans tbc)

• August 23rd - Northern Soul Ball

• August 28th - The Killers Experience (The Killers tribute)

• September 18th - Gypsy (Fleetwood Mac tribute)

• September 25th to October 5th - Helios touring art installation

• October (tbc) Oktoberfest Beer Festival
