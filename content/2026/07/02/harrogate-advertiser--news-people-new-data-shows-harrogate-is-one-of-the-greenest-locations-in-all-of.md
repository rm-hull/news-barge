# New data shows Harrogate is one of the 'greenest locations' in all of England

## ‘Green’ Harrogate stands among England's top 10 best towns and cities for access to outdoor spaces according to new data and it looks like it will get even better in future.

As summer heats up and Brits chase or, sometimes, avoid, the sun, new research reveals Harrogate is among the top 10 locations to live for easy access to outdoor spaces.

To identify England’s greenest areas, My Home Move Conveyancing analysed the country’s top 100 largest towns and cities, focusing on factors such as local park provision, number of residents within walking distance of green space, garden accessibility, and affordability for renters and homeowners.

Harrogate was ranked in 10th position, nationally, thanks to an impressive variety of green areas, home to 789.44 hectares of country parks.

The rankings also also projected that the Harrogate area was one of the best for projected changes to green spaces over the next 20 years, at 0.42%.

Commenting on the research, Alistair Singer - director of [https://myhomemoveconveyancing.co.uk/](https://myhomemoveconveyancing.co.uk/) – said: “Towns and cities like Harrogate rank highly because of their strong balance between access to green spaces and the projected changes over the next two decades.

"Most buyers still have quick and easy access to their local park, and others have access to a garden right on their doorstep without stretching their budget too far.

Overall, Milton Keynes offers the best access to green space in England, coming out tops with 1,536.71 hectares of parkland and 64.63 square meters of green space per person.

On the other hand, Solihull ranked least green, with only 44 gardens per 10,000 households of available for sale properties on Rightmove, and 38,054 residents living more than a 10-minute walk from green space.

**Methodology**

The population of cities across England, based on population figures in ONS statistics or World Population Review

Average housing prices to buy a property (ONS)

Garden accessibility on properties to buy for “this area only” and divided per 10k population (Rightmove)

Population not within a 10-minute walk of green space (Fields in Trust)

Green space provision per person (Fields in Trust)
