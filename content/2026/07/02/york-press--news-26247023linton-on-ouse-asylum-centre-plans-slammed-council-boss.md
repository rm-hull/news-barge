A former RAF base near York is a “completely inappropriate” place to house asylum seekers, the leader of North Yorkshire Council has said.

Cllr Carl Les joined the county’s Labour mayor in criticising Home Office plans to house asylum seekers at RAF Linton-on-Ouse.

The Conservative council leader said it is understood that 1,200 single adult males could be housed at the former base as part of a government effort to reduce the use of hotels as accommodation.

It comes after plans to house 1,500 asylum seekers at the base in Linton-on-Ouse were previously considered in 2022, but the controversial scheme was abandoned following local opposition and a legal challenge from the then Hambleton District Council.

Cllr Les stressed that North Yorkshire Council does “not object to the government’s desire to house asylum seekers with dignity in safe and secure accommodation”.

But he said Linton-on-Ouse is not an “appropriate location to house potentially 1,200 single adult males”.

It follows other politicians, including the area’s MP Sir Alec Shelbrooke, saying the village would be “overwhelmed” by the arrival of more than 1,000 new residents.

Cllr Les said the council shared the views of “many leading voices in our region that this plan by the Home Office is flawed, that the site is completely inappropriate and that it was found to be so following a thorough investigation only a few years ago”.

He said Linton on Ouse is a “small, rural village with extremely limited infrastructure and services”, adding: “There are no appropriate facilities in the immediate vicinity to support a cohort of this size in terms of healthcare, transport connectivity, community integration opportunities or access to essential day-to-day services.”

Cllr Les said the plans will “create extra financial burden, which is currently unfunded, on the police force and wider agencies providing services”.

“The proposal would place significant and unsustainable pressure on local provision, while also risking the wellbeing of those accommodated at the site, who would be placed in an isolated setting lacking the support networks they require,” the council leader said.

## Council wasn't told about plans before they were released to press, leader says

He also said the council was “seriously disappointed that a media announcement was made by the Home Office before we were notified”.

“This information was released to press the evening before we were informed under an embargo which did not prevent details being published. This was very unhelpful, is not good practice and did not support a coordinated understanding or response to very sensitive information. We have been clear on our position on this with the department.”

Cllr Les said the council is having meetings with Home Office officials who have “confirmed that while government is ‘minded to’ progress this plan, no decision has yet been made”.

He said the Home Office will now “begin a period of ‘due diligence’ on their plan, and we will seek to influence that consideration”.

Any decision will be made by the end of this year, Cllr Les said.

He noted that the government’s plans will need planning permission to proceed but said it is “unlikely this will be for North Yorkshire Council to determine”.

Cllr Les said he has written to the county’s mayor and deputy mayor and the leader of City of York Council to ask for an urgent meeting about the proposals.

He also said North Yorkshire Council has “retained legal counsel to advise on options as we progress”.

He stressed that the council will “press government to reconsider this decision, engage meaningfully with us, with the aim that the Home Office come up with a more suitable solution”.

Meanwhile, the county’s mayor, David Skaith, said RAF Linton-on-Ouse is “simply the wrong place” to house asylum seekers, saying the site should instead be used for permanent housing.

Border security and asylum minister Alex Norris said Labour is “moving asylum seekers into ex-military sites that are a far cry from the hotels the last government left us with”.

"This is a system being brought back under control – and we will not stop until the job is done."
