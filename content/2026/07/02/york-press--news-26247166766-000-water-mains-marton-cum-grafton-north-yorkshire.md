WORK has begun on a major project to upgrade the water mains in a North Yorkshire village.

Yorkshire Water has launched a £766,000 scheme to replace two kilometres of water mains in the village of Marton cum Grafton.

The project is part of a £8.3 billion infrastructure investment plan which aims to improve the reliability of the drinking water supply, reduce leaks, and prevent bursts by upgrading ageing pipes.

Victoria Corbett, project manager at Yorkshire Water, said: "We’re investing £406m in upgrading our clean water mains network by 2030, in which we’ll be replacing 1,000km of pipework to enable us to deliver quality and reliable service to more of our customers.

"We’re just over a year into the programme, and the project in Marton cum Grafton follows a number of other successful projects across North Yorkshire."

The water company is installing more durable plastic pipes to replace the older network and is being carried out by Yorkshire Water’s contract partner, OCU Group.

They said that the work is expected to take around two months to complete.

Victoria added: "We’ll be working quickly and doing everything that we can to keep disruption at a minimum whilst we deliver this important work."
