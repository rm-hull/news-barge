## How a thriving city sports venue became York CAMRA's Club of the Year for 2026

The Club of the Year presentation at York Sports Club. *(Image: Pic supplied)</i>*

York Sports Club has scored a win as the 2026 Club of the Year, as chosen by beer lovers.

Members of York CAMRA presented a plaque to club manager Kevin Johnson in the club, at Clifton Park, Shipton Road, on a recent hot evening.

Branch chairman Chris Tregells said afterwards: “It's a very busy club with a lot going on and Kevin is always at the centre of activity. He runs a special club and one which is building a really good reputation but CAMRA is primarily here for the beer.

“Despite record temperatures, Kevin is still keeping his cask beer at optimum temperature and members were on hand to enjoy two Tim Taylors: the Landlord and Blonde.

“His beer range varies and he showcases local breweries' cask beer so the club is a venue for the casual drinker as much as the avid sport enthusiast. A worthy winner.”

Kevin says the presentation was well-supported and that evening he thanked CAMRA for the award “which is a real honour and recognition of all our hard work.”

He confirmed the award was for his beer, which “shows that we are one of the very best in pubs and clubs for real ale in the area.”

This is the second such award, for which he thanks the staff and “Steve from Clear Brew for keeping and serving the beer so well.”

The club is a Cask Marque member and often receives mystery shopper visits to ensure quality is maintained.

  The Club of the Year presentation at York Sports Club. 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

Kevin says the win raises the club’s profile, which leads to “more really happy drinkers which is great for trade.”

The Sports Club is enjoying a busy summer with teams from the Ladies T20 World Championships training there in the last few weeks, including the world No 1 team Australia, and the Yorkshire Ladies have also played there. On July 29 and 31, the Yorkshire Mens Cricket team will play 2 matches.

The club has five TVs in the bar and two large function rooms, to make the most of the World Cup. The Nations Cup in Rugby starts this Saturday and Wimbledon is now in full swing.

Kevin said: “All in all, it’s a really exciting time for sport here at the moment.”

He wanted to thanks CAMRA and his staff for their support, saying he is proud of his beer.

Kevin added: “The award is also a great reminder that the club is not just a sports venue and the home to York Cricket Club, York Rugby Union Club, York Squash Club and York Tennis Club, it is also a fantastic social destination with a great sports bar huge patio and fabulous event space.”
