## Sam Smith's pub in village near York to reopen this month

Paula Dawson outside the Tankard Inn, which is due to reopen later this month. <i>(Image: Darren Greenwood)

A village near York will this month see the reopening of its only pub.

Paula Dawson has taken on the Tankard Inn, a Sam Smith’s Brewery-owned pub at Rufforth.

The pub has been closed since September 2023.

Prior to that, after a long period of closure, The Tankard had been open for three months under the management of young couple Aaron Ridsdel and Ebony Wheatland, from York.

At the time, the couple, who were aged just 19 and 20, said they and the brewery agreed to the closure due to lack of sales.

  Paula Dawson outside the Tankard Inn, which is due to reopen later this month 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Now, Paula is planning on re-opening the Tankard in later this month. Her husband will continue at Paula’s Kitchen, keeping the popular venue open.

Electricians are on site to change the pub’s lighting and “fixing a few faults.”

Paula was born in Acomb and she spent her childhood living in pubs, which were run by her parents.

They included the Red Lion in Poppleton and the Yorkshire Hussars in North Street, which is now the Whippet Inn.

  Paula Dawson outside the Tankard Inn, which is due to reopen later this month 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Paula has previously worked in the care sector but during the pandemic, opened the Paula’s Kitchen café, which operates from the garden centre on Mill Lane and won York Press Best Sandwich Bar earlier this year.

She told the Press: “I always wanted to follow in mum and dad’s footsteps.

“I have always looked at country pubs as I love the atmosphere of locals meeting up and chatting about their lives.”

“I think the Tankard Inn is something special. I love the decoration and lay out. It’s a quirky pub. There is also plenty of shading in the large garden area.”

Paul says she will initially just offer drinks, but after a couple of weeks, she will begin offering food, starting with Sunday Lunch.

She said: “It’s had a new kitchen put in.”

  Paula Dawson outside the Tankard Inn, which is due to reopen later this month 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Paula expects the re-opening to be in mid-July, after she completes her training with the brewery.

Paula added: “I'll also like to get a darts team going. I would just love everyone to support their local to keep it open and be a big part of the community.”

The re-opening follows other recent and pending re-openings of pubs owned by Tadcaster-based Sam Smith’s Brewery.

  The Tankard Inn, Rufforth, in earlier times. 

*(Image: NQ)*

A management couple from Pontefract has reportedly been found for the Wellington Inn on Alma Terrace, Fishergate. It is said to be re-opening this month.

In May, Sally Watkins re-opened the Ebor Inn in Bishopthorpe, which for around 40 years had been run by her much-loved father Gordon, until his death of Covid during the pandemic in 2021.

The Blacksmith Arms at Skelton also re-opened in May after the brewery found management couple, John and Margaret’ to take on their reins following a month’s closure due to the previous management couple leaving.
