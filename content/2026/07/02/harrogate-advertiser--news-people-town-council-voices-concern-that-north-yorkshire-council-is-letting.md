# Town Council voices concern that ‘North Yorkshire Council is letting down people of Harrogate’

## For the second time in a month a damning vote of ‘No Confidence’ in North Yorkshire Council has been passed in the Harrogate district.

The latest surprise vote against the giant unitary authority which was launched in 2023 comes from Harrogate Town Council just two weeks after Knaresborough Town Council voted almost unanimously for a motion of ‘No Confidence’ in North Yorkshire Council.

Harrogate Town Council argues the town’s interests are being “let down” after recent changes in local government in the county which, it claims, puts its prize buildings at risk.

The Lib Dem run parish council passed a motion declaring it had no confidence in North Yorkshire Council's management of Harrogate's assets, including its portfolio of important arts venues.

Coun Paula Stott, chair of the Town Council’s Culture, Heritage, and Events Committee, and a board member and usher at Harrogate Theatre, spoke about her experience at the theatre, saying: “The fact that North Yorkshire Council has repeatedly failed to respond to the Theatre’s requests to renew their lease has made it much more difficult for them to apply for many types of funding.

"Organisations like Harrogate Theatre are being let down by North Yorkshire every day.”

The Vote of No Confidence cited what Harrogate Town Council argues is North Yorkshire Council’s “inability to effectively manage, maintain, or safeguard services and assets within the town of Harrogate”.

Councillors made it clear that, while frontline staff were working hard to do the best job possible, the direction, governance, and culture established by the Conservative leadership of the unitary authority at Northallerton “was not fit for purpose”.

Councillors at the meeting said they would not stand for “the neglect that North Yorkshire Council are showing the assets and services of the county”.

The motion included several examples of “North Yorkshire’s mismanagement”, including "failures in planning enforcement and coordination of roadworks”, and expressed dismay at the decline of public facilities such as benches and footpaths on the Stray.

The motion argued that the centralised management model operated from Northallerton was “fundamentally failing the people of Harrogate”.

Harrogate Town Council resolved not to sit idly by while “Harrogate’s basic facilities crumble due to bureaucratic apathy and a total lack of local accountability”.
