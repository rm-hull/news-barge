A primary school has received a donation of educational tools worth £2,000.

Twenty Five (York) Ltd, which owns a joinery workshop branded Shaw & Riley in Hessay, has given the resources to Rufforth Primary School, part of the Pathfinder Multi Academy Trust.

The tools are intended to promote design technology in the classroom.

The donation is in line with the company's effort to promote women in construction, highlighted by the presence of Isabelle and Daisy, two students from Wolgarston School in Staffordshire, currently on work experience at the company.

Design technology is viewed as essential for developing creativity, problem-solving skills, practical knowledge, and cross-curricular learning, preparing children for future education and careers.

The subject encourages students to think creatively and innovatively, allowing them to design and make products that solve real-world problems.

It also enhances problem-solving and critical thinking, with students learning to identify problems, brainstorm solutions, and evaluate outcomes.

Design technology also integrates knowledge from mathematics, science, computing, art, and literacy, reinforcing learning across the curriculum.

It provides opportunities for hands-on learning, allowing children to create tangible products they can see, touch, and use.

Working on design technology projects also involves teamwork and communication, helping children develop collaborative skills and learn to share ideas effectively.
