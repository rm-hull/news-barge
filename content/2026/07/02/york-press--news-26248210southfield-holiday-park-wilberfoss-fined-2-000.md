A YORKSHIRE holiday park has been fined more than £2,000 for putting up illegal roadside advertising.

Southfield Holiday Park Ltd was prosecuted for erecting two signs without permission along the A1079 at Wilberfoss, near Pocklington.

The company pleaded guilty through a solicitor at Beverley Magistrates' Court on Friday, June 24.

  


Carl Skelton, director of streetscene services at East Riding of Yorkshire Council, said: "Our officers will investigate and remove any signs put up illegally on the highway, and we will prosecute wherever we can.

"Signs like these could be a major hazard to motorists."

The park was fined £1,000 by the court and ordered to pay £817.46 in costs and a £400 victim surcharge, bringing the total to £2,217.46.

East Riding Council’s enforcement officers found the signs, which advertised Southfield Holiday Park, on public highway verges between August 1, 2025, and January 7, 2026.

The signs were placed on public land without the council’s permission.

The council reminded the public that placing signs on highways without approval is a criminal offence under the Highways Act 1980.

Such unauthorised signs can obstruct visibility, create a nuisance, or pose a danger to road users by causing distractions.

The council can issue a £500 fixed penalty notice per illegal sign.

If a case goes to court, the maximum fine rises to £2,500.

All illegal signs will be removed by the council, and the costs can be reclaimed from those responsible.

Advertising signs are allowed on private land with the landowner’s consent but may still require planning approval.

Further advice is available from the council’s planning department.
