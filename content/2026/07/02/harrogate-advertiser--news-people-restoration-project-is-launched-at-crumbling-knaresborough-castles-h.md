# Restoration project is launched at crumbling Knaresborough Castle's historic walls

## North Yorkshire Council has started to deliver on its promise to repair crumbling Knaresborough Castle after a public petition highlighted fears for the future of the ruined fortress.

A team of tree groomers and abseilers were spotted in the grounds of the historic 900-year-old castle this week, following advice from Historic England and local experts.

As well as removing and treating the growth of random vegetation and weeds on the lower castle walls, they have also been climbing the walls with ropes to tackle problems higher up the regal location.

The repair team is also expected to work on the Castle Tower.

Last month saw the launch of a petition calling for urgent action to protect and restore the castle which was presented to North Yorkshire Council’s Harrogate and Knaresborough area committee meeting.

The petition quickly garnered more than 4,500 people concerned about the condition of the venue.

Kathy Allday, chair of the Knaresborough Museum Association, who launched the petition with Liz Baxandal, told the meeting that, while the castle was in good condition in 2012 when the Tour de France visited the town, it had been neglected by both Harrogate Borough Council and North Yorkshire Council since.

In response, Paul Fieldhouse, Head of property maintenance, compliance and facilities management at North Yorkshire Council, said it had emerged following local government reorganisation in 2023 that maintenance of council-owned historic sites was “not up to standard” with concerns that contractors were not qualified to carry out the required work.

It was also revealed that county council staff were working with Historic England and the council-owned Align Property Partners to improve maintenance at these sites.

The earliest version of Knaresborough Castle was built by the Normans, though the first written evidence of a castle in Knaresborough in the records dates to the reign of Henry I, (1100AD to 1135).

King John (1199 to 1216) took possession of the Castle and the Forest of Knaresborough in 1204 , gaining a base for his favourite sport of hunting.

The monarch would go on to spend £1,290 to turn Knaresborough Castle into a military fortress.
