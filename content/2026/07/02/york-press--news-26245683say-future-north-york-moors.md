Search the Public Notice PortalA fresh vision has been launched for one of England’s most treasured landscapes.

The North York Moors National Park Authority has unveiled the first stage of preparing a new Local Plan, and is inviting residents, businesses, communities and landowners to help shape its future.

The Local Plan will guide how decisions are made across the National Park.

Tom Hind, chief executive officer of the North York Moors National Park Authority, said: "The decisions made through the Local Plan will help shape the future of the North York Moors for many years to come.

"That is why it is so important that people get involved right at the beginning of the process."

At this early “first steps” stage, the authority is seeking views on key challenges and opportunities for the area.

  A special focus will be on sites that could help address the shortage of housing for local people 

*(Image: NYMNPA)*

A supporting paper, Current Thinking, outlines 10 questions under four themes: Nature and climate change, Tourism and recreation, Where new development may happen, and Local housing sites and policies.

A special focus will be on sites that could help address the shortage of housing for local people and those working in the National Park who are struggling to find suitable accommodation.

The authority is running a “Call for Sites” to gather suggestions from landowners and communities for locations that may be suitable potential sites for future development.

The North York Moors is a vital landscape for wildlife and supports more than 11,000 jobs.

Feedback can be shared at northyorkmoors.org.uk/first-steps with the consultation open until August 31.
