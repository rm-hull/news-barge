## Major York beer festival with 90 ales aims to raise £25,000 tomorrow and Saturday

The beer festival is extremely popular and a vital charity fundraiser. *(Image: Pic supplied)*

The spectacular Merchant Adventurers Hall in York’s city centre will once again be hosting a major charity festival tomorrow and Saturday.

Held in the Medieval splendour of the Great Hall and the Undercroft, the annual event will feature 90 different beers from more than 30 breweries.

There will also street food, snacks, plus the YO1 Radio Stage featuring a line up of local artists providing the soundtrack.

The popular event, spread across four sessions, is already a sell-out on Friday evening and Saturday afternoon. Tickets, however, are still available for Friday afternoon, from 12-noon until 4.30pm and Saturday evening, from 5.30pm to 10pm.

It aims to raise £25,000 for the Merchant Adventurers’ Education Trust. Last year’s event raised £28,000.

Sarah Brown, Governor, Merchant Adventurers’ of the City of York, said: “Our fourth Beer Festival, is bigger and better than ever, offering an exciting range of beers for the discerning drinker, given extra flavour by the unique and ancient fabric of our medieval hall.

“This has been a place of feasting and festivity since the foundation of the Company in 1357, but it has also always been a hub for business, enterprise and charity.

  The festival is staged in the most magnificent surroundings. 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

“The Beer Festival enables us to throw open our doors to the city at large, and the money raised will be invested in our programme of enterprise education benefitting young people throughout the city.

Sarah added: “None of this could be achieved without the hard work of our organisers and volunteers and the generosity of our sponsors and breweries. On behalf of the Company of Merchant Adventurers of the City of York, I thank you all most warmly and wish everyone an enjoyable and memorable Festival!”
