Shed Seven have announced their seventh album – and their frontman has said it is the band’s “best yet”.

The York band will release their new album, called Shed Seven, on January 8, 2027.

It will be their first release since 2024’s A Matter of Time and Liquid Gold both charted at number one – meaning the band joined a unique group of artists that have topped the album charts twice within the same year.

Sheds’ frontman Rick Witter said the band was “excited” to announce their eponymously titled new album, to be released via Cooking Vinyl.

The band have also released the new single, Stand Together, which Rick described as a “call to arms and a rallying cry for some kind of unity in the world we live in today”.

“We have taken great care in making this album what we believe to be our best yet, and we hope these songs are taken to people’s hearts,” he said.

“The brand new comeback single ‘Stand Together’ shows a statement of intent.”

The announcement of the new album comes as the Sheds prepare to play a sold-out acoustic show from London’s iconic 100 club on Thursday night (July 2).

At the end of the year, the band will head out for their Shedcember UK tour which includes two dates at the O2 Academy in Leeds on December 12 and 13.
