A Yorkshire children's charity is marking 15 years of making a difference.

The Principle Trust Children’s Charity, launched in 2011 by Mike Davies MBE, has raised more than £2.2 million to fund free respite breaks for nearly 6,000 vulnerable children and families across Yorkshire.

The charity was inspired by Mr Davies' own childhood, in which holidays were out of reach due to financial hardship and family trauma.

Mr Davies said: "Today, more than 400,000 children in Yorkshire are living in poverty, around 31 per cent of all children in our region.

"For many, a respite break is not just a holiday; it’s a chance to reconnect, rebuild, and create precious memories that last a lifetime.

"In many cases, families tell us these breaks are life-changing.

"Having helped nearly 6,000 children and families, I am immensely proud of our entire team, who work tirelessly.

"I want to thank every supporter personally; every penny makes a difference."

The Principle Trust currently operates eight respite homes in Blackpool, the Lake District, and Filey, supporting more than 300 families each year.

To celebrate its milestone, the charity will hold a fundraising event, 'A Night at the Seaside,' at the Queens Hotel in Leeds on September 26.
