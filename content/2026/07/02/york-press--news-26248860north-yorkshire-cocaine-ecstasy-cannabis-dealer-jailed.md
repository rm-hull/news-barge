## 'You are spineless' - drug dealer may have used child to deliver to addicts

Joe Windle sold cocaine, Ecstasy and cannabis *(Image: North Yorkshire Police)*

A COCAINE, Ecstasy and cannabis dealer may have used a child to deliver his drugs to North Yorkshire addicts, York Crown Court heard.

Joe Windle, 27, alleged he had been forced into selling bulk texts advertising drugs for sale and peddling drugs by beatings and threats to him and his family from another drug dealer to whom he owed a debt.

Prosecution barrister Tom Jackson said Windle had got someone else to deliver drugs for him and “that person was a child”.

Referring to a text found by police on Windle’s phone telling a drug buyer to look for “some kid”, he asked: “You accept sending that message?”

“Yes,” said Windle, “But it would not have been a small kid.”

He was giving evidence at York Crown Court in a bid to persuade the Recorder of York, Judge Sean Morris, to give him a smaller sentence.

Mr Jackson said police arrested Windle three times between October 30, 2023, and February 23, 2024, for drug supply offences. In total they caught him with cocaine worth £1,180, 44 Ecstasy tablets worth £100 and ketamine and cannabis.

His actions had contributed to the courts backlog by causing other trials for offences such as child sexual abuse to be postponed to make way for his trial which was then adjourned three times.

“You should hang your head in shame,” he told Windle. “I think you are spineless.”

He jailed Windle, formerly of Ure Bank Terrace, Ripon, for five years and six months and put six penalty points on his driving licence.

Imran Khan, for Windle, said after the death of his father, to whom he had been close, Windle turned to drugs.

“That has been the greatest regret of his life,” said the barrister.

By the time of the drug dealing offences, Windle had been a heavy drug user.

Windle was now drug free. He had moved away from the area where he had been involved in the drug scene, got work on a farm and turned his life around. He had also got medical help.

He had married recently and had a stable lifestyle.
