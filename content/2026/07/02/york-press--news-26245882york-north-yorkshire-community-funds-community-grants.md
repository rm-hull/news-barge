A community fund is offering up to £30,000 for projects that improve safety, reduce crime and support early intervention.

The York and North Yorkshire Combined Authority’s Community Fund, which has already distributed more than £3 million to groups across the region, is now accepting new applications.

Jo Coles, deputy mayor of policing, fire and crime, said: "The Mayor David Skaith and I are incredibly proud of the impact the Community Fund has had on our communities over the past two years.

"We know that support for community projects, led by local people, prevents crime and supports victims to feel safer in the places where they live and work."

The fund supports a wide range of community-led schemes including those that reduce anti-social behaviour, support victims, and promote community cohesion.

Recent recipients include Inspire Youth Yorkshire, which received funding for its mobile youth clubs in Haxby and New Earswick.

Jess Ward from Inspire Youth Yorkshire said: "The Community Fund has allowed us to engage with more young people within York, providing them a safe space to receive support from trusted adults who are there to listen."

Other supported projects focus on physical activity for children, addiction recovery, and targeted youth interventions.
