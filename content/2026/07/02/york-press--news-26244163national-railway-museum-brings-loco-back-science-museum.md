## 'A significant milestone' - 18-tonne Victorian technology returns home to York

Matt Cobain, Museum and Heritage Projects Manager, alongside Columbine Loco *(Image: Trustees of the Science Museum Group)*

A HISTORIC steam locomotive has returned to York after more than two decades away.

Columbine, a key part of Britain’s railway heritage, is now back on display at the National Railway Museum, where it has been reunited with its tender for the first time since 2000.

The 18-tonne locomotive was previously on display at the Science Museum in London, while its tender had been housed at the Science and Innovation Park in Swindon.

  


  Columbine Loco and Tender being transported back to National Railway Museum, (NRM) York on the 18th of June 2026 

*(Image: Trustees of the Science Museum Group)*

Becky Peacock, conservation and collections care manager at the National Railway Museum, said: "The return of Columbine to York and its reunification with its tender is a significant milestone, both for the locomotive itself and for the national collection.

"This project has brought together specialist expertise from across the Science Museum Group, from conservation and collections care to transport logistics and installation.

"We're delighted that visitors can once again experience Columbine as a complete vehicle and appreciate its important place in Britain's railway story."

  Charlie Bird, Senior Workshop Technician, with Columbine Loco and Tender 

*(Image: Trustees of the Science Museum Group)*

Columbine was removed from the National Railway Museum in 2000 to be featured in the Science Museum’s 'Making the Modern World' gallery.

Space limitations meant only the locomotive could be displayed, leaving the tender in storage in Swindon.

Now, both parts have completed long journeys to York and are on display together in the museum’s Great Hall.

The move took place in the early hours of June 17 and involved a team of 26 specialists using an electric forklift and a large low-loader to transport the 4.5-metre-long, three-metre-high locomotive.

  Columbine Loco and Tender settled in National Railway Museum, (NRM) York 

*(Image: Trustees of the Science Museum Group)*

Columbine is described as one of the most important locomotives in the national collection.

It was the first example of the standard Crewe-type locomotive with six-foot driving wheels and played an important role in the evolution of British locomotive engineering.

Engineers W.B. Buddicom and Francis Trevithick designed Columbine with an innovative outside-cylinder configuration, addressing weaknesses in earlier designs that used inside cylinders and crank axles.

Their approach influenced British locomotive engineering for more than 40 years.

The locomotive is displayed without its cab, as it would have appeared in the early 1870s, allowing visitors to see the engineering features that made it a milestone in railway history.

Its return to York is expected to attract visitors of all ages interested in transport heritage and engineering.

The museum has encouraged anyone interested in seeing Columbine to plan a visit.

Visitors can book free tickets to the National Railway Museum through the museum’s website.

The return of Columbine forms part of the museum’s ongoing efforts to showcase the full breadth of Britain’s railway innovation and engineering history.
