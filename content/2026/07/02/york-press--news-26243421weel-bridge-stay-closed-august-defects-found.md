Search the Public Notice PortalThe reopening of a key road bridge has been pushed back by a month after hidden defects were discovered during improvement works.

Weel Bridge, a 70-year-old structure spanning the River Hull between Beverley and the village of Weel, was removed in April and taken to Sheffield for refurbishment as part of a £1 million upgrade.

While work was underway, engineers uncovered previously undetected aluminium corrosion, prompting additional repairs and safety checks.

Councillor Paul West, East Riding of Yorkshire Council’s cabinet member for environment and transport, said: "We are sorry not to be able to reopen the bridge as we planned in July, but there's no way we can avoid this.

"These extra defects found need to be repaired before we can return it to Beverley."

The new issues have delayed the project by around four weeks, with the bridge now expected to reopen in August.

The extended closure also affects the nearby Weel Household Waste Recycling site, which will remain closed until the bridge returns due to access problems.

Residents are advised to use alternative sites in Hornsea, Driffield, North Ferriby (Humberfield), or Market Weighton/Holme on Spalding Moor.

Temporary traffic diversions and closures will remain in place until Weel Bridge returns.
