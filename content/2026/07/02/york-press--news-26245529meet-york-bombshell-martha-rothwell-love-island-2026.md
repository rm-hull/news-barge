## Meet the York bombshell contestant turning heads on Love Island 2026

Love Island is now in its 13th series. *(Image: ITV)*

A personal shopper who hails from York is in the limelight as one of the contestants on ITV's Love Island.

Martha Rothwell is from York but now lives in London, and is in the latest series of the reality dating show featuring single contestants, or 'Islanders', who are looking for love.

They stay in a secluded, luxury villa with cameras watching their every move, and must consistently pair up to avoid being eliminated, with the public ultimately voting for their favourite couple to win a cash prize.

Martha arrived at Casa Amor as one of 12 new 'bombshells' whose arrival was designed to turn heads among the existing contestants.

In media interviews before the show, she shared a few snippets about herself including her love of football and that fact that she plays for a five-a-side team but admits she is 'not very good' at it.

Like all the contestants, she was on the show to find love, describing her type as 'tall, dark and handsome', though she says her ex-partners have looked different and thinks personality, and someone who makes her laugh, is key.

Earlier this week, she came out as bisexual on-screen, saying her most recent relationship was a nine-month relationship with a woman.

Martha is on Instagram and can be followed at @martharothwell. At the time of writing, her Instagram handle has 6,120 followers.

She is the latest person from York and North Yorkshire to make it onto the popular show. Javi Shephard, now in his mid-30s, was a surveyor from York when he appeared in series two as part of the initial line-up. He remained single and was evicted from the island when he didn't couple up with anyone.

Georgia Steel joined the villa for series four as a 20-year-old a performing arts student and barmaid from York who later returned for Love Island: All Stars.

Series eight welcomed Tasha Ghouri, from Thirsk, who was a model and the show’s first deaf contestant. She went on to be a runner-up on Strictly Come Dancing in 2024, and was part of the line-up on Celebrity SAS: Who Dares Wins last night.

In 2024 Tasha also launched her book Hits Different, which she has co-authored with Lizzie Huxley-Jones, and is described as a "sweet summer romance about learning to love yourself, the power of friendship, and the importance of representation."

Tasha now has 1.3million followers on Instagram.
