## 'People have wanted this for years' - York city centre to get new attraction in 2026

Artist's impression of Thor's on Ice coming to York Museum Gardens in November

YORK will get a new attraction this Christmas – an ice rink in the heart of the city and next to the historic bar walls, bringing ice skating back to the city centre for the first time in 16 years.

The people behind Thor's popular tipis will set up an ice rink in York Museum Gardens, where they also run a tented bar during the festive season.

Thor's pop-up ice rink will be built on a paved patio at the back of the York Art Gallery in the city's Museum Gardens.

Access will be through gates to the right of the art gallery, with Christmas trees and fairy lights guiding skaters to the rink as well as food and drink stalls on site.

  Artist's impression of Thor's on Ice coming to York Museum Gardens at Christmas


  Entrance to York's new Christmas attraction - Thor's on Ice, behind York Art Gallery in Museum Gardens. Photo: NQ


Revealing the plan exclusively to The Press, Amanda Monaghan, of Thor's, said: "I 100 per cent believe there is an appetite for this.

"For years, people have said they'd love to have an ice rink in the city centre.

"People love a Christmas experience. It gives visitors a reason to come to York and is also lovely for residents to be able to skate in the city surrounded by the York walls in the most idyllic setting.

"Just being on the ice makes you feel Christmassy."

She said people had missed skating in the city centre ever since the Ice Factor seasonal ice rink moved from the Eye of York in 2010 and re-established itself as Yorkshire's Winter Wonderland at York's out-of-town Designer Outlet at Fulford.

  Artist's impression of Thor's on Ice coming to York Museum Gardens for 2026


  Location of York's new Christmas attraction - Thor's on Ice, behind York Art Gallery in Museum Gardens. Photo: NQ


So when the chance arose to bring an ice rink back to the city centre, she said it was hard to resist.

"The York Museums Trust approached us and to see if it was something we would be interested to do as part of our Christmas offer," she told The Press.

She said the Trust and the City of York Council had been hugely supportive to the Thor's On Ice project.

"They have been hugely helpful; there's an appetite to bring this to the city."

A spokesperson from York Museums Trust said: “We are excited to confirm we will be hosting an ice rink in the York Museum Gardens this winter.

"This will bring a new experience to the heart of the city for both residents and tourists.

"We are thrilled to be working with local company Thor's on this project, creating a bespoke experience in a historic setting."

  Thor's festive tipi in Museum Gardens next to the Multangular Tower when it first opened in 2023


Thor's On Ice will run from November 13 to January 3. The ice rink will measure 19m by 22m and will be able to accommodate 150 skaters. "It will feel intimate, it will not be huge. I imagine it will be a great place to go on a date!" said Amanda.

The rink will have a clear cover, allowing skaters to take in the surroundings which will include a boundary with York's historic walls.

Skate hire will be available and tickets will be priced competitively with season tickets available for frequent skaters, said Amanda.

More information will be available at: www.thorsoniceyork.co.uk.

Seasonally-themed cabins will sell a range of food and drink, including hot Aperol, which Amanda believes will be a UK first. "We tried hot Aperol in Cologne and it was delicious; but we have developed our own recipe."

  Amanda Monaghan of Thor's at the location of York's new Christmas attraction - Thor's on Ice, behind York Art Gallery in Museum Gardens. Photo: NQ


It is the first time Thor's will have set up and run an ice rink. Amanda explained the elaborate process of building a temporary rink.

"You build it over freezer pads – literally freezers underneath the base. Then you have to hosepipe layers of water, literally layer after layer of water, to build up the ice. It is a nine-day process."

Thor's opened its first festive tipi in Museum Gardens in 2023. It already runs summer and winter tipis in Parliament Street and in the grounds of the Milner Hotel by York Railway Station as well as outside York at sites in Leeds, Lincoln and London.

Amanda said York's Museum Gardens offered a unique setting, not just for Thor's but other events too, such as music and comedy shows which have returned to the gardens in recent years.

"It has become a really wonderful space for events such as Ghosts in the Gardens – it is giving a real heart to the city.

"It is under utilised and can bring a real economic benefit to York."
