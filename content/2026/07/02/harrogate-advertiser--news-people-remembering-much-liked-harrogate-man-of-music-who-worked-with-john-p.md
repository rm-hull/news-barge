# Remembering much-liked Harrogate man of music who worked with John Peel and Annie Nightingale

## The team behind one of Harrogate's most popular regular music events are paying tribute to a late member of the team who worked with John Peel for a time in a memorable musical life.

The forthcoming Vinyl Sessions event for Harrogate Hospital and Community Charity at Starling Independent Bar Cafe Kitchen on Wednesday, July 8 promises a treasure trove of classic moments by Irish rock icons Thin Lizzy.

But volunteer organisers and audience regulars will also be remembering Jim Dobbs, who created the videos for the classic rock music albums night which was first launched in 2018.

The technologically versatile and gifted Jim was for many years a freelance sound engineer and DJ at North Yorkshire Sound Machine and, also a member of Re-Act Party Band

A special evening celebrating the life of Jim Dobbs in Harrogate was held in April this year with live music by well-known local acts including Les Lomax & The Re-Act Band, Robin Van Zelst, Lee Gains, Eddy and the band.

Now the team at Vinyl Sessions are also paying tribute to the late, great Jim.

The event’s founder and MC Colin Paine said: "It will soon be a year since the late Jim Dobbs, amazing team member, did his final popular slideshow at Vinyl Sessions.

"Sadly Jim passed away peacefully on February 13 this year.

![In younger days - A special evening of live bands celebrating the life of Harrogate sound engineer and musician Jim Dobbs was held in April this year. (Picture contributed)](https://www.harrogateadvertiser.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOmU2Yjc3ZjZkLTk1NmUtNDYzNS1hOGQ5LWM2M2JhOThhY2FiNDoxYTk1NjEyNS0zOGZjLTQ1MWEtOTE0Yy1lM2I3YzZkNTRhZjA=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



"Born in Wakefield in 1953, Jim moved around a fair bit due to his father’s job as a police officer.

"Eventually settling in Harrogate, upon leaving school he took an apprenticeship as an electrical engineer with the then West Yorkshire Road car Co.

"As an amateur musician Jim took an interest in PA amplification and loudspeakers - investing in a rig and a bit of home-made late 70s started a hire business as a sideline.

"This grew well with local bands plus Leeds University working with BBC radio legends John Peel, Annie Nightingale and, even, hit act Bob Carolgees & Spit the Dog, among many others

"Local bands Jim Dobbs worked with included Toybox, Red Lorry Yellow Lorry, Menphis Roadshow & Les Lomax along with North Yorkshire Mobile Discos (Steve Chatterton & Lee Gains) using Jim’s systems for large events.

"Jim made a career change in 1996, qualifying as a teacher.

"From our point of view, we remember his key contributions to the Vinyl Sessions events and his fantastic videos.

"In point of fact, Jim “accidently” joined Vinyl Sessions in 2018 after being inspired by Ken Scott’s video show at our David Bowie event.

"He offered to join the team, becoming part of the success in raising more than £25,000 for Harrogate Hospital Community Charity.

"The whole Vinyl Sessions team loved him to bits and miss him terribly.”
