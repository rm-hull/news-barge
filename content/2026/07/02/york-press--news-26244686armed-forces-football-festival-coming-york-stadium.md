An Armed Forces Football Festival will take place in York later this month.

The family-focused event will be held at the LNER Community Stadium on Sunday, July 12, as part of a wider programme of activities marking Armed Forces Day.

Organised by City of York Council and partners, the festival aims to honour and celebrate currently serving military personnel, veterans, cadets, and their families.

Councillor Claire Douglas, leader of City of York Council and Armed Forces Champion, said: "We join communities across the city in thanking all in the Armed Forces and recognise the sacrifice they and their families make.

"This celebration forms part of our commitment to them under the Armed Forces Covenant.

"In their honour, we will proudly fly the Armed Forces Day flag above the Mansion House as part of the celebrations here in York.

"The Armed Forces Day Football Festival is a fantastic way for everyone to get involved in celebrating sport and service.

"Don’t hesitate to book your free tickets and join in what promises to be a fun, action-packed community event."

Two football matches will headline the festival, with turnstiles opening from 10.30am.

The first match, a veterans’ and over-35s game, will run from 11.30am to 1.30pm, with the trophy sponsored by the Combined Authority.

Following a short break, the second game will see 2 Royal Signal Regiment take on 21 Multi-Medical Role Regiment from 2pm to 4.15pm, with the trophy sponsored by York College.

A range of entertainment is planned for the day, including a performance by a service children’s choir and the screening of a film between matches.

A dedicated family zone will offer free activities for all ages, such as circus skills, face painting, magic shows, badge making, and friendship bracelet crafting.

Tickets to the festival are free, though attendees can opt to pay a £1 booking fee.

There is also an option to donate to Combat Stress and York Against Cancer, and £5 all-day parking is available.

In addition to the festival, City of York Council continues to offer ongoing support for the Armed Forces community through a weekly drop-in centre at the Spurriergate Centre (YO1 9QR).

The centre provides social and welfare support to serving personnel, veterans, and their families. Details can be found on the Live Well York website.

The council also maintains a directory of support services for military families, available online or in print.

Copies will be distributed at the festival.

For more information and to book tickets, visit the City of York Council website and search for York Armed Forces Day.

More details about the Armed Forces Covenant and related support services are also available via: [https://www.york.gov.uk/ArmedForcesCovenant](https://www.york.gov.uk/ArmedForcesCovenant)
