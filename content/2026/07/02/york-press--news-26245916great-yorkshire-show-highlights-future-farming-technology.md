Farms will be powered by new smart solutions at this year’s Great Yorkshire Show.

The Agri-Innovation Village, running from Tuesday, July 14 to Friday, July 17, will showcase cutting-edge technology to help future-proof agriculture.

Among the exhibits are Archimedes spiral-style wind turbines, biomethane fuel for farm machinery, and a ‘smart’ weather forecasting app.

CatchmentAI's Probcast app will offer optimal weather windows for farming tasks.

Bennamann's modular bio-CNG refuelling station will also be on show, converting on-farm biogas into fuel for methane-powered tractors.

The village, powered by a hydrogen unit from TCP Group, is hosted by the Farmer Scientist Network at the Yorkshire Agricultural Society.

  The new Innovation Centre at The Great Yorkshire Show. 

*(Image: Yorkshire Agricultural Society)*

Dr Dave George, Chair of the Farmer Scientist Network, said: “It’s an exciting time for innovation in agriculture.

“Challenges like we have seen around fuel supplies really sharpen the focus on how the industry can navigate global events, and the Agri-Innovation Village shows just how far technology is marching on to make a difference at farm level.”

Other features include a British-built hydrogen fuel cell-powered Hilux prototype, and Black Soldier Fly breeds farmed as alternative protein sources.

Tickets are on sale now, with prices frozen at last year's rate, and can be purchased via: [https://greatyorkshireshow.co.uk/ticket-information/](https://greatyorkshireshow.co.uk/ticket-information/)
