More than one million tickets have been sold at a York-based stadium in a landmark moment for sport and entertainment.

The LNER Community Stadium, which opened its doors five years ago, has reached the milestone after hosting football, rugby, and high-profile events.

It has been the venue for international competitions and community activities, and serves as the home ground for York City FC and York RLFC.

Cllr Claire Douglas, leader of City of York Council, said: "Reaching one million ticket sales is a fantastic achievement and a testament to how important the LNER Community Stadium has become to our amazing city.

"Over the last five years, it has brought people together for unforgettable sporting moments, major events, and community activities.

"Crucially, it has delivered real benefits for our residents of all ages, local clubs, and businesses.

"We are incredibly proud of what has been achieved so far and look forward to even more memorable moments and building on this success in the years ahead."

The stadium has hosted a range of high-profile fixtures, including the 2021 Women’s Rugby League World Cup, the 2025 Women’s Rugby Union World Cup, Women’s Rugby Six Nations matches, and a Challenge Cup semi-final.

City of York Council recently extended its operational contract with GLL, the stadium’s operator, for a further five years until 2035.

Peter Bundey, chief executive of GLL, said: "As a cooperative social enterprise, sharing many common values with York City Council, we are delighted to have reached this important milestone together.

"The excellence of our offer is not only demonstrated by the numbers through our doors but also by being industry recognised - being awarded Regional Public Centre of the Year - at the most recent UKActive awards.

"Looking ahead, we will continue to support the successes of our two resident clubs: York City FC and York Knights RLFC, grow our popular GP referral exercise programme, Healthwise, and welcome new and existing customers to the Leisure Complex."

GLL has invested more than £1 million into the stadium, funding improvements such as new large screens, upgraded broadcasting infrastructure, an additional changing room, and a training room.

The not-for-profit operator has also invested £440,000 in gym upgrades and introduced a new soft play area at the Energise Leisure Centre in west York.

Further improvements are planned across the city’s leisure facilities.

Earlier this year, solar panels were installed at the stadium through a partnership between the council, North Yorkshire Combined Authority, and GLL.

The panels are already reducing energy bills and lowering the stadium’s carbon footprint.

City of York Council has welcomed the milestone as a sign of the stadium’s growing role as a destination for sport, entertainment, and community events.
