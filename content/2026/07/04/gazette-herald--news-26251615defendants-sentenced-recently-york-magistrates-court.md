A woman guilty of violence at York Railway Station, two thieves and a sex offender were among defendants sentenced recently at the city's magistrates court.

Luke Jackson, 31, of no fixed address, pleaded guilty to assaulting a man, theft of items worth £31.95, £31.97, £35 and £18.67 in separate raids on Heron Foods, items worth £32, £79.95 and £28.80 in separate raids on Sainsbury’s, items worth £22.20 from Tesco and obstructing police, all committed in York.

He was jailed for four weeks and ordered to pay £50 compensation to the man and £280.54 compensation in total to Heron Foods, Sainsbury’s and Tesco.

Chelsea Hall, 33, of Mindrum Terrace, Percy Mains, North Shields, pleaded guilty to causing actual bodily harm to a woman at York Railway Station.

She was given a 12-month community order with 60 hours’ unpaid work and ordered to pay £300 compensation, £85 prosecution costs and a £114 statutory surcharge.

Callum Docherty, 42, of Falsgrave Road, Scarborough, pleaded guilty to stealing clothing accessories worth £90 from JD Sports, clothes worth £150 from Trespass and alcohol worth £40 from Marks and Spencers, all in Scarborough.

He was jailed for six weeks and ordered to pay £100 compensation.

Matthew Walker, 36, of South Back Lane, Bridlington, pleaded guilty to two offences of failing to comply with the sex offenders’ register by not revealing his address.

He was given an eight-week prison sentence suspended for 12 months and ordered to pay £85 prosecution costs.
