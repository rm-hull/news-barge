  **'Why brewery boss Humphrey Smith was swimming against tide'**

I AM sorry to hear of the death of Humphrey Smith of the Sam Smith's Brewery, of Tadcaster.

A character no doubt but with an attitude to how a proper pub should be and how it should be run.

His rules regarding no music, TV or mobile phone on the premises smacks of a bygone era.

Harking back to the late 1960s early 70s, pubs and working men's clubs were filled with people socialising and communicating but, with the introduction of TVs, pub games, darts, dominoes, and cards, they went into a terminal decline.

Humphrey unfortunately tried in his own way to hold back time with his own principles on how a pub should be run but unfortunately he was swimming against the tide.

His passing is another nail in the coffin of how traditional drinking establishments should be run and now is disappearing into the mist of time

  *D M Deamer,*

  *Penleys Grove Street,*

  *Monkgate,*

  *York*

  **Questions need answering over flooding at key project**

FLOODING at the Station Gateway project cannot simply be dismissed as an outstanding issue on a large scale project.

Proper drainage is fundamental on a scheme like this and one would expect all the drains to be in place before road surfacing works took place – not afterwards.

So the head of development at York Council should explain exactly how and why “storm drains have not been connected to the wider drainage system” and who is responsible for this failure.

  *Matthew Laverack,*

  *Lord Mayors Walk,*

  *York*

  **Hope new PM gets defence spending correct**

AS we know, the world is an increasingly dangerous and volatile place.

We have Putin's War in Ukraine, Trump's War in Iran and China's dragon beginning to stir as a result of these events and, in the meantime, Keir Starmer is struggling to raise the odd £5 billion here for military drones because we can't afford war ships and the odd £28 billion there to pay for basic military defence - which is apparently the minimum amount suggested by the military advisers.

Amid all this, one wonders how budget for the defence of our small country seems to be worth less than the cost of HS2 - £102billion and rising - a white elephant of a rail link that still only goes from London to Birmingham and it's not not even high speed any more! Hopefully the next Prime Minister will pull their finger out and prioritise safety over shareholders and speed.

  **Nigel Cummings,**

  **Charlton Street,**

  **Case for sponge gardens in York**

ON the radio I heard about an initiative in Liverpool – sponge gardens.

Like York, Liverpool is concerned about unbearably hot temperatures and flooding.

Both can be somewhat reduced by replacing concrete surfaces with gardens - 'sponge gardens'. These are not only pleasant to look at, attract pollinators but also cool temperatures and soak up excess rainfall.

Unfortunately, I don't have a garden but would not pave it over if I did. In addition there are places next to roads where a garden could be created such as the space in front of the car park on Bishy Road.
