A SON ruined his 78-year-old father’s plans to leave mementos for his loved ones after his death by stealing his cherished coin collection, York Crown Court heard.

The father told police he was using his limited income during retirement to buy souvenir sovereigns which would be distributed to his family after his death, said the Recorder of York, Judge Sean Morris.

By stealing the collection, worth £6,145, Luke Elliott Clayton, 45, had not just stolen from his father but also from other members of his family and himself, he said.

“It is a nasty offence,” the judge told Clayton as he jailed him for nine months, to be served consecutively to a 38-month sentence for robbery.

Clayton, of no fixed address, pleaded guilty to theft.

Kelly Clarke, prosecuting, said Clayton went to his father’s home in June 2024. There, he went to his father’s bedroom to change into some clothes he had left at the address.

He was there for some minutes and the father heard him say “I’ve got it”.

After he left the house, the father felt he should check on his coin collection and found it had gone. It had included sovereigns minted to celebrate royal events such as Queen Victoria’s 1887 Jubilee and King Charles III’s coronation.

Rachel Webster, defending, said Clayton was working on tackling his drug habit.
