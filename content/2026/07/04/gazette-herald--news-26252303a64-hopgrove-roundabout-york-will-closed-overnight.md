A MAJOR York road is set to be closed overnight for over a month starting next week.

The A64 is set to undergo resurfacing work between Hopgrove Roundabout and Grimston Bar from 8pm to 6am on a number of days between Monday (July 6) to August 7.

On July 6, the inner ring road at Hopgrove Roundabout will be closed overnight.

Eastbound traffic will be diverted via the A1237 to Little Hopgrove roundabout and back, while westbound traffic will be redirected at Grimston Bar.

From July 7 to 10 and July 13 to 15, the Hopgrove Roundabout will be partially closed, with the westbound lane shut.

Eastbound traffic will continue to be diverted via the A1237 to Little Hopgrove roundabout and back and westbound through-traffic will be rerouted at Malton, using the A169 and A170 to Thirsk, then along the A19 south to the A1237 to rejoin the A64 at Askham Bryan.

Local westbound traffic will be diverted from Little Hopgrove through the city centre to rejoin at Grimston Bar.

From July 20 to 24, the A64 westbound at Hopgrove roundabout will be closed, with traffic redirected from the A64 to Little Hopgrove, through the city centre to rejoin at Grimston Bar, or by the A1237.

On July 27 and 28, the link between Hopgrove Roundabout and Little Hopgrove Roundabout will be closed; traffic will head south on the A64 to Grimston Bar, then through the city centre and out on Malton Road to Little Hopgrove.

From August 3 to 7, the A64 eastbound at Grimston Bar will be closed and traffic through the city centre to rejoin at Hopgrove, or by the A1237 as an alternative route.
