A DEVOTED sister has raised more than £2,000 for a children’s cancer charity after her younger brother was diagnosed with leukaemia.

Seven-year-old Sophia Franklin, a pupil at Heworth CE Primary School in York, organised a school-wide fundraising day inspired by her family’s experience of childhood cancer.

  York pupil Sophia Franklin raises £2,000 for Candlelighters following her brother's leukaemia diagnosis. 

*(Image: Family)*

Her brother Teddy, who is five, was diagnosed with acute lymphoblastic leukaemia (ALL) in March and is undergoing intensive chemotherapy treatment at Leeds Children's Hospital.

Teddy has T-cell ALL where too many T-cells - 'leukaemia cells' or 'blasts' - are produced in the bone marrow and do not fight infections properly.

Teddy is undergoing a two-year course of chemotherapy at LGI until March 2028.

  Teddy Franklin with big sisters Sophia and Alexa at LGI in Leeds where is undergoing chemotherapy treatment. 

*(Image: Emma Franklin)*

Their mum, Emma Franklin, said they received the shock diagnosis after they returned from a half-term trip.

Emma said: "When we got back Teddy seemed quite tired and kept falling asleep after school. After he got a heat rash that didn't fade under a glass, we ended up at York Hospital. Within hours we had a diagnosis of leukaemia; it is incredibly gruelling, like nothing you can ever imagine."

Despite the challenges, she said Teddy had responded well so far.

“He’s now in the third block of chemotherapy and we’ve had positive results back - there were no cancer cells detected. Teddy has handled it brilliantly, he’s such a stoic little character. He largely accepts all this and does understand it. The team has explained it to him that he has 'bad guy cells’ and he is taking ‘super hero’ medicine to get rid of them."

  The Franklin family who say they are incredibly grateful for the support of Heworth CE School and the local community after Teddy was diagnosed with leukaemia. 

*(Image: Emma Franklin)*

Throughout their time in Leeds, parents Emma and Jamie, along with Teddy, Sophia and big sister Alexa, 11, have received invaluable support from children’s cancer charity Candlelighters, which provides practical and emotional help.

Emma said Sophia had enjoyed chatting to the Candlelighters team who greeted them, sporting cheery pink t-shirts while offering smoothies and games to the children.

"She is very energetic and a problem solver. When she found out about Teddy's diagnosis, the first thing she said was 'we are going to blast those cancer cells out of his body!'. She could see that Candlelighters was helping our family and others, and she said 'we need to help them'."

Sophia organised a Pink it Up! day - an annual Candlelighters' fundraising and awareness campaign - with pupils and staff at Heworth Primary dressing in pink, alongside activities including a bake sale, competitions and a donation drive. The event raised £2,100. Sophia also raised a further £120 at her own homemade lemonade stall in Glen Gardens in Heworth.

  Sophia Franklin is a pupil at Heworth Primary School where a Pink it Up! fundraising day raised more than £2,100 for Candlelighters. 

*(Image: Family)*

Emma said: "We're so proud of what Sophia has achieved, along with the fantastic team at Heworth Primary and the generous donations from the whole school community, family and friends.

“We have really had a village around us. Parents in Teddy's reception class have been aware of what's happening and Miss Corner brought the children up to see Teddy outside in the garden when he got back from hospital. We’re so grateful for the support."

Heworth Primary's assistant headteacher Emily Corner said: “We are incredibly proud of Sophia. To see her take on this challenge is truly inspiring. Her creativity and determination have not only raised significant funds that will make a profound difference to the charity, but she has also shown our entire school community what it means to lead with empathy and a desire to make a difference.

"Through her own brilliant idea and sheer determination, she has raised a phenomenal amount of money for a cause so close to her family's heart. She is a shining example of how one young person can make a massive difference to the world."

Funds raised by Sophia will help Candlelighters continue supporting families across Yorkshire facing childhood cancer.
