An empty York care home has cost the council more than £145,000 since it closed in 2018, new figures show.

Most of the money spent on Morrell House, off Burton Stone Lane in Clifton, has been used for security and related expenses due to a rise in anti-social behaviour at the derelict site.

The council’s director of city development, Garry Taylor, said the local authority is “considering options for the site” after no bids were received to redevelop it into affordable housing.

It comes after plans were approved in 2023 to convert the former council-run care home into 12 affordable flats and a six-person house of multiple occupation (HMO).

*(Image: Dylan Connell)*

One of the firms behind the plans, YorSpace, which is responsible for the Lowfield Green development in Acomb, withdrew its application to redevelop Morrell House in 2024.

But Mr Taylor said the council is continuing to work with its partners, including YorSpace, to “bring forward proposals that deliver genuinely affordable housing and a long-term future for the site”.

James Neward, chief operating officer at YorSpace, admitted the firm “just didn’t have the cash” to redevelop Morrell House into affordable housing – but said it is “actively looking” at how the work can be carried out.

*(Image: Supplied)*

A Freedom of Information request revealed that Morrell House has cost the council £145,217.66 in security and related expenses since it closed eight years ago.

The site also cost the council £3,881.40 per year for a private security firm to make regular visits to the empty premises, according to the data.

  Morrell House in Clifton which has stood empty since closing in 2018 

*(Image: Dylan Connell)*

Mr Taylor said: “Morrell House has been vacant since 2018 and these costs have been incurred to keep the site safe and secure, including inspections and security to prevent deterioration and anti-social behaviour.

“We are considering options for the site as no bids were received from registered providers for affordable housing when it was out to tender. We are working with partners, including YorSpace, to bring forward proposals that deliver genuinely affordable housing and a long-term future for the site.”

Residents near Morrell House and the area’s ward councillor told The Press they welcomed plans to redevelop the site into housing.

## 'It will be safer for us' if former care home is redeveloped, says nearby resident

Parvar Ahmad, 36, a resident of Magdalene House opposite Morrell House, said redeveloping the former care could make the area safer.

Mr Ahmad, who has lived at Magdalene House with his wife and two children for five years, said he has reported anti-social behaviour “so many times”, adding: “If I park my scooter or bicycle it will be taken … Sometimes kids come and try to harass us.”

He said he has witnessed children climbing on the roof of the former care home and causing damage.

Mr Ahmad, who works in customer services, felt the issues would stop if Morrell House was turned into housing.

“When I saw the plans a few years ago I was so happy,” he said. “If the plans [go ahead] it will be safer for us as well.”

A resident of the nearby Meek House, who did not want to be named, also said he would welcome Morrell House being redeveloped.

But he said local residents must have priority over the housing.

The 60-year-old, who has lived in Meek House for 35 years, urged developers to “get on with it” and said: “Do it all out and move us lot in there.”

Clifton ward councillor Danny Myers said any development of Morrell House must deliver fully affordable housing.

“As ward councillors, we want it to be 100 per cent genuinely affordable housing – we don’t want to see one of these houses anything other than that,” he said. “It’s council owned, so it should be to provide for the people who need housing in Clifton and in the city.”
