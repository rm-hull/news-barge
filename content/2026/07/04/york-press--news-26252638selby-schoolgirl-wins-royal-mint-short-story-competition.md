A North Yorkshire schoolgirl has been crowned national winner of the Royal Mint Museum’s annual short story competition after beating nearly five thousand entries from across the United Kingdom.

Seven-year-old Marlowe Askew-Smith, of Selby, impressed judges with her story The Gold Fox, which tells the tale of a girl who creates a magical golden fox that comes to life and warms the heart of a lonely king.

The competition invited children aged seven to 11 to write a 500-word story on the theme of gold.

*(Image: Supplied)*

Dan Thomas, King's assay master at The Royal Mint and a competition judge, said: "I would read The Gold Fox to my own children.

"It is an incredibly uplifting story that shows gold is precious to us all for many reasons other than its value."

As the national winner, Marlowe received a gold half-sovereign coin and a professionally illustrated, framed copy of her story created by paper artist Laura K. Sayers.

  The Gold Fox 

*(Image: Supplied)*

She also won a £5,000 voucher for books and equipment, donated to a school or library of her choice.

Second prize went to 10-year-old Oakley Howe from Somerset, and third prize to 11-year-old Lucas Zheng from Cardiff.

Each received a silver sovereign coin and vouchers worth £2,000 and £1,000 respectively.
