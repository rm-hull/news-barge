## 'Absolutely beautiful' beach named a hidden gem to visit this summer

Cayton Bay beach is a quiet alternative to busier seaside resorts. *(Image: Tripadvisor)*

An “absolutely beautiful” beach in North Yorkshire has been named one of the best hidden gems in the UK.

The outdoor experts, Millets, have analysed a range of beaches with less than 500 reviews to create a top 10 list of the best hidden gem beaches to visit this summer.

Each beach was analysed based on their Tripadvisor ratings, average summer temperature, average daily summer sunshine hours, and water quality classification.

Cayton Bay beach made it onto the list and could be a great spot for a family day trip to the seaside.

  The beach has a little coffee kiosk on the way down to the shore 

*(Image: Tripadvisor)*

## 
  **What is Cayton Bay beach like?**

Compared to the more bustling Scarborough, Cayton Bay beach is a quieter option for those wanting a relaxing spot to spend a day by the sea.

The beach has plenty of fine sand and is backed by coastal cliffs that gets around , according to Millets.

If you love water sports, you might like this area, as it is hugely popular with surfers and windsurfers.

The bay is home to the Scarborough Surf School, which has been offering lessons since 1989 making it one of the longest established surf schools in the UK.

If you like spotting wildlife or history, the area is great for birdwatching and fossil hunters, as the cliffs were formed around 150 million years ago.


The beach is also dog friendly so the whole family can enjoy the seaside trip.

In terms of food, the Salty Dog can be found on the way down to the beach and serves “freshly ground coffee, homemade food and locally baked cakes”.
