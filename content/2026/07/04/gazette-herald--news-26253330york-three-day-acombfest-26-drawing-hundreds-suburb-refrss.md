HUNDREDS of people have made their way to the first festival of its type in a York suburb.

The Press visited AcombFest 26, which is taking place across Acomb over three days from Friday, July 3 to Sunday, July 5, bringing large-scale murals and community events to Front Street, York Road and the surrounding area.

One side of Acomb Road was full of people making their way to the event and Front Street was full of people getting into the spirit of the inaugural event.

It's not just about the murals though - read more here.

The festival features live street art created under the theme "return to nature."

The full map can be seen here.


The huge installations are stopping people in the street and drawing crowds to the community hub.

  Front Street is packed with hundreds of people taking in the detail of the murals on display 

*(Image: Kevin Glenton)*

A spokesperson for The Art of Protest, the curators, said: "We spoke to over 1,100 residents and nine local schools to understand Acomb's rich tapestry of history, flora and wildlife.

"This is a celebration of international and local artists, the beloved High Street and green spaces."

In addition to the murals, the festival will include art markets, workshops and activities throughout the weekend.
