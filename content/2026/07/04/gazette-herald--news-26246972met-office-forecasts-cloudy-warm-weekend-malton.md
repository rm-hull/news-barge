The Met Office has forecast a mix of sunshine and cloud for Malton this weekend.

Saturday will start off cool, with temperatures between 15°C and 16°C.

The morning will see partly cloudy skies, with some cloudier spells around mid-morning.

By late morning, sunny intervals will become more widespread.

There is a 10 per cent or lower chance of rain, so it should stay dry.

Winds will gradually increase from 8 mph at daybreak to around 12 mph by midday.

In the afternoon, temperatures will rise steadily from 16°C to 20°C.

Some cloud will move through during the late afternoon, but there should still be bright spells.

Rain remains highly unlikely, with probabilities generally below 10 per cent.

Winds will become quite breezy, reaching 15–17 mph.

In the evening, temperatures will peak at around 21°C.

Skies will alternate between cloudy periods and sunny intervals, with the brightest conditions returning after around 8pm.

Dry weather will continue with virtually no chance of rain.

Winds will be at their strongest during this period, around 17–18 mph, before slowly easing later in the evening.

Overnight, conditions will remain dry with partly cloudy skies.

Temperatures will gradually fall from 21°C to around 18°C.

Winds will slowly ease to around 12–13 mph by midnight.

Sunday morning will start mostly cloudy with temperatures around 16°C, gradually rising to 19°C by lunchtime.

There's a 20–30 per cent chance of a light shower or patchy drizzle, particularly from late morning, although many places will stay dry.

Winds will remain light to moderate, increasing from 10 mph to around 14 mph.

In the afternoon, cloud will continue to dominate with only occasional brighter breaks.

Temperatures will level off around 20°C.

The chance of rain will steadily decrease from 20 per cent to around 10 per cent, with most of the afternoon expected to stay dry.

Winds will remain steady at 14–15 mph.
