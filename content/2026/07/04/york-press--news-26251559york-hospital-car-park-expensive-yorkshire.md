## £22.50 charges for York Hospital parking causing 'added stress' for patients

The parking facilities at York Hospital have been revealed to be the most expensive in Yorkshire *(Image: Newsquest)*

PATIENTS at York Hospital have lamented £22.50 parking charges for any stays over five hours as creating "added stress" with the hospital revealed to be the most expensive in the county for parking.

Users of the site’s multi-storey car park have hit out at its “expensive” charges, amid concerns of parking availability, and heavy congestion and road closures leading to York Hospital.

Visitors have criticised parking charges at the site, where a full day slot costs £22.50 – more than £10 higher than at Scarborough Hospital.

  


Patients and visitors parking for an hour at York Hospital are hit with a £3.10 charge, which extends to £18.60 for a four to five-hour stay, and the maximum day price of £22.50 for anything exceeding five hours.

Those arriving for appointments say these costs, in addition to limited parking space and lengthy waits along Wigginton Road placed “added stress” on patients and visitors during an “already difficult time”.

Speaking about the parking costs, a spokesperson for York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust said: “Parking charges for patients and visitors at our hospitals are broadly aligned with those charged by local authorities in the areas we serve and are reviewed regularly.

“We offer a wide range of concessions and exemptions to support those who need it, including for those who attend the hospital regularly, parents of sick children, and visitors to patients receiving end-of-life care.”

## 'How can that amount be justified?'

Patient Julie Tickner said she had paid £18.60 to visit a loved one in hospital recently.

She said: “I queued for almost 15 minutes to get in, parked up just before 3pm and left just after 7pm.

“I would have been out just before 7pm, which would have made it cheaper, but we had to wait to be let out of the ward for quite some time.

“I don't expect it to be free, but how can that amount be justified?

“It’s not sustainable to pay that every day for 10 days. I live an hour away from the hospital, so public transport isn’t an option.”

Another visitor Izzy Sellers said that she missed an appointment at the hospital because she was unable to park in time.

She said: “I now have to wait months for another.

“The car park isn’t fit for purpose anymore.”

Another hospital user Jane Nunns added that despite living 10 minutes from York Hospital, she routinely leaves her home 40 minutes early to account for the traffic.

She said: “I am also a wheelchair user and unless you get there at around 8am, there’s no hope of getting a disabled space.

“There have been times when the queues are so bad we have to park elsewhere and my husband has to push me there in my chair.”

The hospital has been plagued by parking issues for many years, former NHS worker Leonie Arthur told The Press.

Despite living 10 miles away in Wheldrake, Leonie – a former service manager at York Hospital – recalls setting off just before 7am for her 8am shift.

She said: “If I didn’t get to the hospital before 7.45am to find a parking space on the nearby roads, I would have to use the multi-storey car park, which I would have to pay for from my own salary.

“At the time, it cost me £10 for a full-day pass, and I remember finding that incredibly hard.”

## 'Having better infrastructure in the city would make such a difference'

Leonie, who did not disclose her surname, retired in 2023, just months after parking permits were introduced to NHS staff living a specified distance from the hospital.

She said: “I remember looking out at Wigginton Road through the hospital window and seeing the endless queues from the moment appointments began at 9am until the end of the day.

“It’s a shame because having better infrastructure in the city would make such a difference.

“Before Covid we had a park and ride service that used to pick people up from the Rawcliffe area.

“Sadly, it didn’t take off due to poor advertisement and ended during the pandemic.

“It would be good to see this service back again with the addition of services running from every park and ride in York.

“I know I would have used it regularly if it set off to the hospital from Grimston Bar or the Designer Outlet.”

Leonie added that the hospital was part of a “wonderful” Trust, full of “dedicated people who want to help” but did not have the parking space to accommodate patients and staff.
