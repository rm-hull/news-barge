# Trump says Netanyahu White House meeting could happen next week

- President Donald Trump said a meeting with Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu could happen next week as the U.S. continues to negotiate with Iran for a deal to permanently end the war.
- Trump said Saturday that Netanyahu asked him for a meeting, and that it could take place as early as next week after the NATO summit in Turkey.
- The potential meeting comes as hundreds of thousands of mourners have begun a dayslong funeral for slain Iranian Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, who was killed in a Feb. 28 airstrike at the beginning of the war.
- The funeral for Khamenei, who ruled Iran for decades before he was killed at age 86, could provide a boost for the country's theocracy and its new supreme leader, his son Ayatollah Mojtaba Khamenei.
- That is crucial as Iran tries to leverage its hold on the Strait of Hormuz in negotiations with the U.S. and as concern lingers that Israel could attack again.
