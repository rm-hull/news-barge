## FIRST LOOK: new coffee shop from team behind one of York's most popular brunch venues

Jack Swire and the team at Rise coffee shop in Museum Street, York *(Image: Kevin Glenton)*

THE team behind one of York's most fashionable and identifiable brunch restaurants has branched out further into the city.

Rise has opened a new coffee shop in Museum Street, on the site of the former Cocoa Joes opposite Museum Gardens, and The Press went along to speak to the owner on its second day (Saturday, July 4).

The new launch followed a 12-week fit out.


Co-founder Jack Swire said the soft opening yesterday was a success and already customers were giving positive feedback about the convenience of an early morning opening.

He said: "We staffed up and the reception was great.

"7am is unusual for coffee shops to open in this area and customers are saying it really makes a difference."

  Jack Swire, co-founder of Rise said the reception from customers so far has been great 

*(Image: Kevin Glenton)*

Jack said the offering in Museum Street is different from the extremely popular Fossgate all day brunch restaurant, reviewed by The Press recently.

"Here, is very much 'grab and go', and you can still sit with your coffee in a relaxed atmosphere.

"The customer interactions are where we can bring a bit of personality to the experience, where we can bring a personal service."

  Customers can takeaway merch as well as mochas 

*(Image: Kevin Glenton)*

The Press popped in for a falafel bagel on Friday lunchtime and enjoyed a relaxing half-hour with prompt service and a friendly introduction from staff to the new premises.

A lot of the produce is home-made - bakes, coffee syrups, bagels and toasties, and Rise pastries are delivered from Bakeri Baltzersen in Harrogate.

The York-set up mirrors the team's operation in Preston, Jack said, and they offer all-day brunches in Liverpool.

  *The new Rise coffee shop is at 11 Museum Street and opens 7am to 6pm Monday to Saturday and 8am to 5pm on Sundays.*
