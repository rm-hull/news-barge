Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce are married!

After months of speculation over how they’d celebrate their nuptials, the mysterious Madison Square Garden rumors became a reality when paparazzi and countless chauffeured cars began pulling up outside the New York City arena Thursday and Friday.

In a statement shared with *The Independent*, Swift’s representative revealed that the ceremony was officiated by Adam Sandler and that Swift chose not to have bridesmaids.

Instead, her brother Austin Swift served as her Man of Honor and Jason Kelce was the groom’s Best Man.

Around 7.30 p.m. Friday, LED signs outside the venue read “JUST&T MARRIED!” The “T&T” is a nod to their shared first initial, which has been featured on guest merchandise for the event.

The couple’s bleary-eyed celebrity guests began pouring out of the venue in the early hours, helping to confirm who was lucky enough to receive an invite... and who might have been left off the guestlist.

## Lena Dunham's risqué wedding speech

Comedy was clearly at the forefront of the wedding.

The Madison Square Garden ceremony was officiated by Adam Sandler and included a dinner speech from *Girls* creator Lena Dunham that had attendees “gasping.”

Dunham — one of Swift’s oldest and most loyal friends — reportedly quipped that “American football is just straight guys reenacting gay porn.”

The risqué gag drew “gasps and laughs” from guests, according to a *Daily Mail*insider, who added that Swift immediately declared Dunham a “genius” after her speech.

## 'GMA' hosts share details after attending Swift and Kelce's wedding

Robin Roberts, George Stephanopoulos and Michael Strahan of *Good Morning America* were among the attendees at Madison Square Garden on Friday.

Roberts began by confirming that Stevie Nicks performed at the wedding, as reports had previously suggested.

Stephanopoulos shared that Swift and Kelce had “little books” at the altar.

He added: “The vows were everything you would hope for. Real, vulnerable, serious and silly, deeply loving.”

Roberts also said that despite the stadium setting, the ceremony was “really intimate” because “they had their neighbors [and] their high school friends [there] … like any wedding you would attend.”

“It’s hard to imagine a place that big and a wedding with such stars could feel so personal,” Stephanopoulos agreed.

## The glaring absence at Taylor Swift’s wedding to Travis Kelce

As Taylor Swift celebrated her union with NFL star Travis Kelce with around 1,000 of their closest friends and family, there was one (seemingly) notable absence.

On Thursday, as workers carried props, food and pink carpet into Madison Square Garden, the pop star’s longtime friend Blake Lively, 38, was spotted with husband Ryan Reynolds, 49, hundreds of miles away in Lake Placid, New York, at their daughter’s horse show.

This was the same day that Swift and Kelce were said to be hosting their rehearsal dinner, with Swift’s close friends Selena Gomez, Lena Dunham and Jack Antonoff among the guests spotted turning up to the venue.

Then, on Friday, hundreds more celebrities were seen making their way into the venue and then leaving in the early hours of Saturday morning, among them Hugh Grant, Gigi Hadid and Bradley Cooper, Gracie Abrams, Ed Sheeran and Cherry Seaborn, Ellie Goulding, the Haim sisters and Graham Norton.

Yet Lively and Reynolds weren’t spotted by photographers, social media, fans or the hundreds of news outlets that have been reporting on the celebrations in the heart of New York City.
