## The Press says extortionate hospital car parking charges need changing

York Hospital *(Image: Newsquest)*

Being able to park at hospital without worrying about the financial hit should be a basic right for everyone.

The Press believes strongly about this as we today launch our special report campaign highlighting the cost of parking at York Hospital which costs a whopping £22.50 for any stays over five hours.

This is in contrast with £11.90 in Scarborough and £5.60 in Bridlington.

Patients do not want to be worrying about how much they will be out of pocket when their healthcare should be at the centre of their concerns.

We believe it is wrong that high parking charges are placing additional stresses on local families at a time when they may be fearing the worst or in severe pain.

Our reporter Abby Backhouse has heard from various patients and the MP for Outer York, Luke Charters, who have voiced their frustrations over parking at the hospital.

The NHS states that one of its six values is "striving to improve health and wellbeing and people’s experiences of the NHS".

This newspaper believes York Hospital would be more aligned with this value for many patients if it was to reduce the price of paying for parking.

  York Hospital 

*(Image: Newsquest)*

People are not attending the hospital for leisure but out of necessity and it is wrong to force them to cough up large amounts of money when they are in need of potentially lifesaving care.

York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust told us the charges are "broadly aligned with those charged by local authorities" with "a wide range of concessions and exemptions to support those who need it".

We do not believe there should be concessions in the first place, and that everyone should be treated equally when it comes to affordable parking prices with regards to healthcare.
