WORK is underway as a water operator starts work on scheme that it hopes will cut sewage discharges into the River Ouse by almost half.

Yorkshire Water has revealed it has launched an £870,000 improvement scheme at York Landing Lane's combined sewer overflow (CSO) that will be completed in about 10 months.

This is the city’s first storm overflow upgrade under a wider £1.5 billion programme, which forms part of the company’s plans to reduce discharges across the region by 2030.

Louise Wright, project manager at Yorkshire Water, said: "Our storm overflows are operating more than we, and our customers, would like, and we’re committed to reducing the number of discharges taking place.

"We’re happy to be getting underway with our York programme, which will see the number of discharges from York Landing Lane reduced by 49 per cent."

The work includes replacing the existing overflow chamber with a larger unit and installing screens to prevent sewer debris from entering the river during discharges.

Once complete, Yorkshire Water says they expect to cut CSO discharges into the River Ouse by 49 per cent and improve water quality by holding back more excess wastewater during heavy rain.

This stored water will be treated once network capacity returns and the project is part of the company's £8.3 billion investment.

They are preparing additional storm overflow improvement schemes elsewhere in York and the regional investment programme aiming to upgrade more than 450 overflows across Yorkshire.

It follows a previous £180 million investment over the last two years, which Yorkshire Water reduced discharges by 12 per cent in 2024 and by a further 24.5 per cent in 2025.
