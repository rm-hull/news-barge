The North Yorkshire Moors Railway is set to take visitors on a chilling journey into the darker side of the moors with an immersive ghost‑themed evening experience returning to the line.

The Mad Alice Ghost Train will run from Pickering to Levisham and back on October 18, 25, and November 1, offering passengers spooky tales, live actors, and ghostly encounters on board.

Charlotte Hawes, events manager at the railway, said: "We can’t wait to welcome back Mad Alice for this year’s Ghost Train.

"Perfect for thrill‑seekers, this is an experience like no other in the historic setting of our heritage railway.

  Getting ready for a spooky evening ride across the moors 

*(Image: NYMR)*

"Back for the second year, whether you’re a returning horror enthusiast or looking for a unique Halloween experience, miss this…if you dare."

The hour‑long experience begins at Pickering Station and features chilling storytelling from the infamous Mad Alice of The Bloody Tour of York.

Passengers will be treated to an eerie retelling of Charles Dickens’ The Signalman, a ghost story inspired by a real‑life train crash witnessed by Dickens.

  The Mad Alice Ghost Train 

*(Image: NYMR)*

Guests aged 18 and over will also receive a mini Mad Alice York Gin & Tonic, courtesy of York Gin.

Minimum age 14.

Mad Alice, a legendary figure said to haunt York’s ancient alleyways, leads the night as host.

She said: "The beautiful North York Moors is the perfect backdrop for an evening of spooks and scares."

The experience runs from 6.15pm to 7.15pm and 8pm to 9pm.

Adult tickets are £40, with under-18s priced at £35.
