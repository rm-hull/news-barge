A WARNING has been issued to dog owners this summer on where they can bring their pooch in North Yorkshire.

North Yorkshire Council has confirmed again that until September 30 - dogs are not permitted on many of the county’s popular beaches.

This includes Filey, Scarborough, Whitby West Cliff, and Sandsend, and could be an unwelcome surprise to dog owners heading on a day out to the coast.

To help clear up any confusion, North Yorkshire Council has released an interactive map - with areas that have a ban in place highlighted in red and areas in blue where dogs must be kept on a lead.

Areas that are not highlighted on the map – such as Robin Hood’s Bay beach, Hunmanby Gap, and some sites along the coastline from popular beaches listed above – do not currently have any seasonal restrictions on dogs.

A spokesperson for the council said: “Annual restrictions for dogs are now in place on sections of Scarborough, Filey and Whitby beaches. The signposted restricted zones are in place until 30 September.

“Dogs are still welcome to splash and play on beaches outside of these zones.”
