## Day three of lane closure in main York road and planned reopening date confirmed

Roadworks were continuing this morning in Wigginton Road (Wednesday, July 1) *(Image: Kevin Glenton)*

A TRAFFIC hotspot in York is reduced to one open lane again this morning.

The Press returned to Wigginton Road, close to York Hospital where contractors for Yorkshire Water have repaired a burst main which resulted in water gushing along both sides on Monday morning (June 29).

  



The York-bound stretch from the junction with Crichton Avenue up to Wigginton Terrace remains closed and diversion signs are in place to move traffic away from the area.

A Yorkshire Water spokesperson said: "We will be completing repairs to consequential damage on the road over the next few days, and we're currently planning to reopen over the weekend.

"The closure is manned at Crichton Avenue from 7am to 7pm and we're allowing emergency services access.

"There are two signed diversion routes currently in place.

"We're very grateful to everyone for their ongoing patience"

As reported on Monday, York Hospital is still accessible although patients and visitors were advised to plan ahead.

  Diversion and road closure signs direct traffic coming into York away from the works 

*(Image: Kevin Glenton)*

On Monday evening, Yorkshire Water said the road was being assessed for damage and a plan was being made for resurfacing.

On social media yesterday, comments were coming in about the congestion and delay caused by the closure.

  The hole in Wigginton Road this morning 

*(Image: Kevin Glenton)*

One person said it took them an hour to get from Monks Cross to Acomb, and another said "Madness at 5pm. Dangerous and multiple incidents, pedestrians on road because of footpath closure, vehicles driving round and turning back in a tight space, and mounting the pavement which was busy on the open side."

One resident living nearby said there was a big workforce out yesterday and paid tribute to them for stopping the leak.

The Press contacted First Bus on Monday for confirmation or detail of any diversions to its routes which typically come into York on Wigginton Road.
