## York hotel plans Yorkshire Day feast with history and entertainment

Middlethorpe Hall will host a themed luncheon, featuring fine dining and storytelling from Mad Alice and Lady Peckett *(Image: Toni Wainwright)*

A special Yorkshire Day celebration will take place at Middlethorpe Hall later this year.

The elegant York country house hotel will host a themed luncheon on Saturday, August 1, featuring fine dining, entertainment, and storytelling in honour of Yorkshire’s heritage.

Guests will be welcomed with a drinks reception and live piano music, before enjoying a three-course meal in the Grill Room.

Throughout the afternoon, entertaining tales of Yorkshire’s past will be shared by well-known performers Mad Alice and Lady Peckett.

Alicia Stabler, creator of The Bloody Tour of York and better known as Mad Alice, said: "Yorkshire has given the world some wonderful things... spectacular scenery, proper Yorkshire puddings, stubborn folk and more executions, ghosts and scandal than you can shake a pitchfork at.

"So what better way to celebrate Yorkshire Day than with good food, good company and a few delightfully dreadful tales?

"Lady Peckett and I promise an afternoon full of history, humour and just enough bloodshed to make you appreciate your lunch."

The menu, created by head chef Ashley Binder, will showcase produce from across the region.

The luncheon will be paired with selected wines, and will conclude with tea or coffee and Middlethorpe's renowned fudge.

Tickets cost £89 per person and can be booked via: [https://middlethorpe.wearegifted.co.uk/yorkshire-day-celebration](https://middlethorpe.wearegifted.co.uk/yorkshire-day-celebration)
