FORMER colleagues, licensees and customers have remembered a “principled” and “no nonsense” figure who left a lasting mark on the industry as tributes pour in for Humphrey Smith.

The owner and chairman of Tadcaster-based Sam Smith’s Brewery who was known for banning smartphones, dogs and swearing at his pubs across the country, has died at 81.

Messages shared in The Press Book of Condolence reflect both personal memories and his influence on traditional brewing and pub culture.

  Yorkshire brewery owner hailed 'a champion in his own way' - tributes to Humphrey Smith, 81, of Sam Smith's Brewery, pictured here in 1988. 

*(Image: The Press)*

Debbie Millington said: “RIP Mr Smith, it’s been a pleasure working for you."

Miss Anna Anna added: “Deep condolences to the family of this fine and principled gentleman. I had the privilege of working for him for 30 good years when we held our heads proudly for working for this family business. A great loss to the brewing world indeed”.

Janet Sullivan, who managed one of the Sam Smith's pubs, said: “He was a lovely man and would help us anyway he could. Rest in Peace Mr Smith - a perfect gentleman."

Others highlighted his beers and legacy. Morton wrote: “You made the BEST alcohol free beer in the UK. Sam Smith's AF Brown Ale and for that I thank you. Rest in peace sir.” Richard Clark added: “A cracking pint of stout…you can enjoy a few Mr Smith at Sam's in the sky”.

Rev. Stephen Marshall said: “May the grace of God be with you....a true honest man, and the holy spirit be of comfort to your wife and family. R.I.P. Mr. Humphrey”.

Several tributes reflected long-standing working relationships. Roger Ringwood said: “You gave me a break as a young student with regular summer work which provided the wherewithal to progress career wise, thank you very much." Chad Clark added: “Mr Smith - it was an absolute pleasure working for you. The only thing I wished was that I listened to you more! God bless you in heaven, sir.”

A dedicated licencee David Mark Thompson said: “Sending my deepest condolences to the family. Mr Humphrey will be sadly missed by many in and beyond the brewing world. I personally always held him in high esteem and respected his principled no nonsense approach as a longstanding licencee with over 36 years in the trade.”

Carl Mccarron recalled a personal exchange: “A great loss. I wrote him a few years back regarding a Smith's pub in Leeds. I got a hand written letter by return; a copy of the pub's original architect's plan from 1800-whenever, and photos of the building throughout the years. I was amazed.”

Others reflected his character and approach. One community contributor said: “God bless Humphrey. You did it your way. It brought conversation back into the pub.”

Another added: “Huge respect for a man who stuck to his guns and didn’t court fame or kowtow to modern innovations. He held his values dear. A champion in his own way.”

Mark Ringwood said: “As a youngster I knew you well. I moved away from Tadcaster but stayed loyal. Everyone has their critics but as far as I'm concerned there's a lot of good to be said for what you have done for brewing.”
