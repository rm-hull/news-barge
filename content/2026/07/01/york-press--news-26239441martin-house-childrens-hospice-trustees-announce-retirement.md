A children’s hospice has announced a new chair of trustees after the announcement of multiple trustees stepping down.

The changes at Martin House Children’s Hospice follow the retirement of three long-serving trustees, Tim Halstead, Mike Millington and Stephen Plews.

Mr Halstead, who served on the board for 13 years — eight as chair — said: "It has been an immense privilege and simply one of the best things I have ever done.

"Martin House is a safe haven for families of children with life-shortening conditions, yet it’s also a joyous place, run with clinical expertise, care and compassion."

Mike Millington, who also served for 13 years, and Stephen Plews, a trustee for 20 years, have stepped down alongside Mr Halstead.

Their tenure saw the completion of The BUILD, a £21.9 million redevelopment project at the hospice.

Tim Straughan, who joined the board in 2017, has now taken up the role of chair.

Matthew Rayner, who has more than 25 years of financial services experience, has also joined the board as a trustee.

Clair Holdsworth, chief executive at Martin House, said: "Their experience and insight will be invaluable as we continue to develop our services and support even more families across Yorkshire."
