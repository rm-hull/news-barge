TWO roads will close near a huge traffic junction scheme being developed outside an East Yorkshire town which will improve the flow of traffic between York and Hull.

East Riding of Yorkshire Council said the two closures in Beverley are for surfacing works and the installation of bus shelters and road markings.

  


On Monday, July 6, Lincoln Way will be closed for two days from Regiment Roundabout to the junction with Shepherd's Lane between 9.30am and 3.30pm.

On Tuesday July 7, Victoria Road North will be closed from Victoria Roundabout to Normandy Avenue between 8pm and 5am.

Diversion routes will be in place during the closures, the council said.

The £86.9m A164 and Jock’s Lodge junction improvement scheme near Beverley began main construction in early 2024 and is scheduled to be completed in late 2026.

  The scheme’s objectives are to cut congestion and improve safety at one of the East Riding’s busiest junctions by separating local and through traffic, shortening journey times on the A164 and A1079, and supporting future growth around Beverley.
