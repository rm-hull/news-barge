Thousands of runners are set to take on a marathon challenge in York this October.

The Altra Yorkshire Marathon returns on Sunday, October 18, and will be among the city’s calendar highlights.

Organisers are inviting runners of all abilities to take part in the 26.2-mile event, which also supports a wide range of charities.

Tristan Batley-Kyle, operations director at Run For All, said: "We’re incredibly excited to be bringing the Altra Yorkshire Marathon back to York this October.

"With just 16 weeks to go, now is the perfect time for anyone considering taking part to sign up and begin their training journey.

"The course is known for being fast, flat and welcoming, making it ideal for runners of all abilities – whether you’re chasing a personal best or taking on your very first marathon.

"It’s a truly special event that brings together people from all walks of life for an unforgettable day in a fantastic city."

The marathon starts and finishes at the University of York, taking participants through historic streets and past landmarks including York Minster.

Runners then head out into the surrounding countryside on a route known for its supportive crowds and lively atmosphere.

Thousands of spectators are expected to line the course, joined by volunteers, schools and community groups.

Fundraising remains at the core of the event, with participants encouraged to support either a personal cause or one of the official partner charities.

These include York Foodbank, Yorkshire Cancer Research, the Jane Tomlinson Appeal, Dementia Forward, Macmillan Cancer Support, York Mind and Children with Cancer UK.

For those not ready to take on the full distance, the Altra 10 Miler offers a shorter alternative with the same electrifying atmosphere.

Run For All recommends starting a structured training plan now to make the most of race day and the 16-week build-up period.
