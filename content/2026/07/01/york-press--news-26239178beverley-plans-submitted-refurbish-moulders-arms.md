A planning application has been submitted to the county council to refurbish The Moulders Arms in Beverley and reconfigure its upper floor for residential use.

The plans, lodged for the site in Wilbert Lane, propose external alterations including replacement windows and new conservation-style rooflights, as well as changes to the living space above the pub.

  


The application covers refurbishment of the ground-floor public house and internal reconfiguration of the upper floors to create two residential units — one to support pub operations and a separate one-bedroom flat.

The proposals also involve the formation of accommodation in the roofspace, replacement of the existing rooflight with a conservation-style alternative, and installation of new white uPVC windows in a like-for-like style.

  The Moulders Arms in Beverley was thought to have closed its doors in the autumn of 2024 

*(Image: Google Street View)*

No extensions are planned and the existing building footprint would remain unchanged.

A design, access and heritage statement submitted with the application said: "The proposals represent a sensitive and considered response to the challenges facing an established Beverley public house."

The statement added: "Through targeted investment and careful design, the scheme seeks to bring a vacant public house back into active use whilst ensuring that the upper floor accommodation functions efficiently and effectively."

The Moulders Arms is located within the Beverley Conservation Area but is not a statutorily listed building.

The pub, which has been closed for some time, has historically served as an important community asset.

Social media posts appear to show The Moulders Arms last closed its doors in the autumn of 2024.

The statement said: "The public house has remained closed for a period of time and, prior to closure, no longer reflected the standards expected of successful contemporary hospitality venues."

"Despite these challenges, the building continues to represent an important component of the local streetscape and retains considerable potential to once again become a valued destination within the community."

Investment and refurbishment are seen as key to securing the long-term future of the venue, the plans say.

The planning documents stated: "At a time when many public houses continue to disappear from towns and cities across the country, the opportunity to bring an established local venue back into active use represents a significant positive outcome."

The plans said the design reflects the character of the surrounding area and would not change the main function of the building.

The statement said: "The proposals represent an efficient reuse of the existing building whilst maintaining its established function as a public house."
