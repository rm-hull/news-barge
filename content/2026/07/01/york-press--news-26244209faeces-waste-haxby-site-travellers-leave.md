## Human waste and piles of rubbish found after Travellers leave York playing field

Cllr Andrew Hollyer and a member of the professional cleaning team dealing with the mess left on Ethel Ward Playing Fields after Travellers left the site. *(Image: Submitted)*

HUMAN waste, raw chicken and dirty food packets have been collected today as part of a major clean-up after Travellers left a community playing field in York.

Professional cleaners have been brought in to deal with the 'large amounts of rubbish including faeces' from Ethel Ward Playing Field in Haxby.

Members of the travelling community pitched up at the site last Friday, June 26, with a local councillor reporting 16 caravans and about 13 cars there by Monday.

City of York Council's Neighbourhood Enforcement Officers served a Direction to Leave Notice on Monday (June 29), giving the group until 2pm today, Wednesday (July 1) to leave.

The group left by Tuesday evening, paving the way for a clean-up operation this morning of the community space which is set to host Haxby's popular carnival on Saturday, July 12.

  Faeces and rubbish on Haxby playing field after Travellers leave the site. 

*(Image: Submitted)*

Families with young children are being asked to stay away until the play area has been sanitised.

A Haxby Town Council spokesperson said in a statement on social media: "The travellers have now left - we are aware that large amounts of rubbish including faeces have been left.

"The area will be professionally cleaned. In the meantime we ask residents to avoid the area until this has taken place. We will update you once the clean is complete and the area can safely be accessed."

Haxby councillor Andrew Hollyer was at the site by 7am today, Wednesday, to start clearing the mess, with a team of professional cleaners arriving shortly afterwards and wasting no time in filling dozens of bin bags.

  The fire service was called to Ethel Ward playing field on Tuesday night to deal with a pile of rubbish that had been set alight. 

*(Image: Submitted)*

Cllr Hollyer said the fire service was called to the scene on Tuesday night to deal with some rubbish that had been set alight.

He said: "There's piles of bin bags. I have been around this morning to pick stuff out of hedges. There's raw chicken packets, bits of a carcass - they had obviously cooked part of a sheep, and there were legs covered in blood and flies.

  Haxby Ward Councillor Andrew Hollyer said he had received "numerous" concerned reports from residents near to Ethel Ward Playing Field (Image Cllr Andrew Hollyer) 

*(Image: Cllr Andrew Hollyer)*

There are areas of the site which they have been using as a 'bathroom'. It needs a proper professional team of people to clean it.

"We have jet-washed the playing area and hard surface and are going around collecting waste. We probably have about 50 black bags and we have a skip coming too. They had spread themselves out across the whole of the field."

Cllr Hollyer said he was unsure at this stage who would cover the clean-up cost, but said that it would ultimately fall to the tax-payer.

"The town council doesn't really have the kind of money to manage the scale of this. That's a decision to be had. The important thing at the moment is to get the site back to being useable.

"It is a case of cleaning it up. It is good that this didn't happen a week or so later when we have Haxby Carnival which is all on the playing field after a parade through the village."

Last summer, Travellers also pitched up at Ethel Ward Playing Field which is open and accessible from all sides.
