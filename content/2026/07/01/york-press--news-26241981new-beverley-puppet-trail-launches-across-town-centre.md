A puppet trail is popping up in shop windows through Beverley town centre.

Created by Puppets, the Beverley Puppet Trail will lead visitors and residents on a puppet hunt around Beverley while gathering feedback to help revive the cancelled Beverley Puppet Festival, which would have marked its 12th edition this year.

The festival was cancelled due to a lack of funding.

Anna Ingleby, outgoing festival founder and co-director, said: “The beloved biennial Festival has run for over 20 years and is an important part of Beverley’s cultural offering.

“We had an exceptionally fun and full programme planned for 2026, but unfortunately, due to a lack of funding, it had to be cancelled.

“I believe this year’s Beverley Puppet Trail will provide the perfect platform to get feedback via our online survey, not just from our 30,000 residents but from all our amazing visitors as well.

“We really do need support from local people if it is to continue in the future…this isn’t just about funding.”

Puppets can be found in shop windows across Beverley, and the trail will run until July 26.

Trail sheets can be collected from the Beverley Tourist Information Centre or participating businesses.

Completed sheets should be returned to a shop for entry into a prize draw, with items donated by featured businesses.

Participants are encouraged to complete an online survey about the festival, available on the trail sheets and via the home page of www.beverleypuppetfestival.com.

The survey also invites people to express interest in becoming involved with the festival, including roles as co-artistic director or voluntary board members.

Philip Guest of Guest and Philips jewellers said: “It’s such a shame that this year’s puppet Festival isn’t going ahead.

“We’re pleased to be part of the puppet trail; hopefully it helps to attract vital Festival funding and support."
