# Hundreds rally behind Mind in Harrogate District as annual Bright Bling Ball raises incredible £37,000

## Mind in Harrogate District is celebrating the success of its biggest annual fundraiser which has raised an incredible £37,000 for children and young people’s mental health services.

The charity's Bright Bling Ball took place on Saturday, June 20, at the Orangery at Newby Hall, bringing together local businesses, community supporters and fundraisers for an evening of entertainment, generosity and celebration.

Guests enjoyed a sparkling drinks reception, a three-course meal, live entertainment and a lively charity auction, all inspired by the event's colourful ‘Bright Bling’ theme.

The money raised will go directly towards Mind in Harrogate District's Children and Young People's Mental Health Service, which provides early intervention and emotional wellbeing support for young people across the district.

![Mind in Harrogate District is celebrating the success of its biggest annual fundraiser, The Bright Bling Ball, which has raised an incredible £37,000 for children and young people’s mental health services](https://www.harrogateadvertiser.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOmU2YjlhYjFmLTFmNTMtNDdjYi1hYTViLTI1YTc1NzQ4NzY0ZTo4MDFiNmQyNC1iMWU4LTQ3YzUtOGIyNC04ZTg2NjE4MWVjZWM=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



The charity has thanked everyone who contributed to the evening's success, including Newby Hall and Gardens for hosting the event, Event Prop Hire for transforming the venue and Stagecoach Performing Arts Harrogate for its performance and fundraising efforts.

The ball was sponsored by John and Pat Barnes, enabling funds raised through ticket sales, the auction and raffle to go directly towards supporting local children and young people.

John and Pat Barnes said: "We're incredibly proud to support this event and the vital work being done for children and young people locally. Seeing the community come together with such generosity and energy was truly inspiring."

A highlight of the evening was the charity auction, hosted by Richard Smailes of FSS Property, whose humour and enthusiasm encouraged generous bidding throughout the night.

Auction prizes were donated by a wide range of local businesses and supporters, including Dart Marina Hotel and the Seahorse Restaurant, Gowan Jewellers, Rudding Park Hotel, Anita Bowerman, The Pickled Fork and Sommelier's Choice.

One of the evening's most sought-after lots was donated by Antiques Roadshow expert Susan Rumfitt, offering afternoon tea at Swinton Park alongside the opportunity to hear from her and fellow Antiques Roadshow specialists Steven Moore and Will Farmer about the fascinating world of antiques.

The charity said every pound raised through the auction will go directly towards helping children and young people across the Harrogate district.

Tina Chamberlain, Chief Executive of Mind in Harrogate District, said: "We're still on such a high from this year's Bright Bling Ball.

"It was an unforgettable evening and a powerful reminder of what our community can achieve together.

"The funds raised will make a real difference to children and young people in our local area."
