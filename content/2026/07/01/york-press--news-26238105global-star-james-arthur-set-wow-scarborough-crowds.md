## Global star set to wow North Yorkshire crowds this week - how to get tickets

Scarborough Open Air Theatre is hosting James Arthur on Friday. *(Image: Cuffe & Taylor)*

A chart-topping singer-songwriter who became a global star after winning The X Factor will be taking to the stage in North Yorkshire this week.

James Arthur is set to headline at Scarborough Open Air Theatre on Friday, following in the steps of other stars during this summer's season, including Rick Astley, Madness, Paul Weller and Anastacia.

*(Image: Scarborough Open Air Theatre)*

The Middlesbrough-born singer-songwriter is one of the UK’s most successful male solo artists since winning the ninth series of ITV’s talent competition in 2012.

He has amassed more than 15 billion streams worldwide and won the 2023 BRIT Billion Award for surpassing one billion UK streams.

Now, fans can expect a more personal and reflective side to his music. His latest Top 3 album, Pisces, released earlier this year, marks a new chapter shaped by self-acceptance and his experiences as a father, partner and friend. The record follows 2024’s chart-topping Bitter Sweet Love and a sold-out UK and Ireland arena tour.

Blending pop, rock, Americana and indie influences, Pisces features some of Arthur’s most heartfelt work to date, including the track Friends, a tribute to the late actor Matthew Perry.

Across six Top 10 albums, Arthur has built a catalogue packed with hits such as Say You Won’t Let Go, Can I Be Him, Cars Outside and You’re Nobody ’Til Somebody Loves You, along with his 5× BRIT Platinum collaboration Rewrite The Stars with Anne-Marie.

The performance will also mark a return to North Yorkshire for Arthur, who previously played to crowds at York Racecourse’s Music Showcase.

With a mix of chart-topping favourites and newer, more intimate material, Friday’s show promises a full-spectrum set of his work.

His Scarborough date comes just as Arthur was announced as part of the Padang Stage line-up in Zone 4 of The Formula 1 Singapore Grand Prix 2026 on October 11, 2026, alongside Janet Jackson and Zara Larsson.

Taking place from Friday, 9 October to Sunday, 11 October 2026, the Singapore Grand Prix will once again combine high-speed racing with a city-wide festival atmosphere.

The latest additions join an already impressive roster of international acts performing across multiple stages and entertainment precincts.
