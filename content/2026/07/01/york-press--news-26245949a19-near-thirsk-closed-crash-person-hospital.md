One person is in hospital after a serious crash that closed part of the A19 in North Yorkshire.

Police and ambulance crews, including two air ambulances, were called to the collision near Thirsk on Wednesday afternoon (July 1).

North Yorkshire Police said the A19 is blocked in both directions after the crash close to Great Thirkleby.

The force has urged drivers to avoid the area, saying the road is “expected to remain closed for some time while emergency services respond and enquiries are carried out".

Drivers are facing delays with slow moving traffic due to the road being blocked between Low Lane and The Avenue, the AA said.

Yorkshire Ambulance Service said its crews were called to the scene shortly after 2.15pm on Wednesday.

"Ambulance resources, including two ambulances, a specialist paramedic (critical care) and an air ambulance, were dispatched to the scene,” a service spokesperson said.

"One patient has been conveyed to hospital."
