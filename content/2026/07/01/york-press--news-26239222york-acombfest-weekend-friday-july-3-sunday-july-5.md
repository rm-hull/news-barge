## Huge mural festival in York area to showcase street artists painting

Charlotte Davies from Greater Acomb Community Forum said the scale of the event has created an incredible buzz since work began on murals ahead of Friday's launch *(Image: Supplied)*

A YORK suburb preparing to burst into colour with the arrival of a three-day mural festival.

AcombFest 26 will run over the weekend from Friday, July 3 to Sunday July 5 and will be turning Front Street, York Road and the wider area into an open-air gallery, blending art, community, and family fun across the west of the city.

Internationally recognised mural artists and multimedia experts are set to create live street art, with large-scale pieces transforming the area under the festival’s “return to nature” theme.

  


Huge fresh artworks will be created live across Acomb, transforming walls into landmarks over the festival weekend, organisers said.

Alongside the works being created are art markets, workshops and activities.

There are 20 festival venues and more than 90 activities and events planned.

  Art of Protest said its work in Acomb involved speaking with residents and schools 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Curators The Art of Protest said it has worked with Greater Acomb Community Forum, Acomb Alive and local to support the ambitions of the festival including Acomb Explore Library, Books & Bevs, Holgate Windmill, The Crooked Tap and The Fox pubs.

A spokesperson for the group said: "We have spoken to over 1,100 residents and nine local schools to understand Acomb's rich tapestry of history, flora and wildlife.

"This is a celebration of international and local artists, the beloved High Street and green spaces."

Charlotte Davies from Greater Acomb Community Forum said the whole area has an incredible buzz at the scale of the event now that the outlines of the murals started to take shape ahead of the big event.

She said: "The size of it is putting people in the picture and they're really getting into it.

"The community is really bought in.

"We've been out dropping off flyers this morning and people we've passed said they're so excited.

"This will have a huge impact and bring more joy to Acomb."

Fran Taylor, treasurer of Acomb Methodist Churches and one of the organisers, said: "AcombFest is a wonderful opportunity to bring people together and celebrate everything that makes our community special.

"We wanted to open our doors and create a place where people can come together, whether for activities, entertainment, a cup of tea and cake, a chat with neighbours, or simply a quiet moment of peace."

The church will also host music therapy sessions, flower arranging with Acomb Flower Guild, drop-in crafts with Crafty Fox, and an art fair with Acomb Artists.

  Preparations are being finalised for the weekend festival in Acomb 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Rae Merriman from Acomb Artists said: "Acomb has such a wealth of creative talent, and AcombFest is a brilliant chance to bring artists, makers, and the wider community together.

"We’re looking forward to sharing our work and being part of such a positive weekend."

Most activities are free, although some workshops and evening events will require tickets.

AcombFest is supported by funding from the York and North Yorkshire Combined Authority’s high street programme.
