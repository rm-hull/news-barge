## Pay for your own!

I HAVE a simple message for Prince Harry: you live abroad, pay no taxes here, then do what every other visitor to Britain does who has security worries, pay for your own.

Meanwhile, what happened at Trent Bridge on the fourth afternoon of the Test Match v New Zealand was stage managed for the benefit of one person - Ben Stokes - at 29 minutes after 3 just as he was bowling we heard of his impending retirement, to then immediately take a wicket was a PR bonus.

But to open the batting was a kamikaze stunt in the middle of a serious Test Match which England have to win to avoid a series defeat. Of course, Stokes has been an outstanding cricketer and captain, but unfortunately not lacking in other human failings which have tarnished a fine career.

  **Peter Rickaby, Moat Way, Brayton**

## 
  **Keep pets hydrated**

With the exceptionally hot weather, don’t forget to keep all pets and wildlife well hydrated with clean, fresh water. A little tip that I discovered for Pippa (the dog) is using a solar-powered water fountain, placing it in a large bowl (I use a washing up bowl) and removing the spray head.

The fountain bubbles away all day long refreshing the water and creating a lovely audible sound.

  **D M Deamer, Penleys Grove Street, Monkgate, York**

## Swap beef for beans

Another record-breaking temperature in the UK is a wake-up call: we’re in a climate emergency, and animal agriculture is fuelling the fire.

According to the United Nations, meat, egg, and dairy production is responsible for about 14.5% of global greenhouse gas emissions – by some estimates, that’s greater than all the world’s transportation systems combined. That’s why the UN is calling for a global shift towards vegan eating.

We all have a responsibility to protect the planet, and one of the simplest, most effective ways to reduce our environmental impact is to choose plant-based foods – it’s as simple as swapping a beef burger for a bean burger.

  **Elisa Allen, Vice President of Programmes PETA Foundation Society Building, London**
