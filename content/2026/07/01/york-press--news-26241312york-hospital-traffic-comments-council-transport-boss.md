## Congestion near York Hospital 'can't be solved overnight'

Congestion in Wigginton Road near York Hospital *(Image: Newsquest)*

CONGESTION near York Hospital cannot be solved overnight, the council’s transport spokesperson has said.

City of York Council’s Labour transport executive member Cllr Kate Ravilious said multiple factors were contributing to queues on Wigginton Road but short-term measures to cope were being looked at.

Cllr Ravilious added a proposed trial ban on private traffic driving through a main York city centre travel corridor was not expected to affect congestion in Wigginton Road.

The executive member’s comments follow calls for congestion to be tackled urgently amid fears it is fuelling dangerous driving and leading to long delays in the area around the hospital.

Labour Guildhall ward councillor Dave Merrett said drivers had been travelling the wrong way down the road while traffic was backed up from Wigginton Road to the hospital’s car park.

He told the council’s executive earlier in June queues had reached as far as Gillygate’s junction with Lord Mayor’s Walk, making bus journeys unreliable and fuelling air pollution.

Council leader Cllr Claire Douglas told the meeting Cllr Ravilious was due to hold talks on the congestion in due course.

Cllr Ravilious addressed the issue when council officials provided new details on their Rougier Route bus corridor plans.

She told the Local Democracy Reporting Service (LDRS): “We’re very much working with the hospital on addressing traffic issues, but there’s no quick, easy, overnight solutions.

“There’s things we’ll be doing in the short-term, our modelling doesn’t suggest the Rougier Route will massively affect how traffic behaves on Wigginton Road.”

A petition launched by Labour Guildhall ward councillors in February signed by 1,960 people was presented to the York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust’s board in May.

The board meeting heard a number of cost-free options were being considered including better advice on getting to the hospital and the location of alternative car parks.

A trust spokesperson said they recognised congestion and parking could be frustrating for patients, visitors and staff and they were reviewing issues raised by the petition.

The Rougier Route is set to see only buses, emergency services vehicles and cyclists allowed to drive from Rougier Street to Micklegate, via George Hudson Street.

A bus lane would run southbound along George Hudson Street with a ‘bus gate’ in place from the road’s junction with Micklegate to the latter’s junction with Skeldergate and North Street.

Private traffic would be sent along a new one-way loop from Rougier Street to Micklegate via Tanner Row and Toft Green, with hackney carriage taxis and Blue Badge holders exempted.

Measures would also be in place so that Bishophill residents can drive to and from their homes.

An 18-month trial of the scheme would start in January if the council’s executive approves it on Tuesday, July 7.
