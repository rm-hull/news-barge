## North Yorkshire libraries launch music-themed summer reading challenge

Library staff, young volunteers and pupils pictured with a banner showcasing this year’s theme, Read to the Beat *(Image: North Yorkshire Council)*

North Yorkshire libraries are launching a music-themed reading challenge to get children reading and singing through the summer holidays.

The Summer Reading Challenge, designed for children aged four to 11, encourages participants to read six library books during the break, earning stickers and prizes along the way.

Running from Saturday, July 4, the challenge includes books about musicians, characters discovering music, and cultural traditions.

The council is supporting the National Year of Reading, which looks to combat the decline of reading for pleasure and inspire people of all ages to read more.

  Primary school pupils Halima and Scarlett taking part in the challenge 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Council)*

Simon Myers, North Yorkshire Council’s executive member for arts and culture, said: "By combining music with reading, there’s no better way to spark a child’s imagination and creative flair.

"It’s important to keep young people reading during the summer break, as it’s a time when learning can dip and take a back seat."

Libraries will also host free events in partnership with musical groups, including rap workshops, drumming sessions, and singing.

Diane Cooper, headteacher at Christ Church CE Primary School in Skipton, said: "The children are excited to again take part in the Summer Reading Challenge.

"Maintaining reading habits over the summer is one of the most effective ways to ensure children return to school confident and ready to continue learning.

  Volunteer Emily Scott reading to Luna, who is taking part in the Summer Reading Challenge 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Council)*

"We will be encouraging all our children to take part in the challenge, discovering new worlds and more."

Volunteers aged 13 to 24 are invited to help with the scheme by registering children, recommending books, and supporting events.

The challenge is delivered by North Yorkshire Council’s library service and organised nationally by the Reading Agency in partnership with Universal Music Group UK.

More information on activities is available via www.summerreadingchallenge.org.uk

Details on volunteering can be found by contacting your local library.
