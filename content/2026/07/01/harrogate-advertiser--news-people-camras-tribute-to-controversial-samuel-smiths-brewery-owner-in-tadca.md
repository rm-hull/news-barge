# CAMRA's tribute to controversial Samuel Smith’s Brewery owner in Tadcaster who has passed at age of 81

## A true titan of brewing who ran Samuel Smith's brewing business based in Tadcaster in a successful if controversial fashion for almost half a century has died at the age of 81 with a tribute from CAMRA.

As well as being a successful businessman and major employer, Humphrey Smith became well known for eccentric decisions, capable of surprising customers, staff and the wider community.

Among the rules firmly imposed at the approximately 300 Sam Smith’s pubs was a ban on mobile phones, music, TVs and swearing.

Humphrey Smith, himself, who had run the family-owned Samuel Smith Old Brewery since the 1980s, was also known to lodge objections to a wide range of planning applications in North Yorkshire.

Perhaps, most famously, the brewery refused to permit the construction of a temporary footbridge over the River Wharfe on land it owned in 2016 after the town's 18th century bridge had been badly damaged by floods the year before.

Blaming the collapse on a “lack of maintenance” by North Yorkshire County Council, Samuel Smith's said the proposed temporary footbridge would cost £500,000 of tax-payers money and the scheme was a "short-term PR based sound-bite".

But the company’s very commitment to its way of doing things also won the venerable company respect from beer drinkers across the North.

On hearing of his passing, a spokesperson for York Campaign for Real Ale (CAMRA) said: "The newspaper headlines may well say that Humphrey was a controversial and divisive figure with many quirks.

"But we'd do well not to forget that he presided over a family brewery that produces exceptional real ale and craft keg beers at very affordable prices and an estate of pubs that provide a huge social impact within their communities."

Established by Smith’s family in 1758, Samuel Smith's pubs remain famous for only stocking Samuel Smith’s products.

Another peculiarity about the famous business Humphrey Smith helmed so successfully as owner and chairman, is that Samuel Smith’s Old Brewery in Tadcaster maintains the world’s largest team of grey Shire horses which are still used to deliver beer to local pubs five days a week.
