Business confidence is on the rise across Yorkshire, pointing to renewed optimism despite a slight dip in the UK’s overall outlook.

Firms saw a five-point increase in confidence during June, reaching 47 per cent, according to Lloyds Bank’s latest Business Barometer.

This boost means Yorkshire is outperforming the UK average.

Martyn Kendrick, regional director for Yorkshire and the Humber at Lloyds, said: "It’s encouraging to see business confidence in Yorkshire moving in the right direction this month, with firms feeling more positive about the wider economy.

"That confidence is being driven by businesses looking ahead with ambition – whether that’s exploring new markets, developing their products and services, or investing in new technology.

"With four in 10 firms also planning to grow their teams over the next year, there’s a real sense of momentum behind Yorkshire’s growth story.

"We’ll continue to support businesses across the region as they invest in the future."

The latest reading sits above the region's 12-month average of 42 per cent, though still below the March peak of 62 per cent.

Companies in Yorkshire reported lower confidence in their own trading outlook, falling 11 points to 45 per cent.

However, optimism in the wider economy rose 21 points to 49 per cent.

Stronger customer demand (64 per cent) and positive economic news (47 per cent) were key drivers of economic confidence, while expectations of new contracts and clients (70 per cent) bolstered the trading outlook.

Looking ahead, businesses identified entering new markets (59 per cent), evolving offerings (51 per cent), and adopting new technologies (43 per cent) as top growth priorities for the next six months.

Nationally, UK business confidence dropped by three points to 44 per cent in June.

Trading outlook declined by two points to 56 per cent, and economic optimism fell by four points to 31 per cent.
