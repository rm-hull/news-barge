## Family breaks silence after boy thrown into crocodile enclosure at zoo

The boy suffered serious injuries after he ended up in a crocodile enclosure at Johnsons of Old Hurst *(Image: Joe Giddens/PA)*

The family of a three-year-old boy who was seriously injured after he was thrown into a Cambridgeshire zoo's crocodile enclosure have broken their silence.

Emergency services raced to Johnsons of Old Hurst, near Huntingdon, and a critical incident was declared by police on June 18.

He was rescued from the pit by staff members, including the zoo's owners Andy and Tracey Johnson, who are said to have leapt into the enclosure.

The toddler received treatment at the scene before being rushed to Addenbrooke's Hospital in Cambridge.

  Police declared a critical incident after the toddler sustained serious injuries 

*(Image: SWNS)*

It has since been revealed he was attacked by one of the reptiles when he ended up in the enclosure on June 18.

The boy remains in a stable condition at Addenbrooke’s Hospital in Cambridge.

In a statement, his family said: "We would like to thank the staff at the zoo who rescued our son from the enclosure.

"We are truly grateful for the public support and well-wishes we have received, as well as to everyone who has been directly involved in our son's care and recovery in hospital.

“Our attention remains focused on his recovery and supporting him through this extremely challenging and prolonged period of time.”

  A 30-year-old man was arrested on suspicion of attempted murder shortly after the incident 

*(Image: SWNS)*

A 30-year-old man from Norfolk was arrested on suspicion of attempted murder shortly after the incident.

The suspect, believed to have learning difficulties, has since been released on bail until September 18.

The force confirmed the man, who is not known to the victim, had been assessed as "not being fit for interview".

It comes just days after allegations two carers "bundled" the suspect into a car moments after the incident.

According to The Telegraph, a source with knowledge of the situation alleged two carers left the crocodile enclosure with the suspect.

However, the source claimed the carers were unable to leave the zoo grounds after staff members intervened.

"They tried to get him into the car," they said.

"Another member of staff was there and prevented them from leaving.”

Detectives are now reviewing CCTV at the premises in a bid to understand the circumstances surrounding the incident.

Johnsons of Old Hurst is in the leafy village of Old Hurst and home to more than 100 animals including tigers and lions.

  Owner Andy Johnson seen outside Johnsons of Old Hurst the day after the incident 

*(Image: SWNS)*

The site includes a farm, café and butcher's shop, which opened 20 years ago, as well as a steakhouse and zoo.

On Saturday (June 27), the zoo confirmed it had reopened its crocodile enclosure and Tropical House.

It has since been revealed no safety concerns were indicated regarding the the zoo's conditions at the time of the incident.

A Huntingdonshire District Council spokesperson said it was not aware of any "previous reportable accidents or incidents" involving the crocodile enclosure.

"No other safety-related incidents have been recorded through the zoo licencing process," they said.

They added that the local authority's environmental health team is "making contact" with zoo managers following the incident .

"As part of this process, we will consider whether any further inspection or follow-up action is required."
