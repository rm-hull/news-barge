# Harrogate bus operator flies the flag for women with new appointment in a traditionally male dominated industry

## A trailblazing former bus driver has been chosen for a senior role at Harrogate Bus Company as part of a new series of appointments of female managers by major bus operator Transdev.

Dawn Thompson-Cullen began her career as a driver with Harrogate Bus Company in 2008 and, 18 years later, she’s back as the new Service Delivery Manager.

A true pioneer, Dawn was the first female Relief Duty Manager and the first female Duty Manager in the company’s history and is returning to Harrogate from sister company FLYER, based in Idle in West Yorkshire.

“Because I’d been doing it for so long, my previous role in Idle was no longer a challenge,” said Dawn Thompson-Cullen.

![Transdev’s new senior appointees - From left, Dawn Thompson-Cullen, Harrogate Bus Company's new Service Delivery Manager, Lucy Harris, Victoria Giannini, and Kat Ayre.(Picture contributed)](https://www.harrogateadvertiser.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOjE3YjU3NjNhLTIzMzQtNDBjNy05YmEyLTA2NzJjZTdiYmNiZjpmODQ4MWUyMS01OWZmLTQ3MWUtOTg5OC1lMDhlM2QwMDMyNjg=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



"That’s why I jumped at the opportunity to step up and be the supervisor for more than 100 drivers in Harrogate.

“People don’t realise how busy you are, all the time – this job never stops, even off the clock.

“I never thought that I’d be doing this role, especially at the age of 60.

"But I’m loving the constant challenge.”

Operating across the north of England, regional bus operator Transdev has strengthened its leadership team, with four female managers and supervisors being appointed in Yorkshire and Lancashire.

Further afield, Kat Ayre becomes the new Operations Manager at Yorkshire Coastliner, Tourism Manager Victoria Giannini now leads CitySightseeing York, while Lucy Harris is the new Central Stores Supervisor covering all Transdev businesses in the north.

Transdev Blazefield run more than 500 buses from nine bases across the north of England, enabling over 32 million journeys each year thanks to a team of 1,400 colleagues.

It has depots in Harrogate, Malton, Bradford, Keighley, Huddersfield and Elland, plus in Lancashire at Burnley and Blackburn.

In an industry which has traditionally been male dominated, Dawn Thompson-Cullen’s new managerial role reflects Transdev’s recognition of the contributions of a diverse workforce.

“You’ve got to have a certain kind of drive to be successful and that’s, actually, what I’m enjoying the most,” said Dawn.

“I’m always learning, learning, learning.

"I’m focused on thinking big picture and driving Harrogate forward.”
