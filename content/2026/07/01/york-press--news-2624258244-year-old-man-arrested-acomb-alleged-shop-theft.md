A 48-hour dispersal order has been issued for the high street of a York suburb following alcohol-related disorder.

North Yorkshire Police said it is stepping up its presence in Front Street, Acomb, and a 44-year-old man was arrested there last night (Tuesday, June 30), for an alleged shop theft and assault.

  


The order was made last night and allows police to direct individuals engaging, or likely to engage in anti-social behaviour to leave the area and not return for a specified period.

A police spokesperson said the force is putting more officers in the area to provide reassurance, prevent further disorder and support local businesses and the community.

Anyone with concerns is asked to contact police on 101, or 999 in an emergency.
