North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service is urging the public to help reduce deliberate fires.

The message comes during Anti-Social Behaviour Awareness Week, which runs from 29 June to 5 July 2026.

In the past six months, firefighters have attended more than 100 suspected deliberate fires in and around areas including Tang Hall, The Clifton Backies, and St Nicks Nature Reserve and Environment Centre.

North Yorkshire Police Acting Sergeant Ben Ambler said: "Fire crews and police units have been deployed to a number of deliberate fires across the city throughout the year, placing significant demand on resources.

"Starting a deliberate fire is extremely dangerous, and it can endanger lives, destroy property and have devastating consequences for individuals, families and the wider community."

Operation FireSafe, launched in March last year, targets fire-related anti-social behaviour through a multi-agency approach, involving North Yorkshire Police and other local partners.

Sy Wray, watch manager at North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service, said: "Intentionally starting fires of this nature is against the law and even a small fire can endanger lives and have serious repercussions for the future in terms of employment prospects for individuals who have historical involvement with anti-social fire setting."

People can complete a FireSafe referral on the North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue website.
