A North Yorkshire GP practice has been hailed ‘outstanding’ after its most recent inspection.

Stockwell Road Surgery in Knaresborough was given the highest rating by the Care Quality Commission (CQC) after its inspection in May.

The CQC’s deputy director of primary care and community services in the north, Linda Hirst, praised staff at Stockwell Road Surgery who she said provide “compassionate high-quality person-centred care” for patients.

She said the teaching and training practice for medical students and GPs, which serves around 8,766 people, was “found to be providing high standards of care and support and are examples of what outstanding care looks like”.

Ms Hirst added that the “hard work of the staff” at the practice is “making a real difference to people’s lives”, adding: “Other GP practices should look at these reports to see if there’s anything they can learn from.”

CQC inspectors, in their report, said staff at Stockwell Road Surgery “kept facilities clean and maintained equipment appropriately to ensure people were kept safe”.

“They assessed and managed the risk of infection well and took steps to control the risk of it spreading. There were enough staff with the right skills, qualifications and experience,” the report said.

Inspectors found that staff supported people to “live healthier lives” and “treated people with kindness, empathy and compassion, and respected their privacy and dignity”.

They also found that the surgery had a “clear vision and strategy” and “staff understood their individual roles and responsibilities”, adding: “Leaders accounted for the actions, behaviours and performance of staff through clear and effective governance processes.”
