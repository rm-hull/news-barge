## 'He did a lot of good for town' - Tadcaster folk lift the lid on real Humphrey Smith

Richard Scott, Kat Scott and step-daughter Grace *(Image: Darren Greenwood)>*

LEADING Tadcaster businesspeople and others have been reflecting on the character and life of Humphrey Smith, both good and bad.

The Press visited Tadcaster earlier today (July 1), following the death of the owner and chairman of Sam Smith’s Brewery at the age of 81.

A couple of business were reluctant to comment, pointing out they were new to town and Humphrey had been their landlord.

But Richard Scott, managing partner at Bromet Jackson Heath solicitors, said he knew Humphrey Smith as he was also ‘effectively’ their landlord.

Richard told the Press: “Humphrey Smith was unique.”

People will comment on how he treats his pub tenants, he continued, but also how he helped the town.

“To be fair to the man, he was a good landlord to his commercial tenants, especially when things went wrong, like with the flood of 2015," said Richard.

"He was very good. I know he stopped all rents and he put a lot of investment into repairing these properties."

“There are things that Humphrey did that people don’t realise. We wouldn’t have the swimming pool were it not for Mr Smith and the brewery and in the various projects he took a personal interest in using his own resources.

“He was almost a character of notoriety but there is another side. That should be recognised. He did a lot of good for the town.”

  Richard Scott, Kat Scott and step-daughter Grace 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

In addition to gifting the land for the swimming pool, Richard says Humphrey was also ‘very good’ to former employees.

“Sometimes he let them live in his properties rent free.”

When it came to Humphrey's pubs, Richard said: “He had a business model he wanted to pursue that was controversial, but it was his vision for what hat he wanted to do with his pubs.

  The frontage of The Old Brewery 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

“It’s a shame many are unoccupied and closed, particularly the one he cared the most about, the Angel and White Horse (in Tadcaster’s high street, next to the brewery). It’s a lovely pub.”

Richard said his wife Kat also knew Humphrey’s wife and family very well.

He added: “Whether you loved him or hated him, what nobody can deny is that he was utterly unique.”

  The Angel And White Horse, which stands empty and is next to Sam Smith's Brewery. 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Architect Anna Craig said she knew Humphrey and his ‘lovely’ wife through the Church. He is also her landlord.

She told the Press: “He’s a very controversial figure. It’s sad to see all these houses boarded up.

“I am hoping and thinking positively that that might change and we can see some change in Tadcaster. It’s got such fantastic potential and a lovely community. It (Humphrey’s death) is sad but it might bring something positive in the longer term.”

  Anna Craig, Architect 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Charles Fox, manager at Tadcaster Pharmacy said: “Whilst Humphrey has his detractors, Humphrey was always fair in his business dealings with us. He was always very polite when he came to deal with the girls.

  Sam Smith's Brewery flies the flag at half mast. 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

He added: “It’s very rare you meet someone who polarises opinion like him. It’s sad that the characters in life are dying.”

  Georgina Platt of Melanie's Florist 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Georgina Platt of Melanie’s Florist said she did not know Humphrey personally, but he was always ‘very kind’ to the shop when it needed anything.

Looking to the future, she said “We will have to see what happens to Tadcaster.”

  The river Wharfe from the bridge which collapsed in 2015 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Now, earlier the Press had been advised to go to the charity-run Tadcaster Community Swimming Pool, where Humphrey had been a regular until recently.

Operations manager Jon Allars said: “Humphrey swam here for years though we haven’t seen him much recently. His son Sam is also a regular at the gym. The place wouldn’t exist without Humphrey and Sam Smith’s Brewery.”

  The rear of the brewery with the flaying flag 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

“I never saw Humphrey Smith in the gym but he was an avid swimmer. He was quiet but he would always say good morning. He would have his swim and then be on his way.

“He was always pleased to be here. For him, it was important Tadcaster had the facility which is why he gifted the land.”

Now, Sam comes to the gym and has taken out a membership and has had a programme of exercises made out for him.

Jon added Sam is ‘fairly quiet’ but “we see quite a lot of Sam.”
