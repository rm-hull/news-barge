## James Martin and local chefs lead packed line-up at new food event - what to expect

Top celebrity chef James Martin will be at the Flavours of Yorkshire Festival. <i>(Image: Submitted)

Ovens across North Yorkshire are set to fire up this weekend as a new food festival dishes up top chefs, local producers and family entertainment.

Flavours of Yorkshire will take over to Galtres Park, the former Pickering Showground on Saturday, July 4 and Sunday, July 5, showcasing some of the county’s best food and drink alongside live demonstrations and activities.

Top TV chef James Martin will headline the event with three cookery demonstrations on Saturday in the the only ticketed element of the festival, with prices starting from £15.

Sunday’s programme will feature free demonstrations on the main stage from a line-up of local talent including Gilly Robinson of The Cook’s Place, Oli Farrar from Forty Six, Richie Stamp-Mason from Tofoo & Co, Kathryn Bumby from The Yorkshire Pasta Company and master pâtissier, Florian Poirot .

  The Whole Hog BBQ competition will be one of the highlights of the Flavours of Yorkshire Festival. 

*(Image: Submitted)*

Organiser Mark Brayshaw, of Rockin’ Horse Promotions, known for Malton’s Food Lovers Festivals, said the event was designed to appeal to a wide audience.

“This is a festival for everybody, with free admission and free car parking, and though food is our major focus, there’s so much more to see and do,” he said.

  The Tofoo Co. will be at the event. 

*(Image: Submitted)*

“All we ask is that you arrive hungry – and we’re pretty sure you’ll leave with full bellies and a smile on your face."

Alongside food stalls and chef demonstrations, visitors can expect live entertainment, a children’s inflatable zone, birds of prey displays and a large screen showing the British Grand Prix.

  Gourmet Scotch Eggs will be featured at the Flavours of Yorkshire festival in Pickering. 

*(Image: Submitted)*

The event will also host Electric Avenue, a gathering of family-friendly electric vehicles offering a great way for anyone interested in buying an electric car or e-bike to browse the environment-friendly offerings from key brands including Kia, Vauxhall, Volvo, Toyota and Chery.

  Three family on Colourful Talio ebikes. 

*(Image: Submitted)*

Local producers will be attending, including Yorkshire Pasta, Heck!, Gourmet Scotch Eggs, Spicy Monkey and Ros Grime Shellfish. Street food vendors will offer everything from hot dogs and barbecue food to noodles and fresh strawberries, while drinks producers such as Spirit of Yorkshire Distillery, Rhucello, Orchards of Husthwaite, Twisted Roots Rum and Yorkshire Dales Brewery will also be on site.

  The Whole Hog Barbecue Competition will take place over the weekend at the Flavours of Yorkshire festival. 

*(Image: Submitted)*

The weekend will also host the Whole Hog Barbecue competition, sponsored by Cranswick, Yorkshire Baker and Weber, with amateur teams competing over open coals in a series of challenges across the weekend.

With warm, dry weather forecast, organisers say the event is set to provide a strong new addition to the county’s food calendar.

Flavours of Yorkshire runs from 10am to 6pm on Saturday and 10am to 5pm on Sunday. Entry and parking are free, with tickets for James Martin’s demonstrations available online.
