Handcrafted jewellery, fresh bakes, and summer fun are all on offer at two upcoming Yorkshire artisan markets.

Little Bird Made is bringing its popular artisan events to the grounds of Knaresborough Castle on Saturday, July 4, and Harrogate Valley Gardens on Sunday, July 5, promising a full day of shopping, food, and family-friendly fun.

The curated markets will feature a variety of independent Yorkshire businesses selling original art, handmade jewellery, organic skincare, home fragrances, preserves, cheeses, and more.

  The curated markets will feature a variety of independent Yorkshire businesses 

*(Image: Little Bird Made Artisan Markets)*

Visitors are encouraged to take their time and explore the surroundings.

Jackie Crozier, founder and managing director of Little Bird Made, said: "We are thrilled to be bringing our curated artisan markets to two of North Yorkshire’s most iconic and beautiful outdoor spaces.

"They are a celebration of community and incredible local talent.

"We want people to make a day of it, bring a picnic blanket, enjoy the historic grounds and stunning floral displays, and spend the weekend relaxing while supporting some of Yorkshire’s finest independent makers."

  Chris Molder, owner of 23rd Street 

*(Image: Little Bird Made Artisan Markets)*

Chris Molder, owner of 23rd Street, said: "We are very lucky to be able to do so many wonderful markets, but Knaresborough has always felt very special.

"We always look forward to coming back."

For more information on Little Bird Artisan Markets, visit: www.littlebirdmade.com
