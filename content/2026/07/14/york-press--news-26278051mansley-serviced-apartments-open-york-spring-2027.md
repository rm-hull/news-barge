Search the Public Notice PortalA vacant York city centre retail unit is set to become a new aparthotel.

Leeds-based Silverstone Building Consultancy has secured a £4 million contract to convert 4-6 Parliament Street, formerly home to fashion retailer New Look, into luxury serviced apartments.

The aparthotel is being developed for Mansley Serviced Apartments, which already operates sites in London, Edinburgh, Inverness and Cheltenham.

Josh Brown, associate director at Silverstone Building Consultancy, said: "We’re looking forward to bringing a vacant property in a prime location back into use for people to enjoy once again.

"There will be challenges due to the property being located in a conservation area and also in a spot that has constricted access, but we’re excited to be on-site and seeing the transformation begin."

The scheme involves redeveloping and merging three separate 19th-century buildings into a single aparthotel, with the central structure being raised to match the height of its neighbours.

A total of 23 apartments will be created across the basement, first and second floors.

  Artists impression of a living area of one of the apartments 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

Sir Richard Rowley, chairman of Mansley Group, said: "We were delighted to find such a strong location in the historic and beautiful city of York to expand our portfolio of hand-picked serviced apartment blocks.

"We are equally excited at the prospect of being able to welcome guests in early 2027.

"Silverstone is project managing a main contractor and design team with focus, transparency and fairness."

Leeds-based Artium Construction started work on the 19th Century building in April. Completion is expected in 10 months.

  The announcement comes after the Parliament Street site’s previous developer, North Star, **went into administration in October 2024** and crashed with a deficit or total loss of £889,185.

A report into the administration of North Star noted structural issues encountered in the development included extra digging underneath which was needed.

For a time, the building was left 'roofless.'
