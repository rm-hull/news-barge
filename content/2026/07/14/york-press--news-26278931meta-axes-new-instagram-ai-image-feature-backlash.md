Meta has announced that it has pulled a new Instagram feature from its services following user backlash.

Earlier this week, Meta rolled out Muse Image, allowing users to create AI-generated images using public Instagram accounts.

The service allowed users of the chatbot, Meta AI, to create fake or altered images using content on tagged public accounts.

It meant that any Instagram user with a public account could have their likeness used without their permission.

Today, we’re introducing Muse Image: our first image generation model from Meta’s Superintelligence Labs. It uses advanced reasoning to understand prompts and lets you turn your ideas into high-quality, relevant visuals that you can share directly to your chat, story, or feed.…

— Meta Newsroom (@MetaNewsroom) July 7, 2026


However, within days of its launch, Muse Image received backlash amid privacy concerns.

## Meta axes Instagram feature, Muse Image

Confirming the service has now been pulled, an update from Meta read, "Earlier this week, we announced that one way for people to generate images in Meta AI is by @-mentioning public Instagram accounts that they want to reference.

"Our intent was to provide a useful creative tool and to give people control over whether their public content could be referenced in this way.

"We’ve heard the feedback that this feature missed the mark, so it’s no longer available."

Muse Images was launched on Tuesday, July 7, but was quickly criticised by some celebrities and public figures urging the public to turn the feature off.


Actors union, SAG-AFTRA, posted a statement on Thursday, July 9, encouraging users to opt out of the service.

Sharing, "Anything other than a clear and conspicuous opt-in for these types of uses of Instagram users’ images is unacceptable, and an utter miscalculation of public sentiment regarding the obvious dangers and harms inherent in such use."

Following Meta's U-turn, SAG-AFTRA said it was the "responsible thing to do", as a union spokesperson said, "With the dangers of nonconsensual digital replicas well known to all, a feature that encouraged that behaviour is unwise.

"We appreciate its discontinuance. It is the responsible thing to do."
