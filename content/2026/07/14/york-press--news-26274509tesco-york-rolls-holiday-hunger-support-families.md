A supermarket chain is stepping up support to help tackle holiday hunger.

Tesco is launching a series of schemes to help children and families in York and across the UK, including pre-filled food donation bags, Kids Eat Free in cafes, and opportunities for customers to round up their bill in store.

The initiative aims to support families who rely on free school meals during term time and may struggle to afford food during the school holidays.

Claire De Silva, head of communities at Tesco, said: "We want to be able to support our charity partners to help those needing a little extra support this summer.

"When you’re out doing your weekly shop this summer, dropping a few tins into a food collection point, picking up a food donation bag in our stores or rounding up our grocery bill, can make a difference to the lives of thousands of children in communities across the UK."

Tesco will sell pre-filled food donation bags in all large stores in England and Wales from July 20 to August 30.

Customers will also be able to round up their bill at the checkout from August 3 to 9.

The funds will be split between The Trussell Trust and Felix.
