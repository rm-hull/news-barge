A section of masonry fell off a well-known York pub earlier today, leading to the affected area to be cordoned off with barriers and red tape.

However, Golden Slipper in Goodramgate, whose building was erected around 1500, remains open.

Landlady Lyndsay Weston told the Press: “We would like to reassure customers and local residents following the temporary closure of part of the footpath outside the pub.

“Earlier today, a section of masonry became detached from the building and fell onto the pavement. Fortunately, nobody was injured.”

“As a precaution, we immediately cordoned off the affected area with barriers and hazard tape and contacted structural engineers to carry out a full inspection.

“We have also been in contact with City of York Council and are working closely with all the relevant parties to ensure the building is made safe as quickly as possible.”

Lyndsay continued: “The safety of our customers, staff and members of the public is our highest priority, and we have taken every step to protect pedestrians while the necessary assessments and repairs are undertaken.

“The pub remains open and trading as normal, and we would like to thank everyone for their patience, understanding and continued support during this unexpected situation.

“We appreciate that seeing barriers outside a historic building can be concerning, but we want to reassure the community that we are dealing with the matter promptly and responsibly and will provide updates as work progresses.”
