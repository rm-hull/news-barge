A PRIMARY school in North Yorkshire has revealed the reason for its unscheduled closure last week.

Topcliffe C of E Academy in School Lane, Topcliffe, near Thirsk, was listed as closed by North Yorkshire Council on Friday, July 10.

It was the only school listed on the site as being closed on that day.

  


As reported by The Press, schools in York and North Yorkshire closed due to extreme temperatures in last month's heatwave where classroom temperatures were recorded as high as 37 degrees Celsius.

A spokesperson for Elevate Multi Academy Trust, which runs the primary school, said: "Topcliffe Primary School reopened on Monday, July 13 after being forced to close on Friday, 10th July due to ongoing water supply issues affecting the village.

"As there was insufficient water available at the academy to enable it to operate safely and effectively the decision was taken to close the academy for the day.

"Water repair works were carried out over the weekend and following the successful completion of these repairs and the restoration of water services, the academy was able to reopen as normal today.

"Topcliffe would like to thank children, parents, carers and staff for their patience and understanding during the disruption."
