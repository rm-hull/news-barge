Vandals have caused around £500 worth of damage at a York community trail – leaving its future in doubt.

Structures along the Foss Fairy Trail, a free trail for families along the banks of the River Foss across from Huntington Road, have been set alight and others thrown into the river.

Sergeant Ben Ambler, from North Yorkshire Police, called the damage “deeply disappointing”, as he urged anyone with information about those responsible to come forward.

  Logs from the Foss Fairy Trail have been thrown into the River Foss 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Tracy Ostle, who maintains the Foss Fairy trail in her spare time, said fires have been started along the route, as well as on the nearby cycle track and at King George’s Field, off Fossway, in recent weeks.

Wooden ‘fairy houses’ along the trail have been destroyed and large logs used to make the route thrown into the River Foss, Ms Ostle said.

  Area left burnt by a fire at the Foss Fairy Trail in York 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Ms Ostle said she and other volunteers have cleared what they can in the area, but the extent of the damage is so severe that they are unable to repair it all themselves.

She appealed for people with construction or joinery experience to help fix the structures.

And she said: “If no one is willing to help, there will be no fairy trail.

“It there’s anyone that’s got any building or joinery skills to build the fairy houses back up, that would be a help,” she added.

## More than 100 incidents of vandalism in five years at trail

The damage is the latest in a long-running spate of vandalism at the Foss Fairy Trail.

Ms Ostle said the route has endured more than 100 incidents of vandalism since it was started around five years ago.

Despite growing increasingly frustrated by the issue, Ms Ostle has continued to maintain the route, while working full-time for the Food Standards Agency.

Sgt Ambler said North Yorkshire Police is “committed to working with the Foss Fairy Trail to identify those responsible and ensure they face the consequences of their actions”.

“The damage caused at this much-loved community attraction is deeply disappointing,” he added.

  Sergeant Ben Ambler, from North Yorkshire Police 

*(Image: Dylan Connell)*

Sgt Ambler urged those with information that could help with the police investigation to come forward, saying: “Anyone with any information should please get in touch via our website, LiveChat or by calling 101.

“Alternatively, you can provide information anonymously via the independent charity Crimestoppers by calling 0800 555 111 or through their website.

“Please quote reference 12260131992 when providing any information.”
