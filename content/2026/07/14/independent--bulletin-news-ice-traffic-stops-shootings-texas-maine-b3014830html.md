# ICE ordered to halt traffic stops in major policy shift

- Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) agents have reportedly been instructed to cease performing traffic stops.
- This policy reversal follows two fatal shootings by ICE officers within a week, involving Joan Sebastian Guerrero in Maine and Lorenzo Salgado Araujo in Texas.
- The new guidance represents a significant shift from the Trump administration's broad mass deportation strategy.
- Both Guerrero and Salgado Araujo were not the intended targets of the ICE stops that led to their deaths, and witnesses have disputed official accounts.
- The incidents highlight ongoing concerns about ICE's use-of-force policies, the lack of body cameras on agents and federal guidelines prohibiting deadly force solely to stop a vehicle.
