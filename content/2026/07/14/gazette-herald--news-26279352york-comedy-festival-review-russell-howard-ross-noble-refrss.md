**Review: York Comedy Festival, Sunday, July 12, 2026, Museum Gardens - 5***

While tourists filled the nearby streets and riverside pubs and restaurants, several thousand comedy fans gathered in the Museum Gardens for a line-up that would grace any festival in the country.

York Comedy Festival is quickly becoming one of the highlights of the city's cultural calendar and, with a bill featuring the likes of Joanne McNally, Suzi Ruffell, Ross Noble, and Russell Howard, expectations were understandably high.

  York Comedy Festival 2026, Museum Gardens. 

*(Image: Mark Ratcliffe)*

Throughout the day, compère Jarred Christmas kept proceedings moving with warmth and quick-fire crowd work, the New Zealander ensuring the atmosphere never dipped between acts.

Early sets from Barry from Watford, Nabil Abdulrashid, Jeff Innocent, and Russell Kane provided an outstanding first half to the day, each of the artists performing at the top of their game.

  Suzi Ruffell at York Comedy Festival 2026 in Museum Gardens. 

*(Image: Mark Ratcliffe)*

Portsmouth-born Suzi Ruffell was up next and was in irresistible form.

Effortlessly turning tales of medical appointments, school memories, neurodiversity challenges, dating experiences, and social anxieties into huge laughs, she combined razor-sharp writing with the sort of conversational delivery that makes audiences feel they are sharing stories with a friend.

The laughter rolled around the gardens from start to finish as Ruffell brought warmth and charm alongside brilliantly observed routines about parenting and growing older, her easy connection with the audience a perfect match to the relaxed outdoor setting.

  The York Comedy Festival 2026 line-up included Joanne McNally, Suzi Ruffell, Ross Noble, and Russell Howard. 

*(Image: Mark Ratcliffe)*

Arriving on stage like a whirlwind, Irish stand-up and podcast star Joanne McNally proved exactly why she has become one of the sharpest and most in-demand voices in comedy.

Within minutes, she had transformed the event into what felt like an unfiltered therapy session as she talked about her friends in the suburbs, parenting her imaginary child Malaga McNally, and experiences of HMO living.

McNally’s comedy remains rooted in brutal honesty as she placed tales of relationships, ageing, and self-delusion under the microscope, her genius lying in an ability to expose her own flaws before anyone can.

  York Comedy Festival 2026 in Museum Gardens. 

*(Image: Mark Ratcliffe)*

The laughs arrived in relentless waves, largely because she veers so far from polite convention with observations that are so recognisable.

In the absence of subtlety, Ross Noble followed with his relentless energy and chaotic style.

He remains one of comedy's great free spirits, starting with little more than a throwaway observation before disappearing down surreal comic rabbit holes, then somehow finding his way back to a punchline.

  Ross Noble brought energy to York Comedy Festival 2026, Museum Gardens. 

*(Image: Mark Ratcliffe)*

This evening, Noble was edgy, controversial, and maverick as he reminded us that no two of his performances are ever the same.

Headliner Russell Howard brought the evening to a close with the confidence of a comedian at the very top of his game.

  Russell Howard at York Comedy Festival 2026. 

*(Image: Mark Ratcliffe)*

Balancing topical observations with stories from everyday life, he mixed heart with hilarity, leaving the Museum Gardens echoing with laughter long after his final encore-worthy punchline.

As the sun began to set behind the ancient city walls, there was a sense that York had hosted something rather special.

The Museum Gardens once again proved an inspired venue, offering one of the most picturesque settings imaginable for an evening of world-class comedy.
