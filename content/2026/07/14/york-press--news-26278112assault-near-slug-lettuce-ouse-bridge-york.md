NORTH Yorkshire Police is appealing for a potential victim of a serious assault to come forward.

The attack took place near the Slug and Lettuce bar on Ouse Bridge at around 4.30am on Sunday, July 5.

Two men have already been traced as victims, but officers believe a third man may also have been assaulted during the incident.

A spokesman for the force said: "We are appealing for a potential victim of a serious assault to come forward, along with anyone else who witnessed the disturbance and has not yet spoken to the police."

They added that it is "important to identify him to check on his welfare and offer any support or medical assistance he may need".

Two men, aged 20 and 22, were arrested on suspicion of assault and have been released under investigation while enquiries continue.

If you believe you may be the victim or have witnessed the incident, email [Alicia.Hoyle@northyorkshire.police.uk.](mailto:Alicia.Hoyle@northyorkshire.police.uk.)

You can also call 101 and pass information to the Force Control Room for the attention of DC Alicia Hoyle of York Criminal Investigation Department.

Information can also be given anonymously to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Those with information are asked to quote the reference 12260126175 when contacting police.
