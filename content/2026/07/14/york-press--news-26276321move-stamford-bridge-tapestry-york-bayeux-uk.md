### Move Stamford Bridge Tapestry to York while Bayeux is in UK

I’ve just pulled the following idea from the suggestions box.

While the Bayeux Tapestry is in temporary residence in the British Museum could the North’s modern equivalent, the wonderful Stamford Bridge Tapestry, be concurrently exhibited at a prominent York venue.

I think that at present it’s viewable to the public a few hours a week in Stamford Bridge. While the historic Bayeux masterpiece is on show in London, how about its Stamford Bridge ‘equivalent’ going on display in a ‘joint venture’ for the same period in a suitable York venue, eg the Minster, Castle Museum, Jorvik Centre or…let’s see…Bettys - lots of people gaze (or graze) in there!

  **Derek Reed, Address supplied___**

## Bring back grass

Back living in Poppleton I am shocked to see how many once attractive front gardens have been block paved or gravelled over.

What was once a pleasant village is slowly becoming utterly bleak. There’s no space for flowers, shrubs, birds, hedgehogs or other wild life. Pera Tree Avenue is particularly cursed.

Why do people spend so much money to create something so dreary? Are they just too lazy to spend a little time gardening? Surely it shows a failure to appreciate their environment? I despair of such thoughtlessness.
