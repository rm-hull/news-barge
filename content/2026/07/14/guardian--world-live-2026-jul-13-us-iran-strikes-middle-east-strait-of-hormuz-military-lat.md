Middle East crisis live: US launches new wave of strikes; UAE says two tankers in strait of Hormuz hit by Iranian missiles

US military says latest strikes will ‘continue imposing a heavy cost on Iranian forces and degrade their ability to attack innocent civilians and commercial shipping’ in strait of Hormuz

During the five-hour mission, U.S. forces successfully struck military targets across Iran including Bushehr, Chah Bahar, Jask, Konarak, Abu Musa, and Bandar Abbas to further degrade Iran’s ability to attack commercial shipping

Centcom said it targeted “Iranian coastal defense systems, missile and drone sites, and maritime capabilities”.

More than 50,000 US service members were currently deployed across the Middle East, it added.

Looking more widely at the latest US strikes, Iranian media have reported explosions in the port city of Bandar Abbas, Iran’s Kish and Qeshm islands and on Abu Musa Island in the Gulf.

Residents in the city of Jam in Iran’s Bushehr province also heard several explosions, Iran’s Fars news agency said, but the exact location of the blasts was not clear. No casualties were reported.

Iranian media reports of explosions continued for more than three hours, Reuters is saying.

Iran’s south-western Khuzestan province was hit by US projectiles early on Tuesday, Iran’s official news agency Irna reported, citing a provincial security official, adding that four people were wounded and rescue operations were under way.

A loud explosion was also heard in Iran’s southern city of Bushehr, according to Iran’s semi-official Mehr news agency.

Iran’s state TV cited the Iranian army as saying that it targeted a “hostile” US vessel with cruise missiles and US facilities and equipment in Kuwait with drones.

Iranian media also said Iran’s Revolutionary Guards shot down a US MQ-1 drone over Hormuz, while sirens sounded early on Tuesday in Bahrain – home to another US military base.

Bushehr in southern Iran has been hit in a renewed US attack, according to Iran’s Nournews.

Explosions had earlier been heard in the port city of Bushehr, according to Iranian media, after earlier reports of several blasts being heard in Bushehr province.

The province is home to Iran’s only civilian nuclear plant.

Oil prices have risen 2% to their highest in four weeks as the renewed US-Iran hostilities heighten uncertainty about energy flows through the strait of Hormuz.

Brent crude futures climbed $1.68, or 2%, to $84.98 a barrel by 0051 GMT on Tuesday, while US West Texas Intermediate crude rose $1.65, or 2.1%, to $79.79 a barrel.

Brent crude surged 9.6% in the previous session, its biggest daily gain since May 2020, Reuters reports.

Oil prices are now at their highest since the US and Iran signed their memorandum of understanding on 17 June aimed at ending their war.

Bahrain’s air defences have intercepted and destroyed Iranian aerial attacks on the country, an adviser to the king has posted on social media.

Sirens sounded earlier in the Gulf nation – continuing their activation a number of times in the past day – and people were urged to seek safe shelter.

Bahrain’s military has accused Tehran of targeting civilians in its latest attacks there, which Iran said targeted US military facilities and infrastructure.

Explosions have been heard again in southern Iran’s port city of Bushehr, according to the Mehr news agency, which reportedly said earlier that several blasts had been heard in the Jam governate of Bushehr province.

The semi-official Iranian agency was earlier quoted as saying US projectiles had hit several areas in Omidiyeh in Khuzestan province to the north.

The US has been carrying out a third day of strikes that Donald Trump says is targeting Iran’s attack capabilities over the strait of Hormuz as both sides assert they control the vital energy route.

Alarm sirens have sounded in Bahrain and people should head to the nearest safe location, the interior ministry is saying on social media.

The country earlier accused Iran of targeting civilians with its latest attacks on Bahrain, after Tehran said it had struck US military facilities and infrastructure there.

Donald Trump’s announcement that the US will start charging ships for safe passage through the strait of Hormuz comes after his administration has until now insisted the waterway should remain open to all without tolls – as it was before the US and Israel attacked Iran in late February.

Any attempt by Washington or Tehran to charge fees would violate global norms on freedom of navigation and raise tensions, likely causing further economic disruption far beyond the region

The price of Brent crude oil, the international standard, rose 7.8% to $81.92 a barrel on Monday, although it is still well below the nearly $120 reached at the height of the war.

UAE says Iran hit two tankers in Hormuz strait with missiles

The United Arab Emirates is saying Iranian cruise missiles have targeted two UAE tankers in the strait of Hormuz and that one crew member has been killed and eight injured.

The defence ministry said the attack in Omani territorial waters caused damage and fires onboard both ships but they had been brought under control.

The Ministry of Defense condemns this brazen attack, which constitutes a serious violation and a clear breach of international law, threatening the security and stability of the region.

The ministry named the ships as the Mombasa and the Bahia and said the attack occurred in the southern passage of the strait. The crew member killed on the Mombasa was Indian, while of the eight injured six were Indian and two Ukrainian, it said. Four of those were seriously injured.
