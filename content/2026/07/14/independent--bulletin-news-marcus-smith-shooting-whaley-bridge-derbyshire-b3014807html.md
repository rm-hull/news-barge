# Man cleared over fatal shooting of burglar at his home

![The man, in his 50s, was arrested on suspicion of murder but will now face no further action](https://static.independent.co.uk/2022/08/11/00/10225841-deb41f7c-1a64-48c1-bc3f-ac26d6f4e2b0.jpg)

- A homeowner in Derbyshire will not face criminal charges after shooting dead an intruder, Marcus Smith, 19, who was pronounced dead on May 1, 2024, at a remote property in Whaley Bridge.
- The man, in his 50s, was arrested on suspicion of murder after discharging a legally-held firearm when confronted by burglars in his home.
- The Crown Prosecution Service (CPS) decided against bringing charges, citing the householder's rights to defend themselves, following a police investigation and review of evidence.
- The homeowner has been released from police bail, with no further action to be taken against him in relation to Mr Smith's death or serious injuries to another person.
- Separately, three men are set to face proceedings for burglary in connection with the incident and a prior burglary at the same property.
