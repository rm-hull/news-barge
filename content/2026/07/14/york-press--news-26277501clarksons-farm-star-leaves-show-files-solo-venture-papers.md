A popular Clarkson's Farm star has left the show and filed papers ahead of a new solo venture.

Harriet Cowan from Derbyshire was a new addition to the cast in the latest season, which was released on Prime Video across May and June last year.

The 25-year-old temporarily stood in for long-time fan favourite Kaleb Cooper as he toured, helping Mr Clarkson at the farm in Oxfordshire.

She left midway through the series once her help was no longer needed, but became popular with viewers despite her short time on screen.

Ms Cowan has now filed papers for a new venture away from Clarkson's Farm, according to a new article in The Sun.

The report said: "Last month, she filed a trademark application at the Intellectual Property Office for clothing and retail services connected with the sale of clothing and accessories.

"The application was made by her company, Farmfluence, on June 26, which she set up in July 2025.

"This success includes her having secured several ad deals since her TV debut."

It is not yet known if Ms Cowan will return to Clarkson's Farm, but she addressed the topic on BBC Breakfast News last year.

"We've always left it very open," she said.

"I'm here if he needs me, but no plans at the minute. If he needs me, I'm here."

Ms Cowan did not appear in series five of the farming programme, which was released across June of this year on Prime Video.

Clarkson's Farm is expected to return to screens once more in 2027, with filming currently ongoing for series six.
