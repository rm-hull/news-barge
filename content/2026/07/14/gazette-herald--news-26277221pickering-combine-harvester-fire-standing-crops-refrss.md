FIREFIGHTERS from North Yorkshire have been called out to fires in fields yesterday evening and overnight (Monday, July 13).

A combine harvester caught fire in a field between Kirkbymoorside and Pickering, prompting a response from crews stationed in Pickering and Helmsley around 9pm.

  


A North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue spokesperson said crews in breathing apparatus put the blaze out which was contained to the harvester only.

Spreaders were used to access the engine compartment and grain storage to dampen down remaining hotspots.

Earlier in the evening, a 400 square metre field of standing crops was on fire in Long Marston.

The service spokesperson said two crews from Acomb, plus firefighters stationed in York, and Tadcaster tackled the blaze after being called around 5.45pm.

A water bowser from Tadcaster was also deployed to support the operation.

The spokesperson said: "Crews used hose reel jets and beaters to extinguish the fire which was believed to had started accidentally."
