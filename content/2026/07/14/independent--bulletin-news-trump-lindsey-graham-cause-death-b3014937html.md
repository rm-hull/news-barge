# Trump appears to contradict preliminary findings on Lindsey Graham’s death

- President Donald Trump contradicted preliminary medical findings regarding the death of Senator Lindsey Graham, stating his death was due to "a totally different thing" unrelated to blocked arteries.
- Graham's office initially reported his death from a "brief and sudden illness," while the Washington, D.C. Medical Examiner's preliminary report attributed it to an aortic dissection linked to hardened arteries from high cholesterol.
- Trump claimed White House doctors explained Graham's death was from an "almost undetectable" condition, similar to what killed Graham's father, and not due to arterial blockage.
- Senator Graham, who had served in Congress since 1994, died on Sunday morning, days after returning from a visit to Ukraine.
- Trump dismissed conspiracy theories circulating among his supporters about foul play and stated he believes the FBI is wasting its time investigating the senator's demise.
