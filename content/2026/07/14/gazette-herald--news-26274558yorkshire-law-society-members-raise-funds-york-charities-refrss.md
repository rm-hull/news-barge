A charity walk has raised vital funds for organisations supporting people in need.

More than 50 people joined the Yorkshire Law Society Charity Walk on July 8, in York, with temperatures reaching more than 28°C.

The walk was organised to raise funds for York Citizens Advice, North Yorkshire Citizens Advice and Law Centre, and York Music Hub.

Duncan Morter, president of the Yorkshire Law Society, said: "The heat certainly made it more challenging than we anticipated, but the turnout was fantastic.

"Around 50 people came together from across the legal profession and advice sector to support three charities which provide invaluable services within our communities.

"Citizens Advice and the Law Centre help people facing a wide range of challenges at some of the most difficult times in their lives, while York Music Hub helps create opportunities for children and young people through music.

"We are delighted to be able to support all three causes."
