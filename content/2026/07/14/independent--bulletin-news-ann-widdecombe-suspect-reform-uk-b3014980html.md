# Police investigating whether Ann Widdecombe suspect had plans to target other Reform politicians

- As part of their probe into the death of Ann Widdecombe, police are investigating whether the suspect had plans to target any other Reform UK politicians.
- Counter Terrorism Policing head Laurence Taylor said the question would "form a line of investigation" and that police would work to ensure all politicians are properly protected.
- It comes after *The Times* newspaper reported that detectives discovered material suggesting that political ideology could be a factor in the motive.
- Mr Taylor also said the attack on Ms Widdecombe last week was "clearly targeted".
- A man, 28, was arrested in Rotherham on Saturday on suspicion of murder. He has since been re-arrested on suspicion of commission, preparation or instigation of acts of terrorism.
