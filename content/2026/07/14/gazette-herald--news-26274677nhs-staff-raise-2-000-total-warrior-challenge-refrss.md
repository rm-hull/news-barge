Six NHS staff took on the Total Warrior Great Northern Mud Run.

They raised more than £2,000 for York and Scarborough Hospitals Charity, supporting the work of York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust.

Five participants, who formed Team Haematology, took on the gruelling obstacle course to raise funds for the Haematology Department.

The money will go toward new equipment and patient room improvements to enhance the care experience.

  Danii Loft taking on the slide obstacle 

*(Image: Y&S Hospitals Charity)*

Natalie Marriott, events fundraiser for York and Scarborough Hospitals Charity, said: "We're incredibly proud of all six participants for taking on the Total Warrior Great Northern Mud Run this year.

"Completing such a physically demanding challenge is a fantastic achievement, and to raise more than £2,000 alongside it is incredible."

The Total Warrior mud run is known for its demanding obstacles, including mud, water, and challenging obstacles.

Funds raised will support enhancements to patient care and hospital facilities that go beyond standard NHS funding.
