A small town in North Yorkshire has previously been recommended among the best in the UK for foodies.

Malton, known for its monthly food market and independent roasters, bakeries, and butchers at Talbot Yard, was highlighted by food, travel, and restaurant publication Olive magazine in July last year.

It was also named among the likes of Alnwick in Northumberland and Lyme Regis in Dorset.

Commenting on its recent food and travel guide, the publisher said: “From Welsh market towns with creative culinary gatherings to Yorkshire boroughs that are home to artisan roasters and gin distilleries, these are 10 of the best foodie towns across the UK for a staycation.”

## Why is Malton one of the UK’s best small towns for foodies?

The publisher shared: “There’s so much going on in Yorkshire’s food capital of Malton right now. Those serious about their food won’t be disappointed.”

It continued: “Over a four year period, 26 food and drink businesses have set up shop here.

“Many cluster around Talbot Yard – a former stables – which now houses an artisan roaster, Roost, the Bluebird Bakery, the Groovy Moo gelateria, macaron-maker Florian Poirot, the Rare Bird Gin Distillery and a butcher, Food 2 Remember.

“Other dates for the diary include the Malton Harvest Food Festival in October, and the monthly food markets.


“These events bring together the wider area’s exceptional producers including Malton Cider, cheese from Botton Creamery, chutneys from Sloemotion and lots more.”

Olive magazine recommends staying at the 26-bedroomed Talbot Inn, owned by aristocrat Tom Naylor-Leyland and his wife Alice, a Vogue contributor.

The inn is said to have “relaxed décor, in a glamorous, playful sort of way.”

It continues: “Eating is when and where you want. Already savoured the views of the watermeadows from the plant-draped Garden Room? Curl up by the log fires in the Snug, or slouch into a leather armchair beneath the wall-mounted wild boar in the navy-walled Upper Bar.”

Over on Tripadvisor, guests have given the Talbot Inn a 4.4/5 rating from more than 2,000 reviews – 1,480 of those are rated as “excellent”.
