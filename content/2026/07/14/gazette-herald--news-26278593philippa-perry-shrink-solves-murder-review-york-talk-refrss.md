  *Psychotherapist Philippa Perry's new book, Shrink Solves Murder, brings “joy, wit and wisdom”, writes Jayne Venables, ahead of the author’s talk at York St John University on Wednesday evening (July 15).*

Shrink Solves Murder is the no-nonsense, unsurprisingly unpretentious title of Philippa Perry's new adventure into crime writing. Unsurprisingly unpretentious because the author's professional style generally, flows with a genuine openness, exuding a transparency which underpins her success as psychotherapist-cum-agony aunt. She's real and that gives those in agony permission and confidence to look realistically at themselves. And it's the same for those of us who look on.

Philippa brings her commitment to sharing psychological insights to this tantalising murder mystery. If you enjoyed Cracker, Patience and Jonathan Creek, you'll find joy, wit and wisdom here.

It's early days but her protagonist, Patricia Phillips, along with Pritchard, her endearing friend and assistant (with lashings of Grayson's charm, eccentricity and humour - not to mention the booming laugh), are surely destined for more adventures.

This one though takes place near Beachy Head and presents as a suicide. I found myself identifying with Pat who, convinced the victim had been suicided, set out to winkle evidence from a case that was closed by the powers that be. Years ago I did the same, researching the death of WMD inspector Dr David Kelly. Pat took a similar common sense approach but with added psychological nous and the advantageous status which goes with her job.

Still, an isolating experience in the colourful community Philippa creates of village 'types'. It'll be interesting watching their development in later episodes as they seem to me to be destined for TV with Pat and Pritchard, who both achieve more depth as the plot thickens.

Despite the light and flighty tone which prevails and almost beckons similar musical accompaniment, there are moments of real pathos. Pat's encounter with the innocent and charming victim's mum was searingly authentic and carried all the recognisable depth of Philippa's compassion.

To describe this mystery as a jolly romp would not do justice to such moments but it does have a comedic air and at times a theatrical absurdity, ultimately with arresting Daliesque flashes.

The genre is familiar but Philippa brings an impish originality even managing to promote her best-selling non-fiction books through her protagonist. All very amusing and acceptable to those of us who enjoy her on Substack and rejoice in her creative courage.

  *Philippa Perry for Shrink Solves Murder takes place at the Creative Centre, York St John, at 7pm on Wednesday.
