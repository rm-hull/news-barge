Police in York are asking a potential victim of a serious assault to come forward after two men were arrested.

Were you in the area of the Slug and Lettuce, on Ouse Bridge, in the early hours of Sunday 5 July? Police want to hear from someone they describe as a “potential victim” of a serious assault along with anyone else who witnessed the disturbance and has not yet spoken to them yet.

It happened at around 4.30am on Sunday 5 July when a fight broke out and at least two men were assaulted.

These two victims have been traced, however, further enquiries into the incident have revealed a third, potential victim who detectives need to speak to.

A spokesperson for the Police said: “It is important that we trace him to check his welfare and offer any support or medical assistance he may require.”

Two men aged 20 and 22 were arrested on suspicion of assault. They have been released while under investigation to allow further enquiries to be carried out.

If you believe you are the victim or are a witness to the incident, please email [email protected]

Or call 101 and pass information to the Force Control Room for the attention of DC Alicia Hoyle of York CID.

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or online via their website.

Please quote reference 12260126175 when passing on information.
