## Man behind Great British Pub Crawl names his top 12 pubs in York

Dale Harvey outside The Swan in Clementhorpe *(Image: Dale Harvey)*

A leading pub campaigner and blogger has revealed his top 12 pubs in York, following a visit to the city last week.

Dale Harvey has spent more than four years on his ‘Great British Pub Crawl’, which he describes as a “one man quest to visit all of the UKs amazing pubs and bars, whilst finding the best beers and the best eats along the way".

Dale, from Nottingham, was in Edinburgh yesterday (Monday), adding he had just visited his 10,000th pub in his mammoth 1,521 day pub crawl.

The pub crawl began as a hobby but it evolved into a job, fuelled by millions of views on social media.

He told The Press: “This is working. This is my full-time job. I’m a pub campaigner.”

  The Slip Inn is also makes the Top 12 from Dale Harvey 

*(Image: Dale Harvey)*

“I worked hard putting my social media out, getting it grow to more than 500,000 followers.”

The Great British Pub Craw Facebook Page has some 238,000 followers and Dale has 33,500 subscribers on YouTube.

Last Tuesday, Dale was in York and he recalls the best two pubs being The Blue Bell in Fossgate, and the Valhalla at Patrick Pool.

Though he also admits, visiting so many pubs make it a bit of a blur.

He continued: “They are both phenomenal pubs, at the opposite sides of the scale. One that’s been there forever, the other is unique. You don’t find many Viking-themed bars in the world.”

  Dale Harvey outside the community-owned Golden Ball. 

*(Image: Dale Harvey)*

Whilst Dale last week published a Top 12 of York on Facebook, he also highlighted a 13th- the Volunteer Arms in Watson Street, Holgate.

He said: “What’s not to like? It’s proper old school, it’s nice, darkly lit, which suits it perfectly. This is totally my kind of thing.”

Dale praised the range of beers, describing the place as “rustic, friendly, nice".

“It’s wicked, definitely right up my street.”

Announcing his list of a dozen top York Pubs, Dale said: “Trust me, there are some beauties in here.”

Dale describes The Slip Inn in Clementhorpe as “one of York’s biggest gems".

  Dale Harvey outside Ye Olde Starre Inn 

*(Image: Dale Harvey)*

He continued: “The Slip Inn is an incredible back street pub outside of the city walls that brings its beer line up to the table.”

The original House of Trembling Madness in Stonegate is “absolutely incredible” and a “truly wonderful little bar".

Ye Olde Starre Inn in Stonegate, York’s oldest licensed inn, which dates back to 1644, is “a little beauty” with “an old time feel".

The Valhalla is further described as an “absolutely incredible Viking themed bar".

Dale also said: “If you haven’t been to Valhalla York, you haven’t lived.”

The Golden Fleece, which is known for its ghost stories, “has a spooky feel as soon as you walk in".

Again, the Blue Bell is “amazing, an absolute stunner, wonderful".

The 15th Century Black Swan in Peaseholme Green was “beautiful".

Dale said the Market Cat in Jubbergate “always has a top line up of beers” and was “a well run pub".

The Three Tuns in Coppergate is a “stunning, historic pub".

The Blue Boar in Castlegate is said to be where Dick Turpin’s body was laid to rest a while and the landlord charged people to see it. Today, there remains a coffin.

Dale said: “How many pubs can boast an actual coffin?”

The Swan in Bishopgate is “just a lovely old pub, old school".

And the community-owned The Golden Ball is “legendary, always a great place to visit".

In his four year quest, Dale has visited pubs in other Yorkshire towns and cities including Leeds, Harrogate, Whitby, Scarborough and Hull, with plans to visit Thirsk.

This week, Dale will be returning from Scotland to his Nottingham home, before continuing his quest, to help save the British pub.

He said: “The government needs to do more to help. We need to do more to stop the rising costs facing pubs.

“I’m a national director of CAMRA (Campaign for Real Ale). Last week, CAMRA released the ‘Beer in the UK report on what is going on.”

The report noted the industry is dominated by four main brewers, with favourite ‘imported’ and ‘continental’ brews, actually made in the UK.

He added: “We need to do more to stop brewers implying beers are brewed in Spain, when they are not.”
