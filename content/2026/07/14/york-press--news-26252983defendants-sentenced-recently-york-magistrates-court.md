Recent cases at York Magistrates Court included a drink driver and a violent woman.

Glynn Banks, 73, of Stillingfleet, pleaded guilty to being in charge of a vehicle at Deighton when two and a half times the drink drive level and driving a vehicle at Deighton when nearly , three and a half times the drink drive limit.

He was banned from driving for four years and four months, fined £480 and ordered to pay £85 prosecution costs.

Alicia Harrop, 21, of The Poplars, Brayton, pleaded guilty to causing grievous bodily harm to a woman on Market Place, Selby.

She was given an 18-month community order with 20 days’ rehabilitative activities and ordered to wear an alcohol abstinence tag for 80 days. She must pay £500 compensation.

Jacob Kendall, 29, of Chestnut Garth, Hemingbrough, pleaded guilty to harassing a woman. He was given a 12-month community order with 20 days’ rehabilitative activities and 90 hours’ unpaid work and ordered to pay £400 prosecution costs and a £114 statutory surcharge.

He was given an 18-month retraining order banning him from contracting the woman, going to her home street or posting anything about her on social media.

Glenn Deering, 48, of no fixed address, pleaded guilty to using threatening words or behaviour in Scarborough and was jailed for two weeks.
