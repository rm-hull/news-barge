DANGEROUS and counterfeit goods - including recalled children's toys that pose a serious chemical risk - have been seized at a coastal car boot sale.

East Riding of Yorkshire Council say its trading standards team confiscated the items in Bridlington during a visit to the Strawberry Fields car boot sale on July 2.

Among the items seized were Needoh products, Labubu Dolls, and Squeezy Dumplings - some of which had already been recalled nationally due to a "serious chemical risk."

The recalled products contained substances which could pose a danger to children and should not be sold or supplied to consumers.

The team also confiscated imitation high-end perfumes and counterfeit Apple-branded goods from a separate stall.

Councillor Lyn Healing, cabinet member for communities and public protection at East Riding of Yorkshire Council, said: "Consumers have a right to expect that the products they buy are safe and genuine.

"Counterfeit and unsafe goods can present significant risks, particularly when they are aimed at children."

Trading Standards officers plan to carry out further inspections at markets, car boot sales, and other retail locations across the East Riding to protect consumers and remove unsafe products from circulation.

Shoppers are urged to remain vigilant when buying from informal outlets, particularly if goods are very cheap, poorly packaged, or missing safety information.

Councillor Healing added: "We will continue to work proactively to identify and remove these products from sale and take appropriate action against those responsible."

Anyone with information about the sale of counterfeit or unsafe products is encouraged to report it to Trading Standards through the Citizens Advice consumer service on 0808 223 1133.
