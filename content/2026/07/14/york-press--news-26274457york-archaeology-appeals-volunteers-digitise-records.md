A new archaeology project in York is on the hunt for new volunteers.

The Fishergate: A District Unearthed project, led by York Archaeology and the Friends of York Archaeological Trust, aims to digitise records from a 1989 excavation at 46-54 Fishergate.

This site once housed the Redfearn National Glassworks and is part of a wider effort to document and share the area's untold history.

Paul Flintoft, regional manager for York Archaeology, said: "Fishergate is a really interesting space – we know there was Roman and Anglian occupation as well as the church and Gilbertine Priory of St Andrew at the site – and we’ve had the opportunity to dig in three neighbouring locations on Fishergate between 1989 and 2023."

The project involves tracing and digitising detailed drawings from the dig to create digital plans for comprehensive site mapping.

Mr Flintoft said: "Digitising these site plans is a big job, but once done, we can not only create a detailed overall of the site itself, but also link photographs of the finds to the place where they were discovered."

Volunteers will work at York Archaeology’s Aldwark office, with remote options available for writing blog content or taking photographs.

Anyone interested can visit [https://research.york.archaeology.co.uk/get-involved/](https://research.york.archaeology.co.uk/get-involved/) or email [fishergate.unearthed@yorkat.co.uk.](mailto:fishergate.unearthed@yorkat.co.uk.)
