- A huge fire has broken out at Malton Hospital sending plumes of smoke over the town.
- Firefighters have been on the scene working to contain the blaze since around 11.30am this morning (July 8).
- The fire is at the hospital's Springwood unit, which provides mental health services for older people, according to the area's ward councillor.
- The York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust, which runs Malton Hospital, says there are no reported injuries with patients and staff safely evacuated from the hospital.
- Anyone with an appointment at Malton Hospital is asked not to attend it, the trust adds.
- Malton councillor Lindsay Burr says she has been informed that "all patients and staff have been evacuated safely" and taken to the nearby Malton School.
- Thirsk and Malton MP Kevin Hollinrake says he has been told that all people are accounted for after the fire.
- Residents have been asked to keep their windows and doors shut due to heavy smoke in the area.
- People have also been urged to avoid the hospital site, with a police cordon in place in Maiden Greve.
