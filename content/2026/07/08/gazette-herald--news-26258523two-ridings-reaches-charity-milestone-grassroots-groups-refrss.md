A community foundation has reached a major funding milestone that marks years of grassroots support.

Two Ridings Community Foundation has distributed £25 million in grants to community groups and grassroots organisations across York and North Yorkshire, Hull and East Yorkshire.

Celia McKeon, chief executive officer of the foundation, said: "Reaching £25 million is an incredible achievement, but what matters most is what that funding has made possible.

"Behind every grant are people who have found support, confidence, connection and opportunity through local community organisations.

"For 25 years, generous donors have helped communities respond to challenges and create lasting change.

"Whether it's supporting families through difficult times, tackling loneliness or creating opportunities for young people, local giving has transformed lives."

Founded in 2000 by former chief constable David Kenworthy, Two Ridings Community Foundation champions the power of giving locally.

Since expanding into Hull and East Yorkshire in 2010, the foundation has distributed hundreds of grants each year to projects in towns, villages, coastal areas and city neighbourhoods.

The £25 million milestone has been reached during the organisation’s 25th anniversary year, following the launch of its Anniversary Appeal.

  L-R: Sarah Wells and Rachel Fellows from Bettys and Taylors Group 

*(Image: Two Ridings Community Foundation)*

In the most recent financial year alone, Two Ridings awarded £1.7 million to organisations working across the region.

Ms McKeon said the foundation is now looking to the future.

She said: "As we look to the future, our ambition is clear: to inspire even more giving so that communities across North and East Yorkshire can flourish today and for generations to come."

Businesses such as Bettys & Taylors Group partner with the foundation to deliver impact in the communities they serve.

They work with Two Ridings to distribute annual grants through its community fund, as well as a family fund that supports young people seeking opportunities in the food and hospitality sector.

Sarah Wells, community manager at Bettys & Taylors Group, said: "As a Yorkshire-based business, we recognise that thriving communities create a stronger foundation for long-term success, which is why we invest in partnerships that support the strength and resilience of local communities.

"Giving through Two Ridings allows us to make a difference in the places we care about most.

"We know that local organisations understand their communities better than anyone, and that even relatively small amounts of funding can have a lasting impact."

The foundation will conclude its anniversary celebrations in July 2026 with the planting of a Community Time Capsule in Alderman Kneeshaw Park in Hull, to be opened in 2050.
