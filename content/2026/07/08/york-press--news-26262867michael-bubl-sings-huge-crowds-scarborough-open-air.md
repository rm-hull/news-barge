## PICTURES - Five-time GRAMMY award-winner performs in Scarborough

Michael Bublé performed to a sold-out Scarborough Open Air Theatre on Monday (July 6) *(Image: Cuffe and Taylor)*

Michael Bublé has delighted fans with his sold-out performance at Scarborough's Open Air Theatre.

The five-time GRAMMY award-winning singer opened with a cover of Nina Simone’s Feeling Good, following with the iconic Haven't Met You, L-O-V-E and Such A Night on Monday (July 6).

He told fans: "Scarborough, we are not here for a show – we are here for a celebration."

  'Scarborough, we are not here for a show - we are here for a celebration' 

*(Image: Scarborough Open Air Theatre)*

The Canadian singer also joined in the World Cup spirit, asking the crowd: "I just want to know… is it coming home?"

  


The comment led to an 8,000-strong singalong of Oasis’ Wonderwall - a hit which has been adopted by Three Lions fans around the world.

  Adoring fans packed the stadium 

*(Image: Scarborough Open Air Theatre)*

Michael's set included classic covers of Cry Me a River and Fly Me To The Moon, before he closed the 90-minute show with Elvis Presly's Always on My Mind.

Supporting pop-duo Appleton, sisters Nicole and Natalie Appleton, treated the audience to a throw-back of All Saints’ songs including Pure Shores, Never Ever, and Black Coffee.

  The show featured a singalong of Oasis’ Wonderwall after Michael asked: 'I just want to know...is it coming home?' 

*(Image: Scarborough Open Air Theatre)*
