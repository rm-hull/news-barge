A drunk off-duty North Yorkshire Police sergeant who assaulted a member of the public, shoved a colleague and hurled abuse at officers during a Christmas party has been sacked.

North Yorkshire sergeant Mathew Baldwin was dismissed without notice after a misconduct panel found he behaved in a “drunken, threatening and abusive” way during the off-duty bash at the Black Sheep Brewery in Masham.

The hearing heard Baldwin had been drinking heavily when an alleged sexual assault, where a man was accused of touching a female colleague's skirt, happened.

The report said Baldwin aggressively confronted the suspect, calling him a "pervert" while holding a glass before telling him "you're getting locked up".

When a colleague, police officer McVay, tried to intervene, Baldwin said: “Get the f*** off me.”

The hearing then found Baldwin later grabbed the suspect by the collar and pulled him backwards, causing him to fall to the floor.

As officers tried to deal with the incident, Baldwin repeatedly attempted to take charge despite being off duty and intoxicated.

The report said he shouted at fellow officer Ferguson that he was giving her a "lawful order" to separate the suspect from the woman he was with.

He then ignored colleagues who tried to stop him following the suspect upstairs.

Once upstairs, the panel found Baldwin refused repeated requests from PC Brogden to leave before telling the officer he was putting himself on duty and issuing what he claimed was a "lawful order" to arrest the suspect.

After the suspect had been arrested and taken to a police van, Baldwin again attempted to direct the investigation.

The report found he physically grabbed the woman the man was with and pushed her towards a hedge.

The panel concluded: "The panel found that this was an assault. Baldwin had no reason to manhandle [the woman] in the manner he did, or at all.

"The force used was unreasonable and not necessary in the circumstances."

When PC Brogden intervened, Baldwin again declared: "I'm giving you a lawful order, just go."

Moments later, the panel found Baldwin pushed police officer Brogden towards the police vehicle.

According to the report, police officer Brogden warned him: "You push me one more time."

The panel ruled that Baldwin's actions amounted to an assault on a serving police officer.

It concluded: "The force used was unreasonable. There was no justification for PS Baldwin to push the officer in the manner he did, or at all."

Body-worn video showed Baldwin "slurring his words" and making "numerous comments that were illogical and repeated".

It also stated he was using "clear and repeated profanity" and displaying behaviour "consistent with a significant level of intoxication."

The panel rejected his version of events, ruling his actions were criminal, caused psychological harm and risked damaging public trust in the police.

While Baldwin claimed he was trying to ensure an arrest and pointed to stress and mental health struggles, the panel said that was no excuse.

“Good intention does not reduce culpability,” the report stated.

The panel concluded the misconduct was so serious that dismissal was the only appropriate sanction.

It said: "The only appropriate outcome, given the high level of seriousness in this case, is dismissal without notice."

Baldwin will also be placed on the College of Policing barred list.
