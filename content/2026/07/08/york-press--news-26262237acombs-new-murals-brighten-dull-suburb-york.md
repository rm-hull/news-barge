**Acomb's new murals brighten up 'dull suburb' of York - but what happens when they weather?**

IN a cautious way, I welcome the murals that have lately adorned walls in Acomb.

The council has shown courage in commissioning art work so uncommon in this country.

We have no tradition in England of murals on outside walls; though my wife, who was recently in Romania, tells me that exterior murals are quite common there.

Acomb is, perhaps a dull suburb; and I say that as one who lives there.

But the murals do liven the place up, which is a good thing.

I prefer the naturalistic ones that show birds, people and trees.

  *Recommended reading:*

A depiction of a crow in the end wall of Boyes impressed me.

It is so lifelike that I thought it to be a low relief, carved into the wall.

I had to get up close to establish that it is only a two dimensional painting, though lifelike.

These murals will start to weather in time, and grow tacky.

What will be done with the walls then?

  *David Martin,*

  *Rosedale Avenue,*

  *Acomb,*

  *York*

  **American celebrations**

The USA put on quite a show for the July 4th celebrations this weekend, but I wonder if those same people realise just what a calamity the insurrection, for that's what it was, for both sides.

Centuries from now a wise Chinese historian will likely jot out an epic work on the decline and fall of the English speaking people, and in it the date 04/07/1776, or as the Yanks insanely write it, 07/04/1776, will be cited as perhaps the most damaging event of them all.

It saw a common people divided forever, a devolution of power that always leads to decline and irrelevance.

Celebrating July 4th is like the Germans having a party to celebrate the Berlin wall going up rather than being torn down.

I wonder if the other side of the Atlantic will ever see it for the disaster in our common history that is was.

  *Dr Scott Marmion,*

  *Woodhouse,*

  *York*
