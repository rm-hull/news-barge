Emergency services are on the scene at a fire in a North Yorkshire forest.

Several fire crews are on the scene in Wykeham Forest, Sawdon, near Scarborough, on Wednesday morning (July 8).

North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service has urged members of the public to “please avoid the area at this time”.

The fire service said its crews, including a water bowser unit, are in attendance.

Forestry England said the firefighters are working to contain the fire and stop it from spreading further.

"Forestry England are working closely alongside fire service crews, providing localised advice about the site and creating fire breaks to contain the fire and reduce the risk of it spreading further in these prolonged hot and dry conditions," a Forestry England spokesperson said.

“As recommended by our colleagues at the fire service, we ask the public to avoid the area at this time. If you discover a wildfire while outdoors, get to a safe place and call 999.”

Wykeham Forest, around six miles west of Scarborough and within the south east corner of the North York Moors National park, contains 1114.6 hectares of woodland.

The forest is used as a centre for research and, according to Forestry England, is one of 12 forest-scale trials in England, Scotland and Wales.
