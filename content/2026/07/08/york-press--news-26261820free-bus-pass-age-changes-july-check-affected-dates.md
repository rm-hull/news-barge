Thousands of people approaching retirement age are facing a longer wait for their free bus pass after a major rule change linked to the rising State Pension age.

The change affects people in England whose eligibility for an older person's bus pass is tied directly to the State Pension age.

As the Government begins the next phase of increasing the State Pension age from 66 to 67, some people turning 66 this year will no longer automatically qualify for a free bus pass on their birthday.

Instead, they may have to wait several extra months before becoming eligible.


## Who is affected this month?

The latest phase of the increase affects people born between:

- 6 July 1960 and 5 August 1960

Anyone in this group will need to wait until they reach 66 years and four months old before they can claim their State Pension and qualify for a free bus pass in England.

For example:

- Someone born on 6 July 1960 will become eligible on 6 November 2026
- Someone born on 5 August 1960 will become eligible on 5 December 2026

## What the Government says

GOV.UK states: "There are 2 broad categories of people eligible for a statutory concession – older people and eligible disabled people.

"Both men and women are eligible for concessionary travel in England when they reach state pension age."

There is an online tool allowing people to check exactly when they become eligible.

## Why the rules are changing

The increase stems from legislation that raises the State Pension age from 66 to 67 between 2026 and 2028.

The Department for Work and Pensions explains: "The Pensions Act 2014 brought the increase in the State Pension age from 66 to 67 forward by eight years."

The department added: "The State Pension age for men and women will now increase to 67 between 2026 and 2028."

## Full list of free bus pass eligibility ages

People born within the following dates will become eligible for a State Pension and free bus pass at these ages:

- 6 April 1960 – 5 May 1960: 66 years and 1 month
- 6 May 1960 – 5 June 1960: 66 years and 2 months
- 6 June 1960 – 5 July 1960: 66 years and 3 months
- 6 July 1960 – 5 August 1960: 66 years and 4 months
- 6 August 1960 – 5 September 1960: 66 years and 5 months
- 6 September 1960 – 5 October 1960: 66 years and 6 months
- 6 October 1960 – 5 November 1960: 66 years and 7 months
- 6 November 1960 – 5 December 1960: 66 years and 8 months
- 6 December 1960 – 5 January 1961: 66 years and 9 months
- 6 January 1961 – 5 February 1961: 66 years and 10 months
- 6 February 1961 – 5 March 1961: 66 years and 11 months
- 6 March 1961 – 5 April 1977: 67 years

## The exceptions to the rule

The changes do not apply everywhere, even in England.

People living in London and Merseyside can still access free travel through local concessionary schemes from age 60, depending on eligibility rules, meaning they are not affected in the same way as residents elsewhere in England.

For others, the age at which a free bus pass becomes available is now gradually increasing alongside the State Pension age, meaning thousands will have to wait longer before they can start travelling for free.
