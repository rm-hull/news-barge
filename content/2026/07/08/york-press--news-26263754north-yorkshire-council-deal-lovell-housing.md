Hundreds of new affordable homes could be built under a proposed joint venture for North Yorkshire.

Councillors in North Yorkshire are set to consider plans for the scheme, which aims to accelerate housing delivery across the county, at a meeting next Tuesday (July 14).

The venture, between North Yorkshire Council and nationally renowned housing developer Lovell Partnerships Ltd, would focus on building high-quality, environmentally sustainable homes, with a particular emphasis on affordable housing in coastal areas.

Councillor Simon Myers, executive member for housing at North Yorkshire Council, said: "This proposed partnership gives us a real opportunity to have greater control and help us better shape how new homes are delivered across the county.

"By working with a trusted partner and using land we already own, we can accelerate the delivery of high-quality housing, including much-needed affordable homes, so that more families have the opportunity to live in the communities they want to.

"Rather than simply selling land to private developers, this approach would also allow us to retain influence over what is built and when – helping us to deliver new homes and the supporting infrastructure they need, while promoting sustainable communities."

North Yorkshire Council has been set a Government target of delivering more than 4,000 new homes each year.

The joint venture would support this goal and help the council achieve its ambition to increase its housing stock by at least 500 new homes.

Under the plan, the council and Lovell would act as equal partners, sharing both risk and reward.

The scheme would unlock the potential to deliver new homes, infrastructure, and green spaces at pace.

Two sites already allocated for development in Scarborough and Whitby could deliver more than 600 properties, including 226 affordable homes.

These sites are located at Sandsend Road in Whitby, where 60 homes are planned, and at Eastfield in Scarborough, which could see approximately 550 homes built.

Lovell would match the value of the council’s land with funding, and profits would be shared equally.

Mary Parsons, regeneration and partnerships director for Lovell, said: "This proposed joint venture is a great example of this and would enable new homes to be delivered in the county at pace, with both partners committed to maximising the number of new council affordable homes that can be delivered and ensuring that local firms and communities benefit from this major investment."

A joint venture board is also proposed, with three representatives from each organisation.
