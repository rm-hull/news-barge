A new £1.2 million 3G sports pitch has been officially opened in Selby.

Located at Selby College, the all-weather facility was launched on Friday, July 3.

It was made possible through funding from the Premier League, The FA, and the Government's Football Foundation, as part of the Lionesses Futures Fund to support women’s and girls' football nationwide.

Keir Mather, MP for Selby, said: "It was absolutely incredible to see the finished pitch - it has been a product of lots of hard work by the team at Selby College, supported by funding from the UK Government.

  Keir Mather, MP for Selby, taking a penalty shootout at the opening event 

*(Image: Selby College)*

"The facility will be a fantastic new asset for our town, benefitting school teams and weekend leagues alike.

"It’ll bring people together around sport, creating moments of real pride in Selby and making sure local people don’t have to travel further afield to access top quality facilities."

Selby College student India highlighted the impact on female players, describing it as "brilliant" to have a reliable, all-weather pitch that won’t be affected by cancellations.

Robert Sullivan, chief executive of the Football Foundation, said: "Good quality playing facilities have a transformative impact on physical and mental health and play an important role in bringing people together and strengthening local communities."
