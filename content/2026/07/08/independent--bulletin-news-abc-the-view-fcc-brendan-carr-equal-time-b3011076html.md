# ABC accuses Trump FCC of threatening free speech over ‘The View’

![ABC contends that this move is an attack on free speech and editorial independence, arguing the government should not determine legitimate news content or compel broadcasters to feature specific guests](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/07/19/19/GettyImages-2284911795.jpg)

- A significant dispute has emerged between the U.S. media, particularly ABC, and the administration over whether its talk show "The View" should be subject to equal time rules.
- The administration's FCC chairman, Brendan Carr, has indicated an intention to argue that "The View" does not qualify as a "bona fide news program."
- ABC contends that this move is an attack on free speech and editorial independence, arguing the government should not determine legitimate news content or compel broadcasters to feature specific guests.
- The network highlighted that over 77,000 public comments have been submitted to the FCC, with an undeniable majority expressing support for "The View" and the principle of free speech.
- This conflict is part of a broader pattern of the administration's criticism of media outlets, including calls for ABC to fire Jimmy Kimmel over a joke.
