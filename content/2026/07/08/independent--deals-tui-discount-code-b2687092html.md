## How to use a TUI discount code

Select your holiday and head to checkout. Under “passenger details”, enter your TUI discount code in the promo box and apply it before payment. Always check the terms to make sure your booking qualifies.

## Why isn't my TUI discount code working?

If a TUI voucher code doesn’t seem to be working, it could be because your booking doesn't meet the promotion's eligibility criteria.

Before trying another code, check that:

- Your package meets the minimum spend
- Your destination is included
- You’re travelling in the selected date range
- You've entered the code and characters correctly

Many TUI offers are automatically applied at checkout, so you may already be receiving the advertised saving without entering a promo code.

## More FAQs

### Does TUI ever offer 10 per cent off?

Yes, TUI occasionally runs percentage-based promotions, including discounts of 10 per cent off selected holidays. However, most TUI discount codes tend to offer a fixed monetary saving, such as £100 or £200 off eligible bookings. Depending on the cost of your holiday, these offers can often work out as a discount of 10 per cent or more.

### Can you use a TUI code on all-inclusive holidays?

Yes, many TUI discount codes can be used on all-inclusive holidays, provided the booking meets the terms of the promotion. Always check the offer conditions, as some discounts may only apply to selected destinations, travel dates or holiday types.

### Can you combine myTUI discounts with voucher codes?

No, TUI only allows one discount code per booking. This means you won’t be able to combine multiple promo codes at checkout. However, you can often pair a discount code with existing deals, such as sale prices, free child places or low-deposit offers.

### Does TUI offer free child places?

Yes, TUI regularly offers free child places on selected holidays when two adults are paying. Availability is limited, so it’s best to book early.

### Are TUI holidays ATOL-protected?

Yes, all TUI package holidays are ATOL protected, meaning your money is financially protected if the company ceases trading.
