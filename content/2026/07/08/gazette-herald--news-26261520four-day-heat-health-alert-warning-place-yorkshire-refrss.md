YORK and North Yorkshire have been issued with a four-day warning of increased risks due to incoming rising temperatures.

The UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA) has issued a yellow heat-health alert for all of Yorkshire and the Humber from 9am this morning (Wednesday, July 8) until 9pm on Sunday, July 12.

The alert follows warnings that rising temperatures this week may increase health risks, particularly for vulnerable groups.

  


An existing warning for the East Midlands, West Midlands, East of England, London, South East, and South West, has been escalated to amber.

The Met Office is predicting high temperatures and high humidity with low winds in York from today until the weekend, peaking on Thursday and Friday, with little or no sight of rain over that period.

There may be some cooler offshore breezes in that period, the UK weather agency said.

Dr. Agostinho Sousa, head of extreme events and health protection at UKHSA, said: "Sustained periods of warm weather can result in serious negative health outcomes across the population, so it is important that everyone takes sensible precautions while enjoying the sun.

"Based on the current forecast, we don’t currently expect the health impacts to be as high as the heatwave we saw at the end of June but older adults and those with pre-existing conditions are still at risk.

"If you have friends, family, or neighbours who are more vulnerable, it is important to check in on them and ensure they are aware of the forecast and are following the necessary advice."

In late June, a handful of schools in North Yorkshire closed due to temperatures recorded in classrooms as high as 37C and uniform policies were altered with PE kits being worn all-day at some.

Dr Sousa added that simple steps such as staying hydrated, avoiding the sun during the hottest part of the day, and keeping homes cool are effective ways to help keep safe during periods of hot weather.

The UKHSA will continue to monitor and update alerts if necessary.
