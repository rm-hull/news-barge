# Harrogate families dedicate daisies in memory of loved ones through Saint Michael’s Hospice campaign

## Hundreds of people across the Harrogate district have come together to remember loved ones as part of Saint Michael’s Hospice’s latest fundraising appeal.

The hospice’s new Dedicate a Daisy campaign, which launched last month, has already seen supporters dedicate hundreds of handcrafted metal daisies in memory of those they have lost.

Sponsored by Dishforth Nursery Gardens, the appeal features 1,500 daisies, with each £25 daisy helping to fund vital hospice care – the equivalent of one hour of nursing support from the Saint Michael’s Hospice team.

Among those taking part is Sue Broomhead, who has dedicated a daisy in memory of her dad, Trevor, after the hospice supported her family during his final days.

![Hundreds of people across the Harrogate district have come together to remember loved ones as part of Saint Michael’s Hospice’s latest fundraising appeal, Dedicate a Daisy](https://www.harrogateadvertiser.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOmZmNTgyMjAzLTIwMDYtNDUwNy04YjY3LTNkNWQ1Yjc0ODUwYzoyMjU4YWE4Ni02OWI3LTQ0ZjQtYWY3Yy1hNmZlOTQ4OWZkMGI=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



Trevor was cared for by Saint Michael’s Hospice last May after reaching the end of his treatment for throat cancer.

Sue said: “My dad went downhill quite quickly and as a family we wanted to keep him at home as this was one of his wishes.

"Saint Michael’s Hospice couldn’t have been more supportive at this difficult time and were there whenever we needed it.

"Nothing was too much trouble – we couldn’t have cared for him without their help.”

For Sue, dedicating a daisy has provided a special way to remember her dad while also supporting the charity that helped her family.

Her family recently continued their fundraising efforts by taking part in the Great Knaresborough Bed Race, raising £1,145 for the hospice.

Sue added: “Since losing my wonderful dad we are doing all we can to raise money for this wonderful charity.

"Dedicating a daisy means we can raise much needed funds and have a very special reminder of my dad in the garden.

"My dad was a huge personality with an infectious laugh and is sadly missed by everyone who knew him.”

The appeal has also been meaningful for Saint Michael’s Hospice volunteer Richard Cyster, who dedicated a daisy in memory of his wife.

He said: “I have dedicated a daisy in memory of my wife, Margaret, who died suddenly and unexpectedly in February.

"I have volunteered with Saint Michael’s Hospice for over 14 years, ten of which as a bereavement support worker for Just ‘B’.

"When it was my turn to face bereavement, it was Just ‘B’ that I turned to for help – they did not let me down.”

The campaign has also sparked acts of kindness from supporters keen to ensure everyone has the opportunity to take part.

One generous supporter, who dedicated a daisy in memory of his father, has also purchased an additional daisy for a deserving member of the Saint Michael’s Hospice community who may be struggling financially but would still like to take part in the campaign.

In July, all 1,500 daisies will be brought together to form a special remembrance installation in the peaceful grounds of Saint Michael’s Hospice.

Supporters will be invited to visit the hospice on July 18 and 19 to handpick their dedicated daisy before taking it home to display as an ongoing tribute.

Alternative arrangements will also be available for those unable to attend.

The Dedicate a Daisy appeal is open to everyone.
