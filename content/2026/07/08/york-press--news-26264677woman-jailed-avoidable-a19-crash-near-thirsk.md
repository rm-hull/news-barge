A woman has been banned from driving after causing an "entirely avoidable" head-on crash in North Yorkshire.

Megan Sanderson veered into oncoming traffic on the A19 near Bagby, close to Thirsk, at around 5.20pm on April 8 last year, North Yorkshire Police said.

Sanderson, 21, from Jarrow in Tyne and Wear, was driving a Citroen DS which crossed the centre white line before colliding head on with a Kia Picanto travelling northbound.

Both she and the Citroen's driver, a local woman in her 20s, had to be cut from their cars by firefighters after the crash.

The Citroen's driver sustained multiple serious injuries including a displaced right ankle, a broken leg, and a deep laceration to her arm, police said.

Sanderson suffered a broken arm along with several minor injuries.

Sanderson was jailed for 12 weeks, suspended for 12 weeks, on Wednesday (July 8) at York Magistrates' Court after pleading guilty to causing serious injury by careless driving. She was also ordered to pay £439 in fines and costs, and banned from driving for a year.

In a victim impact statement, the Citroen's driver said she had been a "confident driver" but "lost my confidence" after the crash.

She said she "felt like I had my independence taken away from me and I needed to rely on other people".

The woman also said she suffers from pain "on a daily basis", adding: "I have good days and bad days."

"Not only has the collision had an impact on me but it has also had an impact on my family and my boyfriend who continue to provide me with the support I need," she continued.

"I am determined to make as much progress as I can, but I worry about the impact that my injuries might have on my future."

PC Ross Moralee, from North Yorkshire Police's Roads Policing Unit, said it was an "entirely avoidable collision caused by a clear failure to maintain proper control of a vehicle".

"The consequences for the victim have been significant, and it is only fortunate that the outcome was not more serious," he said.

"This sentence sends a message that careless driving will not be tolerated on our roads. When drivers allow their standards to drop, the results can be life changing. Road safety is a priority for North Yorkshire Police, and we will take firm action against anyone whose driving puts other road users at risk.

"I urge all motorists to remember that even a momentary lapse in concentration can have lasting and devastating consequences for all involved."
