Arthur Fery bids to continue his stunning breakout run as he faces ninth seed Flavio Cobolli in the Wimbledon quarter-finals.

The 23-year-old, ranked 114th in the world, is the lowest-ranked quarter-finalist at SW19 in 14 years and the first British wildcard to reach the last eight, as well as just the sixth British man to reach that stage in the Open era.

He has achieved that milestone in remarkable fashion, winning two match tiebreaks and twice coming back from two sets and breaks down to complete superb comebacks and keep home hopes of an improbable fairytale alive.

Standing in his way is recent French Open runner-up Cobolli, who Fery beat - albeit when the Italian was physically hampered - at the Australian Open in January.

Elsewhere in the quarter-finals, former Marta Kostyuk is through to her first Wimbledon semi-final after a straight-sets victory over former runner-up Jasmine Paolini, with Taylor Fritz resuming his rivalry with Alexander Zverev later on.

Meanwhile, Novak Djokovic bids to recover from winning the longest ever Wimbledon quarter-final against third seed Felix Auger-Aliassime, after five hours and 15 minutes, ahead of his semi-final clash with Jannik Sinner on Friday.
