York train passengers are facing delays after signalling staff were evacuated by a fire alarm.

Fewer trains had been able to run on all lines through the station after staff at the York signalling centre were evacuated on Wednesday morning (July 8).

Staff had returned to the building by 10am and were working to the services back moving, TransPennine Express said.

The rail firm, whose services run from York to cities including Leeds, Manchester and Liverpool, and to Malton and Scarborough, said all lines have since reopened.

But TransPennine Express said trains running through York may be delayed until 12pm.

“TransPennine Express services will be subject to delays across various routes whilst Network Rail staff aim to get services moving again,” a company spokesperson said.

They said customers who have been delayed by 15 minutes or more can claim compensation for their journey via the TransPennine Express website.
