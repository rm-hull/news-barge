Inter Miami superstar Lionel Messi is set to headline the 2026 MLS All-Star Game roster, leading a 29-player squad against the Liga MX All-Stars. The highly anticipated match is scheduled for July 29 at 8 p.m. ET, taking place at Bank of America Stadium in Charlotte, N.C.

Messi, 39, was among 11 players selected through fan voting results announced on June 5. The Argentine maestro, a two-time league MVP and reigning MLS Cup champion, has demonstrated exceptional form this season, contributing a league-high 20 goals (12 goals, eight assists) in just 14 appearances. He is one of seven forwards named to the prestigious roster.

The full squad comprises 16 players chosen by Charlotte FC head coach Dean Smith, whose club will host the event. Additionally, two players received special selections from MLS Commissioner Don Garber: 18-year-old New York Red Bulls forward Julian Hall and Toronto FC defender Richie Laryea.

Other notable inclusions feature Chicago Fire's Golden Boot leader Hugo Cuypers, who boasts 13 goals, and Los Angeles FC's South Korean star Son Heung-Min. The roster also includes U.S. men's national team members Sebastian Berhalter of the Vancouver Whitecaps, Max Arfsten of the Columbus Crew, and New York City FC goalkeeper Matt Freese.
