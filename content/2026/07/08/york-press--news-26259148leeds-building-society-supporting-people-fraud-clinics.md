A building society has helped more than 2,000 people with face-to-face support to protect themselves from financial scams since the scheme was first launched.

Leeds Building Society branches across the UK, including York, have delivered the support through one-to-one fraud clinics, which form part of the society’s wider efforts to boost financial resilience and prevent fraud.

A recent report found that the clinics, alongside other community-focused services, have generated an estimated annual total of £286 million in social value.

The report highlights the wider benefits to individuals and society beyond financial savings alone.

Stephen Senior, head of financial crime prevention at Leeds Building Society, said: "Scams are becoming more sophisticated and more personalised, so staying safe can feel increasingly difficult.

"Our fraud clinics are designed to give people the knowledge and confidence they need to spot the warning signs.

"Having a presence in York and 50 other locations across the UK means that we can use our position in the community to deliver workshops that give people the tools and knowledge to spot potential scams."

The clinics were launched in August 2025 and offer free, 30-minute appointments led by specially trained staff. Sessions cover topics such as online banking and card fraud, scam mail, and APP fraud.

James McGowan, principal consultant at RealWorth, said: "Our research found that building society branches deliver significant social value through their role in preventing scams.

"We repeatedly heard from customers how staff’s understanding of their usual banking behaviour enabled them to spot unusual requests, ask the right questions, and intervene before any financial harm could occur."

With 86 per cent of society members saying they are concerned about being targeted by fraudsters, demand for the clinics remains high.

Leeds Building Society says its branches continue to play a vital role in supporting in-person customers, particularly as access to traditional high street banking services declines.
