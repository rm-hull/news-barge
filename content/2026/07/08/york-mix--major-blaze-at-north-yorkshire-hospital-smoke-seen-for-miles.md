Firefighters are battling a huge blaze at a hospital in North Yorkshire.

A building on the Malton Hospital site is well alight.

Several fire crews are on the scene and people are being asked to avoid the area.

#### Malton and Norton District Hospital

A North Yorkshire Fire & Rescue Service spokesperson said: “We’re currently responding to an incident involving a building on the Malton and Norton District Hospital site.

“A number of fire crews are on the scene.

“Three main jets are in use and our Aerial Ladder Platform is in attendance.

“Residents and anyone nearby may notice large amounts of smoke. As a precaution, please keep all doors and windows closed.

“Please avoid the area at this time to allow emergency services access.”

Hospital staff have confirmed all people are accounted for.

Malton councillor Lindsay Burr says the it’s the hospital’s Springwood unit which is on fire.

It provides mental health services for older people.

The fire has destroyed the unit.

## No injuries

A **York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust spokesperson** said: “We are aware of a fire within the grounds of Malton Hospital.

“All patients and colleagues have been safely evacuated, and there are no reports of any injuries.

“We are working closely with North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service and other partner agencies to monitor the situation. We will provide further updates as soon as more information becomes available.

“If you have an appointment at Malton Hospital today, please do not attend. Our teams are reviewing all affected appointments, and we will contact you as soon as possible to rearrange or discuss the next steps. Thank you for your patience and understanding.”

**Malton Town Council** said: “There is a fire currently at Malton Hospital. Emergency services are on the scene. 

“Please be vigilant if you are in the area. There is some ash and debris falling from the sky.

“It may be advisable to keep windows and doors closed where possible. Our thoughts are for the safety of the patients, staff and emergency services at this time.”

## ‘A sad day for Malton and Norton’

**Mayor of North Dinah Keal** said: “Huge thanks to the emergency services who are still battling a devastating fire at Malton Hospital. Fortunately it appears that there have been no casualties, but it looks like there is extensive damage to some of the buildings.

“Thanks also to the staff for their efforts to safely evacuate patients to Malton School until alternative arrangements can be made for their care.

“Malton Hospital is a fantastic facility serving our community and its caring staff have supported so many people over many years – today is a sad day for Malton and Norton, but I look forward to it reopening in due course and continuing to serve at the heart of our towns.”

Witness **John Gelson** from Norton told** YorkMix**: “There is a huge black pool of smoke rising up from the area near Malton Hospital, which can be seen all the way across in both Malton and Norton. 

“Lots of people commenting on it as they go around in the town centre this lunchtime.

“I don’t see any special precautions in place, certainly in Norton yet, as far as the smoke is concerned, but I understand there are fire crews coming here from a wide area of both North Yorkshire and the East Riding to take part in this operation.”

## Low water pressure

A **North Yorkshire Police spokesperson** said: “We are assisting our colleagues at NYFRS and supporting road closures in response to this incident.”

**Yorkshire Water** said: “Due to the fire service using our mains supply to manage the fire at the Malton Hospital site, customers may have low pressure or no water in the area. 

“We will continue to update our incident page. Thank you for your patience and understanding.”
