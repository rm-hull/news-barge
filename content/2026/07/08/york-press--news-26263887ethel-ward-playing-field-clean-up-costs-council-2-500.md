## Thousands paid to clean playing field left behind by Travellers in York

Human waste, raw chicken and dirty food packets were collected as part of the clean-up of Ethel Ward Playing Fields after Travellers left the site *(Image: Submitted)*

A TOWN council spent more than £2,500 to clean a community playing field in York after it was left by Travellers.

Professional cleaners were needed to clear Ethel Ward Playing Field in Haxby after it was reported that “large amounts of rubbish including faeces” had been left on June 30.

Haxby and Wigginton Councillor Andrew Hollyer said he visited the site at 7am the following day to begin the cleaning process.

  


At the time, he said: “We have jet-washed the playing area and hard surface and are going around collecting waste.

“We probably have about 50 black bags and we have a skip coming too.

“[The Travellers] had spread themselves out across the whole of the field."

  Cllr Andrew Hollyer and a member of the professional cleaning team dealing with the mess left on Ethel Ward Playing Fields after Travellers left the site. 

*(Image: Submitted)*

Shortly after 2pm on Wednesday (July 1), Haxby Town Council reopened Ethel Ward, assuring residents that the area had been cleaned but that some holes in the ground remained.

The rush to clear the area came days before the town’s annual carnival, which takes place on Saturday (July 11) from 11am till 5.30pm.

The playing field is set to include a family fun fair, market stalls, food vendors, sensory den, a free miniature train and more, Haxby Town Council said.

  Haxby Ward Councillor Andrew Hollyer said he had received "numerous" concerned reports from residents near to Ethel Ward Playing Field (Image Cllr Andrew Hollyer) 

*(Image: Cllr Andrew Hollyer)*

After the weekend activities, the Town Council is set to meet on Monday at 8.30pm to discuss the cost of the Traveller encampment clean-up, Cllr Hollyer said.

Held at the Oaken Grove Youth and Community Centre in Reid Park, the Full Council meeting will be joined by Luke Charters MP, with residents invited to have their say on the issue and other town matters.

Cllr Hollyer said: “We will be discussing the cost of the clean-up and sharing this amount with City of York Council, along with future physical measures to protect Ethel Ward Playing Field, such as bollards.”
