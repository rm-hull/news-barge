A North Yorkshire village has been officially recognised for helping to reduce light pollution.

Sproxton has been named a "dark sky friendly community" under a scheme led by the Howardian Hills Partnership and North York Moors National Park.

The village earned the title after upgrading lighting through three improvement schemes to lower light pollution and a full audit of external lighting.

  Spoxton Project Before Dark Sky lighting 

*(Image: Howardian Hills National Landscape)*

Ellie Hook, manager of the Howardian Hills Partnership, said: "We are delighted to welcome Sproxton into the dark sky fold.

"Light pollution and the threat it poses to nocturnal species is a big issue which is starting to be taken much more seriously."

  Spoxton Project After Dark sky lighting 

*(Image: Howardian Hills National Landscape)*

This is the tenth community to receive the designation, under a UK-first initiative run by the Howardian Hills Partnership and North York Moors National Park.

George Skinner, from Sproxton Parish Meeting, said: "A public meeting in the village revealed clear support to go for designation.

  Northern Lights over the Howardian Hills 

*(Image: Chris Lowther - Howardian Hills)*

"The new lighting looks really good.

"We have a beautiful night sky in Sproxton, and visitors are always amazed.

"We are pleased to be working with the Howardian Hills team to keep it that way."

A light is considered dark sky friendly if it is fully shielded, emits no upward light and uses warm white colouring.
