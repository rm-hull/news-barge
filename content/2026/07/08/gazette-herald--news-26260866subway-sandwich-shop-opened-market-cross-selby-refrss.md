AN INTERNATIONAL fast food chain has officially opened its doors in the heart of a North Yorkshire town.

Sandwich chain Subway opened its doors at Market Cross in Selby on Monday (July 6).

The new shop will occupy the former Elaine’s Gifts and Cards unit and sees a return of the brand, which had previously operated at 33 Gowthorpe in the town but closed in 2022.

This comes as part of a wider programme of improvements at Market Cross and is supported by a £100,000 investment to enhance the centre for visitors and improve the variety of tenants.

The new store features a bright modern interior, digital order screens, and a dedicated seating area.

James Shepherd, managing director of Dransfield Properties - who owns the Market Cross site, said that the team were "really pleased to be welcoming Subway to Market Cross".

  Subway has opened its doors in Selby's Market Cross Shopping Centre 

*(Image: Provided)*

He said: "The arrival of a major franchise is great news for the centre and the wider town, adding to the existing line-up of shops, cafés and eateries.

"This opening is part of our ongoing investment into Market Cross, and we remain committed to supporting the centre as a standout destination at the heart of Selby."

To celebrate the launch, customers have been able to enjoy 30 per cent off all sandwiches, wraps, salad boxes and jacket potatoes, while the first customer through the door received a free signature Footlong sub.
