After being gifted the opening leg of the series, they suffered one of their worst ever humiliations in the second, as Queensland overturned a four-point deficit at the interval to hammer the Blues 44-24 and force a decider in Maroons territory.

Not only have New South Wales only won three of the 10 previous State of Origin deciders staged in Queensland, but they've lost five of the six series they've played under head coach Laurie Daley. They took the lead in three of those five series.

He's made six changes for Game 3 at Lang Park in Brisbane, the headline news being Jack Bostock's debut at the expense of NSW great Brian To’o, who was named the team's best player in 2023 and 2025. Stephen Crichton has also returned to the starting lineup despite being hampered by a shoulder injury. Code-hopping phenomenon Mark Nawaqanitawase scored twice on his State of Origin debut, and will hope to sign off in style before his return to rugby union.

QLD head coach Billy Slater, meanwhile, has kept things familiar, only with Jeremiah Nanai and Pat Carrigan returning to the fold after recovering from a shoulder injury and ankle surgery respectively. Selwyn Cobbo, who scored a second-half hat-trick last time out, retains his place, as do Cameron Munster and Harry Grant – who has been branded a "cheat" by former NSW coach Phil Gould.
