No injuries have been reported after patients and staff were evacuated from Malton Hospital following a blaze, according to the NHS trust in charge of the site.

The York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust said all patients and staff have been safely evacuated from Malton Hospital after the fire broke out around midday on Wednesday (July 8).

People with appointments at the hospital on Wednesday are asked not to attend them, with hospital teams working to contact all affected patients.

The fire is at the hospital's Springwood unit, which provides mental health services for older people, according to the area's ward councillor.

A York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust spokesperson said it is "working closely with North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service and other partner agencies to monitor the situation".

“If you have an appointment at Malton Hospital today, please do not attend. Our teams are reviewing all affected appointments, and we will contact you as soon as possible to rearrange or discuss the next steps. Thank you for your patience and understanding," they said.

Nearby residents are being asked to keep windows and doors closed due to thick smoke in the area from the fire.

Firefighters have been working throughout Wednesday to contain the blaze.
