**AN INVESTIGATION has been launched after an HGV vehicle became stuck under a bridge in a main road in York.**

Gregory Group – a logistics company that operates across the UK – has said that they will be investigating after a vehicle with their branding, driven by an subcontractor, became stuck under the old railway bridge in Huntington Road, York.

Police, emergency services, and council workers were called to the old railway bridge in Huntington Road last month (June 26) after the vehicle became wedged and twisted whilst passing underneath.

The incident is thought to have happened at around 11.05am and the lorry was towed out from under the bridge at around 2.35pm after around three and a half hours.

A spokesperson for Gregory Group said: “We are aware of the incident in York involving one of our trailers.

“We can confirm that the trailer was being operated by a subcontracted transport provider and was not under Gregory Group's control at the time of the incident.

  The truck when it first became stuck 

*(Image: Josh Allenby)*

“Safety is our highest priority, and we take the risk of bridge strikes extremely seriously due to their potential impact on people, infrastructure, and the wider road and rail network.

“Across our operations, we maintain robust policies, procedures, and training designed to prevent incidents of this nature, with a strong focus on route planning, vehicle height awareness, and compliance with road signage.

“We expect all subcontractors working on our behalf to meet the same high standards.

“We are currently working to establish the full circumstances surrounding this incident, and a thorough investigation will be carried out in line with our incident management procedures.”
