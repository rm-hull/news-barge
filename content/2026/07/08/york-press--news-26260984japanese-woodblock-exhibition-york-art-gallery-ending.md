York Art Gallery is gearing up for a Japanese-themed celebration to mark the closing of its current exhibition.

The Making Waves: The Art of Japanese Woodblock Prints exhibition will close during the Bank Holiday weekend in August.

A closing celebration will take place, including Japanese-themed crafts and food, traditional harp playing, and drumming sessions.

  Katsushika Hokusai, The Great Wave. c.1829-32 

*(Image: Maidstone Museum)*

Visitors can also expect performances from local choir groups and Kokedama, which is a traditional nature-based craft.

Throughout the Bank Holiday weekend, the gallery will offer the final chance to see Hokusai’s Great Wave, from Maidstone Museum.

Other features will include the chance to create your own print as part of the exhibition and view kimonos, samurai armour, and swords.

The kimonos are being showcased from Durham Oriental Museum, whereas the armour and swords have come from the Royal Armouries.

  Hashimoto Chikanobu, Evacuation of Chiyoda Palace, from the series ‘The Inner Palace of Chiyoda’, 1896 

*(Image: York Museums Trust)*

More than 38,000 people have already visited the exhibition, and visitors are being encouraged to book in advance to ensure they have the chance to view the exhibition before it closes.

The closing celebrations will take place across Saturday, August 29, and Sunday, August 30.

More details about the celebration can be found on the York Art Gallery's website.

The exhibition will be open for viewing throughout the summer.
