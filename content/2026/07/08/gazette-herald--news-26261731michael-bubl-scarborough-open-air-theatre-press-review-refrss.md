**Michael Bublé at Scarborough Open Air Theatre - Press review by Dave Lawrence**

CANADIAN superstar Michael Bublé's final show of a short visit to Ireland and the UK was a sold-out performance at the Scarborough Open Air Theatre on Monday.

Michael Bublé is the latest world-class star lured to the Scarborough Open Air Theatre by promoters Cuffe and Taylor, following in the footsteps of artists like Sir Elton John, Sir Tom Jones, Sting, Kylie Minogue, Lionel Ritchie, Bryan Adams and Christina Aguilera to name but a few.

The evening began with a pleasant set by Appleton - sisters Nicole and Natalie Appleton - who were members of All Saints back in the day. Songs performed included Don’t Worry from their 2003 album Everything’s Eventual, their latest single Ready to Begin, and the All Saints hits Pure Shores and Never Ever.

Bublé’s orchestra took their seats shortly after 8pm while a clock projected on the stage screen counted down the final five minutes before show time. The audience counted down the last ten seconds enthusiastically and Bublé took the stage opening with the Anthony Newley song Feeling Good, his voice sounding as terrific as ever.

Before the feel-good Haven’t Met You Yet Bublé - referring to the previous night’s epic England victory against Mexico at the World Cup – said “I wonder… I may be being dramatic… I don’t know if I’m right… but is it coming home?” After a huge cheer, he then led a mass singalong of Wonderwall.

The singer then singled out a woman he’d spotted waving in the furthest corner of the seating and told her he’d written a song for her and broke into Everything.

  Michael Bublé at Scarborough OAT. Photo by Cuffe and Taylor


The show delivered everything you would expect from a Michael Bublé performance. Plenty of cheeky chat between songs and forays into the audience that delighted the largely female audience. Most importantly though were the songs – many of them true classics - all delivered with Bublé’s trademark style and panache.

These included Nat King Cole’s L-O-V-E, I’ve Got the World on a String famously sung by Frank Sinatra, the Bee Gees’ To Love Somebody, Clyde McPhatter and The Drifters’ Such a Night and a sublime version of Van Morrison’s Moondance.

Later Bublé turned backing vocalist when he gave the spotlight to Jumaane Smith - his trumpeter and vocalist - who performed You're Nobody Till Somebody Loves You.

  Appleton - sisters Nicole and Natalie Appleton - at Scarborough OAT..jpg


Bublé emotionally recalled his first visits to the UK and of appearing on the Paul O’Grady and Parkinson shows saying his wife and four children were at the show and thanked the audience for their continued support which had given his family such a wonderful life.

Towards the end of the show Trouble opened a short medley of Elvis songs including Burning Love and Can’t Help Falling in Love, before a barnstorming finish that included You're The First, The Last, My Everything and - in his words - “a terrifying song about revenge”, Cry Me a River. He then closed the show with Always on my Mind.

For sheer showmanship, entertainment and an unforgettable evening out a Michael Bublé concert delivers on every level.
