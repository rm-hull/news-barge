A huge fire has broken out at Malton Hospital sending plumes of smoke over the town.

Firefighters have been on the scene working to contain the blaze since around 11.30am on Wednesday (July 8).

The fire is at the hospital's Springwood unit, which provides mental health services for older people, according to the area's ward councillor.

Drone footage taken shortly before 11.40am showed the building's roof on fire.

Malton councillor Lindsay Burr said she has been informed that "all patients and staff have been evacuated safely" and taken to the nearby Malton School.

Thirsk and Malton MP Kevin Hollinrake said he has been told that all people are accounted for after the fire.

Residents have been asked to keep their windows and doors shut due to heavy smoke in the area.

People have also been urged to avoid the hospital site, with a police cordon in place in Maiden Greve.
