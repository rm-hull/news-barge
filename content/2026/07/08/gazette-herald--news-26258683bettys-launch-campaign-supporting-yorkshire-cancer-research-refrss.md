Bettys has partnered with Yorkshire Cancer Research to raise funds through a county-wide tea party campaign.

Supporters who take part in Yorkshire’s Big Brew will receive an exclusive lemon tart recipe from the Bettys Cookery School in Harrogate.

Ruth Burke-Kennedy from Saltaire, who has worked at Bettys for more than 10 years, was diagnosed with breast cancer in 2022.

  Part of the Bettys team for over a decade, Ruth is passionate about supporting life-saving research following her own cancer diagnosis 

*(Image: Yorkshire Cancer Research)*

She said: "Within a month of seeing my GP about my potential cancer symptoms, I had surgery to remove the cancerous mass in my breast.

"The diagnosis was a shock, but the care I received in Bradford was fantastic.

  Everyone who signs up to Yorkshires Big Brew will receive an exclusive lemon tart recipe 

*(Image: Yorkshire Cancer Research)*

"I’m cancer-free now, and that’s largely thanks to early diagnosis."

Every 15 minutes, someone in Yorkshire is told they have cancer.

Yorkshire Cancer Research funds screening and clinical trials, including the DOORstep trial in Hull, which offers free transport to breast screening appointments.

  Lisa, Manager at Bettys Cookery School, has created a beautiful lemon tart for budding bakers and tea-makers 

*(Image: Yorkshire Cancer Research)*

Neil Preston, director of fundraising at Yorkshire Cancer Research, said: "Yorkshire Cancer Research is delighted that Bettys Bakery and Cookery School has chosen to support the charity's life-saving mission.

"By creating this special recipe for Yorkshire's Big Brew, Bettys Bakery and Cookery School is helping supporters make their events even more memorable."

Bettys Bakery and Cookery School chose Yorkshire Cancer Research as a charity partner for 2026.
