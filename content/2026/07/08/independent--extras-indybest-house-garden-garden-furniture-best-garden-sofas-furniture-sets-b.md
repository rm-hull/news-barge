Furniture company Swyft may be best known for selling an attractive range of easy-to-assemble indoor sofas, but its outdoor dining collection offers serious wow-factor. This includes the garden dining set 02, which is the best garden sofa set I tested thanks to its comfortable cushions and high-quality, weather resistant materials.

![best garden sofa indybest review Swyft garden dining set 02]([https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/04/23/10/14/Swyft-garden-dining-set-02.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/04/23/10/14/Swyft-garden-dining-set-02.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp) 608w)

Swyft’s garden dining set is made from acacia wood and comprises a corner sofa, dining table and two stools. These all come topped with stylish, green weather-proof cushions. I really like the corner configuration, which compactly fits up to eight people. As the cushions are thick, I found them comfy to sit on without feeling like I was sinking in. The stools are also light enough to easily move around.

The set comes pre-oiled, which gives the acacia wood a premium finish. Every time it rained, the water seemed to bounce off the surface and was easy to wipe dry. As the covers are dark green, I think it makes it easier to disguise any wear and tear marks over time compared with if they were white or cream. When not in use, you can store it under its tailor-made the garden dining set 02 cover, for an extra £99.

Once placing the order, delivery to my home was swift as the brand name suggests. Choosing the white glove delivery option made set up easy, as upon arrival the team of two were able to assemble the furniture in less than 30 minutes. This costs an extra £49 on top of the delivery charge. For the purposes of this test, I paid close attention to assembly instructions, which seemed straightforward enough as everything was clearly labelled. I was relieved that the delivery team took the packaging away for recycling too, as the set is large and it arrived in a few boxes.

Maintenance has been surprisingly easy, too. Kettler recommends treating the acacia wood a couple of times a year to keep it in top condition but, if I'm honest, I've never got around to doing that.
