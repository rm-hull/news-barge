## 'A tax on serious illness' - York MP calls for end of hospital parking fees

Rachael Maskell MP has called for the government to set out plans to remove hospital parking charges, describing them as a "tax on serious illness" *(Image: Supplied)*

A YORK MP has demanded an end to hospital parking charges, describing them as a “tax on serious illness”.

Rachael Maskell MP said hospital parking charges place an “unnecessary burden” on patients, families and NHS staff, and urged the government to set out plans to fund their removal.

It comes after York Hospital car parking facilities were revealed as the most expensive in Yorkshire, costing users £22.50 for an all-day stay - more than £10 higher than Scarborough Hospital’s rates.

  


In response, a spokesperson for York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust said: “Parking charges for patients and visitors at our hospitals are broadly aligned with those charged by local authorities in the areas we serve and are reviewed regularly.

“We offer a wide range of concessions and exemptions to support those who need it, including for those who attend the hospital regularly, parents of sick children, and visitors to patients receiving end-of-life care.”

  The MP said the parking fees placed an "unnecessary burden" on families, patients and NHS staff 

*(Image: Supplied)*

The York Central MP said she was meeting with the Chief Executive of the Trust, Clare Smith, this week to discuss York Hospital’s parking charges and congestion in Wigginton Road.

She said: “The congestion is caused in part by the queues to get in the multistorey car park; and I want to fully explore all alternative transport options for staff and visitors to the hospital.

“Labour created the NHS to be free at the point of use, so hidden charges of car parking fees are an unnecessary burden on families, patients and NHS staff.

“The underfunding of our NHS for so long under Tory rule meant that many NHS England hospital trusts relied on parking income to try and plug the gap.

"The Government must now bring about a plan to cover the cost of removing hospital car parking charges.”
