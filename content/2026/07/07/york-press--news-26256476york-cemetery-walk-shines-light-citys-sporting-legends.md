## York Cemetery tour to celebrate city's forgotten sporting pioneers

Late champions of Yorkshire sport will be remembered on a guided cemetery tour titled Cemetery Sports People *(Image: Friends of York Cemetery)*

Late champions of Yorkshire sport will be remembered on a guided cemetery tour.

The free event, titled Cemetery Sports People, will take place at York Cemetery on Sunday, July 26, at 2pm, and will highlight individuals who played key roles in shaping the city's sporting legacy.

The tour is led by Jenifer Hawkins, a volunteer with the Friends of York Cemetery, who will guide visitors to around 14 graves connected to figures from cricket, football, rugby, boxing, horse racing, golf, and bowls.

Ms Hawkins said: "One of the things I enjoy most is discovering the stories of the fascinating people buried in York Cemetery and sharing them with visitors on the regular walks organised by the Friends of York Cemetery.

"When my sports-enthusiast husband suggested a walk with a sporting theme, and genealogist David Poole pointed me towards several graves of sporting figures, I was delighted to uncover even more remarkable stories about the people and the sports they played.

"I'd especially love to hear from anyone who knows of women involved in sport who are buried in York Cemetery.

  The Cricketers Arms in Tanner Row 

*(Image: Friends of York Cemetery)*

"Sadly, throughout much of the period covered by the cemetery, women faced significant barriers to participating in sport.

"The Football Association's ban on women's football, which remained in place from 1921 until 1971, is just one example of the challenges they encountered.

"By visiting these graves and sharing their stories, we're helping to ensure that York's sporting heritage – and the people who helped shape it – is remembered for future generations."

One of the figures featured on the tour is Richard Letby, who became landlord of the Cricketers Arms in Tanner Row at just 21 and helped run York Cricket Club for around 50 years.

The pub, and the club’s ground, once occupied land now beneath York’s railway station.

Attendees will also hear about the first captain of Yorkshire cricket, an England green-bowls captain, champion boxers, and figures from rugby and football.

Stories will include those connected to the parents of Joseph Hulme, the footballer who played for Arsenal and also represented Middlesex in cricket.

The walk aims to reveal the lesser-known stories that make up York’s broader sporting heritage, extending well beyond recent headlines.

It follows York City's return to the Football League and York Knights' historic promotion to the Betfred Super League — reminders of the city’s ongoing success.

The tour not only honours sporting legacies but also highlights York Cemetery’s role as a record of social history, linking modern achievements with the many individuals who helped build York’s sporting traditions.

While the event is free, donations to support the work of the Friends of York Cemetery are encouraged.
