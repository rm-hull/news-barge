It has been one year since it was revealed that Christian Horner and Geri Halliwell-Horner were facing a planning permission problem for plans at their £9.2m mansion.

The now former Red Bull Racing chief executive and team principal did spend most of his weekends abroad with Formula 1 prior to his departure from Red Bull.

But when the 52-year-old is back home, he retires to a huge mansion in Marston St Lawrence near Banbury on the Oxfordshire border, with his wife and Spice Girl, Geri.

It was revealed last July that the couple had been embroiled in a planning row with their neighbours over plans to enhance horse stables at their country home.

They had submitted plans to build a walker to exercise their horses and move some gates to allow safer equestrian access at their country home.

Yet it was other work at the house which then caused a major headache for the couple after they pressed ahead with the creation of an outdoor kitchen in April 2025.

This was to complement the plans for a helipad, orangery, stables, boating lake and two swimming pools, with one indoor and one outdoor.

The Daily Mail reported this time last year that construction had started on an oak-framed pergola and gazebo, but planning permission had not been sought.

"In their excitement, the couple neglected to apply for planning permission – a necessity, as their five-bedroom house is not only Grade II-listed but is in a designated conservation area," read the report.

"It's a surprising oversight given that last year the couple's proposal to 'introduce' the 40ft by 16ft outdoor pool was denounced as 'the height of disrespect' by one neighbour, who pointed out that it would be so close to the village church that mourners could be 'distracted by screaming, shouting and splashing' as the Horners and chums and their children cavorted in the pool.

"Evidently keen not to push their luck too far – and aware that the council can order them to demolish any unauthorised work – the couple are trying to rectify matters by applying for retrospective planning permission."

This planning row came just weeks after Mr Horner was sacked as Red Bull team principal.

Early in July, a statement from Red Bull GmbH read: “Red Bull has released Christian Horner from his operational duties with effect from today (Wednesday, July 9) and has appointed Laurent Mekies as CEO of Red Bull Racing.”

The 52-year-old had been team principal at Red Bull since 2005, overseeing eight drivers’ world championships and six constructors’ titles.

He has led them to world championship success with drivers Sebastian Vettel and Max Verstappen.
