New research has revealed two North Yorkshire sites to be among the UK's cheapest coastal towns for buying ice cream.

Whitby and Scarborough were in the top 10 when it came to cheap ice cream, with last week's record-breaking temperatures.

According to credit card experts at Zable, the cost of ice cream varies considerably across the UK.

In Whitby, a single scoop costs £2.38, ranking the historic North Yorkshire town fifth for affordability.

A family of four can enjoy a round of cones for £9.53.

Meanwhile, Scarborough is ranked tenth, with a single scoop costing £2.75.

A family of four can expect to pay £11 for a single scoop each, and those wanting to double up on scoops will pay around £15.

The research analysed the cost of single and double scoop cones at 33 UK coastal destinations to determine where the most affordable ice creams can be found.

The top spot for the cheapest ice cream was Barton-on-Sea in Hampshire, where a single scoop costs £1.95.

Conversely, Porthmadog is the UK's most expensive seaside destination for ice cream, with a single scoop cone averaging £3.85.
