Runners from Picking Running Club faced scorching temperatures in a series of challenging races.

On Wednesday evening, the latest event in the Summer Fell Race League, known as Tom’s Race, took place in Bransdale.

This gruelling 7.5-mile course features a 1,300-feet climb over open moorland, through bracken, and along forest tracks.

Robbie Preston was the first back for Pickering Running Club (PRC), finishing the demanding course in 67 minutes and 59 seconds, placing him 17th out of 78 runners.

Lucy Saggers was delighted with her six-minute course personal best, clocking in at 87:41 and earning her the prize for second in the F55 category.

Paul Smith was third in the M65 category with his time of 91:31, followed by Nick Cousins in 93:30.

Jo Towler’s time of 93:53 earned her the prize for second in the F50 category, and Lynn Humpleby was close behind in 95:54.

The Hardwolds 40, held on Saturday, tested runners' endurance with scorching temperatures.

This race, organised by the Hardmoors team, starts in Beverley, follows the Wolds Way to Settrington Beacon, and then descends into the Vale of Pickering, finishing at Malton Community Sports Centre.

The actual distance is 47 miles with a staggering 4,800 feet of climb.

Out of 102 competitors, 24 were unable to finish due to the intense heat.

Mike Richmond made it look like a pleasant day out on the Wolds, cruising home in eight hours exactly, placing him fifth overall and first in the M50 category.

Kelly Richmond was not far behind and ran the last few metres with baby Daisy.

Kelly won the trophy for first female with her impressive time of 8:33.

Martin Dennison celebrated his birthday by completing an ultramarathon, running with Chris Adams.

Despite the heat taking its toll, they managed to finish in 13:31.

Graham Robinson, accompanied and encouraged by Alice Hayter, made it to the finish in 18 hours, nine minutes.

Graham was second in the M70 category, and walked his way into the Guinness World Record book (to be confirmed) as the only person completing an ultramarathon needing an oxygen cylinder to breathe.

In addition to the kit competitors have to carry, Graham had to carry a cylinder weighing 11lbs.

Choosing a less demanding option, several members of PRC ran the race as a relay.

Serena Partridge, Simon Jones, Lucy Saggers, and Karen Farmer-Scott were the first mixed relay team in 7:51.

Shirley and Simon Tyrell, David Stephenson, and Sue Slack were the second mixed team in 9:55.

George and mum, Lisa Adams, Nicola Wise, and Dean Wise were the third mixed team.

They were led by George, the youngest runner in the event, and Dean, the oldest runner in the event, who brought the baton home in 11:50.

On Sunday, the Mulgrave Castle 10k, organised by New Marske Harriers, took place in the beautiful grounds of Mulgrave Castle.

Kirsty Brown finished in 46 minutes, 25 seconds, placing her 14th out of 94 and fourth female.

Next came Rich Johnson in 56:22, followed by Lynn Humpleby, who had a great race finishing in 56:55.

Suzy Hodgson was next in 57:17, then came Julie Ventress in 57:35, Karen Harper in 65:05, and Lydia Adrian in 67:07.
