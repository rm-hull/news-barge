York-based businesses are investing thousands in digital technology to support growth and modernise their operations.

Two manufacturers from the city, Great Balls of Flour and Andrews Signs & Engravers, are investing a combined total of more than £135,000 in new technology with support from the Made Smarter Yorkshire programme.

It is part of the Government-backed scheme to help small and medium-sized manufacturers adopt digital tools.

  James Talbot, director of Great Balls of Flour 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Great Balls of Flour, a pizza dough manufacturer, secured a £20,000 grant towards a £100,000 automation project, which includes automated vacuum filling, capping, and labelling machines.

James Talbot, director of Great Balls of Flour, said: "I'm really proud of the journey we’ve been on with Made Smarter.

"The transition to automation is a significant milestone for us, not just for the productivity gains, but for how it supports our team’s growth."

Andrews Signs & Engravers received a £15,000 grant for a £35,650 investment in a wide-format digital printing system.

Jill Andrews, director at Andrews Signs & Engravers, said: "We were delighted to secure support through the Made Smarter programme, which has enabled us to invest in technology that will strengthen our manufacturing capabilities and support future growth.

"As a Yorkshire manufacturer, it's encouraging to see initiatives like Made Smarter helping businesses embrace new technology, boost productivity and remain competitive in an increasingly digital marketplace."

  Andrews Signs & Engravers 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Collectively, the two York firms received £35,000 in Made Smarter funding as part of the latest round, which supported 19 manufacturers across Yorkshire investing nearly £900,000 in digital upgrades.

Since launching in 2021, Made Smarter Yorkshire has supported more than 1,250 manufacturers, awarded 112 technology grants worth £1.6 million, and helped generate almost £5 million of investment across the region.

Businesses can access specialist advice, skills programmes, digital internships, and matched funding of up to £20,000 through the scheme.

To learn more, visit: www.madesmarter.uk/yorkshire
