# Harrogate school’s longest serving staff member says she's made 'lifelong friends' along the way

## A Harrogate school’s longest serving staff member has said she feels “privileged to have been with so many children at the very beginning of their learning journey”.

After nearly 30 years of service, the retiring Lorraine Simcock was front-and-centre at the last ever Highfield Prep School Speech Day.

Mrs Simcock began her journey at the school in 1997 and is a much-loved member of the Highfield Pre-School community.

“I have been so fortunate to work alongside such wonderful people,” Mrs Simcock said.

“So many of my friends are colleagues I have worked with here, many of them are lifelong friends.

“I am very privileged to have been with so many children at the very beginning of their learning journeys and I hope they have had as much fun as I have.”

Part of the Harrogate Ladies’ College family of schools, Highfield Prep School is located on Duchy Road in Harrogate.

The annual Speech Day, which saw Highfield pupils receive prizes for their academic and extra-curricular success from the 2025/26 academic year, held extra significance as it was the school’s last Speech Day as Highfield Prep, before becoming Duchy College Prep in September.

Head of Highfield Prep School, Mr James Savile, said: “As we look ahead, we do so at a particularly significant moment.

“The transition to Duchy College Prep marks not just a change of name, but a moment to step forward together with confidence and ambition.

“It is not a departure from who we are.

"It is the continuation of a story that has always been about growth, courage and belief in young people.”

Alongside awards for performing and creative arts, sport, drama and Forest School, Speech Day also featured speeches from the Head Boy and Head Girl.

Highfield’s Head Boy said: “This school shows us the beauty of a community: the sense that everyone is looking out for you and provides a safe place to problem solve and explore your strengths and curiosity.”

Alongside the move to a co-ed senior school, their family of schools will be known as Duchy College from September 2026.
