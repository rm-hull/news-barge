A MAN said “I can’t breathe” before he died after being restrained at a York jewellery store, an inquest has heard.

Sam Diatta lay on his front as he was restrained by four men at the Mappin and Webb jewellery store, then in Coney Street, on July 26, 2022.

CCTV footage played at the inquest showed Mr Diatta throwing punches at the shop’s door supervisor before the supervisor grabbed Mr Diatta’s arm and tried to hold him.

The hearing was told Mr Diatta, 39, of Holgate, had been diagnosed with bipolar disorder and was on medication for heart disease.

His cause of death was given as hypertensive heart disease and the effects of being restrained.

The door supervisor, Gary Prothero, previously told the inquest that Mr Diatta entered the jewellery store and asked to take photographs of three watches for his social media pages, telling staff he had 18,000 followers online.

Staff told Mr Diatta he would have to arrange this with head office, the inquest heard.

Mr Prothero said Mr Diatta became aggressive after being denied access to the watches, so he asked him to leave the store.

Mr Diatta then attempted to grab the Covid mask Mr Prothero was wearing before starting to throw punches at the door supervisor, the inquest heard.

James Ljungdell told the court he had visited the store with his family on July 26, 2022, to buy a watch when the incident took place.

He said he was sitting with his wife and five-year-old daughter at a table to the rear of the store when he heard “loud shouting”.

He turned around and said he saw Mr Prothero “being hit” by Mr Diatta.

“I wasn’t sure what was happening,” Mr Ljungdell said. “I thought to myself, I’ve got to do something here because he [the door supervisor] was being attacked.”

CCTV footage showed Mr Ljungdell and Mr Prothero hold Mr Diatta while upright then take him to the floor resting face down. Two passers-by also stopped to help hold Mr Diatta on the ground.

A police officer arrived at around 4.45pm and rolled Mr Diatta onto his back then started administering CPR when it became apparent that he was not breathing, the inquest heard.

More police and paramedics arrived and Mr Diatta was later taken to York Hospital. He was pronounced dead in hospital shortly after 6.40pm.

Mr Diatta was restrained for around seven minutes before his death, the inquest heard.

Mr Ljungdell said he had acted in a “split second” to protect his family and Mr Prothero after seeing the door supervisor being punched.

Mr Ljungdell told the court on Tuesday (July 7) he initially feared there was going to be a robbery. Mr Diatta’s family has said North Yorkshire Police confirmed the incident was not an attempted robbery.

Mr Ljungdell alleged that Mr Diatta was shouting “I’m going to kill you” at him and Mr Prothero while they attempted to restrain him.

“I didn’t know what it was going to escalate into,” Mr Ljungdell said. “I thought, if he [Mr Diatta] was on the floor we could hold him there until the police [arrived].”

Mr Ljungdell said he and Mr Prothero both struggled to hold Mr Diatta as he was “very strong”.

Mr Ljungdell, who said he weighted around 15 stone at the time, told the hearing he had his right knee on Mr Diatta’s arm and his left hand on Mr Diatta’s head to hold him.

“I was holding him to react to how he was reacting to me,” he told the court, saying Mr Diatta had been moving while being restrained.

Mr Ljungdell said Mr Diatta had been speaking while he was being restrained, adding: “I was checking on his welfare and looking around outside the shop for police.”

A member of the public had knelt by Mr Diatta and was speaking with him while he was being restrained, the inquest was also told.

When a police officer arrived Mr Ljungdell told the court that “Sam said: ‘I can’t breathe’.”

Mr Ljungdell said he saw the police officer “pretty much straight away” after Mr Diatta said he could not breathe.

The police officer told the men to roll Mr Diatta onto his back then started CPR, the inquest heard.

Mr Ljungdell said he later took over the CPR until another police officer arrived and took over from him.

## 'I just went out to buy a watch'

Mr Ljungdell gave his condolences to the Diatta family and said: “I just went out to buy a watch … I never expected to end up in this situation. I didn’t go out with the intention to hurt anyone.”

After Mr Diatta’s death, North Yorkshire Police said four men were arrested in connection with the death and were interviewed and released while under investigation.

But in July 2023 the force said no one would be charged with any offence following Mr Diatta’s death.

Mr Diatta’s family described him as a “wonderful member of our family who will be greatly missed by us, his friends and family around the world”.

  The family members, in a statement **released by police after Mr Diatta’s death,** said Mr Diatta developed mental health issues in his late teenage years “which stayed with him throughout his life”.

Their statement added that Mr Diatta was a “a gentle giant, who was kind and generous and loved by all who met him”.

The inquest, at Northallerton Coroners’ Court, continues.
