# Harrogate MP secures Government backing for Slingsby Gin's new owners

## Harrogate’s MP has won a commitment from the Government to support the new owners of much-loved Harrogate brand Slingsby Gin following its parent company’s financial challenges earlier this year.

The popular gin brand in Harrogate was given a new lease of life in March after being rescued out of administration in a deal to ensure production continued and some jobs were saved after the company behind Slingsby Gin entered into administration.

Tom Gordon, Liberal Democrat MP for Harrogate and Knaresborough, raised the question of the future of the much-loved Harrogate brand in the House of Commons.

Speaking in Business and Trade questions, Mr Gordon asked the Minister to wish new owners Chris and Asma Williams good luck in their new venture and to set out what steps the Government is taking to support them.

Responding, the Minister, Blair McDougall MP, wished the new owners all the best, saying: "It is entrepreneurial spirit like that that drives the economy forward."

He pointed to the major extension of UK Export Finance, which helps businesses find new markets, explaining that "the best way to become a more resilient business is for them to open up and diversify their markets."

The Minister offered to put Chris and Asma in touch with the team at UK Export Finance to discuss the support available.

Harrogate MP Tom Gordon said afterwards: "Slingsby Gin is a brand that Harrogate is rightly proud of, and it was a real blow to see it go into administration earlier this year.

"Chris and Asma are giving it a new lease of life, and I wanted to make sure the Government is behind them.

"I'm pleased the Minister has committed to making sure they can access the support UK Export Finance has to offer.

"Local businesses like Slingsby are the backbone of our economy in Harrogate and Knaresborough, and I'll keep fighting to make sure they get the backing they need to grow and succeed."

Slingsby Gin was founded in 2014, using local spring water and botanicals, and inspired historically by William Slingsby, who first discovered Harrogate’s mineral-rich Tewit Well in 1571.
