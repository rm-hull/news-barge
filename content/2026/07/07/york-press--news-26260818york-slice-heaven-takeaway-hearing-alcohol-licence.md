## 'Risk to local population'- 1am booze sale bid for York takeaway sparks fears

York's Slice of Heaven takeaway has applied to sell alcohol until 1am *(Image: Supplied)*

PLANS to sell alcohol from a York takeaway until 1am poses risks to the surrounding population, public health officials have warned.

The Slice of Heaven takeaway, in Clifton, has applied to sell alcohol with food from its Burton Stone Lane premises.

Its application stated drinks would only be available through deliveries after 11pm and until 1am daily.

But City of York Council public health officials have objected saying it could fuel crime and disorder in the area and risk a rise in domestic abuse and children being neglected.

It comes as the takeaway’s application is set to go before a council licensing hearing for a decision on Tuesday, July 14.

The vendor, which sells pizzas, burgers, fried chicken and other dishes, would be allowed to sell alcohol with food during its opening hours of 11am to 1am daily.

Applicant Taylan Ozer said they would take a zero-tolerance approach to crime, disorder, violence and antisocial behaviour.

He added a strict entry policy would be in place and deliveries of alcohol would only be allowed to people aged 18 and over.

The applicant said: “A very strict age lD policy will be applied at all times, both at the premises and on deliveries, although it is fair to say that the vast majority of customers are adults.

“Staff training will focus very carefully on ensuring that alcohol is not served to children.

“None of the alcohol that will be sold either on premises or via deliveries will be available for the customer to pick up or touch until all checks have been completed, there will be no grey areas where children are concerned – no proper lD, no service.”

The takeaway agreed to install CCTV, keep a log of customers refused sales, ID people who look under 25 and train staff following a North Yorkshire Police request.

Police licensing officers dropped a prior objection following the agreement and no representations were made by any other parties.

But council officials said alcohol was already causing problems locally, with 245 hospital admissions caused primarily by drinking in the north of Clifton between 2016/7 and 2020/1.

They added the admission rate was 40 per cent higher than the national average.

Public health officials said: “Burton Stone Lane features a diverse demographic, including young families and vulnerable adults.

“Introducing extended alcohol sale and late-night home delivery into this neighbourhood creates risks to the local population which have not been acknowledged by the applicant.

“Public health data consistently demonstrates that expanding the hours during which alcohol can be procured triggers an increase in violent crime, public disorder and use of emergency services.

“Whilst premises such as pubs and clubs have on-site supervision and security, takeaway establishments and delivery services do not have the same protocols or controls.”
