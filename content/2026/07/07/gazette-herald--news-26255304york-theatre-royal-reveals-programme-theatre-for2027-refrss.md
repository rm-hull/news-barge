A major new season of theatre has been announced, promising classic drama and exciting collaborations.

York Theatre Royal will stage a revival of Arthur Miller’s Death of a Salesman in partnership with Glasgow’s Citizens Theatre.

The production, directed by Citizens Theatre’s artistic director Dominic Hill, opens on March 2, 2027, with previews beginning on February 24.

Paul Crewes, CEO of York Theatre Royal, said: "We’re very excited about this upcoming season – most notably in a first for us, we will be working with our friends at Citizens Theatre in Glasgow on Arthur Miller’s modern classic Death of a Salesman, directed by the fabulous Dominic Hill.

"This is a wonderful collaboration with a great theatre."

The season also includes a revival of Tristan & Yseult, co-produced with the Emma Rice Company and Nottingham Playhouse.

Dominic Hill said: "I am delighted to be directing a new production of this brilliant Arthur Miller play that is one of the most influential works of the 20th century.

"It could not be more timely in the age of Donald Trump’s politics of greed and toxic masculinity."

Other highlights include the UK premiere of Acosta Danza’s Cuban Baroque, and London City Ballet’s Firebird – A Mixed Bill.

For the full programme, visit: [https://www.yorktheatreroyal.co.uk/](https://www.yorktheatreroyal.co.uk/)
