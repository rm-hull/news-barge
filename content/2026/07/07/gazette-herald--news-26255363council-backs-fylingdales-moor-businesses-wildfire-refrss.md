Businesses forced to evacuate during a major wildfire are being offered financial support from the council.

The North Yorkshire Council is backing firms affected by the Fylingdales Moor blaze with grants of up to £2,500 and hardship payments of up to £12,000.

One of the businesses to benefit is Grouse Hill Caravan Park, located between Whitby and Scarborough in the North York Moors National Park.

The 15-acre site, owned by Andrew and Caroline Butterfield, was evacuated on August 26 last year when flames came dangerously close.

  L-R: North Yorkshire Council’s leader, Cllr Carl Les, the owner of the Grouse Hill Caravan Park, Andrew Butterfield, and the council’s head of resilience and emergencies, Matt Robinson 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Council)*

Mr Butterfield said: "The funding that the council has provided is extremely welcome, and it is giving us much-needed financial support as the effects of the wildfire are still very much evident a year on."

North Yorkshire Council stepped in with funding after the Government ruled out compensation for businesses affected by the wildfire, despite providing £3.2 million for moorland restoration.

Mrs Butterfield added: "The fire had such a devastating environmental impact on the moors, but it is also the psychological effects it's had on the communities here.

"The council has shown it is committed to helping the communities that were affected, and that makes a big difference knowing the support is there."

The council has partnered with the Two Ridings Community Foundation to deliver the targeted funding.

Councillor Carl Les, leader of North Yorkshire Council, said: "We are committed to supporting our communities, but I cannot stress enough that this funding has been made available due to unprecedented circumstances surrounding the Fylingdales Moor blaze.

"As a council, we will not be able to repeatedly step in to provide financial support in the future at a time when our budgets are under intense pressure and demand for our services is increasing."

Cllr Les called for a review of national policy to ensure other communities are supported during natural disasters.

  A sign warning of the extreme danger of fires in the North York Moors National Park 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Council)*

He added: "The Government needs to look very closely at how it can support communities and businesses affected by natural disasters across the country.

"There is a compelling argument that national policies do need to be reviewed as the sad but undeniable truth is that the Fylingdales Moor fire will prove not to be a one-off."

The wildfire, believed to have been caused by a naked flame from cooking equipment during dry conditions, burned 20 square kilometres—a fire 10 times the size of the Great Fire of London.

A new campaign, 'Don't Spark Disaster,' was launched in June to help prevent further wildfire incidents.

The campaign is being delivered by the North York Moors National Park Authority with support from North Yorkshire Council, the Yorkshire Dales National Park Authority, Forestry England, and North Yorkshire Fire & Rescue Service.
