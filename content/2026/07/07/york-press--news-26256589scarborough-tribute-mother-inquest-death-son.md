## Mum praised for 'incredible bravery' after death of teenage son

Scarborough Hospital *(Image: Anttoni Numminen)*

A CORONER paid tribute to the “incredible bravery” of a mother from Scarborough following the conclusion of an inquest into the death of her teenage son.

Anita Sutcliffe was present at North Yorkshire Coroner’s Court in Northallerton on Friday, June 26 at the hearing into the death of her son Adam Jake Morley, 17, who was pronounced dead in Scarborough Hospital on Monday, September 2, 2024.

The inquest, held by assistant coroner for North Yorkshire and York, Catherine Devereux, heard that the 17-year-old died of natural causes with the cause recorded as Bilateral Pulmonary Thromboembolism caused by Deep Vein Thrombosis in his right leg.

The court heard Adam was unresponsive when ambulance crews arrived at his home in Scarborough on the day of his death and CPR was performed before he was transported to Scarborough General Hospital.

On Monday, August 26, 2024, Adam had presented at the emergency department of the hospital after complaining of pains in his right leg with his calf swollen and shortness of breath, the court heard, and he was given injections to thin his blood.

He returned for a scan to check for a blood clot two days later which returned a negative result for deep vein thrombosis with raised D-Dimer levels and returned home the same day.

A second scan was scheduled to be carried out on September 4, the inquest heard, which was within the timeframe issued by the National Institute for Health and Care Excellence to retest for a possible false-negative result.

Adam died two days before that appointment.

York and Scarborough District Hospitals carried out a structured judgement review into Adam’s death and consultant Ed Smith said it identified there were some opportunities to improve its processes around allowing unlabelled medicines to leave the emergency department.

An independent external review was carried out by Dr Saman Khan of Bradford Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust.

Dr Khan said an acute physician was not involved at all in this case, and that a raised D-Dimer may have raised a concern.

At the conclusion of the hearing the assistant coroner asked York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust to write to her within 28 days to explain if their process will be looked at in terms of having an acute physician to see a patient where there is a high D-Dimer result with no other explanation for the result.

She also asked for confirmation that unlabelled medicines were longer being issued from the emergency department.

Catherine Devereux told Anita Sutcliffe how sorry she was for the terrible loss, thanked her for her attendance and for her incredible bravery.

A spokesperson for York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust said: “We offer our sincere condolences to Mr Morley’s family and loved ones.

“We acknowledge the matters raised by the coroner at the conclusion of the inquest.

“We are reviewing our processes and will provide a full response to the coroner within the required timeframe.”
