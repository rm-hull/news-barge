The Kaminari Taiko Drummers wowed the crowd at the Easingwold Summer of Music Festival.

Performing in warm and humid conditions on Saturday, the popular group showcased new routines to an appreciative audience filling the marketplace.

Their performance included intricate rhythms and meticulously choreographed Japanese-inspired movements, underscoring the skill and countless hours of practice involved.

The Kaminari Drummers are well-known in the York community, not just for their performances but also for their charitable work.

*(Image: Frank Johnston-Banks)*

During the festival, they collected donations for the Family Fund, which supports disabled children and their families.

The group also runs Funamachi, a community drumming group with several Easingwold residents as members.

The volunteer group joined Saturday's programme, impressing the crowd with their progress over the past year, particularly in complex drum sequences and dance movements.

Their performance earned a "hearty round of applause".

A highlight of the Kaminari Drummers' visit was an open participation session, inviting newcomers to experience the joy of Taiko drumming.

  The group is anticipated to return to Easingwold next year 

*(Image: Frank Johnston-Banks)*

Encouraged by the familiar invitation of "Come on, have a go!" a group of eager volunteers, many of whom had never held drumsticks before, joined in, spurred on by the audience's encouragement.

Two members of the Kaminari group recently returned from Japan, bringing new ideas and techniques that are expected to inspire future performances.

The group is anticipated to return to Easingwold next year, continuing to be a cherished part of the Summer of Music Festival.

The event once again brought the community together for an unforgettable afternoon of music and entertainment.
