# IOC lifts suspension and paves way for Russia to compete at LA 2028 Olympics

- The International Olympic Committee (IOC) has provisionally lifted its suspension of the Russian Olympic Committee (ROC), a move that could facilitate Russia's full return to the Olympic Games ahead of Los Angeles 2028.
- The ROC was originally suspended in October 2023 for recognising regional Olympic councils in Russian-occupied parts of Ukraine, specifically Luhansk, Donetsk, Kherson, and Zaporizhzhia, following Russia's February 2022 invasion.
- The IOC's decision to lift the suspension was based on a thorough analysis by its Legal Affairs Commission, which determined that the ROC no longer includes these regional sports organisations as members.
- The Russian Olympic Committee has formally pledged that it does not and will not conduct any activities in the disputed Ukrainian territories, a commitment the IOC will continue to monitor closely.
- Despite the lifting of the suspension, the IOC's executive board has not yet decided whether Russia will be permitted to display its national flag, colours, or have its anthem played at future Games, though Russian sports minister Mikhail Degtyarev welcomed the decision.
