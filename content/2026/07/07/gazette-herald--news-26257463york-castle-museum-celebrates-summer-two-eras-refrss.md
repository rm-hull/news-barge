York Castle Museum is reviving summer nostalgia in a unique way.

The museum has launched its summer displays pitting Victorian summers against the Swinging Sixties.

The displays opened on June 22, encouraging visitors to decide which era celebrated summer better.

  Visitors to the 1960s Street at York Castle Museum 

*(Image: Gareth Buddo Furmoto Photography)*

Kirkgate, the museum’s Victorian street, is decorated with floral displays and projections of fireworks, aiming to recreate the grand summer days out of the York Galas from 1859 to the early 20th century.

On the other side of the museum, the Swinging Sixties exhibition is rolling back the years to the summers of love, filled with giant flower power decor, a games table, and 60s music.

The exhibition aims to transport visitors back in time, celebrating an iconic decade and, for some, childhood memories.

In the yard, visitors can release their inner child with space hoppers, swing ball tennis, hobby horses, and retro relaxation in orange floral deckchairs.

  Visitors meet a Victorian on Kirkgate at York Castle Museum 

*(Image: Gareth Buddo Furmoto Photography)*

The museum has decided to decorate for the summer festivities earlier than previous years.

This change offers visitors more time to enjoy the floral displays and festivities outside of the school holidays.

York Castle Museum invites everyone to enjoy a nostalgic adventure into the past, deciding for themselves which era celebrated summer best.
