A MAN from North Yorkshire has been sentenced after assaulting an emergency worker who was trying to help him.

Nathan Mark Drummond, 38, of Briercliffe, Scarborough pleaded guilty to assaulting a Police Community Support Officer at Scarborough Magistrates Court on Monday (July 6).

The incident happened on Friday (June 19), and the court heard how the PCSO had been alerted to Drummond by a concerned member of the public.

When the officer visited Alma Terrace in Scarborough, she saw Drummond, who had a head injury and blood on his hands.

As she attempted to help him, Drummond kicked her in the leg with a roundhouse-style kick, North Yorkshire Police said.

  


Drummond was arrested at the scene for assault, and was sentenced to 24 weeks in prison and ordered to pay the PCSO compensation of £200.

Speaking about this, Superintendent Rachel Wood, Senior Operational Commander of the Scarborough and Ryedale Command, said, "This sentence sends a clear message of how the criminal justice system takes such behaviour.

“All too often police officers and staff are subjected to assaults and threats while carrying out their work to protect members of the public - including the very people who abuse them."
