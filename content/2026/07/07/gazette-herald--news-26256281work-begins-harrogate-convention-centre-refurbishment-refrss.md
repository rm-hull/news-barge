A major upgrade is under way at Harrogate Convention Centre to secure its future in the competitive conference market.

The £9.4 million investment, led by North Yorkshire Council, aims to modernise Studio Two by creating larger breakout rooms and updating key infrastructure.

Work began on Monday, July 6, with a wider package of improvements including new cooling systems, interior refurbishments, and energy efficiency upgrades.

  The main 2,000-seat auditorium of the Harrogate Convention Centre, which has been a major conference venue since it opened in the town in 1982 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Council)*

Councillor Carl Les, leader of North Yorkshire Council, said: "The Harrogate Convention Centre has been such an important asset to the region since it opened more than 40 years ago.

"However, we recognise that the building needs to be modernised to make sure that it can continue to compete alongside its rivals in the conferencing sector.

"We are committed to making sure that the very best facilities possible are provided at the convention centre."

  An artist’s impression of one of the breakout rooms that will be created through the scheme 

*(Image: Infinite 3D Ltd)*

The project is expected to generate more than £1.5 million in additional income per year.

Martin Mann, chief executive of Harrogate District Chamber of Commerce, said: "The Harrogate Convention Centre has been part of the foundations of North Yorkshire's economy for more than 40 years.

"The work that the council is undertaking at the centre is vital to ensuring its long-term future, and the commitment to the venue is very welcome."
