Sport, wellbeing, and community were at the heart of a recent school event.

STAMFEST 2026 took place at Stamford Bridge Primary School on Friday, July 3, bringing together pupils, families, and staff for a full day of activities and celebration.

The event blended traditional sports day competitions with a variety of sporting experiences, ensuring every child from nursery to Year 6 could take part, compete, and try something new.

Nicola Massey, headteacher, said: "STAMFEST has been a fantastic example of what makes our school community so special.

"It was wonderful to see children of all ages participating with such enthusiasm, whether competing in races, learning new sporting skills, or enjoying time with their families.

"The support from parents, staff, and our external partners helped create a memorable day that truly celebrated teamwork, wellbeing, and community spirit."

Children took part in football coaching from York City Football Club, tennis lessons, dance workshops, taekwondo, parachute games, and storytelling.

A family picnic on the school field was a highlight of the day, giving parents, carers, and pupils the chance to enjoy lunch together.

The school extended its thanks to everyone who contributed to the event, including staff, volunteers, families, the PACT team, and all activity providers.
