                                    Students from Rossett School have been given a behind-the-scenes look at the new Harrogate College campus as construction continues on the development.

A group of Year 10 pupils were invited to tour the site after taking part in intervention work delivered in partnership with the college.

The visit was led by Caddick Project Manager Matt Hargreaves, who explained the different stages of the build and answered students' questions about the project and careers in the construction industry.

To take part in the visit safely, students were provided with steel toe cap boots, hard hats and high-visibility jackets, giving them a taste of working on a live construction site.

![](https://mmo.aiircdn.com/409/6a4cbbc8352cdb411be.jpg)


![](https://mmo.aiircdn.com/409/6a4cbbc8d91bbb93eb1.jpg)


The tour gave pupils the chance to see construction work up close, while learning about the teamwork, technical skills and range of career opportunities available across the sector.

A spokesperson for Rossett School said:

"We would like to extend our sincere thanks to Harrogate College for selecting Rossett School students for this unique opportunity, and to Caddick and Matt Hargreaves for providing such an engaging and educational visit.

"Experiences such as these play an important role in helping our students explore future career options and make informed decisions about their next steps.

"We look forward to continuing our excellent partnership with Harrogate College and providing further opportunities for our students in the future."


Rossett School said the experience helped students better understand what working in construction involves and encouraged them to think about their future career options.
