A packed schedule of summer activities is set to keep young people busy over the holidays across the East Riding.

East Riding of Yorkshire Council’s Holiday Activities and Food (HAF) programme for summer includes over 460 activities at numerous locations across the region.

Councillor Victoria Aitken, cabinet member for children, families and education, said: "I’m delighted with the range of HAF activities available this summer.

"Wherever families live, there will be something for children of all ages to enjoy within their community.

"From sports, music and dance to craft, nature activities and animal encounters – there’s lots of fun opportunities to keep children and young people active through the summer holidays."

Children aged four to 16 can access activities.

Families in receipt of benefits-related free school meals or council tax support can access the activities for free.

It offers a variety of activities, including pony trekking, water sports, graffiti workshops, and a SEND (special educational needs and disabilities) programme featuring BMX biking and farm experiences.

New additions to the scheme include cricket, STEM activities and holistic therapies.

Although HAF activities are free for eligible families, others can take part on a paid basis.
