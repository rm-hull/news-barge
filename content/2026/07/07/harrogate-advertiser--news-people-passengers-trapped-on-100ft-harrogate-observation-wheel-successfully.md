# Passengers trapped on 100ft Harrogate observation wheel successfully evacuated by Fire Brigade

## North Yorkshire Fire Brigade have had to rescue three people trapped on the Big Wheel in the heart of Harrogate.

A Harrogate Advertiser reader got in touch to say that a middle aged women, senior woman and small child had been stuck on the 32-metre high ferris wheel at Cambridge Crescent for more than an hour yesterday afternoon.

The giant observation wheel, which first came to Harrogate town centre in November 2021 as part the town's Christmas offer, had only opened on Saturday.

North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service says that its Harrogate aerial ladder platform and its supporting appliance were called out to the 100ft tall Big Wheel opposite Bettys tearooms yesterday, Monday, at 4.15pm to evacuate three people.

“Three persons were stuck approximately 15 feet from the ground in a car on The Big Wheel,” said a Fire Service spokesperson.

"A triple extension ladder was used to bring two adults and one child to earth safely.”

It's not the first time questions have been raised about the presence in Harrogate of the towering tourist feature.

There have been minor grumblings from some about whether the location, next to the town’s main war memorial, was appropriate, in light of the fact the land was donated to Harrogate by the Yorkshire Hotel for remembering the town’s war dead.

Operated by WM Attractions Limited in co-ordination with North Yorkshire Council, the Big Wheel is scheduled to run daily from 11am to 7pm until Sunday, July 26.
