An “honesty” box has been damaged in a North Yorkshire town.

North Yorkshire Police is appealing for information after the unmanned box – typically selling goods with money left in exchange – was damaged in Kirkbymoorside on Friday, June 26.

The force said the aluminium box in West End, near the Kirkbymoorside Business Centre, may have been damaged with a metal object.

Officers are urging anyone with information, or dashcam footage of the incident, to email [mark.burrows@northyorkshire.police.uk](mailto:mark.burrows@northyorkshire.police.uk) or phone 101.

Information can be passed anonymously to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or via its website.

Quote reference 12260119920 when passing on information.
