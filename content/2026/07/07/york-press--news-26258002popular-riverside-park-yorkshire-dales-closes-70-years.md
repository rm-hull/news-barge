## Owners of popular riverside park announce immediate closure after 70 years

Burnsall Park has been permanently closed *(Image: Burnsall Park)*

THE owners of a popular riverside park in the Yorkshire Dales have closed it and warned people against travelling to the area.

Permanent closure of Burnsall Park, in Burnsall, near Grassington after many years has been announced on social media by its owners who urge people not to visit because there will be no access to the site.

It is the only place in the village where people can set up a barbecue and will drastically reduce the amount of parking without blocking narrow roads as Craven braces itself for the busy school summer holidays.

It is normally open from Easter to the end of September with visitors paying £12 per car to park all day.

The closure comes after 13-year-old Bradford schoolgirl Palwasha Akbar tragically drowned while visiting Burnsall with her family at the end of May and also the rescue on June 26 of a man and his young son by three Craven College students after they got into difficulties in the river.

A statement issued by Burnsall Park said: "We are now permanently closed. Please do not travel here as you can not gain access There is nowhere in Burnsall to BBQ. Thank you."

North Yorkshire councillor Richard Foster, who represents Wharfedale, said closure of the site would mean visitors struggling to find a place to park or with something to do.

The field is crossed by the Dales Way public right of way, but people will not be able to access the site with vehicles.

"With the warmer weather coming back, we are expecting quite a lot of people to turn up, but where will they go and what will they do," he said.

  Burnsall Park, permanently closed 

*(Image: Google)*

Mr Foster, said the site, previously known as Burnsall Car Park, had been a place popular with visitors for around 70 years.

"It's really been a victim of its own success. Its a shame, it's been a real asset and a real attraction. It's sad to see it close after such a long time."

Mr Foster said it would be a challenging time for the village but that there would be extra policing and parking enforcement officers from North Yorkshire Council to make sure people did not park illegally and block roads.

He added he would be meeting with council highways officers soon to discuss what sort of parking restrictions could be introduced in the village without pushing the problem on somewhere else.

Edwin Williams, chair of Burnsall Parish Meeting, urged visitors to steer clear of the village. "There will be nowhere to barbecue in Burnsall and legal parking will be limited.

"We have been promised Yorkshire Dales National Park ranger support, police presence and parking enforcement.

"People need to be asked to not go to Burnsall whatsoever unless they are going for a walk. Even then it might be better avoided for a while."

Last month, police urged people to park sensibly, stay safe around water and respect the local community while visiting the southern Dales.

  Burnsall is very popular with visitors. 

*(Image: Burnsall Park)*

A spokesperson said: "We’ve been working closely with partner agencies to respond to concerns from residents about increased visitor numbers, parking issues, road safety, and risks around open water.

"Co-ordinated patrols during peak periods will bring together police officers, PCSOs, public safety officers, park rangers and parking enforcement teams to provide a visible and coordinated presence in key locations.

"They are designed to both reassure residents and address issues quickly, while also helping visitors enjoy the area safely and responsibly."

Burnsall Park is normally open from Easter to the end of September. It has onsite toilets and parking, and was described as its owners as 'perfect for picnics and a family day out by the river' with visitors paying £12 per car for the day

Skipton MP Sir Julian Smith said: "It is more important than ever that the Yorkshire Dales National Park Authority uses the powers available to it to ensure that local communities, such as Burnsall, are protected from excessive visitor pressures.

"Maintaining a safe and sustainable National Park is just as important as encouraging people to enjoy and visit it, and I hope the Park Authority will work closely with Burnsall Parish Meeting to achieve that balance.

"I have had positive discussions with both North Yorkshire Police and North Yorkshire Council, and I am confident that they stand ready to support the Park Authority in fulfilling its responsibilities to protect local residents and ensure public safety."

The Daggett family, owners of Burnsall Park, said: "We have taken the difficult decision to close the site which has been a popular destination for family picnics and leisure activities for 74 years.

"We would like to thank all our customers during this time for their loyal support.

"The site will be closed for the foreseeable future and until further notice. We will not be commenting any further."
