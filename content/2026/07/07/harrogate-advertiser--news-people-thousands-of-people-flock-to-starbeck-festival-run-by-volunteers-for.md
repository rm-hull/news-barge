# Thousands of people flock to Starbeck Festival run by volunteers for the local community

## A truly community gala in Harrogate has attracted one of its best ever turnouts.

The much-loved Starbeck Festival is the latest evolution in the long and winding history of community events in the area since Starbeck Gala was first held in 1977 with changes in organisation and, even, name.

Last Saturday’s free-to-attend event was the third year this community-led extravaganza had taken place in its current guise as Starbeck Festival.

Held at Belmont Field off Starbeck High Street, this vibrant affair was organised by Sarah Khanye from The Joyful Brew cafe and the Starbeck Festival team of volunteers and attracted hundreds of families and visitors.

The live entertainment on a temporary stage featured performances by local musicians, bands and dance troupes such as Neon Brass and DJ Rory Hoy.

"It was a fantastic event,” said Rory.

"Starbeck Festival has just grown and grown each year.

"There must have been at least a couple of thousand people there.

"The sun shone for everyone and Sarah and her team did an amazing job of putting it all together.”

The event was further boosted by MC-ing by DJ Trev who wished “Starbeck legend” Christine Stewart a happy birthday from the stage.

There were plenty of family activities, including children's entertainment, fairground rides, and arts and crafts.

A wide variety of street food was on offer, representing local independent businesses and community groups.

Please note, DJ Rory Hoy will be appearing this Saturday, July 11 at West Tanfield Sports Day, from 7pm to 9pm.
