A vehicle’s trailer carrying cattle overturned on the A1 in North Yorkshire, the fire service has said.

Emergency services were called to the A1 southbound between the Catterick and Leeming Bar junctions at 10.05am on Tuesday (July 7).

Firefighters helped the farmer and vet move cattle from the damaged trailer into another, North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service said.

Cows were reportedly seen on the road after the crash, which resulted in the stretch of the A1 being closed in both directions for more than two hours.

North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service said two of its crews were called to the single-vehicle collision.

“Crews assisted police on scene and relocated the trailer,” a service spokesperson said.

“Crews then assisted farmer and vet with moving cattle from damaged trailer into another.”

They said the incident was left with police and highways officers.

Yorkshire Ambulance Service said two ambulances were called to the scene “but no patients were conveyed to hospital”.
