A night at the pub is coming to life on stage for a new production in York.

Out of Character Theatre Company will perform TWO by acclaimed playwright Jim Cartwright at The Crescent Community Venue from July 21 to 27, offering audiences a glimpse into the lives of pub regulars through humour and heartbreak.

The show is directed by Kate Veysey and takes place in a Northern pub over the course of a single evening.

Ms Veysey, director, said: "We are really excited to be performing at The Crescent.

"When I was choosing the play for the company, it was the ideal venue to set it, and we hope that the audience will enjoy the experience of sitting around tables with their drinks and seeing all the different scenes taking place around them.

"With our landlord and landlady serving at the bar, to our bouncers ensuring that the evening runs smoothly, to the regular customers letting us into a glimpse of their everyday, this play shows us their worlds all within one evening."

Out of Character Theatre Company is a York-based theatre group and registered charity focused on creating theatre that challenges perceptions of mental health through storytelling.

Tickets are £10 plus a booking fee and can be purchased at thecrescentyork.com.
