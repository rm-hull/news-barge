# 'Extraordinary' new film captures the beauty and history of Knaresborough’s ancient woodland

## Knaresborough’s long history of ancient woodland is to be highlighted in an extraordinary new film this weekend.

Called Knaresborough Forest Son et Lumiere, it’s the work of film production company Fuzzy Duck and Knaresborough Museum Association.

For one day only on Saturday, July 11, the stunning 20-minute film will be shown multiple times for free at Knaresborough House.

Filmed in various locations around Knaresborough, Son et Lumiere includes drone footage of Nidd Gorge, Longlands Common, and Knaresborough Castle and riverside and beautiful photography of local wildlife, wild flowers and trees.

![Fuzzy Duck film production team interviewing Melanie Bell (Knaresborough Photography) who discovered and photographed wildlife during lockdown. (Picture contributed)](https://www.harrogateadvertiser.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOjRhNWQ0NDkyLTBhMzEtNDQ0MC1iMjE3LTMxMWYwYzVhODYwZTo5MDJlMGRmOS01YTdkLTQ5MzUtYWEwOC0yMTI2NTRmYjhhMjM=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



Local people were interviewed including Keith Wilkinson, who received an MBE for his campaigning work to save Nidd Gorge Woods from development, Melanie Bell of Knaresborough Photography. who took photos of local wildlife during lockdown, and members of the Knaresborough Forest Park team such as Chris Kitson who explain their exciting current and future plans.

Son et Lumiere is the story of Knaresborough Forest, a royal hunting forest where local people eeked out a living and monarchs like King John ruled the roost.

It is also the story of our local native woods today.

Knaresborough is lucky enough to have some semi-ancient woodland on our doorstep where rare wildlife thrives.

![Looking forward to premiere of Knaresborough Forest Son et Lumiere - Kathy Allday, Chair of Knaresborough Museum Association, and the Fuzzy Duck film production team. (Picture contributed)](https://www.harrogateadvertiser.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOjUyNzE4NjhiLTUzZDQtNGNiOS1iMzViLWU5NjM5ZThjOGNkNTo0YzAwYWIyMy0yNDUzLTRiODctODY3My01YmE2MDg3YWRmODU=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



In Nidd Gorge, the secretive woodcock can be found, where otters have returned to the River Nidd.

Five different types of deer can be found in Nidderdale, and Pikeshaw Woods is rich in rare fungi.

The film is blessed by impressive special effects involving the animation of still images including paintings by James McKay of Knaresborough 8,000 years ago when the land was 95% forest.

The event is supported by David Skaith, Mayor of York and North Yorkshire and funded by York and North Yorkshire Combined Authority.

![Fuzzy Duck interviewed local people for the new film Knaresborough Forest Son et Lumiere, including Chris Kitson and Nick Brewis of Knaresborough Forest Park. This Saturday’s event will see the film projected on the wall and ceiling of Knaresborough House’s Assembly room creating an immersive experience.

It will explain why our woods are under threat and how our future depends on their protection.

Hourly screenings will take place at 10am, noon, 1pm, 2pm, 3pm, and 4pm.

There will also be art installations, stalls, a raffle and refreshments.
