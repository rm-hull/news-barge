## Boost for community campaign to buy North Yorkshire village pub

The campaign fighting to take-over the Golden Lion in Helperby. *(Image: Pic supplied)*

A meeting is to be held in a North Yorkshire village this weekend on how the community can take over its local pub.

Campaigners have been fundraising for more than a year to buy the Golden Lion in Helperby, between Boroughbridge and Easingwold.

The pub has been closed since September 2024, following the death of its landlord, 70-year-old David Lea, who was tragically killed in crash on West Moor Road, Raskelf, earlier that month.

Admiral Taverns placed the pub on the market for £295,000 last February, before reducing the price to £250,000 and now £225,000, using agents Everard Cole.

The reduction gives a boost the Community Interest Company that has launched a share offer, which has raised over £100,000 so far.

In recognition of its importance to the community, North Yorkshire Council recently renewed the Golden Lion’s status as an Asset of Community Value, which it has had since January 2021.

Lizzie Wignall, secretary of the Golden Lion Pub and Hub Community Benefit Society, says a meeting is planned for 11am on Saturday at Helperby Village Hall.

  The Golden Lion at Helperby is now on the market for £225,000. Villagers meet to discuss its future on Saturday. 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

Lizzie told the Press: “We have planned a village meeting to outline what we have been up to and to lay out the options on the table.

“We have raised over £100,000 from shareholders. We are very conscious we have people’s money. We want to lay out all the options possible and give the village the opportunity to see where we are at.”

Lizzie estimates that ‘realistically’ another £125,000 will need to be raised to buy the pub at its current asking price. In addition, at least £50,000-£60,000 will need to be spent on a refurbishment.

She said: “If we get the pub bought, we open the doors to grant funding.

“It’s been year since we have been inside it, but we have had a survey done.”

Lizzie added that she hopes the meeting will also attract new shareholders.
