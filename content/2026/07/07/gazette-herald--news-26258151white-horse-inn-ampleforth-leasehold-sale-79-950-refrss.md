A highly-regarded traditional village pub on the edge of the North Yorkshire Moors has gone on the market.

Barry Crux & Co is retailing the leasehold of the White Horse Inn at Ampleforth for £79,950.

The 18th Century pub with three letting rooms scores highly on the review websites.

TripAdvisor awards it 4.8 stars out of five, based on 335 reviews, ranking it first out of two Ampleforth restaurants. Google awards 4.7 stars out of five, based on 223 reviews.

The agents said: “The property offers characterful ground floor trading areas, including a main bar, snug and restaurant space, with traditional features such as exposed beams, timber and flagged flooring, and open fires.

“To the first floor are three well-appointed ensuite letting bedrooms, together with a small residents’ lounge and manager’s accommodation.

“Externally, the property benefits from rear car parking for approximately 20 vehicles, outside seating and useful storage.

“The business is operated on free-of-tie lease terms and offers scope for new operators to further develop trade in this attractive village and visitor location.”

The sales particulars say the restaurant area is arranged across two separate rooms, with each having room for 12 covers. The bar area also has room for 27 dining covers.

There are also toilers and a catering kitchen with stainless fitments and extraction, a washup area and an upland beer store area.

The first floor has three en-suite double letting rooms, with a small guests’ lounge. There is also a small manager’s flat which comprises of a kitchen diner, bathroom and bedroom.

Outside is parking for 20 cars and outside seating areas.

The pub is presently owned by Ali Moran and partner Lydia, who also have the Blacksmith Arms in nearby Lastingham.

The agents explain the business concentrates heavily on its food offering, and whilst there is reasonable drinking trade from the village, most ‘wet’ sales are ancillary to the food. The rooms also generate an income.

The sales particulars continued: “Our clients have decided to dispose of their leasehold interest due to other business commitments.

“By their own admission, they are not pushing the business as hard as they would like due to lack of time. The pub currently only trades limited hours, being closed from early evening on Sunday through to Wednesday lunchtime.

They added: “While they are open year-round, trade is more seasonal due to its location and busier during the spring, summer and autumn months when there is a large influx of visitors to the area.

“The business recorded a healthy profit during its most recent trading year end of February 2026. More detailed trading information will be provided to interested parties after viewing the premises.”
