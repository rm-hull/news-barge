# Sen. Mitch McConnell’s office goes silent following 84-year-old’s health emergency

- Sen. Mitch McConnell has been hospitalized since June 14 after paramedics responded to his home for an unconscious person, with dispatch audio indicating CPR for cardiac arrest.
- His office states he is “improving” and working with staff, but detailed updates on the condition of the 84-year-old former GOP Senate leader have been scarce, fueling speculation in Washington, D.C.
- Far-right activist Laura Loomer, who has connections to Trump's White House, amplified unverified claims that McConnell is “brain dead” amid the lack of official updates.
- McConnell, whose health has been visibly declining, has recently broken with Trump and the White House on key issues, including opposing funding for January 6 rioters and certain Cabinet appointments.
- His prolonged absence creates difficulties for Republican Senate leadership, particularly Majority Leader John Thune, as the party navigates internal divisions and upcoming midterm elections.
