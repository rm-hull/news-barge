A former dormitory in York is set to be transformed into a creative space.

The special exhibition, featuring work from more than 60 artists and makers from across North Yorkshire and beyond, will transform the dormitories of Ampleforth Abbey into a temporary gallery from July 11 to 19.

The public will be able to explore paintings, ceramics, sculpture, jewellery, millinery, printmaking, and more — all on display and available for purchase.

The exhibition is a collaboration with North Yorkshire Open Studios (NYOS), an artist-run collective that promotes creative talent throughout the region.

  Kate Pettitt, Petrichor 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Open Studios)*

It will take place inside the recently restored Alban Roe House Visitor Centre, a former preparatory boarding house for Ampleforth College.

Garth Bayley, project manager at NYOS, said: "We are thrilled to bring this showcase to the truly unique setting of Ampleforth Abbey.

"We will be transforming the old dormitories into mini-galleries for our artists, giving visitors an incredibly intimate way to interact with the art while exploring a beautifully restored piece of North Yorkshire history."

Featured artists include York’s Andrew Bloodsworth, whose work spans oil, ink, and print, and explores both figurative and abstract scenes of the natural and built world.

His pieces often reflect the weathered charm of historic buildings.

From Scotton, ceramicist Annette Cole will present textured bird baths, planters, and garden sculptures.

Her work combines earthy ceramics with touches of bright turquoise glaze and warm, textured surfaces.

  Justine Warner, Forest of Autumn Light 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Open Studios)*

Hutton-le-Hole’s Alice O'Neill, a muralist and former hand colourist and paper restorer, will also feature.

Her layered mixed-media pieces are complemented by her experience designing theatrical backdrops.

Ampleforth Abbey has been home to a Benedictine monastic community since 1802 and remains a working monastery that welcomes visitors year-round.

Catherine Hodgson, head of marketing at Ampleforth Abbey, said: "We are delighted to be hosting the North Yorkshire Open Studios exhibition at Ampleforth Abbey once again, as part of our commitment to supporting local and independent artists.

"We would encourage everyone to support this celebration of art and creativity in North Yorkshire."

Visitors are invited to make a day of the exhibition by exploring the wider Abbey grounds, which include new waymarked walking trails and an orchard of more than 1,500 apple trees.

  Zosia Olenska Anderson, Rievaulx 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Open Studios)*

Guided tours of the Grade I-listed Abbey Church, designed by Sir Giles Gilbert Scott, are available on Thursdays and Sundays at 2pm.

The church features a striking domed ceiling, vibrant stained glass, and offers insight into the abbey’s history and the monastic way of life.

The Ampleforth exhibition is one of several events planned by NYOS for 2026.

The collective’s recent Summer Open Studios welcomed thousands of visitors to artists’ workspaces across North Yorkshire.

A Winter Open Studios event is scheduled for November 7 and 8.
