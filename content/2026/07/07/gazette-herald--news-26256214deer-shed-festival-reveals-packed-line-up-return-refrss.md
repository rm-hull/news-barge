A family-friendly festival in North Yorkshire will return this month with a bumper line-up of music, comedy, and immersive experiences.

Deer Shed Festival takes place from July 24 to 26 at Baldersby Park in Topcliffe, North Yorkshire, now in its 16th year, and is expected to sell out for the second year running.

Friday will see New York 'discodelic' soul trio Say She She headline the main stage in their UK Festival headline debut.

Other acts performing on the opening day include indie rockers Bar Italia, rapper Murkage Dave, and synth-pop band Formal Sppeedwear.

  Deer Shed Festival takes place from July 24 to 26 

*(Image: James Drury)*

DEADLETTER, Boko Yout, and Bill Ryder-Jones are also on the bill, while comedy fans can look forward to a headlining set from satirical comedian and Mash Report star Rachel Parris.

On Saturday, Sleaford Mods will take to the main stage, joined by special guests Fat Dog, indie-rock songwriter Jasmine.4.t, and rising drum ’n’ bass MC OneDa.

The day also features post-punk quartet LIFE, grunge-pop favourite Gretel, and performances from Working Men’s Club, Gans, Joshua Idehen, and Tenderness.

The comedy tent will be headlined by Stewart Lee, with support from Jessica Fostekew and others.

Sunday’s headliners are Manchester prog-pop band Everything Everything.

They’ll be joined by Scottish indie-rockers The Twilight Sad, seven-piece Afro-psych group BCUC, Brighton’s Lime Garden, Scots folk band Brògeal, and singer-songwriter Dominie Hooper.

  The comedy tent will be headlined by Stewart Lee 

*(Image: Andrew Benge)*

Also performing on Sunday are Getdown Services, Sorry, Katherine Priddy, and Brown Horse.

Sunday’s comedy line-up includes broadcaster and comedian Adam Buxton and musical comedy duo Jonny & The Baptists.

Megan Evans, Deer Shed Festival Director, said: "It’s very exciting to be sharing the stage splits for Deer Shed 2026 with you as it means we’re almost there.

"Seeing everything and everyone we’ve booked on one page is a reminder that this is one of our most ambitious years yet, with musical heroes like Sleaford Mods and Everything Everything rubbing shoulders with the likes of incredible new acts like Boko Yout and Brown Horse whilst the mighty Stewart Lee shares the comedy bill with Lekiddo, Lord of the Lobsters.

"Whether you’re an old festival friend joining us for another weekend of wonder or it’ll be your first time in Baldersby Park, we look forward to welcoming you for what’s shaping up to be our most a-MAZE-ing weekend to date."

  New additions to the 2026 programme include Deer Shed’s biggest ever Wilderwild Retreat 

*(Image: Andrew Benge)*

New additions to the 2026 programme include Deer Shed’s biggest ever Wilderwild Retreat, late-night events, conversations, and fresh comedy.

Family-friendly outdoor activities are also on offer, from wild swimming and kayaking to the Synth Shed.

There, festival-goers will have the chance to build an 808 drum machine kick circuit from scratch.

Organisers have issued a final call for live-in vehicle and on-site bookings.
