A hospice in Scarborough is looking for new trustees to help shape its future and expand its services.

Saint Catherine’s Hospice in Throxenby Lane is seeking volunteers for its Board of Trustees.

Trustees play a key role in helping to set the organisation's strategic direction and ensure effective governance.

Margaret Middlebrook, chairman of the board, said: "As we respond to the evolving needs of our community, we are expanding our services whilst remaining committed to delivering specialist care in our palliative and end of life care unit."

The hospice has recently introduced new services including respite care, a holiday flat, a bathing service, and a sensory room.

Saint Catherine’s is looking for trustees with experience in areas such as healthcare, legal services, property, finance, marketing, HR, and IT.

Responsibilities include contributing to organisational strategy, ensuring compliance with legal and financial standards, supporting the executive team, and representing the hospice in the community.

The expected time commitment is around one to two days per month.

Several trustee positions are available, including opportunities for first-time trustees.

The hospice will host an open session on Wednesday, July 15 at 10.15am for prospective applicants to meet the chairman, chief executive, and executive team.

Those wishing to attend should email [human.resources@saintcatherines.org.uk.](mailto:human.resources@saintcatherines.org.uk.)
