A topless man reportedly threatened someone with a machete-style knife during an affray in York.

The incident allegedly happened in the Bell Farm area, with North Yorkshire Police saying it began in the car park at Birch Close, near South Beck, before continuing onto Bell Farm Avenue and Byland Avenue at about 5.20pm on Saturday, July 18.

It was reported that a man was threatened with a large machete-style knife at Birch Close following an argument, but no injuries have been confirmed.

A 35-year-old man was arrested in connection with the incident and has been released on conditional bail while enquiries continue.

The suspect was described as a bald, white man who was topless and wearing beige trousers and a gold chain.

Following the incident, he is said to have fled into the Bell Farm estate, reportedly running through gardens.

Police say they currently believe there is no wider risk to the community and that those involved are known to one another.

Officers are particularly keen to hear from anyone who witnessed the incident in the Birch Close car park or surrounding area, or who has CCTV, doorbell, dashcam or other video footage of it.

They would also like to speak to any residents who may have seen, or captured footage of, a topless man running through or across their property in the Bell Farm estate.

Anyone with information that could help the investigation is asked, in the first instance, to contact police online by email to [Oscar.Stevenson@northyorkshire.police.uk](mailto:Oscar.Stevenson@northyorkshire.police.uk), via the North Yorkshire Police website.

Those unable to use these options can call North Yorkshire Police on 101.

Alternatively, information can be provided anonymously to Crimestoppers via their website or by calling 0800 555 111. Please quote reference 12260137102 when getting in touch.
