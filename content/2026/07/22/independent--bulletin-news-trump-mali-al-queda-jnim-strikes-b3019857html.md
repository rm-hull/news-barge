# Trump admin reportedly considering military strikes in Mali against al-Qaeda-linked group

- Donald Trump’s administration is reportedly considering military action against an al-Qaeda-linked group in Mali, which could be the eighth country targeted by the president since he returned to office last year.
- JNIM has claimed responsibility for numerous deadly attacks and kidnappings since 2017, including recent assaults in Mali and Niger, while terrorizing Mali's interior with blockades and sieges.
- Mali, a junta-controlled nation, has largely cut Western ties and relies on Russian forces for security, a partnership the administration criticizes as ineffective against terrorism.
- Administration figures, including Sebastian Gorka, are reportedly advocating for strikes, while the White House urges African nations to purchase U.S. equipment and services, according to *The Washington Post.*
- Experts warn that a military solution may not resolve the conflict, noting that previous interventions by France and Russia have failed to improve the security situation in the region.
