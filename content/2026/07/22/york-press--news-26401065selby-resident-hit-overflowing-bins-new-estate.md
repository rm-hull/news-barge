Search the Public Notice PortalA SELBY resident has hit out after dog poo bins have been left overflowing for weeks on a new build estate in the town.

Andy Wilmer - who lives in the Stainer Hall estate in Selby - has hit out at the housing developers Persimmon after bins, including dog waste facilities, have reportedly been left unemptied leading to rodents and unsanitary conditions.

The estate, which Andy has lived on for around a year, is still being developed and is run by Permission instead of North Yorkshire Council – who are required to empty bins and ensure facilities are up to date.

Andy claims that this is something that is not being done and that there is only one bin for rubbish outside the local shop, which fills up quickly with excess items spilling over.

He said: “They’ve just got no regards for the residents here. They’re more than happy to take people’s money, but not actually help keep the estate looking good. It gets better for a few weeks, then just goes back to how it was.”

He added that the bins had started to attract rats and that residents had formed groups to go around and pick up litter.

A Persimmon Yorkshire spokesperson said “Additional bins were installed at Staynor Hall going beyond what was required under the planning permission for the development.

“We continue to work with North Yorkshire Council to progress adoption and long-term management of the site upon completion.

“In the meantime, a new contractor has recently been appointed to manage waste across the development with bins being emptied twice a week.”
