Samsung's second big phone launch of the year is almost here — and while the leaks have given us lots of hints, we're here to untangle all of the official news on the its new foldables and smartwatches. And who knows, maybe Samsung will have a surprise in store too.

According to the rumors, it's going to be a particularly big day for foldables. We're expecting to see the Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 8, Galaxy Z Flip 8, , and Galaxy Z Fold 8 Ultra all make their debuts — and the Z Fold 8 could also be a sneak peak of what to expect from the incoming iPhone Ultra.

But if you've been waiting for a sequel to the Samsung Galaxy Watch Ultra or Galaxy Watch 8, it'll worth tuning in for those too. Both are expected to get successors today, when the event kicks off at 9am ET / 6am PT / 2pm BST / 11pm AEST on July 22.

It's time to move onto smartwatches. Samsung's just revealed the Galaxy Watch 9, and it's promising some handy new software features.

These include sleep apnea detection, which is also coming to the Galaxy Ring — and apparently makes it the only FDA-cleared, over-the-counter ring device with that feature.

The Galaxy Watch 9 will also have "proactive" features that constantly monitor things like heart rate and heart rate variability, and crunch all these together into a new heart health score.

Samsung says it's partnering with iFit for its subscription fitness content, too. But the more exciting smartwatch is coming next...

This should be the final foldable from today's Unpacked — Samsung's Lee Dunham has just announced the Z Flip 8, and it sounds like a decent upgrade.

There's seemingly a much better Flex Display experience, with boosts to Now Brief (including the option of adding your own custom cards). And it's a bit slimmer and lighter at only 6.1mm thick when folded, and weighing in at 180g. You also get the same ProVisual engine as you get on Samsung's other Galaxy phones.

## Samsung's first smart glasses sound like Ray-Ban Meta rivals

 ![The Ray-Ban Meta Smart Glasses sat on a shelf](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/vwUHdMHmSzXBbvHek3F3U5.jpg) 


Samsung could be about to make smart glasses more mainstream, as its first pair is rumored to be included in today's announcements, and the Samsung Galaxy Glasses as we're calling them for now have been extensively leaked.

A detailed new leak on WinFuture tallies with what we've heard before, including that these apparently won't have a screen, and will instead rely on cameras and access to Gemini, which should be able to see what you can.

They sound a lot like the Ray-Ban Meta glasses, and like those they apparently use a Snapdragon AR1 Gen 1 chipset, which is now three years old. Still, with supposedly 9 hours of battery life, Gemini access, and a big brand name behind them, they could be a hit.

## The Galaxy Watch Ultra 2 might live up to its name

 ![Samsung Galaxy Watch Ultra](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/bvps36A4orNceSHvAmFEv.jpg) 


We're also expecting various wearables today, and WinFuture has spilled the beans on the Samsung Galaxy Watch 9 and Samsung Galaxy Watch Ultra 2 in a detailed leak.

The latter could have a slightly larger and much brighter display than its predecessor, coming in at 1.52 inches and 5,000 nits, and joined by a colossal — by smartwatch standards — 800mAh battery, along with a build that's designed with divers in mind.

This is sure to be expensive, but the standard Samsung Galaxy Watch 9 should be a cheaper option for anyone who doesn't need all the bells and whistles offered by the Ultra. Sadly though we're not expecting a new Classic model this year.

## All the specs have already leaked

 ![Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7 REVIEW](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/bbt42jE3HjkgqDu5qyuCD8.jpg) 


Samsung might not have too many surprises today if you've been following the leaks and rumors, as WinFuture.de recently leaked complete specs lists for the Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 8, Z Fold 8 Ultra, and Z Flip 8.

Highlights if this leak is accurate include a Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 chipset for the two Z Folds and — at least in Europe — an Exynos 2600 for the Galaxy Z Flip 8.

We'll also apparently get a higher-capacity and faster charging 5,000mAh / 45W battery in the Z Fold 8 Ultra, but the real highlight could be the base Z Fold 8, since this is widely expected to have a new shorter, wider form factor, which according to this leak will allow for a 7.6-inch foldable screen and a 5.5-inch cover display.

## A self-healing crease?

Samsung's upcoming foldables might not just have smaller creases than their predecessors, they could also have self-healing ones if the latest leak (above) is to be believed.

It's unclear how this would work, but perhaps it would ensure the crease doesn't become more visible over time. It also reminds us of the self-healing finish on the LG G Flex series, which could automatically fill in minor scratches.

It will be interesting to see how Samsung's take on this works, but if this leak is accurate then it could be Samsung's secret weapon against the upcoming foldable iPhone Ultra.

## Brace yourself for price hikes

 ![Someone using the Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7 when unfolded, with three separate apps open at once.](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/ZFsHn3P6MntgAXkjMetkuL.jpg) 


Some slightly less positive news from today's Galaxy Unpacked 2026 event is likely to be price hikes for the Z Fold 8 and Z Flip 8.
