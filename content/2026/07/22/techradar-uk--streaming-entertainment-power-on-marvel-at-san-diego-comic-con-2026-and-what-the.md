The 2026 edition of San Diego Comic-Con (SDCC) is almost upon us, and you know what that means: all manner of rumors and speculation about the surprises Marvel might have in store for us at its now-customary Hall H panel.

To a certain extent, the biggest reveal has already happened. Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) diehards had expected the first official trailer for *Avengers: Doomsday* to be the focal point of the comic book titan's next presentation. With*Avengers: Doomsday*'s main trailer released on Monday (July 20), though, Marvel must have something just as significant around which to center its forthcoming panel on July 25 at 5:30pm PT.

As attentions turn towards the Disney subsidiary's hour-long address at SDCC 2026, chief among fans' immediate thoughts is: what big reveal could Marvel make that a) would rival *Avengers: Doomsday*'s first proper trailer as a major talking point, and b) is so noteworthy that Marvel felt confident enough to unveil* Doomsday*'s trailer earlier than expected?

 ![A press image showing the X-Men walking towards the screen in their 2000 movie](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/GLipm9LFHbRihqok2ZLa6.jpg) 


One project that fits the bill is Marvel's new X-Men movie. The only post-Multiverse Saga film in active development, myriad rumors are circulating online about who could star in the mutant-led flick. You can learn more about which actors Marvel is reportedly talking to via my Marvel's X-Men movie guide.

For what it's worth, industry insiders, albeit ones with sketchy track records, have claimed there'll be no such cast reveals at SDCC 2026. My reading of the situation is that'll be the case, too, especially as Marvel will want to focus to stay on the rest of its 2026 line-up — I'll discuss this in more detail shortly — and the projects it'll release in 2027, such as *Daredevil: Born Again* season 3 and*Avengers: Secret Wars*. We're not expecting the new X-Men movie to arrive until the following year.

That said, I'd be surprised if Marvel doesn't say *something* about the MCU's first X-Men film. Speculation abounds that the comic titan might tentatively discuss other Marvel Phase 7 movies, such as*Thunderbolts 2*,* The Fantastic Four: First Steps*sequel,* Black Panther 3*,* Nova*, and* Midnight Sons* — the last of which might repurpose Marvel's long-gestating plans to introduce Mahershala Ali's Blade to the MCU. It would be bizarre, then, if that quintet is covered, but not the first film of what'll be version 2.0 of the MCU.

 ![Doctor Victor von Doom stopping an attack from Thor with Stormbreaker in Avengers: Doomsday](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/GVXH8jEpgzPWh5xmWvZ4Za.jpg) 


But, onto more pressing matters — namely, the rest of Marvel's 2026 movie and TV slate.

On the film side of things, *Spider-Man: Brand New Day*, which swings into theaters between July 29 and 31 (depending on where you live), is expected to have a big presence at SDCC 2026. I'm willing to bet that attendees will be treated to some exclusive first-look footage, but any spoiler-based content, such as the identity of Sadie Sink's much-discussed mystery character in* Spider-Man 4*, won't be part of proceedings. Could we also get an early* Spider-Man 5* announcement? As a huge Spidey fan, I'd be overjoyed if we did.

As for *Avengers: Doomsday*, I believe Marvel will show off some new clips from one of this year's most anticipated new movies, with the most likely candidates being extended looks at shots from its first main trailer. Where its sequel in* Secret Wars* is concerned, I think we'll get a name-drop and the briefest, spoiler-free hints about its story, but that's all.

Pre-SDCC 2026 chatter about Marvel's forthcoming Disney+ shows is much harder to read. After all, the studio has regularly reserved its biggest TV announcements for D23 Expo (Disney's flagship fan event, which takes place annually in Anaheim, California), so it stands to reason that there won't be a change in strategy this year.

Nevertheless, I predict we'll get some details about its final two TV series of the year — *VisionQuest* and*Your Friendly Neighborhood Spider-Man*(* YFNSM*) season 2 — at SDCC.

 ![White Vision looking intrigued in WandaVision](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/BUv2yvpUKdovByvCbmnio.jpg) 


With *VisionQuest*, I foresee confirmation of its main cast and a behind-closed-doors look at the Paul Bettany-led live-action program. Whether that'll be a full first trailer or a more general sneak peek remains to be seen, but I wouldn't be surprised if, as long as it's the former,* VisionQuest*'s first trailer* isn't* publicly released following its possible unveiling at SDCC.

The same is true of *YFNSM* season 2. I'm of the opinion that Marvel's animated Spider-Man show will be highlighted in greater detail at D23 Expo 2026. That said, I'm fairly confident that Marvel will also deliver some news about it. Is it too much to ask for an official launch date? Not in my book.

Meanwhile, Marvel has already confirmed that it's full steam ahead on season 3 of its critically-acclaimed animated series *X-Men 97*. Will we hear more about it shortly? Not in my view. We've still got three more episodes of* X-Men 97* season 2 to enjoy before it goes off the air, so any conversations about its follow-up season — especially those of a spoiler-based persuasion — will have to wait until D23 Expo 2026 or this year's New York Comic-Con.

Finally, will we hear anything substantial about *Wonder Man* season 2? That's similarly unlikely. Co-creator Destin Daniel Cretton has been incredibly busy with directing *Spider-Man: Brand New Day.* Although Cretton recently said that the cameras could roll on *Wonder Man*'s next chapter as early as next year, this year's D23 Expo feels like a better fit for any major announcements for this series, as well as* Marvel Zombies* season 2, the company's first R-rated animated program.
