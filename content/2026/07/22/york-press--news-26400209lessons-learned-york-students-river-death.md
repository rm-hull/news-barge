Family members of a 19-year-old student who drowned in a York river have said they hope lessons can be learned from his death.

Evan d’Andilly drowned after entering the River Ouse from Ouse Bridge four days before his 20th birthday on March 5, 2025, an inquest heard.

In a narrative conclusion, coroner Jonathan Leach said police, fire crews and the York Rescue Boat were called to the scene but Evan’s life “could not be saved”.

  Evan D’Andilly who drowned after entering the River Ouse in York on March 5, 2025 

*(Image: Family)*

During the inquest, concerns were raised by Evan’s family members about the more than 10 minutes it took to launch the York Rescue Boat to get to Evan.

Evan had already been in the freezing water for 10 minutes when the rescue boat was called, the inquest heard.

The hearing was told Evan, who had drifted into the middle of the river after entering the water, stayed above the water for 20 minutes but throw lines were not long enough to reach him.

  Police search the River Ouse in York for Evan d'Andilly on March 5, 2025 

*(Image: Newsquest)*

It was also told that North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue relies on the volunteer-run York Rescue Boat for water rescues in York, as the fire service’s nearest rescue boats are in Selby and Malton.

The fire service requested its boat from Selby to help rescue Evan, but the boat did not arrive until 3.37am – 14 minutes after the last sighting of Evan.

Speaking outside Northallerton Coroners’ Court on Wednesday (July 22), Evan’s father, Gordon d’Andilly, said there was “no doubt” that Evan could still be alive if other assets, such as a York-based fire rescue boat, were available to the emergency services.

  Gordon d'Andilly and his fiancé Teresa Widd speak to the media outside Northallerton Coroners' Court on Wednesday (July 22) 

*(Image: Dylan Connell)*

North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service said it has improved its emergency equipment since Evan's death.

Mr d’Andilly stressed that he had “nothing but praise and thanks” for the emergency services who worked to rescue his son but said: “In relation to the assets that are available [to the emergency services], my opinion differs. It’s become apparent that the resources that first of all the police thought were available, aren’t there.

“By that, I mean the [fire service’s] York rescue boat that was previously in York was decommissioned a number of years before.”

He questioned why the fire service did not have its own rescue boat stationed in York, saying: "If you’ve got an issue in your house with the plumbing and there’s a leak downstairs but you have the fittings upstairs, you’re going to move the fittings to where the actual issue is.

“York is an issue … That’s difficult to live with.”

Mr d’Andilly’s fiancée, Teresa Widd, said it was revealed during the inquest that York’s fire crews are only trained for riverbank rescues, meaning they are not trained to enter the water for rescues.

She added that the fire service’s own rescue boats are based in other areas such as Malton and Selby.

“People can’t survive for long [in cold water],” she said. “So why are those resources so far away from York?”

Mr d’Andilly added: “I hope lessons can be learned and this inquest can service its purpose – preventing further families from having to go through what we’ve had to.

“We miss Evan terribly, and we will love him always.”

He urged anyone with children to “give your kids a hug”.

  Lorraine d'Andilly speaks to the media outside Northallerton Coroners' Court on Wednesday (July 22)

Speaking after the inquest, Evan’s mother, Lorraine d’Andilly, said Evan was “so much more than the circumstances of his death”.

“He was an awesome human being,” she said. “Kind, loving, generous and incredibly funny.

“He brought joy to so many people and he is deeply loved and deeply missed every single day.

“He filled our lives with laughter and his death has left a space that can never be filled.

“He had so much life ahead of him and he should still be with us today.”

## 'Evan’s life mattered and we remain committed to ensuring that his legacy is one of learning, compassion and positive change'

She added: “Nothing can bring Evan back – no finding today can ever lessen the pain of losing him or undo the devastation that our family has endured.

“But acknowledging what went wrong is an essential step towards accountability.

“And, more importantly, towards preventing another family from suffering the same heartbreak.

“Evan’s life mattered and we remain committed to ensuring that his legacy is one of learning, compassion and positive change," she continued. “We would like to thank everyone who has stood beside us during this horrendous time.”

A North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service spokesperson said: "Our thoughts remain with the family and friends of Evan D'Andilly following the conclusion of the inquest into his death. We recognise the profound loss they have suffered and the impact this tragedy has had on those who responded and on the wider community.

"We remain committed to working with our partners to learn from incidents, reduce risk and help keep people safe around North Yorkshire and York's rivers and waterways.

"Water safety is a shared responsibility, and alongside ongoing prevention campaigns and community education, we continue to strengthen our response capability.

"North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service operates six Swift Water Rescue stations and a powered rescue boat based in Selby, with resources positioned across the county to respond to flooding and water rescue emergencies.

"In York, we have recently enhanced our provision through additional bankside extended pole and reach lines and the imminent rollout of Fast Rescue Deployment Devices (FReDs) to York and Acomb stations.

"Together with the specialist capabilities provided by York Rescue Boat in conjunction with the Service, these improvements strengthen the response available along the city's waterways."

A North Yorkshire Police spokesperson said: “First and foremost, our thoughts remain with the family and friends of Evan d’Andilly.

“The tragic death of Evan is felt deeply by all our officers and staff who, under extremely difficult circumstances, tried to save him.

“The findings of the inquest will be carefully reviewed by all the emergency services and partner agencies involved in the response.”
