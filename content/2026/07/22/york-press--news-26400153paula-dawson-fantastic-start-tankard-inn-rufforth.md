## 'Emotional' reopening of village pub near York as locals queue to get in at noon

A warm welcome from Paula Dawson  landlady of the Tankard Inn at Rufforth. *(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

They were queueing up to get into a village pub near York at lunchtime today for an ‘emotional’ reopening.

With its doors open just a few minutes later than the planned 12-noon, customers at the Tankard Inn were warmly welcomed by landlady Paula Dawson, who said the delay was caused by getting the card machine ready.

Around eight or so entered straight away and Paula and a staffer began serving and a front room began filling up.

Among the drinkers Barbara Bradshaw said she was “over the moon” to see the Tankard Inn back open after being closed for nearly three years.

Barbara told the Press: “The village needs it. I’m from York and for years my husband used to captain the cricket team here. It was the place to go.”

She continued: “It’s a brilliant social set up, plus the village has been needing it.”

Lyndsay Bradshaw said: “I’m here to support Paula. We have been friends and neighbours for many years. My parents knew her parents when they had the Yorkshire Arms [in York, which is now the Whippet Inn].”

  Michael Eastwood with Paula Dawson at the entrance to the Tankard 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

“Paula will be in her element here. I know from her parties at home. She’s the best hostess.”

Jill and Jon Green had walked all the way from Askam Bryan, several miles away.

Jill said: “We have always said it would be nice to have a drink in this pub when we do this walk. The re-opening is brilliant for the village.”

Jon agreed: “It’s great to have somewhere to stop off at. We like walking. This is a great place to break up the journey.”

Michael Eastwood from Acomb said: “I think it’s great to see the pub back open. Every village should have a pub. It’s a community centre and meeting place.”

Michael was pleased to see Samuel Smith’s Brewery opening more pubs.

  Jill and Jon Green enjoy a drink in the sun 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

“Too many village pubs have closed. I came from Skelton originally. When its pub closed, it was sorely missed, but thankfully it is back open.”

So as not to break brewery rules on digital devices, we took photos outside. A sign on an inside wall said, ‘no swearing.’ Though for one quick shot, we stood in the front doorway.

A tearful Paula, who previously ran Paula’s Kitchen café, in Mill Lane at the other side of the village, said the opening was ‘fantastic.’

Initially, the Tankard Inn will be drinks only, but in the next few weeks, Paula plans to offer food, starting with Sunday roast. Her husband will run the café.

  The exterior of The Tankard at Rufforth 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Paula said: “I want to thank the brewery for letting me do it, giving me a chance to prove it.

“I feel very emotional. My mum and dad ran pubs. I just wish they were both here to see me.”

Paula also wanted to thank her family for helping her get the Tankard back open.

“It’s just emotional,” she added.

The reopening comes as Samuel Smith’s Brewery is stepping up its efforts to reopen its pubs- around half of its 300 or so estate currently stands empty.

Among them, The Wellington Inn on Alma Terrace, Fishergate, is now said to be reopening in August, instead of late July as originally planned.
