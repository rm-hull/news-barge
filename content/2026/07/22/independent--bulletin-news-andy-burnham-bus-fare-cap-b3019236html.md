The government has announced that single bus fares across England will be capped at £2 from January.

This initiative aims to provide affordable transport links and offer financial relief to people amidst the cost of living crisis.

The scheme is backed by £454 million in funding, with £400 million to come from switching investment in international climate finance to loans.

The total cost is projected to exceed £500 million, with the remaining funds sourced from the Department for Transport's existing bus budget.

While a national £3 cap is currently in effect until March 2027, some regions, including Greater Manchester, already implement lower single ticket fares.
