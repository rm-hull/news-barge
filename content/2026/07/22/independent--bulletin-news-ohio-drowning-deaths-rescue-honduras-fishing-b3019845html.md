# Two couples visiting from Honduras identified as Ohio river drowning victims

- Four individuals, identified as two married couples from Honduras, drowned in the Scioto River in Ohio on Sunday.
- The victims, Miguel Ángel Guerra, Carmen Idalia Lara Ferrera, José Mario Pineda, and Marina Suyapa Regalado Miranda, were part of a group on a fishing trip.
- They reportedly entered the river to assist a struggling swimmer and were subsequently swept away by the high and fast-moving current.
- Two children, believed to be the children of Pineda and Miranda, are currently under the care of Ohio officials and are expected to be reunited with family in Honduras.
- Authorities have issued warnings about the dangers of river currents and emphasized the importance of knowing one's swimming abilities before entering the water.
