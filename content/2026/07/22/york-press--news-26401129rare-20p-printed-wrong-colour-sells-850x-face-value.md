## Rare 20p that's the 'wrong colour' sells for more than 800 times its face value

A 20p coin with a printing error has sold for more than £170 on eBay *(Image: Getty)*

A rare 20p coin has sold for 851 times its face value as it was printed in the wrong colour.

The "highly unusual" 1983 coin sold for £170.20 on eBay because it was minted with a rare error.

The coin has been struck on a bronze blank rather than the standard 20p blank.

As a result of the striking error, the coin appears bronze in colour, rather than silver.

The coin is accompanied by a letter from The Royal Mint Museum, who authenticated the coin following a 'thorough examination' using 'spectographic analysis'.

They found the error is likely to have happened as a result of a bronze blank coin being struck between two 20p dies.

It's thought to be one of two known 20p coins to have been struck on a bronze blank.

In the listing, the coin is described as "highly unusual" with the error making its value skyrocket.

The listing reads: "Rare 1983 20p Royal Mint striking error, struck on a bronze blank rather than the standard 20p blank.
