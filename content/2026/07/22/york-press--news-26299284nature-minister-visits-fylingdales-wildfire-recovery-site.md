A government minister has visited the North York Moors to see how recovery work is progressing following last summer’s devastating Fylingdales wildfire.

Nature Minister Mary Creagh visited the site nearly a year after the wildfire to meet with members of the National Park Authority and North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service.

Cllr Jenny Kent, environment and climate portfolio holder for the York and North Yorkshire Combined Authority (YNYCA), said: "It was inspiring to be able to show our minister for nature Mary Creagh MP the vital protection and restoration work already achieved on some of our most sensitive ecological and archaeological sites, made possible with mayoral funding."

The wildfire, declared a major incident on August 12 last year, was the largest in the national park’s history, covering approximately 20 square kilometres of moorland.

A major recovery project led by the North York Moors National Park is now underway, supported by more than £5 million in government funding.

The funding will go towards restoring peatland, repairing firebreaks, reinstating public rights of way, and improving the moors' resilience to future wildfires.

Briony Fox, director of conservation and climate change at the North York Moors National Park Authority, said: "The speed of this funding made all the difference."
