# Cyclists remember Jo Cox with 290-mile ride 10 years after her murder

## More than 80 cyclists have set off on a 290-mile ride from Yorkshire to Westminster to remember MP Jo Cox, who was murdered 10 years ago.

The 11th Jo Cox Way event aims to keep alive the legacy of the Batley and Spen Labour MP – who was killed by a far-right terrorist in June 2016 – by championing the causes she was passionate about.

Organisers of this year’s event said it is all the more poignant following the 10th anniversary of the tragedy.

Mrs Cox’s sister Kim Leadbeater – who followed her into the House of Commons – led the riders off from the Princess Mary Athletics Stadium in Cleckheaton. West Yorkshire, on Wednesday morning along with Jo Cox Way founder Sarfraz Mian.

Ms Leadbeater was joined by her parents, Jean and Gordon.

Riders aged from 22 to 80 years old, and from as far away as New Zealand, were part of the peloton which will arrive at Westminster on Sunday.

Ms Leadbeater, now the Labour MP for Spen Valley, said: “It’s wonderful that, 10 years on, Jo’s message is still making an impact, not just here in Yorkshire but around the world.

“Perhaps the greatest tribute we can offer Jo is not merely to repeat her words, but to live by them, and show that we really do have far more in common than that which divides us.

“This is exactly what this bike ride does – women and men, young and old, from different places and different backgrounds, spend time together and real friendships grow.”

Ms Leadbeater, who has taken part every year, said: “It’s a tough challenge but a brilliant five days.

“Jo would have loved it.”

Ruby Boyden, 27, travelled from New Zealand to take part.

She said: “I thought it would be great to join, not only as a lovely way to meet people cycling for a good cause and an excuse to see the English countryside, but as a political choice.

“It’s a reminder to turn toward community not away from it, particularly in times of increasing polarisation and divisive rhetoric.”

Mr Mian said: “I think the ride is important because it is a reminder that there is all this wonderful stuff that goes on up and down the country, from Cleckheaton to London.

“It is a hugely positive thing that brings together all sorts of people from all walks of life.

“So many people have done it multiple times. And they keep coming back.”
