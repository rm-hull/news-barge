## 'It saddens me' - resident defends village over upset at gravel driveways

It saddens me to see letters running down Poppleton’s gardens.

Poppleton is a lovely place to live. It makes sense for car owners to park their cars off-road to keep the roads clear.

It may be that those who have made room for their cars have lovely back gardens.

I do agree with the correspondents about their dislike of gravel.

Three years ago I went back to my old home area in the suburbs of a south coast seaside town.

Weeds grew through the gravel and underlying membrane, with stones spread across pavements – a hazard for people with mobility problems.

Did your correspondents share in Poppleton’s Garden Trail two years ago I wonder? It brought a lot of pleasure. My own garden was described as ‘wildlife friendly’. It’s not neatly manicured. But people enjoyed it and remember it.

I hope readers will come along to Poppleton Village Show from 12.30pm on August 15 to share in the delights of this lovely village.

On the different subject of access to our hospital: I, and many other Poppleton residents, would be delighted if simple procedures such as blood tests could come back to our very underused surgery, as was the practice up to a couple of years ago, when Haxby Group forced us to the stressful effort of going to the hospital.

Poppleton is a friendly, caring, lovely place to live.

## Where are all the city’s little shops?

Nearly every day The Press announces the opening of a new coffee shop, cafe, bar or restaurant in York, and good luck to each and every one of them, but where are the actual shops?

Where are the independent retailers, the interesting, friendly little shops that sold everything and anything?

Perhaps this is one of the reasons that the footfall has fallen so dramatically.
