Ed Miliband's appointment as Foreign Secretary under new Prime Minister Andy Burnham has caused the White House to be “deeply concerned.”

Sources close to President Donald Trump warn he "will not forget" Miliband's past scathing criticisms, including calling him a "racist, misogynistic, self-confessed groper."

Miliband is viewed in Washington as an "unreasonable 'nut zero' extremist" due to his climate stance and perceived blocking of North Sea oil production.

Critics, including Boris Johnson, also blame Miliband for withdrawing support for bombing Syria in 2013, which they claim emboldened Vladimir Putin.

Despite the diplomatic challenges, some, like former Labour foreign minister Bill Rammell, believe Miliband and Trump can build a working relationship.
