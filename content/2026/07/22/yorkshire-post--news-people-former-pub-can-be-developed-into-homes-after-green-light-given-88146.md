# Former pub can be developed into homes after green light given

## A former Elland pub can be developed into homes, Calderdale planners have agreed.

All Property Management applied to Calderdale Council seeking planning permission to convert the Malt Shovel Inn at Briggate into two cottages and re-instating an existing cottage.

The proposals have now been permitted by planning officers.

A planning statement prepared by agents Planning Potential in support of the application said: “The proposed project involves transforming a vacant and dilapidated public house into a modern residential development comprising high-quality cottages.

![The former Malt Shovel Inn, Briggate, Elland, which closed in 2019. Picture: Google](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOmYxYzUxNTk4LWEzNmQtNGYyMy1iYzE0LWNmYjBkYjI1MjM2MzoxOTgyMWQ4NC1iZGRmLTQ4ZTctYWFiYS0yNjhlMTE0NWUyMjk=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



“This initiative aims to revitalise the derelict site, which has become an eyesore within the local community, and to address the increasing demand for much-needed housing in the area.

“The conversion plans have been carefully considered to retain certain historical features of the original pub, maintaining a connection to its heritage while providing contemporary living spaces.”

The town centre pub closed in 2019.

It is within Elland Conservation Area and next to the site is Britannia Buildings, which is a Grade II-listed building, notes the supporting statement.

Externally, the proposals are deliberately limited in order to retain the established appearance of the building and to avoid harm to the conservation area or the setting of Britannia Buildings.

The only alteration to the frontage is for an additional entrance door to provide independent access to one of the proposed homes, and this will match existing openings, according to the application.
