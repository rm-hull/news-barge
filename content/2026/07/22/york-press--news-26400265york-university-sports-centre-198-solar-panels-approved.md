Search the Public Notice PortalALMOST 200 solar panels are set to be installed on a York building following the approval of a planning application.

Plans to install 198 solar panels on the roof of University of York’s sports centre in its Heslington campus.

The university’s plans stated the panels would generate an estimated roughly 91 kilowatt-peak (kWp) in power.

City of York Council planning officers stated the proposed works were acceptable and they had no objections to them going ahead.

Approval of the plans paves the way for the solar panels to be installed on the roof of York Sports Centre, in James Way in the university’s Campus West.

The building is surrounded by neighbouring sports facilities, student accommodation and a car park.

They would be installed on four sections of the building’s roof.

They would mostly be flat and would sit at 25cm above the roof at their highest point.

  Plans showing solar panels of the roof of York Sports Centre, in James Way, at the University of York's Campus West in Heslington 

*(Image: University of York)*

The panels would feature anti-reflective coating to help increase the amount of light absorbed into the cells.

Each bifacial double glass module solar panel would be capable of generating up to 590W.

A council report on the plans stated the panels would not be visible from surrounding public areas.

They would be installed around 45m away from student accommodation blocks to the north of the sports centre.

Council officers stated the height of the building’s flat roof meant people in neighbouring buildings would not be exposed to glare.

Officers stated: “The council is satisfied that proposed works are acceptable with regard to their design and external appearance, and there are therefore no objections to the proposed development.

“Due to the location of the panels and design, the effect on the appearance of the building and neighbour amenity is considered acceptable.”
