Danish authorities have issued warnings to tourists regarding wolf encounters, following incidents of a young pup approaching humans.

Flyers distributed by Varde municipality and Visit Vesterhavet advise tourists to “show dominance” and “keep eye contact”.

The advice includes defending yourself by hitting, kicking and using objects, particularly targeting the wolf's hindquarters if it attacks a person or dog.

The Danish Centre for Environment and Energy noted that “at least one young wolf is exhibiting significantly reduced shyness and approach behaviour towards humans”.

Wolves are repopulating Denmark after a 200-year absence, with 49 recorded this year, according to the Natural History Museum of Aarhus, and their EU protection status was recently downgraded to allow for population management.
