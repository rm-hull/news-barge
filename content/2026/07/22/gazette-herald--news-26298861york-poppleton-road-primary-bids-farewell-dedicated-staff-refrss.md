Emotional tributes have been paid to three 'remarkable' members of staff as they leave a York school after collectively dedicating 76 years of service.

Poppleton Road Primary School has bid farewell to teaching assistants Sue Hall and Kath Nicholls, as well as school business manager Sally Bissett, praising their talent and commitment over the decades.

The school said Sue, who joined the school in 1999, had spent more than a quarter of a century 'guiding, comforting, and inspiring pupils' while Kath, who started in 2001, had 'similarly been a pillar of strength and kindness in the classroom'.

Headteacher Ellie Dawson said: “Sue and Kath have been absolutely wonderful teaching assistants.

“They have given their hearts to our school community, leaving an indelible mark on generations of children who will always remember their patience and encouragement.”

Sally joined in 2002 as part of the office team and later became the school business manager.

Ms Dawson praised her ‘talent and unwavering commitment’ to the school.

She said: "Behind every great school is a smoothly run operation, and we have been incredibly fortunate to have Sally Bissett at the helm of ours.

"Starting in 2002 as a member of our office staff, Sally’s talent and unwavering commitment saw her later step into the vital role of School Business Manager (SBM).

"Her wonderful dedication has kept our school moving forward, always with the best interests of the children at heart. We are not just losing three colleagues this summer; we are losing three deeply cherished members of our school family.

"Sue, Kath, and Sally have devoted their lives to our community, shaping the very fabric of Poppleton Road Primary."

Ms Dawson expressed her gratitude for the contributions they had all made, adding: “While it is incredibly sad to see them go, we are filled with immense gratitude for everything they have given us.

“On behalf of the governors, staff, parents, and, most importantly, the children, past and present, we want to say a massive thank you.

“We wish them nothing but health, happiness, and joy in their well-deserved next chapters. You will always be a part of Poppleton Road.”
