                                    Yorkshire's biggest-ever gathering of pugs is set to take place at Harewood House next month.

The special event will be held on Sunday 2nd August, inviting pugs and their owners to celebrate one of Britain's best-loved dog breeds in honour of renowned photographer Cecil Beaton and his much-loved companion, Simba.

The day will feature a mass pug gathering on the steps of Harewood House, followed by a group photograph.

Owners and their four-legged friends will also be able to board the "Captain Pugwash" ferry for a trip to the Walled Garden, with every pug receiving a complimentary puppuccino from The Bothy.

After the organised activities, visitors can spend the rest of the day exploring Harewood's gardens and grounds with their dogs.

A spokesperson for the event said:

“Don't miss this pawsome celebration and help make Yorkshire pug history!”


Pug owners attending the event will receive complimentary admission for one owner, with additional tickets available for family and friends.
