A roofing firm that flyposted York with illegal advertisements and refused to remove them has been ordered to pay more than £4,500.

A City of York Council spokesman said a representative of Bastfix Roofing and Gutters Ltd told its staff the law on flyposting was “unfair”.

Council officers had found signs advertising the company around the city on signposts and railings including on Stockton Lane and Clifton Moorgate in late November.

Cllr Jenny Kent, the council’s executive member for the environment, said: “Fly-posting is dangerous as it can cause distraction to road users, obstructs visibility and can obscure important information, as well as creating a hazard during installation and removal.”

The company, which has a registered address at The Crescent, Spalding, south Lincolnshire and may have an office in Malton Road, York, did not attend York Magistrates Court and was convicted in its absence of flyposting.

It was fined £2,500 and ordered to pay prosecution costs of £1,089.04 and a statutory surcharge of £1,000, totalling £4,589.04.

The council said it ordered the company by email and letter to remove the signs. The company did not comply, despite the council reminding it by phone, email and letter.

In January, the council removed the signs and sent the company three £500 fixed penalty notices, which were not paid.

The council then tried to interview the company without success, and prosecuted it.

Cllr Kent, said: “Posting any sign to the highway without consent is a criminal offence. The court has taken this seriously with the scale of fine.

“Anyone wanting to put up signs on Council land or property should apply at: www.york.gov.uk/AdvertisementPlanningPermission. To report unauthorised signage, please visit: [https://www.york.gov.uk/Flyposting.”](https://www.york.gov.uk/Flyposting.”)
