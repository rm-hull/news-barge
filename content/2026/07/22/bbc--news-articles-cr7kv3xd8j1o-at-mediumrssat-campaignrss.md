![The headline on the front page of the Metro reads: “Ann 'struck on head 21 times with hammer'”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/828/cpsprodpb/3f24/live/a43d5090-8547-11f1-b976-0b9c15b0ccfc.jpg)

Andy Burnham's first full day as prime minister and fresh details about Ann Widdecombe's death dominate Wednesday's front pages. The Metro leads with reports that Widdecombe was hit on the head with a hammer 21 times on the day she was killed. Prosecutors told Westminster Magistrates' Court it was a "planned and targeted" attack carried out with a "ferocity of violence". Meanwhile, the paper also reports on Burnham's bid to cut bills as he holds his first cabinet meeting.

![The headline on the front page of the Sun reads: “Ann 'hit 21 times with hammer'”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/3840/cpsprodpb/d1ef/live/034183f0-854c-11f1-b976-0b9c15b0ccfc.jpg)

Similarly, the Sun reads: "Ann 'hit 21 times with hammer'". The paper draws attention to the detail that the former MP was eating her lunch at the time of the attack, reporting that a kitchen camera is said to have captured her killer entering the Dartmoor bungalow.

![The headline on the front page of the Mirror reads: “Ann 'hit 21 times with hammer'”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/802/cpsprodpb/e68a/live/7eed7720-8547-11f1-b976-0b9c15b0ccfc.jpg)

A picture of Joshua Kerry, the man accused of murdering Widdecombe, is front and centre on the Daily Mirror. Kerry, 28, appeared at the Old Bailey on Tuesday, after he was charged with one count of murder following an investigation led by counter terrorism detectives.

![The headline on the front page of the Daily Express reads: “Ann was 'hit 21 times on the head with hammer'”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/802/cpsprodpb/05dc/live/979a7390-8547-11f1-bee8-53ce494e1abc.jpg)

In addition to the details about how Widdecombe was killed, the Daily Express says the 8 July attack took place over just two minutes.

![The headline on the front page of the Daily Star reads: “Widdecombe hit 21 times with hammer”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/802/cpsprodpb/890b/live/5e2102f0-8547-11f1-b976-0b9c15b0ccfc.jpg)

"Widdecombe hit 21 times with hammer," says the Daily Star, which reports that it was a gardener who found the 78-year-old "face down" in her kitchen the day after the attack.

![The headline on the front page of the Daily Mail reads: “Who's going to pay the bill for Andy's largesse?”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/810/cpsprodpb/4be9/live/63790b60-8549-11f1-bee8-53ce494e1abc.jpg)

Though Widdecombe features on the front page of the Daily Mail, the paper's main story focuses on Burnham's first pledges as prime minister - and how he might pay for them. It reports that a spokesperson for Burnham was unable to say who would pay for his "many" proposals, adding that they "seemed set to hit taxpayers for hundreds of billions of pounds".

![The headline on the front page of the i Paper reads: “Cost of living, cost of living, cost of living”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/830/cpsprodpb/dc96/live/8e5121d0-8547-11f1-bee8-53ce494e1abc.jpg)

"Cost of living, cost of living, cost of living," declares the i Paper, reporting that Burnham has told his cabinet to find ways to "cut UK prices and offer hope". The paper says senior economists are warning that the new prime minister will need to raise taxes, or else risk failing to deliver on his raft of "bolder policy pledges".

![The headline on the front page of the Times reads: “Burnham signals cuts to pay for spending pledges”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/809/cpsprodpb/72ce/live/73a846a0-854d-11f1-926f-c90d1bcfbc84.jpg)

The Times makes a similar assessment of Burnham's first cabinet meeting, which it says took place against the backdrop of "mounting questions over the scale of his public spending commitments". The paper notes that Burnham has already announced a £340m investment to end rough sleeping and an £850m plan to cut energy bills, after just two days as prime minister.

![The headline on the front page of the Financial Times reads: “Burnham backs away from rise in tax allowance 'to show fiscal discipline'”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/1056/cpsprodpb/d2cc/live/929979e0-8547-11f1-b976-0b9c15b0ccfc.jpg)

The Financial Times writes that Burnham's commitment to fiscal discipline is "immediately in the spotlight" following the announcement of his plan to cut electricity bills this winter, which he says will be funded by axing the digital ID scheme. The paper says it was allowed into the prime minister's first cabinet meeting, where he told his ministers he planned to run a "cost of living government" and urged them to consider "all the possible ways" they could help voters with their overheads.

![The headline on the front page of the Guardian reads: “Burnham implores cabinet to find ways to tackle cost of living crisis”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/786/cpsprodpb/69e3/live/1c31eed0-8548-11f1-b976-0b9c15b0ccfc.jpg)

The Guardian also carries details of Burnham's first cabinet meeting, adding that he is expected to introduce further policies to ease the cost of living later this week. The paper reports the prime minister will announce a reduction on the national cap on bus fares from £3 to £2 on Wednesday.

![The headline on the front page of the Independent reads: “Trump furious at foreign secretary role for Miliband”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/1536/cpsprodpb/4019/live/6a7d1d80-8548-11f1-b976-0b9c15b0ccfc.jpg)

Burnham's cabinet is a focus for the Independent, which says that the US is unhappy with new Foreign Secretary Ed Miliband. According to the paper, the White House is "deeply concerned" by the decision to shift Miliband into the role, as Donald Trump "hasn't forgotten" the former Labour leader's "fierce criticism".

![The headline on the front page of the Daily Telegraph reads: “Pc Harper's killers set for early release”.](https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/ace/standard/1040/cpsprodpb/e59b/live/670ec730-8547-11f1-926f-c90d1bcfbc84.jpg)

"Pc Harper's killers set for early release," says the Daily Telegraph, which reports that two people jailed for the death of a policeman in 2019 are set to be released just halfway through their sentences under an early-release scheme. The front page features a photograph of Angela Rayner on her way to Downing Street on Tuesday, paired with the headline: "Rayner's return".
