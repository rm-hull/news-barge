A NORTH Yorkshire village is preparing to bid farewell to one of its 'cherished local legends.'

Wilfred Dennis Cook, one of Westow's most familiar faces, passed away on June 26, aged 99.

Known as Dennis, his life was woven into the story of the village for almost a century.

Born in Post Cottage, Westow, in 1927, Dennis was a true son of the village.

Raised by his parents Evelyn and Charles alongside his older brother Bill, Dennis grew up surrounded by the rhythms of village life, the values of hard work, and the traditions of a community that shaped him from the beginning.

Dennis learned joinery at his father’s side in the Cook family workshop, continuing the business after his father’s death.

At just 17, Dennis stepped forward when his country needed him. He joined Britain’s secret wartime resistance, the Auxiliary Units, training in the hidden operational base in Howsham Wood.

At 18, he was called up to the Royal Air Force, beginning a period of overseas service that took him to Egypt, Italy, Trieste, Venice and Palestine.

He endured illness, served under pressure, and earned the Palestine Medal for his role during the tense years of the British Mandate. His RAF standard will be proudly displayed at his funeral in recognition of his service.

It was in Venice that Dennis met Maria, the woman who would become his wife and the heart of his life. Together they built a warm, sociable home in Westow.

They raised two children, Angela and Trevor, whose memory remains deeply cherished after his tragic death at age 11. Maria passed away in 2004, and she and Trevor now rest together in St Mary’s Churchyard.

Dennis became one of Westow’s most familiar faces. Living independently in Kirkham View well into his later years until his death.

In 2022, Dennis was honoured as guest of honour at Westow’s Platinum Jubilee celebrations, cutting the cake and giving a speech that captured the affection the village held for him.

David, one of Dennis' grandsons said he was one of Westow's one of its most cherished figures,

"Westow will feel his absence keenly. But his memory — as a devoted husband, loving father, proud grandfather and great grandfather, craftsman, veteran, neighbour and friend — will remain part of the village forever."

The funeral is at St Mary’s Church, Westow at 2pm on Tuesday, July 28.

Donations in lieu of flowers are welcome to benefit St Mary’s Church and Cancer Research.
