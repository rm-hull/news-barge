A GoFundMe page has raised more than £100,000 for a three-year-old boy seriously injured after allegedly being thrown into a crocodile enclosure at Johnsons of Old Hurst, Cambridgeshire, on 18 June.

A 30-year-old man was arrested on suspicion of attempted murder following the incident but was later bailed as he was deemed unfit for interview, with an investigation into his care ongoing.

The boy has undergone seven operations, including a nerve graft on his left arm, with surgeons harvesting a nerve from his leg to aid recovery.

His family, who remain by his side in hospital, expressed hope that the most recent surgery will be his last for now, allowing him to return home soon.

Updates from the family indicate the boy is now talking to nurses, playing with his feet, and smiling again, despite acknowledging a long recovery journey ahead.
