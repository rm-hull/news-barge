Four chocolate bars have returned to Marks and Spencer (M&S) foodhalls today (Wednesday, July 22) in a throwback to the 1980s.

Jet, Now, Sparks, and Coconut Grove bars have made a comeback, each wrapped in retro St Michael packaging and featuring the original logo.

The bars are available as full-size bars or in a 14-piece mini variety pack.

The Jet bar is a milk chocolate treat with soft nougat, peanuts, and caramel, similar to a Snickers, while Spark is a nougat and caramel-filled bar, reminiscent of a Mars.


Coconut Grove combines milk chocolate with a soft coconut centre, much like a Bounty, and Now is a milk chocolate biscuit and caramel bar, similar to a Twix.

An M&S spokesperson told The Sun: "M&S Food is bringing back four retro chocolate bars from the St Michael archives.

"Originally part of our 1980s confectionery range, Jet, Sparks, Now! and Coconut Grove will return to Foodhalls next week, giving customers the chance to rediscover much-loved favourites from the era.

"Each bar, wrapped in nostalgic St Michael packaging, offers its own distinctive flavour and texture."

M&S has been contacted by Newsquest for comment.

## What is the St Michael brand?

The St Michael brand was owned by M&S from 1927, selling many goods under the name.

It was dropped in 2000 after 72 years as part of a rebranding.

However, the retailer revived the brand in 2021, selling menswear using the vintage label after clothing donning the brand name became popular on second-hand clothing platforms.

Another retro chocolate bar was brought back last year, with M&S reintroducing the Take 4 from its archives.
