A royal visitor was in York today.

The Duke of Gloucester arrived by helicopter, landing in the north of the city in Clifton, before visiting three of the city's most significant attractions - the National Railway Museum, the Yorkshire Museum, and York Minster.

His York hosts kept quiet about their VIP guest but the Duke's visit was confirmed on the royal court diary.

  The Duke of Gloucester, pictured last month at Royal Ascot at Ascot Racecourse Berkshire. 

*(Image: John Walton)*

Prince Richard, the Duke of Gloucester whose official residence is at Kensington Palace in London, is the second son of Prince Henry, Duke of Gloucester, and Alice, Duchess of Gloucester.

His father was the third son of King George V and Queen Mary.

The Duke's visit to the National Railway Museum, part of the Science Museum Group, comes as the museum and surrounding area undergo major regeneration works as part of the York Central scheme.

This project is redeveloping former railway land to create 2,500 homes and a commercial quarter, which will create up to 6,500 jobs.

  The Duke of Gloucester and the Duchess of Gloucester, pictured when they were returning by carriage to Buckingham Palace, London, following the Trooping the Colour ceremony in central London, as King Charles III celebrated his official birthday. 

*(Image: Press Association)*

A new Central Hall at the NRM is being built as part of this scheme, following the signing of a contract for the works, which are now underway.

The new-look museum has been described as the "cultural anchor" of York Central, one of the largest city-centre brownfield regeneration projects in Europe.

His visit to York follows an earlier royal visit by King Charles in May when he went to York Hospital and toured the site ahead of the opening of the Sir Robert Ogden Macmillan Cancer Centre.

The Duke also visited the Yorkshire Museum which this year received a £2 million cash boost to help fix the Grade I listed building’s leaking roof.

The government’s funding will allow for the first phase of works to redesign and upgrade parts of the 1827 building’s roof, with initial work due to focus on the main Tempest Anderson Hall and the museum entrance area.
