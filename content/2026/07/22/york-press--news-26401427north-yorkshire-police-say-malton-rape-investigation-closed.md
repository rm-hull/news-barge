AN investigation into a reported rape in a North Yorkshire town has been closed.

North Yorkshire Police have issued an update in relation to the investigation into the reported rape of a woman in Malton on February 1, 2026.

A police spokesperson said: “We understand that reports of serious offences can cause significant concern within the community, particularly those involving allegations of sexual violence. Public safety remains our highest priority, and we treat all reports of this nature with the utmost seriousness and concern.

“Following a thorough investigation into the reported incident, including extensive enquiries and a detailed review of all available evidence and information, the investigation is now closed.

“There is currently no information to suggest any wider risk to the public in relation to this report.

“We want to emphasise that anyone who comes forward to report such experiences will be treated with compassion, respect, and support. Reporting offences of this nature can be extremely difficult, and we remain committed to supporting victims and encouraging people to come forward.

“Our investigation has been conducted carefully and diligently, and we will continue to review any new information should it come to light. We also continue to work closely with partners to ensure support is available for those who need it.

“We ask the public to avoid speculation and to respect the privacy of those involved including victims of sexual crimes who have anonymity for life under English law meaning nothing can be published anywhere - including social media - that could lead to their identification.”

Reporting rape and sexual assault

If you have been raped or sexually assaulted, we urge you to get help. You can report it to the police on 101, but if you are in danger, always call 999

If you prefer not to go direct to the police and are not in immediate danger, you can contact North Yorkshire’s Sexual Assault Referral Centre (SARC), on 0330 223 0362 or visit acerhousesarc.co.uk they can provide confidential advice and support including healthcare and access to medicine.

IDAS is an independent charity that supports victims of sexual violence and abuse regardless of whether you have reported it to the police.

Anyone concerned about a child can contact the NSPCC Helpline for advice on 0808 800 5000. Adult victims of non-recent sexual abuse can also get in touch for support.

Childline is available for young people on 0800 1111 or at Childline
