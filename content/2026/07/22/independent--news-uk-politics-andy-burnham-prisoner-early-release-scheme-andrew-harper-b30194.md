Andy Burnham will review the previous government’s early release scheme following backlash over the early release of two of the perpetrators in the killing of police officer Andrew Harper.

Referring to concerns from the family of PC Harper about early release of two of his killers, the new prime minister said: “I feel for the family, it’s an appalling crime that was committed. The main perpetrator will not be subject to any early release. Of course, others involved have potentially been identified.

“But I’ve spoken to the new justice secretary. He will review the policy together and make further statements in due course.”

The mother of police officer Andrew Harper said it feels like an “insult” that two of her son’s killers may be eligible for early release from prison.

Earlier on Wednesday, transport secretary Heidi Alexander confirmed that the driver of the car which fatally dragged Pc Harper along a road in 2019 would not be eligible for automatic release, although his two passengers could be eligible to get out halfway through their sentences.
