# A beloved SportsCenter anchor is the latest casualty of ESPN’s cuts

- ESPN has implemented a new round of layoffs, affecting several prominent figures including MLB announcer Karl Ravech, NFL MVP Cam Newton, and longtime SportsCenter anchor David Lloyd.
- Karl Ravech, a 31-year veteran and lead MLB play-by-play announcer, reportedly learned his position was in jeopardy just hours after calling a game, though he had not received official notification.
- Fans reacted with significant anger and disappointment on social media, particularly regarding Ravech's potential departure, criticizing ESPN's decisions and comparing them to the salaries of other personalities.
- Despite the uncertainty surrounding his employment, Ravech professionally continued his 'Dirt From The Dugout' podcast, choosing not to discuss the ESPN news during the episode.
- The layoffs are reportedly connected to ESPN's recent integration with NFL Network, a deal that involved ESPN acquiring NFL Network and the NFL gaining a 10 percent stake in ESPN.
