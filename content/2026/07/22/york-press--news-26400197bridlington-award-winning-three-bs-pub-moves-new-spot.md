## Award-winning East Yorkshire pub moves and owners talk of new 'crowd pleaser'

The pub has retained its look and styling on the outside *(Image: Supplied)>*

AN award-winning pub in an East Yorkshire town has relocated closer to its centre and owners said they’ve added “a real crowd-pleaser” to the new premises.

Three B’s Micropub opened on Saturday, July 11 at 2, Wellington Road in a former office of Jackson Robson.

Owners Mark and Cheryl Bates said around £200,000 has been invested in the new premises.

  


Mark said: “The larger capacity is for around 80 people and we’re already being well received.

“The real crowd-pleaser is a very large suntrap beer garden which can accommodate over 50 customers.”

The couple have been running Three B’s for six years and said the moved has been long planned, starting in the summer of 2024.

  Owners said the new beer garden is a "real crowd pleaser" 

*(Image: Supplied)*

The pub won CAMRA’s joint Yorkshire pub of the year 2024 and was a top four finisher in the national equivalent.

Mark added: “The transformation of the beer garden from the former overgrown state has been amazing.

“We are thankful to all those who assisted us over the last couple of years to get the new pub open, we are so glad it is finally open.”

Food service begins this Friday as the new pub has kitchen facilities.

  Owners Mark and Cherly Bates said around £200,000 was spent investing in the new pub and its location 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Mark said the old pub had more basic fixtures and fittings for food, but pub classics will now be on the menu and a member of staff brought on board for service.

The owner said the pub offers an extended range of drinks with more than ten draught beers, 12 draught ciders, draught lagers, wines, prosecco as well as an expanded spirits selection.

The name of the pub draws inspiration from the three letter Bs on the town crest.

Upper floors have been converted into a residential let and a holiday let will follow later this year.
