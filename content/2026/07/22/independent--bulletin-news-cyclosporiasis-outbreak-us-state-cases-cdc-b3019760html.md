Parasite outbreak hits 41 states with 4,000 cases as Taylor Farms recalls lettuce

The United States is experiencing a significant surge in cyclosporiasis cases, with 4,173 confirmed and 7,400 unconfirmed infections across 41 states, nearly four times the average annual total.

Michigan has reported the highest number of cases, with 6,571 infections, many of which have been linked to contaminated lettuce.

The parasitic illness, spread through contaminated produce, causes severe symptoms including explosive diarrhea, vomiting and fatigue, though it is not typically life-threatening.

Health Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. stated the outbreaks are "under control" following a voluntary recall of iceberg lettuce from central Mexico by Taylor Farms.

Despite an FDA clarification regarding a "false positive test," the agency maintains the validity of the recall, and case counts are expected to continue rising through August, with prevention focusing on thorough food cleaning and cooking.
