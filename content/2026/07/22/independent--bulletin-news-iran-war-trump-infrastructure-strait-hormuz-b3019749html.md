US launches fresh strikes on Iran as Trump threatens to attack nuclear site

Iran has threatened to retaliate with strikes after President Donald Trump said he would “bomb and destroy” Iranian civilian infrastructure every time Tehran targets a ship in the Strait of Hormuz.

An Iranian military source, cited by the Tasnim state news agency, has said Iran will retaliate with strikes on regional infrastructure linked to the U.S. - including bridges and energy sites - if Washington does carry out its attacks first.

Both sides continue to wrestle for control of the vital waterway, with the U.S. president warning a bridge or power plant would be bombed if further attacks on ships occurred.

Trump's threat followed an 11th night of strikes and a breakdown in diplomatic efforts to resolve the ongoing conflict

Traffic around the Strait of Hormuz was still subdued on Wednesday as clashes between the U.S, and Iran continue to escalate.
