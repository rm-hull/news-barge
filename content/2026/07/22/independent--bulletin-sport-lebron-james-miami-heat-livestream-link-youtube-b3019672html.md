The Miami Heat sparked confusion on Tuesday night after publishing then deleting a live-stream link titled "LeBron James Introductory Press Conference” on YouTube.

A team spokesperson said that the club's social media department mistakenly posted the link, which included the date July 27, while preparing for the possibility of the superstar forward rejoining the Heat.

LeBron James, 41, is currently a free agent for the first time since 2018, having announced last month that he would not re-sign with the Los Angeles Lakers.

Beyond Miami, other reported potential destinations for the NBA's all-time leading scorer include the Philadelphia 76ers, Cleveland Cavaliers, and the Golden State Warriors.

James averaged 20.9 points, 7.2 assists and 6.1 rebounds in 60 games with the Lakers in 2025-26. The four-time NBA champion owns career averages of 26.8 points, 7.5 rebounds and 7.4 assists in a record 1,622 games over 23 seasons.
