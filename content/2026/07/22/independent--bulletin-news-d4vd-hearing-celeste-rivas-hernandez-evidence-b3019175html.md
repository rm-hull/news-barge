# New details emerge in D4vd preliminary hearing over teen’s death

![](https://cdn.jwplayer.com/v2/media/jrF05ZFu/poster.jpg?width=720)

- Singer D4vd, legally David Burke, is accused of murdering and dismembering 14-year-old Celeste Rivas Hernandez.
- A homicide detective testified Burke ordered a body bag, chainsaws, an inflatable pool and a shovel online under an alias, using his own bank cards, and had them delivered to his Hollywood home.
- Prosecutors allege Burke used these items to dismember Rivas Hernandez's body after stabbing her to death, fearing she would expose their relationship and harm his music career.
- Evidence presented includes the discovery of most ordered items in Burke's garage, a body bag matching one containing the victim's remains in his Tesla, and a 'Celeste' tattoo on Burke's finger.
- Burke has pleaded not guilty to murder, continuous sexual abuse of a child and unlawful mutilation of human remains, with his attorneys denying he caused Rivas Hernandez's death.
