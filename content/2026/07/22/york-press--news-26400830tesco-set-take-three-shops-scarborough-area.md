## Tesco to take over three North Yorkshire grocery stores

Tesco has acquired Proudfoot Group stores in Newby, Seamer and Manham Hill in Scarborough *(Image: PA)*

A MAJOR supermarket is set to take on three grocery shops in North Yorkshire.

Tesco is set to transform three local stores in the Scarborough area.

The supermarket chain has acquired Proudfoot Group stores in Newby, Seamer and Manham Hill, which are set to close on Sunday (October 4) this year for a refit.

  


Proudfoot’s shop in High Street, Eastfield, will remain unchanged.

  Stores in Seamer and Manham Hill will reopen as Tesco Express branches in autumn. The larger site in Scalby Road is set to become a Tesco Superstore 

*(Image: File photo.)*

Stores in Seamer and Manham Hill will reopen as Tesco Express branches in autumn following a transitional period, Tesco has confirmed.

Meanwhile, the larger site in Scalby Road is set to become a Tesco Superstore in the New Year after undergoing a refit.

The stores will continue trading under the Proudfoot independent name until October 4.

Proudfoot has operated in the Scarborough area for 80 years.

Its first shop was opened by Wilf Proudfoot in Main Street, Seamer, in April 1948.

Wilf drew inspiration from the self-service method of selling produce on a trip to the US and decided to incorporate this into his shop.

The company now employs 205 full and part-time staff.

The Press has approached the Proudfoot Group for comment.
