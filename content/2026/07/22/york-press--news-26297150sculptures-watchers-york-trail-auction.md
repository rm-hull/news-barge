People have the chance to take home a distinctive sculpture artwork from a York public art trail, with bidding now open.

The sculptures are part of The Watchers of York - a trail inspired by the medieval stone carvings that have watched over York Minster for centuries and were produced by Make It York in collaboration with York Minster.

Sarah Loftus, managing director of Make It York, said: "The Watchers of York auction is a wonderful opportunity to own a unique piece of the trail while making a real difference.

"Every bid will help create a legacy supporting vital work at York Minster and investing in future projects that benefit our city and its communities.

"We hope people will bid generously, knowing they're not only taking home an incredible piece of art, but also contributing to two fantastic causes."

Each sculpture is individually designed and painted, reflecting the spirit and identity of its location around York.

Bidding closes on Thursday, September 10, and funds raised will go towards conservation efforts at York Minster and the York Creates Fund, which supports creative and cultural projects in the city.

Two sculptures made with charities are not included in the auction and will instead be returned to those organisations to continue inspiring their communities.
