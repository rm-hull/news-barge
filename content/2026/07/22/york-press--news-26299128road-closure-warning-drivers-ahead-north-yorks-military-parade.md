## Road closure warning to drivers ahead of North Yorkshire military parade

REME1. WTC

A PARADE will celebrate the long-standing relationship between a North Yorkshire town and the Royal Electrical and Mechanical Engineers.

Residents and visitors to Whitby have been invited to witness the Freedom of Whitby Parade, which will mark the formal granting of the Honorary Freedom of the Town to 1 Close Support Battalion, Royal Electrical and Mechanical Engineers (1 CS Bn REME).

The parade will take place on Saturday, July 25 and marks the bestowal of the highest civic honour the town council can grant.

Cllr Sandra Turner, town mayor of Whitby, said: “This will be a proud and memorable occasion for Whitby.

“The Freedom Parade provides an opportunity for our community to recognise the outstanding service of the Royal Electrical and Mechanical Engineers, celebrate their long-standing relationship with our town, and show our appreciation for the dedication and sacrifice of all those who serve in our Armed Forces.”

The parade will begin at 11.30am from Church Street Car Park and will proceed across the Swing Bridge, New Quay Road, Bagdale, and Chubb Hill, before arriving at Pannett Park around 12pm for a formal ceremony.

A rolling road closure will be in effect along the parade route.

Cllr Turner added: “I would encourage as many people as possible to come along and support this special civic event and give 1 CS Bn REME a fitting Whitby welcome as they exercise the Freedom of the Town.”

On the day, soldiers will march through the town with bayonets fixed, drums beating, bands playing, and colours flying.

Motorists are advised to plan journeys in advance and follow the directions of event stewards and police officers on the day.
