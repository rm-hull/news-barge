Samsung has just unveiled a host of new Galaxy devices at its Galaxy Unpacked event – the most interesting of which is the shorter, squatter and wider Galaxy Z Fold 8, which closely resembles the form factor expected for the iPhone Ultra.

Like Apple, Samsung can’t escape the global memory crunch. The Galaxy Z Fold 8 Ultra, which is effectively the successor to last year's Z Fold 7, and the Galaxy Z Flip 8, have both received a hefty price hike. That said, Samsung’s new wider, shorter Galaxy Z Fold 8 is more affordable and might end up being the most popular in terms of design (yes, the names are all very confusing).

Available to pre-order now, here’s everything you need to know about the Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 8, Galaxy Z Fold 8 Ultra and Galaxy Z Flip 8’s price, plus the best pre-order deals in the UK as they drop.

## Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 8 price

While the two main foldables have received a price hike, the company has introduced an all-new, more affordable Galaxy Z Fold 8 alongside the Z Fold 8 Ultra, meaning there are now two book-style foldables and one flip-style foldable.

The Galaxy Z Fold 8 Ultra starts at £1,899. That means it’s £200 more expensive than last year's Galaxy Z Fold 7. The Galaxy Z Fold 8 is the new passport-style foldable. It ditches the tall and narrow form factor and starts at £1,699, which is £100 cheaper than the previous Fold 7. The main downgrade is in the camera.

The Galaxy Z Flip 8 has also received a price hike. It now starts from £1,199, which is £150 more than last year's Galaxy Z Flip 7.

The Galaxy Z Fold 8 comes in lavender, cream and graphite, with pistachio available exclusively at Samsung. It comes with 256GB and 512GB storage sizes. The Galaxy Z Fold 8 Ultra comes in violet shadow, cream and graphite. There are 256GB, 512GB and 1TB storage sizes.

The Galaxy Z Flip 8 comes in pink, cream and graphite, with mint available exclusively from Samsung. It starts from £1,199 for the 256GB model, with a 512GB version also available.

Samsung has previously launched double data bonuses when pre-ordering one of their phones, meaning customers have been able to get a 512GB phone at no extra cost when they pre-order the 256GB model, or getting the 1TB model when they pre-order the 512GB model. We’ll have to see if that continues this year, with the global memory shortage looming large.
