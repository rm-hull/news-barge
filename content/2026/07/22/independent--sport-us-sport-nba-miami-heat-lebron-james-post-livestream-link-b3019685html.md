The basketball world was buzzing Wednesday morning after the Miami Heat inadvertently published a video hinting at the potential return of LeBron James.

James has yet to announce what team he’ll play for in his 24th NBA season.

On Tuesday night, a live stream titled "LeBron James Introductory Press Conference" and scheduled for July 27 appeared on the Heat's YouTube channel before being swiftly removed.

The gaffe immediately fueled speculation among fans about whether the 41-year-old superstar is poised to rejoin the franchise with whom he secured two championships.

A team spokesperson told *ESPN* that the Heat's social media department was preparing for the possibility of James' signing and mistakenly uploaded the link to the team's YouTube page. The Heat did not provide an immediate comment when contacted by*Reuters*.

James' departure from the Los Angeles Lakers last month has made his next move the most compelling storyline of the NBA off-season.

The Cleveland Cavaliers, Philadelphia 76ers and the Heat are all reportedly leading contenders for his signature. The intense anticipation has even extended to the betting markets, with BetOnline Sports & Casino accepting wagers on James' announcement date, with July 24 currently favored, and Miami emerging as the most likely destination.

However, Rich Paul, James' long-time friend and agent, stated on a podcast episode released earlier this week that his client would not be rushed into a decision.

"We're not going to be rushed," Paul said on *Game Over with Max Kellerman and Rich Paul* on Monday. "It's his choice to make and when he makes the choice, he'll make it."

James said on July 16, “I won’t hold you guys up too much longer.”

“It’s not just about the team,” he continued. “There’s so many other factors that I’m factoring in right now, on what best fits me as a player, what best fits me as a person and what best fits my happiness, and also my family as well.”

“LeBron James is one of the greatest athletes in history,” the Los Angeles Lakers said on social media. “We will always be thankful for his eight years with the Lakers – including the title he led us to in 2020 under the toughest imaginable circumstances and the countless records he broke in purple and gold.”
