## Parents face £2,200 summer food bill as supermarkets slash price of family favourites

Morrisons is today cutting the price of a further 490 products and extending a commitment to its customers that it will have over 1,000 prices locked low for at least eight weeks. *(Image: Morrisons)*

The long summer holidays have begun, and many parents are already feeling the strain on the family budget.

New research suggests households could spend an extra £205 a week on food while children are off school, taking the total grocery bill over the six-week break to around £2,256.

The survey of 1,000 parents found the average weekly food shop rises from £171 during term time as children eat more meals and snacks at home.

More than a third (37%) of parents said they are "pestered non-stop" for food during the holidays, while almost eight in 10 (78%) said their children already eat more than the adults in the household.


Nutritionist Sophie Trotman said: "The summer holidays can feel like a never-ending snackathon for parents.

"Children are naturally more active, more social and often at home for longer periods, which means they're thinking about food more frequently."

## Morrisons launches summer savings

To help families make their money go further, Morrisons has introduced money-saving offers on hundreds of family favourites throughout July and August.

The deals are available in stores nationwide and online, with discounts on fresh fruit, lunchbox staples, picnic favourites, snacks, drinks and easy meal ingredients.

"That's why we've focused these savings on our Market Street fresh fruit alongside popular snacks and drinks, helping customers enjoy fresh, quality food and make the most of summer for less."
