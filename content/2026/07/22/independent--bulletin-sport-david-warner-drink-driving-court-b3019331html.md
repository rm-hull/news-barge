# Ex-Australia batter David Warner admits drink-driving

![Former Australia batter David Warner has pleaded guilty to drink-driving](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/22/09/01KY4D3ZGXCGC4B554XB8A3WV2.jpg)

- Australian cricketer David Warner has pleaded guilty to a drink-driving charge following an incident on Easter Sunday, 5 April, in Sydney's eastern suburbs.
- Warner recorded a blood alcohol reading of 0.104, more than twice the legal limit, after being stopped by police and undergoing a secondary breath test.
- His lawyer, Bobby Hill, confirmed the guilty plea, stating Warner accepted responsibility for his 'reckless' and 'foolish decision' to drive after consuming three glasses of wine.
- The admission of guilt has placed Warner's captaincy of the Sydney Thunder Big Bash League team in doubt, particularly as Cricket NSW and the team are involved in an anti-drink-driving campaign.
- Warner, who retired from international cricket in 2024, faces potential penalties including a minimum six-month driving ban, a fine of up to A$2,200, and a possible prison term of up to nine months, with his sentence to be handed down on 18 August.
