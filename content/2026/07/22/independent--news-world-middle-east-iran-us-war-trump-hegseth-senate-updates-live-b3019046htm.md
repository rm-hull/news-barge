The third US soldier killed by an Iranian attack on a military base in Jordan has been identified by the Pentagon.

Sgt. Angel S. Rampersad, a 28-year-old from New York, was killed by the attack at the Muwaffaq Salti Air Base in Jordan on Friday, the Pentagon announced Tuesday.

The other two fallen soldiers, 1st Lt. Tyler James Feehan, a 25-year-old from Hawaii, and Pvt. Isabella Gonzales, a 19-year-old from Texas, were identified Monday.

At least 18 American troops have died since the beginning of the war, and more than 400 others have been injured.

US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth said during his testimony to the Senate Appropriations Committee Tuesday that US President Donald Trump should be praised for the war.

“President Trump deserves the kind of praise he should get for undertaking this effort,” Hegseth said. “Having the courage to ensure that crazy Islamists can’t have a nuclear weapon.”

Hegseth also revealed that the price tag of the Iran war has reached at least $37.5 billion. The defense secretary is asking for another $67 billion from Congress to cover mounting costs for the war, among other demands.

## MAGA congressman shrugs off Americans killed in Iran as the ‘nature of warfare’

A Republican US congressman and close ally of Donald Trump dismissed the deaths of American military members in the Iran war as “the nature of warfare”.

Rep. Carlos Giménez, who sits on the House’s Armed Services Committee, made the remark while discussing the escalating conflict with CNN Monday.

“You know my condolences go out to the families of our servicemen and women that are killed – have been killed – in Iran. That’s the nature of warfare; we will have some casualties,” Giménez told “The Lead”.

## Three tankers carrying Saudi crude make U-turns in Red Sea

Three oil tankers loaded with Saudi crude for China and India made U-turns in the Red Sea following a warning from Yemen's Iran-aligned Houthi militia.

The three tankers – Xin Long Yang, the Rodos and the Amazon – made turns towards the Suez Canal to skil the Yemeni coast andswitched off their transponders on Tuesday.

The Houthis on Monday declared a naval blockade against Saudi Arabia, opening a potential new front in the US-Israeli war with Iran and raising the threat to global energy supplies and trade beyond the Gulf.

The group, in an email sent to shipping companies, warned them not to load or discharge cargo at Saudi Arabian ports and said such activity may result in being targeted "in any location".

"Vessels calling at Saudi Arabian ports are advised to reconsider transiting the Red Sea and to consider implementing enhanced mitigation measures," British maritime security company Ambrey said, adding that it assessed vessels calling at Saudi Arabian ports "were at high risk".

## US completes latest round of strikes that targeted Iranian military infrastructure

US Central Command has announced that its latest round of strikes that targeted Iranian military infrastructure has been completed.

The strikes, which concluded at 8:15 p.m. ET Tuesday/12:15 a.m. GMT Wednesday, “targeted Iranian military operations centers, maritime capabilities, aircraft hangars, drone storage facilities, and military logistics infrastructure to further degrade Iran's ability to threaten commercial shipping in the Strait of Hormuz”, CENTCOM wrote on X.

“Over the past three months, Iran has attacked more than 30 commercial vessels transiting the international waterway vital for regional and global trade. The unwarranted attacks have endangered hundreds of innocent mariners and undermined freedom of navigation.

“Despite Iranian aggression, the Strait of Hormuz remains open for commercial vessel transit. Since early May, CENTCOM forces have helped facilitate the transit of approximately 900 commercial vessels and 450 million barrels of crude oil”, the US military said.

Despite CENTCOM proclaiming that the strait is open, maritime traffic through the key oil waterway in the Middle East remains diminished.

## Number of US troops injured in Iran war higher than publicly reported: official

The number of American forces injured in the Iran war is higher than publicly reported, a US official told the Associated Press.

The official said well over 500 troops have been injured, despite the Pentagon’s tally on its Defense Casualty Analysis System currently showing 482 wounded.
