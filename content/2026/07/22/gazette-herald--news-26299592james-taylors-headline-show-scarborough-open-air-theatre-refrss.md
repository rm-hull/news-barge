A multiple Grammy Award winner is set to perform in North Yorkshire this weekend.

James Taylor will play a headline show at TK Maxx presents Scarborough Open Air Theatre on Saturday, July 25, joined by his All-Star Band.

The show is sold out, and follows two other sell-out gigs this week by Teddy Swims.

Taylor's career spans more than 50 years, and he has sold more than 100 million albums, earning gold, platinum, and multi-platinum awards since the release of his self-titled debut album in 1968.

As a recording and touring artist, James Taylor has won over audiences with his warm baritone voice and distinctive style of guitar-playing while setting a precedent to which countless young musicians have aspired.

In 2015, Taylor released Before This World, the first-ever Number 1 album of his career.

He has been inducted into both the Rock and Roll and the prestigious Songwriters Halls of Fame.

In February 2006, The National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences selected him as its MusiCares Person of the Year.

Taylor was also awarded the distinguished Chevalier of the Order of Arts and Letters by the French government and the National Medal of Arts by President Barack Obama in 2012.

In November 2015, Taylor was presented with the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the United States’ highest civilian honour, and in December 2016, he received the Kennedy Center Honors.

In early 2020, Taylor released Break Shot, a deeply personal and authentic audio-only memoir detailing his first 21 years.

He also released his newest album, American Standard, his 19th studio album, which earned him the 2021 Grammy Award for Best Traditional Pop Vocal Album.

This achievement gave him the honour of being the first artist to have a Billboard Top 10 album in each of the past six decades.

Taylor was among the first headline announcements for the Scarborough Open Air Theatre’s 2026 concert series.
