A much-loved 19-year-old student “could not be saved” despite the efforts of emergency services before he drowned in a York river, a coroner has concluded.

Evan d’Andilly drowned after entering the River Ouse from Ouse Bridge four days before his 20th birthday on March 5, 2025, an inquest heard.

In a narrative conclusion, coroner Jonathan Leach said police, fire crews and the York Rescue Boat were called to the scene but Evan’s life “could not be saved”.

  Evan d'Andilly who drowned in the River Ouse in York on March 5, 2025 

*(Image: Family)*

The hearing was told Evan, a University of York business management student from Scunthorpe, had been on a night out with friends in the city centre when he entered the water at around 3.03am.

Evan tried to stay afloat in the freezing river for 20 minutes as emergency services worked to save him.

In CCTV footage played in court, Evan could be heard calling for help as police officers stood on the riverside shouting advice.

Police arrived within two to three minutes, and an officer threw a life ring into the water to reach Evan, who was in the middle of the 41-metre-wide river, without success.

  Police search the River Ouse in York for Evan d'Andilly on March 5, 2025 

*(Image: Newsquest)*

  Evan’s mother, Lorraine d’Andilly, told a **previous inquest hearing** the available riverside rescue equipment – 18-metre reach poles and 20-metre throw lines – were unsuitable and had no chance of reaching him, meaning "Evan lost a substantial chance of survival".

North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service said it has improved its emergency equipment since Evan's death.

York fire crews were notified at 3.09am on March 5, 2025, with Acomb and York appliances arriving by 3.14am and 3.18am.

But the inquest heard that the fire service’s rescue boat was in Selby and did not arrive until 3.37am – 14 minutes after the last sighting of Evan.

  Ouse Bridge in York 

*(Image: Newsquest)*

Volunteer-run York Rescue Boat, moored in King’s Staith next to where Evan was in the water, was the nearest vessel capable of a water rescue and could reach locations quickly, but Ms d’Andilly said the charity boat was not immediately called.

The inquest heard police called for the York Rescue Boat at about 3.10am and it was launched at 3.26am – three minutes after Evan was last seen above the water.

The York Rescue Boat's crew arrived at the boat at 3.22am, with it taking four minutes for the crew and two police officers to get on board and launch the vessel, the inquest heard.

Having the police officers onboard the rescue boat increased the search’s chance of success, the court heard.

  Evan d'Andilly 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Police)*

Evan’s body was found in the river by underwater police divers at 11.38am. His death was confirmed in hospital at 12pm.

Ms d’Andilly said her son “did everything he could to survive” and questioned why York Rescue Boat had not been called sooner.

The inquest heard that North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue relies on the York Rescue Boat for water rescues in York, as the fire service’s nearest rescue boats are in Selby and Malton.

Ms D’Andilly said her son had “fought for another 10 minutes” to stay above the water after the rescue boat was called.

“I will never understand how a safety protocol can be so flawed,” she added.

Ben Illsley, North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service’s director of operational support and assurance, told the inquest there are “no plans” to move the fire service’s rescue boat from Selby to York in the next four years.

He also said that if the rescue boat was moved to York, it would take one to two years for the fire service’s staff to be trained to use it.

## 'Evan should still be here, it’s that simple'

Ms d’Andilly said her son was a strong swimmer and did everything he could to stay above the water, adding: “I’m so incredibly proud of him.”

Ms d’Andilly said Evan “brought life into our eyes” and had a smile that could “light up the room”.

“He had a generous soul and is loved unconditionally,” she said, adding: “Evan should still be here, it’s that simple.”

Ms d’Andilly said the circumstances surrounding Evan’s death have deeply affected her and continue to do so.

“I hope that this time passes and I’ll be able to focus on all the happy memories of Evan … I miss him,” she said.

Evan’s father, Gordon D’Andilly, described his son as someone “full of life” who “got on with everyone really well”.

The inquest heard Evan struggled with his mental health and had previously attempted suicide, but Mr D’Andilly said he saw his son the week before his death and he was in a “good place”.

“Ev was everything,” Mr D’Andilly said. “He was such a good boy. He was a thoughtful boy and so funny.”

A North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service spokesperson said: "Our thoughts remain with the family and friends of Evan D'Andilly following the conclusion of the inquest into his death. We recognise the profound loss they have suffered and the impact this tragedy has had on those who responded and on the wider community.

"We remain committed to working with our partners to learn from incidents, reduce risk and help keep people safe around North Yorkshire and York's rivers and waterways.

"Water safety is a shared responsibility, and alongside ongoing prevention campaigns and community education, we continue to strengthen our response capability.

"North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service operates six Swift Water Rescue stations and a powered rescue boat based in Selby, with resources positioned across the county to respond to flooding and water rescue emergencies.

"In York, we have recently enhanced our provision through additional bankside extended pole and reach lines and the imminent rollout of Fast Rescue Deployment Devices (FReDs) to York and Acomb stations.

"Together with the specialist capabilities provided by York Rescue Boat in conjunction with the Service, these improvements strengthen the response available along the city's waterways."

A North Yorkshire Police spokesperson said: “First and foremost, our thoughts remain with the family and friends of Evan d’Andilly.

“The tragic death of Evan is felt deeply by all our officers and staff who, under extremely difficult circumstances, tried to save him.

“The findings of the inquest will be carefully reviewed by all the emergency services and partner agencies involved in the response.”
