# Pan Am flight 526A wreckage discovered 74 years after tragic crash

- The wreckage of Pan American Airways flight 526A, known as the "Clipper Endeavor", has been discovered 74 years after it crashed off the coast of Puerto Rico.
- The Douglas DC-4 aircraft, which took off from Puerto Rico on 11 April 1952, was found in June almost 2,000 feet deep in the Atlantic Ocean by the Discovery Channel's series *Expedition Unknown,* alongside the Air/Sea Heritage Foundation and other groups.
- The flight, bound for New York City, experienced engine failure shortly after take-off, leading to a water landing nine minutes later.
- Although everyone initially survived the impact, 52 of the 64 passengers on board subsequently drowned, while 12 passengers and all five crew members were rescued.
- An investigation determined the crash was caused by inadequate maintenance and faulty parts.
