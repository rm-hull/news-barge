Legendary singer Sir Tom Jones will take to the stage in North Yorkshire this weekend.

The Welsh icon will bring his 2026 Come Gather Round tour to Scarborough Open Air Theatre on Sunday, July 26 after performing for thousands more fans at the Piece Hall in Halifax the evening before.

He will be joined on both nights by British soul favourites Stone Foundation, an eight-piece collective famed for their uplifting live shows.

The shows mark a welcome return to both venues for Sir Tom who performed two sold‑out nights at The Piece Hall in 2022 and 2024, while this will be his fifth performance at Scarborough OAT - his last being a sold-out show in 2024.

  Tom Jones is performing at Scarborough Open Air Theatre on Sunday, July 26.

Thousands of fans from York, North Yorkshire and beyond are expected to descend on the coastal venue - many of whom missed out on a much-anticipated show back in the summer of 2018 when he was booked for York Racecourse's Music Showcase Weekend which had to be cancelled due to heavy rain, lightning and hailstones the size of golfballs over much of the country.

The show follows performances on the same stage by multiple Grammy Award winner James Taylor who will play on Saturday, July 25, joined by his All-Star Band which follows two sell-out gigs this week by Teddy Swims.

Sir Tom has enjoyed an impressive career spanning more than six decades, selling more than 100 million records worldwide, resulting in numerous accolades. He remains one of the most enduring and influential performers in music history.

Having been knighted by Queen Elizabeth II in 2006, he has also clinched multiple BRIT Awards, the Silver Clef Award, the Music Industry Trusts Award, and a Hitmaker Award from the US Songwriters Hall of Fame.

The octogenarian's global popularity shows no sign of abating with audiences attracted to his timeless catalogue, commanding vocals, and charismatic stage presence.

It’s Not Unusual, What’s New Pussycat?, Delilah, and Green, Green Grass of Home are among the favourites fans will be listening out for.

Support act The Stone Foundation has its own devoted following, known for delivering top-notch performances, characterized by skilled musicianship and full-band energy.

Later this year, Sir Tom will be taking his Come Gather Round Tour to the United States, with a series of concerts in theatres, halls, performing arts centres and parks.
