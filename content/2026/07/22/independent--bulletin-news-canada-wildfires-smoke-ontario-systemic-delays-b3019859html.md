Here’s why Canada’s wildfire season seems impossible to stop

Terrifying footage shows train engulfed by wildfire in Canada

Canada is experiencing a severe wildfire season, with over three million hectares burned, and federal forecasts predict continued high fire danger through August.

Despite leasing a fleet of firefighting aircraft for the 2026 season, their deployment is reactive and request-based, hindering effective early suppression, which is crucial for containing small ignitions.

Systemic delays were evident when Ontario's request for federal aid faced bureaucratic hurdles, and communities like Namaygoosisagagun First Nation suffered destruction due to slow response times.

Ontario's plan to purchase 11 new aircraft, a four-year commitment, will not address current fires and faces challenges with long delivery times and a shortage of trained pilots for existing equipment.

The article concludes that Canada lacks a proactive national strategy for deploying firefighting resources, leading to mass evacuations becoming the de facto and undesirable wildfire response.
