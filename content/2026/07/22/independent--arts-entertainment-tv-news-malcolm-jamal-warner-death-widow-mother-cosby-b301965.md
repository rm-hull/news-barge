Malcolm-Jamal Warner’s widow Tenisha Warner has filed a lawsuit against the actor’s mother, seeking to recover $1.2 million from his estate.

*The Cosby Show* star died in July 2025 in a drowning accident while on vacation in Costa Rica. He was 54.

The suit was filed Monday in DeKalb County Superior Court in Decatur, Georgia, against Pamela Warner, who the legal paperwork names as “successor trustee of the Warner family trust.”

Warner and his wife married on May 23, 2022, and signed a prenuptial agreement on May 15 of that year, according to the suit. They also share a nine-year-old daughter.

According to *CNN*, the suit claims that Malcolm-Jamal had “agreed to purchase and maintain a term life insurance policy on his life in the amount of $1,000,000.00” and would name his wife as the beneficiary, but failed to do so before his death.

Tenisha also alleged that other areas of their prenup were not fulfilled. The filing stated that the actor failed to make a pre-agreed payment to her of $16,000 per year on their marriage anniversaries, which would now amount to $50,542.46.

She also alleged that he did not maintain a Roth Individual Retirement Account for her with the maximum contribution per year, and he also did not pay her a promised salary of $5,000 per month for acting as his chief of staff, amounting to $195,000.

“In total, the amounts due and owing to Plaintiff under the Agreement total no less than $1,276,042.46, together with prejudgment and post-judgment interest and Plaintiff’s attorney’s fees and costs recoverable under the Agreement,” the suit stated.

The financial terms have also been seen in the prenuptial agreement, which was attached to the suit.

In a statement to*The Independent* provided by her attorney, Tenisha said that for the past year she “attempted to privately settle my beloved husband’s complicated estate and honor his last wishes.” 

![Malcolm-Jamal Warner died in July 2025 in a drowning accident while on vacation in Costa Rica. He was 54.](https://static.the-independent.com/2025/07/25/15/50/GettyImages-1456525939.jpg)

She said that her husband “had every intention” to provide for her and their daughter and “was close to finalizing a new estate plan to replace the stale plan created in 1996 when he was 26 years old, 20 years before we met and well before we gave birth to our beautiful daughter.”

“Sadly, he was unable to do so before we lost him. I have been struggling to care for our daughter as a single mom and now as her sole provider,” she said in the statement. “Legally, I had no choice but to take action to protect our rights before any potential statute of limitations expired.”

On Monday, the actor’s mother made a post on the Instagram account @mjwlivinglegacy to mark the one-year anniversary of his death, writing, “As a parent, we would never think that our children leave before we do. It is not the order of things... But what do you do when this is out of order and the child/children leave first?”

“This is where I found myself July 20, 2025. And this is what I awoke to July 20, 2026,” his mother, who early in Malcolm’s career also served as his manager, wrote. “How can I be here and my child is not?”

![Tenisha Warner, widow of Malcolm-Jamal Warner, filed a lawsuit against the actor’s mother, seeking to recover more than $1.2 million from his estate](https://static.the-independent.com/2026/07/22/15/53/Screenshot-2026-07-22-at-11-17-29.png)

She continued: “During my year long journey, I have discovered how cruel, evil and greedy a human can be. I have also discovered the depth of live, care, concern and support that humans are capable of. All of which I have been a recipient of. The good, the bad and the ugly. These are the lessons that apparently I will need as I continue my journey.”

She added that she was her son’s business partner for more than 40 years. “We together built the name and the business. He was always the front man.”

During a preview for an interview with*CBS Mornings* slated to air Thursday, Tenisha was asked whether she is close with her late husband’s mother.

“Right now, everybody — like, all of his family members and [those] who have lost him — they are grieving,” Tenisha told Gayle King. “You know, it’s been the biggest loss for all of us.”

“Right now, my daughter and I are just, like, in terms like [a unit],” she continued. “Look, the emotional capacity that I have can be very limited sometimes, and so, I have to for myself decide what do I have to give right now in this moment.”
