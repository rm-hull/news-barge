# Leeds bereaved families 'relieved' at Yvette Cooper pledge to prioritise maternity crisis

## Yvette Cooper was appointed Health Secretary on Monday, just days after the inquiry into maternity care at the Leeds Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust started.

The Leeds bereaved families group has said it is “relieved” to see new Health Secretary Yvette Cooper committing to improving maternity services as an “urgent priority”.

Ms Cooper was appointed on Monday, just days after the inquiry into maternity care at the Leeds Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust started.

It is being led by senior midwife Donna Ockenden, who recently found that around 520 mothers and babies suffered potentially avoidable harm or died due to “deeply embedded systemic failures” at a “toxic” NHS trust in Nottingham.

Ms Cooper said: “It’s a real honour to be appointed Health Secretary by Andy Burnham - 27 years after I worked on the first NHS Cancer Plan as a junior health minister.

“I’m looking forward to working urgently to improve maternity services, to take forward plans for social care, and to once again play a part in supporting our National Health Service, Labour’s proudest achievement.

“Most of all I’m looking forward to working with the dedicated NHS staff, carers, healthcare professionals and volunteers on whom we all depend.”

As the MP for Pontefract, Castleford and Knottingley in West Yorkshire it is likely Ms Cooper is already well aware of the maternity issues in the region.

In Leeds, a BBC investigation revealed the deaths of at least 56 babies and two mothers over the past five years may have been prevented, while legal firm Irwin Mitchell is representing more than 30 families in action against the trust.

Leeds Maternity Families Group, which represents bereaved parents, said in a statement: “We are relieved to see that the newly appointed Health Secretary, Yvette Cooper, has noted her commitment to improving maternity safety as an urgent priority, and we hope that she will meet with bereaved and harmed families across the country to hear directly what needs to change.

“Many families in Leeds have shouldered the immense burden of re-living traumatic experiences, sharing thoroughly collated research and explaining our reasons why further action is needed from the Department for Health and Social Care to address the harm occurring at a local and national level.

“Our hope is that Yvette Cooper will already grasp the extent of the problem, as many of her constituents rely on the care of Leeds Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust.

“We are imploring her to support and engage with the work being done by Donna Ockenden’s team in the Independent Review into its maternity services.

![Donna Ockenden](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/jpim-static/image/2026/07/19/18/cs-1436639f-9e2f-4f1b-ad5f-7f8c601715e1.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



“Families across the country are calling for a statutory public inquiry to establish the full extent of failings in maternity and neonatal care across the country. Those calls must be listened to by the new Health Secretary.

“We would welcome the opportunity to meet with Yvette Cooper and work with her to ensure the voices of families help shape the urgent improvements that are needed.”

On Saturday, Ms Ockenden launched the inquiry at the Park Plaza Hotel in Leeds on Saturday, inviting families to contribute to the terms of reference.

She said: “The review cannot start its work until the terms of reference have been agreed, and it is vital that those who have been affected by maternity and neonatal care at the trust have the opportunity to help shape the terms of reference.

"We appreciate that, for the families taking part in this review, it will involve revisiting some of the most painful experiences of their lives.

“That's why it is essential that the Ockenden Review is underpinned by a trauma-informed approach, and we are working to ensure that families feel informed, listened to and supported at all stages."
