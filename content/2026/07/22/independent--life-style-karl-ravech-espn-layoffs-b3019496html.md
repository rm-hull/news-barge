ESPN fans have raged against the company’s most recent round of layoffs that axed several of the network’s mainstays.

MLB announcer Karl Ravech, NFL MVP Cam Newton, longtime *SportsCenter* anchor David Lloyd, injury analyst Stephania Bell, former All-Pro linebacker and studio host Bart Scott and NFL Network analyst Charles Davis have all reportedly been impacted.

A 31-year veteran of the network, Ravech served as ESPN’s lead MLB play-by-play announcer, covered college basketball and anchored *SportsCenter*. He called the Dodgers-Phillies game Monday before reportedly learning his position was in jeopardy. When contacted by* The Athletic* Tuesday, 61-year-old Ravech said he had not yet been informed of his status.

Fans were furious to learn of Ravech’s fate, with one writing on X: “ESPN laid off Karl Ravech one month before the Little League World Series. Shameful. He was as good as it gets for that event, and another part of my childhood is no more.”

“I watched Karl Ravech host Baseball Tonight every night as a kid. 10 pm sharp. I loved the game and relished the highlights. He was a true pro. That era of ESPN, and sports coverage in general, is long gone,” another lamented.

“Karl Ravech has no seat at @espn while blowhard @stephenasmith makes millions upon millions of dollars per year and rarely has any clue what he's talking about. Unbelievable, ESPN,” someone else wrote while another said, “In the bigger picture: class, integrity, respect are all disappearing everywhere.”

Still, Ravech continued his personal podcast with professionalism only hours after learning his fate at ESPN.

On his *Dirt From The Dugout* podcast Tuesday night, Ravech said, “For many of you that are on here, you’re members and soon-to-be (guest) Adam Wainwright, there’s a lot going on with ESPN, we’re well aware of it, we’re not going to discuss that tonight.

“This is all about the parents’ dugout, it’s all about *Dirt From The Dugout*, it’s all about trying to help parents, which ultimately here is our goal and young players on their journeys.”

Ravech has not spoken out further about the ESPN news.

Many of the cuts are reportedly connected to ESPN’s integration with NFL Network, including the expected departure of NFL insider Tom Pelissero, one of the network’s top reporters, according to the outlet.

A memo from ESPN Chairman Jimmy Pitaro to employees Tuesday morning, obtained by *The Independent*, did not identify those impacted by the layoffs but said the cuts followed a review of the company’s teams, resources and structure after integrating acquired NFL assets.

ESPN acquired NFL Network in late January as part of a deal with the league that also gave the NFL a 10 percent stake in ESPN.

ESPN declined to provide further comment.
