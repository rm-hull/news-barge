Chart-topping artist Sam Smith has partnered with BBC Introducing to find 10 emerging acts to open their upcoming UK residency shows in Manchester and London.

The 34-year-old announced the initiative on Radio 1’s New Music Show with Jack Saunders.

Aspiring musicians can upload their music to the BBC Introducing Uploader between Wednesday 29 July and Wednesday 5 August, using the "Sam Smith" tag.

A BBC Introducing panel will shortlist entries, with Smith personally selecting the final 10.

Successful artists will perform at a residency show and be featured across BBC Introducing, the broadcaster's platform for new talent. Winners will be revealed later this summer.

Smith said: “It’s exciting to be part of this amazing new initiative. The discovery process for artists and audiences is so important and being able to play a part in that is fantastic.”

The singer will play two nights at Manchester’s Albert Hall on September 3 and 4, followed by eight nights at London’s Coliseum from 8 to 19 September.

The shows will follow the release of Smith’s latest album, *Hazel Eyes*, on 21 August.

Smith rose to fame when they were featured on Disclosure’s 2013 single, Latch, and earned their first number one as a featured artist on Naughty Boy’s La La La the same year.

They released their debut album, *In The Lonely Hour*, in 2014.

They have since won five Grammy Awards, three Brit Awards, an Academy Award and a Golden Globe.
