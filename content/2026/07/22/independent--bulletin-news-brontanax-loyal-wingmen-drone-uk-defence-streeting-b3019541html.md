Wes Streeting unveils ‘loyal wingman’ drones in ‘groundbreaking moment’ for UK’s defence

Defence secretary Wes Streeting unveiled the new British-made Brontanax drone, developed by BAE Systems, at the Farnborough Airshow, hailing it as a "groundbreaking moment for UK defence."

The Brontanax, an uncrewed autonomous Collaborative Combat Aircraft designed to fly alongside piloted jets as a "loyal wingman," is expected to be operational in the skies by 2027.

The Ministry of Defence awarded BAE Systems a £708 million contract extension to continue developing a next-generation combat aircraft as part of the Global Combat Air Programme.

Business secretary Jonathan Reynolds announced a £600 million package to support the British aerospace sector during his visit to the Farnborough Airshow.

Prime minister Andy Burnham, in a video message, highlighted the aerospace and defence sectors' role in reindustrialising the nation and creating jobs, particularly outside London and the South East.
