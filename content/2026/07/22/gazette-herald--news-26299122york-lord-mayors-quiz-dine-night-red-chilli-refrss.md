An evening of a Chinese banquet, quizzing and live entertainment is being laid on in York to raise money for the Lord Mayor’s Charity Appeal.

Organised by the Lord Mayor of York, Cllr Margaret Wells, the fundraiser is the civic party’s first official event as part of the 2026–2027 charity appeal.

It will take place at Red Chilli restaurant on George Hudson Street.

Cllr Wells said: "Help us make it a wonderful evening for my charities in York.

"We look forward to seeing you there."

Guests will be treated to a Chinese banquet, followed by a quiz hosted by York’s Town Crier, live music and a charity raffle.

Prizes will be awarded to the winning team, with a consolation prize for those finishing last.

The menu includes a chicken and sweetcorn or hot and sour soup, a salt and pepper sharing platter, and main dishes such as garlic broccoli, mapo tofu, curry chicken, black bean sizzling beef and jade fried rice.

Tickets are £25 per person, plus Eventbrite fees, with every penny of the £25 going to support the Lord Mayor’s Charity Appeal.

The organisers have confirmed that dietary requirements can be accommodated and tables can be arranged flexibly.

Guests are welcome to attend individually or as part of a group.
