YORK'S visitor information centre is set to close its doors ahead of a move to a new location.

The tourist information centre, which is currently based at 21 Parliament Street and run by Make It York, will move to 28 Back Swinegate later this month into what was previously Mayor David Skaith's shop Winston's.

The new centre will officially open its doors on Saturday, August 1 - Yorkshire Day.

Sarah Loftus, managing director of Make It York, said: "We’re delighted to be moving to our new home and look forward to welcoming everyone into the new space.

"The new premises will allow us to provide an even better experience while continuing to offer the expert advice, friendly service, and local knowledge that our team is known for."

During the move, which will take place between July 27 and July 31, a temporary visitor information point will operate on Parliament Street.

Make It York says the new location will offer more space for visitors whilst remaining 'easily accessible' in the heart of the city centre; the visitor centre is open seven days a week and provides personalised recommendations, free maps, guides, and ticketing services for attractions and transport.

It also sells the Visit York Pass, bus pass top ups, and offers a selection of locally made gifts and souvenirs.

More information about the new centre will be shared ahead of the opening.
