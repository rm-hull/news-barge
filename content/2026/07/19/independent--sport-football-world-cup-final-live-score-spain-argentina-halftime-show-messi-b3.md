Footballing giants Argentina and Spain will clash in a blockbuster World Cup final, as Lionel Messi graces this stage for perhaps the final time.

All-time great Messi, 39, will look to lead Argentina – who broke English hearts by coming from behind to win their semi-final – to a second straight World Cup trophy and the nation’s fourth overall, after triumphs in 1978, 1986 and 2022.

But they face formidable foes in the reigning European champions: Spain, who are seeking their second-ever world championship, having previously lifted the trophy in 2010. *La Roja* were superb as they**downed France with a clinical 2-0 win** in the last four and also* * have 19-year-old star Lamine Yamal in their ranks, a winger who can change any game in an instant.

There have been chaotic scenes outside the New York New Jersey Stadium ahead of the match however, with a Fifa system glitch meaning tickets are unable to be scanned properly and lengthy queues forming.

## System glitches causes chaos as fans descend on New Jersey

Fifa recommended that fans get to the New York/New Jersey Stadium four hours before kick-off - and we’ve begun to see why.

There are reports of long queues forming outside the ground with security staff saying that Fifa system glitches are not allowing their passes to be scanned.

Fans are also being misdirected around the stadium due to a lack of stewarding.

Meanwhile, Spanish outlet AS have claimed journalists were left queueing for hours to get into the ground.

## How is the golden boot race shaping up?

It’s been Lionel Messi vs Kylian Mbappe for the golden boot all tournament - but after yesterday’s remarkable 10-goal “bronze final”, Argentina’s talisman will require a performance for the books.

Mbappe took a giant leap towards the individual accolade with his double, which not only took him two clear of Messi but also broke the Argentinian’s all-time World Cup goalscoring record, which the 38-year-old only snatched from Miroslav Klose at the beginning of the campaign.

Messi will now need at least a hat-trick - or two goals and an assist - to beat Mbappe and top the standings.

## Messi v Yamal shows why there will never be another World Cup final like this one

It’s in elevated moments like this, when all the usual talk in football takes a more metaphysical hue, that even the most single-minded players can’t help looking for signs and omens.

In Spanish training, where Lamine Yamal is “wrapped in cotton” due to recent injuries, they have still been noting every turn and finish in training. “That’s how you’re going to win the World Cup final.”

It’s quite a prospect to consider.

With Argentina, there has meanwhile been more wistful commentary about how this is Lionel Messi’s first appearance at the Metlife Stadium since the 2016 Copa America final. At that point, his entire international legacy was completely different. Argentina had lost a third tournament final in three years and it looked like it was never going to happen. Messi himself seemed to confirm that as he announced his retirement. “I’ve done all I can, I’ve been in four finals and it hurts not to be a champion.”

## World Cup 2026 team of the tournament: The Independent’s best XI from historic event

**ForwardsKylian Mbappe (France)**- A third World Cup for the 27-year-old and a third outstanding display. Some of Mbappe’s goals have been sensational: a long-range stunner against Senegal, a dazzling solo effort against Sweden and an exquisite curling finish against Morocco. On eight goals, with a bronze final to come, he is also on the cusp of a second consecutive golden boot.

**Lionel Messi (Argentina)**- What more can be said about Argentina’s magnificent captain? At 39, he has made a mockery of those who thought his time at the top level was over. From his first-ever World Cup hat-trick in the opener against Algeria, it wasn’t until the quarters that he was shut out for the first time. And even in the semis, he set up both of Argentina’s goals. A near-certainty to win the golden ball, for what would be a third time, a second World Cup would be the icing on Messi’s unparalleled career cake.

**Erling Haaland (Norway)** - Led his country to a best-ever World Cup run, the last eight, and looked bang in the mood from the opener against Iraq, where he scored twice. However, his highlight was undoubtedly his brilliant double against Brazil; the first a stunning header, the second a pinpoint long-range strike. The only oddity? His relative anonymity in the quarter-final against England.

## Fans experiencing chaotic scenes at World Cup final stadium

Fans are experiencing chaotic scenes outside the New York New Jersey Stadium after a Fifa system glitch, reports Sky News, with lengthy queues already building.

Fans have also been warned to arrive four hours before kick-off, with panic spreading about entering the stadium on time.

Tickets and passes are not scanning properly, with extra security required and some have experienced misdirection to different entry points and gates around the stadium.
