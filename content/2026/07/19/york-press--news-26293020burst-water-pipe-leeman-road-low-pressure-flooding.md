## Homes left without water after pipe bursts in York suburb - roads closed

A burst water pipe in Leeman Road *(Image: Daniel Innes)*

A BURST water main has reportedly left homes without water in York and a main road has been blocked by water.

Reports are coming in of a burst pipe in Leeman Road this evening (July 19) which has caused flooding in the area and blocked a number of roads around Salisbury Terrace.

People across Acomb and Holgate are reporting low water pressure and some homes appear to have been left without water, with the Yorkshire Water website reporting low pressure for those in YO24 and YO26.

North Yorkshire Police are at the scene and have asked the public to avoid the area.

  An area cordoned off in Leeman Road 

*(Image: Jay Loft)*

A spokesperson for the force said: "Avoid Salisbury Road (off Water End) due to water in the road

"There is a significant flood affecting Salisbury Road, Salisbury Terrace and surrounding roads.

  Burst water main in Leeman Road, York 

*(Image: Daniel Innes)*

"We are at the scene now, assisting residents, alongside Yorkshire Water and North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service.

"Roads are impassable and closed. Please avoid the area and find alternative routes for your journeys."

  Burst water main in Leeman Road, York 

*(Image: Daniel Innes)*

Daniel Innes, who is at the scene, confirmed that at 9.55pm that the flow of water had stopped.

Yorkshire Water have been contacted for comment - once we receive their reply, we will update this story.

On their website, the water company has said the following: "You may have no water or low water pressure at the moment. We'll get your water back to normal as soon as possible.

  Fire crews in Leeman Road 

*(Image: Jay Loft)*

"It may be cloudy or discoloured for a while when it comes back, but you can clear this by running your tap for a few minutes.

"We're on-site dealing with the problem."
