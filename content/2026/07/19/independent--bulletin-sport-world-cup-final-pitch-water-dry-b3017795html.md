# Viewers hit out at ‘bobbly and dry’ World Cup final pitch

- Ian Wright led criticism of the World Cup final pitch at MetLife Stadium, describing it as 'bobbly and dry' and 'ridiculous, poor, terrible' after a goalless first half between Spain and Argentina.
- The playing surface, natural grass laid over an artificial one, has been a consistent point of contention throughout the World Cup due to its dry and patchy condition.
- Brazil's Vinicius Jr and France coach Didier Deschamps previously voiced concerns, stating the pitch made games slow, hindered rhythm, and was tough on players' muscles.
- The hot, dry North American summer weather is cited as a factor, with stadiums featuring retractable roofs not experiencing similar issues.
- Extended pre-game and half-time shows, which took place on the pitch, are also believed to have contributed to the poor condition of the playing surface.
