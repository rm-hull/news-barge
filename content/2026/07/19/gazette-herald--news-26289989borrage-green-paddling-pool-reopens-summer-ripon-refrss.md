A refreshed city paddling pool in North Yorkshire has reopened for the summer season.

Located in Ripon, the pool at Borrage Green became available to the public on Friday (July 17), following extensive surveys.

The works were carried out after issues were identified during routine maintenance checks.

Malcolm Taylor, executive member of highways and transport at North Yorkshire Council, said: "We would like to thank the public for their patience while essential work has been carried out to the three paddling pools.

"We have seen families making the most of the Harrogate pool in the good weather, and we are pleased that Ripon can open in time for the school holidays.

  The paddling pool in Borrage Green in Ripon is now open to the public 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Council)*

"The pool in Knaresborough is due to open soon, and we will keep the public updated when we have a date confirmed."

Maintenance at the Ripon pool included stripping old paint, treating the surface, applying a fresh coating, and checking the draining systems.

In Harrogate, the paddling pool in Valley Gardens reopened last month after being fully repainted, and a survey of the drainage system was carried out.

Work at Bebra Gardens in Knaresborough is entering its final stages.

Further updates about the paddling pools are available on the council’s website at www.northyorks.gov.uk/games-in-parks.
