Search the Public Notice PortalA housing development in North Yorkshire has moved a step closer following the sale of land to a national housebuilder.

Hallam Land has sold a site in Thorpe Willoughby near Selby to the Vistry Group to build 140 new homes, including 28 affordable properties.

Planning permission for the development was secured in December 2025.

Iain MacSween, managing director at Hallam Land, said: "Alongside providing homes for future generations, the development will deliver significant investment in local services and infrastructure, helping to ensure both new and existing residents can benefit from sustainable growth.

"The sale demonstrates the continued demand for well-planned, sustainable development opportunities that can be brought forward quickly to help address housing need.

"By securing planning permission and creating a scheme that delivers benefits for both future residents and the wider community, we have helped create an opportunity that can now move forward into delivery."

The development, which Hallam Land has promoted since February 2023, will include 2.1 hectares of public open space, children’s play areas, a sustainable drainage basin, trim trails, and circular walking routes.

Transport improvements will feature new bus stops along Leeds Road and a new pavement connection to enhance accessibility.

The scheme will also generate more than £1.17 million in Section 106 contributions toward local education, healthcare, public transport, and other community services.

Hallam Land is one of the UK’s leading land promotion businesses and has facilitated the delivery of more than 52,000 homes since 1990.

The company holds one of the UK’s largest strategic land portfolios, with the potential to deliver more than 100,000 new homes in future projects.

The Thorpe Willoughby development marks Hallam Land’s latest success in supporting housing growth, contributing to the continued growth of Thorpe Willoughby and the wider Selby area.

Hallam Land is also behind moves to build 1,100 homes on the site of Tockwith Airfield, near York, a 130-development that would double the size of Tockwith, which is between Wetherby and York.
