# Spain crowned World Cup winners in final against Argentina followed by ugly scenes

- Spain claimed their second World Cup title, defeating 10-man Argentina 1-0 in extra time.
- Ferran Torres scored the decisive goal in the 106th minute, securing the victory after a goalless 90 minutes.
- Argentina's Enzo Fernandez was sent off just before the end of normal time for a second yellow card, leaving his team with ten players.
- The result meant Lionel Messi was denied a World Cup triumph in what was likely his final appearance in the tournament.
- Ugly scenes marred the post-match celebrations. Argentina defender Nahuel Molina appeared to punch a Spain player, while midfielder Leandro Paredes was involved in a violent clash. Paredes was pictured shoving Spain's Eric Garcia by the throat, wrestling Gavi to the ground, and appearing to kick him.
