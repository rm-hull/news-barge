### What is the Public Notice Portal?

The Public Notice Portal carries statutory public notices published in local newspapers and is the fastest and most effective way of finding out what is happening in YOUR neighbourhood.

Search the Public Notice PortalYork is set to introduce new traffic and speed regulations in various areas.

The changes, proposed by the City of York Council, include prohibiting certain vehicle movements at junctions and altering speed limits in specific locations.

At the junction of Grimston Lane and Eastfield Lane in Dunnington, vehicles will no longer be allowed to turn right from Grimston Lane into Eastfield Lane.

Similarly, at the junction of Tea Room Square and Station Road in York, right turns from Tea Room Square into Station Road will be banned.

Church Lane in Dunnington will become a one-way street, allowing only north-west travel from its junction with York Street to its junction with Pear Tree Lane.

Speed limits will also be adjusted under the new proposals.

Grimston Lane and Pasture Croft in Dunnington will have a new speed limit of 20mph.

Eastfield Lane in Dunnington will have a new 30mph speed limit between certain points, replacing the current national speed limit.

The same 30mph limit will apply to North Lane in Huntington over a specified stretch.

Monks Cross Lane in Huntington will have a 40mph speed limit between two points, again replacing the national speed limit.

These changes are being made under the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984, following consultation with the Chief Officer of Police.

Anyone wishing to view the draft orders, reasons for the changes, and relevant maps can do so at the Reception, West Offices, Station Rise, York, during business hours.

Objections or representations must be submitted in writing by August 7, 2026.
