A 5.5-magnitude earthquake shook the Andes region of Peru, killing at least six people, authorities said Sunday. More than 30 people were injured and 300 have been displaced.

The US Geological Survey reported the quake struck on Saturday at 9.24pm, with its epicentre located 2km (1.2 miles) west-south-west of the city of Sicaya in Huancayo province. It was 10km (6.2 miles) deep.

Peru’s National Civil Defence Institute said in a statement that the total number of missing people is still unknown. Several buildings collapsed or suffered structural damage, including the local church and convent.

Luis Vásquez, head of the local civil defence office, told journalists that the use of rustic adobe materials in construction in that Andean area “has contributed to the greater impact and damage”.

Earthquakes are frequent in Peru, which is located in the Pacific “Ring of Fire”.

Images broadcast by local media captured the anguish of victims’ relatives in one of the hardest-hit areas, the agricultural region of Chongo Bajo, where residents huddled under blankets outside severely damaged homes. Animals were also seen under the rubble.

![Residents gingerly navigate the rocks and debris of buildings that collapsed after the earthquake](https://i.guim.co.uk/img/media/fa9f0d5c759e68df5b9084853e626b2c41bbfc09/0_0_3681_2454/master/3681.jpg?width=445&dpr=1&s=none&crop=none)

“My home has been destroyed,” Hermenegilda Guamalato told a local radio station as she sought a place to stay with her three children in the neighbouring district of Huayucachi.

In 2007, a 7.9-magnitude earthquake struck the province of Pisco in the Ica region, killing nearly 600 people.
