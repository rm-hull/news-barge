Three York and North Yorkshire drivers who failed to respond to court summons telling them they were charged with motoring offences have been sentenced.

All their cases went ahead at a dedicated motoring offences court at Harrogate Magistrates Courthouse.

Jack Rowntree, 19, of Cleveland Road, Catterick Garrison, was convicted in his absence of failure to tell police who was driving his car when it was allegedly breaking traffic laws. He was banned from driving for six months, fined £600 and ordered to pay £120 prosecution costs and a £266 statutory surcharge.

Joseph Hunter Smith, 38, of Water Lane, York, was convicted in his absence of using a vehicle at Clifton Moor without insurance and given six penalty points. He was fined £180 and ordered to pay £120 prosecution costs.

Liam Bowman, 30, of Falsgrave Road, Scarborough, was convicted in his absence of using a car on the A170 near Scarborough without insurance. He was given six penalty points, fined £900 and ordered to pay £120 prosecution costs and a £360 statutory surcharge.
