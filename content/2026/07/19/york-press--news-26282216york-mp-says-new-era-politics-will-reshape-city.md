## York MP says new era of politics will 'turn the tables' for the city

Rachael Maskell, right, joins the campaign for Andy Burnham, second from left, in the Makerfield by-election Image: Supplied

It’s not just a fresh start for Labour, it’s a completely new approach for everyone, WRITES YORK CENTRAL MP RACHAEL MASKELL.

Things are about to radically change, and I hope you are ready, because everyone has a part to play.

I have long been calling for politics to change. The privileged politics of the elite fails communities like ours, where MPs are expected to follow the whip, not the people’s priorities. Well I refused to play this game, instead speaking for us, for York.

When I listened to the elderly and refused to support Winter Fuel Payment cuts, Government eventually relented and reversed their decision. When I called out child poverty, we lifted the child poverty cap. And knowing how hard disabled people find getting by, I fought the cuts to Personal Independence Payments, forcing Government to change its mind, and drop its proposals; losing the Labour whip.

Whether calling for action on Gaza; a clear, competent and compassionate immigration system which is both fair and firm; the right to protest; or more sustainable nature and climate policies, I have stood up for the many in our community, not the few in Westminster, because this is the Labour approach I believe in and it is the local approach I trust.

I have been ‘punished’ but never regretted making sure you were seen and heard, and when forcing climbdowns was simply relieved. But I haven’t just come into politics to fight, but to create a better tomorrow, to rebuild a society that works for all, and to confront the social injustices which hold people back.

Andy Burnham MP is about to turn the tables. The stale, failed politics of Westminster is about to be turned on its head, embracing the regions and nations, towns and cities; and with a ‘can do’ approach, seek to sort out the challenges people and place face.

Leadership is about listening. Ensuring that all can be on board, bringing communities together around shared priorities, following the evidence, the experiences and the express wishes of people. Labour is about ensuring that no-one is left behind, that we have a more equitable and just society. Put the two together and here in York we have the opportunity for everyone to have a real say, breaking with the past system, to solve the challenges of today and to dream and work towards our hopes for the future.

It is now our chance to get things working and get things right.

While out on the doorstep and in the community every weekend, as I will be over the summer, and while I consult you on major policy shifts, now we can go deeper, using all our experiences to seek out the solutions we need.

This gives us a real opportunity to rebuild pride in place, to snag the little things that trip us up and stop us getting on, while tackling the major policies that make life tough, unfair or challenging.

In a volatile point in history, where the geopolitical risks are high and impacting on our day to day, we have to rebuild our resilience and our readiness for the future. How we respond to the climate crisis, how we sustain our values in the global economy, and how we embrace and control technology while building energy security, financial security, national security and personal security.

Whether health, justice, education or local government, we need better accountability and better services. Focusing on transition points to optimise the lasting impact while ensuring everyone is included and everyone belongs. The amazing people working across our public sector know only too well how things can get better for all. Businesses too need space and scope to flourish, creating jobs and economic success, innovating and strengthening our high street and city.

As Andy Burnham MP brings us together to solve the challenges of our age, it will be a fresh start. I am excited, because I know the difference it will make in York if all play their part, and I mean all.

So let’s come together as a city and make every moment count in this new era of politics. You all have your part to play and as our new Prime Minister says, so we can have “hope in every heart”.
