Marking its 20th anniversary, Leeds Pride was held over two days for the first time, with Sunday's parade bringing a sea of colour, music and celebration to the city's streets.

The inaugural event, then known as Leeds Gay Pride, attracted around 6,500 people, but organisers expected around 75,000 visitors across the weekend in 2026.

Nearly 250 local businesses backed this year's celebrations, with organisations, community groups and performers joining the parade through the city centre.

Rainbow flags, live music, colourful costumes and joyful crowds transformed Leeds into one of the UK's largest Pride celebrations.
