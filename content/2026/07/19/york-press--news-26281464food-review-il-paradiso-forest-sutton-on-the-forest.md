## Italian restaurant worth the drive for delicious food and friendly service

The Crostino Di Burrata was a work of art in its presentation *(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

A few weeks back, we said farewell to our former editor Nigel Burton.

We decided to go out for a meal to Il Paradiso Del Cibo in Walmgate, close to our office.

As always, everyone enjoyed their lunch, the food was great, as was the service; and it was also excellent value.

This got me thinking about its sister venue at Sutton-on-the-Forests, Il Paradiso on the Forest.

It too has great reviews so I thought it would be worth the ride out one weekend.

  Bruschette Alla Romana made for a tasty entree 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Dad was driving but he didn’t fancy lunch and instead we arrived just after 3pm on a Saturday.

The place was empty, with some of the staff enjoying their lunch.

We were shown to a table and shortly afterwards we ordered drinks.

  The kings prawns were very large and a treat to eat 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Dad and I went for a pint of Moretti (£6.50) and mum a 175ml glass of pinot Grigio (£8.40). As it was hot, we also ordered two bottles of sparkling water (£3.25 each).

We ordered a couple of starters to share, going for Bruschetta alla Romana (£7.95) and Crostini di Burrato (£10.50).

I went to spend a penny and, on my return, there was a paper bag with slices of crusty bread inside and on the table, plates containing olive oil and balsamic vinegar.

  A delightful and decent portion of seabass with the trimmings 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

This is not something we do at home, but mum and dad thoroughly enjoyed dipping the bread in the olive oil and vinegar. Possibly a whole new experience for them. I enjoyed dipping the bread as well.

Not long after the starters arrived and both dishes looked at treat. They were too as we all enjoyed a piece of the bruschetta, which had a pleasant tomatoey flavour.

We shared the Crostini di Burrato, so mum and dad could try some too, and the thinly cut parma ham. The ham and the cheese paired well and the bread had a pleasing grilled aftertaste.

  The Ravioli Special tasted much better than it looked 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Next came the mains and mum went for the gamberoni alla sarda (£22.95), dad went for the seabass (£22.95) and I went for the Ravioli special (£22.95)- all from the extensive special shown on a blackboard on the wall.

The regular menu also had an extensive range, delivering something for everyone.

The menus are traditional Italian, using seasonal ingredients (95 per cent from Italy, the rest sourced locally from well-known organic producers).

  Go on, enjoy dipping your bred in olive oil and balsamic vinegar! 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Again, the wait wasn’t too long and with mum and dad’s appetites not being what they were, I got to try some of their meals as well.

Mum had been warned by the manager, who served us, that the king prawns were served in their shell and they would be messy.

As advertised on the specials board, the king prawns were very large. They had been oven baked with vernacca wine, rosemary and chilli and served with a rocket risotto.

  The lemoncello tiramisu was a light and fluffy treat to finish a lovely meal 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Yes, the prawns were messy but they were a decent size, there was plenty of them, they were easy to peel and very tasty. The risotto was also smooth and creamy and again, well recommended.

Dad was pleased with his seabass and soon polished it off. It came with mixed leaves salad and roasted potatoes.

My Ravioli Special featured fresh pasta filled with porcini mushroom served in as slow-cooked low temperature sauce, with pieces of beef, pork, herbs and topped with parmesan shavings.

  You won't be bored with these specials! 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

There was a pleasant taste of mushrooms in the large ravioli pieces and the pieces of pork and beef were deliciously tender. It might not have looked the prettiest of plates, but it was enjoying, filling and a decent portion.

Now, normally, I tend to be a little full for dessert but the range looked interesting and it included lemoncello tiramisu (£6.50). It was delightfully light and fluffy with a strong flavour of both lemon and tiramisu, making for a perfect end to a delicious meal.

By this time a young family had arrived and they were ordering pizzas and a lasagne.

Mum had commented on the restaurant lacking atmosphere as it was empty when we first arrived. This did not bother me too much, so if you prefer to dine in a more buzzing setting, perhaps you might want to dine later.

  Traditional yet contemporary interior 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

We were told that they had plenty of bookings for later in the day, with the last bookings at 7.30pm due to the England game against Norway that evening.

I asked for the bill and it came to a very reasonable £119.70, considering we had picked our main courses from the specials menus. The pizza and pasta options tend to be much cheaper.

Well, it was time to go and we reflected on the pleasant meal that we had enjoyed, even if the restaurant had been quieter than we would have preferred. But, of course, it would be busy later.

  Under starters orders for these authentic Italian delights! 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Il Paradiso on the Forest is most certainly a destination venue, due to its rural setting several miles north of the York Outer Ring Road.

It is well worth the drive and it is also worthy of the high ratings given on the reviews websites for friendly and efficient service, not to mention the delicious, authentic Italian food, served in pleasant surroundings.

  A pleasant and contemporary interior 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

TripAdvisor awards 4.6 stars out of five, based on 2024 reviews.

The most recent review gave five stars for “fantastic Italian Food.”

It also said: “Fantastic restaurant serving delicious meals with a great vibe. We were looked after by Elisa who made sure we had a great time by being attentive but not overbearing. 8 of us in the party and we all agreed we’d be going back soon.”

Google gives an even better 4.8 stars out of five, based on 453 Google reviews.

The latest also gave five stars and said: “Amazing place, I've been in Paradiso a few times with my family and the food is always amazing. The staff and friendly, and in general the atmosphere is great.

  “Last visit we were on the outside garden which was fabulous.

  Thank you, and see you soon!”

Indeed, I might have to call back one evening with my vegetarian friends when it is a little busier. I am sure they will enjoy it too!
