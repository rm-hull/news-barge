# Third US service member death within days as Iranian drone explodes during ‘controlled detonation’

- An American service member was killed and another wounded during the controlled detonation of an Iranian drone on Saturday, raising the US death toll in the conflict to at least 17.
- This incident occurred after two other service members were killed in an attack on Friday, with unidentified remains later recovered during a search for a missing US troop.
- The ongoing conflict has resulted in over 400 US troops wounded, and a collapsed ceasefire now risks a full-scale escalation.
- US forces have conducted eight consecutive nights of strikes, while Iran has intensified missile and drone attacks on US allies and assets in the Persian Gulf.
- President Donald Trump's administration initiated a new wave of strikes, prompting Iranian retaliation and warnings of a "full-scale offensive" if US attacks continue, with Iran's supreme leader dismissing a previous peace deal.
