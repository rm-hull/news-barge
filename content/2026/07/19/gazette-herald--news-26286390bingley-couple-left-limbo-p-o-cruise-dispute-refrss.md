A COUPLE have criticised communication from P&O Cruises after a dispute involving accessibility arrangements for friends they were due to holiday with left them unsure whether a long-planned cruise would go ahead.

  Jonathan Meares, from Harden, had booked a September 2025 cruise aboard *Iona* alongside friends David and Tracy Gough and their two sons, who use wheelchairs.

Mr Meares said the difficulties began when the Gough family were informed shortly before departure that there were concerns about arrangements for their sons.

## 'Nobody seemed able to give clear answers'

According to Mr Meares, the uncertainty that followed became one of the most frustrating parts of the experience.

He said both families spent weeks trying to understand whether the cruise could proceed and what options were available to them.

Mr Meares said communication around the situation left them feeling confused about whether a solution could be found.

The families had already arranged accommodation and transport ahead of the sailing.

## P&O says it worked to support both families

P&O Cruises said it took steps to resolve the situation and ultimately secured two evacuation chairs to enable the Gough family's sons to travel.

A spokesperson said: "We are very sorry these guests had issues with their booking. We subsequently did everything we reasonably could to support all parties in being able to travel."

The company said the Gough family later accepted a transfer to a different sailing, which it described as a goodwill gesture following an error by the travel agent.

## Disagreement over linked bookings

A key point of contention concerned the relationship between the two families' bookings.

Mr Meares said he felt that because the holidays had been planned together, any solution should have considered both families.

However, P&O said the bookings were separate and linked only for dining purposes.

The cruise line said Mr and Mrs Meares were not prevented from travelling and that no restrictions were placed on their reservation.

## Complaint response delays acknowledged

P&O acknowledged that it took longer than expected to respond to complaints about the booking and offered future cruise credit as a gesture of goodwill.

Despite that, Mr Meares said he remains unhappy with how information was communicated throughout the process.

The company, meanwhile, maintains it acted appropriately and did everything reasonably possible to enable the families to travel.
