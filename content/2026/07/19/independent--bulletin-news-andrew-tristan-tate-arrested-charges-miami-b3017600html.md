Influencers Andrew and Tristan Tate were arrested by U.S. Marshals in Miami on Saturday following the announcement of 38 new charges by UK officials.

The new charges include rape, arranging or facilitating trafficking for sexual exploitation, and offences relating to indecent images of a child.

The UK is seeking to extradite the brothers to face prosecution for these new allegations, which reportedly involve four victims and occurred between July 2010 and August 2017, in addition to several pre-existing charges.

Andrew Tate faces charges including trafficking, assault occasioning actual bodily harm, and indecent images, while Tristan Tate faces charges including sexual assault, rape, and trafficking.

The Tates deny all wrongdoing, with their lawyer describing the charges as “madness” and alleging “political persecution” aimed at disrupting judicial processes in the United States.
