In a summer when football has dominated conversations, from World Cup fever to pre-season preparations closer to home, York City supporters know better than most the emotional swings the game can bring.

Here we take a nostalgic look back on some of the football highs and lows in old photos for York City FC including the triumphs and disappointments.

Supporters may still remember the low of April 2016, when relegation was confirmed and the reality hit hard. It was a familiar kind of anguish, the sort football has a habit of delivering, whether on the world stage or at York City's former stomping ground, Bootham Crescent.

Yet the beauty of the game lies in those contrasts of despair to joy.

Even now, as friendlies against Peterborough United, Crawley Town and Bristol Rovers offer a first glimpse of the road ahead, there is comfort in looking back.

  1995: York City's Paul Barnes takes on Manchester United's Denis Irwin in the first leg at Old Trafford - which ended in a 3-0 win for City. 

*(Image: The Press)*

There was that extraordinary FA Cup run in 1955, when Bootham Crescent resounded with belief. Tottenham Hotspur arrived as giants of the game, and left humbled. In front of 21,000 fans, City surged to a famous 3–1 win, a performance so assured it left Spurs captain Danny Blanchflower admitting they were “speechless”. The run carried the Minstermen all the way to a semi-final replay.

  February 1955, York City v Spurs Alf Ramsey rushes across in vain as Billy Fenton swoops to score City's second goal. 

*(Image: The Press)*

Two decades later came another leap. Between 1974 and 1976, York mixed it in the Second Division, taking on some of the country’s biggest names.

  York City skipper Roger Jones lifts the 1984 Division Four championship trophy. 

*(Image: The Press)*

Then came history in 1983/84, a record-breaking season that saw City become the first Football League side to reach 100 points.

  1984: City celebrate becoming the first football league club to break the 100 points barrier. 

*(Image: The Press)*

Cup magic followed in 1985, when Arsenal were beaten under the Bootham Crescent lights with Keith Houchen’s late penalty sealing a famous victory.

And who could forget Old Trafford in 1995?

  May 21, 2012. York City manager Gary Mills holds both Wembley trophies as an open top bus tours the city centre. 

*(Image: The Press)*

A fearless York side dismantling Manchester United 3–0 away from home. Then, there was Wembley in 2012 - two visits, two wins, and a city united in celebration.

From World Cup highs to local heartbreak, football is never short of drama. For York City supporters, the memories remain and reminds fans that brighter days have a habit of returning.
