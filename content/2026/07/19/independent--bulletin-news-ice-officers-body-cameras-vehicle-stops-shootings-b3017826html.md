# ICE introduces new body camera rule after multiple fatal shootings

- Immigration and Customs Enforcement agents will now be required to record traffic stops with at least one body camera, according to White House border czar Tom Homan.
- The new mandate comes after ICE agents, operating without cameras, fatally shot two people within a week in Houston, Texas, and Biddeford, Maine, prompting widespread protests and calls for accountability.
- Homan, a proponent of body cameras, stated they “exonerate more law enforcement than they convict” and allow the public to see officers' actions, while also attributing delays in their rollout to previous Democrat-led government shutdowns.
- ICE confirmed that over half of its field offices are already equipped with body cameras, with the remaining offices slated to receive them within 60 days.
- The shootings have intensified scrutiny on ICE, with critics arguing cameras are essential given the administration's history of misleading claims regarding lethal force, and President Donald Trump ordering the resumption of vehicle stops after a brief pause, calling them “effective Crime Fighting tools.”
