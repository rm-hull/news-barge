It’s finally here – at 3pm ET / 8pm BST, Spain and Argentina step onto the pitch at the MetLife Stadium in New Jersey for the 2026 World Cup Final.

Arguably, today’s match is *the* sporting event of the year – and as such, fans will be tuning in from every corner of the globe.

If you’re hoping to catch the match but want to keep your connection secure, or want to access streams from your home region while travelling abroad, a VPN is a great way to establish a fast, safe connection to your streaming service of choice.

It can be tempting to pick up one of the best free VPNs for your World Cup watch party, but these aren’t always as reliable as their paid counterparts. PrivadoVPN is one of the more cost effective options on the market for a reliable, fast, and secure paid VPN, offering real upgrades over its free counterpart.

## Free VPNs come with compromises

 ![PrivadoVPN app on a mobile phone](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/zF4fnJoUbs2RFkDCZZ9rR.jpg) 


Free VPNs include restrictions that aren’t present on their paid counterparts. These might not be an issue for everyday browsing, but can be a real problem for video streaming.

Issues with free VPNs can include restrictive data usage limits, limited server locations or connection to a random location, and slower network speeds.

For crucial sports events like the World Cup Final, the last thing you want to deal with is buffering, a blurry, low-resolution picture, or a connection to the wrong country.

Luckily, upgrading to a paid VPN doesn’t have to be expensive. For a competitive price, PrivadoVPN’s paid edition does away with its free version’s 10GB data cap and ups its single connection limit to 10 devices at once.

## Other options

If PrivadoVPN isn’t for you, there are other options on the market. TechRadar’s VPN Editor Rob Dunne has made his pick for the World Cup Final with NordVPN, which tops our list of the best VPNs overall.

If you’re looking to save money, our pick for the best cheap VPN is Surfshark VPN, which offers a comprehensive feature set but isn’t *quite* as cheap as Privado VPN.
