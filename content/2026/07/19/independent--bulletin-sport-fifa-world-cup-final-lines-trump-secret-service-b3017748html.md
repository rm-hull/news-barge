# World Cup final chaos as attendees face long lines and Secret Service screenings

- World Cup fans faced some chaos at the New York New Jersey stadium Sunday ahead of the final featuring Spain against defending champions Argentina.
- Spain is aiming for their second world title, while Argentina seeks to retain their crown, hoping to cap off Lionel Messi's illustrious career with another victory.
- Concerns over air quality from Canadian wildfires and recent high temperatures have dissipated, with clear skies and pleasant weather greeting attendees in East Rutherford.
- Heightened security measures were implemented due to the expected attendance of President Donald Trump, resulting in extensive Secret Service screenings and long three-hour queues for journalists, entertainers, and volunteers. At one point, fans reported a Fifa system glitch that was not allowing passes to be scanned.
- Fans, dressed in their national colours, began arriving hours before the 3 p.m. ET kickoff, with a pre-match closing ceremony featuring performances by Post Malone and Tom Cruise scheduled.
