A children’s cycling club in York could receive a £3,000 grant to help get more young riders on the move.

Pedal Pals Club, an inclusive cycling group for children with special educational needs and disabilities (SEND), has been shortlisted for the funding through Caremark’s UK-wide Community Care Fund.

The club supports children to gain confidence, develop independence and enjoy physical activity in a welcoming, after-school setting.

David Glover, chief executive of Caremark, said: "Caremark’s work is built around helping people live well in the place they call home, but we know that a good quality of life is also shaped by the community around you.

"It is shaped by whether people feel safe, supported, included and connected.

"That is why we launched the Community Care Fund.

"We wanted to recognise the local projects and organisations that are making life better for people in their communities, often with limited resources and without the recognition they deserve."

Pedal Pals is one of 12 regional finalists across the UK.

If it wins, the group will use the funding to purchase adapted bikes so that more children of all abilities can take part in regular cycling sessions.

Public voting is open via Caremark’s Facebook page until September 1, 2026.
