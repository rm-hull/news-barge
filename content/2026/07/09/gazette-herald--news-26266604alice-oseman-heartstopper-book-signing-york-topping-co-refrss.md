HUNDREDS of people lined up in York city centre last night in a heatwave to attend a book signing with an international best-selling author.

Queues stretched down Museum Street and into Lendal yesterday evening (July 8) for an event at Topping and Company Bookshop which saw 450 people turn out to meet author Alice Oseman.

She visited York as part of a nationwide tour of bookshops across the UK and Ireland to celebrate the release of the final book in her best-selling graphic novel Heartstopper series - which tells the story of teenage couple Nick and Charlie as they fall in love.

The event, whilst ticketed, still attracted huge crowds who queued from 5.15pm for several hours for a chance to meet the author, take photos, and have their books signed.

Saskia Topping, company director said that the night had been a “huge success” – marking one of the bookshop’s largest events since opening its doors in the city last month.

She added that a number of the shop’s booksellers are huge fans of Alice Oseman's work and have found that the novels have resonated profoundly with many people.

  The queues stretched to Lendal 

*(Image: Ben Hardy)*

One bookseller, Alice, said: "Her characters and creations have given readers a sense of belonging, relatability, and comfort - especially those who are embarking on a journey to discover their identity and sense of self."

The books, whilst focused on the relationship between Nick and Charlie, also explores a range of other topics including sexuality, gender, mental health, intimacy, and coming of age, with a diverse cast of characters.

In total, the graphic novels have sold over 10 million copies worldwide, going straight to number one on The Sunday Times and the New York Times bestseller lists.

  Author Alice Oseman 

*(Image: Topping & Co)*

Heartstopper has also found success on the small screen with an Emmy award-winning, BAFTA-nominated live-action Netflix adaptation starring Kit Connor and Joe Locke.

Oscar winning actress Olivia Coleman was previously part of the cast, but has since been replaced by Anna Maxwell-Martin for a feature-length finale film – titled Heartstopper Forever – which will be released next Friday (July 17).
