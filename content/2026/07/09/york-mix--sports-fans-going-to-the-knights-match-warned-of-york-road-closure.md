York Knights are in action this evening, hoping to make it three wins on the bounce.

They take on Hull KR at the LNER Community Stadium in round 18 of the Super League, kick-off 8pm.

But fans going to the match are being warned that roadworks could affect their journey.

On the York RLFC website, supporters have been given this information: “On Thursday evening there is a partial closure at Hopgrove Roundabout, near to the stadium, from 8pm to 6am.

“The eastbound carriageway will be open, but westbound carriageway will be closed. This will affect match traffic leaving the game heading west on the A64, and on towards the A1079 towards Hull. Diversions will be in place.

“Eastbound traffic will be diverted using A1237 to Little Hopgrove roundabout and back.”

Local westbound traffic will be diverted from Little Hopgrove through the city centre to rejoin at Grimston Bar via this diversion:

The A64 westbound North Lane to Grimston Bar closure runs nightly from 8pm to 6am, finishing on Saturday morning.

National Highways says it’s for carriageway improvements.
