A LARGE fire in a North Yorkshire village has been stopped by fire crews.

Five North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service crews from Snaith, Easingwold, Thirsk, Acomb and Tadcaster were called to a fire in Kelfield yesterday (July 8) at 6.18pm.

The fire affected 35 acres of stubble and standing crops.

"Crews surrounded the fire before it spread to properties and extinguished it using multiple hose reel jets and wildfire tools," said a spokesperson for the force
