A local legal firm has announced double-digit growth, a rise in revenue, and new senior hires.

Knights, which has an office in Victorian Hudson Quarter in York, reported a 28 per cent increase in annual revenue to £207.7 million and a 19 per cent rise in profit to £33.2 million for the 2026 financial year.

The firm, which employs 1,400 people nationwide and 138 in Yorkshire, also welcomed four new partners to its commercial team.

David Beech, chief executive of Knights, said: "I am pleased to report that we grew revenues by 28 per cent year on year, doubling them over the last five years.

"We also maintained strong underlying profit before tax margins while investing in strengthening our operational platform.

"We delivered a strong performance across Yorkshire during the year, as we did across the business, with double-digit organic growth in the second half, contributions from recent acquisitions, and good cash generation assisting the self-funding of acquisition costs in the year."

The company advised on several major transactions during the year, including the £11 million sale of The Ridings Shopping Centre in Wakefield and supported Yorkshire Trading Company with seven store acquisitions.

Knights also promoted seven staff members and welcomed two newly qualified solicitors across its Yorkshire offices.
