There are more allergens in the air around us than ever before. Smoke particles, dust, pet dander, you name it, and don’t get me started on the dreaded pollen – making it feel like hay fever season’s lasting all year long. Outdoor pollution, even allergens in bedding and soft furnishings that get recirculated around the home, can contribute to low air quality.

The best air purifiers filter particles that can irritate the respiratory system that can trigger the sneezes, so you should notice your home starts to feel less dusty and stuffy as soon as you get one. “For many people living with an indoor allergy, their home is not a sanctuary. Instead, it can be a hazard to their health,” says Simone Miles, chief executive at Allergy UK. “Having access to cleaner air can make a world of difference,” she adds.

## What are the different types of air purifier filters?

“If I was buying an air purifier, I would only stick to a filter-based air purifier,” Henry Burridge, associate professor in environmental fluid mechanics at Imperial College London, says. “I would also look for the clean air delivery rate (CADR) through the filter system.” The different types of filter include:

- **HEPA (high-efficiency particulate air):** Trap approximately 99.97 per cent of airborne particles of at least 0.3 microns in size, making them great for allergens and germs.
- **Activated carbon:** Captures gases, VOCs (Volatile Organic Compounds) and household odours to make the air smell fresher.
- **UV-C and photocatalytic:** Can help to eliminate bacteria and viruses.

Studies have shown air purifiers to work effectively in the home. In one 2021 study, researchers from Public Health England found that portable air purifiers successfully removed 22.6 and 92 per cent of particulate matter 2.5 microns in size found in homes.

I’ve tracked how each performs against everyday irritants like dust and smoke, and how quickly they make a noticeable difference to indoor air quality.
