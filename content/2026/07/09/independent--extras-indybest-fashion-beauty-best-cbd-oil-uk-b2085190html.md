From CBD shampoo and chocolate to skincare and even lubricants, you've no doubt seen CBD in just about every form imaginable over the past decade. But CBD oil – the original oral supplement taken as drops – is where it all began. Today, millions of people around the world use CBD oil as part of their daily routine to help maintain balance in both mind and body.

Its popularity exceeded vitamin C at one point, and many avid fans swear by the broad, sometimes transformational effects, ranging from improving sleep, boosting mood, soothing physical and emotional discomfort and even enhancing skin health. But how can one supplement apparently have such far reaching benefits? Although it’s not a panacea, there is well understood science behind the mechanisms that make this possible.

CBD oil is extracted from the cannabis sativa (hemp) plant, from the cannabinoid rich flowers, leaves and stem, not the seeds, which is where it differs from hemp seed oil.

These flowers contain cannabinoids and terpenes that interact with your body via your Endocannabinoid System (ECS) - a complex network of receptors which makes up one of the most important physiological systems in the human body. This system works alongside other systems, such as your immune system and nervous system, and internally produced endocannabinoids (Anandamide and 2-AG) to keep everything in balance. You’ll find it just about everywhere in the human body – from the surface layers of your skin right down into your muscles, bones and even cell walls.

For best results, take CBD oil twice a day for a least a month. Hold the oil under your tongue for one minute to allow the oil to absorb directly into the bloodstream for the highest bioavailability (and best value for money!).
