## Power station near North Yorkshire town named UK's biggest emitter

Drax Power Station near Selby *(Image: Pic supplied)*

A North Yorkshire power station has once again been named the UK's largest emitter of carbon dioxide equivalent (CO2e).

Drax Power Station near Selby topped the list for the eleventh consecutive year, releasing a record 14.1 million tonnes of carbon dioxide equivalent (CO2e) in 2025.

The analysis comes from energy think tank Ember, which found that Drax's biomass emissions were four times higher than those of the Pembroke gas power station, the UK's second-largest emitter.

But Drax said Ember’s analysis overlooked globally recognised carbon accounting practices, contradicting what leading climate scientists say about sustainable biomass.

Ember analyst Josie Murdoch said: "Drax’s eleventh year running as the UK’s biggest emitter is coming at a high cost.

"Burning biomass for power generation will never stop being bad value for billpayers or our environment and must be phased out entirely."

Drax also emitted more CO2e than the top six gas power stations combined.

Despite the high emissions, Drax received £999 million in subsidies in 2025 for biomass power generation—equivalent to £13 per UK household.

The majority of fuel used at the site is imported, with more than 99 per cent sourced from outside the UK.

Most of this (86.7 per cent) comes from the US and Canada, while another 13.2 per cent is sourced from Europe, mainly Latvia.

Less than one per cent of feedstock is currently sourced from the UK.

Independent bodies have previously recommended the development of domestic biomass supplies.

Drax entered its final year of high subsidy payments in April 2026 before a new public subsidy scheme begins in April 2027.

Emissions under the new scheme are expected to fall to about 5.8 million tonnes, but Drax is still likely to remain the UK’s largest emitter until at least 2030.

A Drax spokesperson said: "Ember’s analysis once again overlooks globally recognised carbon accounting practices, contradicting what the world’s leading climate scientists at the UN’s IPCC say about sustainable biomass.

"Last year Drax Power Station generated a record 15 TWh of dispatchable, renewable power, demonstrating the critical role our business plays in supporting the country’s energy security and helping to keep the lights on for millions of people no matter the weather.

"The support arrangements that underpin our sustainable biomass generation are not unique and are similar to the support granted to other forms of UK power generation.

"We are paid for the power we produce; this includes our hydro and pumped storage sites alongside biomass power generation at Drax Power Station."
