A NEIGHBOUR has spoken out after a blaze severely damaged a house in York suburb.

  **As reported by The Press this morning** (Thursday, July 9), the fire broke out just before 10pm yesterday in Rowan Avenue, New Earswick with three crews of firefighters called out. The flames started in the garden of the property and spread to the house and hedgerow.

The neighbour, who wanted to remain anonymous but lives nearby, told The Press that she had become aware of the fire when smoke began to come in through her windows and a crowd of people formed outside in the street.

She said: “It looks like the fire started from the homeowner's shed, although we don’t know what caused it, and it quickly spread through the roof of the house. I think the two houses either side were impacted too and the heat shattered some of the windows.”

The neighbour said that she was told that the homeowner was uninjured – something confirmed by North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service and Yorkshire Ambulance Service – and is being supported by family.

She added: “The emergency services responded pretty quickly - probably within 15 minutes of us noticing the fire - and got it under control in around 30 minutes.

“The cherry picker continued through the night until quite late, probably around midnight to strip the roof and ensure all the embers were put out.”
