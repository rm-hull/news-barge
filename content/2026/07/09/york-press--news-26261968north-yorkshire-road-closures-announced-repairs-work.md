Search the Public Notice PortalSeveral roads and footpaths across North Yorkshire are set to be temporarily closed.

The closures began on July 6 and will last until September 30.

The affected roads are Front Street and Back Lane in Sowerby, Eldmire Lane in Dalton, Leaming Lane and Leases Road in Leaming Bar, St Lamberts in Burneston, and The Crescent and Hatfield Road in Northallerton.

Others include Farndale in Romanby, Mill Lane in Stillington, Hambleton Way in Easingwold, Percy Cross Rig and New Row in Kildale, Beak Hill Row in Chop Gate, the road from Bank Foot Farm in Ingleby Greenhow, Holme Lane in Seamer, the road between Seamer and Milton on Leaven, and Springfield Gardens in Stokesley.

These roads will be closed for spray injection patching works.

Meanwhile, a footpath in Stokesley will be closed for six months, which started on July 6 and will last until January 5, 2027, due to construction traffic.

The path affected is 10.140/3/1 in the Parish of Stokesley.

Richmond will also see the temporary closure of Sleegill from July 20 to July 31 for landslip repair works.

The maximum duration of this order is until January 17, 2028.

West Layton to A66 Road in West Layton will be closed from July 20 to July 22 for works to connect onto the new main for the final connection; a shut-off will be needed.

Hill Top in Newsham will be closed from July 20 to July 24 for the installation of two new 10-metre lights in the verge.

The footway from Rudby Bank East to Rudby Lea in Hutton Rudby will be closed from July 20 to August 4 for works.

Grammar School Lane in Northallerton will be closed from July 20 to September 8 for works.

The A6108, from Darlington Road to Barracks Bank, will be closed from July 20 to August 16.

This includes Darlington Road, Richmond (from the electrical substation between Richmond and Skeeby to its junction with Richmond Road, Skeeby); Richmond Road, Skeeby; Richmond Road to Scurragh Lane, Skeeby; Scurragh Lane, Moulton (from the junction with Richmond Road to Scurragh Lane, Skeeby to the junction of Barracks Bank) and Barracks Bank, Scotch Corner (from its junction with Scurragh Lane to the Blue Anchor roundabout).

These closures are due to works by OCU Utility Services Ltd to install the new 300KVA cables from Skeeby Substation through to Scotch Corner as part of other works.

All closures are subject to access to premises, and the periods will be indicated by traffic signs.

Local alternative routes will be signed on-site where appropriate.

Residents are advised to check the North Yorkshire Council's roadworks map for the latest updates.
