# Harrogate-based disability charity criticises David Lloyd Clubs for £5m refurbishment 'accessibility failures'

## A Harrogate-based disability charity has raised concerns about accessibility at a well-known gym club.

Disability Action Yorkshire has expressed deep frustration over what it says are “systemic accessibility failures” at the David Lloyd Clubs York which, it says, was refurbished recently as part of a £5 million refurbishment.

The charity said that, while the leisure giant had agreed to widen a “wrongfully narrowed” accessible shower door, disabled members are still being left behind due to unaddressed structural barriers and poor communication.

Although work to rectify the shower door is scheduled to commence on July 13, Disability Action Yorkshire argues that more still needs to be done on accessibility .

Jackie Snape, Chief Executive of Disability Action Yorkshire, said: “We are extremely concerned that changes at David Lloyd York appear to have reduced, rather than improved, accessibility for disabled people.

"Alterations such as narrowing an accessible shower door and the absence of an emergency cord are not small oversights, they create real barriers and potential safety risks.

"Disabled people exercise, too, and inclusive access to leisure facilities should not be an afterthought.

"It is disappointing that concerns raised have not been meaningfully addressed.”

David Lloyd Clubs says it understands their frustration and are working to address the issues raised to ensure their gyms are “inclusive”.

But Disability Action Yorkshire argues that improvements are still needed in regard to the following:

Outdoor sauna steps: The entrance features two unchamfered steps measuring 20mm and 30mm and featuring flat, sharp 90-degree edges rather than sloped profiles.

Missing safety features: The newly refurbished accessible shower cubicle lacks a red emergency cord.

Loss of autonomy: Wheelchair users have been stripped of their independence, requiring active third-party assistance simply to access the Spa Retreat and outdoor facilities.

The situation was first raised by Harrogate man Andrew Simister, a Trustee for Disability Action Yorkshire and a long-standing club member who uses a wheelchair when removing his prosthetic to use the swimming facilities.

Andrew Simister said: "I have lost my independence.

"Despite months of largely one-way contact with David Lloyd Clubs, I am still blocked from using the new outdoor sauna without assistance.

"Due to the two unchamfered steps, I need help to get in there.

"The reason I am speaking out is because David Lloyd Clubs have spent an eye-watering sum on this refurbishment, and they haven’t got it right.

"They have made it so that wheelchair users now need assistance.”

In a statement to the Harrogate Advertiser, David Lloyd Clubs said: “At David Lloyd Clubs, we're committed to creating welcoming, inclusive Clubs where everyone feels they belong, and to making our facilities as accessible as possible.

"We understand Andrew's frustration and are sorry for the issues he's experienced.

"We're working closely with our construction partners to find solutions to the accessibility concerns that have been raised.

"We have met with Andrew on a couple of occasions to discuss his concerns, and hope to meet with him again soon to talk through our plans and listen to his feedback.

"We thank him for bringing these issues to our attention."
