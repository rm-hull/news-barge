# Harrogate special needs school faces strike action next week in dispute over staffing

## Staff at a special needs school in Harrogate are to take strike action next week in a dispute over “unsafe working conditions”.

Members of the National Education Union (NEU) at Springwater Academy in Harrogate have confirmed that they will take strike action from Monday for four days in response to their continuing concerns over unsafe working conditions, staffing levels, and the use of support assistants to cover the teaching of classes.

The decision follows a prolonged period during which staff have raised worries about the safety and sustainability of working practices within the school.

Among the issues cited are staffing shortages and the routine expectation that support assistants undertake duties that should be carried out by qualified teaching staff.

The NEU has been engaged in ongoing discussions with Ascent Academies Trust in an effort to resolve the dispute.

While both sides have made progress in negotiations and some areas of agreement have been reached, significant concerns remain regarding staff welfare and measures needed to ensure a safe and supportive working environment.

Gary McVeigh-Kaye, North Yorkshire Branch Secretary of the National Education Union, said: "No teacher wants to stand on a picket line.

"Every educator wants to stand in a classroom. Yet there comes a point when silence is no longer responsible and inaction is no longer acceptable.

"Our members have shown patience, professionalism and a genuine desire to find solutions.

"Negotiations with Ascent Academies Trust have been ongoing and, in many respects, productive.

"However, there remain important sticking points, particularly around staff welfare, that cannot simply be brushed aside.

"A school is only as strong as the people who work within it.

"When staff are stretched beyond reasonable limits, when vacancies remain unfilled, and when support assistants are routinely expected to cover teaching responsibilities, the whole school community feels the strain.

"Support assistants are highly skilled and immensely valued colleagues.

"They are not substitutes for properly staffed teaching teams.

"Asking them to regularly fill gaps created by inadequate staffing is unfair to staff and unfair to pupils.

"This dispute is not about confrontation; it is about protection.

"It is not about creating disruption; it is about preventing further damage.

"It is not about looking backwards; it is about securing a better future for staff and students alike.

"Our members have not taken this decision lightly.

"They have chosen to act because they care deeply about the quality of education provided at Springwater Academy.

"The union remains committed to meaningful dialogue and we continue to hope that a negotiated settlement can be reached.

“The door remains open, and we urge the Trust to work with us to resolve the outstanding issues."

Located on Starbeck High Street, Springwater Academy describes itself as a “vibrant" special needs (SEN) school based in Harrogate which caters for students aged 2-19 years old.

The school prides itself on being highly skilled in supporting autistic children and young people to flourish and achieve.

Last year saw the completion of an expansion at the school, in partnership with North Yorkshire Council.

The major project involved the redevelopment of two nearby buildings to create modern and inclusive spaces to better meet the needs of pupils with Special Educational Needs and Disabilities (SEND).

The new facilities included new purpose-built classrooms, group rooms, a fully equipped sensory room and a secure outdoor learning area.

The NEU says it remains committed to working constructively with Ascent Academies Trust to achieve a resolution that addresses their concerns and ensures a safe environment for everyone at Springwater Academy.

The strike action will take place on the following dates:

Monday, July 13.

Tuesday, July 14.

Wednesday, July 15.

Thursday, July 16.
