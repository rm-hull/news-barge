A surprise donation from a North Yorkshire housebuilder is helping a homelessness charity keep the lights on and doors open for those in need.

Sleepsafe Selby, which has offered support to rough sleepers since 2018, received a £3,000 contribution from Barratt and David Wilson Homes through The Barratt Redrow Foundation.

Tracey Romaine, founder and manager at Sleepsafe Selby, said: "We were absolutely delighted to receive this donation.

"We were not expecting it, so it was a lovely surprise.

"We are constantly looking for ways to generate funds to keep our charity running, so this donation of £3,000 will ease a lot of that pressure.

"Our energy bills can be very high, and the funds will go directly towards covering those bills.”

The charity helps individuals tackle barriers to stable housing and supports their transition to independent living.
