THE Mayor of Malton has said he was overwhelmed by the 'outstanding teamwork and compassion' shown during yesterday's (Wednesday) major incident at the town's hospital.

Cllr Jason Aldrich said the fire at Malton Hospital had caused extensive damage to part of the building.

  


He said: "Thanks to the swift and professional actions of our emergency services and hospital staff, no one was seriously injured. A temporary ward was rapidly established at Malton School, with a rest centre for staff set up at the Milton Rooms to support the ongoing response.

"I visited all affected sites throughout the day and was overwhelmed by the outstanding teamwork and compassion shown across our community. Alongside trained professionals, Malton’s residents stepped forward without hesitation; directing traffic, helping transport patients safely to Malton School, and offering practical support wherever it was needed. "

  


"Local businesses also provided food and drink to tired volunteers, demonstrating once again the strength of Malton’s community spirit.

"I met with patients being cared for in the makeshift ward at the school. Although understandably shaken, they were in good spirits and deeply appreciative of the care and kindness shown to them.

"I want to express my sincere thanks to our emergency services, hospital staff, Malton School, local businesses, and every member of the public who went out of their way to help on what was a terrible day for our town. Your kindness, calmness, and willingness to act will be a lasting memory for all involved.

"A heartfelt thank you to everyone who played a part in keeping our community safe."

Cllr Lindsay Burr, who represents Malton on North Yorkshire Council said she noticed the fire whilst on the road and saw black smoke bellowing into the sky.

She said: "I knew immediately that this was not just a small fire and followed the smoke into Malton.

"I established straight away that the fire was at Springwood and put out alerts.

"Straight away I was reassured that all in the building were safe and put out a post to establish exactly what the fire was to stop the confusion. I would like to thank all the emergency services the residents in the area and the school for their help."

"Thank goodness there were no fatalities."

Fifteen patients along with staff were evacuated from the Springwood unit, which provides mental health services for older people, and taken to the nearby Malton School after the fire.

The Tees, Esk and Wear Valleys NHS Foundation Trust (TEWV), which runs the Springwood unit, said all patients and staff are safe.

A spokesperson said anyone with an appointment at Malton Hospital on July should not attend.

"Patients with affected appointments will be contacted directly as soon as possible to rearrange or advise on the next steps.

"The safety of our patients, visitors and colleagues remains our absolute priority. We would like to thank our staff, the emergency services and partner organisations for their swift response and support."

North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service said the cause of the fire is yet to be established.
