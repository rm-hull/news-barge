A YORK supermarket has reopened its doors with a fresh new look.

Aldi in Fulford Road has welcomed back customers today (July 9) after a major refurbishment and extension.

As reported by The Press in May 2025, the upgrade brings the store to 1,146sqm, with the overall footprint of the site expanding from 1,265sqm to 1,557sqm.

The supermarket giant says their changes offer a more spacious and streamlined layout, clearer signage, and easier navigation for shoppers.

Kevin Howells, store manager, said: "The team and I are really looking forward to welcoming back some familiar faces as well as new customers and supplying our local community with the everyday amazing products we have available.

  A new self check out area in Aldi 

*(Image: ©UNP)*

"Hopefully the new-look store will make shopping at Aldi York that bit easier and more comfortable."

The shop also features new, user-friendly freezers, and the popular Aisle of Aldi - offering special buys every Thursday and Sunday - remains a key attraction.

  Kevin outside the new look Aldi 

*(Image: ©UNP)*

The bakery section has been relocated to the back wall to reduce the risk of products being squashed during shopping.

The Fulford Road Aldi branch is open from 8am to 10pm Monday to Saturday, and from 10am to 4pm on Sundays.
