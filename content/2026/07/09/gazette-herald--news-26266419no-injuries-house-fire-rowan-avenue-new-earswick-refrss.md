EMERGENCY services have provided an update on a blaze which severely damaged a house in York suburb last night.

North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service confirmed that nobody has been injured in a house fire that took place in New Earswick late yesterday evening (July 8) – with flames and clouds of smoke visible in nearby towns.

As reported by The Press this morning, the incident took place just before 10pm yesterday in Rowan Avenue, New Earswick, with fire crews rushed to the scene.

Three fire crews from Huntington, Ripon and Selby were called in after a fire that started in the garden of the property spread to the house and hedgerow.

The cause of the fire is not yet known, but in images shared by North Yorkshire Weather Updates, it shows that the roof of the property has been severely damaged by fire with a big visible hole in the roof.

“Significant damage to roof and kitchen of property. Crews used numerous hose reels and jets to extinguish the fire,” said a spokesperson for the service.

They added that after speaking to the officer, it was confirmed that whilst the property was occupied at the time of the fire, there was thankfully no injuries.

Eyewitnesses said smoke from the blaze could be seen across the ring road in Haxby and Wigginton.
