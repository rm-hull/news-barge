York is one of the top cities in the UK for staying active, according to a new national index.

The UK Active Cities Index, published by Nature’s Best, analysed 10 different data points related to fitness and wellbeing across the country, including gyms, saunas, Pilates studios, green spaces and parkrun locations.

York secured sixth place with a score of 49.36 out of 100.

Charlotte East, nutrition expert at Nature’s Best, said: "These results show how strongly our surroundings can shape our daily habits, especially during the summer months.

"That consistency matters.

"Regular movement supports mobility and plays a wider role in overall health, with research suggesting people are also more likely to make healthier food choices when they spend more time outdoors, driven by improvements in mood and wellbeing."

The city was praised for its range of fitness facilities, with six padel courts, 377 cycle routes and 91 walking trails.

Cambridge topped the list with a score of 84.87, boosted by its green spaces, gyms and 11 parkrun locations.

Oxford followed in second place with a score of 64.82, while Chelmsford took third place with 58.80.

Gloucester (48.68), Blackpool (44.98) and Edinburgh (39.40) also featured in the top 10.
