A FIRE broke out in the garden in a North Yorkshire village that damaged the decking of a house.

North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service says that its crews from Harrogate and Thirsk were called to a property in Easingwold Road in Huby last night (July 8) at 6.06pm after a fire in the back garden.

“Fire spread from decking to conservatory doors and had been extinguished with a hose pipe prior to Fire Service arrival,” said a spokesperson for the service.

They added that crews used small tools to remove sections of the decking, and a thermal imaging camera to check for fire spread and hot spots, before ventilating the ground floor, which suffered some smoke logging.
