## Partners back Mayor's development zones at York Central, Selby and Scarborough

York Central is one of three Mayoral Development Zones *(Image: Pic supplied)</i>*

A bold new £10 million plan is setting the stage for growth has won the backing of the partners that have a role in its delivery.

York and North Yorkshire’s Combined Authority has announced three Mayoral Development Zones (MDZs) covering York Central, Scarborough and Selby.

The zones aim to accelerate regeneration, housing, and job creation across the region by attracting investment and fast-tracking development.

David Skaith, Mayor of York and North Yorkshire, said: "Mayoral Development Zones gives us the opportunity to go further and faster making a real difference to people’s everyday lives.

"Scarborough, Selby and York Central will deliver thousands of new homes, unlock thousands of new and better jobs, and attract billions of pounds of investment into our region.

"Some of the sites are ready to go and just need that final push, others need the final pieces of investment to get them going.

"Each area will be tailored to get development going and delivered quicker."

The three zones will focus on the Selby Growth Zone, Scarborough, and the York Central site, each chosen for its potential to drive economic growth and improve quality of life.

The Selby Growth Zone alone is expected to deliver more than 7,000 new jobs.

  York and North Yorkshire Mayor David Skaith 

*(Image: matt radcliffe photo)*

In York Central, the MDZ designation is set to speed up the delivery of more than 2,500 homes and a new central business district anchored by the Government Hub.

Scarborough’s zone will focus on town centre regeneration, tourism, and new housing in one of the region’s most deprived areas.

Tom Bridges, executive director for North East and Yorkshire at Homes England, said: "The creation of Mayoral Development Zones in Scarborough, Selby, and York Central will help accelerate regeneration and housing and economic growth of these huge opportunities for York and North Yorkshire.

"Through the combination of mayoral leadership, and the York and North Yorkshire Combined Authority, local authorities and Homes England working and investing together, we can support the delivery of thousands of new homes and jobs."

Funding will come from a newly approved £10 million MDZ Regeneration Fund, which aims to unlock further government and private investment.

Mayor Skaith said: "This investment will ensure we can work with our local authority partners to ensure we deliver the government funding and private sector investment needed.

"It is an important step in setting out how we can bring investment into our communities and make sure growth benefits everyone."

The MDZ approach allows the Mayor to coordinate efforts between developers, landowners, and local councils, streamlining decision-making and funding.

Mr Bridges welcomed the approach and said that Homes England would play a significant role as an investor and regeneration partner, especially as a landowner and developer at York Central.

He said this level of collaboration would help deliver "thousands of new homes and jobs" for the region.
