A primary school pupil in York recovering from a brain tumour has inspired a wave of community support and fundraising.

Seb, a Year 3 pupil at Huntington Primary Academy (HPA), was diagnosed with a brain tumour last summer.

After successful surgery, he has continued to make good progress on his recovery journey.

Seb’s strength, bravery and determination throughout this time have been truly inspiring.

The pupil's family has launched a fundraising campaign to support the L52 Paediatric Neurology Ward at Leeds Children’s Hospital.

The campaign also aims to raise awareness of childhood brain tumours.

Seb’s father completed the Leeds Half Marathon in May and plans to take on the Great North Run in September to help reach their fundraising goals.

HPA organised a fundraiser to show their support.

On Friday, June 12, all pupils took part in a mile-long run or walk around the playground.

Collectively, the school covered the distance of a half marathon and raised around £600.

Mrs Holton, Headteacher, said: "HPA is incredibly proud of our pupils, staff and families for coming together to support such an important cause.

"The whole-school challenge was inspired by Seb's remarkable courage, resilience and determination throughout his treatment and recovery journey.

"It was wonderful to see every child taking part and contributing to a collective and meaningful effort.

"HPA would like to thank everyone who donated, encouraged and participated, making this such a memorable and successful occasion.

"Seb continues to be an inspiration to us all."
