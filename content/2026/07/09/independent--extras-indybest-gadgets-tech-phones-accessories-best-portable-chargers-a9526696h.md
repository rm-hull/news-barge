As IndyBest’s tech editor, I’m always lugging around gadgets and gizmos. A laptop and a tablet in my bag, two phones in my pocket, a handheld games console in my hand. You name it – I’m never without a device. But that also means I’m forever charging my kit. If you’re anything like me and are always on the go, then getting one of the best power banks is essential.

Over the past decade, I’ve tested my way through more than a hundred different power banks to unearth the best. What I’ve learned is that, while most people look at the capacity of the power bank (mAh), that’s only half the story. You want a power bank that can rapdily recharge itself, little quality-of-life extras like built-in cables and a durable design that can survive getting scuffed up and dropped about.

While Anker remains my go-to, newer brands such as Cuktech and Iniu are offering more affordable power banks with the same durability and power. Even semi-solid power banks, which have less flammable material than traditional lithium-ion chargers, are starting to enter the market. From the affordable power banks to hevy-duty options that are perfect for travelling, these are the best power banks worth buying in 2026.

BMX is one of the first brands to launch a solid-state range, though it’s worth noting these are semi-solid-state rather than fully solid. What that essentially means is that there’s less flammable liquid, which is found in traditional lithium-ion cells. That should make them less prone to overheating, less at risk of swelling, and safer to carry around if the battery is damaged. That flammable lithium-ion chemistry is why so many power banks get recalled every year.

Much safer to use, BMX’s solidsafe power bank is a lot like a regular 10,000mAh magnetic power bank. It features Qi2 wireless charging and MagSafe, snapping securely onto my iPhone and delivering a steady 15W charge, while the 30W USB-C output was fast enough to charge up my phone and tablet without having to wait hours. I love the durable, built-in USB-C cable as well – like some Anker models, it doubles up as a lanyard loop, so you can carry it around and don’t need to remember to bring a separate cable.

I really like how the LCD display shows real-time charging speeds and the remaining battery life – really handy if you’re trying to conserve battery for the journey home. It also supports pass-through charging, so I could recharge both the power bank and my phone at the same time. However, it does still get warm, so don’t expect to have a completely cold power bank in your pocket, even if it is semi-solid.

The main tradeoff is the price. You’re paying a premium for newer battery tech, and the charging speeds aren’t that different from what you get from cheaper rivals. Still, if you like the idea of a future-forward, safety-first power bank, you can’t go wrong with the BMX solidsafe. The only thing I’d say is that it’s quite thick and weighty.
