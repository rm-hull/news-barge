## What will happen to Tadcaster's economy after Humphrey Smith's death?

What is the economic future of Tadcaster in the post-Humphrey Smith era? *(Image: NQ)*

Business leaders and property experts are divided over the economic future of Tadcaster now that Humphrey Smith has passed away.

The former brewery chairman has been praised by supporters for helping projects in the town, often anonymously, whilst critics blame Mr Smith for empty pubs and other properties in the area.

One leading businessman told The Press that ‘everyone’ is speculating right now about Tadcaster’s future, but so soon after Humphrey Smith’s death, it is “purely speculative.”

He also told The Press: “There have been some positive signs recently. For example, two housing sites have been submitted to planning by the Brewery.

“The town has also recently submitted a Neighbourhood Development Plan to North Yorkshire Council which supports the renewal of the town in line with the Brewery's vision.

“North Yorkshire Council are also in the process of preparing a Town Investment Plan for the town (alongside every other town in North Yorkshire) which will show funders such as the Mayor's Office, what projects are needed, supported and potentially shovel ready.

  Humphrey Smith, who passed away last week, aged 81. 

*(Image: SWNS)*

“We are also expecting the long awaited Flood Alleviation Scheme to go to planning in September, as well as the large out of town 11 Arches housing scheme [of 400 homes by Gladman Developments]."

The businessman added: “So there are many reasons to feel confident about the town's future which has been stifled by a combination of factors for many years.

“How Mr Smith's passing contributes to this, well I have absolutely no idea but live in hope that the Brewery may become more proactive and outward facing over time.”

Tadcaster estate agent Brian Bartle agreed: “Nobody knows anything fresh. It’s a matter of wait and see.”

Wishart Estate Agents declined to comment.

However, James Stuart of property consultants Malcolm Stuart says Humphrey Smith closed the Swan pub in the High Street and the Brittania, by the bridge. But he notes a recent shift from the brewery to fill empty pubs.

The brewery also owns many shop units in Tadcaster.

James said: "I understand that there’s plenty of interest from prospective tenants for the town but the Brewery are quite particular on who they let their shop units to. Empty commercial and residential units have fallen into disrepair throughout the town."

James continued: “You would hope for fresh thinking. I’m told his son Sam has different ways so there may be changes.

“The brewery could be the hero. It could be the local hero, whilst lining their own pockets. The buildings could be put back to use.”

James says Tadcaster has a housing shortage, with development taking place instead in Sherburn-in-Elmet and Selby.

Tadcaster recently lost a Costcutter and its Costa café, and whilst they had nothing to do with the brewery, James blames some of its activities on helping prevent a growth in footfall to support the high street.

He added: “I just hope there’s a relaxation. You can keep the tradition of a market town, whilst allowing it to flourish.”

  Managing director Chris Houston and the team at Tadweld 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

Chris Houston, managing director of Tadweld called Humphrey Smith’s passing a ‘milestone’ for Tadcaster, noting the Smith family’s ‘huge influence.’

Chris told The Press: “Fundamentally there is not a huge expectation of change. Sam has been involved in the family business for a long time, and will more than likely employ a consistent business strategy.

“That said, more recently the brewery has been brewing less and recent estimates are that over 120 of the 300-plus pubs owned by the brewery are closed. Logic would say that that isn't a long-term sustainable state.”

He added: “Whilst the brewery has been reticent to sell property previously, there is some hope that some of these dormant properties might come back to market. There is not an expectation to see large scale further investment.

“It's too early to tell, but the expectation is that Sam will bring his own perspective, but that any change will likely be slow.”
