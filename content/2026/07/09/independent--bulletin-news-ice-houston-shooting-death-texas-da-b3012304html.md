# Texas DA accuses feds of blocking investigation into migrant’s death by ICE agent

- Harris County District Attorney Sean Teare accused federal authorities of blocking local officials from investigating the fatal shooting of Mexican national Lorenzo Salgado Araujo by an Immigration and Customs Enforcement officer in Houston.
- Salgado Araujo was killed during a traffic stop arrest, with federal authorities claiming he was in the country illegally and forced an officer to fire in self-defense after attempting to strike agents with his vehicle.
- Family members and community advocates describe Salgado Araujo as a dedicated construction worker and father of three U.S. citizen sons, who had lived in the U.S. for 35 years and was close to obtaining legal status.
- The killing has sparked international outrage, with Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum vowing legal measures beyond diplomatic complaints, and Salgado Araujo's son learning of his father's death via social media.
- Critics, including former DHS official Juliette Kayyem, question the agency's credibility due to a pattern of self-defense claims in shootings later contradicted by evidence, and data shows at least 16 other immigration enforcement shootings in the last year.
