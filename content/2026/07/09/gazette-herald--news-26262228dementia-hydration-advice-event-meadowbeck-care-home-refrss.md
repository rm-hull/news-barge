A care home in York is set to host a community event focused on dementia support.

Barchester’s Meadowbeck Care Home will hold a dementia awareness session on hydration on Tuesday, July 28, from 2.30pm.

The session aims to support residents’ families, friends, and the wider community.

Annaliza Kemp, general manager at Meadowbeck Care Home, said: "We want to help and support relatives, friends and members of our local community who have loved ones who are living with dementia.

"We are inviting everyone to come along and listen to our talk to raise awareness of the condition and to help give people some information and coping strategies."

The session will be led by Margaret Ferguson, a dementia adviser from York Alzheimers Society, who will offer practical advice on supporting people with dementia to stay hydrated.

The event will include time for questions and open discussion.

It will take place at Meadowbeck Care Home, 1 Meadowbeck Close, Osbaldwick Lane, York.

Meadowbeck Care Home is operated by Barchester Healthcare, one of the UK’s largest care providers, and offers dementia, nursing, residential, and respite care.

Those wishing to attend are asked to RSVP by emailing [meadowbeck@barchester.com.](mailto:meadowbeck@barchester.com.)
