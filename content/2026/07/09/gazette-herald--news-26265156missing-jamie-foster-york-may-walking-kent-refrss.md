POLICE believe a a missing man from York may be walking the length of the country.

North Yorkshire Police have said that they believe missing 34-year-old Jamie Foster - from York - may be walking to Kent on the south coast of England.

As reported by The Press earlier this week, he was last seen at around 11pm on Sunday, July 5 from Hob Moor area of Acomb, York.

He is described as a white male, 1.85m tall and of a slim build, with shoulder-length brown hair and a fair complexion.

He was last seen wearing a red long-sleeved top, blue jeans, trainers, and a bandana, and was carrying a backpack.

Jamie is described as a keen walker who may have travelled some distance since he was last seen, with officers adding that he is known to visit churches while walking.

A police spokesperson said: "Following extensive enquiries in York and the Doncaster area of South Yorkshire, where Jamie is thought to have passed through on Tuesday (7 July), it is now believed that he could be walking to Kent on the south coast of England."

North Yorkshire Police has alerted the Greater Manchester, West Midlands, Northamptonshire, Bedfordshire, Cambridgeshire, Suffolk, the Metropolitan Police, the City of London, Devon and Cornwall, Hampshire, and Kent forces to look out for him over the coming days and weeks.

British Transport Police is also assisting on the off-chance that Jamie uses the rail network.

Anyone who believes they may have seen Jamie is asked to contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, quoting reference number 12260126753.

Immediate sightings of Jamie can also be reported by calling 999 so we can quickly make sure he is safe.
