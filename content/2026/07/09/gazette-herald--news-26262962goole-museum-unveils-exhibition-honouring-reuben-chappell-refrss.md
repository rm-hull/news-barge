A new exhibition in East Yorkshire is celebrating the life of one of Britain’s finest maritime artists.

Master of the Masts, on display at Goole Museum, honours ship portraitist Reuben Chappell (1870-1940) and was launched as part of Goole’s bicentenary weekend, which takes place between July 17 and July 20.

Members of the public can attend the opening event on July 18 from 10am to 12pm.

Councillor Nick Coultish, cabinet member for culture, leisure and tourism at East Riding of Yorkshire Council, said: "Goole is hosting a fascinating programme of events as part of the Goole 200 celebrations and we cannot wait to welcome people to this new exhibition."

Running until Saturday, October 24, the showcase features paintings and personal items that have never been seen by the public before.

A new mini-documentary about Mr Chappell premiered at the opening, along with a ceramic sculpture by artist Peter Donnelly, commissioned to mark the town’s 200th anniversary.

Dr Alex Ombler, curator at Goole Museum, said: "People can learn about Chappell’s humble origins in Victorian Goole, where he established himself as a pierhead artist, painting highly accurate pictures of ships for British and foreign sailors who arrived in port."
