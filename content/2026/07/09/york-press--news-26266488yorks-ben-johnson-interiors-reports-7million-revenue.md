## York firm exceeds expectations with £7m of sales in nine months after relaunch

The interior of City West Leeds *(Image: Pic supplied)*

A rebranded York-headquartered interiors firm has exceeded expectations.

Ben Johnson Interiors, which has offices in Bishopthorpe and Newcastle, reported more than £7 million in revenue in its first nine months of trading under its new brand.

The interiors specialist returned to the market under a new brand last year and has since completed a number of high-profile commercial fit-out projects across the UK.

Emma Dodsley, director at Ben Johnson Interiors, said: "Ben Johnson has been part of Yorkshire’s business community since 1855.

"Whilst our roots were in printing, today we’re focused on creating workplaces and specialist environments for organisations across the UK.

"In March 2025, we relaunched and rebranded to Ben Johnson Interiors, which reflects the business we’ve become, creating environments that support collaboration, wellbeing and productivity.

"Generating more than £7 million in revenue during our first nine months exceeded our expectations and we’re building on this in 2026."

The firm’s commercial interiors business recorded the turnover between March and December 2025 and expects continued success in the year ahead.

Among its recent projects is the Leeds office relocation for Sweco, Europe’s largest architecture and engineering consultancy.

Ben Johnson Interiors designed and delivered a 15,000 sq ft space at 1 Whitehall Riverside for around 200 employees, incorporating a modern reception, work areas, collaboration zones, and welfare facilities.

The company also revamped the 2,185 sq ft reception at 2 City West in Leeds for Davlea Estates, giving it what the firm described as a "boutique, hotel-inspired feel."

In Leicester, Ben Johnson Interiors completed an 18,000 sq ft fit-out at retail giant Next’s headquarters, transforming a vacant two-storey shell into a high-performance workplace, including a canteen, restaurant, commercial kitchen and training centre.

The business also operates in the healthcare sector and recently delivered a new medical facility and doctors’ surgery in central Sheffield for One Medical.

This contract included a full category A and B fit-out, such as mechanical and electrical services, partitioning, specialist treatment rooms, and surgery spaces.

Ms Dodsley said the company is expanding its reach in the south.

She said: "We’ve enjoyed a hugely successful 16 months since then and we’re also building our presence in London, with current projects for insurance company Aviva and L&Q, which is one of the UK’s largest housing associations and residential developers."

Looking ahead to 2026, Ms Dodsley said the company remains confident.

She said: "We’ve already enjoyed a strong first half of the year and have secured a healthy pipeline of work.

"With new projects beginning across England in the landlord, healthcare, education and office sectors, we’re looking forward to another year of growth."
