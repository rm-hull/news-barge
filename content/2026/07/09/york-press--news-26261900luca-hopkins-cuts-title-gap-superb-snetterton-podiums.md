## Hemingbrough rider Luca Hopkins cuts title gap with Snetterton double podium

Luca Hopkins strengthened his challenge for the 1pMobile National Junior Sportbike Championship with a superb double podium at Snetterton *(Image: Supplied)*

Hemingbrough rider Luca Hopkins strengthened his challenge for the 1pMobile National Junior Sportbike Championship with a superb double podium at Snetterton, cutting the gap to championship leader Marley Mackenzie from 33 points to just 13.

The 18-year-old Team SBR powered by DKS Direct Drilling rider arrived in Norfolk third in the championship, just one point behind Ben Jolliffe in second.

Two determined recovery rides left Hopkins as Mackenzie’s closest challenger after another impressive display in the British Superbike support class.

There were encouraging signs from the outset as Hopkins topped Friday’s only free practice session, finishing more than a quarter of a second clear of the field.

His weekend then threatened to take an unexpected turn in qualifying on Saturday when a faulty transponder meant none of his opening laps had been recorded.

Forced back into the pits for a replacement with just eight minutes remaining, Hopkins had little margin for error.

He responded brilliantly.

  Luca Hopkins celebrates his podium after cutting the gap to the championship leader to just 13 points 

*(Image: Supplied)*

His first timed lap put him sixth before successive improvements lifted him to fourth and then third.

A final lap of 2:02.924 secured a place on the front row, just 0.635 seconds away from pole position.

Starting third for Saturday’s opening race, Hopkins was slow away from the line and dropped to seventh by the opening lap.

He immediately began working his way back through the pack, passing Ben Jolliffe before moving into fifth after another rider ran wide.

After moving up another place when Luca Wilkinson crashed, Hopkins closed rapidly on Lewis Mullen and Laken Payne, making decisive overtakes at Nelson and Riches to move into second.

With clear track ahead, he began chasing South African debutant Ryan van Nieuwkerk.

The gap came down to under seven-tenths of a second with two laps remaining, but despite continuing to close, Hopkins crossed the line in second place.

The result proved even more valuable when championship leader Mackenzie was slowed by a technical problem and slipped to seventh, promoting Hopkins to second in the standings.

Sunday’s second race began in similarly frustrating fashion as another slow launch dropped Hopkins from the front row to seventh before the field had completed the opening lap.

Once again, however, he wasted little time recovering the lost ground.

He was back to fourth within two laps before another move on Mullen lifted him onto the podium.

As Mullen and Payne began battling over second place, Hopkins closed onto the back of them to set up a thrilling finish.

Starting the final lap with less than half a second covering second, third and fourth, Hopkins first passed Mullen before out-dragging Payne along Bentley Straight and making a decisive move into Nelson.

Payne fought back all the way to the chequered flag, but Hopkins held on by the narrowest of margins to secure his second runner-up finish of the weekend.

  Luca Hopkins battles with Lewis Mullen at Snetterton 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Hopkins said: "We were quick from the first session on Friday, and that gave us a lot of confidence.

"Qualifying was stressful after the transponder issue because we suddenly had very little time left, but the team stayed calm, and we managed to get ourselves back onto the front row.

"I made life difficult for myself with the starts in both races, but the pace was there all weekend and I knew we could fight back.

"To come away with two second places and take another big chunk out of the championship lead is a brilliant result.

"Thank you to everyone at Team SBR powered by DKS Direct Drilling, all of my sponsors, my family and everyone who’s supported me.

"We’ve put ourselves in a really strong position going into the next round."

Hopkins now heads to the next round at Brands Hatch GP Circuit second in the championship, just 13 points behind Mackenzie.
