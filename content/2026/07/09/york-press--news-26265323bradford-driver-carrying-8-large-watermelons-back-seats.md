A DRIVER has been reported by police after being caught not wearing a seatbelt while transporting eight large watermelons in Yorkshire.

Officers from West Yorkshire Police's Operation Steerside team spotted the motorist this week, a spokesperson for the force said.

While officers saw the funny side of the unusual load, they warned the fruit could have posed a serious risk if not properly secured.

A spokesperson said: "You'd have to be a right melon not to wear your seatbelt these days."

The team added that watermelons typically weigh between 16lb and 20lb each and could cause significant damage if thrown around inside a vehicle during a collision.

Police pointed out that loads should always be properly secured to reduce the risk of injury to vehicle occupants.

Officers reported the driver for the seatbelt offence and ensured the watermelons were secured before allowing the journey to continue.

The driver was stopped in Bradford.
