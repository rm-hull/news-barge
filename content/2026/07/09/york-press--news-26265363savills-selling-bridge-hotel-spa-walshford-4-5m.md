## Popular North Yorkshire hotel and spa on the market for £4.5m

The Bridge Inn at Walshford is on the market for £4.5million freehold *(Image: Savills)*

A popular North Yorkshire weddings, dining and events hotel has gone on the market for £4.5 million.

Savills is selling the freehold of the Bridge Hotel & Spa at Walshford, just off the A1 (M), a few miles north of Wetherby.

The hotel has 37 bedrooms and the established restaurant and bar has 72 covers.

Tripadvisor awards the venue, a former 17th Century Coaching Inn, which by the 1970s and 1980s, had gained a reputation for fine dining.

Savills describe the Bridge Hotel & Spa as a “charming, full-service hospitality asset set within a picturesque riverside location.”

  The agents continued: “The property comprises 37 well-appointed guest bedrooms alongside a high-quality restaurant and bar offering, catering for both residents and non-residents.

  

  “The hotel benefits from a well-established leisure proposition, including spa facilities, treatment rooms and gym, providing a diversified income stream and strong guest appeal. In addition, the property is a popular destination for weddings, events and corporate functions, supported by flexible meeting and function spaces.

  

  “Set within a tranquil, semi-rural environment, the hotel combines countryside appeal with excellent accessibility, making it well positioned to capture both leisure and corporate demand.”
