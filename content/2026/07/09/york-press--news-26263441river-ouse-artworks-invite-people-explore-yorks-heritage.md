New public art installations have gone on permanent display along the River Ouse.

The pieces, created by local creatives Sarah Schiewe of Schiewe Ceramics and Rob Hight of Hight Design, are part of the York Rivers Trail and invite people to experience the river through art, storytelling, and digital experiences.

The three permanent installations, made from Corten steel and located at Scarborough Bridge and Tower Gardens, each tell a different story of the River Ouse.

  The three permanent installations are made from Corten steel 

*(Image: Schiewe Ceramics)*

Ms Schiewe, artist and founder of Schiewe Ceramics, said: "It's been a privilege to contribute to a project that connects creativity, heritage and the environment, helping people engage with the River Ouse in a meaningful and lasting way."

The pieces encourage visitors to "pause, reflect, and connect" with the river from a new perspective.

  The pieces are created by Sarah Schiewe and Rob Hight 

*(Image: Schiewe Ceramics)*

The project is part of the wider Ousewem programme, which helps communities better understand and adapt to the changing relationship between people and water.

Ousewem is led by City of York Council in close collaboration with North Yorkshire Council and delivery partners JBA, Natural England, Yorkshire Dales Rivers Trust, and the University of York.

The York Rivers Trail is free to explore and aims to engage residents and visitors of all ages.
