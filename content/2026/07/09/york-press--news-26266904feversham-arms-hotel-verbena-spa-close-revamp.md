## Top North Yorkshire hotel and spa to close for over a year for 'significant' revamp

The Feversham Arms Hotel and Verbena Spa *(Image: Pic supplied)*

A top-rated North Yorkshire hotel and spa is to close for more than a year to allow for a ‘significant’ refurbishment.

The revamp is at the four star Feversham Arms Hotel & Verbena Spa in Helmsley, which will encompass its 33 bedrooms and spa facilities.

Rockliffe Hall Limited bought the hotel in August 2025, which followed the previous hotel operator entering administration. It had been placed on the market for £3.25 million.

The Feversham Arms Hotel & Verbena Spa is well-regarded, receiving 4.4 stars out of five on Google, based on 548 Google reviews.

TripAdvisor awards 4.1 stars out of five, based on 3,217 reviews, ranking it first out of four Helmsley hotels.

Rockcliffe says this investment forms part of the company's wider vision to develop outstanding hospitality destinations in the North East of England that attract visitors from across the UK and internationally.

  The Feversham Arms Hotel in Helmsley 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

The company owns Rockcliffe Hall in County Durham, a five star hotel noted for gastronomy and a golf course.

It also owns The Pheasant Hotel in nearby Harome, a few miles from Helmsley.

Rockcliffe says during the renovation, the focus will be on creating a reimagined Feversham Arms that brings the quality, innovation and guest experience synonymous with the Rockliffe name to an intimate, boutique setting.

Guests can look forward to newly designed rooms and suites, an elevated spa experience, and a refreshed dining offering.

The company says the investment underscores confidence in Helmsley and the wider Yorkshire tourism economy, as well as a commitment to the warmth and characterful service that defines hospitality in this region.

  The Feversham Arms Hotel 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

Shauna Robb, Group Chief Operating Officer of Rockliffe Hall Limited, said: “The Feversham Arms Hotel & Verbena Spa has a history of welcoming guests from all over the world to relax, indulge and experience the famously warm Yorkshire hospitality that we are incredibly proud of, in a well-considered boutique retreat.

“This project offers an exciting opportunity to continue to offer all these elements while raising the standard of the decor and facilities. We can't wait to welcome guests back through our doors in 2028 to experience it for themselves.”

The hotel will close on September 1 2026 to allow work to begin, with reopening projected for early 2028. Further details on the renovation, including the in-house interior design team and plans, will be announced in the coming months.
