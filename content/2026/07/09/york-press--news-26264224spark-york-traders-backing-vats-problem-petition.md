## 'It’s about having a life again' - York hospitality businesses call for VAT changes

Becca Bowers of Spark *(Image: Alice Kavanagh)*

TRADERS at a popular York venue are backing a campaign to lower VAT for hospitality businesses – with the support of a city’s MP.

Spark:York – a community food drinks, and events venue in Piccadilly – has come out in support of national petition which is calling for the government to reduce VAT costs to the hospitality business - something that would bring it in line with European countries.

The VAT's the Problem petition, which is being spearheaded by chef Tom Kerridge, is calling for a reduction from 20 per cent to 10 per cent and currently has over 260 thousand signatures and has gained parliamentary support, including from York Central Labour MP Rachael Maskell.

One Spark trader, who is standing behind the petition, is Jack Jenner, who owns Baby Boy’s Burgers and says that VAT has been a constant worry among the independent hospitality businesses that he has worked for.

Jack said: “It seems to be a very common theme that people think that businesses are most likely to fail in the first year and I think I agree, but a lot of it tends to be around the nine month mark, which is when most people hit their VAT registry.

“It happened to me, I hit it, and I thought if I’m paying a VAT bill now, I’m gone. We had to put our prices up – reluctantly, we didn’t want to – but we had to. I think more and more businesses are dreading those VAT bills coming in.

“I get that it's not just hospitality that struggles with it, but a lot of other industries have a lot more to offset against the 20 per cent and we just don't have that. The government do have a low-rate scheme in already, but it’s only to a certain threshold.

  Jack Jenner of Baby Boy's Burgers 

*(Image: Harry Booth)*

“I applied for it, but was rejected before we even had our first year of accounts. It’s not making a new elaborate plan – they could also make that option more accessible for more people.”

Jack added that a change to VAT would make a huge impact on his business and life, where he is currently only able to afford to hire one additional full-time member of staff.

He added: “For me, I had a chef leave and so, I’ll be working every day for 45 days until our new one starts, then the time it takes to train them.

"That isn’t directly due to VAT, but at the same time, if I wasn’t paying so much out, we wouldn’t have to rely on one person and it’s unfortunately a position that quite a few businesses find themselves in.

“I think a change would be key in a place like this or a small local business, it’s not just a money grab - it’s about having a life again. It gives us room to grow or for reinvestment like having a team, more equipment, or a chance to expand.”

Becca Bowers, head of creative for Spark, said that the venue were happy to be standing behind their traders on this, after the petition was brought to them by Mike of Clucking Oinks – which has recently closed its Castleghate branch, due to rising costs – including VAT – affecting the trade.

She said: “We keep our basic overhead costs as low as it’s possible to make them for our traders. VAT is something that is becoming difficult for them to keep up with, because they're putting a lot into paying tax, but they're not getting anything out of it.

  Becca Bowers of Spark 

*(Image: Alice Kavanagh)*

"Mike from Clucking Oinks gave us a great analogy when he brought the petition to us at one of our community meetings. He said it's like you buy a potato from the shop, chop it up, prepare it, cook it, then you have you give a chunk of it away at the end.

“With utilities, food, energy costs, it makes it virtually impossible to run a small business, especially if you want to pay your employees a living wage - which is something that we take a lot of pride in here and expect our traders to do too.

“In the same way that we champion having an excessive space, our traders also want to be able to offer accessible food. We want to make sure that people can afford to come here for a meal without paying an arm and a leg.

“By working with our traders to get the petition out there, we're trying to amplify their voices.”

Ms Maskell said that she was ‘really supportive’ of the campaign and added that she is pleased that Labour leadership candidate Andy Burnham has committed to cutting Business Rates if he becomes Prime Minister.

  Rachael Maskell, MP for York Central 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

She said: "I want the UK to really value our small, independent businesses on the high streets, they are vital to our communities and deserve the recognition and status they deserve as places we can go, to socialise, to enjoy life, to be together and support one and other.

"This is why I asked the Prime Minister about business rates for small businesses earlier this year, and trust that we see some action from Government soon to recognise our local cafes and hospitality businesses.

“I know how hard business leaders are working to make their cafes, bars and hotels a real success in York as part of a strong high street offer."
