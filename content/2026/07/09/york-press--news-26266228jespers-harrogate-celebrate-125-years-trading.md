## How well-known North Yorkshire retailer has lasted 125 years

Lynn Cummings in from of the Harrogate store *(Image: Pic supplied)*

One of Harrogate’s longest standing independent retailers- that used to have operations in York- is getting ready to celebrate a major milestone.

Jespers of Harrogate will mark 125 years in September with a week of celebrations including a live artist in the window, a prize draw and an evening party on September 10.

The Jesper family opened an engravers in Harrogate in 1901, which has grown into the independent department store we know today.

The store has outlasted two world wars, the decline of letter-writing, the rise of the internet, and a global pandemic.

At a time in the world when there is so much uncertainty, Jespers it is so important to have bastions of local life that remain steady.

Store manager Lynn Cummings said: “Jespers has never been interested in the transactional. It is a shop that looks to build lifelong relationships with its customers. Jespers earns the kind of loyalty that outlasts the years.

  An Austin A40 van 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

“Customers who moved away and still order from it, visitors who make it a stop every time they're in Harrogate, parents who bring their children because their own parents brought them. A place that sees you through the bad times and the good.”

Lynn continued: “So many independent shops on British high streets, are facing this moment of uncertainty. The world is changing rapidly: online retail, rising costs, shifting footfall.”

During the 1950s and 1960s, Jespers expanded into ‘business machines’ and business stamps. In the 1960s, it moved into Oxford Street and added more stationery and pens and later, office furniture.

  A stand at the Yorkshire Show in the 1980s 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

From the 1980s for about 20 years, Jespers had a store in Castlegate, York, and also a commercial operation at Dunnington.

However, after 118 years and four generations, the Jesper family decided to sell the business and retire in 2019.

The Vickers family — who also own Vickers Oils a 200 year old family business — took it on. Not to modernize it into something unrecognisable, but because they saw exactly what it was and wanted to protect it.

  Business Machines in the 1980s 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

Lynn continued: “Under their ownership, Jespers has found new energy while keeping everything that made people love it in the first place: the fountain pens, the artist supplies, the thoughtful gifts you can't find anywhere else in Yorkshire.”

This has seen the business diversify into a wider range of gifts, which Lynn says is “doing phenomenal for us.”

There is also a big arts department and the store also sells toiletries, soaps, hardware and jewelry. There are also board games and socks!

Lynn told the Press: “It’s amazing how many new products we pack into the store.”

However, it is the luxury pens that it is famous for, with Jespers attracting visitors from afar to see them.

  14 Oxford Street, Harrogate, in the 1980s 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

Lynn said: “We had two in from Manchester last week. There’s nothing in Manchester, no pen shop in Leeds. Last week we also had a chap from Pontefract to see is. We are the only pen shop within a two-hour drive of here.”

And with such a wide range of products, Jespers now sees itself as an independent retail store.

Lynn continued: “Sadly, the demise of other store has worked for us and we are still here trading successfully.”

Looking to the future, Lynn added the business will be focused on improving the store and expanding its ranges, as it has in recent years.
