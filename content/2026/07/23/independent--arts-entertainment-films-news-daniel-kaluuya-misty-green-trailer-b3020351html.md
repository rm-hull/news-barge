After winning the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor in 2021, Daniel Kaluuya has starred in just one live-action film – but now he’s making a comeback.

Despite a couple of voice performances, the British actor, 37, has not featured on screen since 2022’s *Nope*, having won the Oscar the previous year for his turn in the acclaimed biopic* Judas and the Black Messiah*. 

This week, the first trailer for Kaluuya’s new film, *Misty Green*, was released online. Directed by Chris Rock, the film follows a celebrated actor (played by Rosalind Eleazar) as she struggles with personal demons.

The trailer arrives a year after it was reported that Kaluuya had signed with the major US representation agency WME.

![Daniel Kaluuya photographed in 2025](https://static.the-independent.com/2026/07/23/15/55/GettyImages-2250190210.jpeg)

While his last live-action appearance was in *Nope*– a genre-bending sci-fi horror directed by Jordan Peele, who previously directed Kaluuya in* Get Out*– the actor has lent his voice to multiple projects, including the animated superhero sequel* Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse*, and the Arsenal Football Club docuseries* All or Nothing*.

He co-wrote, produced and co-directed the independent film *The Kitchen*, released in the UK back in January 2024, though did not act in it.

Kaluuya also took on a role as associate artistic director at London’s Roundhouse arts venue in 2023.

After *Misty Green*, which will be released in cinemas later this year, Kaluuya is set to appear in the thriller* Hotel Hotel Hotel Hotel*, directed by* Together*(2025)**filmmaker Michael Shanks, and will reprise the role of Hobie Brown (aka Spider-Punk) in the forthcoming* Spider-Verse*follow-up. 

He is also linked to a movie adaptation of the popular children’s property Barney the Dinosaur, reportedly still in development.

![Kaluuya in the trailer for 'Misty Green'](https://static.the-independent.com/2026/07/23/15/48/Screenshot-2026-07-23-at-16-18-28.png)

Kaluuya, who got his breakthrough on E4’s teen drama *Skins*, is widely considered one of the best actors of his generation, and is known for roles in films including* Get Out*,* Widows*, and* Black Panther.* 

In a 2024 ranking of the greatest film actors of the 21st century, Kaluuya was placed at No 6.

Though he appeared in a supporting role in the smash-hit Marvel film *Black Panther* in 2018, Kaluuya did not return for the sequel, 2022’s *Black Panther: Wakanda Forever*.

At the time, he confirmed that the reason for this was due to scheduling conflicts with the production of *Nope*.
