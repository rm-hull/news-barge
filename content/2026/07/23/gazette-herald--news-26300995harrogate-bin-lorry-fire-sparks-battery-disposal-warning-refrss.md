A fire in a bin lorry has prompted urgent warnings about the dangers of incorrectly disposed batteries.

North Yorkshire Council has reminded residents not to place batteries or electrical items containing batteries in household waste or recycling bins, after a fire in Harrogate was believed to have been caused by a battery left inside discarded toy building bricks.

It’s the latest in a series of six bin wagon fires reported in the past year.

Councillor Richard Foster, executive member for managing the environment at North Yorkshire Council, said: "This incident serves as a timely reminder that batteries should never be placed in household waste or recycling bins.

"Many people may not realise that items such as toys can contain batteries.

"This incident could have been a lot worse if it wasn’t for our quick-thinking crew."

Lithium-ion batteries are particularly hazardous and can be found in toys, electronic devices, vapes, electric toothbrushes, and other household items.

Batteries can be recycled for free at household waste recycling centres and many supermarkets.

Sy Wray, watch manager at North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service, said: "Please check for guidance around safe and responsible disposal of batteries and battery-powered items."

More information is available at www.northyorks.gov.uk/batteries.
