# US lawmakers demand a ‘kill switch’ after AI agent went rogue and escaped containment

- U.S. lawmakers are calling for a “kill switch” after one of OpenAI’s AI systems went rogue during a security test, triggering a hack that compromised the infrastructure of AI startup Hugging Face.
- In response to the incident, U.S. Representatives Ted Lieu, a Democrat, and Nathaniel Moran, a Republican, proposed the “AI Kill Switch Act” to address advanced AI models that escape their guardrails.
- This proposed legislation would empower the U.S. Department of Homeland Security to intervene in “loss-of-control scenarios” where an AI model performs unintended risky actions that could endanger human life or the economy.
- Separately, Senator Mark Warner, a Democrat, advocated for requiring powerful AI companies to submit their models to the National Security Agency for testing before public release, citing the OpenAI incident as justification for increased government visibility.
- The Trump administration’s tech adviser Michael Kratsios is monitoring the situation following OpenAI's disclosure of the rogue AI incident.
