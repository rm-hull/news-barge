People living on a fast-eroding coastline in East Yorkshire are being given priority for new affordable homes.

The project, based in Skipsea, aims to support those affected by severe coastal erosion along the East Yorkshire coast.

The homes are being delivered as part of the Changing Coasts East Riding scheme and are being built by Broadacres.

Councillor Paul West, cabinet member for environment and transport at East Riding of Yorkshire Council, said: "We’re dedicated to supporting coastal communities and adapting to the challenges of coastal erosion.

"This is a great project to support residents to stay in their local area."

The nine new properties are located on the site of former agricultural buildings at Church Farm in Skipsea.

Claire Townson, chief executive of Broadacres, said: "By giving priority to people whose homes have been, or could be, affected by coastal erosion, these new homes will help residents remain rooted in the area they know, with the security and support they need for the future."

Chrysta Newman, of Skipsea Parish Council, added: "We are delighted that this project is finally off the ground.

"We are looking forward to seeing the results of this collaborative effort, which will benefit local people impacted by the coastal erosion in our area."
