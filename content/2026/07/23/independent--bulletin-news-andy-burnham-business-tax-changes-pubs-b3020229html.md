# Burnham’s plan to slash pub taxes dismissed as ‘political manoeuvre’

- Andy Burnham announced a 20 percent reduction in business rates for approximately 32,000 pubs, clubs, and live music venues, aiming to provide relief from cost pressures and prevent closures.
- The policy, which is expected to save a typical pub around £1,100 annually, has been criticized by hospitality leaders like celebrity chef Tom Kerridge and Great British Menu winner Nick Beardshaw as insufficient.
- Critics argue the reduction is an 'absolute drop in the ocean' and a 'political maneuver,' warning that many businesses will still close before the measure takes effect in April.
- The initiative is projected to cost around £100 million per year, with funding planned through cuts to business rate relief for vape shops and stricter enforcement on online businesses failing to pay VAT.
- Despite the government's insistence that the policy is fully funded, the Conservative opposition has demanded details on how Burnham plans to pay for this and other recent proposals, including removing VAT from electricity bills.
