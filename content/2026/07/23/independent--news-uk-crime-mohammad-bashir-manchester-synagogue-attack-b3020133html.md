A “committed jihadist” who plotted a terror attack on a UK defence base with Manchester synagogue knife assailant Jihad Al-Shamie has been jailed for life.

Mohammad Bashir, 31, described as a “long-standing, committed jihadist”, drove Jihad Al-Shamie, 35, on a 10-hour round trip to conduct hostile reconnaissance at the UK’s Defence Academy in Shrivenham, Swindon, on 14 August last year, Manchester Crown Court heard.

On 2 October, two men were killed in a separate incident as worshippers gathered at Heaton Park Hebrew Congregation synagogue to mark Yom Kippur, the holiest day of the year for Jews.

Syrian-born UK citizen Al-Shamie, who wore a fake suicide belt, was shot dead by police outside the building.

![MoD Shrivenham is the home of the UK Defence Academy, the court heard, which provides professional defence and security education to the British Armed Forces, wider UK government, UK industry and overseas personnel](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/22/21/56/The-UK-Defence-Academy-in-Shrivenham-Swindon.png)

Assistant Chief Constable Rob Potts, who holds operational responsibility for Counter Terrorism Policing North West, said:“Mohammed Bashir is a dangerous individual with extremist views who held conversations about preparing for ‘war’.

“He was caught on camera discussing carrying out a terrorist attack with a man who would later go on to conduct an attack himself. Together they made a ten-hour round trip to conduct hostile reconnaissance at a military base and noted it’s security arrangements.

“The pair tried to cover their tracks by disposing of their mobile phones but, thanks to the meticulous work of investigators, evidence of damning internet searches that show research for attack planning were uncovered.

“Bashir is now facing the consequences of his actions and I hope his sentence will act as a deterrent to anybody considering taking part in extremist activity.

“Although there is no evidence to suggest that Bashir knew in advance about plans to conduct a terrorist attack at the Heaton Park Hebrew Congregation Synagogue, I know today’s sentencing will prompt public discussion on that incident.

“My thoughts today are with all those affected by the attack, particularly the loved ones of those whose lives were taken and those who were seriously injured. They have been at the forefront of our minds throughout the investigation and will continue to be so.”
