A YORK nursery has become the first in the city to receive a 'strong' rating across the board under the new Ofsted framework.

Liliput Day Nursery York, which is located at Ings Lane in Nether Poppleton, is celebrating becoming the first early years setting in York to achieve a Strong Standard rating in every category under Ofsted’s new inspection framework.

The owners say this marks a landmark moment for the small, family-run nursery, which is now celebrating recognition for its high standards, supportive environment, and inclusive approach.

Being a small, home-from-home nursery, Liliput Day Nursery York Ltd was praised by inspectors for creating a setting where children “develop confidence and resilience".

A spokesperson for the nursery says: "For families, the Strong Standard judgement simply confirms what many already felt: that Liliput Day Nursery York Ltd is a truly exceptional place for children to learn, grow and flourish.

"The recognition under the new Ofsted framework not only celebrates the nursery’s achievements but also sets a benchmark for early years excellence across York.

"As the team celebrates this historic result, their focus remains unchanged -providing a loving, inspiring environment where every child can grow, belong and flourish every single day."

The report states that "staff develop caring, nurturing relationships with each child and treat them with kindness and respect", which then influences the way the children treat each other.

They added that the staff are consistent in their approach and children behave well, which creates a very positive environment with well-embedded routines.

The nursery’s success is also attributed to its unique curriculum based on the ethos of Grow, Belong, Flourish - an approach which owners say helps children build confidence, curiosity, and independence.

Support for children with Special Educational Needs (SEN) was identified as a particular area of excellence.

Liliput’s team has established a reputation for delivering highly effective support for children with additional physical, emotional and developmental needs, with inspectors praising "their highly inclusive culture” which "helps children thrive and succeed".

Leadership at the nursery was praised as "highly reflective, and the decisions they make lead to consistent, high-quality care for all children,” with strong oversight and daily involvement that creates a culture where staff feel “supported and motivated.”

In the report, inspectors said: "Leaders build close and positive relationships with all families, communicating effectively and consistently with parents.

"This is particularly evident for those children who are known (or previously known) to children's social care, and children who face other barriers to their learning.

"Leaders work collaboratively with outside professionals and parents to put in place effective strategies that have a positive impact for children with special educational needs and/or disabilities."

In daily practice, the report found children were well prepared for the next stage in their learning.

Older children serve their own food, while younger children help with tidying up and learn to wash their own hands, building valuable independence skills.

The report added: "Children arrive at this setting feeling excited to learn. They enjoy spending time with their friends and developing their independence skills.

"Staff ensure that children develop a sense of belonging and feel safe and cared for."

The full report can be found on the Ofsted website.
