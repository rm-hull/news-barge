Tropical Storm Bertha makes landfall in Louisiana

Tropical Storm Bertha made landfall in St. Bernard Parish, southern Louisiana, on Wednesday with maximum sustained winds of 45 mph.

A tropical storm warning was issued across the Gulf Coast from Alabama to Louisiana, encompassing New Orleans, where residents were already contending with extreme heat.

The storm's primary threats included winds and storm surges up to 3 feet, though the heaviest rains remained offshore, reducing widespread flooding risks.

Louisiana Governor Jeff Landry called the forecast "encouraging" but advised residents to prepare for potential localized flooding.

Bertha, the second tropical storm of the Atlantic hurricane season, is expected to weaken and dissipate inland in Texas by Friday.
