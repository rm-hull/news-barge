A TOP actor from North Yorkshire has revealed the time that his lifelong condition caused “pandemonium” at an event with top Hollywood stars.

James Norton revealed he almost fainted during a high-profile event due to his Type 1 diabetes.

The 41-year-old actor - known for his roles in Happy Valley, Grantchester, and House of Guinness - was diagnosed with the condition at 22 and uses a continuous glucose monitor (CGM) to help manage it, along with daily doses of insulin.

He recalled the incident on the Table Manners podcast while attending a GQ event where he had taken insulin in anticipation of eating carbohydrates.

James said: "The starter arrived, and it was this little bit of smoked trout with no carbs.

"I was sitting there, having done my insulin, having eaten this trout, going, 'I’ve got 10 minutes before I start to faint, basically.'"

Unable to interrupt the speeches, he began to feel faint and approached a member of staff for help, explaining he was diabetic and needed food urgently.

He was given chocolate and juice, but didn't realise "pandemonium had broken out backstage" as people believed he was experiencing hypoglycaemia.

James added: "In the middle of Nicole Kidman’s speech, someone randomly brings me a steaming plate of potatoes. It basically looked like: ‘This is bull. James is hungry. Give him some potatoes.’"

James Norton is a long-time patron and supporter of Breakthrough T1D (formerly JDRF), a leading Type 1 diabetes research and advocacy charity.
