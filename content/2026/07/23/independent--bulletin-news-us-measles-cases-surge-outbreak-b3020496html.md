Measles outbreak: Are you protected?

Measles cases in the U.S. have reached a 35-year high, with 2,295 cases reported nationwide in 2026, surpassing previous outbreaks.

The uptick in cases is primarily linked to vaccine hesitancy in certain communities, which has allowed the life-threatening virus to circulate more widely.

Most confirmed infections were among unvaccinated or under-vaccinated individuals, with many cases occurring in young children who face higher risks of severe illness.

Outbreaks have been observed across multiple states, including South Carolina, Utah, Texas and Virginia, spreading in various settings such as religious communities and schools.

The recent surge threatens America's longstanding measles elimination status, with a meeting scheduled in November to determine if this status has been lost.
