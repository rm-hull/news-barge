Astronomers have discovered a unique celestial object in the CD-35 2722 star system, which may be the first moon found outside our solar system.

The object is unusual because it does not orbit a planet, but instead circles a brown dwarf that itself orbits a star.

Due to its atypical orbit and significant mass, scientists are debating its classification, as it blurs the lines between stars, planets and moons.

Researchers have provisionally named the object an "exosatellite" and detected it using the European Southern Observatory’s Very Large Telescope.

This discovery, published in the journal Nature, marks a significant breakthrough in understanding the formation and prevalence of moons in other planetary systems.
