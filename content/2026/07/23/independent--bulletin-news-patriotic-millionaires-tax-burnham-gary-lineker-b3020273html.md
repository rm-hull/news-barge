Burnham unveils tax cut to ‘bring back hope’ for struggling families

Over 100 UK-based millionaires, including Gary Lineker, Richard Curtis, and Val McDermid, have publicly urged new Prime Minister Andy Burnham to implement a wealth tax on the country's richest individuals.

Organised by Patriotic Millionaires UK, the group sent a letter to Mr Burnham stating, 'We want you to tax us. We can afford it,' emphasising that the tax should target wealth-derived income rather than earned income.

The millionaires argue that taxing the super-rich is a 'patriotic' act that would contribute to a 'better Britain' and help fund public services, addressing the cost of living crisis and reducing wealth inequality.

Proposed mechanisms for a wealth tax include a 2 per cent levy on assets exceeding £10 million, which Patriotic Millionaires UK estimates could generate £24 billion annually, alongside reforms to capital gains tax.

The intervention comes as Prime Minister Burnham, who has not ruled out a wealth tax, faces scrutiny over how he will fund his recently announced policies aimed at easing the cost of living, such as capping bus fares and cutting electricity bills.
