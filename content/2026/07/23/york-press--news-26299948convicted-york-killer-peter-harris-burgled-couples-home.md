## Woman returned from church to find killer in her York home, court told

Killer and burglar Peter Anthony John Harris *(Image: North Yorkshire Police)</i>*

A woman discovered a convicted killer inside her living room when she returned to her York home, a court heard.

Peter Anthony John Harris, 45, broke into a couple’s home in central York while the wife was at church on the morning of Sunday, May 31 this year, the city's Crown Court was told.

Stephen Littlewood, prosecuting, said the victim had left her husband upstairs and returned at 11.50am to find Harris in her living room.

She asked him who he was and what he was doing in her home and managed to get him out of the house. He smelt of alcohol.

In 2014, Leeds Crown Court heard how on New Year’s Day, Harris broke into the home of Elsie Mowbray, 87, off Aldborough Way, York.

When her son Richard arrived and tried to detain him, Harris pushed Elsie over as he fled. Her head hit the wall hard and she died some days later.

Harris had been drinking and an hour later, in a flat off Fishergate, he robbed an acquaintance and his partner in the presence of a young baby.

Jailing him for 12 years for manslaughter, the burglary and the robbery, Mr Justice Lindblom told Harris: "Her family had hoped her death when it came would be peaceful, not the brutal, violent ugly nightmare it turned out to be."

Harris, of no fixed address, pleaded guilty to the May 31 burglary and was jailed for 876 days.

Judge Simon Hickey said Harris thought the wife had gone to church when she left her husband in the house.

Defence solicitor Neal Kutte said of Harris: “He was intoxicated at the time having drunk a lot of alcohol. It is his only explanation for what he did. He was simply found drunk in a room downstairs and left when he was told to go.”

York Crown Court heard Harris’ long criminal record began in 1998 when he carried out three house burglaries and was jailed for three years.

In 2003, he was jailed for three years and three months for burgling home.

In 2006, he was given a 30-month jail term for aggravated burglary and carrying a knife.

And in 2008, he was jailed for attempted robbery, carrying a knife and theft from a house.

Mr Littlewood said the wife left her home at 10.20am on May 31. The back door was open because it was hot.

CCTV showed Harris and two other men outside the front of their home while she was out. Only Harris went into the house and damage to a wall showed where Harris had climbed over it to get access to the house.

On her return, the wife heard a noise in the living room and investigated.

On finding Harris she got him out of the house via the kitchen and the back gate. He didn’t appear to have taken anything.

Police recognised Harris from the CCTV and arrested him.
