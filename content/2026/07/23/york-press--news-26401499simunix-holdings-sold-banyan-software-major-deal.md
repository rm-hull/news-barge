A York software business has been acquired by an international technology group.

Simunix Holdings, based at St Mary's Court, has been sold to Banyan Software, a global buyer of software companies.

The deal was supported by corporate finance advisers at Azets, who operate a major regional hub in York on Monks Cross Drive.

Founded in 1998 by John Lewis, Simunix provides people and business search, ID verification, and data software for organisations across the public and private sectors.

Its flagship platforms, ORBIS and T2A, are trusted by logistics companies, financial services, compliance organisations, and UK police forces for identity verification and geo-location services.

Banyan Software, which has more than 100 portfolio companies across the UK, Europe, the Americas, and Asia Pacific, follows a 'buy, grow, and hold-for-life' strategy and aims to provide a permanent home for businesses like Simunix.

Nick Barker, partner at Azets Corporate Finance, said: "Simunix has developed an exceptional reputation in advanced software for identity search and verification and, in Banyan, has a new owner who has a proven track record of taking the businesses in its international portfolio to the next level.

"As a long-standing and valued client of the Azets York office, we were delighted to provide support on this important transaction for the business and its exiting shareholders.

"The transaction supports Simunix’s next phase of growth under new ownership that promises to invest in its long-term development and uphold the core values that have made it such a success over the past 28 years.

"Our best wishes go to all parties in the transaction as a new era dawns for the business."

Azets advised shareholders on the transaction from the heads of terms to completion, with business tax advice led by associate director Karen Sadler.

Yorkshire law firm Raworths also acted as legal advisers, with corporate and commercial specialists Jon Healey, Jennifer Watson, Jessie Wray, and employment lawyer Sally Togher supporting the deal.

John Lewis, founder of Simunix, said: "We have developed a longstanding relationship with both Azets and Raworths over many years, so we had no doubts about placing this transaction in their hands.

"We received an outstanding service throughout the process, characterised by commercial insight, responsiveness, and hands-on support to ensure a successful outcome."

He said: "Banyan stood out for their long-term mindset, their respect for our core values, our team, and their genuine commitment to the business and the customers we serve."

The value of the transaction has not been disclosed.
