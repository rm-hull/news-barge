The future of one of York’s most historically significant buildings has been safeguarded after a university agreed to move into the site.

York St John University said it will start moving into King’s Manor, next to York Art Gallery in Exhibition Square, over the summer to welcome students and staff in September.

It follows the university reaching an agreement with City of York Council to take a long-term lease on the Grade I-listed building, originally built for the abbots of St Mary's Abbey before it housed the Council of the North set up in 1484 by King Richard III of England.

York St John University’s vice chancellor and chief executive, Professor Karen Bryan, said it is the “beginning of an exciting new chapter for both York St John University and King's Manor”.

“For centuries, this remarkable building has been a place for education, and we are proud to continue that tradition by creating an inspiring environment for our students, staff and wider community,” Prof Bryan said.

"As part of occupying the site, we are committed to improving public access and links to the wider Abbey Precinct, as well as growing our local partnerships. By bringing new life to this historic building, we look forward to seeing it become a vibrant centre of learning and collaboration."

York St John said the site will provide new teaching and lecture spaces, a home for the public-facing Law Clinic and Business Clinic, dedicated business incubation space, new events facilities, and office accommodation for up to 100 staff.

The additional provision will allow the university to reconfigure spaces across other sites to improve student timetabling and reduce the use of temporary facilities and off-site storage, York St John said.

A York St John spokesperson said the move marks the “latest chapter in the educational legacy of King's Manor". "Over the centuries, the building has been home to the original Manor School, the Wilberforce School for the Blind and, most recently, the University of York”.

The University of York previously leased King's Manor but in 2024 the university announced it would be leaving the site after more than 60 years due to “substantial running and conservation costs”.

At the time, a University of York spokesperson said the running costs for King’s Manor (excluding provision of its library services) were around £500,000 from 2022 to 2023.

There was also a £1 million backlog of “the most urgent maintenance” at the site, the university spokesperson said.

They added that “at least £15 million” would be required in terms of longer term costs to keep the building watertight.

The university also said accessibility at the building was a “major concern”.
