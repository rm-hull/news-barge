If you're thinking about buying a Dyson vacuum cleaner, it's worth understanding the differences between the models before making your choice. Known for powerful suction, clever engineering and innovative features, Dyson offers a wide range of vacuums, so finding the right one for your home isn't always straightforward.

I’ve been testing household appliances for almost a decade, and in that time I’ve tried just about every type of vacuum cleaner you can imagine. From lightweight handhelds and powerful cordless sticks to robot vacuums, wet floor cleaners, cylinders and uprights, I’ve seen how different designs perform in real homes and everyday situations.

To find out which models are really worth buying, I tested a selection of Dyson vacuums across different surfaces in my home. While suction power matters, the best Dyson vacuum will ultimately depend on factors like your flooring, space and lifestyle.

From compact cordless models for quick cleans to powerful machines designed for pet owners and carpets, I’ve rounded up the best Dyson vacuums to suit a range of households and budgets.

Edge cleaning was particularly strong. Keep in mind, however, is designed purely for hard floors, so won’t be effective at deep carpet cleaning.

What makes it so distinctive is the engineering. Everything – motor, battery and dustbin – is housed within the slim handle, which keeps the floor head extremely low-profile and light. It’s noticeably easier to manoeuvre than Dyson’s other cordless options, and far less physically demanding, while it’s also very quiet in operation.

The dusting attachment is excellent for clearing cobwebs from ceilings and corners, and the lightweight build makes above-floor cleaning simple. But if your home is predominantly hard flooring and you want something ultra-light and easy to handle, particularly if mobility is a concern, this is a thoughtfully designed option.

In testing, it made light work of muddy footprints and paw prints after a particularly wet weekend in my hallway. The high-speed microfibre roller scrubs effectively, and my floors were dry in just a few minutes. There’s a small edge gap of around 1.5cm that it doesn’t quite reach, but overall cleaning coverage is strong. An anti-tangle comb also helps prevent pet hair from wrapping around the roller.

The self-clean cycle is a standout feature. It flushes the system before drying the roller with 85C hot air, leaving it dry to the touch and helping prevent odours. Emptying the dirty water tank and debris tray isn’t fully hands-free and can be a bit of a faff at first – you need to keep the tank level to avoid splashing – but it becomes easier with practice.

Slim and manoeuvrable, it lies flat to around 11.3cm, making it easy to get under low furniture. It doesn’t come with attachments, but for a dedicated hard-floor washer, it delivers a thorough, hygienic clean.
