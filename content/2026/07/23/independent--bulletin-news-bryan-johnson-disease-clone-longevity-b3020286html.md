Millionaire biohacker Brian Johnson, 48, claims to have created a "clone" of himself as a newborn baby using induced pluripotent stem cell (iPSC) technology.

The tech entrepreneur, known for extreme health protocols, said this process could offer a new way to reverse ageing and develop treatments.

Johnson's "baby-bryan" currently exists in a petri dish, with the aim of testing therapies, growing organs for transplantation, and injecting young cells.

He recently disclosed a diagnosis of an incurable autoimmune disease, which he plans to "solve" using AI, multiomics, and custom-built DNA.

While Johnson defends his experiments as advancing medical discoveries, some followers have criticised his approach, linking it to a fear of death.
