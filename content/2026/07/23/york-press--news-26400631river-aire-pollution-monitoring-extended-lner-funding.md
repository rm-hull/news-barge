A Yorkshire-based river charity has expanded its environmental efforts.

The Aire Rivers Trust, which works to improve the River Aire and its surrounding environments, extended its Riverfly monitoring programme into North Leeds thanks to funding from LNER’s Customer and Community Investment Fund (CCIF).

This expansion has enabled the charity to highlight some of the most polluted stretches of river in the Aire Catchment.

Sam Riley-Gunn, Citizen Science Officer for the Aire Rivers Trust, said: "The LNER funding helped us to expand our Riverfly monitoring into North Leeds, allowing us to highlight some of the most polluted stretches of river in the Aire Catchment.

"This programme has played a big part in our efforts to protect the river, while working closely with different groups and members of our local community.

"We would encourage other charities to submit a bid for this year's CCIF funding."

The funding also supported the charity’s recruitment and training of more volunteers to collect data.

Rachael Wilson, Community Investment Manager at LNER, said: "Our communities are at the heart of everything we do.

"Through the Customer and Community Investment Fund, we are proud to support organisations delivering positive change for people, places and the environment."
