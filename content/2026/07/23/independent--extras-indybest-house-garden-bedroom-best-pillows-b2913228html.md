The quality of your sleep can have a dramatic impact on your life, and plumping for one of the best pillows is a good way to improve it overnight. Many of us hang onto pillows long after their best when they’ve lost their shape, don’t suit the way we sleep or just aren’t as comfortable as they should be.

I’ve reviewed all kinds of bedding from mattresses to duvets over the years and have discovered that finding the perfect pillow is one of the trickiest elements to get right. It’s crucial to support your head and neck but much depends on sleeping preferences. Side sleepers usually need a higher and firmer pillow to keep the spine aligned, while back sleepers should choose a lower, medium-firm pillow.

Fillings can range from memory foam to feather and down, though these should be avoided if you suffer from allergies when should look for hypoallergenic versions instead. These are some of the best pillows to buy to get you snoozing soundly.

Side sleepers rejoice. This Simba pillow is – pardon the pun – a dream to sleep on. It has three interchangeable layers you can mix and match to find the perfect combination. One has foam springs in a cushioned sleeve, the other two have a soft filling made from recycled PET fibres combed into down-like layers. Back sleepers can whip one of these out entirely for a more medium firmness but I preferred to leave all three layers in for a fairly firm but astonishingly supportive effect which really made a difference to the way I sleep. That also meant I didn’t need to find somewhere to store the layer I had removed.

![Best pillows Indybest review Simba CustomFlex pillow]([https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/22/14/04/Best-pillows-Indybest-review-Simba-CustomFlex-pillow.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/22/14/04/Best-pillows-Indybest-review-Simba-CustomFlex-pillow.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp) 608w)

The cotton cover was a delight to sleep on too – quilted and so soft, I could have happily used the pillow without a case over it. This has thermoregulating Stratos technology used by NASA on one side which is claimed to be up to 3C cooler than non-treated fabric – it certainly seemed breathable and meant I didn’t ever feel hot when using this pillow. The cover can also be easily zipped off to freshen it up. My only gripe is the price which is enough to give me sleepless nights – the Duvalay deluxe pillow is a cheaper option for a similar feel. However, my weekend lie-in has definitely been upgraded since adding this pillow to my bed.
