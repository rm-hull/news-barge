## York pub with on-site brewery nominated for award in annual competition

Regulars at The Rook and Gaskill recently watched an England Mens's football team knock out game at the 2026 World Cup *(Image: Supplied)*

A YORK pub on a site which has been serving the community since the 18th century has been shortlisted for an award.

The Rook and Gaskill in Lawrence Street has been shortlisted by readers of The Press in one of its most popular competitions - the hunt to find the best pub of the year.

Owner-landlord Paul Marshall said he felt good to be in the top 10 and it was fantastic to be recognised.

  Regulars at The Rook and Gaskill recently watched an England Mens's football team knock out game at the 2026 World Cup 

*(Image: Supplied)*

The premises has been known as The Rook and Gaskill since the late 1980s, he said, and was The Queens before then but there has been a pub on the site since the 1700s.

As reported by The Press last year, an on-site brewery has boosted the pub's offering.

Paul said: "We give our customers a selection of more than 50 beers, which we consider something of a USP.

"We normally have 20 keg beers and six cask on offer, and we've been a proud member of CAMRA for the last 15 years.

"We have a small, single barrel brewery where we rotate a batch of our own brew once a fortnight and it's great to be able to offer that extra choice.

Paul said creating a special ambience at the pub is something the team are proud of and work hard at.

He said: "We get great feedback about the vibe here and that’s what it’s all about really.

"Pubs need it to keep people – we welcome families with their kids and everybody up to regulars in their eighties and nineties and we give them a warm environment.

"I measure success by returning customers and being nominated for awards like this is also a good barometer.

"We’ve had customers coming back every week for 15 years."

One tradition that has stood the test of time is the pub’s Thursday night quiz.

Mr Marshall said: It's a big fun quiz and we kept it up during Covid.

"One of the best aspects of that was meeting up with old regulars from the neighbourhood who drank here as students.

"Because of where we're located, close to the city's student population and close to the campuses, we do lose some familiar faces every year, so to see some of those faces return, even if it was virtually, was a real treat.

  *Voting slips for The York Press Best Pub 2026 are in copies of the newspaper up to and including Saturday, August 5 and must be posted off to the address provided to count.*

  *The winner will be announced shortly afterwards.*
