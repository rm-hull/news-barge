## British TV stars join Agatha Christie classic coming to Grand Opera House York

Susan Penhaligon will play Mrs Boyle *(Image: Grand Opera House York)*

Two well-known British TV actors will appear in the 75th-anniversary UK tour of an Agatha Christie classic coming to York.

Nicholas Bailey, noted for his role as Dr Anthony Trueman in EastEnders, and Susan Penhaligon, acclaimed for her performance in Bouquet of Barbed Wire, will star in Agatha Christie’s The Mousetrap.

The production is hailed as the world’s longest-running play.

Mr Bailey will take on the role of Major Metcalf, while Ms Penhaligon will play Mrs Boyle.

The show will run at the Grand Opera House York from Monday, June 21 to Saturday, June 26, 2027.

Mr Bailey, recognised for his diverse roles in a variety of mediums, has returned to Albert Square on several occasions.

Ms Penhaligon, who became a household name through her role as Helen Barker opposite Dame Judi Dench in A Fine Romance, has been a staple on both stage and screen.

The Mousetrap, renowned for its intricate plot, originally premiered in 1952 and celebrated its 30,000th performance in the West End earlier this year.

The story, centred around a London murder, unravels in a remote country guesthouse, as seven strangers become engulfed in a swirling mystery amid a deadly snowstorm.

Further casting and creative details for the tour are yet to be announced.
