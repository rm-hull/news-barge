**Spoilers forRansom Canyonseason 2 ahead.**

I shouldn't love *Ransom Canyon* as much as I do, but the***Virgin River***meets* Yellowstone*Western drama always has a way of hooking me back in.

In season 2, we've explored a lot of overarching storylines that were first established back in season 1.

The land feud between the Double K and Fuller ranches has become more ferocious, local bar owner Quinn (Minka Kelly) has left for New York despite her on-off relationship with rancher Staten (Josh Duhamel), and mysterious newcomer Yancy (Jack Schumacher) has revealed to new love interest Ellie (Marianly Tejada) that he has a secret wife.

But by the time we get to the end of this new batch of eight episodes, everything has changed again... and not always for the better.

So, where on Earth is everyone at now? Here's a full breakdown of the***Ransom Canyon* season 2 ending, and what it could mean for season 3 if Netflix decides to renew the series.

## Ellie gives birth after her explosive fight with Sidney

 ![Ellie looks at something offscreen](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/FLBQyZne3UQfKCHE4GCjH7.jpg) 


As the *Ransom Canyon* season 2 finale begins, we meet Ellie as she regains consciousness in hospital. She's been brought in via ambulance after Yancy's "wife" (as their marriage was never annulled) Sidney (Heidi Grace Engerman) pushed her off a house porch, endangering Ellie and Yancy's unborn baby.

When Yancy gets there, things are touch and go, but the doctors soon confirm that mother and baby are both okay — though the fall has forced Ellie into labour, meaning baby Isabella has arrived much sooner than expected.

However, Ellie is really shaken up by what happened, as is left convinced that Sidney will return to attack her and Isabella. Yancy tries to reassure her as best as he can, telling Ellie that he will protect the pair of them.

Quinn comes to visit to take her mind off things, explaining how well the music festival they held at Gracie's has done, pushing the bar so far in the black that they made more profit that expected.

Quinn also spots Staten on his way to visit Yancy, explaining in the elevator that she only pushed him away because she was "scared." Staten claims that she hasn't been listening to what's actually happening between them, alongside how much he has changed.

## Lucas tells Lauren about Ashley — but Kit's got bigger problems

 ![Lucas looks at something offscreen](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/YpRkt9v3XAjp8VPJonHvuC.jpg) 


Meanwhile, things are getting really messy for Lucas (Garrett Wareing). After a suspected drugs charge, he has to be bailed out by older brother Kit (Casey W. Johnson), and it's clear how much the brothers have swapped personalities since the beginning of season 1.

On his way into the station, Kit is stopped by Sherriff Dan (Philip Winchester), who threatens him for messing up daughter Lauren's (Lizzy Greene) life. But hang on... weren't Lucas and Lauren together instead? They were at the beginning of season 2, but Lauren soon found that her feelings were actually towards Kit.

Dan offers Kit a "start over" opportunity, claiming that he knows of a ranching job in Wyoming that would get Kit back into the work he loves so much — and away from Lucas and Lauren. "Everything you touch turns to sh*t" Dan tells him, with Kit intent on fixing the mess he has made.

Lucas emerges from the jail, and the brothers fight in the street when Kit tells him how urgently Lucas needs to get his life back on track. Lucas then tells Kit that he slept with local girl Ashley (Jennifer Ens), before driving to meet Lauren outside the hospital and tell her the truth too. As you can imagine, it doesn't go down well.

## Quinn makes her next financial investment in Ransom Canyon

 ![Quinn stands in a field in front of a barn](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/HcnwCh3WYpJ9i6jDTYXfpT.jpg) 


Things are going incredibly well at Gracie's, with Quinn's business investor Mary Lou even offering Lauren the chance to design an album cover for a new music artist because of the poster she created for the festival.

In fact, the festival has gone so well that Mary Lou is already thinking about next year's event, with acts already enquiring about being in the lineup. The only problem is accommodation — if Quinn wants to make the festival bigger and better, Ransom Canyon currently doesn't have the capacity to host more visitors.

But Quinn has an idea. She wants to use the extra profits made to invest back into Ransom, which starts with buying abandoned restaurant, The Lone Star, and converting it into a music venue and hotel. She pitches it to Mary Lou as a local version of The Ryman in Nashville, and the pair shake on it.

## Claire pushes Quinn and Staten back together for good

 ![Claire stands at the door facing her two daughters](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/Ai2B6PypzdrehFVHKL7pyJ.jpg) 


Quinn's mother Claire (Patricia Crawford) arrived in town in season 2 episode 1, and now she's about to fly back home again. Before she does, she's got two stops to make.

The first is swinging by Quinn's house, with her daughter revealing the Lone Star investment plans. On top of that, Quinn has also sold her own farm to give Claire the money to buy a new house, giving her mother a chance to start afresh and chase her own dreams, for once.

Claire then swings by Staten's house, and convinces him to pursue Quinn one last time. She believes the pair are each other's true loves, despite Quinn telling Claire earlier that the pair are "done forever." Before she leaves Staten's house, he asks Claire to take something with her.

Claire says goodbye to her daughters at Gracie's and hands Quinn a box from Double K ranch, with a letter from Staten inside. He writes that everything he has said is "wrong," and asks her to meet at the cabin. While we wait to see if Quinn turns up, we see a flashback timeline of their relationship, from meeting each other as young teens to the present day.

Sure enough, Quinn opens the cabin doors to find Staten lighting a fire, as the pair kiss. Finally! The next morning, Staten tells Quinn how many times he's dreamed of having her there, with Quinn replying that she knows that Staten is "her person."

## Freddie convinces Yancy that the oil grift is real — which brings Ellie to breaking point

 ![Yancy talks to a man while leaning forwards on a truck](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/P3GSxzMaybVuttXTMFg4jd.jpg) 


Yancy's having a rough ride once again... but it's all his own fault. After his grift with old prison mate Freddie (Kenneth Miller) to trick Senator Kirkland (Brett Cullen) into believing there is oil on Double K land to stall his plans gets busted, the two reconvene.

Freddie claims he's no longer interested in the $80,000 Yancy allegedly owes him, instead revealing that he's run the survey past a new set of eyes, and their oil ruse could actually be real for Fuller ranch. If so, Yancy and Freddie could come into millions of dollars.

Yancy arrives at the hospital to tell Ellie the good news, but she's discharged herself. He finds her at her house, with officer Kai (Justin Johnson Cortez) keeping a 24-hour watch on the premises.

Between Freddie, the potential oil and being constantly convinced that Sidney will snatch Isabella from her, Ellie is at breaking point. She tells Yancy that she needs to protect their daughter from the "darkness that follows him," asking him to leave the house.

This effectively means that they've broken up, with Yancy starting a physical fight with Kai while leaving the house. We see him drive away in the snow, furious and upset with what has just happened.

## Kit leaves for Wyoming as Lucas and Lauren make an uneasy truth

 ![Kit lifts a wooden beam with both hands](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/ZLpTGhJdGZyumLSG9xxM6j.jpg) 


After finding out about her album art offer, Lauren waits for Kit on the stoop. She tells him that she's excited about their future together, but Kit no longer wants to continue seeing her. he claims that he's finally realised what Lucas sees in Lauren, and how big her success could be — he doesn't want either brother getting in the way of that.

The next day, Lucas finds Kit on the front porch at home, with a bag all packed. Kit tells him that in order to fix things between them, he's going to Wyoming to take the ranching job. The two share a tearful goodbye before Kit leaves.

Later that night, Lauren arrives looking for Kit. Lucas is there instead, and the two both admit how much they messed their relationship up. Lucas says that he hopes the pair will be okay again someday, and the two form an uneasy truce.

Before she leaves, Lucas tells Lauren that Kit is at the bus stop. She arrives just in time before he boards, telling him not to go. Kit says that he has to, with Lauren doubling down by replying that she's never letting him go. The pair kiss, but does he actually end up going?

## Staten asks Quinn to move in as Double K board meeting looms near

 ![Staten and Quinn talk to each other at a diner counter](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/vqg5TytjuUEzFe9GJUxKo.jpg) 


On the night of the board meeting, there's a lot to play for. Senator Kirkland has finally relented, claiming that he'll back Staten as the head of Double K ranch in front of the board, forfeiting his own position. Lucas also says that he'll have Staten's back.

Before he enters, Quinn meets him outside, saying that she wouldn't have missed such an important evening. Staten puts a key to Double K ranch in her hand, as a prompt to move in with him. "You need to live somewhere now you've sold the farm," he adds, as the two kiss one last time.

## Season 3 predictions

 ![Staten and Yancy look offscreen](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/MEpm6WUViKyYL85r2DGhg3.jpg) 


Unusually, there aren't a lot of plot lines left unresolved, but we still have two key stories to follow if Ransom Canyon season 3 gets picked up.

The first is what actually happens at the board meeting. The feud between Double K and Fuller ranches is far from over, even if it doesn't feel like a priority at the end of season 2. Yancy and Freddie are clearly still scheming, so it might not be as straightforward as Staten claiming victory at the meeting.

The second is what happens between Ellie and Yancy. Ellie has pushed Yancy away for her own safety, but he's not going to take be separated from daughter Isabella lying down. Will he made bad decisions in order to see her, and is this really the last we've seen of Sidney?

Of course, there's also a question mark over whether Kit goes to Wyoming, though it's heavily implied that he changes his mind.
