A reliable dog bed will be one of the first purchases you make for your pet, alongside essentials such as dog leads, dog toys and dog collars. Whether you've brought home a tiny puppy, share your house with a senior dog or you’re in dire need of an upgrade for that old, flattened cushion they’ve been laying on, the best dog bed can make a noticeable difference to their comfort, recovery and quality of sleep.

These days, most dog beds have a lot more to offer than a simple padded mat, and long gone are days of wicker baskets and tartan blankets. Here, you'll find supportive memory foam mattresses for ageing joints, cosy bolstered designs for dogs that love to curl up, cooling beds for summer and durable, wipe-clean styles built to withstand muddy paws after long walks. With so many shapes, fillings and fabrics to choose from, finding the right one can quickly become overwhelming.

To help narrow it down, we tested a wide range of dog beds with pups of different ages, sizes and sleeping styles to find the models that were best at keeping our pooches calm and comfortable through daytime naps and full-night sleeps.

Whatever you’re looking for, you can rest assured our canine testers and expert handlers have found you the best dog beds on the market for every budget, size and specific requirement.

This Scruffs box bed combines everything I look for in a dog bed, from a reasonable price point and pleasing design to easy laundering and adequate cushioning for my pup. Nudi, who’s a medium sized rescue, settled into the bed and – as a dog that loves to feel secure – seemed to like the enclosed feel of the bolster sides. The central portion was big enough for him to curl up like a croissant (a common sleeping position in this household), while the raised edges gave him a comfy prop for his head and paws.

Given that Nudi weighs four stone and filled the XL size, I doubt owners of large or XL breeds would find this box bed sufficient. Luckily, Scruffs makes an XXL box bed in a similar design (£149, Dunelm.com) to suit bigger breeds,

The anti-slip grip on the base of the bed is handy if you have hard floors, especially if your dog loves to launch themselves into place. I also appreciated that the entire unit can go in the washing machine. Whether they’re struggling with incontinence or they have muddy paws, sometimes a removable cover doesn’t cut it when it comes to eliminating embedded smells. The XL bed just about fits into a 10kg washing machine, meaning you won’t have to visit the laundrette.

I also enjoyed that the central base cushion is fixed in place. While Nudi likely would have preferred to bury his toys beneath it (as he’s done previously with removable-cushion beds), it was a relief to not think about vacuuming crumbs out from underneath.

As for wear and tear, Nudi isn’t a major chewer, so I can’t speak to its hardiness for destructive dogs. That said, the fabric feels high quality and not paper thin like some polyester designs.
