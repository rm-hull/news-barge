# Devastation as killer wildfires tear through western Europe

- Wildfires are raging across several European tourist destinations, leading to widespread evacuations and significant land destruction.
- In southwestern France, over 10,000 people, many of them tourists, have been evacuated as a rapid wildfire scorched 2,000 hectares near Bordeaux.
- Central Spain is battling a wildfire that has burned over 32,000 hectares in Guadalajara, prompting prime minister Pedro Sánchez to call for a cross-party pact on climate change.
- Italy's Sicily has seen over 1,000 firefighting operations since Saturday, with evacuations in Agrigento due to blazes fuelled by high temperatures and winds.
- Greece and Cyprus are experiencing extreme heatwaves with temperatures exceeding 40C, while parts of southern England face prolonged dry weather, leading to a hosepipe ban.
