  *'York should not have to wait for people in London to solve every problem' says York Outer Labour MP Luke Charters*

THIS week marked a new chapter for both our city and our country, which I’m really excited about, with Andy Burnham taking over as Prime Minister.

Before I turn to that, I want to recognise Keir Starmer, who inherited a country worn down by broken finances, years of neglect, and a loss of standing in the world, and who leaves it having fixed our finances, restored our reputation abroad, and lifted half a million children out of poverty, with a social media ban among his final acts and lasting legacies.

I also want to thank him for how good he was to my family.

So often in politics, you only see personalities, not people.

Keir was very kind after the birth of my second child, Louis, which I’ll always be grateful for, too.

Politics has changed more than any of us expected over the last couple of years.

In a world of polarisation and toxic populism, we face a moment where we simply cannot afford to hang about in delivering the change the public expects, or the consequences will be dire.

Watching Andy Burnham stand on the steps of Downing Street this week, as our new Prime Minister, I am genuinely hopeful about what comes next.

Whether I am out knocking on doors, holding constituency surgeries, or simply chatting to people across York Outer, one issue comes up more than any other: the cost of living.

And in that very first speech, Andy made clear this will be his immediate priority.

People do not just want to survive week to week; they want to enjoy life again, rather than constantly looking over their shoulder.

If there is one thing I know Andy will focus on first, it is ensuring people can live well, not just get by.

That means families across York feeling the benefit, businesses having the confidence to invest, and young people seeing real opportunities on their doorstep.

Andy has always believed that decisions are better made closer to the people they affect, something I have argued time and again.

York should not have to wait for people in a London postcode to solve every problem.

We know our city, we know where investment is needed, and we believe more power should sit closer to home.

That is why having Andy as Prime Minister matters.

As a former Mayor himself, he understands that communities care most about what happens on their own doorstep.

He gets that power needs to be handed back to the people who can use it to benefit their communities.

I feel both genuinely emboldened and excited about what can be delivered for our home under his leadership.

Anyone who has spoken to me over the last year will know how strongly I feel about fixing our water system.

People in York are fed up with paying more while watching sewage pour into our rivers.

That is why I have been pushing for a mutual model of water ownership, where customers have a real stake and money is put back into fixing leaks and cleaning up our waterways, instead of disappearing into dividends.

There is still a huge amount of work to do.

But I believe we can be genuinely excited about what lies ahead.

By starting with the cost of living, giving power back to the communities who need it, and getting a grip on our utilities so they finally work as they should, we will be back on the right track.

Whatever happens in Westminster, though, the most important thing for me will always be standing up for our home in York.

This month marks two years since I was elected to represent York Outer.

In that time, I have voted almost 500 times, spoken in Parliament over 200 times, held countless advice surgeries, and met hundreds of residents at community events, school visits and on doorsteps across the constituency, alongside the many businesses, charities and organisations I support every day.

You are all what makes York great.

Some of the most important work never makes the headlines.

Behind the scenes, my team and I have helped hundreds of local people with the issues that affect everyday life, from housing and pensions to benefits, immigration and simply getting answers from public bodies.

That is one of the most rewarding parts of being your MP.

Alongside that, we have kept pushing for the bigger changes that will benefit York for years to come.

We have already secured funding for the Fulford flood scheme, money for Haxby Station, stood up for our farmers, campaigned for cleaner rivers, and worked hard to bring more investment into York Outer.

There is always more to do, and I am determined to deliver it for the place I was proud to grow up in and remain proud to call home.

My team and I are here to help if you need us, and we will always do our best to point you in the right direction if you get in touch.

And if you are involved in a local charity, community group, school, business or event, I would love to hear from you too.

As we move into the summer holidays, I hope you all have a wonderful time with your families, enjoy the good weather, and enjoy the many wonderful events taking place around York.

Take care, everyone.
