## Scarborough pupils stage board game themed adventure in school production

Key stage two pupils from Hackness Primary School performed their latest production, bringing board games to life *(Image: Supplied)*

Young actors took to the stage in Scarborough for their school’s latest production, bringing board games to life in their latest production.

Key stage two pupils from Hackness Primary School performed Let the Games Begin, a story about a family who roll enchanted dice and are transported into a world of board games.

The fantasy adventure ends in a dramatic showdown with the villainous Lady Fortune, portrayed by Year 6 student Eden.

The production was held at Hackness Village Hall, with pupils learning lines, rehearsing songs, and creating props.

Rowena Thorp, executive headteacher, said: "The audience was delighted by the whole production, commenting that it was ‘just fantastic’ and that the children ‘were all amazing’.

"The great thing about our small school is that it gives every child an opportunity to take the stage and get meaningfully involved.

"We are lucky at Hackness to have dedicated staff that work to bring out the best in every child.

"It's especially rewarding to see children who might typically be more shy or self-conscious find the confidence to get up on stage and be involved.

"The KS2 production is another example of the school's renewed commitment to bringing the community together to celebrate the achievements of its young people."
