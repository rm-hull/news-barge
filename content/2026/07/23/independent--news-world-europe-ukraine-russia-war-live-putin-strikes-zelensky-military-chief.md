Ukrainian president Volodymyr Zelensky has claimed aides to his counterpart in Moscow Vladimir Putin have signalled to him that the Kremlin leader is isolated over the war, and said a “toxic atmosphere” surrounded the Russian president.

“The situation on the front, together with our long-range sanctions and mid-range strikes, is creating a new atmosphere around Putin,” Mr Zelensky wrote on social media.

“A very toxic atmosphere... Putin alone is responsible for the losses Russians continue to suffer.

“His retinue understands this – they are signalling it to us and to our partners.”

Earlier, Ukraine’s new top commander said the armed forces had been told to press harder with their counteroffensives as Kyiv navigated the fallout of a major shake-up at the top of its military leadership.

Mykhailo Drapatyi replaced sacked general Oleksandr Syrskyi as commander-in-chief.

Meanwhile, Russia has struck an energy facility in Ukraine’s northern Chernihiv region, leaving more than 100,000 people without power.

## US to pitch ending Ukraine war as Rubio meets Lavrov today

US secretary of state Marco Rubio is set to meet today with Russian foreign minister Sergei Lavrov in the Philippines, where he’s expected to renew the prospect of the US trying to broker an end to the war in Ukraine.

The meeting between Rubio and Lavrov is slated to occur on the sidelines of a gathering by Southeast Asian foreign ministers, among the most high-profile side meetings the region's top diplomats were having at the Association of Southeast Asian Nations event in Manila.

Rubio told reporters in Manila yesterday that he plans to raise the conflict in Ukraine with Lavrov, saying that the US remained open to playing a role in ending the conflict “if that opportunity presents itself.”

He acknowledged that those efforts have “kind of fallen off a little bit over the last few months” but said the meeting would explore whether there’s an opportunity to renew talks.

“We have to have a relationship with the Russian government, even though we have areas of disagreement,” Rubio said, noting that they are two largest nuclear-armed countries.

Lavrov, speaking to reporters at the ASEAN meeting, said he would ask Rubio about the latest statements by US president Donald Trump predicting that a settlement is getting close.

“I will ask Marco Rubio about it tomorrow,” Lavrov said.

## Zelensky discusses weapons and diplomacy in talks with US envoys

Ukrainian president Volodymyr Zelensky and US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner spoke yesterday about the prospects for pursuing more active diplomacy and moving towards an accord to end the more than four-year-old war with Russia.

Both the Ukrainian leader and the White House confirmed the telephone conversation took place.

US-brokered diplomacy in the war has stalled with Washington largely focused on the conflict with Iran.

“I just spoke with President Trump's envoys Steve Witkoff @SEPeaceMissions and Jared Kushner @jaredkushner," Zelensky wrote in a post on X.

“It was a good, important conversation on how to reinvigorate diplomacy and bring peace closer. Peace is needed – peace with dignity, and Ukraine has long been ready for it,” he said, adding that both sides would stay in close contact.

“I am, frankly, asking for faster action and greater support.”

Posting earlier on X, he said securing Patriot interceptor supplies was a “critical priority now, as massive Russian attacks continue unabated.”

## Zelensky asks US for faster action on Patriot air defence production

Ukrainian leader Volodymyr Zelensky says he has discussed licences for Patriot air defence system production with US ambassador to Nato Matthew Whitaker, to protect Ukrainian cities from missile attacks.

"These past months have been extremely difficult...That is exactly what we discussed with Ambassador Whitaker today - production licences, missiles and everything else that is needed," Mr Zelensky said in his address.

"I am, frankly, asking for faster action and greater support."

Earlier, he wrote that securing Patriot interceptor supplies was a "critical priority now, as massive Russian attacks continue unabated".

President Donald Trump agreed during a meeting with Mr Zelensky at the Nato summit in Turkey this month that the United States would grant Ukraine a licence to manufacture Patriot missile interceptors.

## Russia bans night-time port shipping after drone attacks

Russia has introduced a temporary ban on vessel movements in and out of the port of Novorossiysk between midnight and 5am because of Ukrainian drone attacks, three grain industry sources say.

The sources said the order was verbal and that no official document had been sent to shippers.

Novorossiysk is Russia's biggest port by volume and handles up to one third of Russia's grain exports. The ban follows traffic restrictions introduced earlier this month in the Sea of Azov and the Kerch Strait.

Novorossiysk also ships oil, metals and goods in containers.

The sources said the port was functioning as usual outside the restricted night-time hours, and grain shipments had not been affected.

## EU envoys fail to agree on new package of Russian sanctions

EU ambassadors have failed to agree on a 21st package of sanctions against Russia over its invasion of Ukraine, an EU diplomat said.

The envoys will meet again on Thursday, the diplomat added.

The new package targets Russia's banking sector in an effort to squeeze Moscow's financial system at what the EU sees as a vulnerable time.

However, Greece wants the EU to soften already agreed restrictions on Russian liquefied natural gas (LNG) supplies and has become the main obstacle to adopting the new package.

## Recap: Burnham speaks to Zelensky and Trump in first calls after taking office

Andy Burnham has spoken with Ukraine's Volodymyr Zelensky and US president Donald Trump as part of his first set of phone calls with world leaders as prime minister.

Burnham promised that Britain's support for Ukraine would remain unchanged under his leadership, just hours after he replaced Sir Keir Starmer in No 10.

Zelensky confirmed the call on his Telegram channel and said the two sides reaffirmed their unwavering support for Ukraine and agreed to speak on bilateral cooperation in the near future, including within the Anti-Ballistic Coalition.

"I was happy to speak with Andy Burnham and congratulate him on taking office as prime minister of the United Kingdom. I truly appreciate that the support for Ukraine and our people will remain steadfast," he said.

Zelensky thanked Burnham for “warm words of respect for the Ukrainian people and for your condolences to the families and loved ones of those killed in Russia's recent attacks”.
