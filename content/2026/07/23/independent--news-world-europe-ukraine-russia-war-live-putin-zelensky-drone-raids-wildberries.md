Ukrainian president Volodymyr Zelensky has claimed aides to his counterpart in Moscow Vladimir Putin have signalled to him that the Kremlin leader is isolated over the war, and said a “toxic atmosphere” surrounded the Russian president.

“The situation on the front, together with our long-range sanctions and mid-range strikes, is creating a new atmosphere around Putin,” Mr Zelensky wrote on social media.

“A very toxic atmosphere... Putin alone is responsible for the losses Russians continue to suffer.

“His retinue understands this – they are signalling it to us and to our partners.”

Earlier, Ukraine’s new top commander said the armed forces had been told to press harder with their counteroffensives as Kyiv navigated the fallout of a major shake-up at the top of its military leadership.

Mykhailo Drapatyi replaced sacked general Oleksandr Syrskyi as commander-in-chief.

Meanwhile, Russia has struck an energy facility in Ukraine’s northern Chernihiv region, leaving more than 100,000 people without power.

## Putin's aide Lavrov signals readiness to end Ukraine war – but sticking to Alaska terms

Russian foreign minister Sergei Lavrov and US secretary of state Marco Rubio discussed the Ukraine conflict and the normalisation of US-Russia relations in a meeting in Manila this morning, the Russian foreign ministry said.

Lavrov "reaffirmed Russia's readiness for a political and diplomatic settlement of the conflict," the ministry's statement said, and told Rubio that Moscow remained committed to proposals put forward by presidents Donald Trump and Vladimir Putin at their August 2025 meeting in Alaska.

The two men, who met on the sidelines of the ASEAN Regional Forum, agreed to continue contacts, the Russian statement said.

The Kremlin has repeatedly said it wants to end the Ukraine war along the lines of terms discussed at that Alaska summit, which included a significant concession of territory by Ukraine. Kyiv has said it cannot abide by those terms, and the US has said no deal was ever finalised during the those talks.

## One killed and four injured in Ukrainian attack on Crimea

One person was killed and four injured in a Ukrainian attack on the Russian-controlled peninsula of Crimea, the Russian-installed governor said this morning.

There are two children among the injured, Crimean governor Sergei Aksyonov wrote on Telegram.

Air defence units downed 223 Ukrainian drones overnight, Russian news agencies said, citing the defence ministry.

The governor of the Ulyanovsk region east of Moscow said that Ukrainian drones had attacked an energy facility, adding it had caught fire.

Civilian sites were attacked in Russia's Voronezh region during the night, governor Alexander Gusev said, adding that some people were injured.

## Rubio and Lavrov wrap up meeting in Manila

Russian foreign minister Sergei Lavrov and US secretary of state Marco Rubio have held their meeting on the sidelines of the ASEAN forum in Manila, the TASS news agency reported.

The meeting lasted over half an hour, it added. Details of what was discussed are yet to be released.

Earlier Rubio told reporters in Manila that he planned to raise the conflict in Ukraine with Lavrov, saying that the US remained open to playing a role in ending the conflict “if that opportunity presents itself”.

## German nuclear fuel plant cleared to work with Russia's Rosatom

A German nuclear fuel assembly plant has won regulatory approval to jointly produce fuel with Russia's state-owned Rosatom, underscoring how the nuclear sector remains largely exempt from European sanctions on Russia.

A federal state regulator cleared Advanced Nuclear Fuels, part of French nuclear power company Framatome, a subsidiary of EDF, to produce fuel rods in Lingen, Lower Saxony, for Russian-designed nuclear power plants with Rosatom, Germany's federal environment ministry said on Wednesday.

The federal ministry said it regretted the collaboration but added the regulator legally had no choice but to give its go-ahead.

There is so far no EU agreement on an extension of sanctions on Russia to the nuclear power sector but Germany was working towards that, a ministry spokesperson told Reuters.

## Russia rules out giving up Ukraine territory in peace deal – report

Russian president Vladimir Putin still insists he will keep all occupied Ukrainian territory as part of any peace deal to end the war, according to a report.

Sources close to the Kremlin said Putin wants to hold these occupied regions near the border and use them as buffer zones, reported Bloomberg.

At the same time, Ukrainian president Volodymyr Zelensky has also ruled out formally ceding any territory illegally occupied by Russia since the war began in 2022.

In June Putin signalled that Russia was ready to reach a compromise to end the war, but no details were shared.

## Putin facing 'toxic atmosphere' at home amid Russian losses, says Zelensky

Ukraine leader Volodymyr Zelensky said his Russian counterpart Vladimir Putin is under pressure as a result of his country's losses in the war on Ukraine.

“He alone is the reason this war began and hasn't ended. Putin alone is responsible for the losses Russians continue to suffer,” Zelensky said in his nightly address.

“The situation on the front, together with our long-range sanctions and mid-range strikes, is creating a new atmosphere around Putin. A very toxic atmosphere,” he said.

Zelensky said Russian society is starting to feel the impacts of the war more than ever. “His retinue understands this – they are signalling it to us and to our partners. Russian society is beginning to feel it more and more as well. We need decisions to bring about peace.”

## Russia bans vessel movement at night at major port amid Ukraine drone attacks

Russia has introduced a temporary ban on vessel movements in and out of the port of Novorossiysk between midnight and 5am because of Ukrainian drone attacks, three grain industry sources said on Wednesday.

Novorossiysk is Russia's biggest port by volume and handles up to one third of Russia's grain exports. The ban follows traffic restrictions introduced earlier this month in the Sea of Azov and the Kerch Strait.

The sources said the order was verbal and that no official document had been sent to shippers. They said the port was functioning as usual outside the restricted night-time hours, and grain shipments had not been affected.

A government order published on 21 July banned vessels from anchoring in areas without air defences at the port of Azov in the Sea of Azov and the port of Kavkaz in the Kerch Strait.

## US to pitch ending Ukraine war as Rubio meets Lavrov today

US secretary of state Marco Rubio is set to meet today with Russian foreign minister Sergei Lavrov in the Philippines, where he’s expected to renew the prospect of the US trying to broker an end to the war in Ukraine.

The meeting between Rubio and Lavrov is slated to occur on the sidelines of a gathering by Southeast Asian foreign ministers, among the most high-profile side meetings the region's top diplomats were having at the Association of Southeast Asian Nations event in Manila.

Rubio told reporters in Manila yesterday that he plans to raise the conflict in Ukraine with Lavrov, saying that the US remained open to playing a role in ending the conflict “if that opportunity presents itself.”

He acknowledged that those efforts have “kind of fallen off a little bit over the last few months” but said the meeting would explore whether there’s an opportunity to renew talks.

“We have to have a relationship with the Russian government, even though we have areas of disagreement,” Rubio said, noting that they are two largest nuclear-armed countries.

Lavrov, speaking to reporters at the ASEAN meeting, said he would ask Rubio about the latest statements by US president Donald Trump predicting that a settlement is getting close.

“I will ask Marco Rubio about it tomorrow,” Lavrov said.
