A new exhibition is shedding light on untold stories behind one of history’s most infamous conspiracies.

The Gunpowder Plot: York’s Untold Story is running at Bar Convent in York until December 5 and explores the events of November 5, 1605, through rare objects, personal stories and historical context.

Dr Hannah Thomas, special collections manager at Bar Convent, said: "Remember, remember 5th November, gunpowder, treason and plot; we all know the rhyme, we have heard of Guy Fawkes, and we mark Bonfire Night with fireworks, but how much do you really know about the context, conspiracy and consequences surrounding The Gunpowder Plot?

"The Gunpowder Plot is part of York’s rich history, and here at the Bar Convent we are in a unique position to host this new exhibition."

The convent was established during a time when Catholicism was illegal and holds items directly connected to the plot.

Among the artefacts on display is the Gunpowder Crucifix, which belonged to York priest Edward Oldcorne, who was executed in the aftermath of the conspiracy.

Visitors can also take part in a self-guided walking trail and attend a lunchtime talk or an evening event.

The exhibition is open Monday to Saturday from 10am to 5pm, with last entry at 4pm.
