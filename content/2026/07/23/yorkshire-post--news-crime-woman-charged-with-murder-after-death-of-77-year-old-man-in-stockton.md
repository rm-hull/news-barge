# Woman charged with murder after death of 77-year-old man in Stockton

## A woman has been charged with murder after a 77-year-old man died following an incident at a property in Stockton.

On Tuesday (Jul 21), police responded to reports of an incident at Cedar House in Foxwood Drive, Stockton, at 2:44am.

A 77-year-old man, later confirmed to be Harry Heslop from Stockton, died following the incident.

His family are being supported by specially trained officers, police have said.

![Cedar House in Foxwood Drive, Stockton](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/jpim-static/image/2026/07/21/15/04/Cedar-House.png?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



Detectives investigating the death charged a 41-year-old on suspicion of murder and possession of a bladed article.

The woman is due to appear at Teesside Magistrates’ Court on Thursday (Jul 23).

A second woman, aged 54, remains on bail while police enquiries continue.

Detective Chief Inspector John Bonner, Senior Investigating Officer, said: "Our thoughts remain firmly with Harry's family and loved ones at what is an extremely difficult and distressing time for them. We will continue to ensure they are supported throughout this process.

“I would like to thank the dedicated officers and staff who have worked tirelessly over the last 48 hours. Specialist search officers, forensic investigators, homicide detectives and police staff have demonstrated exceptional professionalism and dedication throughout this investigation.

“I would also like to thank members of the public and the local community for their patience and support whilst extensive enquiries have been carried out. There will continue to be a visible police presence in the area and officers remain available to provide reassurance to residents.

“As a criminal investigation is ongoing, we would ask people not to speculate as this may prejudice legal proceedings. We would also ask that the privacy of Harry's family is respected at this time.

“The investigation is ongoing. Anyone with relevant information who has not yet spoken to police is asked to contact us on 101, quoting reference number 143672.

“Crimestoppers can also be contacted anonymously online at www.crimestoppers-uk.org or by calling 0800 555 111.”
