# Dodgers to visit White House after criticism and calls to boycott Trump meeting

![The Dodgers will make the visit during an off-day between series in Philadelphia and New York](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/23/15/48/GettyImages-2221397990.jpg)

- The Los Angeles Dodgers are scheduled to visit the White House on Thursday to be honored for their 2025 World Series victory, continuing a long-standing tradition for championship teams.
- The visit has drawn criticism from some fans and immigrant-rights advocates, who protested outside Dodger Stadium in March over the team's response to federal immigration raids in the Los Angeles area and urged the team to skip the visit.
- "As was the case one year ago, the Dodgers' upcoming visits to the White House and Capitol Hill follow the longtime tradition of visits by other World Series champions," the team said in a statement earlier this month. "We appreciate these tributes in recognition of our back-to-back championships."
- Not all players are expected to attend, with shortstop Mookie Betts opting to spend the off-day with his family after the recent birth of his child, and utility player Kiké Hernández missing due to a rehabilitation assignment, though he indicated he likely would not have attended regardless due to his outspoken views on immigration.
- Following the protests, the Dodgers pledged $1.1 million to groups assisting immigrant families and prohibited Immigration and Customs Enforcement from conducting operations at Dodger Stadium.
