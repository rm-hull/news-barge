## Michelin-star chef unveils special menu to mark restaurant's 20th anniversary

Tommy Banks is celebrating 20 years at The Black Swan at Oldstead *(Image: Supplied)*

A Michelin-starred chef is marking a major milestone by unveiling a special anniversary menu.

Tommy Banks is teaming up with executive chef Callum Leslie and head chef Alice Power to celebrate 20 years of The Black Swan at Oldstead.

Priced at £119 for two, the menu is available to order online for nationwide delivery on August 20, 21, 27, and 28.

  


It includes hyper-local ingredients including wild garlic, lovage, elderflower and Crapaudine beetroot that have shaped the menus over the past two decades, as well as lamb and beef that has been reared on the Banks family farm.

  Inspired by dishes from The Black Swan’s tasting menu, the limited-edition six-course 20th Anniversary Celebration Menu brings the spirit of the restaurant to dining tables across the country 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Diners can expect snacks including duck liver and blackcurrant croustade, goat’s cheese, wild garlic and pickled walnut brioche, and tomato ‘steak’ with lovage and fennel pollen.

The menu continues with braised beef cheek with laminated ale and onion brioche, and loin of Yorkshire lamb with kohlrabi and hispi, ending with desserts including elderflower parfait, sheep’s yoghurt and caramelised white chocolate and honeycomb.

It finishes with one of The Black Swan’s signature creations: a savoury-sweet Crapaudine beetroot finished with beetroot sherbet.

The collaboration forms part of wider anniversary celebrations during August, including a special 2006 vintage wine pairing celebrating the year the family took over the restaurant.

Released in the UK on the same date, the Champagne will be available exclusively at The Black Swan, with just 90 bottles in existence and 30 waiting in its cellar to be enjoyed, the restaurant said.

The special milestone also sees the launch of the 20 ingredient Negroni, developed over 18 months, and running for a limited time only.

Serving as a showcase for the herbs and fruits from The Black Swan’s Kitchen Garden, alongside locally foraged ingredients, the Sbagliato-style Negroni is made up of Oldstead Bitter Herb Liqueur – a Yorkshire interpretation of Campari – brewed with locally foraged ingredients including woodruff, wormwood, tagette marigold and bay leaf; Sweet Vermouth and Hogweed Tincture; topped with Charles Palmer Classic Cuvee.

The wine pairing will run alongside the tasting menu from August 1.
