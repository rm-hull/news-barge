**York Hospital is in wrong location - why this site is better**

WAS there no one with a vision of the future expansion of York in the 1970s?

Building a hospital in a wedge-shaped piece of land between a narrow Wigginton Road and the Scarborough railway line and with no possible way of extending the hospital beyond its limited boundaries was an absolute folly.

Anyone with any vision would have realised a bigger site with easier vehicle access roads was required for future expansion.

The Naburn Hospital closed in 1988 and came up for sale – instead of selling the site to the designer outlet why didn't the NHS hang on to the site for a future, better and greatly enlarged hospital at Naburn?

On the outer ring road and easily accessible for all motorists, with ample room for parking and potentially an ambulance station, with a direct route to Leeds, Selby and Malton.

Most of the villages on the periphery of York use motor transport and would have found Naburn convenient.

All we needed was someone with a vision.
