Matcha, a finely ground green tea, is rich in antioxidants and polyphenols, offering health benefits such as fighting oxidative stress and supporting heart health.

Concerns have emerged regarding matcha's impact on iron absorption due to its high concentration of tannins, which bind to non-haem iron in the gut.

According to nutritionist Sonal Jenkins and dietitian Nichola Ludlam-Raine, a daily matcha is unlikely to significantly affect iron levels for most, however vegans, vegetarians, and individuals with low iron or anaemia should be particularly mindful.

Ms Ludlam-Raine advises spacing out matcha consumption from iron-rich meals by “at least an hour or two” to minimise its effect on iron absorption.

Ms Jenkins said increasing vitamin C intake, through supplements or foods, can help boost non-haem iron absorption.
