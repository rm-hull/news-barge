## Singer who sparked ticket frenzy delights fans at sell-out Yorkshire gig

Teddy Swims at Scarborough Open Air Theatre. Photo by Dave Lawrence

  *Teddy Swims at Scarborough Open Air Theatre - review and photos by Dave Lawrence*

GRAMMY-nominated artist Teddy Swims performed the first of two sell-out shows at Scarborough’s Open Air Theatre on Wednesday evening.

The second show was announced after a rush for pre-sale tickets for the first date, highlighting the scale of demand for the US-born artist’s UK and Ireland tour.

Swims – real name Jaten Dimsdale – from Atlanta, Georgia began his rise to prominence with the release of the song Lose Control back in summer 2023 and he has since gone from strength to strength. From posting cover videos on social media he now performs regularly to audiences in the tens of thousands.

Swims began his 90-minute set at 9pm, coming on stage to the opening bars of The Door. Beginning with such a huge hit was a pleasant surprise and a cracking way to start the show with the lighting blazing over the stage, guitar solos a plenty and all topped with Swims’ distinctive, powerful vocals.

Dressed down in white t-shirt, light blue trousers and baseball cap, from the very first lines of the opening song Swims had the audience entranced as they sang along in unison with the charismatic singer. Hammer to the Heart and Are You Even Real followed.

  Teddy Swims at Scarborough Open Air Theatre. Photo by Dave Lawrence


There were a couple of glitzy touches, like the gold plated microphone and a ceramic mic-stand covered in slogans and graffiti in the shape of a woman, but otherwise Scarborough experienced a somewhat stripped down stage set with some steps up to a raised area and a sofa where the backing singers were largely based.

Crowd favourites came thick and fast including The Funeral, What More Can I Say, She Loves The Rain, Mr Know It All and, of course, Bad Dreams.

  Teddy Swims at Scarborough Open Air Theatre. Photo by Dave Lawrence


The new single, Break Up in Reverse, “I wish we could break up in reverse / Every night would get better, instead of getting worse” addressed the end of a recent relationship.

Swims slowed the pace of the show for the piano driven ballad Some Things I’ll Never Know and his cover of Thomas Rhett’s country tune Somethin’ ‘Bout a Woman was one of the highlights of his performance. During the latter Swims managed to pull off the impressive feat of signing some items for audience members at the front while still singing.

A gentle-giant of a man, Swims is unafraid of sharing his vulnerabilities in his songs be they related to love, loss, toxic relationships or mental health issues. He talked about a friend who had recently passed away and impressed upon everyone the importance of checking up on your friends to ensure their well-being and thanked the audience for saving his life in so many ways.

  Teddy Swims at Scarborough Open Air Theatre. Photo by Dave Lawrence


Swims and his talented band – Freak Freely – succeeded in blending pop, soul, R&B, and threw in several dashes of rock with guitarists Addy Maxwell and Jesse Hampton cutting loose several times notably on You’ve Got Another Thing Coming, Goodbyes and during a lively cover of Van Halen’s Jump.

After Jump, the show finished strongly with All That Really Matters, Bed on Fire, Guilty and closing with Lose Control, another hit from his debut album I’ve Tried Everything But Therapy (Part 1).
