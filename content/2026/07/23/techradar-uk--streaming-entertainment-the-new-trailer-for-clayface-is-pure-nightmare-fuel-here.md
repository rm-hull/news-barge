DC Studios has released a new trailer for its next movie *Clayface* — and, as someone who's not a fan of body horror, it made me squeamish in the best way possible.

The forthcoming DC Universe (DCU) film, which will take shape in theaters worldwide on October 23, will see the Batman villain star in his first-ever solo flick. And, while its latest round of footage answers some big questions that audiences had about the comic book movie, it leaves casual viewers with even more.

![Clayface - Official Trailer - Warner Bros. UK and Ireland - YouTube](https://img.youtube.com/vi/qwacKDJOESw/maxresdefault.jpg) 

Indeed, the teaser might finally pull back the curtain on how the titular shapeshifting character is created and why he embarks on a Gotham City-based quest for revenge. Nevertheless, there are myriad questions that the DCU Chapter One film's latest trailer, which arrived three months after *Clayface*'s first "super freaky" trailer, leaves unanswered.

For what it's worth, my guide on everything we know so far about *Clayface* provides clarity on some of the queries that are likely rattling around your head. If you don't have time to read through that extensive article right now, though, I've moulded a quick-fire Q&A below that'll shed light on your biggest burning questions. Bear in mind that**potential spoilers immediately follow for***Clayface*, so proceed at your own risk.

## Is Clayface in the same universe as The Batman?

 ![Robert Pattinson's Caped Crusader staring directly into the camera in The Batman Part II](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/VUEeNWwAAYtSG4NNnWLXJb.jpg) 


Let's get one of the most notable questions out of the way first: no, *Clayface* is not set in*The Batman* Universe.

That franchise, which stars Robert Pattinson as the titular vigilante and is helmed by Matt Reeves, is a completely separate entity to James Gunn's DCU. It's the latter — which also comprises *Superman*,* Supergirl*,* Creature Commandos*, and* Lanterns*— that* Clayface* is a part of.

## Will Batman be in Clayface?

 ![Batman looking down through a skylight as lightning flashes behind him in Creature Commandos episode 6](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/85By3nEjSa5QGPnjLKecVS.jpg) 


Another popular query I've seen floating around the internet since *Clayface*'s second trailer dropped. In short: no, Batman isn't expected to appear in this film.

"Why not?" you might ask. The short answer is Gunn and Safran haven't started the search for the DCU's Batman, let alone publicly announced which actor will play him.

Unless a stand-in — one likely silhouetted against the background, like the legendary Caped Crusader was in episode 6 of *Creature Commandos* on HBO Max — makes the briefest of cameos in*Clayface*, then, I'd temper your expectations over a possible appearance from the Dark Knight.

## Is this the same Clayface who appeared in Creature Commandos?

 ![Clayface attacking Rick Flag Sr. in Creature Commandos season 1](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/cXgqw7e3n7VHuVBV26o35e.jpg) 


Yes. Speaking to Entertainment Weekly, Safran confirmed that the Clayface who appears in this film is the same one who had a role to play in *Creature Commandos* season 1.

Prior to Safran's comments on the matter, there had been some confusion as to which Clayface we'd see in this movie. After all, there have been numerous iterations of the character in DC Comics. Of that contingent, Basil Karlo and Matt Hagen are the most famous — and it's the second of that pair who'll not only appear in *Clayface*, but who also showed up in* Creature Commandos*.

## Where does Clayface sit on the DCU timeline?

 ![Matt Hagen moulding the skin across his entire face while lying in a bathtub in Clayface](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/MCuSeJy9k5DbdUno8PwWj5.jpg) 


*Clayface* is the fifth DCU Chapter One project that'll see the light of day. Timeline-wise, though, it'll be the first one that audiences should watch if they choose to watch the DCU films and TV shows in chronological order — Gunn confirming as much on Threads in April.

## Who plays Clayface?

 ![Matt Hagen smiling and posing for photographs on a red carpet in Clayface](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/5wRtQw6wzvBYj6yuA8M6a5.jpg) 


That'll be Tom Rhys Harries, a Welsh actor who some readers will recognize from *Doctor Who* episode 'Dot and Bubble'.

According to Gunn, he and fellow DC Studios co-head Peter Safran were "blown away" by Harries' audition for *Clayface*'s lead role. Based on the footage we've seen this far, Harries seems like a great fit for the character, but we'll have to wait until the movie is actually released to be able to put Gunn's comments into their full context.

## How did Clayface become Clayface? What's the plot of the DCU movie?

 ![A close up of Matt Hagen's heavily scarred face looking at someone holding a giant needle in Clayface](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/LMRQkbLYq6ZeYqSGMCY3bP.jpg) 


Where the latter question is concerned, here's the flick's updated story synopsis: "Matt Hagen’s meteoric rise from Gotham street kid to Hollywood leading man is tragically cut short when a local crime boss' actions leave him horribly disfigured.

"Abandoned by his team and with nothing left, Matt seeks out a cutting edge treatment that miraculously returns him to his former self… but, also unexpectedly affects his grip on reality, spiraling him down a destructive path of all-consuming revenge."

As for Harries' Matt Hagen becomes the tragic, metamorphosizing villain, *Clayface*'s latest trailer and plot brief confirm that he'll be patient zero for an experimental serum that initially seems to heal him. However, it doesn't take long for this ground-breaking treatment's side effects to* ahem* take shape and turn him into a transmutating monster.

## What are Clayface's powers?

 ![A close up of Matt Hagen with a wide eye and bathed in red light in Clayface](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/uNeydMxmphrHW2fXqvYicP.jpg) 


As his villainous alias implies, Clayface's mud-like physiology gives him the ability to mould his body in any way he sees fit. That means he can grow and shrink in size, alter his density at will, and is impervious to traditional physical attacks, including punches, kicks, and all kinds of weapons.

Furthermore, the metahuman possesses superhuman strength, durability, and reflexes. Lastly, he has the ability to regenerate, although that's a process that takes a long time, especially if, say, he's torn apart by an explosion of some sort.

## Who's written and directed Clayface?

 ![Clayface as seen in Batman: The Animated Series](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/jy8b7rVcGnkGPruVGxYAA6.webp) 


*Batman: The Animated Series*' 'Feat of Clay' storyline for this flick (Image credit: DC)

Horror auteur Mike Flanagan is the main man behind *Clayface*'s story. However, Hossein Amini (* Obi-Wan Kenobi*,* 47 Ronin*) also helped to pen its screenplay.

As for its filmmaker, James Watkins is the man behind the camera. His directing credits include *Speak No Evil*,* The Woman in Black*, and* Black Mirror* episode 'Shut Up and Dance'.

## What is Clayface's movie rating?

 ![Matt Hagen holding a pair of scissors in a plan bathroom in Clayface](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/csaELJ9frya5h39nshHReP.jpg) 


Perhaps unsurprisingly, *Clayface* will carry a hard R-rating in the US. That's the equivalent of an 18 age rating in the UK and an Restricted (R18+) rating in Australia.

The DCU's other films in *Superman* and*Supergirl* were suitable for kids accompanied by an adult, but this one certainly won't be. Expect plenty more gross body horror moments, hyperviolent action, and other mature content to be woven through its sub-two hour runtime.
