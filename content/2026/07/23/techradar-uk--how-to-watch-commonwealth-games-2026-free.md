## Commonwealth Games 2026 Preview

Kerr arrives in Glasgow fresh from breaking the men's mile world record in London last Saturday, in a time of 3mins 42.66secs. The Scot goes for gold in the return of the historic Commonwealth Mile, as he aims to add another medal to his World Championship gold in 2023 and Olympic silver two years ago.

The track and field line-up also includes England's Zharnel Hughes in the 200m, global superstars Shericka Jackson and Andre De Grasse representing Jamaica and Canada respectively in the 4x100m relays, and a highly-anticipated javelin showdown between India's Neeraj Chopra and Pakistan's Arshad Nadeem, the two most recent Olympic champions.

Welsh rocket Emma Finucane is one to watch in the track cycling, with the 23-year-old aiming to add Commonwealth glory in the women's sprint to her Olympic, world and European golds. There will also be plenty of attention on Matthew Richardson, who claimed gold in the sprint and team sprint for Australia in Birmingham four years ago but has since switched allegiance to England, the country of his birth.

In the pool, Olympic gold medalist and world record holder Adam Peaty goes in the 50m and 100m breaststroke events as he looks to make waves for England. Meanwhile, South Africa's Chad Le Clos - who beat Michael Phelps to 200m butterfly gold way back at London 2012 - needs one medal to become the most decorated male athlete in Commonwealth Games history, having already stood on the podium 18 times.
