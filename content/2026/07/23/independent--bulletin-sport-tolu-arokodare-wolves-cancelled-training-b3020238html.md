Wolves striker Tolu Arokodare has strongly denied "inaccurate" media reports claiming he forced the cancellation of a training session by refusing to leave the pitch, allegedly to push for a transfer to Fiorentina.

Arokodare stated on social media that he has consistently conducted himself professionally and maintained high training standards throughout his career, emphasising his commitment to his team and club.

The player was notably absent from a recent friendly match against Maidenhead United, amidst reports that Wolves accepted an offer for him from Trabzonspor in Turkey but declined a potential deal with Fiorentina.

Arokodare, who signed with Wolves for £24 million last summer, scored three goals in his debut season as the team was relegated from the Premier League.

He affirmed his intention to honor his contract with Wolves, focusing on training, performing his duties, and contributing to the team professionally.
