## University team seeks homeowners for energy efficiency project

The University of Salford’s Energy House Labs is looking for residents of a Barratt-built Wellington house *(Image: Supplied)*

A university research team is searching for a specific type of home as part of a project to improve energy efficiency.

The University of Salford’s Energy House Labs is looking for residents of a Barratt-built Wellington house from the 1980s to take part in a short study.

The selected homeowner will receive a £500 Amazon voucher and will also be invited to visit Energy House 2.0 to see the research in action.

Professor Richard Fitton, who leads the research at Energy House Labs, said: "We're looking for a homeowner living in a Barratt Wellington property who can help to ensure our research accurately represents real homes built during the 1980s.

"The insights we gather will contribute to research that helps improve the energy efficiency, comfort and affordability of homes for future generations."

Energy House 2.0 is one of the world's most advanced facilities for testing housing performance and can recreate temperatures from -23°C to 50°C, as well as wind, rain, snow and solar radiation.

Researchers are building a full-scale replica of a 1980s-style Wellington home inside the University's Energy House 2.0 facility.

Anyone taking part will only need to allow a single 90-minute home survey with no changes made to their property.

Those interested in taking part can email: [energyhouselabs@salford.ac.uk](mailto:energyhouselabs@salford.ac.uk)
