A popular bakery item sold at Waitrose is being recalled due to fears it may contain hard plastic and metal pieces.

The supermarket chain is recalling its eight-pack of Chocolate Chip Brioche Rolls.

Customers are being warned that the item could be "unsafe to eat," and if you have bought the affected product, you should package it up and send it back to your local Waitrose shop for a refund.

The Food Standards Agency (FSA) has warned shoppers "do not eat it."

## Shoppers warned 'do not eat' Chocolate Chip Brioche Rolls sold in Waitrose due to recall

The recall only applies to one batch of Chocolate Chip Brioche Rolls.

To check a pack of the brioche rolls, look out for the batch code B26177 and best before date of July 26, 2026.

Waitrose has apologised to customers: "We apologise that it has been necessary to recall this product and for the inconvenience caused."

If you’d like to contact the supermarket about this recall, you can call customer care on 0800 188 884 and choose option four.
