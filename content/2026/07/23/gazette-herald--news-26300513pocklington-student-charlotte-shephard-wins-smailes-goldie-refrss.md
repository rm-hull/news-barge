A student's entrepreneurial skill has earned her the Smailes Goldie Award.

Pocklington School pupil Charlotte Shephard received the award in recognition of her leadership and business success, after guiding her school's Tycoon Enterprise Team to reach the finals of the national Peter Jones Tycoon Enterprise Competition.

Charlotte's team finished first in the country for return on investment and fifth for profit.

Gareth Shephard, head of business and economics at Pocklington School, said: "Although this was a Pocklington School Tycoon Team effort, the team agreed that it was Charlotte's entrepreneurial skills and leadership that had been a driving force in their success."

The team is now waiting to find out if their overall financial performance could see them named national winners.

The award was presented during a ceremony at Pocklington School on July 3.

Charlotte perfected her beeswax wrap design and built up stock to meet demand while facing her Lower Sixth exams.

Ian Lamb, managing partner at Smailes Goldie, said: "Charlotte’s success is clear to see and so we are immensely proud to sponsor these awards that celebrate her achievements.

"I am sure she has a very bright future ahead of her."
