### TechRadar Verdict

Sometimes, subjectivity wins out. Ransom Canyon season 2 was never going to be the best Western TV show on the market, but I can't deny how much whimsical fun I had while watching. The cowboy drama is weak, but as a soap opera, it's operating on top form.

#### Pros

- +Romantic storylines are as delicious as they always have been 
- +Patricia Clarkson is a fantastic new cast member 
- +All of the ensemble cast feel fully utilised and fleshed out 
- +The scenery alone gets one star 
- +Functions exactly as a soap opera needs to 

#### Cons

- -The ranch feud and cowboy drama doesn't live up to other Western shows we've seen this year 
- -As always with soap operas, it's more of the same 
- -Fairly anti-climactic season finale 

Why you can trust TechRadar

In life, you often have to meet something at what it is, not what you want it to be. This is the logic that I need you to apply to *Ransom Canyon* season 2 when you watch it, as all eight episodes drop on Netflix on July 23.

This Western drama soap opera hybrid serves to entertain, but it doesn't ever aim to cross a boundary. It's not striving to be the best TV show of 2026, or do anything remotely challenging or ambitious. *Ransom Canyon* is a show that stays in its subtly cringe and often stereotypical lane... and for that, I'm thankful.

I don't need all of my television to be profound, life-changing or even that meaningful. Sometimes, I just want to switch my brain off and pretend I live in a picturesque, rural town on the other side of the world where everyone has unrelenting drama except for me. Enter Double K ranch owner Staten (Josh Duhamel), local bar owner Quinn (Minka Kelly) and an eclectic ensemble of characters.

When Netflix commits to the soap opera bit, it ticks the genre boxes incredibly well. Season 1 of *Ransom Canyon* was already a strong outing, but now that the show knows itself a little more, season 2 is easily on par with the strongest seasons of*Virgin River*, the streamer's most iconic "cozy drama" IP.

The catch? Season 2 is releasing just weeks after the epic *Yellowstone* spinoff *Dutton Ranch* released on Paramount+, which was widely praised by fans and critics (including me) alike. With both shows in such close proximity of each other, it's easy to see how weak the cowboy drama in*Ransom Canyon* actually is.

## Ransom Canyon season 2 is a love story with cowboying on the side — that's an important distinction

![Ransom Canyon: Season 2 | Official Trailer | Netflix - YouTube](https://img.youtube.com/vi/2XKyPE92owY/maxresdefault.jpg) 

Here's where the two shows differ dramatically: *Dutton Ranch* is a Western drama that sprinkles complex love stories in between. *Ransom Canyon* is a love story that sprinkles Western drama in between.

Once we've understood this, we know that we can't directly compare them... as much as we want to. The two have completely different functions and goals, and it's highly likely that the fanbases won't fully cross over. That being said, I think following straight after the return of Beth Dutton (Kelly Reilly) and Rip Wheeler (Cole Hauser) in the *Yellowstone* spinoff will undoubtedly hurt Staten and Quinn's second outing.

On the surface, season 2 puts a lot of emphasis on the ongoing land feud between the Double K ranch owned by Staten and the Fuller ranch next door, now owned by dubious newcomer Yancy (Jack Schumacher). But by the time we've reached the season 2 finale, it feels completely inconsequential.

This is because we're really being driven by the romantic drama that intertwines the Ransom Canyon residents rather than the mechanics of the town itself. Nobody really cares who owns what or who works where, as long as the right people settle into the relationships that are clearly destined for them.

If Netflix hadn't have hyped the feud up in its marketing, I don't think I'd have noticed that the cowboying actually lets *Ransom Canyon* down so easily. Taylor Sheridan ain't in these parts, and I think it will always show.

## But it's a sensational soap opera — as is cast newcomer Patricia Clarkson

 ![Claire stands at the door facing her two daughters](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/Ai2B6PypzdrehFVHKL7pyJ.jpg) 


As a flat-out soap opera,*Ransom Canyon* season 2 is delicious. The indecisively infuriating will-they-won't-they dynamics run wild, the dramatic breakups are deployed at the perfect time, but when it comes down to it, things draw to a close in exactly the way you think they will.

It's almost a comfort to know what you're going to get at an end result, to have something that's often more stable than your actual life (well, if you remove all of the fictional chaos ). Even though tensions between Staten and Quinn remain front and centre, not a single supporting character feels underused.

With each connection feeling like a fulfilling one, we're really getting a lot of bang for our buck when it comes to narrative sustenance. An extra shoutout must go to newcomer Patricia Clarkson, who joins the series as Quinn's firecracker mother, Claire. I've honestly got no clue how the show scored such a uniquely legendary talent, but they've certainly not been complacent about it.

Is *Ransom Canyon* season 2 more of the same? Essentially, yes. Is that a bad thing? No, that's exactly what we want. If you overlook the cowboying, you'll probably fall in love with each and every character as much as I did... and soap opera historically aren't my thing.
