The war in Iran appeared to be spreading on Wednesday after the Iran-backed Houthi rebels bombed two Saudi oil tankers off the coast of Saudi Arabia, threatening to further disrupt oil supply.

The Houthis claimed an attack on two vessels 70 nautical miles southwest of the portof Al Shuqaiq, saying they had violated a maritime blockade.

The incident had earlier been reported by the United Kingdom Maritime Trade Operations (UKMTO), who said crews were attempting to extinguish a fire onboard. No casualties or environmental impact have been reported.

The Houthis have recently threatened to attack ships transiting through the Bab al-Mandab Strait, the Red Sea’s southern access point.

It came hours after Iran’s foreign minister warned of an “eye for an eye” response if Donald Trump continues with his threat to bomb a bridge or power plant for every ship targeted in the Strait of Hormuz.

In a post on X, Abbas Araghchi said: “Our defence doctrine is clear: eye for an eye. Any aggression against Iran, including our infrastructure, will compel a powerful and decisive response.”

## Saudi state media confirms attack on Red Sea vessel

Saudi state media has confirmed that a Saudi-owned vessel came under attack while sailing in the Red Sea.

According to the Saudi Press Agency (SPA), which cited an official from the General Transport Authority, the incident caused a fire at the front of the ship.

The official said all crew members aboard the *Encelia* were safe and that authorities had taken steps to secure the vessel, protect those on board and prevent environmental damage.

The statement followed claims by Yemen’s Iran-backed Houthi group that it had launched missile and drone attacks on two Saudi oil tankers in the Red Sea on Wednesday.

The Houthis identified one of the ships as the *Encelia*, saying the strikes were part of what they described as a maritime blockade on Saudi Arabia linked to the US-Iran conflict. 

## House Democrat criticizes newly adopted Iran war funding proposal: 'Focus on investment here at home'

US Representative Ro Khanna, a California Democrat, has criticized a budget proposal adopted by the Republican-led House Wednesday, which includes funding for the Iran war.

“This money could fund free public college and free childcare for every American while expanding health care coverage for seniors”, Khanna wrote on X. “It’s time to end the war and focus on investment here at home”.

The $95 billion package, which was adopted in a 216-214 vote, includes $60 billion for the Pentagon and $13 billion for other national security issues, $12 billion in aid for farmers and $10 billion to help implement changes in voting laws, according to the Associated Press.

“Vietnam War: 19 years, 58,220 DEAD. Korean War: 3 years, 36,574 DEAD. Iraq War: 9 years, 4,600 DEAD. Afghanistan War: 20 years, 2,000 DEAD”, it stated.

## Trump claims without proof that soldiers killed in Iran war shared his view that Tehran can’t have a nuclear weapon

Donald Trump has claimed that US service members killed in the Iran war had shared his view that Iran cannot be allowed to develop a nuclear weapon.

The US president made the remarks Wednesday — seemingly without proof — before heading to Dover Air Force Base to attend the dignified transfer of four soldiers who died in the Middle East conflict over the past week.

“We’re going to Dover…to honor our heroes, and they are indeed great heroes, actually”, Trump told reporters while standing in front of Marine One.

“They said, and all of them said very strongly, ‘We can’t let, we cannot let Iran have a nuclear weapon. They won’t have a nuclear weapon’”, the president continued. “So, we’re going to honor them, and one of the hardest things to do — for me, it’s one of the hardest things to do as a president — but has to be done”.
