# Burnham declares era of Whitehall resisting devolution ‘over for good’

- Andy Burnham has opened a new No 10 North office in Manchester, describing it as the "biggest rebalancing of power" the UK has seen.
- The Prime Minister will work from the Manchester outpost, located in Heron House, once a week during his premiership.
- His initial engagement involved chairing a National Economic Council meeting, which will include regional mayors alongside senior ministers.
- Burnham stated that regional mayors will play a key role in UK policymaking, declaring the era of Whitehall resisting devolution "over for good".
- The initiative aims to accelerate devolution, drive reindustrialisation and growth across the UK and shift power away from Westminster to local levels.
