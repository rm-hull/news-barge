A RESIDENTAL care service has been rated as ‘good’ following a visit from a watchdog.

In a report published by the Care Quality Commission (CQC), Holgate House in Holgate Road, York was rated 'good' - marked progress from their last assessment in 2023 where deemed the home 'requires improvement.'

The Milewood run home is a specialist residential care home supporting adults with learning disabilities and mental health conditions, which provides care for 18 residents - both over and under 65 years of age.

  


The report highlighted that “care was kind, respectful and person-centred, and people told us they were treated with dignity and as individuals.”

Inspectors noted an inclusive atmosphere, saying: “There was an inclusive and welcoming approach at the service to ensure everyone felt valued and differences were embraced”

They added that found that residents felt safe living at the service, supported by a consistent staff team who knew them well and responded promptly to their needs.

The leadership at Holgate House received praise, with the report adding: “leaders and the culture they created promoted high-quality, person-centred care.”

It also highlighted the improvements in leadership, with positive feedback from residents, staff, and professionals regarding the culture of the service and 'strengthened governance systems aimed at supporting ongoing improvement'.

Jo Rowntree, the registered manager at Holgate House, said that they were very proud of the rating and added that it shows their progress whilst giving them a strong foundation for improvement.

She said: “Achieving an overall ‘good’ rating is a testament to the hard work and commitment of the team.

"It reflects the compassion, professionalism and dedication our team shows every day to ensure people feel safe, respected and valued.

“We have worked hard to create an environment where people are supported to make their own choices, maintain their independence and enjoy fulfilling lives.”

Martyn Heginbotham, chief executive of Milewood, described the rating as an important milestone, saying, “This demonstrates what can be achieved through strong leadership, committed colleagues and a clear focus on continuous improvement.”

He thanked the team for their hard work and commitment, promising continued support as they build on this success.

While the report highlighted many positive aspects, it also noted areas for improvement; some systems, including care planning and risk assessment, needed further development to ensure support was "consistently personalised and evidence-based".

On this, the report said: "Leaders had recognised these shortfalls and had begun to make improvements, but these were not yet fully embedded."

It also added that the environment, while safe, was described as 'worn' in places - however it stated leaders are aware of this and there are plans in place for refurbishment.

Overall, the report indicated that people had a largely positive experience at Holgate House, feeling safe and well cared for; relatives reported that staff were kind and responsive, with one relative stating, “[Relative] is safe there… they look after [them] and make sure [they’re] eating.”

The inspection concluded that Holgate House is "on a positive trajectory", with a strong foundation for continued improvement.
