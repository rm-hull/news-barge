A YORKSHIRE restaurant has been named among the best of the best by top food critics – around four months after first opening its doors.

Inlamentation – a new fine dining restaurant run by reowned chef Michael O'Hare, who is known for his appearance on the Great British Menu and for creating innovative and creative dishes – has been commended in the Michelin Guide this month.

The 16-seat restaurant, which is located in Boston Spa, is designed to be ‘both immersive and sincere’ offering a tasting menu where ‘every detail serves the experience of cooking and eating at the highest level’.

The restaurants website says: “Menus are not published online, as they are ever-evolving—and part of the experience is preserving an element of surprise for our guests.”

In their listing, the Michelin inspectors dubbed the small restaurant as a ‘a platform for Chef Michael O’Hare to express his personality and signature cooking style’.

They added: “There are just six tables and four coveted kitchen counter seats in the understatedly modern dining room where white-topped tables contrast with black leather chairs.


“Creativity and complexity abound across the constantly changing menu of around a dozen small servings, sometimes incorporating Asian influences such as Peking-style roasted duck.

“Thoughtfully created wine flights complement the food perfectly.”
