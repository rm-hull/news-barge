Duda, a dedicated website-building platform for agencies, has rolled out new AI tools that aim to combine the speed and ease of AI website building with the enterprise-grade security, predictability, and customization requirements of professional agencies.

In a press release shared with TechRadar Pro, Duda shared details on the new tools and features that include vibe coding, a custom widget editor, and brief-driven site building, adding to Duda’s existing suite of AI tools.

## Connecting agencies to AI capabilities

This most recent update includes both new tools and updates to already established features.

- **Duda Vibe**(new feature): A conversational web application editor that allows agencies to ‘vibe-code’ web tools and dynamic web applications with plain-English prompts.
- **In-editor custom widget generator**(new feature): This feature was previously a separate developer tool; the update brings it into the core website editor. Users can now prompt AI to generate drag-and-drop elements.
- **Brief-driven site building**(expanded features): Duda has expanded the capabilities of this tool, allowing users to upload media files, client briefs, and call transcripts. AI then digests the provided information and uses it to create a multi-page website.

Duda’s update further cements its position as a leading website builder for agencies. Although platforms like Wix and Squarespace also offer AI website building capabilities, these typically target DIY website builders, failing to address the processes and levels of complexity required by website agencies.

“Small businesses still need a strong digital presence, but the digital landscape is becoming more complex, not less,” said Itai Sadan, CEO and co-founder of Duda. “AI makes it easier to create a starting point, but businesses will continue to rely on agencies to deliver secure, high-performing websites that are easily found by LLMs and drive results. Our role is to give the professionals building tens, hundreds, or thousands of sites the AI-powered platform they need to do it faster, more predictably, at higher quality, and profitably—all at scale.”
