A ROAD in York city centre has been forced to close to motorists.

The entrance of George Street, off Walmgate, close to The Walmgate Sandwich Bar and Cafe and Tabanco Ambiente, has been closed for works, with an area of road taken up by diggers on Thursday (July 23).

The works are being undertaken by Yorkshire Water.

  


It comes after Walmgate was closed for works on February 8 for works following a burst pipe which had continually spilled in the street for six days in January.

  The works have opened up a section of the road, which has been cordoned off by Yorkshire Water 

*(Image: Newsquest)*

Speaking to The Press on January 14, a representative for Yorkshire Water said: "Periods of below zero temperatures followed by much warmer weather can often lead to an increase in leaks as frozen ground shifts when it thaws, causing pipes to move and potentially fracture.

"We will be replacing just under 9 kilometres of mains in York by April 2027, with more to come by April 2030, as part of our £406m mains replacement projects across Yorkshire."

The footpath remains open in George Street.

The Press has approached Yorkshire Water for further comment.
