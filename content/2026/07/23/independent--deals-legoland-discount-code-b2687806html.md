## Legoland FAQs

### Are Legoland Windsor tickets cheaper online?

Yes, booking in advance saves you more than 50 per cent compared with buying tickets on the day. Online bookings start from £32, whereas you’ll be paying upwards of £68 when you get there.

**Do children get free entry to Legoland?**

Yes, children under 90cm tall get free entry to Legoland. If you’re staying overnight, those aged less than three years old get free accommodation and eat for free in hotel restaurants.

**Does Legoland offer NHS discounts?**

Keyworkers, including those in the NHS and armed forces, can enjoy discounts and offers on Legoland Windsor tickets through the Blue Light Card website. All you need to do is verify your eligibility through the portal to see the available offers.

### How can I get 2-for-1 Legoland tickets?

Legoland is currently running an offer that enables you to get two theme park tickets for the price of one. This offer is running until 27 July, and both visits must take place by 25 September 2026. Choose from a free visit to Chessington World of Adventures, Alton Towers, Thorpe Park or a revisit to Legoland.

### Can I buy cheap Legoland tickets with Tesco vouchers?

Yes, you can exchange your Tesco Clubcard points for Legoland vouchers, with every 50p in points worth £1 towards tickets. To redeem, go to the Tesco app, open the “Clubcard” section, and search for “Legoland” under reward partners. Convert your points into vouchers at checkout, and you’ll receive a reward partner code to use when purchasing your tickets.
