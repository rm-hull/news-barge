A new exhibition will celebrate one of Britain’s most influential artists – who was born in York.

York Art Gallery has announced the exhibit inspired by John Flaxman, whose sculptures and drawings were world-renowned, opening on September 18.

Dr Beatrice Bertram, senior curator at York Art Gallery, said there is “no more fitting place to tell Flaxman’s story than in his home city”.

Turner, Wedgwood & Beyond: Inspired by Flaxman at the gallery will mark the 200th anniversary of Flaxman’s death.

  York City Art Gallery Technician Terry Jones in 2001 with a pastel picture of York artist John Flaxman, by Archibald Skirving 

*(Image: Nigel Holland)*

Renowned as a draughtsman, designer and sculptor, his work had a profound influence on his contemporary artists including J.M.W Turner, William Blake, Jean-Auguste-Dominique Ingres, and fellow York-born artist William Etty, whose statue is outside the art gallery in Exhibition Square.

York Museums trust, which runs York Art Gallery, said Flaxman’s celebrated designs for the potter Josiah Wedgwood helped shape some of the most recognisable works of British ceramic art.

Flaxman’s published outline illustrations to Homer and Dante, along with other ancient poets, secured him an international reputation and inspired artists across Europe.

In York, more than 70 loans from leading museums and galleries across the UK will be on show, along with works from the gallery’s own collection.

  Joseph Mallord William Turner, 1775–1851. Ulysses deriding Polyphemus - Homer's Odyssey, 1829 

*(Image: The National Gallery Photographic Department)*

Among the exhibition’s highlights is J.M.W Turner's striking 'Ulysses deriding Polyphemus – Homer's Odyssey', from the National Gallery's collection.

Other key works include Turner’s 'The Fall of Anarchy', William Blake's 'Beatrice Addressing Dante from the Car', both on loan from Tate, and Jean-Auguste-Dominique Ingres’s ‘Paolo and Francesca’, lent by the Barber Institute of Fine Art.

Dr Bertram said Flaxman was “one of Britain’s most celebrated artists” during his lifetime, but “modern audiences tend to know Flaxman’s legacy better than they know Flaxman himself”.

“Today his sculptures, monuments, illustrations, and Wedgwood designs are often encountered without recognising the remarkable artist behind them,” she said. “Flaxman’s ideas influenced the work of his contemporaries, such as Turner, Blake and York’s own William Etty, and continued to inspire artists across Britain and Europe long after his death.

“Our exhibition explores these connections, inviting visitors to discover an artist whose imagination helped shape iconic works of art.”

“The phenomenal range of loans we have generously received from collections across the UK enables us to explore the breadth of his artistic practice,” she added. “And we are thrilled to be working with the University of York on this show.”

Co-curator of the exhibition, Dr Richard Johns from the University of York, said seeing Flaxman’s work alongside Turner, Wedgwood and “a host of other great artists is a revelation”.

  *Turner, Wedgwood & Beyond: Inspired by Flaxman runs from September 18 until January 17 and is included in general admission to York Art Gallery.*
