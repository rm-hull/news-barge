## Popular York-based milk brand partners with Yorkshire Air Ambulance

Yorkshire Air Ambulance (YAA) has launched a new partnership with St Helen’s Farm *(Image: Yorkshire Air Ambulance)*

Shoppers across York are being encouraged to help save lives with each carton of goat’s milk.

Yorkshire Air Ambulance (YAA) has launched a new partnership with St Helen’s Farm, a Yorkshire-based goat dairy producer, as part of a nationwide fundraising campaign.

St Helen’s Farm goat’s milk cartons now feature a Yorkshire Air Ambulance QR code, allowing shoppers to donate by simply scanning the pack.

Angus Wielkopolski, who founded St Helen’s Farm alongside his wife Kathleen, said: "Supporting local communities has always been important to us, and Yorkshire Air Ambulance is a charity that makes a real difference to people across the county every single day."

The on-pack donation campaign runs for 12 weeks from July 12, with all donations matched by St Helen’s Farm up to £10,000.

St Helen’s Farm has also pledged £10,000 as a Gold Sponsor of Yorkshire Air Ambulance’s Big Air Base Build appeal, bringing the partnership's potential value to £30,000.

Helen Berriman, corporate fundraising manager at Yorkshire Air Ambulance, said: "By bringing Yorkshire Air Ambulance into thousands of homes through cartons of goat's milk, St Helen’s Farm is helping introduce our Charity to new audiences, while making it incredibly easy for customers to support our work."
