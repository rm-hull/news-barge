## Poets of all ages invited to submit verses for East Yorkshire festival

Children, care home residents and budding poets of all ages are being invited to submit their verses *(Image: East Riding of Yorkshire Council)*

Children, care home residents and budding poets of all ages are being invited to submit their verses.

The call comes from organisers of the High Wolds Poetry Festival, taking place in Bishop Burton on Saturday, September 26 and aiming to inspire creativity across all generations.

Sarah Mills, festival director, said: "We’re delighted to support residents of all ages to engage with poetry, no matter their experience.

"We highly encourage everyone to find out more and get involved."

As part of the festival’s outreach, Ms Mills and Caroline Coath, community engagement manager at East Riding of Yorkshire Council, led an interactive poetry workshop for 90 pupils in Years 3 and 4 at Nafferton Primary School.

The festival has already received submissions from across the age spectrum, including a handwritten entry from a self-proclaimed octogenarian.

Councillor Nick Coultish, East Riding of Yorkshire Council Cabinet Member for Culture, Leisure and Tourism, said: “It’s fantastic to see residents of all ages get involved in this unique Festival, which is a landmark cultural event for our region. It’s great to bring poetry to the heart of our communities, and we encourage residents to get involved.”

In addition to public submissions, organisers plan to run a workshop for care home residents in Stamford Bridge, drawing on the area’s historical links to the Bayeux Tapestry.

The festival theme is Weaving Words, Harnessing History, in recognition of the tapestry’s ties to East Yorkshire and its 10-month installation at London’s British Museum.

Entries are welcome on any topic, regardless of experience.
