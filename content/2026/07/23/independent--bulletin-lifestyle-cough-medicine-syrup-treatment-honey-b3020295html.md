A review of 14 studies found that honey can be as effective as, or more effective than, many cough medicines in reducing cough frequency and severity.

Most coughs are caused by viral infections and typically resolve on their own, with limited evidence supporting the effectiveness of many over-the-counter cough syrups and lozenges.

While some cough suppressants like dextromethorphan show evidence of efficacy, they can have side effects, and others like pholcodine have been withdrawn due to safety concerns.

Medicated hot drinks primarily offer relief through the warmth and paracetamol for fever, with little evidence for other ingredients like phenylephrine as a decongestant.

It is recommended to consult a GP for any cough lasting longer than four weeks or accompanied by severe symptoms such as difficulty breathing or chest pain.
