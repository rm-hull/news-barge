## 'A vibe that nowhere else in York can match' - award nomination for iconic city bar

Stone Roses Bar has gained iconic status in its lifetime and there are ambitions for a second place in a second city *(Image: Supplied)*

A POPULAR York bar which has developed iconic status is in the running for first prize in a popular competition.

Stone Roses Bar in King Street, co-owned by Gregg, Sharon and Joel Bray, has become a staple of the city’s nightlife for almost 21 years and was recently shortlisted as one of the top 10 pubs in York by readers of The Press.

Joel Bray said: "Recognition like this is exactly what we work so hard for, so it’s fantastic to be acknowledged by the people who matter most – our customers."

The bar, known for its student-friendly drinks menu but with offers across the board - with one standout served more than 200,000 times in the last 12 months - also attracts visitors from across the UK.

Its owners have invested heavily in recent upgrades, including more than £100,000 spent on refurbishing its facilities and adding new artwork throughout the venue.

  Stone Roses Bar co-owner Joel Bray said whilst there is a dedicated student drinks menu, there are plenty of other drinks offers available 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Mr Bray said: "We genuinely believe we offer a vibe that nowhere else in York can match, and in my opinion, that’s one of the biggest reasons we’ve been so successful and are loved by thousands of people from across the UK."

The bar’s upbeat atmosphere, value-for-money drinks, and loyal customer base have helped it earn a 4.7-star rating on Google and a large following on social media, with more than 90,000 followers across platforms, he said.

The team recently celebrated its 20th anniversary with a live performance by The Manchester Anthems, and in 2024, Stone Roses Bar was named Thwaites Pub of the Year.

Mr Bray said: "We’re incredibly proud of the awards we’ve won, including those for investment and innovation."

Joel also praised the bar’s staff.

He said: "None of this would be possible without our amazing team.

"No business can be successful without fantastic staff, and we’re very fortunate to have an incredible group behind the bar who help deliver the experience our customers have come to expect."

  The bar has just had a £100,000 facelift including new artwork 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Looking to the future, he confirmed that a long-term ambition is to open a second bar in another city.

Joel said: "We regularly hear from people who want us to bring the venue to where they live, so expanding has always been part of our vision.

"It’s something we’re constantly exploring, and it’s only a matter of time before Stone Roses Bar opens its second venue.

"We've recently started posting out our merchandise in the UK and further afield too - I've sent t-shirts, hoodies and bucket hats to Australia, Spain and the USA."

  *Voting slips for The York Press Best Pub 2026 are in copies of the newspaper up to and including Saturday, August 5 and must be posted off to the address provided to count.*

  *The winner will be announced shortly afterwards.*
