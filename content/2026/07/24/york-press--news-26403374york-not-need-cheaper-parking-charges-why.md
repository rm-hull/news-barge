  **'York does not need cheaper parking charges - here's why'**

WITH regard to the call for cheaper parking in our city: we do not need more cars in York.

The pollution caused by them is bad for our health and obstructs essential services and public transport.

The fall in sales is not due to parking fees. Even as a cyclist I am less likely to pop into shops than when I walk through the centre and this must be even more the case for those in cars.

Is the fall in sales not due to other causes – economic uncertainty, higher energy costs, extra bureaucracy for EU citizens entering our country?

If our city centre were even more pleasant to walk in without exhaust fumes, noise from motorised vehicles, more reliable public transport and more green spaces this would attract visitors and residents more than lower parking fees.

  *Rose Berl,*

  *De Little Court,*

  *Vine Street,*

  *York*

  *How the new PM is helping communities*

CONTINUING his drive to build an economy that works for every postcode, the Prime Minister has taken action to cut business rates bills for pubs, social clubs and live music venues across Yorkshire and the Humber from April next year.

For too long, the pubs, social clubs, and live music venues that form the backbone of local high streets have been replaced by boarded up windows and for sale signs.

The announcement forms part of the Labour Government’s wider plan to drive good growth in every postcode by reducing the burden on local businesses, boosting investment on high streets and helping communities to thrive.

Nationally, new business rate cuts will benefit nearly 32,000 pubs, clubs and live music venues, saving the typical pub an estimated £1,100 in the next financial year, providing much-needed certainty for businesses looking to invest, grow and create jobs.

Today’s changes will be fully funded, including through reviewing reliefs for businesses that do not make a positive contribution to local communities, such as vape shops.

The Government will also crack down on businesses that sell through online marketplaces but do not comply with their tax obligations, putting them at an unfair advantage over businesses that play by the rules.

The Government is consulting on measures to make online marketplaces more responsible for preventing non-compliant sellers from avoiding their tax obligations and further detail will be set out in due course. Revenue raised from these reforms will be reinvested in improvements to the business rates system.

  **Some overnight showers would make summer 'perfect'**

AFTER the latest extremely hot dry period of 30 degrees celcius plus, with extremely hot humid uncomfortable nights for sleeping, it's nice to back to the normal weather that we're more used to.

Cooler nights for a more comfortable sleep, a gentle breeze and pleasant mid 20 degrees afternoon.

The only thing missing to make it perfect summer weather is a few heavy showers of rain (preferably overnight) to freshen the air, water the earth, refill the reservoirs and refresh the river.

That's what I call a perfect English summer without the extreme weather we've had of late.
