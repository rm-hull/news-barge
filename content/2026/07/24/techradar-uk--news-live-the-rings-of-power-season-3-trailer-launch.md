Get ready, *The Lord of the Rings* fans, because the first trailer for*The Rings of Power* season 3 is almost here.

Set to be released today (Friday, July 26) following its premiere at San Diego Comic-Con (SDCC) 2026, the teaser will give us our first proper look at the Amazon TV Original's next season, which is set to make its Prime Video debut in mid-November.

So, grab some Lembas bread and join TechRadar's senior entertainment reporter Tom Power as he counts down towards the trailer's grand reveal. The Dark Lord Sauron demands it...

## New season, new faces

 ![A collage image of Eddie Marsan and Jamie Campbell Bower posing at different read carpet events](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/fFZSxPxcUEXcQbpjPgy9UG.jpg) 


Leaping ahead years into the future isn't necessarily a bad thing. In fact, one positive it can have is allowing for the introduction of characters we wouldn't otherwise see until much later in the story.

We've already received confirmation of five new names who've joined next season's cast, too. Last February, *Stranger Things* alumnus Jamie Campbell-Bower and* Heartstopper*'s Eddie Marsan were added to* The Rings of Power 3*'s ensemble. Four months later, three fresh faces in Andrew Richardson, Zubin Varla, and Adam Young also joined* The Rings of Power* season 3.

At the time of their hirings, Amazon declined to say who they'd be playing. Thanks to Empire's recent cover feature, though, we now know the identity of Campbell-Bower and Varla's individuals...

## Time jump!

 ![Galadriel looking ahead of her with Nenya back on her hand in The Rings of Power season 2 episode 8](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/CL7K5pkSMoinU9j5d6yWwE.jpg) 


So, why we won't see the War of the Elves and Sauron from the very beginning? Because season 3 takes a giant leap forward in time.

Per this season's official story synopsis, it'll jump forward "several years from the events of season 2" and "takes place at the height of The War of the Elves and Sauron, as the Dark Lord seeks to craft the One Ring that will give him the edge he needs to win the war and all of Middle-earth at last."

What does that mean for what we'll actually see? Hopefully, season 3 will dispense with any mundane parts of the conflict and depict its most exciting albeit deadly encounters, including the Siege of Rivendell and pivotal Battle of Gwathló.

## The Last Homely House East of the Sea

 ![Elrond, Gil-galad, Galadriel, and Arondir standing on a cliff top looking at their elf forces in The Rings of Power season 2 finale](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/L5CJcRBgQVDPc8qAn8WLgh.jpg) 


Sauron's desire to make the titular, all-powerful, and manipulative piece of jewelry will stem from those who continue to resist his aim to achieve world domination.

Indeed, last season's finale all but confirmed that its follow-up would depict the War of the Elves and Sauron, aka one of the longest-running and bloodiest conflicts of Middle-earth's Second Age. And, while we won't see how this war plays out from the get-go — more on why shortly — we're bound to witness the rise of Rivendell.

Season 3 isn't the first time we've seen the famous Elven city, mind you — or, at the very least, that land upon which it'll be built. Towards the end of season 2 episode 8, the elves who fled Eregion's destruction regrouped at an area that, thanks to its nearby waterfall, would be very familiar to *Lord of the Rings* diehards. That being, Imladris, otherwise known as Rivendell.

In an exclusive chat with TechRadar in October 2024, Robert Aramayo and Benjamin Walker, who portray Elrond and Gil-galad, refused to confirm if last season's finale represented the founding of Rivendell. However, Walker admitted it was a "very astute observation" on my part, so I took that as confirmation without him actually confirming it.

Regardless, if we don't see Rivendell's formation and/or it being used as a base of operations for the elven crusade against Sauron this season, I'll eat my hat (**NB:** I won't actually, but you don't know that...).

## Forging the One Ring

EXCLUSIVE 🔥 The Rings Of Power Season 3 will finally see Sauron forge the One Ring in Mordor.“No-one is safe,” co-showrunner Patrick McKay tells Empire. “This third season is darker, more dangerous.”Read more: [https://t.co/5tYakApFOD](https://t.co/5tYakApFOD) pic.twitter.com/WMLHW9spcHJuly 20, 2026


One thing that's been clear since last season ended was that the show's next installment would finally show us how the One Ring was forged.

Speaking exclusively to TechRadar in October 2024, Charlie Vickers, who plays Sauron, said the One Ring's creation was "the next piece of the puzzle" where*The Lord of the Rings* franchise's infamous villain was concerned.

At the time, Vickers had no idea if he'd get to forge it in the fires of Mount Doom in season 3. However, in a recent cover piece for Empire magazine, Vickers and the show's co-creators J.D. Payne and Patrick McKay confirmed season 3 would include one of the most significant moments in Middle-earth history.

Per Vickers, it took two weeks to shoot the entirety of the Ring's creation, with McKay also saying it was "the single most ambitious, technically challenging sequence we’ve ever done on the show". Big words, indeed — let's hope the pay-off is worth it!

## Ringin' in the release date

One ring to rule them all. The Lord of The Rings: The Rings of Power is coming to San Diego Comic-Con. New Teaser this Friday. pic.twitter.com/Xk0k1zXKdOJuly 21, 2026


We might not have a trailer — yet — for season 3, but we do have an official launch date: **November 11, 2026**. Amazon confirmed* The Rings of Power* season 3's late 2026 arrival in mid-May, so we've been counting down the days to its return for over two months at this point.

And, honestly? I'm glad it'll be here before the end of the year. Filming only wrapped on *The Rings of Power 3* in December 2025 and, given how the lengthy cycle of its predecessors' post-production stages, I fully expected the forthcoming season's post-production phase to not only be similarly long, but also mean it wouldn't arrive until early 2027. Thank God I was wrong!

Let's get the big question out of the way first: what time should we expect this season's first trailer to drop?

Well, we know it'll be sometime today — aka Friday, July 26 — but its actual launch time hasn't been announced.

That said, an Amazon MGM Studios SDCC 2026 presentation will tell us more about *The Rings of Power 3* and another of its forthcoming TV shows in*Blade Runner 2099*. That panel kicks off at 10am PT / 1pm ET / 6pm BST, and is expected to last for 90 minutes, so the trailer will be with us by 11:30am PT / 2:30pm ET / 7:30pm BST at the very latest.
