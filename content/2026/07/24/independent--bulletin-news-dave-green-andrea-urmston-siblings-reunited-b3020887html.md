Dave Green, adopted as a baby, was reunited with his birth family after discovering he had unknowingly worked with his long-lost sister, Andrea Urmston, for two years.

As reported by the BBC, Mr Green, a bus station supervisor, and Ms Urmston, who serviced vending machines, developed a friendship at Manchester Airport.

The siblings realised they shared the same mother after a conversation about fostering in 2007, when Mr Green mentioned his adoption and Ms Urmston spoke of her brother who had been put up for adoption.

Mr Green later met his birth mother, who was 15 when she gave birth to him, discovering he had regularly driven past her Altrincham home as a bus driver.

Despite growing up separately, the siblings found they shared similar tastes, including how they take their tea and a love for “tomato sauce butties”.
