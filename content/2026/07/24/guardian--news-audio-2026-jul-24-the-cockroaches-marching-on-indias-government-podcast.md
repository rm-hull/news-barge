‘I’ve never seen this many young people out on the streets,’ says **Hannah Ellis-Petersen**.

For days, tens of thousands of Indians have been marching under the banner of the Cockroach Janta party.

The Guardian’s south Asia correspondent tells **Helen Pidd** the protesters are reclaiming a slur after India’s chief justice reportedly referred to unemployed young people as ‘cockroaches’ and ‘parasites’. Their anger erupted after leaks of exam papers forced millions of students to resit tests. The scandals have also been linked to dozens of suicides in recent years.

Since then, the movement has gathered momentum, with protests against corruption and unemployment, and demands, at the very least, for the resignation of the education minister.

It is perhaps the biggest challenge to Narendra Modi’s government in years. But will this week’s concessions be enough to keep the Cockroach movement off the streets?
