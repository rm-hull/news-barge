Search the Public Notice PortalA stretch of High Lane is set to be shut for patching works at the end of the month.

The closure will affect the section between the junctions of Main Street and Sandhill Road on July 31.

While the legal order allows for the closure to remain in effect for up to 18 months, the council expects the work to be completed within a day.

The closure is to ensure public safety while the work is carried out.

An alternative route will be signposted, directing traffic via Back Road, Wilberfoss Road, York Road, Hull Road, Sutton Lane, The Street and Sandhill Lane.

The diversion will take motorists through Newton on Derwent, Allerthorpe, Sutton upon Derwent, Barmby Moor, Thornton, Wilberfoss, the Pocklington Provincial Ward, and the Wolds Weighton Ward.

Access will be maintained for emergency vehicles.

Residents and businesses along the affected route are advised to plan accordingly.

Anyone with questions about the works can contact East Riding of Yorkshire Council’s Highways Customer Care team on 01482 393939, quoting reference RJH.

The full public notice has been published on the council’s website and can be viewed under the traffic and travel section.

This latest patching work forms part of an ongoing programme of maintenance by the council.

The scheme aims to keep roads in a safe condition.
