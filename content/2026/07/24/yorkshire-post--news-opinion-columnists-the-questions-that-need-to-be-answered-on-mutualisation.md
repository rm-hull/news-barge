# The questions that need to be answered on mutualisation of water companies

## The anger that the public feels towards water companies, as a result of their catalogue of failures over recent years, is evident. There has been a clamour to get the water industry back into public hands, after communities were left with sewage on their beaches, in their rivers and lakes. All the while facing ever-increasing bills for the privilege of such poor performance.

This is illustrated by the research from the Good Growth Foundation (GGF), which found that only 10 per cent of adults in Yorkshire want water companies to remain owned by private shareholders.

But the problem with nationalisation is the cost to the taxpayer. The same report found that over half, 55 per cent, would not sign off on a costly state buyout, while less than one in five, 18 per cent, say public ownership would be worth it whatever the cost.

One other way of taking back control of the water industry that is being mooted is converting water companies into not-for-profit cooperatives, owned by customers and run for their benefit. It is an idea that has the backing of Yorkshire’s Mayors.

![A household water tap. PIC: Rui Vieira/PA Wire](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOmU5MzE4OTE0LWFkMjktNGNhMC1iNzk4LTU4N2MwMjUxY2RjYzo0YjNkYWIyMC00Nzc0LTRjZTEtODc0YS00YjMzZmNlNjM1ZWQ=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



Even this idea comes with challenges. The water industry needs billions of pounds to tackle the issues that have blighted it. It’s the sort of capital that is not easy to come by, whether through private investors or mutual members.

Indeed Yorkshire Water’s CEO Nicola Shaw outlined earlier this year to this newspaper how it was undertaking an £8.3bn investment programme. The unprecedented level of transparency offered by the company must be acknowledged.

More detail is going to be needed on how mutualisation would be able to provide the sustainable financial footing required for water firms to deliver the needed investments.

The fact that the utilities are at the forefront of the new PM’s mind is to be welcomed.
