A popular Channel 4 sitcom featuring a huge Inbetweeners star has been cancelled after just two series.

Everyone Else Burns was first aired in 2023, with its second series broadcast in October 2024.

The show featured Simon Bird, who you might know from The Inbetweeners when he played Will McKenzie.

If you want to watch all of the series’ episodes, you can still stream it on Channel 4.


## Everyone Else Burns cancelled after just two series

Everyone Else Burns will not be returning for a third series, as revealed by TVZone.

The show, which was created and written by Dillon Mapletoft and Oliver Taylor, is a coming-of-age sitcom that follows a family in Manchester who are a part of a puritanical Christian sect.

Simon starred as David Lewis, the father of the family, with a strong passion for keeping his family in line with the scripture of the Order.

He is desperate to become influential in his chapter, which can often push him away from his wife and children.

Kate O’Flynn, who you might recognise from My Lady Jane or Bridget Jones’ Baby, stars as Fiona Lewis, the matriarch, who starts to explore life outside of the Order and realises how her dreams are incompatible with it.

The synopsis for the show reads: "Meet the Lewises: your average, everyday Manchester family who belong to an ultra-religious order.

"Oh, and they reckon the world's going to end in the next 10 years."


The cancellation of Everyone Else Burns might come as a surprise to many fans, as the show has a 90% rating on Rotten Tomatoes from critics.

Nine top critics gave it a fresh rating, with Kate earning a nomination at the BAFTA TV Awards for Female Performance in a Comedy for her role.

Channel 4 has also cancelled another comedy series earlier this year, with Rosie Jones’ show, Pushers, ending after just one season.

Newsquest has approached Channel 4 for comment.
