## York and North Yorkshire MPs respond to the PM's 20% business rates cut for pubs

L-R: Luke Charters, Kevin Hollinrake and Rachael Maskell *(Image: NQ)*

York’s Labour MPs have welcomed Prime Minister Andy Burnham’s announcement of a 20 per cent cut in business rates for pubs, clubs and live music venues.

However, North Yorkshire Tory Kevin Hollinrake says the measures do not go far enough, noting they also don’t come into affect until next April and they follow measures such as higher living wages and National Insurance rates, which have impacted on the sector.

The rates reduction also does not help cafes and restaurants.

But Downing Street says a typical pub should benefit by £1,100 a year. The policy will cost around £100 million and will, the government says, be funded by a review of tax reliefs given to businesses "such as vape shops."

York Central MP Rachael Maskell praised Mr Burnham for starting to “soften the cost of living and business” in his first week.

  Rachael Maskell, Labour MP for York Central 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

  Ms Maskell told The Press: “While he will set out his comprehensive plan over the next few weeks, he is clearly signalling his priorities in cutting bus fares to £2, electricity bills by an average of £45 and now taking 20 per off business rates for bars and music venues*.*

"I really welcome these first steps, but with a promise of complete reform, ensuring his priorities align with the public we can be hopeful for the future.”

The York Central MP continued: “I very much want to involve businesses in York in how Labour bring together an offer that works for them, supporting the ‘VATs the Problem’ campaign, having attended the launch and met with York businesses about, while also working with residents over their priorities, as I will this summer.”

  Luke Charters, Labour MP for York Outer 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

Luke Charters MP called the rates cut “really good news” adding they will help venues survive.

The York Outer MP says he spends “a lot of time in pubs” and says they are “much more than a place to drink” and are a huge part of “what makes our communities what they are.”

Mr Charters says he was recently at the Black Horse Inn in Wigginton chatting with the team about the pressures facing the hospitality sector.

The MP added: “A 20 per cent cut in business rates will give many landlords and venue owners some extra breathing room, helping them invest in their businesses, support local jobs and keep serving their communities.”

However, Thirsk and Malton MP Kevin Hollinrake said: “Any cut is welcome, but £1,100 a year is about £21 a week and pubs won’t see it until next April. This Government has taken far more out of the sector through the jobs tax than it is now handing back."

  Thirsk and Malton Conservative MP Kevin Hollinrake 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

The MP, who is also Chairman of the Conservative Party and last year was declared “Beer Drinker of the Year” by the All-Party Parliamentary Beer Group for his campaigns to help the sector, continued: “In rural North Yorkshire, the pub is often the café, the shop and the community centre rolled into one, yet cafés and restaurants get nothing.”

He added: “Kemi’s Conservatives have already announced the complete abolition of business rates for all pubs, restaurants, hotels and high street shops, up to a maximum of £110,000 per business.

“If Andy Burnham is serious about the great British pub, he should reverse the jobs tax and properly reform business rates, not announce a headline and hope nobody does the detail.”
