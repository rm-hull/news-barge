## Potter helps uncover story behind Pocklington school's clay dragon

A clay dragon with a 29-year-old story has been rediscovered thanks to a recent school visit *(Image: Pocklington CE Infant School)*

A clay dragon with a 29-year-old story has been rediscovered thanks to a recent school visit in Pocklington.

The sculpture, made at Pocklington CE Infant School in 1997, recently caught the attention of visiting potter Lyn from Fangfoss Pottery during a workshop at the school.

Jenny Rogers, headteacher at Pocklington CE Infant School, said: "As soon as I started at the school, I asked the staff if they knew why and when the clay dragon had been created, but no one knew.

"I was really glad when Lyn came to the school and could tell us the story of how it was created."

This term, children took part in a clay workshop led by Lyn, where Year 1 and Year 2 pupils created leaf and minibeast sculptures linked to their art and science curriculum.

Lyn and fellow potter Gerry later glazed and fired the children’s work at their Fangfoss pottery.

The visit was arranged by art leader Callum Hobson and funded by parents and the Friends of Pocklington CE Infant School.

All 135 of the children’s creations will be displayed on a wall outside the school in the new school year, where they will be enjoyed by pupils and families for the next 30 years.
