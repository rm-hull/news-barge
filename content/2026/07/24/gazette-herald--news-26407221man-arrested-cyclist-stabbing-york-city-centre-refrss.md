A MAN has been arrested after a cyclist was 'stabbed' in York city centre.

North Yorkshire Police says a 37-year-old man was arrested last night (Thursday, July 23) and the area around Museum Gardens was cordoned off after a cyclist was allegedly attacked.

The cyclist, who is in his 50s and from Leeds, suffered slash wounds from a carpentry-type knife in Museum Street in York at 8.08pm, police said.

The alleged victim was discharged from hospital after receiving treatment for injuries to his head, face, and hand, the force added.

A 37-year-old man was arrested at 8.17pm – nine minutes after the alleged attack took place, police said.

“The suspect was arrested by taser-trained officers due to the nature of the incident. They did not need to fire the taser and the knife was recovered,” said a spokesperson for the force.

The nearby area, including Museum Gardens, was cordoned off to assist the investigation including a forensic examination of the scene.

The suspect remains in police custody for questioning in relation to an alleged offence of Section 18 wounding with intent to cause grievous bodily harm, the police spokesperson said.

If you witnessed the incident or have any relevant dashcam footage, please contact North Yorkshire Police on 101; or contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or by making an online report.
