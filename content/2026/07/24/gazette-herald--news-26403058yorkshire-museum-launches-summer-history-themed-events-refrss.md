The Yorkshire Museum has revealed its summer 2026 line-up brimming with historical activities.

Visitors can expect a variety of interactive events, including encounters with historical characters such as famous fossil hunter Mary Anning and a Roman.

The events will take place on Tuesdays and Fridays.

Dinosaur-themed backpacks, aimed at primary-aged children, are also making a return.

These are free to borrow and packed with items to entertain children.

Creative activities are set for Wednesdays, inspired by the museum’s latest exhibition, "Chariots, Treasure and Power: Secrets of the Melsonby Hoard."

Visitors can also make Viking-themed beaded jewellery and bug brooches daily, to take home as keepsakes.

York Museum Gardens are joining the fun with fortnightly Thursday outdoor activities, also inspired by the Melsonby Hoard, offering a unique way to engage with the Iron Age collection.

A nature trail will also be available to guide visitors through the green space in the city centre.

All activities are included in the annual passes, which come with every general admission ticket.

The Yorkshire Museum's summer programme presents a blend of fun and education, offering families a chance to delve into different historical periods, from the age of the dinosaurs to the Viking era.
