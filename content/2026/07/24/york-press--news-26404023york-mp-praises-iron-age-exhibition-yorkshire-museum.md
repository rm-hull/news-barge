## York MP hails Britain's largest Iron Age hoard exhibition

Glynn Davis, Andrew Woods and Rachael Maskell with the Melsonby Hoard *(Image: York Museums Trust)*

A major Iron Age exhibition has drawn praise from York’s MP during a recent visit.

Rachael Maskell explored the Yorkshire Museum’s Chariots, Treasure and Power: Secrets of the Melsonby Hoard exhibition, held during the nationwide Festival of Archaeology.

The exhibition centres on the Melsonby Hoard, a collection of more than 800 Iron Age objects discovered in 2021.

Ms Maskell said: "It is incredible that our city museum is holding the largest hoard of Iron Age metalwork to ever be found in Britain.

"This exhibition has highlighted the power of archaeology to tell stories about our past and to widen our imagination to a range of possibilities.

"The exhibits are presented in such an accessible way to appreciate the real value of this find and to reconsider assumptions made of this period of history."

The visit was supported by the Chartered Institute of Archaeologists and the Council for British Archaeology, who organise the annual celebration of archaeology.

Rob Lennox, head of policy and influence at the Chartered Institute for Archaeologists, said: "It is brilliant to get the chance to celebrate what an important asset the Yorkshire Museum is to York with the local MP."

The exhibition will remain on display at the Yorkshire Museum until summer 2027.
