Tuesday evening saw the final event of the York & District Road Race League, the Reverse Handicap Race, which was held at Heslington Sports Village.

The results for the six 10k races were collated, and runners were given a start time based on their average.

The slowest runner set off first and the fastest set off last.

This means, in theory, everyone should finish the 10k route through the Sports Village at the same time.

The first 30 runners to cross the finish line won a T-shirt.

It was therefore, with much excitement, that the 303 competitors arrived at the start line to wait for their start time to be called.

It was a warm evening, with a strong breeze, and a flock of geese joined the supporters on the course.

The first runner started at 7:00 pm and the last started at 7:43 pm.

It wasn’t long before there was some overtaking and competitors could shout encouragement to teammates on the two-lap course.

The first to finish and so the first recipient of a T-shirt was Ste Robson (York Knavesmire) ahead of second place Ian Martin (Vale of York).

Amanda Cooper (Haxby) was third.

After the frenzy of the race, runners enjoyed drinks and supper followed by the series presentations in the lecture theatre.

There was no surprise that the prestigious gold trophy for overall winner went to Dom Carter (York Postal) for the second year in a row, silver went to William Torgerson (City of York), and Robin Stacey (York Acorn) won bronze.

Laura Thompson (City of York), also for the second year in a row, was presented with the gold trophy for first female overall.

Charlotte Bush (York Acorn) won silver, and Rose Mather (York Knavesmire) won bronze.
