Scarborough and Whitby's MP has welcomed news of a 20 per cent cut in business rates for pubs and clubs.

The reduction, which takes effect from next April, will benefit venues in the region, and is expected to save the average pub around £1,100.

Alison Hume, MP for Scarborough and Whitby, said: "Our pubs, clubs and live music venues are at the heart of community life in Scarborough and Whitby.

"They're where people meet and make new friends, celebrate special occasions, enjoy live music and support local jobs.

"These businesses have faced enormous pressures in recent years, so this cut in business rates will make a real difference and provide owners with confidence."

The support package was unveiled by Prime Minister Andy Burnham and will be funded partly through a review of reliefs available to businesses that do not make a positive contribution to local communities.

It also includes measures to tackle tax avoidance by online sellers.

The announcement comes alongside other cost-of-living measures such as VAT relief on electricity bills and the £2 bus fare cap.

Labour has said further reforms to the business rates system will be brought forward as part of a broader plan to support high streets and local economies.
