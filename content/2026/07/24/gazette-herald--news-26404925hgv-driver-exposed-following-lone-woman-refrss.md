A sex offender who followed a woman while performing a sex act on himself is a growing danger to the public, York Magistrates' Court heard.

Nicholas Lawton, 61, was already subject to a community order for having indecent images of children when police got a tip-off he was again accessing sexual images of youngsters online, said Kathryn Walters, prosecuting.

Police arrested him and on one of his phones, they found evidence of Lawton performing a sex act while following the woman and exposing himself while walking in a park and sending a sexual image of a child to an online contact.

He had seven phones in total and refused to give police their PIN numbers so they could examine them in detail.

“These offences were committed while subject to a community order for having indecent images of children,” district judge Mark Daley told Lawton.

“You are demonstrating an escalation of the risk you present to the public. Your offending is not just on the internet, you have followed a lone female while exposing yourself.”

He said there was a risk that Lawton would commit further offences if released on bail.

He rejected a plea by duty solicitor Michelle Stewart that Lawton be granted bail and remanded him in custody.

HGV driver Lawton, of Grange Road, Tadcaster, pleaded guilty to distributing an indecent image of a child on July 16 and exposing himself on July 16.

His case was adjourned for probation officers to prepare a pre-sentence report on Lawton. He will be sentenced on August 12.

Lawton was given the 12-month community order with 150 hours’ unpaid work and 15 days’ rehabilitative activities on April 1 when he was sentenced for having four indecent images of children of the most serious category, six of the middle category and 111 of the least serious category. He had admitted all three offences which were committed in Tadcaster between May 4, 2021, and May 5, 2022.

Ms Stewart said that Lawton had told her shortly before his most recent court appearance that he was himself the victim of abuse when he was a child and that affected his behaviour as an adult.
