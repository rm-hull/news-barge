A total of 15 West Yorkshire police officers were sacked in 2025–26.

The officers were dismissed in 2025–26, according to new Home Office figures, contributing to a record 461 terminations across England and Wales.

This is the highest annual total on record, up from 426 the previous year, and represents the third consecutive year of rising dismissals.

Force-level data shows that nationally West Yorkshire accounted for 3.3 per cent of all officer dismissals in the latest figures.

Nationally, the number of officers leaving the profession has been climbing steadily since 2019–20, when only 164 officers were sacked.

The Metropolitan Police accounted for the largest share of dismissals last year with 168 officers sacked, making up more than one-third of the national total.

Greater Manchester and West Midlands Police recorded 21 and 19 dismissals respectively.

Voluntary departures from the profession have also remained high.

A total of 4,877 officers left policing of their own accord in the year to March 2026, the second-highest total on record and slightly above the previous year’s figure of 4,806.

Fifteen Essex officers were also dismissed, along with 13 from Merseyside, 12 from Humberside, and 11 each from Thames Valley and Surrey.

Chris Philp, shadow home secretary, said: "If people feel less safe today, it’s because Labour have abandoned public safety.

"Labour have slashed budgets, hiked taxes, let out criminals early and left forces with no other choice but to cut officer numbers.

"Today we have seen the result with over 1,800 fewer officers since Labour came to office, and a decline in over 1,500 frontline officers in the most recent year.

"The Conservatives have a fully funded plan to clear up Labour’s mess and deliver 10,000 extra police officers as part of a plan to take back our streets."

A Home Office spokesperson said: "The number of neighbourhood police officers has skyrocketed by 22 per cent in just one year to fight crime, catch criminals and keep communities safe.

"We scrapped the pointless rules which saw forces hire thousands of officers that filled back-office roles, including HR and IT.

"We are investing a record £18.4 billion into our police, putting the right officers in the right places."

As of March 2026, there were 145,886 full-time equivalent police officers in England and Wales – down 0.4 per cent from the previous year, and 1.3 per cent below the recent peak of 147,745 recorded in March 2024.

Most police forces – 34 out of 43 – reported a net increase in officer numbers over the year, but nine saw a decline.

The Metropolitan Police recorded the largest decrease, losing 1,798 officers, a fall of 5.4 per cent.

Ministers have committed to recruiting 13,000 more neighbourhood policing officers by 2029.
