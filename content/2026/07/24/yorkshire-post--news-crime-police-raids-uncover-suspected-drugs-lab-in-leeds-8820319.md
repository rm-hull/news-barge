# Police raids uncover suspected drugs lab in Leeds

## Four arrests have been made after a suspected drugs lab was uncovered following warrants in Leeds, police said.

At around 7am on Tuesday July 21, officers from the Economic Crime Unit carried out a number of warrants at addresses in the Adel and Wortley areas of Leeds and a lab was found on Whitehall Road.

Three men aged 46, 44, 38 and a woman aged 36 have been arrested on suspicion of money laundering and the supply of drugs.

They have been bailed pending further enquiries, police confirmed.

![Four arrests have been made after a suspected drugs lab was uncovered following warrants in Leeds earlier this week](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOjE3NDc0ODRjLThlY2UtNGY4OS04MjY5LWFlNDcxNmE3Y2JlYjozNGE5Y2E5Ny1mMmIzLTRhODMtOWExNS1hNDIwOWYyNGNmZjk=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



Nine firearms were also seized in a second warrant on Farrar Lane.

It’s believed the set-up was producing illicit medication.

Detective Inspector Chris Lord of the Economic Crime team who is the operational lead for the operation, said: “We take a proactive approach to tackling organised criminality and we know the supply of drugs fuels crime and violence in our communities.

“The substances found at the property are not for human consumption and we urge people to avoid buying illicit medication.

“It is advised that the public avoid the current sites while officers gather evidence but continue to report anything they see in their communities that could be suspicious.”
