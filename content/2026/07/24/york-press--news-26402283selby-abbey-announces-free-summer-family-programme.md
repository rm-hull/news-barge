Selby Abbey is offering free family activities every Wednesday this summer.

From July 22 to September 2, the Abbey will host a range of drop-in sessions as part of its £1.4 million Origin Stories project, funded by the National Lottery Heritage Fund.

The activities, which don't require booking, include illuminated letter-making, stained glass craft activities, a bug hunt, and a search for the Abbey's resident swans.

The summer programme will conclude with a Living History Day on September 5, featuring craft stalls, history displays, and guided tours.

Each session will take place from 10.30am to 2.30pm, led by artist Helen Brook and the Abbey's team of volunteers and staff.

The Abbey is also seeking volunteers for the Origin Stories project in roles such as gardening, heritage research, family activities, and project evaluation.

Those interested can email [originsvolunteers@selbyabbey.org.uk.](mailto:originsvolunteers@selbyabbey.org.uk.)

The Abbey's summer programme includes:

- July 29: Medieval Masterpieces: Illuminated Letters
- August 5: Stories in the Windows: Stained Glass Crafts
- August 12: We're Going on a Bug Hunt!
- August 19: Pressed Flower and Herb Collages
- August 26: Treasure Boxes
- September 2: Swanning Around Selby Abbey
- September 5, 10am–3pm: Selby Abbey Living History Day

For more information, visit www.selbyabbey.org.uk/selby-abbey-origin-stories-project.
