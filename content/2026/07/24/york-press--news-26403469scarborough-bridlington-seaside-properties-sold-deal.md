## Agent reports 'strong demand' for East Coast properties following sale of 14

Scarborough is enjoying 'strong demand' for property *(Image: Pic supplied)>*

A portfolio of seaside properties has been sold, amid reports of strong demand in Scarborough and Bridlington.

The 14-property portfolio, made up of former guesthouses, hotels, care homes and a public house, was marketed by Watling Real Estate on behalf of administrators from Grant Thornton.

Alex Sweetman, director at Watling Real Estate’s Leeds office, said: "We had interest from both landlords and developers who were attracted by the opportunities offered by a positive property market in the Yorkshire region where 4.5 per cent annual house price growth was reported in 2025.

"They recognised the potential for refurbishment and renovation leading to rental uplift and asset management opportunities."

The Scarborough sites include the Tennyson Arms on Dean Road, which closed in 2020 and now has the upper floor converted to provide seven self-contained apartments.

Also included is the former St Helens Care Home in the South Bay area, which is pending refurbishment, and Hotel Columbus on Columbus Ravine, a well-regarded detached guesthouse.

In Bridlington, the sale features Vernon Villa on Vernon Road, a former guesthouse with multiple en-suite rooms, and Acomb House on Windsor Crescent awaiting renovation.

The portfolio also includes several other properties offering redevelopment potential, including care homes and large terraces in need of refurbishment.

The transaction follows the appointment of administrators over 11 properties in Bridlington and three in Scarborough.

The portfolio was sold for an undisclosed sum.

Other Bridlington properties in the deal include Blantyre Guest House on Pembroke Terrace, Pentrich House (a former care home on Vernon Road), Spinnaker Hotel, Mont Millais on Trinity Road, Brentwood Hotel on Princess Street, and Park View Hotel on Tennyson Avenue.

Ashford House on Trinity Road and Victoria House on Victoria Road, both former guesthouses, were also part of the portfolio.
