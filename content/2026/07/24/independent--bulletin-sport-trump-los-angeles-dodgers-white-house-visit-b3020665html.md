# Dodgers present Trump with special gift during White House visit

![](https://cdn.jwplayer.com/v2/media/z3BHAsVV/poster.jpg?width=720)

- President Donald Trump hosted the World Series champion Los Angeles Dodgers at the White House, celebrating their second consecutive title in the Rose Garden.
- Trump lauded the Dodgers as "one of the great brands anywhere in the world," specifically recalling their thrilling Game 7 victory against the Toronto Blue Jays.
- He expressed excitement about meeting star player Shohei Ohtani, whom he compared to baseball legend Babe Ruth, and playfully speculated about a third consecutive championship.
- The Dodgers presented Trump with a "Trump 47" jersey and a replica World Series ring, prompting a lighthearted remark about reporting the gift.
- During the event, Trump briefly mentioned being "busy with Iran" and joked about adding Dodgers players to the Rose Garden's "Walk of Fame" instead of presidents.
