Huzzah, another weekend is upon us — and, amid the lack of new movies landing in theaters today (July 24), you might be inclined to stay at home and check one of the following seven must-see films and TV shows instead.

There are a couple of new releases we couldn't squeeze into this week's streaming round-up — sorry, *Star Trek: Strange New Worlds* season 4 and*The Dink*. Nevertheless, I'm sure you'll find something to your liking from those we've listed below. So, take a look, load up one of the best streaming services, and sit back and enjoy one or more of them. —* Tom Power, senior entertainment reporter*

## Stuart Fails to Save the Universe (HBO Max)

![Stuart Fails to Save the Universe | Official Trailer | HBO Max - YouTube](https://img.youtube.com/vi/_blrWb1yZiY/maxresdefault.jpg) 

And what if I said that *The Big Bang Theory* spinoff*Stuart Fails to Save the Universe* was better than the original series? Nobody was expecting awkward comic book store owner Stuart's story to continue from the main show, but in hindsight, it's a glorious move.

Our titular heroic lead, on-off girlfriend Denise, and friends Kripke and Bert are brought back together when Stuart triggers a multiverse armageddon by accidentally breaking a device built by Sheldon and Leonard.

Has 'King of Sitcoms' Chuck Lorre done it again? You'll need to read my *Stuart Fails to Save the Universe* review to find out if it's th TV triumph that I never expected. *— Jasmine Valentine, entertainment reporter*

## Avatar Aang: The Last Airbender (Paramount+)

![Avatar Aang: The Last Airbender | Official Movie Trailer | Paramount+ | Avatar - YouTube](https://img.youtube.com/vi/7b20GRFZBFE/maxresdefault.jpg) 

It's been 13 years since the gang defeated Lord Ozai and the Fire Nation, and attentions have turned to a new airbender called Tagah. Together, Aang and Tagah embark on a global quest to find the relic before a group called The Denied.

Aang is all grown up in *Avatar Aang: The Last Airbender**,* but that doesn't mean the original magic has gone anywhere. The movie's understanding of perspective, movement, and detail is absolutely astonishing, moving through the Four Nations with such awe-inspired wonder that you'll want to live in each frame.

Time may have changed how they look, but their goofy and fun-loving personalities definitely haven't... and the animation captures exactly what has been missing from Netflix's live-action adaptation. *— JV*

## 72 Hours (Netflix)

![72 Hours | Kevin Hart | Official Trailer | Netflix - YouTube](https://img.youtube.com/vi/N-J-HR3quc4/maxresdefault.jpg) 

In this new Netflix movie, Kevin Hart stars as a 40-year-old who ends up at a three-day bachelor party with strangers, resulting in yet another silly Hart-led venture that's perfect if you need of a laugh this weekend.

How did he end up there, you might ask? Well, he was mistakenly added to a group chat and went along for the ride, traveling from New York to Miami to join in the celebrations. Any excuse to party by the beach, I suppose.

It's not so idyllic though, as the trailer teases trouble with the law and other mishaps, including getting horribly stung by a jellyfish. Rather him than me. — *Lucy Buglass, senior entertainment writer*

## Mortal Kombat II (HBO Max)

![Mortal Kombat II | Official Trailer II - YouTube](https://img.youtube.com/vi/b24oG7qCwp4/maxresdefault.jpg) 

Flawless victory! *Mortal Kombat II* is now available to stream on HBO Max, so you can relive the action-packed video game adaptation whenever you'd like.

As far as weekend watches go, you really can't beat an over-the-top, violent thrill ride like this one. If you're a fan of *The Boys*, Karl Urban's portrayal of Johnny Cage has been widely praised, with some noting it's worth watching just for him. —* LB*

## Pompeii: Out of Time with Tom Hiddleston (Hulu/Disney+)

![Pompeii: Out of Time with Tom Hiddleston | Official Trailer | National Geographic - YouTube](https://img.youtube.com/vi/7k52aJRs3gA/maxresdefault.jpg) 

Think you know the complete story of Pompeii? Hold that thought, because Marvel star Tom Hiddleston sheds new light on this cataclysmic event in one of the more poignant docudramas I've seen in a while.

A three-part National Geographic series, *Pompeii: Out of Time* follows Hiddleston as he retraces the steps of a trio of individuals caught up in the natural disaster. Armed with fresh archaeological evidence and backed up by a team of experts, Hiddleston explores how these people not only navigated one of the worst humanitarian crises of the ancient world, but who might also have even survived the deadly events of 79 A.D.

Part educational, part moving drama, *Pompeii: Out of Time* is out now in full on Disney+ and Hulu. —*TP*

## Ransom Canyon season 2 (Netflix)

![Ransom Canyon: Season 2 | Official Trailer | Netflix - YouTube](https://img.youtube.com/vi/2XKyPE92owY/maxresdefault.jpg) 

I don't need all of my television to be profound, life-changing, or even that meaningful. Sometimes, I just want to switch my brain off and pretend I live in a picturesque, rural town like *Ransom Canyon* on the other side of the world where everyone has unrelenting drama except me.

In season 2, the land feud between the Double K and Fuller ranches has become more ferocious, local bar owner Quinn has left for New York despite her on-off relationship with rancher Staten, and mysterious newcomer Yancy has revealed to new love interest Ellie that he has a secret wife.

Season 1 was already a strong outing, but now that the show knows itself a little more, season 2 is easily on par with the strongest seasons of *Virgin River*, the streamer's most iconic "cozy drama" IP.*— JV*

## Masters of the Universe (Prime Video)

![Masters of The Universe – Official Trailer - YouTube](https://img.youtube.com/vi/X21JsHLHnY8/maxresdefault.jpg) 

One of numerous mid-2026 film releases that have bombed at the box office, Amazon has hoisted the proverbial white flag on its live-action *Masters of the Universe* movie's theatrical run. In short: that means you can now watch it on Prime Video.

Based on the Mattel-owned media franchise of the same name, the action fantasy flick tells the tale of Adam, the crown prince of Eternia and owner of an mystical weapon called the Power Sword that, when wielded, turns him into the superhuman known as He-Man. Together with his allies, the previously exiled Adam must realize his potential and overthrow the nefarious Skeletor, who seized control of Adam's home city of Eternos — and its throne — 15 years earlier.

Not sure if you'll like this flick? Read my *Masters of the Universe* review to see what I thought before you stream it. —*TP*
