Hotels in Dunedin, New Zealand, are already fully booked for the total solar eclipse on 22 July 2028, two years in advance.

The city will experience 2 minutes and 51 seconds of darkness at 4:17 pm, marking New Zealand's first total solar eclipse since 1965.

The eclipse path will cross from the Indian Ocean, through central Australia, before reaching New Zealand, with Dunedin at the centre of the line of totality.

About 50,000 visitors are expected to flock to Dunedin for the event, which coincides with Matariki, offering a unique cultural and scientific celebration.

Separately, a significant partial solar eclipse, with around 90 per cent coverage, will be visible across the UK and Ireland on 12 August 2026, the most extensive since 1999.
