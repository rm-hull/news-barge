A young man twice strangled his partner - causing her to black out and fear she was being killed, York Crown Court heard.

The victim was left terrified by Ashton Patrick Head, 23, said Erin Kitson-Parker, prosecuting.

She is now preparing to move house as he knows where she lives, she cannot relax and is scared to go shopping in case she encounters him.

The probation service told Judge Simon Hickey police had arrested Head three times in 2023 and 2024 regarding his conduct with another partner but did not charge him.

“Even though you are young, you seem to have an entrenched attitude towards females and female partners,” the judge told Head.

The most recent partner wrote in her personal statement that “she doesn’t know whether this defendant will ever truly understand the damage he has caused to her,” said Ms Kitson-Parker.

“Every person deserves to feel safe in their own home, their mind, and their life.”

Head, of Selby and no fixed address, pleaded guilty to two charges of strangulation and was jailed for three years.

He was also banned under a 10-year restraining order from contacting his latest partner in any way or through a third person, going within 100 metres of her home or place or work and from posting about her or getting another person to post about her on social media.

Ms Kitson-Parker said the latest partner was older than Head and started a relationship with him in March 2025. They separated on several occasions and during separations Head would call her 40 or 50 times a day.

On May 29 last year, during an argument, he put both hands round her neck and squeezed until she struggled to breathe and blacked out. When she came round, Head was crying and said he was sorry. The incident left marks on her neck.

On February 1 this year, Head put his arm round her neck and choked her until she felt weak from lack of breath. He threw her against a door frame, banging her head.

She thought he was killing her and she blacked out. When she came round, he was leaving the house, and she realised he was not going to change his behaviour.

On March 5, he slapped her on the face and she managed to get away and barricade herself in a bedroom where another person in the house phoned police.

Defence barrister Celine Kart said: “Custody has been a life changing experience for him.” Head has been remanded in prison since his arrest.

In jail, he had had time to realise that his actions had put him where he was and that he needed to change.

He had referred himself to the in-prison alcohol and drug rehabilitation and mental health teams and was being assessed for possible attention deficit and hyperactivity disorder.

His childhood had included domestic violence both as a witness and a victim.
