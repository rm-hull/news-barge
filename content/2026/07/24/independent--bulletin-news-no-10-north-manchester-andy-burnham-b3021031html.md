Overjoyed PM Burnham calls opening of new No 10 North base as 'best day of his life'

Andy Burnham officially opened No 10 North in Manchester, a new headquarters for the centre of government, expressing it was the "best day of my life" and a "full circle" experience for him.

The prime minister is scheduled to work from this new Manchester base weekly, aligning with the government's strategy to decentralise power from Westminster and distribute opportunities across the country.

Mr Burnham articulated his vision for the initiative, aiming to "get Britain hoping again" and cultivate a "one-team feeling" throughout the UK, encompassing England's regions, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland.

The prime minister underscored the importance of No 10 North in fostering "good growth" nationwide, asserting that it will ensure every postcode feels heard and has the potential for development.

Addressing potential detractors, Mr Burnham countered criticisms that the project might be a "gimmick," particularly from London-based commentators, by highlighting its role in creating a more balanced nation where significant progress can originate from the North.
