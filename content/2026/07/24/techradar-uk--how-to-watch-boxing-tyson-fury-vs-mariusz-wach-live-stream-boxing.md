But before the former world heavyweight champion can think about that long-awaited showdown, he must take care of Polish veteran Wach - and it promises to be a unique bout for several reasons.

Fury has always done things his way and this fight is no different. The event was organised with only three weeks' notice and takes place at the Max Muay Thai Stadium in Pattaya, Thailand, which holds just 1,500 people. Ticket profits are set to be donated to local charities as a gesture of thanks from Fury, who is grateful for the way he has been treated by locals while training in the country. Add in the fact that Fury's bout will be the first on the card, rather than the last, and it is set to be a surreal occasion.

While all eyes are on the 37-year-old's meeting with Joshua, this fight is a chance for Fury to gauge his condition ahead of that expected showdown. The Gypsy King marked his return from his latest retirement by beating Arslanbek Makhmudov via unanimous decision at London's Tottenham Hotspur Stadium in April but this is just his second outing since December 2024, when his second defeat by Oleksandr Usyk forced him to take an extended break.

Wach will be a useful test for the Briton, albeit one he should pass. The 46-year-old Pole has lost three of his past four fights, including one against Fury's cousin Hughie, but is a credible opponent that the Gypsy King labelled "a real fighting man". There is some jeopardy, though, as promoters Frank Warren and Eddie Hearn have made it clear that a defeat for either Fury or Joshua in their warm-up bouts - AJ faces Albania's Kristian Prenga in Saudi Arabia on Saturday - would put paid to the Battle of Britain event.

Here are the streaming details for Fury vs Wach wherever you are. We've also got details of the start time, undercard and tale of the tape further down the page.

## Can I watch Fury vs Wach live?

Unfortunately not. Another unusual aspect of Fury vs Wach is that it will not be broadcast live, despite the continued global interest in the Gypsy King ahead of his proposed fight with Joshua.

Footage of the bout is expected to feature in season 3 of *At Home with the Furys*, the fly-on-the-wall Netflix documentary about the boxer and his family. A release date for the new season is yet to be announced, but it is expected to arrive later this year or in early 2027.
