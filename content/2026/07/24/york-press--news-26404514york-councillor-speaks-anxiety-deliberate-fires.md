## Spate of deliberate fires in York a potential 'disaster' putting 'lives at risk'

Councillor Andrew Waller said deliberate fires started near homes in the west of York were particularly worrying *(Image: Kevin Glenton)*

A SPATE of deliberate fires in an area of York is putting lives at risk and could end in disaster, a city councillor has said.

Andrew Waller, Liberal Democrat ward councillor for Westfield met The Press in Hob Moor Nature Reserve, close to Thanet Road in Acomb, where a "suspected arson" over the weekend is being investigated by North Yorkshire Police.

  The Hob Moor blaze of July 19 is being investigated by police as "suspected arson" 

*(Image: Max Downing)*

If follows the force being notified on July 12 of three "deliberate fires" or "suspected arsons" in the space of 90 minutes - one in Foxwood Lane, another where grassland had been set alight in a Thanet Road park, and then reports of youths starting a fire in Bellhouse Way.

On July 19, youths were reported to have emptied a can of petrol over a hedgerow in Kingsway West.

Cllr Waller said deliberate fires are potentially putting lives at risk.

He said: "Clearly there’s a lot of anxiety about the consequences of these fires.

"You're looking at the dangers to firefighters, farmers and others involved in tackling them.

"And then the consequences of fires getting close to houses - like the one in Askham Lane - were particularly worrying for people who live in that sector of the city.

  Wagstaff Farm crew were in the burnt field off Askham Lane the day after the blaze on Sunday, July 12 and Cllr Waller said there was anxiety over the proximity to houses 

*(Image: Supplied)*

"There's also a loss of habitat, wildlife areas, and people are concerned about the cattle here on Hob Moor and wild animals.

"People are concerned about the deliberate nature and it’s all about getting the message home of the consequences."

North Yorkshire Police neighbourhood policing sergeant Ben Ambler said: "Arson is a serious offence that endangers lives and can have a devastating impact on individuals, communities and the environment.

“What may start as a seemingly minor act can quickly escalate into a major incident, causing extensive damage, putting lives at risk and placing significant pressure on emergency services.

"With the current warm and dry weather conditions, even a small fire can spread rapidly and have devastating consequences.

“We would urge parents, carers and young people to be aware of these risks and to have conversations about the very real dangers and potential consequences of setting fires in open spaces.

“We have a problem-solving plan in place to tackle this issue and will continue working closely with our partners to educate young people about the dangers and consequences of deliberate fires as we approach the summer school holidays.”

Cllr Wallter added that "the price we're all paying" for the fires is significant, with resources and funding being taken from elsewhere.

"It’s older people involved in this too, it’s not just kids," he said.

"They’ve got to understand the consequences both to themselves and to the public, where these fires are putting lives at risk."

He added that local schools have been involved with fire service personnel in targeted days to explain the consequences of fires to young pupils.

Cllr Waller said: "With tinder-dry conditions, and the Met Office forecast of more drought conditions for August, we’ve got to get the message through that this is a disaster in many respects if this continues."

North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service station manager Nick Allenby said: "By working with partners, including North Yorkshire Police, we aim to educate children and young adults about the dangers of fire and help them understand the wider consequences of their actions before someone is seriously harmed.

“We are appealing to anyone with parental responsibilities to explain the hazards, risks and potentially devastating consequences of setting fires during the summer holidays.

"If anyone is worried about a young person playing with fire, they can complete a FireSafe referral on the North Yorkshire Fire & Rescue Service’s website."
