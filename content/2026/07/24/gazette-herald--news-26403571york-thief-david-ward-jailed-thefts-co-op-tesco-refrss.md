A shop thief who claimed he couldn’t do his court-ordered punishment because he was on a foreign holiday has been jailed.

A probation officer told York Magistrates' Court that David Michael Ward, 54, had provided evidence of the dates when he was away from York.

But the holiday didn’t cover the dates when he should have attended appointments as part of a 12-week suspended prison sentence with requirements.

It was the third time the probation service had sent him back to court after he was initially sentenced for six shop thefts in York.

As he was taken to the court cells, Ward, of Westfield Place, Acomb, said jailing him would mean he would be homeless when he got out and he would lose the support he was getting in the community.

Ward, of Westfield Place, Acomb, pleaded guilty to breaching a suspended prison sentence order and was jailed for 10 of the 12 weeks previously suspended.

District judge Mark Daley said he took into account that Ward had done some of the requirements of the suspended prison sentence order, but he had prioritised his own activities over the punishment.

Defence solicitor Craig Robertson said Ward could lead a law-abiding life and he did want to work with the probation service on the conditions of the suspended prison sentence.

His sister and her partner had taken him on holiday to a Mediterranean country to give him a taste of normal life.

Ward was originally given a 12-month community order with six months’ drug rehabilitation and 20 days’ rehabilitative activities in January for theft of meat and laundry products worth £106 and meat worth £320.55 on separate dates from Co-op’s Beagle Ridge Drive store in Acomb, meat worth £208.12, meat worth £165.20 and alcohol worth £97.50 on separate dates from the Co-op store in Beckfield Lane, Acomb, alcohol and cleaning products and dog treats worth £158.80 from the Tesco store on Acomb Wood Drive, Woodthorpe.

He admitted breaching the community order on March 13 and on April 30, when the order was changed to the 12-week prison sentence suspended on condition he did six months’ drug rehabilitation and 30 days’ rehabilitative activities.
