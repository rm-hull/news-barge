Competition was fierce at the York Croquet Club as the doubles title was decided by a matter of nine hoops.

Simon Taylor and Chris Atkinson emerged victorious after an exciting day of friendly competition.

The victory marked Mr Taylor's first win of a York Croquet Club trophy.

The third round also saw Mr Taylor hit a memorable "golden hoop", which gave the pair a timely confidence boost.

They maintained this momentum into the fourth round, delivering a commanding 14–0 win that put them firmly in contention.

However, the title was not easily secured.

Four pairs, Helen Griffiths and John Harris, Dave Hudson and John Crompton, Simon Taylor and Chris Atkinson, and Sue Longcroft and Tom Oulton, all finished with three wins each.

It was a close call even then, with Mr Taylor/Mr Atkinson and Mrs Longcroft/Mr Oulton both finishing with three wins and four points each.

The final decision came down to the number of hoops scored throughout the day, with Mr Taylor/Mr Atkinson narrowly beating Mrs Longcroft/Mr Oulton by just nine hoops, finishing with 42 to their opponent's 33.

The annual doubles competition was described as a wonderful day of croquet with close matches, impressive shots, and a lot of fun.

Meanwhile, in the Northern League, York and Belsay ended up sharing the spoils in a hard-fought 2-2 draw on July 19.

The day started on a high note for York, as Richard Lambourne secured a narrow win over Belsay's David Okell, with a final score of 26-22.

The other morning doubles match, however, was a less memorable affair.

Belsay's Steven Pratt and Roger Peters slowly but surely secured a 16-13 win over Mark Simmerson and Mr Oulton in a match that was a test of the players' endurance.

Nonetheless, the afternoon brought a change of pace with Mr Oulton bouncing back from his morning troubles, delivering a commendable 26-4 victory.

The last game of the afternoon was a close-run affair, with both teams scoring well.

However, Belsay narrowly won the match with a score of 22-20, resulting in a draw for the day.

Both teams ended the day sharing points as both contests proved to be evenly matched.

Visitors of all ages are invited to try their hand at croquet in the stunning surroundings of Goddards, Tadcaster Road, on Yorkshire Day.

On August 1, York Croquet Club will be joining forces with the National Trust to offer croquet demonstrations and free "have-a-go" sessions.

Whether you are a newcomer to the game or someone dusting off old skills, members of York Croquet Club will be available to explain the rules, showcase the skills required, and provide help with the first shots.

Croquet is a strategic and precision-based sport, making it entertaining for all ages.

It has a social aspect too, with friendly competition leading to conversations and laughter.

The attractive lawns of Goddards provide an ideal location for Yorkshire Day celebrations.

York Croquet Club is keen to welcome newcomers, offering various opportunities for beginners through to experienced players, including regular club sessions, coaching, and competitions throughout the season.

So, if you're on the hunt for a relaxing, enjoyable way to mark Yorkshire Day, Goddards might just be your perfect spot.

York Croquet Club ensures visitors are welcomed and equipped with everything they need to give croquet a try.

Meanwhile, York Croquet Club is also hosting Croquet-4-Kids sessions every Thursday throughout August from 2pm to 4pm.

For £5 per child, these sessions present an opportunity for children to learn a new sport in a playful, screen-free environment.

The sessions are designed for children to be taught the basics through games and challenges in a fun and relaxed environment.

Qualified club members are ready to offer instruction.

The only requirement is to bring an adventurous spirit, as all necessary equipment and experience will be provided by the club.

There are no prerequisites – children are not required to have any previous experience; all that is needed is a sense of adventure.

The use of colourful hoops and the large club lawns add to the experience.
