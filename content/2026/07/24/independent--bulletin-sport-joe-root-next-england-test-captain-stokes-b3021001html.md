England Test cricket captain Ben Stokes announces his retirement

Joe Root has emerged as the leading candidate to be reappointed as England's Test captain for the upcoming three-match series against Pakistan, which is set to commence on August 19.

The captaincy position became vacant following Ben Stokes' unexpected international retirement and Brendon McCullum's shift to overseeing only the white-ball teams, leaving England without a Test captain or head coach.

Although Harry Brook was considered a viable option, his significant workload leading the ODI and T20 teams makes Root a more likely choice, as the England and Wales Cricket Board seeks to prevent overburdening the all-format star.

Root previously held the England Test captaincy for a record 64 matches between 2017 and 2022, and despite the demanding nature of the role, he appeared receptive to a return after a recent stand-in appearance.

The process to appoint a new Test head coach is anticipated to be lengthy, with Jonathan Trott, Richard Dawson, and Stephen Fleming among the potential candidates, which may result in Marcus Trescothick serving as interim head coach for the Pakistan series.
