Comedian Simon Brodkin is bringing his latest stand-up tour to Yorkshire.

The comic, known for handing Theresa May a P45 and showering Sepp Blatter with cash, will perform at York Barbican as part of his new show Unleashed.

Mr Brodkin said: "I can’t wait for my biggest ever tour.

"The timing feels perfect, I’ve not toured for two years, so I’ve got a lot to say, and my teenage kids no longer find me funny."

Described as "blisteringly funny," he has been called "a prodigious talent" by The Times.

  The UK and Ireland leg of the *Unleashed* tour is currently scheduled to begin in September 2026 and will continue through to March 2027, with the York Barbican show set for Sunday, March 14, 2027.

Mr Brodkin first rose to fame with his popular TV characters Lee Nelson and Jason Bent, and later pivoted to stand-up in 2019.

His previous show, Screwed Up, toured for 170 dates and sold out the Hammersmith Apollo three times.

He also made an appearance at the Royal Variety Performance.

Unleashed promises Brodkin’s trademark "outrageous punchlines and electric crowdwork," taking aim at everything from broken Britain to wild swimmers.

Tickets are available now through the York Barbican website.
