Joe Root has emerged as the frontrunner to be reinstalled as England Test captain for next month’s three-match series against Pakistan.

England are without a Test captain or head coach following Ben Stokes’ sudden international retirement after last month’s 2-1 loss to New Zealand, which led to Brendon McCullum’s role being downsized to overseeing the white-ball teams.

According to *The Athletic*, Root is poised for another captaincy stint and while the it’s understood claims his reappointment is a done deal are wide of the mark, he is now a clear favourite to succeed Stokes.

Harry Brook is the only other viable candidate and he said last month it would be a “great honour” to assume the Test mantle, while Stokes even endorsed his deputy at his farewell media conference.

![Joe Root, left, is favourite to succeed Ben Stokes as England Test captain (Jacob King/PA)](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/24/13/98d19bb785d0c8f3bb40d7eb1208afa8Y29udGVudHNlYXJjaGFwaSwxNzg0OTgyMDY1-2.85126576.jpg)

However, Brook already has a massive workload skippering the ODI and T20 teams, and the England and Wales Cricket Board will be wary of overburdening the 27-year-old all-format star, as well as doing anything to disrupt the progress of the limited-overs sides.

Fellow Yorkshireman Root’s brief could therefore extend to next summer’s Ashes, allowing Brook and McCullum to concentrate on the 2027 50-over World Cup in southern Africa a few months later.

Root led England in a record 64 Tests between 2017 and 2022, logging 27 wins, but he fell to as many losses when standing in for Stokes during the second Test against the Black Caps at the Kia Oval last month.

He spoke about the heavy toll the job took first time around, although he appeared reinvigorated by his one-off return in Kennington and did not rule out presiding over the Test team in the future.

![Marcus Trescothick could be England’s interim head coach against Pakistan (Adam Davy/PA)](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/24/13/24077ffe4b98bec4203cca7a6eb6fca7Y29udGVudHNlYXJjaGFwaSwxNzg0OTgyMTYy-2.81502676.jpg)

With less than four weeks until the first Test against Pakistan on his home ground at Headingley starts on August 19, Root seems set to answer England’s SOS once again.

It was initially thought England would wait for a new head coach to name the new captain but the process to replace McCullum in the Test hotseat is not expected to be completed any time soon.

With Andy Flower last week ruling out a comeback to focus on his T20 franchise commitments, former England batter Jonathan Trott, who has coached Afghanistan, plus homegrown contender Richard Dawson and former New Zealand captain Stephen Fleming are in the frame to replace McCullum.

England are prepared to wait to get the right person in situ, which could lead to Marcus Trescothick, one of the assistants under McCullum, stepping in as interim head coach against Pakistan.

Trescothick, a former England batter and instrumental in their seminal 2005 Ashes success, has filled in for McCullum in the past, most recently during the side’s white-ball tour of the Caribbean in late 2024.
