## York cancer test firm launches life-saving product in the US

Allan Syms of Cizzle Biotechnology *(Image: Pic supplied)*

A UK developer of blood tests for the early detection of cancer has launched a commercial test in the United States.

Cizzle Biotechnology Holdings began offering its CIZ1B Biomarker Blood Test for lung cancer in Dallas-Fort Worth and San Antonio.

This marks the first stage of the company’s US commercialisation strategy and follows its initial commercial readiness programme and targeted sales launch.

The test is designed to support early and less invasive detection of lung cancer.

Allan Syms, executive chairman of Cizzle Biotechnology, said: "With accreditation and initial logistics in place to manage expected demand, BIO is now focused on the marketing and deployment of the CIZ1B biomarker test to enable US nationwide testing coverage.

"The strategic decision to commence full commercial sales in such a populous area of the USA should provide an excellent base to achieve rapid uptake and serve as a model to increase capacity in other areas of the US, Canada and the Caribbean in the short term."

The launch follows successful test validation at OmniHealth Diagnostics in Texas.

Cizzle Bio plans to expand physician access by partnering with additional CLIA-certified laboratories across the US.

The ELISA-based blood test detects the proprietary CIZ1B biomarker, which is strongly associated with early-stage lung cancer.

The technology, supported by more than 30 years of research originating from the University of York, is currently the biomarker of choice in a clinical study at the Moffitt Cancer Centre in the US.

Lung cancer remains the leading cause of cancer-related deaths in the US, with many patients diagnosed only after the disease has progressed.

Low-dose CT screening is currently recommended for high-risk individuals, but uptake remains low.

Under its licensing agreement with Cizzle Bio, Cizzle Biotechnology has granted exclusive commercial rights to the test in the US, Canada, and the Caribbean, generating royalty-bearing revenue opportunities as adoption grows.
