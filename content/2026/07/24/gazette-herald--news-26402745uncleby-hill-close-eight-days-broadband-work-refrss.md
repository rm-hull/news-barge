Search the Public Notice PortalA road in East Yorkshire is set to close for eight days for fibre works.

Uncleby Hill will be shut from 8am on August 18 until 5pm on August 25 for the installation of ducting and chambers for a new fibre network.

The work is being carried out on behalf of Quickline Communications.

East Riding of Yorkshire Council says the closure will be "consistent with ensuring the safety of the public".

While the order for the closure is valid for 18 months, the council anticipates the work will be completed within the eight-day period.

A signed diversion route will guide traffic via Uncleby Road, Howe Lane, Painsthorpe Lane, Roman Road and Uncleby Hill.

The closure will affect all through traffic, although residents, emergency vehicles, and pedestrians will still have access.

The public is advised to contact the council's Highways Customer Care for more details on 01482 393939.
