# Mining billionaire to reward Commonwealth Games medallists with bonuses

![Australia's Ariarne Titmus competes in the women's 400m freestyle heats](https://static.independent.co.uk/2022/08/03/13/AFP_32FW9QP.jpg)

- Mining billionaire Gina Rinehart has established an incentive scheme for Australian swimmers competing at the Commonwealth Games in Glasgow.
- Individual gold medallists can earn up to A$50,000 ($35,000) for individual gold medals won in world record times, or A$20,000 (£10,500) for gold, A$15,000 (£7,880) for silver and A$10,000 (£5,250) for bronze.
- Relay swimmers will earn between A$2,500 (£1,312) and A$5,000 (£2,625) for finishing on the medal podium.
- Rinehart stated the bonuses are a way to thank athletes and show appreciation for bringing national pride.
- Top swimmers like Mollie O'Callaghan and Cameron McEvoy are expected to be major beneficiaries.
