# 'Extraordinary partnership' between Harrogate Theatre and much-loved cafe promises an unforgettable experience

## It may be the quirkiest of ideas but afternoon tea on stage at Harrogate Theatre is proving to be the hot ticket this summer.

Called Afternoon Tea Served Centre Stage, this unique event is a collaboration between the theatre and Harrogate’s much-loved Mama Doreen’s Emporium.

It’s never been tried before but this mouth-watering on-stage dining experience combining live musical theatre performances with a beautifully-crafted afternoon tea from the award-winning, independent cafe is already proving popular.

There is now only limited availability for Afternoon Tea Served Centre Stage on Wednesday, July 29 at 6.30pm, though there may be some tickets left for Monday, July 27.

Originally founded in 2009 by owner Jessica Wyatt and her mother Justine, Mama Doreen’s Emporium grew from a market stall to become a hugely popular café at the top end of James Street, one Harrogate’s most prestigious addresses.

"Harrogate theatre is such an important part of the community and to create something unique that brings people into this space, to experience and enjoy it in a new way, means a great deal to us,” said Jessica.

Guests at these two truly immersive events, will be seated at elegant round tables set directly on the theatre stage, surrounded by theatrical lighting and the atmosphere of one of Harrogate’s most beloved cultural venues.

As they enjoy a traditional Mama Doreen’s afternoon tea, the experience will be accompanied by live musical performances from Singtasia, a popular Yorkshire based company, who will bring the magic of musical theatre and hit movies to life with a selection of well-known show tunes and stage favourites.

Expect tiered stands filled with savoury delights, freshly baked scones and handcrafted sweet treats, served with tea and coffee as ticket holders soak up the ambience of the theatre from a perspective usually reserved for performers.

Natalie Rawel, Head of Marketing and Development at Harrogate Theatre, said: “It’s very exciting to be working on this one-of-a-kind event,

"We love working with Mama Doreen’s.

"They’re a wonderful business to partner with and so loved in the town.”
