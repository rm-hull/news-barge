## York landmarks celebrated in 'thrilling' new art

(From left to right) York Council Holgate ward Labour councillors Lucy Steels-Walshaw and Jenny Kent with Wood Street Walls' Tom Jackson with artwork on a utility box in Holgate, York *(Image: Jenny Kent)*

A PROJECT which saw York children design artworks themed on local landmarks has been celebrated.

The project, which reached around 120 children across five schools, has seen art featuring Holgate’s Severus Hill water tower, windmill, trains and animals, painted on local utility boxes.

Tom Jackson, of Wood Street Walls which ran the project, said it was a privilege to help young people contribute to art which reflects their ideas, creativity and local connections.

Teacher Neil Neil Brookfield and head Lee Haynes said children at their schools absolutely loved taking part and now being able to see their art on display every day.

It comes after Holgate’s Labour ward councillors approached Wood Street Walls and offered to fund the project to paint five City of York Council utility boxes.

Art designed by the children was sprayed on to them in late May before being coated in anti-graffiti resin in June.

Designs include foxes, birds and Viking longships, along with York landmarks such as the Minster and Clifford’s Tower and the names of schools which took part.

Wood Street Walls’ Mr Jackson said it was particularly rewarding to be involved in a project so close to home.

He said: “As someone who has been creating murals across the UK for more than 15 years, I’ve had the opportunity to work with communities, schools and organisations nationwide, but collaborating with Holgate schools in Holgate makes this project especially meaningful.

“It’s a privilege to create something that the wider community can enjoy for years to come.”

  Artwork on a utility box in Holgate, York, created as part of the project 

*(Image: Jenny Kent)*

Acomb Primary School head Mr Haynes said it was great to see children actively engaging with the area where they live.

He said: “The children are thrilled with the results, projects like this are vital to show children how important it is to be part of a community and to care about where they live.”

Poppleton Road Primary School Year 4 teacher Mr Brookfield said artist Mr Jackson was brilliant at getting children to think about local landmarks, plants and animals.

The teacher said: “My class all designed their own work and Tom has done an incredible job at combining and bringing their ideas to life.

“The class are thrilled with the end result which Tom has sprayed onto the electric box virtually opposite our school, we get to see this every day on our way to school.”

  (From left to right) York Council Holgate ward Labour councillors Lucy Steels-Walshaw and Jenny Kent with Wood Street Walls' Tom Jackson with artwork on a utility box in Holgate, York 

*(Image: Jenny Kent)*

Pupil Taylor Smithey said: “I loved how Tom came in and we got to design our own work and how Tom put it all together, it looks amazing.”

Holgate ward’s Cllr Kent said the project had aimed to show children they could influence how their local area looks and bring cheer and colour to their streets.

Cllr Kent said: “People of different ages have already said they have been for a walk to spot them all, which is a great reason to get outside, look at our local area with fresh eyes, maybe spark ideas for further improvements, and be more active.

“They have received a lot of love and spread cheer.”
