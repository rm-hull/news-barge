OVERGROWN plants, fallen branches and debris have been cleared from a York cycle and foot path.

York Council’s Neighbourhood Caretakers have been out clearing the Foss Islands path from James Street to the Osbaldwick and Derwenthorpe area since late June and the start of July.

Cllr Jenny Kent, the council’s Labour environment spokesperson, said it was great to see additional investment into the team made earlier this year making a difference on the ground.

Works to clear the path come as the Neighbourhood Caretakers, which launched in September, are currently focused on clearing priority active travel routes.

Environment Executive Member Cllr Kent said the work would make the path safer, easier and much more enjoyable for those walking, wheeling and cycling around York.

The executive member said: “The team are focussing on priority active travel routes in most need like the Foss Islands path, which is a vital link for people walking, wheeling and cycling, where overgrown vegetation and fallen branches can make them difficult to use.

  The Foss Island Cycle Way after being cleared by Neighbourhood Caretakers 

*(Image: City of York Council)*

“This work was on the stretch from James Street at Foss Islands to Osbaldwick and Derwenthorpe, cutting back vegetation, removing debris and helping keep the paths open and accessible for everyone,

“I’d like to thank the team for their work, people have already got in touch to say they appreciate the visible difference.”

It comes after the Neighbourhood Caretakers had completed 31 walkabouts across all of York’s 21 council wards by mid-May.

A total of 254 issues including problems with plants, street furniture, roads and pavements, along with fly-tipping and graffiti have been reported since October, council figures showed.
