A North Yorkshire based frozen food manufacturer has celebrated a new wave of staff graduating from an in-house training scheme designed to build skills across its UK factories.

McCain Foods, located in Scarborough, marked the achievements of 18 employees, who completed an in-house training programme at its headquarters on Friday, July 3.

The graduates completed McCain’s Manufacturing Academy, which was launched in 2022 to upskill staff across its four UK sites: Scarborough, Whittlesey, Wombourne, and Hull.

Alison Hume, MP for Scarborough and Whitby, said: "Congratulations to this year’s Manufacturing Academy graduates.

"They should be incredibly proud of what they’ve achieved.

"This is the kind of investment that creates a lasting legacy for Scarborough."

The academy offers nationally recognised qualifications (NVQs) from level 2 to level 5 and combines on-the-job learning with formal training.

It supports career development in areas such as logistics, food and drink production, business operations, and leadership.

Alison Rance, vice president of manufacturing at McCain GB&I, said: "It is a real privilege to celebrate this year’s Manufacturing Academy graduates and the dedication they have shown to building their skills."

Since its launch, 118 employees have enrolled in the programme, with new cohorts joining every four months.
