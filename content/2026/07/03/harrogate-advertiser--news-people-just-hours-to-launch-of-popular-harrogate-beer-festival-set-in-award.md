# Just hours to launch of popular Harrogate beer festival set in award-winning brewery

## The organisers of a major beer festival in Harrogate this weekend have released the final wave of tickets as the clock ticks down to the gates opening.

The fourth edition of the much-anticipated Suds With Buds beer festival will take place on Saturday, July 4 at Rooster’s Brewery on Hornbeam Park.

Featuring more than 80 craft beers, diverse street food and live music and DJs across their indoor and outdoor areas, the annual invitational beer festival is a highlight of the beer lovers calendar in a town known as a stronghold of independent brewers.

Queues have been know to form before the gates open at 2pm.

With just hours to go, the family team at Rooster’s are advising that there is still time - just - to book your ticket.

"We cannot wait to welcome everyone to join us for another fantastic year,” said Rooster’s Commercial Director Tom Fozard.

"The team are working hard to get the whole brewery ready for Suds With Buds - cleaning the yard, hanging bunting, checking the sound system.

"With over 80 beers, delicious street food, live music, and a fantastic atmosphere, it’s set to be another one to remember.”

The incredible line-up of breweries joining Rooster’s this year includes favourites like Arbor, Burnt Mill, Track Brewing Co, Neptune, Elusive Brewing, and Polly’s.

Among the other names in independent brewing which will be showcased one stands out in particular – Brasserie de la Senne from Brussels in Belgium which boasts beers that are neither filtered nor pasteurised and with no additives added.

From Galashiels comes Tempest Brewing Co, one of Scotland's most revered cult breweries, while also making an appearance will be Loch Lomond Brewery.

Alongside great beers, there will also be a fantastic mix of street food traders serving everything from comfort food classics to more adventurous bites, with plenty of options for different dietary requirements.

There will be live music throughout the day in the Beer Garden, as well as DJs spinning tunes in the brewery to keep the good vibes flowing all the way to 8pm.
