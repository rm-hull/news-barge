Neighbours across the city are being invited to come together and create new community events this summer.

The scheme, which encourages residents to plan gatherings in their streets, parks or neighbourhoods, aims to build connections, reduce isolation and celebrate our city.

Ideas for events could include street parties, music sessions, family games, gardening days, or skill‑sharing workshops.

City of York Council and Make It York are providing practical support to help residents plan their events, including an easy online toolkit, guidance on event safety and promotion, and access to funding.

Councillor Peter Kilbane, City of York Council’s executive member for economy and culture, said: "This summer we’re making it easier for people to get together in their communities, have fun, get to know each other better and create shared memories.

"There are already some fantastic annual events across the city, and we’re keen to see more communities create new ones."

No prior experience is necessary to get involved.

Lisa Grimbley, head of markets and events at Make It York, said: "Events are a wonderful way to bring people together.

"We look forward to working with local organisers to deliver great events throughout the summer months."

More information is available at www.makeityork.com/summerinyork.
