A KEEN nature lover has written her first book which promotes kindness to other people and animals.

Nicola Wise, who lives near Malton, decided to write 'The Search for Jack's Brain' after retiring.

She said: "I have always loved writing stories, despite struggling with spelling, and have stories queueing up inside my head waiting to be released. It is only now that I am retired that I have the time to seek publication.

"The idea for this story came to me as I listened, yet again, to a parent complain about her child, where I worked at Malton School. I asked her if she would change her child's character, if such a thing was possible. Sadly, she said yes. It then occurred to me that my own mother would have jumped at the chance to change mine and it set me thinking."

Nicola added: "A theme through my book is kindness, both to each other, to animals and to the environment. I grew very fond of Daisy the pig, as her character developed. Another theme is being comfortable with oneself, quirks and all, and using humour as a strategy to help cope with them. They say write what you know about, well I know a bit about coping with quirks."

'The Search for Jack's Brain' features Shifty (15) and Scabs (12) who visit their cousins on the Scottish island of Farrensay, where they are horrified at the change.

Jack (12) had been fun, impetuous, daft and very kind. The new Jack is obsessed with cleanliness, neatness and order. Milly(10) had been mischievous and cheeky. The new Milly is proud to be perfect. Shifty and Scabs desperately want their old cousins back. Shifty hatches a ridiculous plan. They embark upon an adventure to uncover the truth aided by a pig with attitude and a bumbling family friend, who is more of a hindrance than a help, but has a much-needed boat.

The quest is fraught with silly mishaps on a boat overcrowded with six humans and a large pig as they race against time to uncover an evil plot.

Nicola said that apart from writing, she loved spending my time playing silly games with her grandchildren and enjoying the surrounding countryside.

"I am keen on nature conservation generally and love trotting through the countryside on the edge of the Yorkshire Wolds, often tripping because I am too busy admiring the beauty.

"Most Saturday mornings I am either volunteering at, or running Dalby Forest parkrun, a wonderfully inclusive weekly event enabling and celebrating people completing a 5k loop through the forest. I enjoy writing the weekly run report for the sports section of the Gazette & Herald."

'The Search for Jack's Brain', by Nick Wise, is available from Kemps in Malton, along with Foyles, Waterstones and Amazon.
