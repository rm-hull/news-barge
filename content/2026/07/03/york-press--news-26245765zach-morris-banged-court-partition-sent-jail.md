A MAN who had a dagger at a railway station shouted insults at York magistrates as he was jailed.

Zach Morris, 30, formerly of Garfield Terrace, off Leeman Road, York and now living in Stoney Street, Burnley, had denied carrying a dagger on a train but was convicted at a trial.

It was the third time he had been convicted of having a weapon in public and he was also on a suspended prison sentence passed at Leeds Magistrates Court on April 30, 2024.

As dock officers handcuffed him and led him to the court cells, he yelled: “It happened two years ago” and repeatedly insulted the magistrates.

He banged his hand against a wooden partition as he was taken from where defendants on bail sit to the entrance to the secure dock and down its steps to the court cells.

The dagger offence was committed on July 13, 2024, at Wakefield Westgate Railway Station, the court heard.

Magistrates jailed him for 38 weeks in total; 26 weeks for the dagger offence plus all of the 12 weeks previously suspended.
