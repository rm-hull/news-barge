Four people have been arrested in connection with a “cloned” car and an attempted van theft in York, police said.

North Yorkshire Police said it received intelligence that a cloned red five-door Peugeot 207 was active in York during the early hours of Friday (July 3).

It followed a report at 12.15am of an attempted theft of a van in The Wandle, Chapelfields, when the alleged suspects fled after the alarm went off, the force said.

Officers later received information that a red Peugeot had been seen driving in convoy with a white Fiat motorhome.

The motorhome was spotted on the A64 heading for York at 2.07am before travelling south on the A1(M), police said.

A police spokesperson said a “pursuit was authorised when it was confirmed that the vehicle had just been stolen from an address in New Earswick”.

“The driver failed to stop for the police and the pursuit continued into West Yorkshire,” they said.

“Assisted by West Yorkshire Police, a stinger device was used to deflate the tyres of the motorhome as it travelled south towards Darrington.

“The vehicle was stopped at 2.27am and the driver was arrested at the scene.”

Meanwhile, in York, officers located the cloned car in the Osbaldwick area and a further three arrests were made, they said.

Police said four men – aged 35, 31, 30 and 20 – remained in custody for questioning in relation to multiple offences including theft of a motor vehicle on Friday afternoon.

Officers are urging anyone with information about the incidents to contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, or to contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111. Quote reference number 12260124584 when providing details.
