Drivers have been fined after being caught on camera littering near Beverley.

CCTV footage captured two separate incidents of littering in a layby on the A1079 near Walkington, resulting in £200 fixed penalties.

The first offence took place on April 22 at around 7.30am, when a vehicle was seen leaving the layby and a cup was thrown from the car.

The second incident occurred on May 5 at around 2pm, when a man was filmed getting out of his vehicle, smoking a cigarette, and discarding the butt on the ground.

Both men were identified through number plate recognition and admitted to the offences when contacted by East Riding of Yorkshire Council’s streetscene enforcement officers.

Carl Skelton, director of streetscene services at East Riding of Yorkshire Council, said: "Cups and cigarette butts, like in these cases, may be small items, but any kind of litter is a blight on our countryside.

"The council takes all reports of litter seriously, and we will take action against offenders where we can."

Littering is an offence, and those caught could face a fixed penalty or a maximum £2,500 fine if prosecuted in court.

Anyone who witnesses littering is urged to report the matter to the council at eryc.link/streetcare
