An apprentice healthcare worker has been named Apprentice of the Year in a national awards scheme.

Jacob Naylor, who works at York Hospital, received the Apprentice of the Year title as part of the Northern Council for Further Education (NCFE) Aspiration Awards 2026.

These awards celebrate learners, apprentices, educators, and educational organisations across the UK.

Mr Naylor began his career with the trust at just 16 years old and has since become known for his patient care and professionalism.

He said: "I am delighted to have received this Aspiration Award.

"My apprenticeship with the trust has helped me build on my previous care experience, develop new skills and knowledge, and grow in confidence when communicating with patients."

Jacob’s training took place at Leeds City College, where he is supported by course leader Jamie Jaffray.

Mr Jaffray said: "He is an exceptional example of the transformative power of apprenticeships within the NHS."

Mr Naylor has also been recognised with an NHS England Chief Nursing Officer’s Award.

He manages dyslexia alongside his apprenticeship and has developed strategies to support his learning.

Aspiring to grow professionally, he has observed in surgical theatres and volunteered for a three‑month secondment on the hospital’s Winter Pressures Ward.
