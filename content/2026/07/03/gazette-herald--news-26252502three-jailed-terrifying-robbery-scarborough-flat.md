THREE robbers who subjected a man to a "terrifying ordeal" in one of their homes have been jailed.

Kelly Clarke, prosecuting, said the victim was shouted at and put up against a wall, Lloyd Fitzgerald threatened him with a mirror and a bottle, Sadie Metcalfe punched one of his teeth out and put the cord of a dressing gown round his neck and Luke Elliott Clayton rifled through his pockets.

In a personal statement, the victim said he believed he was “lured” by people he trusted to Metcalfe’s address where the robbery took place on March 26.

He now only feels safe in his own home, is terrified to go out and feels vulnerable and isolated. He no longer trusts people.

“All that was so that you three could go off and buy some more drugs because you were under the influence of drugs at the time,” the Recorder of York, Judge Sean Morris, told the three at York Crown Court.

They had all shown a “complete disregard for the law”.

All three pleaded guilty to robbery, Metcalfe on the basis that the victim rang her and that the robbery was not premeditated. The judge said he made no finding about whether the victim was “lured” to the address.

  Robber Sadie Metcalfe of Scarborough 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Police)*

Metcalfe, 42, of Seamer Road, Scarborough, who has 84 previous convictions including for violence on her record, and who was on prison licence at the time, was jailed for 42 months.

Clayton, 45, of no fixed address, and Fitzgerald, 29, of Valley Road, Scarborough, were each jailed for 38 months. Both were on bail at the time of the robbery for other matters.

  Robber Luke Elliott Clayton 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Police)*

Clayton was given an extra nine months for stealing his father’s cherished coin collection in a separate offence, which he also admitted.

All three were banned from contacting the robbery victim or going to his home or place of work for five years under a restraining order.

Following sentencing, investigating officer Detective Constable Sam Rivers, of North Yorkshire Police’s Scarborough and Ryedale CID, said:

“This was a terrifying ordeal for the victim, who was left with several injuries, including the loss of a tooth.

“These three individuals thought they could intimidate and assault someone for little more than mobile phone, a vape and £15 in cash.

“Violence and intimidation of this nature are completely unacceptable.

"No one should be subjected to such an ordeal, and we’ll keep doing everything we can to make sure offenders are caught, taken to court, and held accountable.

“I am pleased with the sentences handed down at court, and that all three are now behind bars where they can no longer pose a risk to the public.”

For Metcalfe, Beatrice Allsop said she was “extremely remorseful” and apologised for her behaviour.

She had had a life full of “traumatic experiences” and was regularly using heroin, crack cocaine and drinking at the time.

She was prone to recklessness, may have a personality disorder and was on medication after being diagnosed with depression and anxiety.

  Robber Lloyd Fitzgerald 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Police)*

For Fitzgerald, Rhianydd Clement said he was ashamed of his behaviour which was different to the rest of his criminal record.

At the time, he had been taking a cocktail of drugs and drinking. He was on prescription drugs for anxiety and his family would help him on release.

For Clayton, Rachel Webster said returning to Scarborough “had been his biggest mistake”.

He planned to move to a completely different area when he was released. He was now free of drugs and alcohol and doing a mental health course.
