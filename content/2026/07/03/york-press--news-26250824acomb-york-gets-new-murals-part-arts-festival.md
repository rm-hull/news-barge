  *Acomb in York gets new murals as part of arts festival - here's what's going on...*

A CORNER of York has been transformed with a new brightly coloured mural.

The striking piece of street art has been created as part of a unique arts festival in Acomb.

The mural is just one of several being painted across this part of York as part of AcombFest26.

Today's photo was taken by Press Camera Club member Sue Gabbatiss.

AcombFest 26 will run over the weekend from today to Sunday and will be turning Front Street, York Road and the wider area into an open-air gallery, blending art, community, and family fun across the west of the city.

Internationally recognised mural artists and multimedia experts are set to create live street art, with large-scale pieces transforming the area under the festival’s “return to nature” theme.

Huge fresh artworks will be created live across Acomb, transforming walls into landmarks over the festival weekend, organisers said.

Alongside the works being created are art markets, workshops and activities.

There are 20 festival venues and more than 90 activities and events planned.

Curators The Art of Protest said it had worked with Greater Acomb Community Forum, Acomb Alive and local residents and schools to support the ambitions of the festival including Acomb Explore Library, Books & Bevs, Holgate Windmill, The Crooked Tap and The Fox pubs.

For all the details, check out the dedicated AcombFest website at: acombfest.co.uk
