Headteachers across the East Riding have come together in a collective effort to strengthen inclusion.

The annual East Riding headteachers' conference focused on supporting every child to achieve and thrive.

Marie Gentles OBE, a national expert in behaviour, relationships and inclusion, delivered the keynote speech.

She began by emphasising the importance of understanding the experiences behind pupil behaviour.

The conference explored SEND reform and opportunities to shape a more inclusive future for schools.

Merlin Joseph, executive director of Children, Families and Schools, said: "The Headteachers conference is an opportunity to think together, challenge together and create inclusive schools where every child is seen, valued and able to thrive.

"There is something special about bringing so many leaders together in one room, united by the same purpose."

Councillor Victoria Aitken, cabinet member for Children, Families and Education, added: "Across children’s services, one of our clearest priorities is inclusion.

"We are also placing a strong focus on the earliest years of a child’s life.

"We want a shift from crisis intervention to early help."

Attendees committed to creating an inclusive, trauma-informed system built on partnership and early intervention, with a focus on giving every child the best chance to succeed.
