# Harrogate faces 400 rivals in battle to win £3m in UK Town of Culture 2028 competition

## A specially commissioned postcard highlighting Harrogate’s UK Town of Culture 2028 bid was among those to be seen by Culture Secretary Lisa Nandy as judging gets under way.

Following the launch of the inaugural UK Town of Culture competition last year, the Government has confirmed that it has received 398 bids representing more than 400 hopeful towns from across the UK.

The rivals facing Harrogate for the title and a substantial £3 million prize include everyone from Scarborough to Stornoway, Loughborough to Hemel Hempstead.

In order to celebrate the local pride expressed in the submissions, every bidding town was asked by the Department for Culture, Media and Sport to submit a postcard to champion their bid.

![The new postcard commissioned by Harrogate Town Council to celebrate Harrogate's bid to become the UK Town of Culture 2028. (Picture contributed)](https://www.harrogateadvertiser.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOmJjMWRlYTAzLTcyNDUtNDNmYS05YzNjLTA4Y2Y5MzM4MDNiNjoxMGNlM2ZmMy01ODI3LTRiOWUtODg2Mi01MjRiNGMzZDI5ODU=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



These are now being showcased by National Museums Liverpool in a digital display in the Museum of Liverpool to celebrate the diversity of each and every town that has participated in the competition.

Harrogate, like all of its rivals across the UK, was given only four days to come up with its postcard.

The result, designed in conjunction with Harrogate Town Council, is a colourful celebration of everything that makes the town a special place to live, work and visit as part of its campaign to become UK Town of Culture 2028.

Mayor of Harrogate, Coun Viv Poskitt, said: "When we received the invitation to create a postcard, we had just four days to develop something that genuinely reflected Harrogate.

![The reverse side of Harrogate Town Council's specially commissioned postcard with words written by the son of Harrogate Mayor Councillor Viv Poskitt's aunty. (Picture contributed)](https://www.harrogateadvertiser.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOjY0YTIxODA2LTAzMjUtNGZiMS04ODU2LWY3MzE5ODk1NmVlYjpiZjAyZWI4MC03MzA0LTQ1OGUtYWVmNS02ZDYxZTIyZjVkMDQ=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



"I'm incredibly proud of what was achieved in such a short space of time.

"We wanted our postcard to tell Harrogate's story through genuine eyes and I believe the finished design reflects the creativity, inclusivity, pride and collaborative spirit that make Harrogate such a special place."

Harrogate Town Council hopes residents, businesses and community organisations will support the town’s bid by sharing the postcard on social media and helping to showcase Harrogate's bid to audiences across the country.

Culture Secretary Lisa Nandy visited the Museum of Liverpool to see a digital display of postcards, as judges engage on the tricky task of whittling down hundreds of applications to a final shortlist.
