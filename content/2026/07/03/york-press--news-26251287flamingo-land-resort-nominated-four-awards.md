A theme park that is easy to reach from York has been nominated for four awards.

The nominations for the 2026 UK Theme Park Awards have been announced, with the Flamingo Land Resort being shortlisted for four categories.

The popular theme park has been nominated for Best Theme Park for Families, Best Theme Park for Thrills, Best Theme Park Animal Exhibit, and Best Theme Park Holiday Village.

The shortlisted theme parks were decided using public nominations and help from a panel of judges, including Paul Kelly, the Chief Executive of the British Association of Leisure Parks, Piers and Attractions, and Andy Hine MBE, the Chairman of the Roller Coaster Club of Great Britain.


## What attractions are there at Flamingo Land Resort?

The Flamingo Land Resort has plenty of rides to check out, including family-friendly options like the Muddy Duck Tractor Ride and Mischief Mansion, and more thrilling rides like Cliff Hanger and the Navigator.

There are also a few different play areas for children to let off some steam, including an interactive water playground that could be perfect for a hot day.

The zoo is also worth a visit during your trip, with a variety of animals and insects to spot, from huge African lions to penguins.

You can easily extend your stay at the theme park by booking one of the cottages or holiday homes that offer easy access to the Flamingo Land Resort and different services depending on your family’s needs.

In terms of food, there are many choices to pick from, such as cafés if you want a quick coffee with a slice of cake or restaurants and pubs to enjoy a more substantial meal.


You can vote for the theme park from Monday, July 20, on the UK Theme Park Awards website.

The awards ceremony will be held at Blackpool’s Pleasure Beach Resort on Thursday, September 10.

## What do visitors of Flamingo Land Resort say?

On Tripadvisor, one reviewer said: “We had a fantastic day taking part in the zookeeper experience at flamingo land where we spent the day feeding animals, cleaning out and going behind the scenes of this amazing zoo.

“We were able to get up close to some of the animals including rhinos, giraffes, wallabies, otters and lemurs.

“The zookeepers were all very welcoming, friendly and informative as we walked around, so it was all very interesting.

“It was a fantastic day for us and we would definitely want to take part in this again.”
