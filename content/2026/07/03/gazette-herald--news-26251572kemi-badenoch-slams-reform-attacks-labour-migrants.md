OPPOSITION leader Kemi Badenoch has attacked Labour for not having a grip on illegal immigration, adding that Linton-on-Ouse is not an appropriate site to house asylum seekers.

The Essex MP also slammed Reform and Nigel Farage, telling voters that people wanting conservative policies will have to vote Conservative, and she outlined how the party would help businesses.

Earlier today (July 3), the Conservative Party leader, with Thirsk and Malton MP Kevin Hollinrake, visited Twisted Automotive at Thirsk, which converts and rebuilds Land Rovers and Range Rovers.

The meeting also included a round table with a handful of local business leaders, including celebrity chef Tommy Banks.

In an interview with The Press, she said Linton-on-Ouse was the “wrong place” to house asylum seekers and “it is still the wrong place.”

She said: “The reason why we are in this mess, the problem is getting worse, is Keir Starmer stopped the Rwanda plan. We need to stop the boats.

"This is the thing that is creating these issues. Until we stop the boats, we are going to have this question again and again.”

  Kemi Badenoch with Kevin Hollinrake MP 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Ms Badenoch replied: “It’s always difficult housing asylum seekers. There are better sites. There are better areas. But right now I am concerned that labour hasn’t got a grip on this problem at all.”

But where would they go?

Ms Badenoch continued: “There are other sites. I've got an RAF site that is housing illegal immigrants. It is not great but it’s in a totally different sort of area.

“We have to bring some common sense back. I don’t have a full list of sites or anything but I am also worried about people being thrown into communities.

  Kemi Badenoch works on one of the vehicles 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

“We had a great big sexual assault in Epping, these people are not detained. They can walk around. We don’t know who they are. Many of them have a criminal background. So we have go to do something stronger, we have to stop them coming in."

Sending a certain number to Rwanda, the UK-Rwanda Asylum Plan, would have worked, she said.

“We do need a deterrent and Labour doesn’t have an answer to that.”

The Conservative leader also rubbished any talk of pacts with Reform, even if a deal with Nigel Farage might prevent a second Labour government.

Ms Badenoch said: “It’s a matter of how do you work with someone who is getting these strange amounts of money, £5 million bungs, he can’t explain that.

“How do you work with people who caused many of the problems in your party. They are people who do not know how to do the job.

“I’m not trying to unite the right, I am trying to unite the country. I want people who are centre right to support the conservatives. We are the only real serious party of the centre right.

  Kemi Badenoch and the business people who attended the event 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

“Nigel Farage wants more welfare, he wants to nationalise. We are the party of Margaret Thatcher. We can be the party of business.

“I have just been at a round table talking about how we get business to do the growth creation. Nigel Farage thinks government can do that as long he is in charge.”

“I said ‘no deals’. I am afraid it’s a competitive marketplace. People keep talking about coalition partners. Coca Cola doesn’t talk about going into coalition with Pepsi. If people want people want conservative policies, they have to vote Conservative.”

Ms Badenoch, who became party leader in November 2024, says her party mmade “mistakes” in the past but “the party is changing.”

She said: “I want people to see that the party they loved, the brand they loved, is back and all of the stuff they didn’t like, we are doing away with.”

  A business roundtable with Kemi Badenoch and Kevin Hollinrake MP 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

She outlined measures a Conservative government would do.

“We are going to control the borders. We re going to leave the convention on human rights. We are going to scrap the net zero targets that are killing off business. We are going to get people off welfare.”

For businesses, especially, the were five things “which we will do.”

She said: “Bring down energy cost which will help everybody, we’ve got a cheap power plan. Cutting business taxes. Cutting red tape. Everybody [this morning] talked about some silly bureaucracy stopping them from doing their job.”

  Darren Greenwood after interviewing Kemi Badenoch 

*(Image: Conservative party staffer)*

“Hiring, we want to get people working, young people are not getting jobs. Let's bring down the cost of employing young people. We are going to scrap Labour’s employment rights act. It’s not necessary. It’s the wrong thing.”

“We are going to change the culture on talking about business. We talk about business as if it’s a problem when it is the solution. They are the people who are going to create the growth for the country.”

And Ms Badenoch’s final message to Yorkshire was that the Conservatives have “made mistakes in the past” but it was still the best option, getting better and it would “bring common sense back. She would also visit York “soon.”
