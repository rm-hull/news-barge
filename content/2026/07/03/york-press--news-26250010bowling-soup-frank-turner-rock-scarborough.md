## Two ‘genre-defying’ bands join forces to rock North Yorkshire coast – in pictures

Fans at Bowling for Soup and Frank Turner & the Sleeping Souls at Scarborough Open Air Theatre *(Image: Cuffe and Taylor)*

Two “genre-defying” bands have joined forces to rock the North Yorkshire coast.

Texan pop-punk Bowling For Soup joined English folk-punk group Frank Turner & The Sleeping Souls for a co-headline show at the TK Maxx presents Scarborough Open Air Theatre on Thursday night (July 2).

The acts and long-time friends delivered high-energy sets under summer skies.

  Frank Turner & the Sleeping Souls at Scarborough Open Air Theatre 

*(Image: Cuffe and Taylor)*

Frank Turner & The Sleeping Souls kicked things off with a powerful, singalong-filled performance featuring fan favourites including I Still Believe, Photosynthesis, 1933, Recovery and a rousing finale of Four Simple Words.

Frank recalled his previous last visit to Scarborough, asking fans: “Do you remember what you were doing on 11th August 2007? I was playing a beach festival here in Scarborough which was something like show 330 – and I’ve not been back here for nearly 3000 shows. I’ve been practising for 19 years to be better at what I do for the wonderful people of Scarborough.”

  Bowling for Soup at Scarborough Open Air Theatre 

*(Image: Cuffe and Taylor)*

Bowling For Soup closed out the night with a hit-filled set, opening with Almost before tearing through crowd favourites High School Never Ends, Ohio (Come Back to Texas) and Today Is Gonna Be A Great Day – also known as the theme song to Disney's Phineas and Ferb.

The band brought the night to a close with Star Song and 1985.

Opening the evening were alternative rockers American Hi-Fi, who got the crowd warmed up ahead of the night on the coast.

TK Maxx presents Scarborough Open Air Theatre 2026 – which sees a record 26 headline shows from some of music’s biggest and best-known stars – continues on Friday when multi-platinum singer-songwriter James Arthur heads to the venue, followed on Saturday by David Grey, Sunday’s Alanis Morissette show and Monday’s performance from Michael Bublé.
