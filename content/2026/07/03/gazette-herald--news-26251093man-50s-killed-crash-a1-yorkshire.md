A MAN in his 50s has been killed after a crash in a major Yorkshire road this morning.

West Yorkshire Police is appealing for witnesses and footage after a man was killed in a crash in the A1(M) just before 5.30am this morning (July 3).

It happened between Junctions 42 and 43 of the northbound carriage and involved a single vehicle - a grey Suzuki motorcycle.

“The rider, a 58-year-old man, was found on the grass verge a short time after the bike had been reported to police by a concerned passing motorist. He was sadly pronounced dead at the scene,” said a spokesperson for the force.

He was wearing a white helmet and a green high-visibility motorcycle jacket at the time of the collision.

As reported by The Press, the Air Ambulance attended the scene and the road was closed from around 7am, so that investigation works could take place.

The Major Collision Enquiry Team says it is investigating the incident and is appealing to anyone who saw what happened in the time leading up to it, or anyone who has footage that may assist to contact them.

  The team can be contacted on 101, or on **www.westyorkshire.police.uk/LiveChat**

The log reference is 378 of 3 July.
