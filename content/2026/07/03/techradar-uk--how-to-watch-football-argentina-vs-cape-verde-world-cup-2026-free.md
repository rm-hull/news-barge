Two teams writing two very different fairytales meet in Miami as Argentina take on Cape Verde in the last 32 of the FIFA World Cup 2026 — and you can live stream the game around the world for free.

Argentina were one of just three sides to emerge from the group stage with a 100 per cent record, thanks largely to talisman Lionel Messi. The forward scored six goals across the holders' three games and became the first man to find the net in seven successive World Cup matches. The 39-year-old will fancy adding to that tally in the city in which he plays his club football. The crowd will be overwhelmingly pro-Argentina (or simply pro-Messi) as the Albiceleste skipper continues his bid to retain the trophy on 'home' turf.

Cape Verde have been the tournament's feel-good story, drawing all three games in Group H to become the smallest nation to reach the knockout stage of a World Cup. The Blue Sharks, marshaled by their 40-year-old goalkeeper Vozinha, sealed their last-32 place with their second 0-0 draw in three matches, against Saudi Arabia, and have already held European champions Spain to a stalemate. A win for this nation of just 530,000 people would be the greatest World Cup shock ever.
