A new magazine is shining a light on untold stories from Clifton’s past.

Stories of women, war and community are at the heart of the new project, launched by the York Museums Trust, exploring the heritage of a city suburb.

It's part of the latest Heritage Hunters magazine, focusing on the often overlooked history of Clifton in York.

The edition shines a light on stories spanning from the Civil War to the modern day and celebrates the contributions of individuals and communities across generations.

Samantha Lukic-Scott, community participation manager at York Museums Trust, said: "This edition of Heritage Hunters has been a really rewarding project, bringing to light fascinating history in Clifton and supporting local people to develop new research skills.

  Participants working on the Heritage Hunters Magazine at York Art Gallery 

*(Image: York Museums Trust)*

"All the York residents who have taken part in the project have worked so hard on their research, and we’re so excited to share their fantastic work with the public."

The project highlights lesser-known histories, including the story of Jane Horsman, who won a court case after being forcibly incarcerated in an asylum, and fresh research on Edna Annie Crichton, York’s first female Lord Mayor.

Other topics featured include the evolution of Clifton’s sports clubs, residences linked to Clifton Hospital, and the lives of men commemorated in Clifton’s WWI war memorial.

Heritage Hunters is designed to empower residents in York’s historic neighbourhoods to discover and celebrate their local heritage.

Previous projects have explored Acomb and Westfield, the Groves, Hull Road, and York Museum Gardens.

This year’s edition is the result of collaboration between Clifton residents, returning Heritage Hunters contributors and local creatives.

York Museums Trust worked in partnership with Explore York Libraries and Archives and Accessible Arts & Media to deliver the project.

Ms Lukic-Scott said: "We’ve been thrilled to once more partner with Explore York Libraries and Archives to open up a wealth of local history and support participants to develop their research skills.

"We’ve also been delighted by a new partnership with Accessible Arts and Media, who have been essential in supporting local people to create a range of inspiring creative responses to the history of Clifton."

A public celebration event will take place on Saturday, July 4 from 11am to 1pm at Clifton Explore.

Organised in partnership with Accessible Arts & Media and Clifton Explore, the event will mark the conclusion of the Clifton project and recognise the contributions of everyone involved.

Printed copies of the magazine will be available at the event and the Yorkshire Museum, and can also be accessed online through the York Museums Trust website.

The project has been funded by Arts Council England.

Residents and visitors are encouraged to attend the event, pick up a copy of the magazine and help celebrate Clifton's place in York’s living heritage.
