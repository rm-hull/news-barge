A four-bedroom Victorian terraced townhouse with period features is on the market.

The property, in Earlsborough Terrace, York, has a guide price of £525,000.

Estate Agents, Your Move Anscombs, said the home is in a "fabulous location" overlooking the River Ouse and is conveniently situated for walking into the city centre via a pathway alongside the river.

  Lounge with bay window, cast iron fireplace and river views 

*(Image: Your Move Anscombs)*

The house is set back in an elevated position above the river with a split-level south-facing patio garden.

The garden is split by the flood defence wall, which was modernised in 2024.

The entrance hallway has a laminate floor under which is the original tiled floor, and there is a cloakroom/WC under the stairs.

  Kitchen with wood-effect worktops overlooking the rear courtyard 

*(Image: Your Move Anscombs)*

The lounge features a deep bay window to the front overlooking the river, a cast iron fireplace with tiled slips and a wooden surround, and a corniced ceiling.

The separate dining room has a window to the rear, and both rooms have stripped pine floorboards.

The kitchen opens and overlooks the rear courtyard, and is fitted with a range of wall and base units with wood effect worktops, space for a cooker with an extractor hood over, and spaces for a fridge/freezer, washer dryer and dishwasher.

  Bathroom with freestanding bath and separate shower cubicle 

*(Image: Your Move Anscombs)*

On the first floor, there are two double bedrooms and a spacious bathroom/WC with a freestanding bath and a separate shower cubicle.

Stairs lead up to the top floor where there are two further bedrooms.

The property has gas central heating, double glazing and an energy performance rating (EPC) of E.

  Split-level south-facing patio and paved rear courtyard with rear gate 

*(Image: Your Move Anscombs)*

There is a rear courtyard garden which is paved and has a pedestrian gate to the rear.

The courtyard has been used as an off-street parking space in the past and could be used this way again if the rear fence was removed or replaced with gates.
