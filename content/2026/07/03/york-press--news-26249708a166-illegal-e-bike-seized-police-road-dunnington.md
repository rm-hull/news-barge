## Illegal e-bike seized on major road near York village

North Yorkshire Police said the machine was illegally powered and had defective brakes *(Image: North Yorkshire Police)*

AN illegal e-bike has been seized after being stopped on a major road by police.

The incident took place on the A166 near Dunnington following reports of road safety concerns in the area.

Neighbourhood police stopped a young man riding the illegally adapted, electronically powered mountain bike during proactive patrols in response to issues raised by residents.

  


The bike was found to be illegal, with defective brakes.

It was seized under Section 165 of the Road Traffic Act 1988 for being used on a public road without insurance.

PC Dave Ellison said: "If you know of anyone riding around on suspected illegal electric bikes please contact police via 101.

"Always call 999 in an emergency.

"Alternatively please speak with a member from your local Neighbourhood Policing Team."

Police encourage the public to report similar incidents through their online reporting tools at northyorkshire.police.uk, by speaking to an operator via web chat, or by calling the non-emergency number 101.

Alternatively, information can be provided anonymously through Crimestoppers by calling 0800 555 111 or using their online form at crimestoppers-uk.org.
