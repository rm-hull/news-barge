# Tom Gordon MP welcomes forced adoption apology from Sir Keir Starmer in the House of Commons

## Harrogate and Knaresborough’s MP has welcomed the Government’s apology for forced adoption but called for the Prime Minister to go further.

After Sir Keir Starmer apologised in the House of Commons on behalf of the British state for its role in historical forced adoptions in England and Wales, MP Tom Gordon asked the Prime Minister to establish a new digital hotline to help victims.

“I welcome this long overdue apology and the work the Government has set out today to provide information and support to mothers, and to their now grown-up children, who were victims of forced adoption,” said Mr Gordon.

“The Prime Minister’s commitment to a National Advice Line will ensure that no one is left behind because they can’t get online.

“This apology must now be followed by real, practical action, so that these injustices are never repeated.”

Harrogate and Knaresborough’s Lib Dem MP pressed Sir Keir Starmer on what support would be available to those who are digitally excluded, and asked whether a national hotline could be created so that all victims, not just those online, could access support and information.

The exchange followed a statement in Parliament yesterday, Thursday, in which the Prime Minister formally apologised, on behalf of the Government, to the tens of thousands of mothers who were coerced, bullied or misled into believing they had no choice but to give up their babies for adoption.

An estimated 185,000 babies were taken from their mothers in the 1950s, 1960s and 1970s, with women pressured into giving up their children because they were unmarried.

The Prime Minister acknowledged that some women experienced treatment that amounted to exploitation and abuse, and said that the shame they were made to feel was never theirs to carry - it rightly belonged to the State.

Speaking in the House of Commons, the Prime Minister said what happened to "tens of thousands of mothers, children and families" was "a stain on our history".

In 2022, the Conservative government declined to apologise, saying the “state did not actively support these practices” and instead said it was sorry “on behalf of society”.
