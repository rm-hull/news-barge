## Best Italian food in and around York? Our top picks may surprise you

Where can you grab the best Italian food in and around York? Image: Supplied

IF you love lasagne and limoncello and can't get enough of pizza and Peroni, chances are you will have strong views on the best Italian restaurants in York.

Or at least the best place to grab Italian grub in the city.

Tripadvisor and Google may guide you too, but Press reviewers can also help.

Here is a round-up of our best-rated spots in York to eat Italian food.

Note, they are ranked in alphabetical order; but safe to say, if they make the list, they come recommended.

And if you are looking for recommendations of where to eat out in and around York, make sure you pick up a Press every Friday for our Food and Drink supplement which includes our weekly eating out reviews, you can also read recent reviews on our website at: www.yorkpress.co.uk/eatingout/

  **Best places to eat Italian food in and around York**

This restaurant has an unusual location – based in a rather non-descript, converted hanger, overlooking an airfield with planes, on the outskirts of York. But the food – and dining experience – won over over reviewer. An extensive menu features a full range of starters, raviolis and pastas, risottos, pizzas, plus mains of lamb, pork, chicken, steak, as well as a daily specials board. Our reviewer reported that prices were a bit higher than he normally paid with a meal for three with drinks coming in at £131.50, but said: " you do get an experience and a meal that is a level above the others, so you can expect to pay a little more". He did note that "the pizzas start from £9.95, with most around £15-16, and judging by the size of them, they look to offer excellent value".

A STALWART of the York eating-out scene, Marzano has been popular for many years, moving from its original home in Fossgate to a larger building further down the street in the Grade II-listed Foss Bridge House in late 2024 (the former home of Loch Fyne restaurant and FR Stubbs ironmongers). Our reviewer had enjoyed the pizzas there on her first visit but on a second trip in March with her family was able to give more of the menu a thorough test run. And it didn't disappoint, with the seafood casserole highlighted as a "standout showstopper". She concluded that Marzano's was reasonably priced "for an enjoyable evening, with lovely food, unhurried service in an attractive setting with a relaxed ambience."

  *Marzano, Foss Bridge House, Walmgate, York*

  *T: 01904 611122*

  *W: marzanoitalian.co.uk*

This might come as a surprise inclusion, but did you know that this tap room also sells some of the tastiest pizza in town? Opened for less than a year, it is a go-to spot if you want to catch up with friends over a drink and some tasty budget-friendly food. Slice Culture Pizza are in charge of the kitchen here.   Bruschetta on the Spark 7 menu - deals under £7 - running midweek at the Piccadilly venue. Photo - NQ


IF you want to combine drinks, fast but tasty food, and socialising, then Spark is hard to beat. Housed in reused shipping containers, the enterprise is home to several start-up street food vendors. We enjoyed sampling its Spark 7 menu, which featured two great deals from vendors providing tasty Italian food: Bruschetta Pomodoro (£6) from Tiro Sicilian, which had a nice crispy base, topped with tangy tomato and sweet balsamic – perfect for a light summer bite, and the pizzette (£6) from RAD Pizza Shop, which was quite lovely, with pillow-y bread dough topped with a creamy, almost sweet topping. The Spark 7 deal is running every Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday from 12pm – 9pm, where selected traders are serving up a midweek menu of budget-friendly food for £7 or less.

  *Spark: York, 17-21 Piccadilly, York*

  *T: 01904 217555*
