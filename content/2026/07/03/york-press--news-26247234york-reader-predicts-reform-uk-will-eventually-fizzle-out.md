## 
  **Reform UK will eventually ‘fizzle out’ - predicts York reader**

I never thought I would hear myself think this but I must confess I’m quite impressed by Kemi Badenoch in the current party leadership line-up.

She comes across as sharp, intelligent, determined and focused. She’s got the ‘kemistry’ - pity about her politics! Notwithstanding, she would have made a good Labour leader with a bit of tweaking and adjustment, or actually quite a lot, after further reflection.

We’ve yet to see Mr Burnham in action at the highest level though he’s hit the ground running or rather ‘railing’, Manchester Piccadilly to London Euston!

As Prince Minister (likely to be crowned) Burnham is talking about devolution and the North having more say and role in government. I’m hoping he’ll bring back the Council of the North with Sir David Skaith installed in the King’s Manor. ‘Don’t forget Andy; the Pennines have two sides!’

Forty-five years ago a comet streaked across the political firmament, the SDP. The ‘chattering classes’ were all agog, a new force had burst on the scene but it was not to last and all but fizzled out; at least merged with the Liberals. Equilibrium was regained and the status quo rescued. That’s how most of us like it in this country. Not so much ‘Rule Britannia’ as ‘Steady as she goes’.

I believe a similar fate awaits Reform UK. In the not-too-distant future the political scene will be nostalgically familiar with…in the blue corner; the Conservative Party and in the Red corner, the Labour Party with the Lib Dems refereeing, the natural order returned.

Things will settle down which will suit most of us. We like ‘settled down’. Then the UK can get on with what it’s particularly good at; being moderate in all things, where we’re the envy of the world.

  **Derek Reed**

  **Middlethorpe Drive**

  **York**

## 
  **Us 'wrinklies' prefer a personal service**

Today I received delivery of a heavy package. The doorbell rang and I opened the door to a Mr Nobody - just the sound of vehicle being driven away.

I glanced down to see the box on the ground outside, blocking the entrance.

Uncaring, thoughtless couriers seem to be the norm these days, as packages are regularly left for us in this way - so different to the friendly, considerate and helpful Royal Mail and Parcel Force posties who happily hand their deliveries over to we two ancient Wrinklies.

## 
  **Mystery Plays were equivalent of our TV**

With the York Mystery Plays now showing on its quadrennial cycle (every four years), it's so easy to forget with our smartphones, computers and near instant ability to seek and find knowledge, that when the plays were first started (records show they were being played in 1376 ) that illiteracy was the norm.

People were born, lived and died often within a few miles of their homes. The plays were the equivalent of our TVs and cinemas; a source of entertainment and knowledge. Dormant for over 400 years, they reappeared in 1909 in static places rather than on wheeled carts - the wheeled carts started in 1998.

The plays, from Creation to the Last Judgement, were originally performed by 48 different Guilds (people of the same trades or professions and setting standards of their works). It's fantastic delving back in history to show how the stories of the Bible were portrayed to the masses.
