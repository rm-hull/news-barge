## Well-known North Yorkshire pub and brewery is back on the market

This well-known North Yorkshire brewery is back on the market. *(Image: Ernest Wilson & Co)*

A well-known North Yorkshire pub and brewery has gone on the market.

Ernest Wilson & Co is marketing the Cropton Brewery, New Inn public House and camping site for an undisclosed amount.

The brewery, in Cropton, near Pickering, was founded by brothers Philip and Paul Lee in 1984. It has grown to be a well-regarded operation making its own ales as well as making beers for other breweries under contract.

The businesses had been on the market several years ago for a reported £7.5 million but Philip told the Press they had been taken off the market and he was seeking capital investment.

Now, Ernest Wilson & Co have published the sale of the businesses online, including showing a video of the brewery, saying ‘price on application.’.

The sales particulars note the New Inn is a traditional stone-built family-run pub with 11 bedrooms, with two more also available in an adjacent cottage available by negotiation on either a freehold or leasehold basis.

  The New Inn at Cropton is also on the market 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

  The pub is connected to the adjoining brewery giving a unique identity, which attracts both local customers and tourists through brewery tours, events and the supply of award-winning beers.

  

  Weekly sales average £14,000 a week, with ‘substantially more’ in the summer.

In addition to the pub and brewery are two nearby camping fields held under a long-standing tenancy, providing a further income stream.

The extensive outdoor areas already host an annual festival and other events and there is scope to host more, including private functions, weddings and corporate gatherings.

The agents said: “Overall, this represents an outstanding opportunity to acquire a profitable and characterful hospitality business in an attractive rural setting.”

The brewery, they said, was comprehensively equipped, with bottling, canning and cask conditioning facilities, capable of commercial scale output.

Although currently mothballed, the brewery is maintained and cleaned with production able to restart within two weeks, subject to staffing and raw materials. It can produce 35,000 litres of beer a week, but this could be expanded.

The agents said: “The brewery is available either as part of the overall acquisition or as a standalone purchase, offering a range of ownership opportunities.

  “Combined with the public house, it provides an attractive integrated brewery and hospitality model with significant heritage, tourism appeal and diversified income streams. Alternatively, it represents a well-equipped production facility suitable for independent brewing, contract production or wider distribution.

  

  “Overall, the brewery significantly enhances the opportunity by providing a scalable commercial asset with an established reputation and substantial potential for future development.”

They added: “The current owners would also consider the sale of the freehold property with the grant of a lease back to the business at an annual rent of £65,000.”
