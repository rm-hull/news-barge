A major parking company is under investigation over its practices at petrol stations.

The UK's Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) has launched an inquiry into Euro Car Parks, which manages more than 3,000 parking locations across the UK and Ireland, following complaints about tickets issued to drivers at petrol stations.

The investigation will focus on whether it is fair to issue fines to motorists who are queuing to use fuel pumps or other forecourt services such as car washes, and will also examine the company’s wider appeals processes.

Emma Cochrane, executive director of consumer protection at the CMA, said: "It’s time for all private parking operators to comply with consumer law or risk action from the CMA."


## Euro Car Parks under investigation over petrol station fines

In February, the CMA fined Euro Car Parks £473,000 for failing to respond to repeated information requests over a three-month period.

The parking company unsuccessfully sought a High Court injunction to prevent the CMA from naming it publicly.

On Thursday, the regulator published an open letter to private parking operators, raising concerns about unfair tickets that aren’t cancelled even after appeals.

Some drivers have received tickets for not paying within just five minutes of entering a car park.

The CMA has made recommendations to the Government on a new code of practice it has consulted on, including that parking companies should be required to make drivers aware of their rights and improve their appeals processes.

Newly published data analysis by the Press Association found that private parking companies are issuing nearly 48,000 tickets a day across Britain.

During the final nine months of last year, 13.1 million tickets were issued, a 19 per cent rise compared to 11 million during the same period in 2024.

With each ticket costing up to £100, drivers could be facing nearly £4.8 million in daily fines.


Simon Williams, head of policy at the RAC, welcomed the CMA’s involvement, calling it "a major step forwards in ensuring drivers are treated fairly by private parking operators."

He said the current system is failing road users.

Mr Williams added: "Drivers aren’t consistently getting fair outcomes when they challenge tickets."

Jack Cousens, head of roads policy at the AA, said: "Drivers are already feeling the pinch with rising pump prices, so the last thing they’d want or expect is to be hit for a parking charge just for sitting in the queue.

"Forecourts and parking operators need to ensure that customers can get the fuel they need without being penalised for simply waiting their turn."

Lisa Webb of consumer group Which? said the complaints are widespread.

She said: "We often hear from people who feel frustrated and confused as they fight unfair charges and tickets issued by private parking operators, including for absurd reasons like queuing for a petrol pump or car wash."

Euro Car Parks was approached for comment.
