Wasps can be quite annoying in the summer when you are trying to enjoy some time in your garden or hosting a BBQ.

They can become an even bigger problem if they start to create a nest on your property.

Throughout the summer, wasps are more active and can find their way into our homes when the weather is hotter.

It is much easier to prevent wasps from coming to your garden and entering your home rather than removing a nest when it has become established.

  Disco balls can be placed around the garden to deter insects. 

*(Image: Getty)*

## How to prevent wasps from coming to your garden and home?

A garden expert has revealed that you can deter wasps from coming into your garden by hanging a few disco balls.

Using a disco ball to deter wasps is a harmless and non-chemical way of keeping them away from you.

The light that reflects off the disco ball can disrupt how the wasps and other insects navigate, which puts them off coming near your plants.

Richard Barker, a horticultural expert and commercial director of LBS Horticulture, said: "Gardeners can use disco balls to deter pests as insects like flies rely on light and reflection when navigating, and their sense of direction can be affected by the reflections cast by a disco ball spinning in direct sunlight.

"This will not harm them but can be annoying enough that they may start to avoid the area.

"The reflections cast by the disco ball can interfere with pests' sense of direction, as they can be reliant on light and reflection to navigate.

"In order for this to work, the disco ball needs to be in direct sunlight with some wind to make it spin."


Where you place the disco balls is important, as you need to put them near the areas you want to deter wasps from, but also somewhere they can catch a breeze.

If you want to keep them away from seating or eating areas, you can hang them over outdoor furniture and around patios.

The hack works best when there is enough sunlight and wind to spin the disco ball.

To make sure the disco balls last longer, you should store them indoors throughout the autumn and winter when they are not needed.
