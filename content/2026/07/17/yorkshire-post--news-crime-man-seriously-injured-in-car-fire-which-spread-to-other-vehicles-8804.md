# Man seriously injured in car fire which spread to other vehicles

## Police are appealing for witnesses after a man was seriously injured in a car fire in Huddersfield.

Kirklees CID would like to speak to anyone who has information about or footage of the incident at The Triangle off Market Street in Paddock on Thursday July 16.

Officers were called to the scene at about 3.50pm by the ambulance services who had located a man who had suffered serious burn injuries in a car fire.

West Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service had also attended the scene at which several nearby parked vehicles had been set ablaze by flames.

Firefighters contained and put out the fires and the victim, a 25-year-old man, was taken to hospital for treatment to serious burn injuries.

He is described as in a stable condition, police said.

No-one else was injured and a police scene remains in place while investigations continue.

Detective Inspector Will Denton of Kirklees CID, said: “We are investigating the circumstances surrounding this vehicle fire and are appealing for witnesses or anyone with dashcam footage to come forward.

“I would like to reassure residents that, at this stage, we believe this to be an isolated incident and there is no wider risk to the public. "While we are keeping an open mind, our initial enquiries indicate that the ignition of the vehicle, which resulted in injuries to the driver, is not suspicious.

“Our enquiries remain ongoing this evening, and anyone who may be able to assist the investigation is asked to contact Kirklees CID via 101 or through Live Chat at www.westyorkshire.police.uk/livechat, quoting police log 1282 of 16 July.”
