A well-known psychological thriller is making its way to the York stage.

The Silence of the Lambs, a chilling new adaptation of Thomas Harris’ bestselling novel, will run at the Grand Opera House in York from March 15 to 20, 2027.

Directed by Nikolai Foster, the production promises a suspenseful interpretation of the dark relationship between FBI trainee Clarice Starling and imprisoned serial killer Dr Hannibal Lecter.

Mr Foster said: "I have a real personal fascination with thrillers and true crime.

  L-R: Jonny Magnanti (Wertimer) and Nikolai Foster (Director) 

*(Image: Marc Brenner)*

"It’s developed naturally over the years, reading books like Zodiac and the Thomas Harris novels, and watching loads of films and TV shows.

"It’s incredible to be working on the absolute motherload of the genre with The Silence of the Lambs.

"This is the greatest thriller of all time."

The stage version is written by American playwright and screenwriter Gina Gionfriddo, whose credits include Law & Order and House of Cards.

Her stage work has twice been a finalist for the Pulitzer Prize.

  Lottie Amor (Catherine Martin) 

*(Image: Marc Brenner)*

Ms Gionfriddo said: "From the beginning we’ve thought about this as a deep and rich two-character play about the relationship between Lecter and Clarice.

"What’s interesting is that Lecter sees her more clearly than she’s ever been seen, including what she’s hiding."

Mr Foster explained why the story is well-suited to theatre.

He added: "When I was storyboarding it, I was thinking about theatrical elements like where the scare moments are: where we thrill the audience and make them jump.

"But it’s also a psychological thriller, which works incredibly well on stage.

"It’s about this duel of wits between two huge personalities in Clarice and Lecter.

  Sam Jackson (Buffalo Bill) 

*(Image: Marc Brenner)*

"It’s a dynamic, exciting piece of theatre.

"The audience is locked in that space with them."

The production also explores deeper themes of gender, power and trauma.

Ms Gionfriddo said: "She’s a great female protagonist for our age.

"I was very, very excited to write for her.

"She’s absolutely the heroine of the film, but there’s even more in the novel, and in the play – like her anger around class issues and the reasons for her essential loneliness."

  Mary Timbrell Hill (Acting ASM) 

*(Image: Marc Brenner)*

Mr Foster also highlighted the broader social commentary at the heart of the narrative.

He said: "It’s clear that this is a feminist novel: it’s a very powerful story about a woman from a working-class background, who has been ostracised, now training in the FBI’s cutting-edge new behavioural science unit in the basement.

"Clarice is the incredible heroine at the heart of that, with vision and courage and determination both in banishing her own demons while trying to protect women."

Tickets for The Silence of the Lambs are now on sale via atgtickets.com/york.
