Nature-themed fun will return for children this summer as part of a popular events programme near Howden.

East Riding of Yorkshire Council’s Kids Go Wild discovery days are once again set to take place during the school holidays, beginning on July 24 at the Humber Bridge Country Park Local Nature Reserve.

The event, held at the Eastrington Ponds Local Nature Reserve near Howden, features a pond safari and nature-based art sessions, with all equipment provided.

Nick Coultish, cabinet member for culture, leisure and tourism, said: "The local nature reserve team have put together a fun and diverse range of activities for families to make the most of this summer, giving them the chance to come together, explore, and appreciate the beautiful nature that surrounds us here in the East Riding.

"We look forward to seeing everyone at the events, learning, crafting and getting to know what makes our local nature so special."

A full programme of events will also run throughout the school holidays at the Humber Bridge Country Park Local Nature Reserve.

The events are designed for children accompanied by an adult and take place from 10am to 12pm or 1.30pm to 3.30pm.

Children’s tickets cost £5, with free entry for those under three.
