A CAR has sustained thousands of pounds of damage after being hit by a vehicle that failed to stop in a North Yorkshire town.

North Yorkshire Police said an investigation has been launched after a hit-and-run in Harrogate has left a vehicle badly damaged.

The crash happened on Railway Road, close to the entrances to Sainsbury's Carpark, on Monday (July 13) between 2.25pm and 2.35pm.

It involved a grey or blue SUV estate, which failed to stop at the scene, and a black Honda; the SUV turned left at the Wetherby Road junction and was heading towards Woodland’s Corner.

A spokesperson for the force said: "The impact caused several thousand pounds worth of damage to the Honda. The vehicle involved will have some level of front-end damage."

North Yorkshire Police are appealing for witnesses, dashcam footage, or information that could help their investigation.

Anyone with information is asked to contact PC Ben Robinson-Brockhill by email at [ben.robinson-brockhill@northyorkshirepolice.uk](mailto:ben.robinson-brockhill@northyorkshirepolice.uk), on the website or live chat, or by calling 101.
