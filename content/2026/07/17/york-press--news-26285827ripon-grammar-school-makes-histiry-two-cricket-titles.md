## Ripon Grammar under-14 teams crowned North Yorkshire champions

The Under-14 girls made school history by becoming the first girls’ team ever to win a cricket trophy for Ripon Grammar School *(Image: Ripon Grammar School)*

Ripon Grammar's cricket teams have celebrated a double success as both under-14 teams won North Yorkshire School titles.

The under-14 girls secured the first school cricket trophy ever for a Ripon Grammar girls' team after a three-wicket win over Rossett School.

The under-14 boys also became North Yorkshire Schools champions with a 39-run win against St Aidan’s.

Batting first, Ripon Grammar School's boys made 161-2 in their 20 overs with captain Oliver Moorcroft retiring unbeaten at 50.

  The Under-14 boys completed the double by capping an outstanding campaign with a commanding 39-run victory over St Aidan’s 

*(Image: Ripon Grammar School)*

Jacob Nicholson added an unbeaten 38, and Will Lakin scored 25.

The team then bowled out St Aidan's for 122 in 17.3 overs, with Oliver Moorcroft taking two wickets for 14 runs, and Seb Robinson taking two wickets.

James Taylor and Ashvath Koneti also claimed wickets in a strong fielding display.

Meanwhile, Rossett School were bowled out for 97 in 18.1 overs in the girls' final, with Rosie Forsythe leading the way with figures of 3-18.

Chasing the 98 needed for victory, Ripon Grammar School's girls managed to reach 99-6 with eight balls left to spare, securing the win.

Winning both Under-14 North Yorkshire Schools titles in the same season is a tremendous achievement for Ripon Grammar School, reflecting the strength of the school’s cricket programme.
