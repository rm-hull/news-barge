# Keighley man jailed after one-punch attack killed pub patron

## A Keighley man has been jailed after a one-punch attack outside a town centre pub left a 34-year-old man with fatal injuries.

On March 7, 2025, police responded to reports of an attack in Keighley.

Police attended the scene following the incident at the Albert Hotel in Bridge Street where Daniel Illingworth, 34, had been punched in the pub doorway by Joseph Smith, 40 from Keighley, a court heard.

The attack was also captured on CCTV.

Daniel was taken to hospital where he died six days after the attack.

In February this year, Smith was found guilty of manslaughter.

![Joseph Smith](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/jpim-static/image/2026/07/17/11/11/josephsmith.jpeg?trim=0,0,337,0&width=640&quality=65)



On Friday, Smith received a total sentence of 12 years and six months – comprising a custodial term of seven years and six months with five years extended licence.

Senior Investigating Officer Detective Superintendent Damian Roebuck of the Homicide and Major Enquiry Team, said:

“This case highlights the devastating consequences that one punch can have.

“Daniel’s death was entirely preventable. This has destroyed a whole family.

“Our thoughts remain with Daniel’s family and his loved ones at this difficult time.

“Violence is never the answer, and I hope this case clearly demonstrates that a single impulsive act can cost a life.”
