## It’s good to see community spirit is alive.

I have never seen as many fire engines go through Hovingham in a 36-hour period.

The fire at Malton Hospital, then lots of fires in and around Hovingham and further afield in Ryedale and of course York, many on farmers’ land.

What was amazing to see was the total community spirit which just happened like magic; farmers all helped each other and some filled tankers with water for the fire brigade to use to keep fighting the fires, took animals away, helped with removing valuable equipment away from the fires in case they spread.

The fire brigade have had to dig deep for a good few days now and carry on in the most dangerous of conditions and work long hard days some have had to not take days off work longer shifts and travel some distance to do this.

We need rain badly now but we need to praise the fire brigade, the farmers and the people of Malton who helped in so many ways.

So well done all of you - you should be very proud of yourself.

Who said community spirit was dead? It’s alive and it’s good to see again.

## Andy’s challenge is democratic accountability

Following the weekend’s ‘coronation’, King Andy and his courtiers have many problems to face.

Not least of which is the challenge of democratic accountability.

For if all Andy is going to do is to continue with current policies he’s effectively saying, "Look – here are the policies that you hated that other guy for - but unlike him I can give you a warm and cozy feeling when I tell you about them.

"You love ‘em really!”

"That’s treating the electorate with contempt."

But if he introduces new policies, voters haven’t had the chance to deliver their verdict on them.

Yes, I know we don’t elect PMs – but we do elect candidates based on the manifestos.

If you’re going to say, "Here are loads of new policies – I haven’t asked you if you like them – but I’m sure you will."

"Well that’s treating the electorate with contempt too."

Either way round the ordinary voter is being treated with scorn.

Of course, Kemi’s Krazies, the Ludicrous LDs, Gormless Greens and Turquoise Toddlers are worse.

But if politicians are so contemptuous of us, don’t be surprised that the next time we’re asked for our X, we’ll return the favour and usher in a Binface landslide.

And would that be a bad thing?

After all, Zelenskyy was a comedian before a politician – and that’s not turned out too badly, has it?

## All MPs need to have effective security

When I learnt of the tragic murder of Ann Widdecombe, my mind immediately assumed this could be politically motivated.

It’s looking like it is not however, her passing takes your mind to the tragic murders of Airey Neave, Jo Cox and David Amiss.

With politics becoming more and more fractured, we need to ensure that all MPs are offered effective security whilst serving in public life and through retirement.

One loss is too many, and I sincerely hope that MPs’ security arrangements are reviewed and improved so that we lose no more in such heinous circumstances.

I’d also like to offer my sincere condolences to Miss Widdecombe’s family and friends.
