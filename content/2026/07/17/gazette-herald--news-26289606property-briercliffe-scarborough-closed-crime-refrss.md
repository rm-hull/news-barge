A ‘PROBLEM property’ in a coastal North Yorkshire town has been closed down after reports of criminal behaviour.

North Yorkshire Police and partners in Scarborough have secured a closure order on a house they dubbed a “problem property” in Briercliffe after multiple reports of persistent antisocial behaviour, disorder and criminal activity at the address.

They secured the order yesterday (July 16) after presenting a folder of evidence to York Magistrates’ Court – who issued a full closure order on the property.

Sergeant Jonathan Dillon of Scarborough Neighbourhood Policing Team, said: “Closure orders enable police and partners to act collaboratively, ensuring that enforcement is balanced with support for those who have been displaced or affected.

“My thanks go to everyone who has worked to ensure a fast and decisive response in this case to prevent further anti-social behaviour and crime that has a detrimental knock-on effect on the local community.”

Sarah Hall, Director of Housing at Beyond Housing, added: “We have worked closely with North Yorkshire Police and our partners to address the serious concerns linked to this property. The closure order is a positive step in helping local residents to live peacefully in their homes.

“Everyone deserves to feel safe in their home and community, and we will continue to support partner agencies to tackle anti-social behaviour and criminal activity wherever it occurs. We would also like to thank local residents for reporting concerns and providing information that helped secure this outcome.”
