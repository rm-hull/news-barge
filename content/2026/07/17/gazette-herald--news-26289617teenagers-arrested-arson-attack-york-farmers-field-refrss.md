FOUR teenagers have been arrested in connection with an alleged arson attack on a farmer’s field in York, which caused £30,000 worth of damage.

The incident saw much of Russell Wagstaff’s fields close to Askham Lane go up in flames at around 3.30pm on Sunday (July 12).

The fire is believed to have started from a lighter, North Yorkshire Police confirmed.

  


Officers arrested four 14-year-olds – two boys and two girls - in connection with the suspected arson on Friday (July 17).

  North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service have been praised in their response to the blaze, along with farm heroes Jay and Cameron, and the local community 

*(Image: Jon Noble)*

Police have confirmed that they are not seeking anyone else in connection with the case.

An investigation has indicated that the fire was started using a lighter before rapidly spreading across the field, requiring emergency service and local intervention after it became uncontrollable.

Two teenage girls, aged 15 and 17, who were previously arrested in connection with the incident are no longer being treated as suspects but remain under investigation for a separate arson reported later on July 12, police said.

  Russell Wagstaff paid tribute to the "community spirit" involved in helping tackle the blaze at his fields in Woodthorpe 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Sgt Ambler of York Outer Neighbourhood Policing Team praised colleagues at North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service, along with two heroic farm workers who raced to disc the field, creating a firebreak and preventing its spread to nearby homes in Woodthorpe.

He said: “I want to recognise Jay and Cameron, who acted quickly and courageously before the arrival of the fire service by creating a firebreak that helped prevent the fire from spreading further.

“Their actions undoubtedly assisted in limiting the damage caused, and it is pleasing to see them receiving the recognition they deserve for their efforts.”

  Russell and the Wagstaff Farm crew were in the burnt field today with combine harvesters preparing for a September planting after crops were ruined in last Sunday's fire 

*(Image: Supplied)*

He added: “I would also like to place my sincere thanks to our colleagues at North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service for their swift and professional response in tackling what became a significant fire, along with members of the local community for their support and assistance.

“This incident serves as a stark reminder of the dangers associated with fire-setting.

“What may begin as a seemingly minor act can quickly escalate, causing extensive damage, placing lives at risk, and placing significant demands on emergency services.

“With the current warm, dry weather conditions, even a small flame can have devastating consequences.

“We would urge parents, carers and young people to be aware of these risks and to discuss the very real dangers of starting fires in open spaces.”

  The fire was advancing close to nearby homes in Woodthorpe 

*(Image: Supplied)*

North Yorkshire Police has been working alongside North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service and other partners in tackling suspected deliberate fires across the city as part of a joint plan, Operation Firesafe.

In the last six months North Yorkshire fire crews have attended over 100 fires that are believed to be deliberate across the city.

These includes areas in Tang Hall, The Clifton Backies and St Nicks Nature Reserve and Environment Centre.

A fire service spokesperson said: “If anyone is worried about a young person playing with fire, they can complete a FireSafe referral on the North Yorkshire Fire & Rescue Service website. FireSafe - North Yorkshire Fire & Rescue Service

“Always report acts of deliberate fire-setting in your community. If there is an immediate risk to life or property, call 999. For non-emergencies, contact North Yorkshire Police on 101."
