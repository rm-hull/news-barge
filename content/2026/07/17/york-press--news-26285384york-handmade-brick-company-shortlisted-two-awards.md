## North Yorkshire company up for top awards for work in Manchester and Oxford

Schwartzman Centre for Humanities in Oxford *(Image: Pic supplied)*

A North Yorkshire brick manufacturer has been shortlisted for two awards at the 2026 Brick Awards.

York Handmade Brick Company, based in Alne, near Easingwold, has been recognised for its work on 3 Circle Square in Manchester (Commercial category) and the Schwarzman Centre for Humanities in Oxford (Education category).

The Brick Awards, organised by the Brick Development Association and now celebrating their 50th anniversary, recognise excellence in clay brick architecture across the global built environment.

Guy Armitage, managing director of York Handmade, said: "We are tremendously proud to have been shortlisted for these two fantastic projects this year.

"Huge thanks are due to the management team and employees at York Handmade for their imagination, enterprise and hard work, which all combined to make these projects so successful and so memorable.

  Schwartzman Centre for Humanities in Oxford 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

"It is important to stress that these two short-listed projects are completely different jobs in design and execution, graphically illustrating our ability to work in a wide variety of colours and styles.

"We believe we can tackle any brickwork project successfully."

The 3 Circle Square project in Manchester involved 385,000 specially manufactured bricks and was one of the most significant contracts in the company’s 37-year history.

Mr Armitage said: "The contract was worth £580,000 and was a resounding endorsement of our recent decision to invest £1.5m in brand-new machinery."

  Circle Square in Manchester 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

Russell Bridge, architect for 3 Circle Square, said: "The brick choice for 3 Circle Square was critical to the facade design's success, which incorporated both standard and long format bricks."

The Stephen A. Schwarzman Centre for the Humanities in Oxford is a £185 million development that used prefabricated brickwork envelopes manufactured by York Handmade.

This project required more than 116,000 Byland Genuine Handmade/Bench Thrown bricks, including Bow-Tie Keys for the prefabrication process.

York Handmade has a strong history of success at the Brick Awards.

  Circle Square in Manchester 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

In 2023, the company won the sustainability category for its work at York Racecourse and was highly commended in the Individual Housing and Refurbishment categories for projects in Surrey and Sunderland.

The company’s Magdalene College Library project in Cambridge won the 2022 RIBA Stirling Prize.

Other notable projects include St Albans Cathedral, Halifax Library, and the Westgate Centre in Oxford.

The awards ceremony will be held at the Royal Lancaster Hotel in London’s West End on Wednesday, November 4.

Shortlisted entries span 17 categories and include submissions from housebuilders, developers, architects and contractors.

For more information on the Brick Awards and this year’s nominees, visit www.brick.org.uk/brick-awards.
