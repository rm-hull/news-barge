## North Yorkshire primary school given one-star food hygiene rating

St Joseph's Junior School in Pickering has acted following a one-star rating for food hygiene following a visit by inspectors recently. *(Image: Newsquest)*

A North Yorkshire primary school has received a one-store food hygiene rating, meaning ‘major improvement necessary.’

St Joseph’s Catholic Primary School in Pickering, which was rated ‘good’ by Ofsted in 2023, received the one-star rating following a visit by inspectors on June 3.

The Food Standards Agency website says for hygienic food handling, the rating was ‘improvement necessary.’ The same rating was awarded for the cleanliness and condition of the facilities and the building.

However, for management of food safety, it was a matter of ‘major improvement necessary.’

The Press has requested a copy of the inspection report from North Yorkshire Council under the Freedom of Information Act, but this has yet to be provided.

The Food Standards agency website has reported a response from the school.

It says the venue was given a deep clean and a new catering manager is in place.

Other measures include relocating a fly killer away from the food preparation area and a survey is underway to install a new £50,000 fan extraction system.

A spokesperson for Impact Food Group, which provides catering services at St Joseph’s, said: “Impact Food Group is disappointed to have received a Food Hygiene Rating of 1 following the inspection at St Joseph’s School. The findings did not meet the standards we expect, and we took immediate action to address the issues identified.

“Our specialist Safety Team implemented an improvement plan, including additional food safety training, enhanced on-site support and monitoring and reinforced procedures for managing temporary issues within the kitchen.

“A follow-up audit has since confirmed that significant operational improvements have been made, and we have requested and will pay for a reinspection by the local authority and we are confident of receiving a much improved rating.

“The inspection also identified structural matters within the kitchen, including the extraction system. The school has commissioned a specialist survey, and we continue to work closely with them to support the required improvements.”

The spokesperson added: “While we take this outcome seriously, it is important to recognise that this is an isolated case and the only Food Hygiene Rating of 1 across the more than 1,700 schools where Impact Food Group provides catering services.

“The local authority did not require the kitchen to close or stop serving meals following the inspection, and pupils continued to receive meals throughout.

“Our priority remains providing safe, high-quality meals for every pupil. We are committed to ensuring the improvements made are reflected when the kitchen is reassessed and that every one of our kitchens achieves the high standards expected by the schools and communities we serve.”

St Joseph’s headteacher Gerard Eddy said: “We would like to apologise to all our families and pupils for the issues that were identified in this report. We know the findings will come as a shock, as St Joseph’s has held a five-star hygiene rating for many years.

"St Joseph’s is proud to serve tasty, healthy food, which is enjoyed by our staff as well as the children, and it’s important to say that the cleanliness of our kitchen was not questioned by the inspection.

"We can assure all our families that we will continue working diligently alongside our catering partner to ensure the highest possible standards are maintained in the future."

The Press will report on any re-inspection.
