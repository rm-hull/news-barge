# Autopsies find most Bangkok bar fire victims died within minutes

- Autopsies have found 33 people who died in a Bangkok bar fire perished from inhaling cyanide and carbon monoxide, which stop tissues from taking in oxygen and can kill within minutes.
- The blaze occurred at Rong Beer Na Lat Phrao, a beer hall and live-music venue, on 12 July, also injuring over 70 people, 24 critically.
- Investigators suspect an overloaded electrical system as the likely cause, noting the venue had applied for less electricity than it was drawing and reportedly carried out unauthorised electrical work.
- Attention has also focused on an emergency exit near the toilets that was bolted shut, potentially hindering evacuation efforts, with police examining how this affected the evacuation.
- Police are conducting an expedited investigation, questioning over 106 people, and plan to charge the owner.
