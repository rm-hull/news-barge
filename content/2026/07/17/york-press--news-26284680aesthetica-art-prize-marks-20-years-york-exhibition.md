An international platform for contemporary art is marking its 20th anniversary with a landmark cultural programme.

Aesthetica Art Prize, based in York and established in 2006, has grown into a global celebration of emerging and established voices in contemporary art.

This year, the prize culminates in a public exhibition and a wider programme under the banner of Aesthetica 20, which also explores the ever-evolving boundaries of artistic practice.

This year’s programme is anchored by the States of Becoming exhibition at York Art Gallery, which will run from July 17 to November 15.

Cherie Federico, director of Aesthetica and curator of the Art Prize, said: "Now marking its 20th anniversary, the Aesthetica Art Prize stands as an international platform for contemporary art, amplifying voices that shape and challenge the cultural conditions of the present day.

"Over two decades, it has evolved into a space where artistic practice is not only exhibited but activated, supporting careers, fostering global dialogue, and championing work that responds directly to the urgencies of now.

"This is art with purpose—work that operates as a force of disruption and recalibration, unsettling fixed ways of seeing and opening up new ways of understanding a world in flux."

The exhibition features photography, film, installation, and digital media.

This year’s shortlisted artists explore themes of environment, identity, culture, and well-being.

The programme also includes the Future Now Symposium, a live event taking place on July 16 at York St John University.

Designed as a live forum for exchange, interrogation, and critical development, the symposium brings together artists, curators, institutional leaders, and thinkers to discuss the technological, cultural, and institutional forces shaping contemporary art.

The Aesthetica Art Prize has helped launch the careers of artists who have gone on to exhibit at Tate Modern, MoMA PS1, the Guggenheim, and the Barbican.

Notable alumni include Larry Achiampong, Jenn Nkiru, and Jane and Louise Wilson.

The 2026 exhibition includes works that examine ecological change, memory, and the instability of meaning in a rapidly shifting world.

Highlights include films and installations by Felipe Castelblanco, Claudia Behrensen, Edgar Martins, and Hope Strickland.

The prize attracts applications from more than 60 countries, reflecting York’s designation as a UNESCO City of Media Arts and reinforcing its role as a site of international cultural exchange.

Aesthetica 20 frames contemporary art as participatory, discursive, and continuously evolving.

Aesthetica 20 defines a cultural framework in which contemporary art operates as a globally connected system of practice, discourse, and exchange.
