The Oscar-winning Irish actor Brenda Fricker, who starred in the Daniel Day-Lewis drama *My Left Foot* and stole scenes in the Christmas classic*Home Alone 2: Lost in New York*, has died at the age of 81.

In a statement, Fricker’s agent said: “We will never see her like again and the world is lesser for the lack of her,” adding: “I was honoured to know, love and work with her and she will always have a place in my heart and in the heart of so many film and TV fans the world over.”

Fricker made history as the first Irish star to win an Academy Award for best supporting actress – taking home the gong in 1990 for playing Daniel Day-Lewis’s on-screen mother in *My Left Foot*. 

![Brenda Fricker in ‘Home Alone 2: Lost in New York’](https://static.the-independent.com/2025/09/18/10/newFile-2.jpg)

However, she’s also known for her role as Pigeon Lady in cult Christmas classic, *Home Alone 2: Lost in New York*. Fricker played a homeless lady living alongside the Central Park pigeons who befriends young Kevin (Macaulay Culkin) as he cause chaos across the city. 

The Dublin-born actor started out as a journalist, becoming an assistant to the art editor of the *Irish Times* before moving into acting at the age of 19. She took on a small role in 1964’s*Of Human Bondage,* an adaptation of W. Somerset Maugham’s novel of the same name, before being cast in Ireland’s very first soap: *Tolka Row* and acting on stage at the Royal National Theatre, the Royal Court Theatre and the Geffen Playhouse. 

She joined the cast of *Casualty* as nurse Megan Roach in 1986 and left four years later, however she returned as the character several times of the years. 

It was in 1989 that she became an international star when she was cast as Christy Brown’s mother Bridget in Jim Sheridan’s critically acclaimed biopic *My Left Foot*. The film told the story of Irish artist and writer Brown, who was born with cerebral palsy and only had mobility in his left foot. 

*My Left Foot* was inundated with award nominations and ultimately took home the Oscar for best supporting actress and for best actor, which was won by star Daniel Day-Lewis. He also took home a Bafta Award for the role.

In her acceptance speech, Fricker thanked Christy Brown for “just being alive” and his mother, saying: “Anybody who gives birth 22 times deserves one of these.”

![Brenda Fricker with her Oscar](https://static.the-independent.com/2026/07/17/14/01KXR0WES3Y2RHRDZNFPTWDF2H.jpg)

Fricker reunited with *My Left Foot* writer-director Jim Sheridan a year later to star in his film *The Field*, acting alongside Richard Harris, John Hurt and Sean Bean. 

She later starred in ITV drama *Seekers*, 1994 sports comedy* Angels in the Outfield*, 1997’s* Masterminds*and 2011’s* Albert Nobbs*as well as her memorable role in * Home Alone 2*before retiring from big screen roles in 2015. 

Despite her illustrious career as a character actor on both stage and screen, Fricker said that she would have been offered more work had she not won an Oscar.

Speaking to *The Times* in 2024, Fricker said: “What did happen was the old curse of the Oscars, as they call it. The word ‘career’ is on everybody’s lips now, but it wasn’t then at all. If you had a career it meant that you were a bank manager.” She added that after winning the award, she was “passed over” and “typecast” in other roles. 

“So there’s a lot that’s not great about an Oscar. And you don’t get any money,” she added. “They could give you a few bob with it, at least.”

Fricker’s final roles were in Canadian TV show *Cam Boy*, ITV drama* Holding*, Channel 5’s* The Catch*and British film* The Miracle Club* in 2024. 

Last year, the actor opened up about her health struggles, admitting that she spent every day in pain and was confined to her bed in Dublin.

“I’ve never known tiredness ever in my life,” she told *The Guardian*. “I’m having a dreadful death. I’m just dying – every day in pain.” She added that when she was unable to sleep, she would watch* The Real Housewives of Beverly Hills*, describing it as “better than sex and better than getting drunk”.
