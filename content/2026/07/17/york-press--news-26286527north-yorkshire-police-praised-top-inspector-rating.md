North Yorkshire Police has been ranked a Level One performing force by inspectors.

The rating, awarded by His Majesty’s Inspectorate of Constabulary and Fire & Rescue Services (HMICFRS), means that no action or intervention is required from the performance system at this time.

Chief Constable Tim Forber said: "We welcome our position in Level One and feel it is reflective of the excellent progress we continue to make in our ambition to be an outstanding force.

"North Yorkshire Police exists to provide the very best service to the people of York and North Yorkshire, and we will never be complacent in our promise to the public.

"Our Level One tiering demonstrates how hard our officers and staff are working to keep the public safe and in our commitment to service.

"We are an improving force and look forward to building on our strong performance in the future."

The rating is linked to the force's most recent PEEL (police effectiveness, efficiency, and legitimacy) inspection, published in April 2026, which awarded North Yorkshire Police six 'good' gradings.

Inspectors highlighted a 9.5 per cent reduction in crime over the past two years.

The force was praised for its focus on preventing and reducing crime, effectively responding to crime and safeguarding victims.
