RAIL passengers could face disruption as train drivers prepare to vote on further strike action as part of a long-running pay dispute.

Drivers from York-based train operator LNER - which operates services on the East Coast main line - are set to be balloted in the next few weeks on potential industrial action.

The dispute centres on a three-year productivity deal reached after nine months of talks, which ASLEF said it had been told by the company that it "did not have a remit to negotiate".

Dave Calfe, general secretary at ASLEF, said: "The company’s failure to make a suitable offer is unacceptable and that’s why we are balloting our members for industrial action."

Nigel Roebuck, ASLEF’s organiser for the North East of England, said the breakdown in talks was 'beyond disappointing' and that a new timetable introduced last December 'had insufficient resources from day one'.

He said: "After nine months of talks, and after agreeing a package that benefits both our members and LNER, being told that third-party interference in the collective bargaining process has blocked the way forward is beyond disappointing.

"The company’s passenger timetable is, yet again, dependent on goodwill and favours and endless overtime. Have they learnt nothing from recent history?"

An LNER spokesperson said: "We are continuing to meet with Aslef colleagues as we work together to try to reach an agreement."

Balloting is expected to begin in the coming weeks.
