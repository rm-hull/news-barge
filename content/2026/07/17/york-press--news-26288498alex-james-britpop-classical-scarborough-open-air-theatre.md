A BRITPOP star is set to bring a night of anthems and nostalgia to the Yorkshire coast tonight.

Bassist Alex James, best known for his time with Blur, will take to the stage today (July 17) in Scarborough for Britpop Classical- a celebration of the soundtrack that defined a generation.

He will be joined by singer Saffron from Republica, Gary Stringer of Reef, and actor Phil Daniels.

North Yorkshire Police have shared a tongue-in-cheek warning for concert-goers, referencing classic 90s hits and offering practical advice for the evening.

A spokesperson for the force said: "We are hoping that you Stay Together as it can get busy. Find yourselves a meeting location before the show starts, in case you become separated. The mobile signal isn’t the best. You might not make a great Connection.

"Some might think that it’s a Chemical World. You don’t want to be Caught By The Fuzz, or security entering the Popscene thinking you are Sorted For E’s And Wizz. I’ve gotta tell you, The Drugs Don’t Work, they just make you worse, and you find yourself sitting in a cell again. Talk to the team if you think you need some help. Referrals can be made.

"As we come To The End of the performance, the road outside the venue will close. They may be some delays in the area. We don’t want any Road Rage so chill your beans, Alright.

"Before you leave the venue, if the call of nature is there, please use the toilet. We have too much Animal Nitrate when tiddling starts in the bushes. This isn’t Trash talk, but it does result in a fine."#

They also warned concert goers to avoid going in the sea with high tide expected on nearby North Bay at 6.42pm
