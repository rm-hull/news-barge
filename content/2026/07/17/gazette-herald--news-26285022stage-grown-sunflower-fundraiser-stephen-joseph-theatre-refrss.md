Sunflowers grown on stage are helping raise funds for charity and the arts.

The blooms, seen in Calendar Girls the Musical and currently running at the Stephen Joseph Theatre (SJT) until July 25, are being sold to support the theatre and Blood Cancer UK.

In the show, actors Christina Meehan and Neil Moors plant real sunflower seeds on stage.

Ben Vickers, the production’s sound engineer, suggested turning them into a fundraiser.

Mr Vickers said: "In the show, Neil has a line that goes ‘Never let beauty go to waste!’ and I thought, we shouldn’t let these little seedlings go to waste."

  Ben Vickers, the production's sound engineer 

*(Image: Chrissie Lewis)*

The seedlings, now replanted into terracotta pots, are being sold through the theatre’s box office for £5 each.

Ms Meehan and Mr Moors play Annie and John Clarke, based on the real Angela Baker (now Knowles) and her husband John.

The story behind Calendar Girls began when Angela’s husband John died from blood cancer, inspiring the famous WI calendar which went on to raise more than £5 million, paying for a groundbreaking research programme.

The sunflower was John’s favourite flower and featured in every photo of the original calendar.

Seedlings can be purchased in person from the SJT box office.
