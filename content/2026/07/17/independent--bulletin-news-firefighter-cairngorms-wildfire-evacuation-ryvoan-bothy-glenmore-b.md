A major wildfire has been raging in the Cairngorms National Park for a third day, having ignited in heathland near Ryvoan Bothy on Wednesday morning.

The blaze has engulfed an area spanning approximately six kilometres, with the RSPB reporting that 741 acres of land have been burnt so far.

Several homes, businesses, campsites, and ski resorts within Glenmore Forest Park have been closed and evacuated as a precautionary measure, though no casualties have been reported.

Fire crews, supported by RSPB staff and volunteers, are continuing to battle the 'protracted incident', with the Scottish Fire and Rescue Service confirming adequate resources are deployed.

A strict cordon remains in place, and the public is warned to avoid the affected area, as a very high wildfire risk persists across parts of Scotland until Monday.
