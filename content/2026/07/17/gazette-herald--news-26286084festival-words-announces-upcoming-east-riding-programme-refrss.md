A literary festival celebrating books, ideas, and storytelling is set to return this October.

The East Riding Festival of Words, organised by East Riding Libraries and funded by Arts Council England, will celebrate books, stories and ideas with a programme featuring best-selling writers, performances and events exploring everything from crime fiction and romantasy to history, creativity and contemporary culture.

Councillor Nick Coultish, East Riding of Yorkshire Council’s Cabinet Member for Culture, Leisure and Tourism, said: "I’m delighted to see the East Riding Festival of Words return this year.

"The festival brings so many esteemed local and national authors to our area and is both a celebration of literature and of libraries, both of which form a key part of the rich culture of the East Riding.

"It is great to bring affordable entertainment to the East Riding, which is truly great news for lovers of the Festival of Words."

Guest speakers and authors will include Patrick Gale, Mark Billingham, M.J. Arlidge, John Ironmonger and Dave Haslam.

'Suspense Saturday' will take place on Saturday, October 3 at Toll Gavel Church in Beverley.

The event will bring together top crime writers including Tom Hindle, Gay Marris, J.M. Hall, M.J. Arlidge, Ajay Chowdhury, Remi Kone and Mark Billingham for a full day of crime fiction.

'Romantasy Day' will take place at Bridlington Spa on Sunday, October 4.

The event will showcase authors from the rapidly growing genre that blends romance, fantasy, magic and mythology.

Another key event will be 'Magic Between the Shelves' at Driffield Centre on Wednesday, October 7.

The event will feature author August Bloom in conversation with Louise Beech about cosy fantasy and the inspiration behind The Tiny Magic Bookshop.

John Ironmonger will appear at South Cave Library on Thursday, October 8, discussing his latest novel, The Angel of St. Piran.

For more information or to purchase tickets, visit www.festivalofwords.co.uk.
