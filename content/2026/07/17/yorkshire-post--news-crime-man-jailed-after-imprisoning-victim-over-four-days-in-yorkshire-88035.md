# Man jailed after imprisoning victim over four days in Yorkshire

## A criminal who was in court just 24 hours after his arrest for imprisoning a woman in Kirklees has been jailed.

Kirklees detectives have praised the courage of the victim for coming forwards following the sentencing of Artur Borisenkov, 32 at Leeds Crown Court on Wednesday July 15.

Borisenkov, of no fixed address, was sentenced to six years in prison for one count of false imprisonment, a count of coercive and controlling behaviour and two counts of criminal damage which involved the victim’s property.

He had admitted the criminal damage offences but was found guilty of false imprisonment and coercive and controlling behaviour at a trial at Leeds Crown Court last month.

![Borisenkov, aged 32, of no fixed address was sentenced to six years in prison for one count of false imprisonment, a count of coercive and controlling behaviour , and two counts of criminal damage which involved the victim’s property](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOjAwYTA4OWQ0LWVhYjAtNDcwNi04Zjg1LTVhMTZhNWY1Mjg4Yzo5ZDQzY2QzNy1mMmQyLTRiYzItYjVmZS0zYzM1YWJjMjlmNzc=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



His offending came to light after the victim contacted police on December 3, 2025 to report she had been falsely imprisoned by him over a four day period.

Officers arrested Borisenkov on the same day and following questioning he was charged to appear before Leeds Magistrates’ Court on December 4.

Dc Yvonne Brear of the Kirklees District Police , said: ”The victim in this case has shown real courage in coming forwards and reporting Borisenkov for the awful offences he subjected her to. Her bravery resulted in this man being swiftly taken off the streets and being put behind bars.

“The spend with which he was initially charged demonstrates the strength of the evidence against him, and we welcome the jury’s verdict in finding him guilty of imprisoning and coercively controlling his victim, offences which he tried to deny to the end.

“I hope she can take some closure from knowing he is now behind bars and that he has also been made subject of a restraining order to prevent him from contacting her again.”
