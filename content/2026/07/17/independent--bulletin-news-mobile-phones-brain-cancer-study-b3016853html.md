Scientists: Cell Phone Cancer Fears Debunked

The World Health Organization (WHO) commissioned 13 reviews in 2019 to investigate the health effects of electromagnetic waves.

A new study, analysing these reviews, found no evidence that electromagnetic waves emitted by mobile phones cause cancer in the brain, head, or neck.

The research specifically concluded there was no association between mobile phone use, regardless of duration or frequency, and brain cancer.

It also found no link between radio or television transmission and childhood leukaemia, addressing a long-standing concern.

While some reviews noted poor data quality for other health effects, the evidence regarding brain cancer was rated highly, suggesting current exposure limits are likely safe.
