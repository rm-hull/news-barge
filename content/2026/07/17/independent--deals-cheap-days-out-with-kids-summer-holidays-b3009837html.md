The summer holidays are here, and many parents will be looking for ways to keep the kids entertained without breaking the bank. With six weeks to fill with activities, childcare and days out, the costs can soon add up. However, with a little forward planning, you can save up to 50 per cent on tickets to some of the UK's most popular family attractions.

From theme parks and zoos to aquariums and historic landmarks, I've rounded up the best deals on family days out this summer, to help you stretch your budget further.

## Best deals on summer days out with the kids

## Alton Towers

![Alton Towers](https://static.independent.co.uk/2025/08/29/8/14/AT-discount-code.png)

This summer, Alton Towers is hosting its Meet the Mobs event, during which families can enjoy hands-on activities, meet fan-favourite Minecraft mobs (including Creepers, Pandas and Axolotls) and follow the interactive Baby Mob Trail. The event is included in the price of a regular day ticket and runs from 18 July to 28 August, with tickets starting from £29.75, thanks to the resort's current summer VAT-saving offer.

If you’re not a Minecraft fan, fear not, as the theme park is open as normal, so there’s plenty more to enjoy, too.

## Chessington World of Adventures

![Chessington’s brand new Paw Patrol land](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/05/14/15/06/May-half-term-Paw-Patrol-Land-Chessington.png)

Chessington World of Adventures is a great choice for families with children of all ages, with its brand-new Paw Patrol-themed land offering plenty to keep pre-schoolers entertained alongside the resort's bigger rides.

Book in advance and Chessington tickets start from £29.75, including access to the theme park, zoo and Sea Life aquarium.

## Legoland Windsor

![](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/06/08/16/41/Legoland-Lego-festival-2026-creativity-zone.png)

Lego fans can enjoy a packed day out at the Lego festival, which is running between 20 July and 31 August at Legoland Windsor. There’ll be everything from building challenges and live entertainment to interactive experiences for all ages. Tickets start from £29.75 with Merlin’s summer saving offer.

## English Heritage sites

![](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/06/12/13/07/Hadrians-wall-english-heritage.png)

If you're planning more than one family day out to English Heritage’s vast collection of sites this summer, a membership could be well worth considering. There are more than 400 historic sites across England, including Stonehenge and Hadrian’s Wall, many of which host family-friendly events and activities during the school holidays.

Right now, you can save 25 per cent on annual memberships, which give you unlimited access to all English Heritage sites, so you'll often recoup the cost after just a handful of visits.

## Warwick Castle

![Embrace the best of British history with a trip to Warwick Castle](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/03/16/17/57/Warwick-Castle-deals-and-discount-codes-2026.png)

Warwick Castle has an action-packed itinerary this summer, with live shows, entertainment, castle tours and more. The Falconer’s Quest is the UK’s largest birds of prey display, with more than 60 different birds. There are also meet and greets with Zog the dragon for younger guests, and you can pay a visit to the dungeons, if you’re feeling brave enough.

Tickets start from £25.38 instead of £29 when booked between 25 June and 1 September, as part of the summer VAT savings offer.

## Sea Life Centres

![Legoland Discovery Centre and Sea Life Centre are a stone’s throw away from each other](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/02/18/14/13/sea-life-hero.png)

A trip to a Sea Life centre is a great option if you're looking for an indoor activity during the summer holidays. With locations across the UK, including London, Birmingham, Brighton and Manchester, families can get up close to sharks, rays, turtles, penguins and thousands of colourful sea creatures through interactive exhibits and underwater tunnels.

Tickets are cheapest when booked online in advance, with prices starting from around £21 at selected locations. You can also save more by booking a combined ticket with other nearby Merlin attractions, such as Birmingham’s Legoland Discovery Centre, or by taking advantage of the current second-day-free offer before the promotion ends.

## Thorpe Park

![](https://static.independent.co.uk/2025/07/24/15/21/thorpe-park-hyperia.png)

Thorpe Park's full lineup of thrill rides will be open throughout the summer holidays, including favourites such as Hyperia, Stealth and Tidal Wave. If you book in advance online, day tickets start from £29.75.

## How to save on travel during the school holidays

### Cheap train tickets and railcards

If you're travelling by train this summer, a ‘friends and family’ railcard is one of the easiest ways to keep costs down. It gets you up to a third off most adult fares and 60 per cent off children's fares, for up to four adults and four children travelling together, making it ideal for family days out. You'll often recoup the cost of the railcard after just a couple of trips.

### Budget-friendly coach travel

Coach travel is one of the easiest and cheapest ways to travel the UK when it comes to long journeys. You can often save up to 20 per cent on travel with National Express and fares are reasonably priced, starting from as low as £4.80 on selected routes.
