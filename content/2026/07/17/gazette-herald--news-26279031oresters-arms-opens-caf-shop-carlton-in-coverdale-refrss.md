A historic Yorkshire pub is branching out to offer even more to its neighbours.

The Foresters Arms, a Grade II-listed 17th-century pub in Carlton-in-Coverdale, has opened a new café and village store to support residents and visitors in the remote Yorkshire Dales village.

Located in the Yorkshire Dales National Park, the pub was bought by the community 15 years ago and now operates as a co-operative.

It said the new facilities would offer vital services in an area with no public transport and where the nearest shop sits a few miles away.

  Café manager Julie Heseltine and team are serving up great coffee and homemade treats 

*(Image: KEITH MEATHERINGHAM / DOBSON AGENCY)*

Linda Heseltine, the pub’s café and store manager, said: "The new café and store have already really enhanced the quality of life for many local people.

"We’re so proud to now offer these essential local amenities, with the new shop and café offering a true local hub where people can enjoy connecting with others.

"Locals now don’t have to drive for miles to stock up on essentials, with the shop set to be even more invaluable during the winter, when driving conditions deteriorate.

  The Foresters Arms' team is proud of the positive impact the new community café and shop are already having 

*(Image: KEITH MEATHERINGHAM / DOBSON AGENCY)*

"The community café is proving a thriving social hub for a wide range of people, including elderly people living on their own and young families."

The store, named Auntie Betty’s after the long-closed village post office that served the community for 60 years, offers around 400 items including bread, milk, ready meals, and toiletries.

It also champions products from nearby producers such as Dales Dairy in Grassington and Two Dales Bakery in Reeth.

  The new shop and café have further strengthened the pub’s position at the heart of the local community 

*(Image: KEITH MEATHERINGHAM / DOBSON AGENCY)*

The shop operates on an honesty-based model, where payments can be made at the bar or café during opening hours, or via QR code when the pub is closed.

The 26-seat community café serves cooked breakfasts, hot drinks, and homemade cakes, and is proving a popular meeting spot for people to connect with others.

Plans are underway to expand the service, including selling allotment-grown produce using the QR system, hosting a Chatty Café table, and introducing a book club.

Support and guidance for the project came from Pub is The Hub, with funding from the Department for Business and Trade’s Hospitality Support Fund.

  The new store has over 400 products for locals to stock up on, including local produce 

*(Image: KEITH MEATHERINGHAM / DOBSON AGENCY)*

Nigel Williams, regional advisor for Pub is The Hub, said: "The Foresters Arms is a fantastic example of a pub at the heart of its community.

"It’s wonderful to see how the shop and café are helping to bring people together and strengthen social connections further."

Kate Dearden, Minister for Employment Rights and Consumer Protection, added: "Rural pubs like the Foresters Arms are vital to their communities.

"This is exactly why we are working to secure the future of rural pubs with our Hospitality Support Fund."
