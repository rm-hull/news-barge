## Historic bridge damaged in popular North Yorkshire seaside village

The edge of the bridge has been damaged *(Image: Provided)*

  **AN investigation has been launched after a bridge in a popular North Yorkshire seaside village was damaged**

Parts of East Row Beck Bridge, in Sandsend near Whitby, were cordoned off earlier this week after the structure was hit by a vehicle.

The bridge, which lies on the A174, was originally built in 1777 by one of York’s most celebrated architects – John Carr – before it was replaced with the current structure in 1910, following flooding in the area.

Whilst there were orange barriers in place at the scene, the bridge still remains usable to the public and the nearby footbridge is undamaged.

  A wider shot of the bridge 

*(Image: Provided)*

North Yorkshire Council’s assistant director for highways and transportation, Barrie Mason, said: “We have been made aware that East Row Beck Bridge, in Sandsend, has sustained minor damage to its stonework and a railing following a collision involving a vehicle.

“The incident is believed to have taken place at around 7.30am on Tuesday (July 14).

  The incident has been reported to the police 

*(Image: Provided)*

“The bridge remains open to pedestrians and vehicles. The incident has been reported to North Yorkshire Police and we are making enquiries with local residents and businesses to identify any CCTV footage that may assist.”
