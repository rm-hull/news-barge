A Yorkshire brewery is bringing back its popular Oktoberfest beer festival.

Wold Top Brewery, based in Wold Newton, will host the event on Saturday, September 26, running from 3pm to 11pm and featuring a day of food, drink, and music.

Kate Balchin, managing director at Wold Top, said: "We had such good feedback from last year’s event and our brewers were excited to have another opportunity to brew a special Oktoberfest beer that we’re repeating our German beer festival with a Yorkshire twist.

"We’re all really excited for what will be a fabulous, fun-filled afternoon and evening."

Visitors can look forward to a specially brewed Oktoberfest beer, German-inspired food with a Yorkshire twist from Nabs Nosh, and live music from an Oompah band.

Tickets cost £18, including a pint or soft drink.

Camping, caravan, and motorhome pitches are available for £12 per pitch.

Tickets can be booked via [https://bit.ly/WTEvents26](https://bit.ly/WTEvents26) or by calling 01723 892222.
