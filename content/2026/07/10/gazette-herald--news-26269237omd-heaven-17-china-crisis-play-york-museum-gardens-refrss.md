**OMD, Heaven 17 and China Crisis play York Museum Gardens - photos by GARRY HORNBY**

POP stars from the 80s rocked York's Museum Gardens last night as a four-day festival of music and comedy got underway.

Taking to the stage in the iconic historic setting were legends of the 80s: OMD, Heaven 17, and China Crisis.

Our photos are by Garry Hornby, who shared them in our Facebook group, Press Camera Club.

  Back to the 80s at York Museum Gardens. Heaven 17 on stage. Photos by Garry Hornby


The gig was a part of Live at York Museum Gardens - a music festival led by Futuresound Group.

The event is running until Sunday.

Taking tonight's slot is Mercy Prize nominated artists Self Esteem, supported by London-based indie group the Big Moon, South African “future ghetto funk” pioneer Moonchild Sanelly and Nigeria-born now Sweden-based spoken word artist and musician Joshua Idehen.

  Back to the 80s at York Museum Gardens. With China Crisis. Photos by Garry Hornby


Super Furry Animals will close the music festival on Saturday from 4pm, with indie musician Baxter Dury, indie-pop's Los Campesinos!, Nottingham-based alt-country band Divorce and Welsh Music Prize nominees Pys Melyn.

  *Recommended reading:*

On the Sunday, York Comedy Festival will see performances from Russell Howard, Ross Noble, Joanne McNally and more from 3pm.

  Back to the 80s at York Museum Gardens. Photos by Garry Hornby


The Museum Gardens – one of the city's biggest parks – will be closed to the public during the four-day festival.
