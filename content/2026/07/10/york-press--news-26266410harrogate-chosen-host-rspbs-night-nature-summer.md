## RSPB's Night of Nature event heads to Harrogate for Yorkshire debut

The RSPB will host its Night of Nature event at the Harrogate Convention Centre Auditorium *(Image: RSPB)*

An event blending music, nature, and immersive storytelling is heading to Yorkshire this summer.

The Royal Society for the Protection of Birds (RSPB) will host its Night of Nature event at the Harrogate Convention Centre Auditorium on August 15, 2026, following a successful debut in London last year.

Designed to inspire action for the natural world, the immersive evening blends live music, wildlife storytelling, and cinematic visuals from across the UK.

Beccy Speight, chief executive of the RSPB, said: "Following the overwhelming success of Night of Nature 2025 – and the incredible feedback we received from our guests – we are delighted to bring this special event back for 2026.

"This year, we’re taking Night of Nature north from the capital to Yorkshire, sharing the magic of this unforgettable evening with even more of our supporters."

  Headline performance will come from vocal group G4, with support from the Blackheath Quartet 

*(Image: RSPB)*

The event will be hosted by RSPB vice president and broadcaster Kate Humble.

Headline performance will come from vocal group G4, with support from the Blackheath Quartet.

Wildlife footage will be projected on the big screen throughout the evening.

Tickets start at £35 and are available via [https://www.rspb.org.uk/whats-happening/night-of-nature](https://www.rspb.org.uk/whats-happening/night-of-nature)

Teams from nearby RSPB reserves will also attend, including Bempton Cliffs, Leighton Moss and Aire Valley.
