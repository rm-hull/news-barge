## Casino bonus offers: How The Independent rates and reviews

When considering how good a casino bonus is, it is not simply a case of choosing the biggest bonus to recommend. The biggest bonus is not always the best bonus; rather, the best offer is usually the one with the best mix of value, simplicity and fair terms.

When evaluating offers, we consider the following factors:

**Bonus value**

While it is not the most important thing, the overall value of the bonus is worth considering. This can include the total bonus funds amount, the matched deposit size and the quantity of free spins.

The more valuable an offer is, the more likely it is to catch the eye.

**Wagering requirements**

Ideally, we want to see no wagering requirements, or as few as possible. An offer with no wagering is always going to rank among the stronger offers as lower playthrough is better for prospective customers.

All offers featured on this page are compliant with the January 2026 changes to casino bonus offers.

**Minimum deposit and stake**

Of course, lower minimum deposit and stake barriers score better in our rankings. Some casino offers come with a minimum deposit and stake as little as £5, though an amount of £10 is the most common.

**Ease of claim**

It goes without saying that offers that are accessible and easy to claim score highly in our rankings. Casino bonuses should have a clear opt-in path, no awkward activation steps and minimal code friction (unless the code adds real value).

**Eligible games**

Most casino sites have restrictions where UK casino bonuses can be used on their platform. A good casino bonus will provide customers with a broader game choice for using their bonus funds and free spins.

Single-game-only offers score lower in our rankings.

**Expiry window**

All bonus funds and free spins come with certain expiry windows. Seven days is the industry standard, though some offers have shorter periods. Of course, longer validity is always better, such as Virgin Games’ 30-day window.

**Restrictions**

Common restrictions on offers include excluded payment methods, max cashout limits and limited game contribution.

Ideally, online casino bonuses should allow for straightforward deposits across a range of methods, with higher cashout limits on wagers and a wider game contribution where applicable.

**Brand trust**

Of course, a casino bonus from a known operator is always likely to rank well, with many of the most trusted names in the UK industry offering bonuses.

Casinos should also provide the full suite of safer gambling tools for their customers.

## What makes a good casino bonus?

Certain factors help determine what makes a casino sign up offer a good one. The value and the range of the offer is important, but other relevant factors that we consider include:

- **No wagering requirements -** A lack of wagering requirements is often the most valuable feature that an offer can have, as it makes the promo more accessible and comes with more potential value for the user.

- **Low minimum deposit -** Low deposit requirements are necessary for recommending an offer, as we don’t want users to have to fork out too much initially. £10 offers are easier to access, though matched deposit offers can be good for customers who want a bit more range.

- **A no deposit element -** This is useful for trying a site before committing more money, and offers an initially risk-free way to get started on casino sites.

- **Good game flexibility -** It is better if spins work across multiple popular slots. Even if these are tied to a certain series, it still gives users more variety.

- **Longer expiry periods -** Longer expiry periods make offers more player-friendly. Seven days is industry standard for bonus expiry, but some offers do come with shorter windows.

- **Fewer payment restrictions -** We prefer offers that come with fewer payment restrictions, meaning they accept more methods for initial deposits. Almost all offers will accept debit cards, but we prefer offers that don’t exclude e-wallets, prepaid methods or pay by mobile casino deposits.

- **Simplicity -** Of course, it is vitally important that bonus offers are simple to access and easy to understand. Our recommended bonus offers avoid confusing multi-step unlock conditions.

## Types of casino offers explained

In this section, we’ve provided some extra detail on the more common types of casino bonus offers that users can expect to encounter.

### Casino welcome bonus offers

Welcome bonuses are simply standard sign-up bonuses for new players. These can include free spins, bonus funds or both, and they are only open to new customers.

### No deposit casino bonuses

These bonuses are also sometimes available on sign-up, before making a deposit. They usually include free spins and are often smaller, but they are attractive because they reduce upfront risk for users, as they don’t need to use their own money.

### Free spins bonuses

This is the most common format for current UK casino offers, with users awarded free spins both as part of welcome offers and occasionally as a reward for regularly using a site. Free spins are usually tied to specific slots, and can only be used on certain titles.

### Deposit match bonuses

This is another common form of bonus that usually comes as part of a welcome offer. The casino simply matches part of your first deposit, usually either by 50 per cent or 100 per cent.

These offers can work well, but often they come with more restrictive terms, such as wagering requirements on the bonus funds. Deposit matches often come along with free spins to sweeten the deal.

### No wagering casino bonuses

This type of bonus is the easiest to understand, as it offers funds or free spins without having the wager the bonus funds or winnings a certain amount of times over before being eligible for a withdrawal. This essentially means that any winnings from the casino bonus can usually be withdrawn straight away.

### Bonus codes

Some sites require casino bonus codes during sign-up or in the promo hub. For example, among our recommended online casinos, Paddy Power, Betfair and MrQ all require bonus codes to sign up, which we have outlined above.

## Terms to consider for casino bonuses

Casino bonuses come with plenty of terms that readers need to be mindful of. When opting in to any offer, look out for the following common terms:

**Wagering requirements:** As mentioned, casino offers often come with wagering requirements attached to bonus funds and/or free spins. Be sure to check that these wagering requirement are fair before opting in. The maximum allowed is 10x. 

**Short expiry periods:** Most offers will have terms stating that bonuses and free spins expire within seven days, which is the industry standard. However, some offers have shorter windows, such as three days or 24 hours.
