An award-winning new housing development has been named the best in the city for setting high standards in design and sustainability.

Passivhaus' Duncombe Square in York, designed by Mikhail Riches on behalf of City of York Council, won the Lord Mayor’s Award at this year’s York Design Awards.

The scheme brings 34 eco-friendly homes to the city, designed to the demanding Passivhaus standard, and was praised for its sustainability and design quality.

Ann Reid, chair of the York Design Awards, said: "Duncombe Square was a worthy winner of the prestigious Lord Mayor’s Award and is a fantastic addition to the city.

"Through collaboration and a commitment to design quality, the scheme creates a distinctive, low-carbon neighbourhood that inspires future housing in York.

"I would like to congratulate all the winners in the respective categories as the entries this year were top class.

"A special thanks goes to our volunteer committee members, sponsors and partners who all help to make this the special event it has now become."

Located in Burton Stone Lane, Duncombe Square comprises 23 terraced houses and 11 one-bed apartments.

The development includes 21 homes available for social rent or through shared ownership schemes.

The awards, now in their 18th year, recognise architectural excellence in York.

The full list of winners includes:

- 99 East Parade and 14 Marygate (Single dwelling)
- Duncombe Square and Rialto House (Multi-residential)
- Askham College teaching block and 105–110 Micklegate (Non-residential)
- Station Hall, National Railway Museum, and 77 Walmgate (Heritage)
- 60 Goodramgate (John Shannon Conservation Award)
- Duncombe Square (Sustainability Award)
- An extension on Marygate (Young People’s Award)

Other prominent winners included Rialto House in Fishergate, the National Railway Museum’s Central Hall refurbishment, and Askham Bryan College’s teaching block.

The York Mix People’s Choice Award, voted for by the public, went to the medieval-inspired hotel at 105 Micklegate.

The Young People’s Choice Award, voted for by under-18s, went to a residential extension on Marygate by ArkleBoyce architects.

Last year’s winners included York Minster’s Centre of Excellence for Heritage Skills, York Racecourse, and the Cocoa Works.

Sponsors for this year's awards included O’Neill Associates, York Civic Trust, Simpson & Brown, York Conservation Trust, YorHUB, and York Architectural Association.

The York Design Awards continue to celebrate projects that demonstrate innovation, sustainability, and respect for the city’s character and historical legacy.
