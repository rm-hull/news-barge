People in Yorkshire are being urged to save water during the latest heatwave.

Yorkshire Water is calling on customers to reduce usage as temperatures are expected to reach 30°C across the region.

Andy Shaw, director of control, production and process at Yorkshire Water, said: "We’re experiencing another heatwave in the region this week and we’re urging customers to think carefully about their water use.

"In the last heatwave, we saw water usage increase by 190 million litres during the highest temperatures - which is what we supply to Leeds and Harrogate combined on a daily basis."

Latest research reveals only 15 per cent of adults in Yorkshire and the Humber understand how much they use, with most underestimating daily consumption by around 96 litres.

The Let’s Save Water campaign aims to raise awareness of water scarcity and promote everyday changes to reduce consumption.

Professor Lizzie Kendon, strategic head of climate processes and projections at the Met Office, said: "More rainfall doesn’t automatically mean more usable water.

"Climate change is driving increasingly extreme weather patterns, with wetter winters, drier summers, and more intense bursts of rainfall.

"This growing imbalance is placing mounting pressure on our water resources, and there is an urgent need for action."
