## Mystery surrounds 'private' family funeral of Sam Smith's boss Humphrey Smith

Humphrey Smith *(Image: Newsquest)*

The funeral of Humphrey Smith, the former chairman of Sam Smith’s Brewery, has already taken place, the Press has been told.

Reflecting the private nature of the businessman, the event took place without any fanfare or public announcement.

The Tadcaster-based brewery did not respond to several requests from The Press for any information on Humphrey Smith’s funeral.

A close, family friend also told the Press he knew nothing.

However, another source told the Press that the funeral took place “very soon after he died.”

“It was a very small ceremony, for close family only, just as he wanted.”

However, our source declined to say which church staged the ceremony or the actual date.

But Humphrey Smith and his family are believed to have attended church in the Tadcaster area.

St Mary’s Church Tadcaster posted on Facebook on July 1: “Following the death of Mr Humphrey Smith, St Mary's Church and the whole of the Tadcaster Benefice extend their sincere condolences to his family and friends.

“Our prayers and thoughts remain very much with them at this time, and we hope that they will be given the space and time they need by the community to be able to grieve.

  Humphrey Smith, who died last week, aged 81. His funeral has already taken place. 

*(Image: SWNS)*

“We also hope that all of the residents of Tadcaster and the surrounding area will show the greatest of kindness and respect at this difficult time.

“May he rest in peace.”

Humphrey Smith died on Monday June 29, aged 81.

The cause of his death has never been given, though it was well reported he had been ill for some time.

However, it was also reported Mr Smith would still visit the brewery offices in Tadcaster and until recently, he was also a regular at the Tadcaster community swimming pool, which he supported financially, gifting the land, to make it happen.

Sam Smith’s Brewery has not responded to any requests for comment or tribute from The Press, though it did confirm his death verbally over the phone to us on Tuesday June 30 as his death was being reported on social media sites that comment on the brewery.

As the Press has reported, opinion over Mr Smith’s legacy has varied, with some Tadcaster people praising his charitable efforts in the town, usually done in private.

  The Angel and White Horse Inn, next to the brewery.

Others, however, raise the matter of the empty pubs owned by the brewery across the country, in addition to empty properties he owned in Tadcaster.

His policies, which were strictly enforced, included banning dogs from pubs, banning children from pubs (unless they are dining) and most controversially, banning digital devices.

His son Sam has run the brewery's pubs in London to much success for several years, with such rules less strictly enforced. Mr Smith’s critics see this as a template for Sam to roll-out nationally, now he is reported to have taken over the whole business.

However, as the Press has also reported, such policies, though unique to the sector, have much support from drinkers who like the traditional nature of Sam Smith’s pubs.
