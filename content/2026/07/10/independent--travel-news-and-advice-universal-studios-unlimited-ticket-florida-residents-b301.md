Universal Studios theme park just offered a huge deal to Florida residents, giving them an opportunity for unlimited fun from now until December.

Florida residents can now go to Universal Studios and its sister park, Islands of Adventure, as many times as they want this summer and fall with the “2-Park Unlimited Days Park-to-Park” ticket.

The unlimited ticket for adult Florida residents costs $199, excluding tax. A regular one-day adult ticket to both parks, Universal Studios and Islands of Adventure, costs at least $179.99, excluding tax.

“It’s good to be a Florida Resident!” the blurb on Universal Orlando Resort’s website reads. “Visit Universal Studios Florida and Universal Islands of Adventure as often as you want with a Park-to-Park ticket, now through Dec. 16, 2026. Whether it’s summer days or holidays, thrills are waiting.”

![Universal Studios theme park just offered a huge deal to Florida residents, giving them an opportunity for unlimited fun from now until December](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/05/21/18/18/iStock-458701663.jpeg)

To buy an unlimited ticket, Floridians will need to provide proof of residency, according to Fox 35 Orlando.

*The Independent* has reached out to Universal Orlando Resort for comment. 

Ticketholders can experience Diagon Alley in The Wizarding World of Harry Potter, Illumination’s Minion Land, DreamWorks Land, The Simpsons’ Springfield and more at Universal Studios.

Islands of Adventure features eight lands, including Jurassic Park, Marvel Super Hero Island and Seuss Landing.

![The deal, which costs $199, includes unlimited access to Universal Studios and its sister park, Islands of Adventure](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/09/1/40/iStock-522793608.jpg)

The deal does not include admission into Universal Orlando Resorts’ other two parks, Epic Universe or Volcano Bay. Separately ticketed events such as Halloween Horror Nights are also excluded from the promotion, according to Fox 35 Orlando.

But some special events that ticketholders can look forward to are Grinchmas and Christmas in The Wizarding World of Harry Potter, which start on November 14, the local outlet reported.

This isn’t the first time Universal Orlando Resort has offered an unlimited deal to locals.

In 2024, the Orlando resort offered Florida residents a ticket to visit Universal Studios and Islands of Adventure any time they wanted for months.

For Florida residents who are not planning to visit the theme parks that often, Universal Orlando Resort is giving away a second day free with the purchase of a “1-Day Park-to-Park” ticket for Universal Studios and Islands of Adventure.
