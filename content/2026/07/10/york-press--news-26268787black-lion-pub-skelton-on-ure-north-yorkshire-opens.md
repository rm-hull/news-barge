## Community-owned pub in North Yorkshire reopens

The community-owned pub re-opened last night after being closed since May. *(Image: PA)*

A community-owned village pub in North Yorkshire is back open.

The Black Lion at Skelton-on-Ure, between Ripon and Boroughbridge, re-opened last night (Thursday, July 9) at 5pm.

A post on social media said: “The Beer garden looks beautiful and awaits your company.

“The cafe re opens next week (17th) put it in your diary….. the show stopper cakes will be returning!"

“We are really looking forward to seeing you.”

The Press rang the pub just after 5pm last night but was told the pub was ‘too busy’ for anyone to comment.

  The Black Lion in Skelton-on-Ure

A second Facebook post this morning said: “Wow wow wow!!! what an amazing night we had last night!

“Thank you so much for coming to our opening night. If you couldn’t make it fear not we are open all weekend. Friday from 5pm, Saturday from 3pm and Sunday from 1pm.”

The Press understands it is the community interest company (CIC) that owns the pub that is actually running it now.

Previously, the Skelton Black Lion Pub and Hub was run by tenants.

The former tenants Tadgh Berry and Mary Benson ran the pub and hub from last November until May this year to much acclaim.

However, they left “with very heavy hearts” in May saying the business model was “unsustainable.”
