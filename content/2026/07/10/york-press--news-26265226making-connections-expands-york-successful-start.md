A networking group in York has announced plans to expand following its first four successful networking events.

Making Connections was launched by the York City Football Club Foundation, The Family Fund, Azets, and Wilkin Chapman Rollits to foster relationships between businesses, particularly those based around Monks Cross, Vangarde, and Huntington.

The group has been hosting bi-monthly events since November, including business breakfasts and activity-based sessions designed to engage business leaders and organisations of all sizes.

Paula Stainton, CEO of the YCFC Foundation, said: "We are very excited for our next event as it has a proper Yorkshire theme with a Yorkshire Quiz, Flat Cap hunt, refreshments and cream tea, alongside the key opportunity to network.

"We just couldn’t let Yorkshire Day pass without a proper Yorkshire celebration and also what a great way to end the week.

"This event is being hosted by our friends at Bo Concept, Monks Cross on Friday 31st July 4-6pm."

Gill Bell, events and community fundraiser for The Family Fund, said the events also serve a broader purpose.

She said: "What is unique about Making Connections events is that these regular events, also raise vital funds for the two charity organisations involved, with £500 raised for the two charity organisers, as well as making over 100 connections at previous events."

The Family Fund supports families raising disabled or seriously ill children, while the YCFC Foundation works to improve lives through community engagement and promoting active, healthy living.

As the group continues to grow, event organisers have expanded the team to reflect a wider geographical reach, now including All Aspects Travel in Haxby to support engagement with businesses in North East York and surrounding areas.

Organisers encourage anyone interested in connecting with like-minded professionals to book onto upcoming events and follow the group’s Eventbrite page for future updates and notifications.
