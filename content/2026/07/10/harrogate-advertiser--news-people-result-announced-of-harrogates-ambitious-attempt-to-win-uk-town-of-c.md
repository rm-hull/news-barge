# Result announced of Harrogate's ambitious attempt to win UK Town of Culture 2028 title

## The 15 towns shortlisted in the inaugural UK Town of Culture 2028 competition have been revealed and the news is bad for Harrogate.

Following the launch of the inaugural UK Town of Culture competition last year, the Government confirmed that it had received 398 bids from more than 400 hopeful towns as part of the competition.

The rivals facing Harrogate for the title and a substantial £3 million prize included everyone from Scarborough to Stornoway, Loughborough to Hemel Hempstead.

Fifteen towns will now compete for the inaugural UK Town of Culture 2028.

The shortlisted towns are:

Small towns: Ilfracombe, Isle of Bute, Lerwick, Sandown, Strabane, and Stockton Town Centre Ward.

Medium towns: Corby, Great Yarmouth, Leith, Pontypridd, and Port Talbot.

Large towns: Basildon, Birkenhead, Grimsby and Rotherham.

It was only in March that Harrogate launched its bid to win the UK Town of Culture 2028 contest and a potential £3 million payout.

The aim of Harrogate’s passionately mounted bid was to create a year of "joyful, visible and participatory culture”, bringing activity into streets, parks and public spaces across Harrogate.

Had the attempt been successful, Harrogate’s green spaces, including Valley Gardens, Crescent Gardens and the Stray, which celebrates its 250th anniversary in 2028, would have provided distinctive settings for outdoor cultural celebration alongside activity in cultural venues, community spaces and the town centre

Shortlisted towns will now receive £60,000 each to develop their full bids, which will be assessed by the independent judging panel.

One finalist will then be selected from each category with the overall winner being announced early next year.

Among the other towns who joined Harrogate in the contest but had their hopes dashed were Ripon, Helmsley, Halifax, Barnsley, Keighley, Richmond, Whitby, Scarborough, Alnwick, Jarrow, Hastings, Loughborough and Stornoway.

The UK Town of Culture competition is part of the Government’s ambition to restore pride in local areas in every part of Britain and deliver a decade of national renewal.
