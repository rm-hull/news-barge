A POST Office in York is set to close next month after a major supermarket unveiled plans to shut around 100 of its convenience stores.

Woodthorpe Post Office is housed within Morrisons Daily in Moorcroft Road.

It is one of at least 52 branches set to disappear on Saturday (August 1).

The move follows the supermarket’s decision to close around 100 “under-performing” convenience stores – most of which were sold as part of its £190 million deal with McColl’s in 2022.

  


Announcing the store closures in May, Morrisons said the shops had been unprofitable for years and that the situation had been made worse by significant cost increases linked to government policy decisions.

It described the cuts and associated job losses as a “tough but necessary decision”.

The Post Office is understood to be looking for replacement shops that could host services in affected communities across England, Scotland and Wales.

Despite this, a representative confirmed in an email to local ward councillors last month that no "viable options" remained for Woodthorpe, with requests to keep the store open until a temporary or permanent home was found denied.

The statement read: "We have carefully considered all comments and suggestions received during the consultation period.

"Unfortunately, there are no viable options to achieve even a break even position and the store will close on 01 August 2026."

According to Better Retailing, the closures, and those in TG Jones stores could put the Post Office network close to falling below the Government's minimum requirement of 11,500 branches.

The Post Office has been contacted by The Press for comment.
