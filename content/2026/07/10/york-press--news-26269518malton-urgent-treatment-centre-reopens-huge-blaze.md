## Investigations into major fire at Malton Hospital as site begins to reopen

An aerial view of Malton Hospital following the fire

INVESTIGATIONS into a major fire are continuing as a North Yorkshire hospital begins to reopen services.

Malton Hospital reopened the Malton Urgent Treatment Centre today(Friday), however the main building currently remains closed.

Firefighters had worked through the night to extinguish the fire that started at around 11.30am on Monday and ripped through the hospital's Springfield unit.

Fire investigation teams have since been on site to establish the cause of the blaze.

Fifteen patients, along with staff, were evacuated from the hospital and taken to nearby Malton School.

The York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust, which runs Malton Hospital, said: "We're pleased to confirm that Malton Urgent Treatment Centre has now reopened and patients can attend as normal.

"The main hospital remains temporarily closed while work continues to safely restore services.

"If you have an appointment at Malton Hospital, please do not attend unless you have been contacted and advised to do so.

"Patients who require a blood test are asked to attend the Community Diagnostic Centre at Eastfield, Scarborough, or York Community Stadium until further notice.

"Thank you to our colleagues, emergency services, partner organisations, and the local community for your continued support, patience and understanding."

Cllr Lindsay Burr, North Yorkshire councillor for Malton, said she had been in close touch with North Yorkshire's Environment and Transport Services at North Yorkshire Council and York and Scarborough Teaching Hospital.

She said: "I would first like to extend my sincere thanks to all the emergency teams who arrived at the scene so quickly and all the business residents and Malton School for all their help during this very alarming fire in our town.

"As we all know the fire started at Springwood. A dementia care unit, this unit belongs to the Tees Esk and Wear Valley NHS Foundation Trust.

"The Fitzwilliam ward, where patients were evacuated from, belongs to the York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust and suffered four broken windows and smoke damage."

Cllr Burr added: "15 patients were on Fitzwilliam ward and of those13 patients were helped to Malton School and two discharged themselves to go home

"Those patients were then transported to Bridlington Hospital, along with members of staff.

"Huge thanks must also go to the school for there help."

Joe Hague, Chief Nurse, York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust, thanked everyone involved for their help and support.

He said: “One of the things that will stay with me from that day was the incredible way the local community rallied around us.

"Staff from the local school stepped in to prepare meals for patients while we worked through the immediate response, local supermarkets donated food and bottled water, and partner organisations offered support without hesitation. It was a wonderful reminder of what community really means.

"Moments like this also remind us of what the NHS is all about. Every day, our colleagues work together to provide outstanding care, often without recognition, but in a crisis that teamwork, compassion and professionalism become visible for everyone to see.

"I couldn't be prouder of how our staff responded. I also want to recognise that this was a very difficult and emotional experience for many of the colleagues involved. They remained calm, cared for our patients and supported one another throughout, and we're incredibly grateful and proud of everything they did."

A spokesperson for North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue said: "Crews worked through the night on Wednesday to extinguish the fire and checked for hotspots. "The cause of the fire is still under investigation at this time."
