Calls are growing for a stronger national approach to wildfire prevention and recovery.

Scarborough and Whitby MP Alison Hume is launching a cross-party parliamentary group on Wildfire Prevention in response to last summer's major blaze on the North York Moors.

The group will unite MPs and peers to explore policies to improve resilience and support communities affected by wildfires.

Ms Hume said: "Last summer's wildfire showed just how devastating these incidents can be, not only for our environment but for the people and communities left to deal with the consequences long after the flames have been extinguished.

"My colleagues and I want this APPG to become a strong voice for wildfire prevention and resilience, while also making the case for better support for those still living with the consequences of these disasters."

The fire was the most significant ever experienced on the North York Moors, burning across up to 25 square kilometres of moorland and threatening RAF Fylingdales.

Ms Hume has since met regularly with farmers and gamekeepers who helped protect homes and land.

She added: “All-Party Parliamentary Groups are uniquely placed to bring together politicians, experts, charities, emergency services and communities to drive meaningful change.

"The fact that this fire threatened RAF Fylingdales underlines the seriousness of what happened."
