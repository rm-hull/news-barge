A MAJOR road linking York to East Yorkshire and Hull has started melting in the summer heat.

The A1079 as it approaches the new A164 Jock's Lodge roundabout outside Beverley is affected by old bitumen - the "glue" for keeping roads together - has been seeping to the surface, the county council said.

  


East Riding of Yorkshire Council said the affected area is not part of the new £86.9m upgrade which began in January 2024 and is scheduled for completion in late 2026.

Gritters were spreading sand on the affected areas last night and repairs were scheduled yesterday evening (Thursday, July 9th).

The road remained open but drivers were advised to avoid the area.

The £86.9m A164/Jock’s Lodge upgrade near Beverley is East Riding Council’s largest road scheme, adding new link roads, a grade‑separated junction and dualling. Main works began January 2024 and are scheduled to complete in late 2026, easing congestion and improving safety on the A164/A1079 corridor.
