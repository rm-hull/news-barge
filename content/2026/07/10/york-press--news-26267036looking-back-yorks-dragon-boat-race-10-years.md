## On this day: York Dragon Boat Race raised £63,000 for charity

On this day 10 years ago, Dragon Boat teams battled it out for worthy causes along the River Ouse 

On this day 10 years ago, a vibrant display of colourful outfits and an outpouring of grit and determination took place on the River Ouse as Dragon Boat teams battled it out for worthy causes.

The banks of the river were packed for a day of drum-beating and paddle-waving as passionate crews raced against each other over a short stretch of water to take home the top prize.

Some teams donned multi-coloured Mohawk wigs to stand out from the crowds, while others applied their war paint for one of the city’s fun days.

Four teams from across the region raced each other between Scarborough Bridge and Lendal Bridge for the 14th consecutive year, with 20 people packed into a boat to race for victory.

The drum-beating and team spirit made the event stand out as one of the city’s most memorable days for those who took part.

The main winner was a team from the Fourth Battalion, the Yorkshire Regiment, while Escrick Playing Field Association were crowned the Best Dressed Team.

Eileen Davis, president of organisers York Rotary, said: “The weather was very kind and it was a wonderful atmosphere.

"We have raised more than £63,000 with everyone’s sponsorship.

“It was a great day, but they are always a bit different because we have different teams, but everyone had lots of fun."

The charities supported were the Family Fund York and The Island York.
