A comedian is encouraging families to swap screens for stories this summer.

Sara Pascoe has teamed up with LNER, Northern, Southeastern and TransPennine Express to support The Reading Agency’s Summer Reading Challenge.

Schoolchildren were treated to pop-up reading sessions on trains between Leeds, York and London as part of the campaign launch.

Train company staff will distribute 65,000 free activity packs in the coming months.

  Packs include a copy of Storytime Magazine, a sticker booklet, an activity sheet, and a certificate 

*(Image: Northern)*

Ms Pascoe said: "I've always loved reading when I'm travelling.

"It has a brilliant way of making the journey feel like part of the adventure rather than just the bit in between.

"It was such a joy to surprise families on the train, share stories together and see children getting excited about the books."

The packs include a copy of Storytime Magazine, a sticker booklet, an activity sheet and a certificate.

They are designed to inspire children aged four to 11 to read during the summer holidays.

Research from The Reading Agency shows 64 per cent of parents are worried about children being bored during the summer holidays.

More than two-thirds feel the rising cost of activities adds pressure.

The Reading Agency’s Karen Napier MBE said the scheme turns every journey into "an opportunity for imagination, creativity and shared family moments."
