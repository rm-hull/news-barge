### What is the Public Notice Portal?

The Public Notice Portal carries statutory public notices published in local newspapers and is the fastest and most effective way of finding out what is happening in YOUR neighbourhood.

Search the Public Notice PortalSeveral North Yorkshire roads will face temporary closures for up to six months over the summer.

The temporary closures will be in place to allow for access kerbing and utility works.

In Pickering, a one-week closure will affect five roads—Hungate, Cuddy Brown Close, Firthland Road, Middleton Road and Pike Road—sometime between July 13 and December 31.

In Malton, Newgate will be closed for a two-week period between July 14 and December 31 for kerbing work.

Middleton Carr Lane in Middleton will be closed between July 27 and August 7 for gas main replacement work carried out by Northern Gas Networks, though the order allows for the closure to be extended until August 14.

Lodge Road in Church Houses will be closed between July 27 and September 11 for essential works carried out by United Infrastructure.

While the closure is set for this period, the order permits the restriction to remain in force until September 30 if required.

Public notices relating to the road closures have been issued under the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984.

They propose a temporary prohibition of traffic on the affected roads and outline the timeframes during which closures may be implemented.

The notices also state that, where appropriate, diversion routes will be clearly signed during the periods of closure, and access to premises will be maintained for residents and businesses.

For further details, members of the public can refer to the official notices, which are available to view on the public notice portal. Further updates and information can also be accessed via the North Yorkshire Council roadworks map at: northyorks.gov.uk/roadworks-map.
