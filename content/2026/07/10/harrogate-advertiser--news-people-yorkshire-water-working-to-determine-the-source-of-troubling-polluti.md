# Yorkshire Water 'working to determine the source of troubling pollution' at well-known Harrogate spot

## New information has come to light about the mysterious appearance of strange coloured water at a well-known water course in Harrogate.

Following the Harrogate Advertiser’s publication of a reader’s troubling photographs, there were fears about possible water pollution at Crimple Beck, a lengthy tributary of the River Nidd which flows through the Harrogate area and has a history of pollution problems.

Recent years have seen Yorkshire Water come under pressure from local politicians, community groups and charities for its record in the Harrogate area over sewage spills and polluted water.

In this case, Yorkshire Dales Rivers Trust, told the Harrogate Advertiser, the bright green water emitting from a Crimple Beck outfall is tracing dye, which has been added to determine the source of the ongoing and concerning pollution the charity identified at the outfall.

That, the environmental charity said, indicates that Yorkshire Water are now working to determine the source of pollution which the charity had previously alerted the water company to.

"The surface water network is part of the UK’s drainage infrastructure and is designed to manage excess surface water from our roads and pavements during periods of heavy rainfall, preventing cities and towns from becoming flooded,” said a spokesperson for Yorkshire Dales Rivers Trust, which is based in Pateley Bridge.

“Due to our old, complex sewage system, misconnections exist in our drainage infrastructure, wrongly connecting toxic wastewater and sewage from households and businesses and directing it into our nearest beck.

"The outfall in the reader’s photo is exactly that, a surface water outfall.

![One of the photographs taken by a reader of the strange coloured water at Crimple Beck in Harrogate. (Picture contributed)](https://www.harrogateadvertiser.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOmJiYTA0NGY1LTg2NDktNDZkNi1hNDA1LTY0YmFhZmI0OWVjZjo2YTRkZDg5My1hNjg5LTRlNTAtYmNiMy1lY2ViOThkMTg0YzU=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



"The charity has used ammonia and phosphate checkers to test for pollutants during dry weather, when the outfall should be running dry, with results reading high for both contaminants.”

Earlier this year, Yorkshire Water launched a £15 million upgrade to the wastewater infrastructure in Harrogate, part of a £8.3 billion improvement programme to reduce discharges from storm overflows and improve the health of watercourses across the county.

The Yorkshire Dales Rivers Trust was founded in 2004 that works to enhance, protect, and restore the rivers and catchments of the Yorkshire Dales.
