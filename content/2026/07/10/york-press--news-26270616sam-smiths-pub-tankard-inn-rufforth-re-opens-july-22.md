## Re-opening date announced for Sam Smith's village pub near York

Paula Dawson outside The Tankard in Rufforth *(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

The opening date of the only pub in a village near York can now be revealed.

Paula Dawson has taken on the Tankard Inn, a Sam Smith’s Brewery-owned pub at Rufforth.

Paula, who also runs the Paula’s Kitchen café at Mill Lane, Rufforth, will re-open the pub on Wednesday July 22.

The pub has been closed since September 2023.

Paula told the Press: “My training with the brewery is all done, the beers are ordered, everything is ordered. I am raring to go!”.

At present, workmen and other contractors are in checking the cellars, electrics and so on, to ensure everything is ready for the big day.

The plan is to open at 12-noon on the Wednesday, if all goes to plan.

The only thing Paula still needs is some staff, to cover 12 hours a week in the bar and 27 hours in the kitchen.

  Paula Dawson outside The Tankard in Rufforth 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

Paula will be running the place by herself, while her husband runs Paula’s Kitchen.

The move appears to be a shift from Sam Smith’s Brewery typically seeking management couples to run its pub.

It comes as the brewery is also stepping up its efforts to re-open its pubs- around half of the 300 or so brewery-owned pubs have been listed by Malton man Tom Taverner as closed.

Contractors have also been sighted at several Sam Smith’s pubs across Yorkshire, suggesting other re-openings could be imminent.

The Wellington Inn in Alma Street, Fishergate, is said to be re-opening later this month. It has been widely reported that a couple from Pontefract will be its management couple.
