An East Yorkshire town crier has died after almost 30 years of service.

Michael Wood served as the town crier for East Riding of Yorkshire Council since 1997 when he won the ceremonial role in a competition.

The council’s chair, Cllr Linda Johnson, described him as a “tremendous town crier” who will be “sorely missed”.

Mr Wood, dressed in his green and gold robes and tricorn hat, became a familiar face at events across the county.

He would attend council meetings, agricultural shows, open days and celebrations.

Paying tribute, Cllr Johnson said: “Michael has been a tremendous town crier and has become synonymous with the East Riding.

“He opened and attended so many events and was always enthusiastic and smiling.

“He will be sorely missed and our thoughts are with his family and friends at this time.”

Mr Wood was named the best town crier in Britain in 2012 – and the best dressed.

  He took part in that year’s **Diamond Jubilee** British Town Crier Championships in Hebden Bridge and mentioned how East Yorkshire was the “chosen county” of artist David Hockney, who died last month aged 88.

Speaking after scooping both awards in 2012, Mr Wood said: “I guess by now you could say I have become a face which rings a bell.”

Then East Riding of Yorkshire Council leader Stephen Parnaby said Mr Wood had helped raise the county’s profile “and is always a valuable and popular addition to any event, civic and social”.
