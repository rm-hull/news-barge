## Hundreds of schoolchildren take part in North York Moors education programme

Nearly 500 pupils from 14 schools took part in Let’s Learn Moor, held over three days on the North York Moors *(Image: The North York Moors Moorland Organisation)*

Hundreds of children have explored the outdoors during a major upland education event.

Nearly 500 pupils from 14 schools took part in Let’s Learn Moor, held over three days on the North York Moors on July 6, 7 and 8.

The hands-on programme taught pupils about conservation, rural land management, and the people who work on the moors, including gamekeepers, farmers, and emergency services.

  The hands-on programme taught pupils about conservation and rural land management 

*(Image: The North York Moors Moorland Organisation)*

Curtis Mossop, head of training, education and outreach at BASC, said: "There is something truly special about watching hundreds of children step off the bus, their faces lighting up as they take in the vast landscape before them.

"Those moments perfectly capture what Let's Learn Moor is all about: connecting young people with the uplands in a way that inspires curiosity, appreciation and lasting memories."

Many of the pupils were visiting managed moorland for the first time.

  Pupils learned about the people who work on the moors, such as gamekeepers, farmers, and emergency services 

*(Image: The North York Moors Moorland Organisation)*

Tina Brough, coordinator of the North York Moors Moorland Organisation, said: "Seeing young people engage with the countryside, ask questions and develop a greater understanding of the environment makes all the hard work worthwhile."

The project, which takes place between June 29 and July 10, is overseen by BASC and supported by more than 50 partners.

For more information about Let’s Learn Moor, visit: [https://basc.org.uk/lets-learn-moor/](https://basc.org.uk/lets-learn-moor/)
