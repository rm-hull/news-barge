## PayPal vs Other Casino Payment Methods

PayPal isn’t the only fast payment option available at casino sites, which provide a selection of banking methods to customers. Here’s how PayPal compares to some of the most popular payment methods.

- **PayPal vs Skrill/Neteller:** Skrill and Neteller are two of the biggest e-wallet rivals to PayPal, and there are several Neteller and Skrill casinos on the market. However, there are still a good number of casinos that won’t accept Skrill or Neteller but take PayPal, while those that do can often exclude payments from either provider when qualifying for the welcome bonus.
- **PayPal vs Apple Pay:** Apple Pay is the fastest-growing payment method among online casinos, and in many cases, it can match PayPal for deposit limits. It also offers high levels of security, like PayPal, but where the two differ is Apple Pay can’t be used on Android devices. More and more casinos are starting to accept Google Pay, but PayPal is still more widely available and can be easily used on both iOS and Android.
- **PayPal vs Pay by Mobile:** A pay by mobile casino lets bettors make deposits by adding them to their mobile phone bill, but has plenty of shortcomings when compared to PayPal, including smaller deposit limits and the difficulty of completing withdrawals.
- **PayPal vs debit card:** A debit card is still the most widely accepted payment method at online casinos and can offer smaller deposit amounts or larger withdrawals than PayPal. But PayPal still has the edge when it comes to security, as punters don’t need to share bank details with a casino, while withdrawals are often quicker using PayPal.
- **PayPal vs bank transfer:** Bank transfers have been greatly improved by services like Pay By Bank, which has drastically reduced the wait time for withdrawals without compromising on security. Some online casinos have pivoted away from PayPal in order to focus on instant bank transfers, and if PayPal isn’t available at an online casino, this is a strong option.
