Search the Public Notice PortalThe East Riding of Yorkshire Council has announced that a road will soon be closed to install new broadband infrastructure.

Middle Lane in Skirpenbeck will be temporarily shut as work begins on the installation of the BDUK fibre network.

The closure, beginning at 8am on August 3, will affect the stretch heading east from its junction with Main Street, then right onto Brickyards Lane, Bugthorpe, right onto A166 Bridlington Road, Full Sutton, right onto Dolegate, Skirpenbeck, then right onto Main Street, Skirpenbeck.

The road will be closed until 5pm daily until August 7.

The work is being carried out by Quickline Communications and is expected to be completed within five days.

The closure is to ensure the safety of the public during the work.

An alternative route will be signposted, taking drivers east from the junction with Main Street, then right onto Brickyards Lane, Bugthorpe, right onto A166 Bridlington Road, Full Sutton, right onto Dolegate, Skirpenbeck, then right onto Main Street, Skirpenbeck.

Access will still be maintained for residents, emergency vehicles, and pedestrians.

The work is being carried out by East Riding of Yorkshire Council under the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984, Section 14 (1) Temporary Prohibition of Through Traffic for part of Middle Lane, Skirpenbeck.

The order for the closure is valid for 18 months, but the work is expected to be completed within five days.

The East Riding of Yorkshire Council has stated that the road closure is necessary to ensure the safety of the public while the work is being carried out.

The fibre network installation is part of a broader initiative to improve internet connectivity in the area.

Residents seeking further information can contact the council's Highways Customer Care on 01482 393939, quoting reference CB.
