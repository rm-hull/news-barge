## 'Impressive transformation' of York cemetery which was facing 'dismal outlook'

Fulford Cemetery, in Fordlands Road, York, in June 2025 *(Image: City of York Council)*

THE transformation of York’s main cemetery a year on from finance and maintenance woes has been praised as changes to the way it is run were approved.

City of York Council’s Executive backed the permanent take over of the running of Fulford Cemetery, in Fordlands Road, on Tuesday, July 7.

Fulford Parish Council Clerk Rachel Robinson told the executive their operation of the site had been unsustainable due to lacking the necessary resources.

Labour executive member Cllr Kate Ravilious, whose Fulford and Heslington ward covers the cemetery, said it was incredible to see the turnaround a year after the council first stepped in.

Tuesday’s decision comes after York Council first took over the management of the site temporarily in August 2025.

It followed mounting financial losses which totalled £102,451 in 2023/4 and 2024/5, as people look to cut funeral costs including by moving away from burials and opting for cremations.

Volunteers stepped in last year after the state of the cemetery, which had become overgrown, sparked a backlash including negative comments online.

A council report stated budget constraints meant it could not be maintained to the standards expected by the public.

A rise in burials and changes made since the council agreed to temporarily run it resulted in the cemetery making a profit of £30,733.36 in 2025/6.

The executive heard on Tuesday site services had stabilised, standards had improved and the cemetery financial surpluses had returned since the council stepped in.

  Fulford Cemetery, in Fordlands Road, York, in June 2026 

*(Image: City of York Council)*

Fulford Parish Council Clerk Ms Robinson said the site was the only cemetery in the country managed by such an authority.

She added the benefits of the professional support they had received from York Council since last year were evident.

The clerk said: “It’s been clear for some time that a city-wide operation such as Fulford Cemetery requires city level resources.

“The parish council currently consists of seven volunteer councillors, one part-time clerk and two grounds staff.

“In the past couple of years it’s become apparent that the provision of burial services at Fulford with only the resources available to a parish council has become unsustainable.

“The volunteers have been extremely valuable in helping with maintenance.”

Transport Executive Member and Fulford and Heslington’s Cllr Ravilious said the situation facing Fulford Cemetery a year ago had been really challenging.

The executive member said: “It’s a really unusual situation to have a parish council managing such a huge city cemetery, they were in a financially difficult position and were unable to manage it in the way it needed to be.

“The grounds had become overgrown and residents and visitors were distressed when visiting their loved ones.

“The transformation’s been impressive and it’s really great to see it heading in the right direction.”

Cllr Claire Douglas, the council’s Labour leader, said she hoped people would be able to see the positive benefits.

The council leader said: “Last year during August it was a dismal outlook and we were trying to understand how this could be turned around, we now have a viable cemetery again and it’s given a lot of people a lot of confidence.”
