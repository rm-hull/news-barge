*The Pitt* has become the award-winning breakout TV hit of the moment, but the show is making a tweak to its winning formula in the next season.

Noah Wyle, who plays lead character Dr Michael “Robby” Robinavitch, is currently co-writing and starring in season three of the medical drama, set in a busy Pittsburgh emergency room.

However, he said that, while the first two seasons were set in the present day, raising awareness of topical subjects ranging from vaccine conspiracy theories, gun ownership and the influx of AI, season three will move the needle back slightly.

![Noah Wyle in 'The Pitt'](https://static.the-independent.com/2026/03/27/12/34/Screenshot-2026-03-27-at-12-27-05.png)

The new season, which will premiere in January 2027, will be set two months before, on 12 November 2026.

“Up until now, we've plotted our show almost in real time when people are watching,” Wyle told *Deadline*.

'We've written the shows eight months before we air, but we've written them to take place in the time and place in which they are being watched. We didn't do that this season.”

He said that, for the first time, the show will reference events that have already happened, “and not where we’re going”.

Wyle said this decision stemmed from the “anticipation and fear and preparation going into January and the executive of the Big Beautiful Bill that will have a lot of ramifications in Americans' lives”.

The bill – signed by US President Donald Trump – is designed to slash taxes and will lead to cuts in funding within the healthcare arena, as well as in the food assistance and energy sectors.

As a result, the new season of*The Pitt* will focus on “what most people are going to have to do to prepare themselves for a very harsh reality in the next seven to eight years, to underscore the point of how desperate this particular moment in time is”.

*The Pitt* is the most nominated show at this year’s Emmys, which take place in September. It received 25 nods in total, including for Wyle and his co-stars Katherine LaNasa, Sepideh Moafi, Fiona Dourif, Taylor Dearden, Gerran Howell, Patrick Ball and Shawn Hatosy.

Another change in season three will be the departure of Supriya Ganesh, whose senior resident Dr Samira Mohan controversially won’t be a part of the action. There will also be a location switch up for Victoria Javadi (Shabana Azeez), who’ll move from the ER to a psychiatry rotation.

Speaking about his future on *The Pitt*, Wyle told* The Independent*: “I’m committed, not only contractually, but also emotionally, to exploring this for several more seasons. I have felt that we’re doing a hospital show that has embedded into it a very specific mental health journey of one individual, and we’re not even to the halfway point of that journey. So I’m really curious and committed to seeing it through.”
