AROUND eight acres of moorland was destroyed in a wildfire involving multiple agencies yesterday afternoon (Thursday).

Crews from Kirkby Moorside, Helmsley, Northallerton and Stokesley were called at 1.08pm to the incident at Chop Gate.

North York Moors Moorland Organisation (NYMMO) said their members had also been altered.

"Within a very short space of time there were 26 gamekeepers, from nine different estates, estate staff, six farmers and multiple fire crews.

"A massive haul of equipment including four Argo Cats, four tractors, two Polaris, three Quad Bikes, water bowsers, slurry tanker and two to three fire engines at any one time were used to try and bring the fire under control.

"By 3pm yesterday the face and the sides of the fire were out, but the peat is continuing to smoulder and could flare up again at any time."

  


"The fire crews have now stood down from the scene and the gamekeepers will remain on site for the rest of today at least, watching and dampening down any hot spots, they are using thermal drones throughout the area to do this and these will hopefully help them keep on top of any flare ups.

"The total of the area burnt out is looking to be about eight acres, this is a large amount but could have been catastrophically worse should the fire not have been on an area where controlled burning and fire breaks were in place, just look at the huge devastating fire on the North York Moors last year which took place at Fylingdales."

The NYMMO has issued the following advice to people.

"Please - no barbecues or camp fires - the moor is tinder dry - use common sense and please appreciate and Support the need for controlled burns to protect our moorland - without this the outcome here would have been severely worse.

"Thank you to all that attended, helped and offered assistance we really are a team."
