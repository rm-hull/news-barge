AN immersive family event combining film and live music is coming to York, featuring some of the best-known examples of British animation now known across the globe.

Hosted at York Barbican, the one-day show Aardman in Concert will take place on August 29, 2026, and marks 50 years of the British animation studio behind some of the nation’s most beloved characters and winner of four Oscars.

Audiences will be treated to a celebration of five decades of Aardman’s creativity, featuring Wallace & Gromit, Shaun the Sheep, Morph, Creature Comforts, and more.

  


  Wallace & Gromit are now a British institution and millions have tuned in to watch their capers and antics 

*(Image: Supplied)*

The show blends classic film moments with a live orchestral performance, offering an experience for both long-time fans and newcomers.

Rachel Whibley, managing director of production partner Carrot Productions, said: "Celebrating 50 years of Aardman with a live orchestra feels incredibly special.

"These are characters many of us grew up with, and now we're seeing families bring their own children to experience them live.

"York is a city with such a rich cultural heritage and a strong tradition of welcoming audiences for live performance, so it feels like a wonderful place to celebrate Aardman's milestone year.

"Creating something that parents, children and grandparents can enjoy together is exactly what Carrot, and our friends at Aardman, are about."

  As well as Wallace and Gromit's most-daring adventures, other familiar Aardman characters will feature in the one-day Barbican extravaganza 

*(Image: Supplied)*

The concert begins with a journey through Aardman’s five-decade history, featuring a specially created montage of iconic moments set to a new score.

The second half of the show presents a full screening of Wallace & Gromit: The Wrong Trousers, accompanied by a live orchestra.

Wallace & Gromit remains a beloved staple of British animation, with the recent 2024 Christmas Day release of Wallace & Gromit: Vengeance Most Fowl becoming the UK’s most-watched animated film since modern records began.

It attracted 21.6 million viewers in just 28 days.

Molly Van Den Brink, senior attractions and live experiences manager at Aardman, said: "What better way to continue our long-standing partnership with Carrot Productions than celebrating our 50th anniversary together, allowing fans of all ages the chance to experience our favourite Aardman moments from the last 50 years in such a joyful and uplifting concert, while introducing children to the wonder of a live orchestra."

Carrot Productions has previously worked with Aardman on projects such as the 2019 tour Wallace & Gromit's Musical Marvels, which won Best Licensed Live Event at the Licensing Excellence Awards 2020.

  Aardman brings together some of the most recognisable live animation to have hit British TV screens in 50 years 

*(Image: Supplied)*

The company is familiar to York audiences through its annual sold-out performances of The Snowman with live orchestra at York Barbican.

Aardman in Concert is part of a wider national tour that will visit cities including Birmingham, London, Salisbury, Guildford, Bristol, Newcastle, Manchester, Leicester, Nottingham and Chester.

Aardman in Concert is at York Barbican on Saturday, August 29.
