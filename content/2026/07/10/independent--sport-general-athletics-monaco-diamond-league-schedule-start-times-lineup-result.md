The Diamond League season continues with a fascinating field in action in Monaco.

The men’s 100m looks packed with quality, with Oblique Seville, Letsile Tebogo and Jordan Anthony all in action – though Marcell Jacobs has been forced to withdraw after a rapid wind-assisted time recently.

The women’s sprinters will be on show in the 200 metres, too, with Olympic 100m champion Julien Alfred battling Gabrielle Thomas, winner over the longer distance at Paris 2024.

British interest includes middle-distance star Jake Wightman and Alex Yee, with the triathlete looking to lower his personal best in a rare track outing in what could be a fast and furious 5000m.

Here’s everything you need to know.

**When is the Monaco Diamond League?**

The Diamond League event in Monaco will take on place on Friday 10 July.

**How can I watch it?**

The BBC has the United Kingdom television rights to every Diamond League meeting, and coverage will be available via the red button from 7pm. A live stream should be available on the BBC iPlayer or BBC Sport website.
