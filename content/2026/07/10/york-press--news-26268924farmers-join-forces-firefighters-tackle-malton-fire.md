## Farmers join forces with firefighters to tackle massive blaze

THE owners of land badly damaged by fire have thanked everyone who came to their assistance.

THE owners of land badly damaged by fire have thanked everyone who came to their assistance.

Two crews from Malton, along with colleagues from York, Harrogate, Sherburn, Robin Hoods Bay and Selby were mobilised at 2.01pm to a standing crop fire on Broughton Road, Malton.

The fire had spread to a further three fields due to extreme heat and winds.

  


Posting on social media Abi Monkman said the spirit of farmers and country people had really shone through.

"We really cannot thank everyone enough for all that they did yesterday.

"A simple plea for help on Facebook saw farmers down tools and come to help us, and we will be forever grateful to all of you that did, from people evacuating horses and dogs, helping empty the house of sentimental stuff, dicing up land for fire breaks and bringing tankers of water - the amount of people offering help has really blown us away.

"Biggest of thanks to the fire service who do an incredible job, and work tirelessly to keep everyone safe.

"Without everybody rallying together, we have no doubt that we would have lost our home.

"We promise to have a party to thank everyone, but might wait til harvest is over, we really hope we don’t have to relive this or watch anyone else go through it, and wish everyone a safe and smooth harvest. From the bottom of our hearts, thank you all so much.

"If your back is against the wall, call a farmer, they get things sorted.

"Please share this so that everyone who helped us gets to see it.

"We’ve had so many messages, and currently can’t keep on top of them. we will reply, but for now, our heads are a shed and we are monitoring it so that it hopefully doesn’t spark up again."
