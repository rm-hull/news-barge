# These are the winning teenage designers from Harrogate school that have impressed national judges

## A team of extraordinary students from a Harrogate school have triumphed in national awards after creating innovative new smart bandages to heal injured dogs.

The team of teenage designers from Harrogate Ladies College won the Best Research category Years (12-13) in the TeenTech Awards Finals in London having become the only school to fly the flag for Yorkshire.

The project called PetMedD, gained outstanding feedback from the judges for the work of students Sophia Lo, Smillia Meister and India Thomas.

“It was an incredible experience and we were so excited to win our final,” said PetMedD team member, India Thomas.

“Every single project there was well researched and of a high level, so to go home with the trophy is a fantastic feeling."

The talented trio were not the only success story for the Harrogate school in the prestigious national awards.

Harrogate Ladies College entered three teams in the competition and all three reached the finals.

Joining PetMedD at the TeenTech Finals were Ruby and Harriet with their project on regenerated methane panels and Reewa, Eunice and Ryanna, who researched improving living conditions in Sub-Saharan African countries.

The projects focus on ethical innovation and research, highlighting issues and providing creative and sustainable solutions to issues facing society.

In total, 52% of this Harrogate school’s university applications were in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics) courses in 2026, double the national average of female applications in 2025.

PetMedD team member, India Thomas, said: “I am so proud of all three teams, we put in so much effort and had incredible support from the school.”

Harrogate Ladies’ College was established in 1893 under the name of Harrogate College.

Originally a boys’ school, it later became a girls’ senior school, pioneering girls education.

It opened a co-ed pre-school in 1997 followed by a co-ed prep school 25 years ago,

In June 2025, the school announced plans to begin a transition to a fully co-ed school from 2-18 years from September 2026.

Alongside the move to a co-ed senior school, their family of schools will then be known as Duchy College.
