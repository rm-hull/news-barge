A KEY car park in the centre of York will close for five consecutive days next week.

City of York Council said St George's Field car and coach park in Tower Street will close from Monday, July 13 to Friday, July 17.

A council spokesperson said the closure relates to the Environment Agency's flood defence scheme works in the area.

The parking area holds spaces for approximately 280 cars and 27 coaches and is only a ten-minute walk to York's city centre.

An Environment Agency spokesperson said: "The project to deliver critical flood defence works ensures homes and business are better protected from the impacts of flooding into the future.

“The ramp into the car park forms part of the existing flood defences being improved.

"The closure is necessary for starting these works - we have worked with City of York Council to agree that the ramp will then be reopened as one-lane under traffic management, the work is being undertaken in stages to ensure minimum disruption to parking space availability.

“We apologise for any disruption this will cause.”
