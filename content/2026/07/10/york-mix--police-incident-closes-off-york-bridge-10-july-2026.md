There was a police incident on a York bridge tonight (Friday).

Scarborough Bridge was sealed off at both ends for a number of hours. It has now reopened.

Police officers were stationed at the steps to the bridge on either side, directing people back to Lendal Bridge if they need to cross the River Ouse.

Firefighters and paramedics were also stationed on both sides of the water.

And team from York Rescue Boat was on standby on the Ouse.

It was thought to be due to a person on the wrong side of the bridge.

Many rail services that used Scarborough Bridge were cancelled this evening.

TransPennine Express posted on X at 7.42pm: “Due to trespassers on the railway between Scarborough and York all lines are blocked.

“Train services running to and from these stations may be cancelled or delayed by up to 60 minutes. Disruption is expected until 21:00.”

The train company then added: “To help you complete your journey, rail replacement transport has been requested to run between Malton and York and between Scarborough and York.

“Please see station information posters for the location of rail replacement pick up points and be advised that journey times.”

Finally, at 8.47pm, it posted: “Following trespassers on the railway between Scarborough and York all lines have now reopened.”

The cycle and footpaths remain open on both sides of the river.
