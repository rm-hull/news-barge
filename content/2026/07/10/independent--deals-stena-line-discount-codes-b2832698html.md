## Stena Line saving tips explained

### Stack savings with Stena Line’s loyalty programme

If you sail with Stena Line regularly, its loyalty scheme, Stena More, is an easy way to save on top of vouchers or discounts. Earn points on travel, upgrades and onboard purchases, which can be redeemed on future bookings and extras. There are also exclusive member prices and flash deals.

There are three Stena More tiers: blue, gold and platinum. Here’s a breakdown of the benefits and how to climb them:

- **Blue**(entry level): Earn five points per £1 spent.
- **Gold**(7,500 points within the first 12 months): Earn 10 points per £1 spent, plus you’ll get no service charges, free wifi and Stena Plus lounge access on selected routes.
- **Platinum (** Earn 30,000 points within 12 months): The same benefits as the gold tier, plus on selected routes you’ll get dedicated boarding, late checkout and free cabin upgrades.

## Helpful booking information

### Stena Line pet policy

Pets (including dogs, cats, rabbits, guinea pigs and hamsters) are allowed if booked ahead in a pet-friendly cabin or kennel. There is a maximum of two pets per cabin booking and smaller pets must stay in a carrier and remain inside it while on the ferry.

If your pet stays in the vehicle (selected routes), there may be an extra charge, but amenities such as pet lounges or kennels usually must be booked in advance. Stena line has pet guidance for each of its routes, so that you know what documents are required to travel with your animal.

### Amendment and cancellation terms per fare type

Stena Line offers three different fare types (economy, flexi and premium). The more flexible the fare, the more leeway there is for refunds or changes.

- Economy is the cheapest but typically non-refundable and any changes will incur a fee.
- Flexi often allows amendments for free and a full refund on cancellations up to 24 hours before departure.
- Premium offers maximum flexibility and full refunds up to two hours before departure, minus a small cancellation fee.

If you amend your booking and your new fare is more expensive, then you’ll have to pay the extra cost. We recommend always checking the small print before booking and taking out travel insurance as extra cover.

## Stena Line FAQs

**How do I redeem my Stena Line discount code?**

Look for the offer code box when choosing your route and dates; you will find it under the button "Search trip". This enables you to check whether your code works before completing the booking process.

### When can I check in at Stena Line?

Stena Line’s check-in windows vary by port and route. In general, aim to arrive two to three hours before departure for crossings with vehicles and no later than one hour before sailing in many cases. If you’re travelling by foot, check-in opens up to two hours before departure and closes 30 minutes before. You can see a breakdown of timings, along with what to bring with you, on the check-in information page.

### When are Stena Line fares cheapest?

You can often find the lowest Stena Line fares by travelling midweek, booking outside school holidays and using the low fare calendar. Day sailings and overnight crossings booked several weeks in advance are usually cheaper than last-minute trips.

**Which Stena Line routes let kids go free?**

Children aged three and below travel for free on all Stena Line routes. However, the company’s current kids go free offer extends to children up to the age of 15 on routes along the Irish Sea. This includes Cairnryan to Belfast, Liverpool to Belfast, Holyhead to Dublin and Fishguard to Rosslare.

### Can I use my NHS discount on Stena Line bookings?

Stena Line doesn’t currently offer NHS or keyworker discounts. However, deals are updated regularly, so you can still take advantage of those listed above.
