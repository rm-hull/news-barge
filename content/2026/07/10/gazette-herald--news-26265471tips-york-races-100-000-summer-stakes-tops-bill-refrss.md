The Knavesmire is set for two great days, with conditions perfect, a sunny forecast, and the ground likely to be Good to Firm, Good in Places.

Tomorrow is all about the richest middle-distance handicap in Europe, the £200,000 John Smith’s Cup, while today is a more relaxed high-value, seven-race card.

The feature is the richest ever William Hill Summer Stakes, now a £100,000 Group 3 run over 6 furlongs, exclusively for fillies and mares, making it a fascinating clash of the generations.

That said, the race has been won by a 3-year-old for the last five years, and thirteen horses have been declared for this year’s renewal (2.45 pm).

2024 winner Flora of Bermuda, representing the yard of Andrew Balding, who boasts an excellent strike rate at York, is a leading player here if you can forgive her hugely disappointing run at Royal Ascot last month, while the older generation include Celandine, who won the 2024 Group 2 Sky Bet Lowther at the Ebor Festival.

The three-year-olds make up half of the field, with the leading hope amongst them looking like Spicy Marg, owned by impresario Emma Banks, as the filly was second in the Group 1 Coronation Cup last month.

Others to consider would be the Malton-trained Catching the Moon, who won a decent race for the Fahey team at Ayr last September.

Or Fitzella, who looks a decent each-way bet if you factor in that top Irish rider Billy Lee is flying in for just this one ride.

Punters will need their brains early as without doubt the first race, the William Hill sponsored 7-furlong handicap (2.10 pm) is the toughest to solve on the day.

It also has the biggest field.

The Fahey team send OUR HAVANA back to the Knavesmire, where he just scrambled home in an apprentice handicap last month as one of three winners for Musley Bank so far this season; it is the excellent Warren Fentiman on board.

He has to overcome being drawn widest of all in 19 but looks a good each-way chance, with the likes of Goldmoyne and Morte Point, both drawn low, on the short list of dangers.

A fortnight ago, Irish trainer David Marnane and owners MRC International landed a juvenile race here with Sirocco Sands and promised they’d be back.

They’ve been true to their word and run HAMBELTON (not to be confused with our local hills), a winner at Cork last time, in the mile handicap at 3.55 pm.

Leave it just over half an hour, and there is a good chance many eyes will be on handicap debutant KAHIN from the William Haggas yard; the resident of Somerville Lodge won a Maiden at Hamilton last time and goes in the Azets Handicap (4.30 pm).

With the weather set fair, York have relaxed the Dress Code, meaning jackets are not required for County Stand and Hospitality Areas.

Free water will be available from all main bars and all racegoers are advised to ensure that they take steps to avoid dehydration.

Tickets for all enclosures will be on sale at the gates, which open at 11.15am for a first race at 2.10pm.
