A mystery-drama series will air on the BBC soon, which stars the likes of John Simm and Nikki Amuka-Bird.

I, Jack Wright comes from Unforgotten creator Chris Lang, and it follows a wealthy family in disarray after the death of their patriarch.

Following the death of Jack Wright (played by Trevor Eve), a dramatic will reading leads the family to turn on each other.

Alongside that, the police begin to question whether Jack's death really was what it seemed, or whether there could have been foul play.


The series initially aired on U&Alibi in 2025, but news of a second season was thin on the ground for a long time afterwards.

However, in March 2026, the BBC announced it had acquired the show's second season, meaning it would air on BBC One and iPlayer once it was completed.

The broadcaster also shared that it had picked up the show's first season, which it will now be showing in July 2026.

Sue Deeks, head of scripted pre-buy acquisitions at the BBC, said in a statement: "I, Jack Wright is a terrific family thriller.

"Full of enjoyable performances, with really clever twists and turns, it is simply pure entertainment."


Ahead of the series initially airing in 2025, cast member Ruby Ashbourne Serkis (who plays Emily Wright) shared what the dynamic was like between the main family members.

Speaking to the Radio Times, she said: "And I think one of the interesting things about the show is they're all really messed up.

"They're all terrible people, but they're all messed up in their own way, mainly because of what Jack Wright has done to them."

She continued: "And it manifests in so many different ways, but it's made them all selfish and cruel and, for Emily particularly, there’s this big fat barrier where you never really know what she's thinking for real.

"You feel like she's always got an agenda, and to an extent she has, but she's steely and she's sharp, and she's got a lot of her granddad in her."

## When will I, Jack Wright be on TV?

The first episode of I, Jack Wright will air at 9.15pm on BBC One on Sunday, July 12, with the second episode airing later that same evening at 10.30pm.

After that, the third and fourth episodes will air at 9.15pm and 10.40pm on Monday, July 13.

At the time of writing, scheduling times for the fifth and sixth episodes have not been revealed.

Episodes will also be available to watch on BBC iPlayer.
