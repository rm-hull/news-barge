A man was discharged from a psychiatric hospital days before he died after being restrained by four men at a York jewellery store, an inquest has heard.

Sam Diatta, who was diagnosed with bipolar disorder, had been discharged from Foss Park Hospital four days before his death on July 26, 2022.

Mr Diatta, 39, of Holgate, went into cardiac arrest while being restrained at the Mappin and Webb jewellery store, then in Coney Street, for around seven minutes, the inquest heard.

The hearing was told Mr Diatta was on medication for his mental health and heart disease at the time of his death.

CCTV footage played at the inquest showed Mr Diatta throwing punches at the shop’s door supervisor before the supervisor grabbed Mr Diatta’s arm and tried to hold him.

  The door supervisor, Gary Prothero, **previously told the inquest** that Mr Diatta entered the jewellery store and asked to take photographs of three watches for his social media pages, telling staff he had 18,000 followers online.

Staff told Mr Diatta he would have to arrange this with head office, the inquest heard.

Mr Prothero said Mr Diatta became aggressive after being denied access to the watches, so he asked him to leave the store.

Mr Prothero told the hearing that Mr Diatta then attempted to grab the Covid mask the door supervisor was wearing before throwing punches at him.

One man who was in the jewellery store with his family helped the supervisor restrain Mr Diatta with two passers-by also stopping to help hold him on the ground face down.

A police officer arrived at around 4.45pm on July 26, 2022, and rolled Mr Diatta onto his back then started administering CPR when it became apparent that he was not breathing, the inquest heard.

More police and paramedics arrived and Mr Diatta was later taken to York Hospital. He was pronounced dead in hospital shortly after 6.40pm.

His cause of death was given as hypertensive heart disease and the effects of being restrained.

The inquest on Thursday (July 9) heard that Mr Diatta had been released from Foss Park Hospital on a community treatment order on July 22, 2022. The order allows people previously detained under the Mental Health Act to be discharged from hospital to receive supervised medical treatment in the community.

Mr Diatta had been recalled to hospital on July 18, 2022, after reports of him being confused and acting hostile while on a previous community treatment order, the inquest heard.

The hearing was told Mr Diatta had also been recalled to hospital on several previous occasions, including two months earlier in May 2022.

## 'Robust assessment' carried out before release from hospital, says physiologist

Shona McIlrae, an NHS consultant psychiatrist who had treated Mr Diatta since 2014, said it was “not particularly” concerning that Mr Diatta had been recalled to hospital on previous occasions.

Asked by the Diatta family’s lawyer, Parissa Najah, if Mr Diatta should have been kept in hospital longer, Ms McIlrae said Mr Diatta had been given a “robust assessment” before he was deemed fit to be released.

Ms McIlrae also said the Mental Health Act, which Mr Diatta had been recalled to hospital under, only allows patients to be held in hospital for 72 hours before they are eligible to be discharged.

Ms McIlrae added that Mr Diatta declined the offer to become an informal patient at the hospital but a bed was left available for him the day after he was released in case he decided to return, which he did not do.

Asked by Ms Najah if Mr Diatta’s release should have been handled differently, Ms McIlrae responded: “I think that assessment, at that time, was made on how Sam presented and how we knew him, and using the principles of the Mental Health Act.”

Ms McIlrae told the hearing that Mr Diatta said he wanted to return home and was given a care package, which included a suggestion to increase his medication, and a team in the community to support him.

“I think Sam very rarely wanted to be in hospital,” Ms McIlrae said. “He preferred to be in the community.”

After Mr Diatta’s death, North Yorkshire Police said four men were arrested in connection with the death and were interviewed and released while under investigation.

But in July 2023 the force said no one would be charged with any offence following Mr Diatta’s death.

Mr Diatta’s family described him as a “wonderful member of our family who will be greatly missed by us, his friends and family around the world”.

  The family members, in a statement **released by police after Mr Diatta’s death,** said Mr Diatta developed mental health issues in his late teenage years “which stayed with him throughout his life”.

Their statement added that Mr Diatta was a “a gentle giant, who was kind and generous and loved by all who met him”.

The inquest, at Northallerton Coroners’ Court, continues.
