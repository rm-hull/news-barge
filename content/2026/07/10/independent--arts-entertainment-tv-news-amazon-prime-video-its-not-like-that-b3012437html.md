Prime Video has swung the axe on acclaimed heartfelt series *It’s Not Like That*– and the lead star has expressed his frustration over the news.

Months after being written out of police procedural***Will Trent*,* Scream 3* star Scott Foley saw his latest project be unceremoniously cancelled by the Amazon streamer despite apparently high ratings, and a perfect score of 100 per cent on review aggregate site Rotten Tomatoes.

Foley, 53, shared an Instagram video in which he shared his honest feelings about the news.

![Scott Foley said he's 'disappointed' by show's cancellation](https://static.the-independent.com/2026/07/10/7/20/Screenshot-2026-07-10-at-08-19-50.png)

“After six or so weeks in Prime Video’s Top 10, I think getting as high as like three or four, really great reviews — I think we have like 100 per cent on Rotten Tomatoes — our little show called *It’s Not Like That* has been cancelled at Amazon Prime,” he said, before shrugging in confusion.

The actor, whose credits also include *Grey’s Anatomy* and*Scandal*, admitted he was “disappointed” but “grateful for the chance”.

“I got to make a show I’m so proud of with people I grew to like a lot, and now we don’t get to make it anymore, and you don’t get to watch it.”

He continued: “If you watched it, thank you. I appreciate it. If you didn’t, it’s still on Amazon Prime. It’s not going to change the cancellation, but I wanted to thank everybody who took the time to check it out.

“Whether it was you found it on your own, whether it was a recommendation you saw on social media, it’s appreciated. So thank you.”

*It’s Not Like That*, created by Ian Deitchman and Kristin Robinson, starred Foley as Malcolm, a widowed pastor who strikes up a friendship with divorced mum-of-two Lori (Erinn Hayes), with the pair bonding over being single parents. 

![Scott Foley and Errin Hayes in ‘It’s Not Like That’](https://static.the-independent.com/2026/07/10/7/56/Screenshot-2026-07-10-at-08-18-21.png)

Addressing the show’s cast and crew, Foley said: “I loved making the show with you, and I would work with you again and again and again.”

In response to his post, Foley’s co-star JR Ramirez praised the actor for his leadership during filming.

*It’s Not Like That* premiered as part of Prime Video’s Wonder Project add-on subscription in January before being released globally in May. 

Deitchman and Robinson previously worked as writers on the US comedy-drama *Parenthood*.

Loosely based on the 1989 Ron Howard film of the same name, *Parenthood*– considered one of the best family dramas of all time – originally aired on NBC from 2010 and 2015. It follows three generations of the Braverman family in Berkeley, California, and stars Craig T Nelson,* Six Feet Under*’s Peter Krause and* Gilmore Girls* star Lauren Graham.
