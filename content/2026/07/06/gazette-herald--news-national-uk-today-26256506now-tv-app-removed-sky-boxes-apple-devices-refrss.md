A popular TV app is set to be removed from millions of older devices, affecting millions across the UK.

Sky's NOW TV app is being taken off some older boxes and Apple products, as well as its original boxes and TV sticks.

The shutdown is due to Roku, the company that supplied the NOW TV devices, ending its support for older devices.

The change is expected to take place in the coming weeks, but here are the key dates you need to be aware of.


## Which devices will the NOW TV app be removed from?

There are several devices that will be affected by the NOW TV app removal.

These are:

- NOW TV Smart Box (model 4500SK)
- Roku Streaming Stick (model 3500X)
- Roku model boxes (2700X, 2710X, and 2720)
- iPhone 7 and iPhone 7 Plus
- iPhone 6s and iPhone 6s Plus
- iPhone SE (first generation, 2016)

NOW used to sell its own streaming boxes and TV sticks, but stopped making them in 2021 after it switched its focus to the NOW app on other companies’ devices.

To see if you have a model that is affected, check the model number on the underside of the box.

There will be two separate cut-off dates for different groups of devices.


## When is the NOW TV app being removed from older devices?

The NOW TV app will stop working on the Smart Box, Streaming Stick and Roku model boxes on Monday, August 3.

After this date, NOW TV will be unable to be opened on any of these devices.

Despite this, other NOW hardware will be unaffected by the change.

These are:

- The NOW TV Black Box (model 4200SK)
- The NOW TV Smart Box with 4K and Voice Search
- The NOW TV Smart Stick

The second cut-off is expected to happen around mid-July, affecting older iPhones.

This is due to support ending for iOS 15, as the NOW TV app stopped receiving updates with this version.

If you are affected by the old smart boxes, you can use a streaming device and load NOW TV on a Fire TV stick, a games console, or a smart TV.

If you have an old iPhone but it can update to iOS 16, update it, and the app will work.

If you have one of the older models mentioned above, NOW will work perfectly in a web browser or on any newer phone, tablet, TV or streaming device.
