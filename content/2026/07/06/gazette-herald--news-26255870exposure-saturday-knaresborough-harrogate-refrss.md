POLICE have launched an investigation after a person exposed themselves twice in one day in a scenic North Yorkshire location.

North Yorkshire Police is appealing for information about two exposure offences that took place on Saturday afternoon (July 4) in Knaresborough and Harrogate.

It happened at around 1pm and 3pm, close to the Bilton Viaduct and Nidd Gorge area along the River Nidd.

“It is believed that two exposure offences have been committed on the same day by the same suspect,” said a spokesperson for the force.

The suspect is described as a white man between the ages of 25 and 35, around 5ft10in, of a stocky muscular build, who was wearing a black baseball cap and carrying a black rucksack.

The police added that they are ‘particularly appealing for information about any witnesses that may have been present in the area and have yet to come forward to police’.

If you have any information that could help the investigation, please contact the police on their website, live chat, or by calling 101.

Please quote reference 12260125733 when providing information.
