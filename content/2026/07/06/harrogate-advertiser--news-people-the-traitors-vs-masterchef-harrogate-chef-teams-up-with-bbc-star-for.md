# The Traitors vs MasterChef: Harrogate chef teams up with BBC star for live cookery shows at Great Yorkshire Show

## Visitors to the Great Yorkshire Show will be treated to a unique culinary crossover event blending television-style drama with live cookery demonstrations.

A Harrogate-based MasterChef quarter finalist and private chef will join forces with a star of BBC’s The Traitors for two live cookery showcases promising entertainment, expertise and audience engagement in equal measure.

The special ‘The Traitors Meets MasterChef’ collaboration will see chef Owen Diaram team up with television personality Faraaz Noor for back-to-back demonstrations at one of Yorkshire’s flagship summer events.

The pair will appear at 2pm in the Main Cookery Theatre before returning at 4pm in the Game Theatre, giving visitors two opportunities to catch the live sessions.

![Owen Diaram, a MasterChef quarter finalist, will team up with Faraaz Noor, star of The Traitors, for two live cookery demonstrations at the Great Yorkshire Show in Harrogate](https://www.harrogateadvertiser.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOjY4MzQwMjhkLTRhMzQtNDkzMS04NTBkLTJjYTUxOWQ4ZGI3ZTo1ODNhMzA2Ny1lZmMwLTRiNjMtYTFhNC1kM2M1YjU3ZGMwMzQ=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



Adding even more culinary prestige to the occasion, the sessions will be supervised by renowned Yorkshire chef and food ambassador Steph Moon, one of the region’s most respected culinary figures.

Visitors can expect live cooking, expert tips, entertaining conversation and plenty of laughs as Owen and Faraaz showcase their passion for great food in front of a live audience.

Speaking ahead of the event, Owen said the collaboration is designed to offer something fresh and entertaining for showgoers.

“It’s fantastic to be bringing something a little different to the Great Yorkshire Show.

"Faraaz and I are looking forward to cooking together, meeting visitors and creating a show that’s packed with flavour, fun and a few surprises.”

The Great Yorkshire Show remains one of the UK’s leading agricultural and countryside events, drawing thousands of visitors each year alongside a packed programme of food, farming and rural life attractions.

This latest addition to the cookery theatre line-up offers a rare opportunity for audiences to see two well-known television personalities sharing the stage in a live culinary setting.

Whether attending for food inspiration, entertainment, or simply a memorable day out, visitors can expect a lively and engaging experience that brings a touch of television excitement to the showground.
