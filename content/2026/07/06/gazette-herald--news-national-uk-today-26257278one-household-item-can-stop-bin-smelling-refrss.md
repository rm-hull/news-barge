You can use one simple and cheap household item to stop your bin from smelling throughout the summer.

The hotter weather in the summer usually comes with a stronger-smelling bin with food rotting faster.

The heat means food in your bin will break down and rot much faster and release a distinctive smell.

The rotting attracts lots of insects, such as flies, ants, and wasps, into your home.

  The hotter weather makes food in your bin rot faster. 

*(Image: Getty)*

## How can I stop my bin from smelling in the summer?

You can easily use bicarbonate of soda to prevent your bin from smelling bad.

For most people, you might already have some in your home, but it is also quite cheap to buy from a supermarket.

Bicarb is a natural deodoriser and helps to neutralise both acidic and alkaline odours.

To stop your bin from smelling, you can easily sprinkle a bit of bicarb at the bottom of your bin before putting the bin bag in it.

If you want to make sure it is properly distributed and broken up, you can use a sieve to spread it out.

The bicarb should last for around three months, but in the hotter weather, it is worth checking it more regularly and refreshing it when needed.

To get rid of it, you can use a damp paper towel or vacuum it up.


You can also find scented bin deodorisers in shops that can be added directly to your bin bag if you want something to cover up the smell.

## How else can I stop my bin from smelling?

There are other ways of stopping your bin from smelling in the summer rather than using bicarb.

You should keep your bin out of the sun and make sure it is properly sealed.

You can also empty it more regularly during the summer and avoid letting it overflow.

If your bin often leaks, you can use newspaper to line it, which makes it easier to clean up.
