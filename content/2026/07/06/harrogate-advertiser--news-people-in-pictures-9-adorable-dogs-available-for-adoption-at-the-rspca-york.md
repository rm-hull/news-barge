## 1. Cooper

Cooper is a two-year-old Akita Cross Husky who is a sweet boy that came to the centre via an an inspector after his previous owner could no longer meet his needs. Cooper is a shy boy when meeting new people so will need a nice patient adopter who will bring out the best in him. Once he has settled and built a bond with you, he is the sweetest, most loving and playful boy you could want. Cooper is a very loyal dog and looks to his handlers to keep him safe when he is in unfamiliar surroundings. Cooper is a fun and playful boy who will make the most wonderful addition to the family. Photo: RSPCA

## 2. Frog

Frog is a two-year-old Lurcher who is a lovely lad that came to the centre via the local stray dog service. Frog was extremely nervous and very shy when he first arrived at the centre but with lots of gentle training and TLC, he has has matured into a smashing dog. Frog loves playing with a ball, even though he still has to master the concept of bringing it back to you. Being a lurcher, it's more about the thrill of the chase for him. Frog really is a very sweet boy who has missed out on so much in his young life. He is now ready to face the big wide world and all it has to offer. With his family by his side to keep him safe he looking forward to a brighter future. Photo: RSPCA

## 3. Jerry

Jerry is a nineteen-month-old Collie Cross Sheltie who is a lovely lad that came to the centre via an inspector after he was found living in squalid conditions along with many other dogs. Jerry is a very sweet natured boy who is learning that there is a whole world out there for him to explore. He is beginning to understand that life can be fun and exciting but it all has to be done at his own pace. Jerry is a very friendly and affectionate dog who loves being with his people. He will make a fantastic addition to the family lucky enough to adopt him. Photo: RSPCA

## 4. Paul

Paul is a four-year-old Border Collie who is a lovely lad that came to the centre via an inspector after he was found living in squalid conditions along with many other dogs. Due to his past being very restricted, it has left Paul unsure when trying to adapt to new situations and surroundings. He is a very sweet natured boy who is learning that there is a whole world out there for him to experience. He is beginning to understand that life can be fun and exciting but it all has to be done at his own pace. Paul is a very friendly and affectionate dog and just loves being with his people and spending as much time with you as possible. Photo: RSPCA
