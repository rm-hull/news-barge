## Which pub is the best in York? Now is the chance for you to tell us your favourite

Readers have their say to vote for the city's best pub in the latest York Press competition *(Image: Newsquest)*

WE'RE pretty sure 'Best York pub' is one of the city's most-searched terms every year and now you have the chance to add your voice to that enquiry and see your favourite crowned as part of our latest citywide competition.

The Press has launched its Best Pub 2026 competition, giving readers the chance to nominate their favourite spots across York.

The contest aims to shine a spotlight on the establishments that help make York unique, with each of the top ten finalists featured in the newspaper and online.

Each year, this competition sees thousands of votes, and 12 months ago The Crooked Tap in The Green, Acomb made off with first prize.

Manager Jimmy Hebbron said the theme behind a lot of pubs in last year's competition seemed to be the community feel created for the locals, and Acomb spoke loudest in 2025.

  Jimmy Hebbron, manager of The Crooked Tap in Acomb 

*(Image: Provided)*

Nominations for the 2026 competition are open until Sunday, July 12.

Residents can submit their favourite pubs by following this link or scanning the QR code below.

After nominations close, the ten most-mentioned pubs will be shortlisted, and readers will have the opportunity to vote for the overall winner.

The Press will feature each of the finalists and share their stories.

  Readers can submit their favourite pubs by following the link in the article or scanning the QR code below 

*(Image: Newsquest)*

Ben Hardy, editor of The Press and Gazette and Herald, said: "York and the surrounding area have an excellent selection of pubs which are cherished by locals and tourists alike.

"Not only do they bring in footfall and boost the local economy, they also create a sense of identity.

"Pubs act as a community hub for people to meet and our area has plenty of watering holes which are thriving with unique character."

He also paid tribute to the recent success of North Yorkshire pubs on the national stage.

Ben said: "We were lucky to see four North Yorkshire pubs crowned among the best in Britain by The Good Food Guide for 2025, namely The Abbey Inn at Byland, The Star Inn at Harome, The General Tarleton at Ferrensby and The Owl at Hawnby.

"This is a testament to the hard work of staff keeping these places operating to such high standards on the back of the Covid pandemic and ensuing economic uncertainty.

"Pubs in York are also excellent of course.

"My partner recently remarked how the city is blessed with so many excellent establishments while we were sat in the garden at Guy Fawkes Inn.

"Don't miss out on the opportunity to vote for your favourite pub and keep an eye out for our coverage in print and online to see who emerges as the winner."
