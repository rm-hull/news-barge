Sir Keir Starmer and Andy Burnham appear to be at odds over whether football fans should be allowed to drink alcohol in the stands.

In a social media video over the weekend, prime minister-in-waiting Mr Burnham suggested a ban on football fans drinking alcohol in the stands is “wrong”.

The Everton supporter criticised the ban after rugby league fans were able to drink while watching matches at the home of his football team.

Asked about the position on Monday, the prime minister’s official spokesman said the current rules are “sensible to follow”.

“Police chiefs have warned against the relaxing of the rules of drinking in the stands and we think that's sensible to follow,” he said.

![Andy Burnham suggested he would look again at the drinking rules, as he attended Hill Dickinson Stadium with friends, including Liverpool metro mayor Steve Rotheram, to watch the Leigh Leopards defeat Warrington Wolves on Saturday](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/06/12/01KWQ2J1AJE17964ZYVD0F7E8T.jpg)

“But fans who go to matches in the football league can drink before the game in the concourse during half time, they can stay late after the match or head to the pub, so there's plenty of chances to get a drink.

“But as I say, we think it's sensible to follow police advice.”

Mr Burnham suggested he would look again at the rules, as he attended Hill Dickinson Stadium with friends, including Liverpool metro mayor Steve Rotheram, to watch the Leigh Leopards defeat Warrington Wolves on Saturday. Both were seen holding pints of beer.

“I’ve only got one question, though, about this day today,” Mr Burnham said in a video.

“How come these lads can do that in my seat here when we can’t do that at an Everton home game? Something wrong there. I think we need to look at that.”

The Sporting Events (Control of Alcohol) Act 1985 prohibits drinking in sight of the pitch in England and Wales, but a fan-led review of football governance in 2021 called for the law to be re-examined.

However, police chiefs warned against relaxing the law.

![Downing Street admitted Sir Keir is a ‘touch sleep-deprived’ after he stayed awake to watch England’s 3-2 victory over Mexico with his son](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/02/20/08/GettyImages-2185023532.jpeg)

Meanwhile, Downing Street admitted Sir Keir is a “touch sleep-deprived” on Monday after he stayed awake to watch England’s 3-2 victory over Mexico overnight with his son.

The prime minister supported the team from upstairs in the Downing Street flat, his official spokesman confirmed.

“He’s a touch sleep-deprived this morning, but incredibly proud of the team, and incredibly proud to be English after that performance,” he said.

“He’s seen England play over many years, but this morning’s victory in the heat of the Azteca Stadium, and at altitude and against a host nation, was up there for him as being one of England’s best ever performances.”
