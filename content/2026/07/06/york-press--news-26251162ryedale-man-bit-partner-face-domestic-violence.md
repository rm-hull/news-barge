A MAN who bit his partner on the face during an argument has been spared an immediate trip to prison.

The Recorder of York, Judge Sean Morris, told Benjamin Newton, 35, that his action was an “unpleasant domestic assault” and that he normally jails anyone who assaults their female partner.

But Newton had also been accused of doing “pretty terrible things in public” to the woman and a York jury had acquitted him of three charges relating to them.

The judge said during Newton’s trial, she had admitted lying in court when she had told the jury she had not seen him since the last incident between them.

The defence had produced evidence that the woman had cooked meals for him and shared his bed.

The judge said there was no evidence Newton had threatened her or intimidated her or had been forced to be in his company.

“Doing justice to both sides, it is a prison sentence, but it is going to be suspended,” said Judge Morris at York Crown Court.

He passed a nine month prison sentence suspended for two years on condition Newton did a 26-session rehabilitative programme run by the probation service, 10 days’ rehabilitative activities and 200 hours’ unpaid work.

He must also pay £1,000 prosecution costs and be subject to a five-year restraining order banning him from having any contact with the woman.

His barrister Charlotte Noddings said: “The defendant doesn’t wish to contact the complainant in any way.”

Newton, of Back Lane, Middleton, near Pickering, had denied all charges and been convicted by the jury of a single charge, causing actual bodily harm to the woman in 2024.

Ms Noddings said since the biting incident, Newton had turned his life around. He had a job and was doing well at work.
