# More than 160 competitive cheese classes at Great Yorkshire Show 2026 to showcase dairy brilliance

## A celebration of Yorkshire’s love of cheese is being extended to food businesses across the county and beyond at this year’s Great Yorkshire Show in Harrogate thanks to a new digital option.

The Cheese and Dairy Show at the Great Yorkshire Show is always a prestigious celebration of cheese and dairy from across Britain.

It includes more than 160 competitive classes, cheese-making demonstrations and chat shows with cheese producers at the Cheese and Dairy stage hosted by Homage2Fromage.

For the very first time in the show’s history, there will be three digital classes to find the Champion Cheese Board, Grazing Platter and Cheese Celebration Cake.

![Great Yorkshire Show cheese judge Nigel Barden showcasing a cheese board entry in advance of this year's Great Yorkshire Show in Harrogate. (Picture contributed)](https://www.harrogateadvertiser.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOjkwMmQxNWE3LTZmNTAtNGM4Mi1iODI3LThmMTBkZGYzNzI2YToxN2U4MDQyNi02Mzk5LTRiNDgtYTExMS02MTdlNTVmZGM5ODY=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



By taking these classes digital, the Show’s organisers seek to encourage a wider network of farm shops, delicatessens, cheesemongers, grazing platter businesses, restaurants and hotels across Yorkshire and beyond to celebrate their creativity.

Caroline Bell, Chief Steward of the Great Yorkshire Show’s Cheese and Dairy Show said: “It’s so exciting to be taking these classes out into the community in a way that makes it easy for businesses to be part of the Great Yorkshire Show and showcase their talent.

“The digital classes will create a visually engaging competition that celebrates how cheese is presented and enjoyed across Yorkshire and beyond.

"It is another great way to shine the spotlight on a food that is produced with amazing craft and is enjoyed by cheese-lovers across the country.”

An annual Cheese Auction on Thursday, July 16 is also expected to raise thousands of pounds for charities.
