Wimbledon will crown a new women’s champion after shock defeats for Iga Swiatek and second seed Elena Rybakina were followed by Naomi Osaka’s brilliant victory over world No 1 Aryna Sabalenka in straight sets.

With Barbora Krejcikova also knocked out before the quarter-finals, Wimbledon will crown a 10th different women’s champion in 10 years, with Swiatek stunned by Alexandra Eala and Rybakina beaten by in the third round by Elise Mertens.

Meanwhile, reigning men’s champion and top seed Jannik Sinner is gathering form after a slow start at the Championships, while seventh seed Novak Djokovic remains a possible semi-final opponent for the Italian as the 39-year-old bids to win a record-breaking 25th grand slam title

On the men’s side, a wildcard is through to the quarter-finals at Wimbledon for the first time since 2014, with Britain’s Arthur Fery setting up a clash with ninth seed Flavio Cobolli after battling from behind to beat Grigor Dimitrov in five sets.
