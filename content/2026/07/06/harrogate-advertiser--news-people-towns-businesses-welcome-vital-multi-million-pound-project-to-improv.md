# Town's businesses 'welcome vital multi-million pound project' to improve Harrogate Convention Centre

## Work is beginning today on a new multi-million pound redevelopment of Harrogate Convention Centre.

The £9.4 million scheme is part of a wide-ranging package of improvements backed by North Yorkshire Council to ensure the venue can fulfil its potential and boost the local economy.

First opened in 1982 when it famously hosted the Eurovision Song Contest, the centre, which is the only facility of its kind in North Yorkshire, can still boast one of the largest purpose-built auditoriums in the UK.

But the absence of sizeable breakout spaces amid its vast 13,000 square metres of event space has limited its ability to host larger conferences in an increasingly competitive market place.

The headline part of the long called-for overhaul of Harrogate Convention Centre involves creating new larger breakout rooms in the venue’s Studio Two.

The planned transformation, which will provide breakout conferencing facilities for approximately 1,200 delegates, is expected to generate more than £1.5 million annually by the tenth year.

Other smaller improvement projects are already being carried out to update the building and bring a far more contemporary feel to the venue, including replacing outdated cooling systems and introducing new carpets and wooden frontages for interior walls.

Business leaders in the town have already welcomed news of the redevelopment and claimed that the convention centre is a central factor in Harrogate’s economy, bringing in hundreds of thousands of delegates from across the country every year.

Harrogate District Chamber of Commerce’s chief executive, Martin Mann, said: “The Harrogate Convention Centre has been part of the foundations of North Yorkshire's economy for more than 40 years.

“The work that the council is undertaking at the centre is vital to ensuring its long-term future, and the commitment to the venue is very welcome."

Contractors from Galliford Try have been appointed for the upgrade to Studio Two and work is beginning today, Monday, July 6.

The project is expected to cost in the region of £9.4 million after final design refinements are made.

North Yorkshire Council says the investment is forecast to be repaid within a decade through the additional income generated by the enhanced venue.

North Yorkshire Council’s leader, Coun Carl Les, said: “The Harrogate Convention Centre has been such an important asset to the region since it opened more than 40 years ago.

“However, we recognise that the building needs to be modernised to make sure that it can continue to compete alongside its rivals in the conferencing sector.

“The overhaul of Studio Two is at the heart of this programme to upgrade the convention centre, but there is also a whole package of other works which will make a real difference."

Last month saw Harrogate Convention Centre named as the venue of the year with less than 20,000 square metres of event space at the prestigious Association of Event Organisers’ Excellence Awards, which are often referred to as the Oscars of the events industry.

Plans for the new improvements began after local government reorganisation in 2023 when a marketing exercise was undertaken by a consultancy firm, 31Ten, which has wide experience of the conference market.

The decision to tender the most cost-effective solution for the redevelopment of Studio Two was approved by members of the council’s executive in December 2024.

The project is expected to be completed in Spring 2027.
