York Barbican concert is set to be part of a North Yorkshire music festival heading into what organisers say is a record-breaking summer.

The Ryedale Festival runs from Thursday, July 10 to Sunday, July 26, with 60 events staged across North and East Yorkshire, including nine new venues.

One of the highlights is at York Barbican on Sunday, July 12 at 4pm, when John Wilson and the Sinfonia of London perform a concert titled An English Summer.

Christopher Glynn, festival director, said: "This is shaping up to be a very special year for the Ryedale Festival.

"Ticket sales are stronger than ever, but what matters just as much is that the festival is reaching further than ever before, into new places, new communities and the lives of more young people."

The wider programme also includes Sheku and Isata Kanneh-Mason, the Gesualdo Six, Tenebrae, Eliza Carthy, the Van Baerle Trio and the Royal Liverpool Philharmonic Orchestra.

The festival is also expanding its outreach through work experience placements, free concert tickets and partnerships with schools, universities and music hubs.

Kate Hall, festival producer, said: "We want young people to see that the arts are for everyone, whatever their background or aspirations."

For the full programme and ticket information, visit ryedalefestival.com.
