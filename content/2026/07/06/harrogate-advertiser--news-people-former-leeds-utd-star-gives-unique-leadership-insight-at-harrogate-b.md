# Former Leeds Utd star gives unique leadership insight at Harrogate business gathering

## To celebrate England’s success in the FIFA World Cup 2026 a Harrogate-based serviced office provider welcomed a former England footballer to share his experiences.

Hosted at WorkWell’s offices at Copthall Bridge, the event starring ex-Leeds Utd defender Danny Mills brought together members of the local business community to explore how the mindset, resilience and teamwork demanded in elite sport can be applied to the workplace, with Danny sharing reflections from his career.

The event connected professionals from across Yorkshire, exploring a variety of topics relevant to leadership in business.

Danny, who played for England in the 2002 FIFA World Cup, shared insights around performing under pressure, responding to setbacks and building successful teams, drawing parallels between professional football and the challenges faced by modern business.

Guests also had the opportunity to put questions to Danny, covering everything from career-defining moments to the importance of communication, preparation and maintaining confidence in high-pressure situations.

Oliver Corrigan, Founder and Managing Director at WorkWell, said: “It was a pleasure to welcome Danny Mills to WorkWell, particularly at such an exciting time for football as England continues its World Cup journey.

"As Danny has experienced the pressures and pride of representing his own country on the international stage, it gave our guests a unique perspective.

“Danny’s insight about leadership, resilience and teamwork, really resonated with everyone.

"It was fantastic to see so many local businesses come together.

"Creating opportunities like this is a key part of what WorkWell is all about.”
