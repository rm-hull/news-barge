MOTORISTS are being reminded about the closure of a major roundabout in York tonight.

The A64 Hopgrove roundabout will close from 8pm on Monday (July 6) till 6am on Tuesday (July 7) for resurfacing works, a spokesperson for National Highways has said.

The closure is part of a summer scheme to renew the roundabout, along with parts of the A64 main carriageway, in both directions, between the roundabout and Grimston Bar.

  


“Where we are not renewing the carriageway surface, we will be refreshing the road markings”, National Highways confirmed.

A spokesperson added: “The scheme had previously been planned for later this year; however, the surface has recently deteriorated and it is clear it needs to be resurfaced sooner to help prevent further deterioration and unplanned closures over the summer.

“We’ll be carrying out these improvements in phases, closing different sections of the carriageway and roundabouts over a period of around five weeks.

“To minimise disruption to the public, we’ll carry out the resurfacing and associated activities overnight.”

The closure taking place tonight will divert all Eastbound traffic using the A1237 to the Little Hopgrove roundabout, with all Westbound traffic diverted via Grimston Bar.
