A SPEEDING York driver has escaped a driving ban after he told magistrates he ran a farm business and shop.

Stephen Swales, 64, of The Knoll, off Boroughbridge Road, Acomb, was caught driving at 37mph in a 30mph zone in Sutton upon Derwent, Hull and Holderness Magistrates Court heard.

Because of points already on his driving licence, he faced a driving ban of at least six months.

But he told the court he needed his driving licence to run his farm business and his family’s butchers shop, which he said employs 24 people. He also gave reasons relating to his family for keeping his licence.

Magistrates decided he would suffer exceptional hardship if he lost his licence.

Instead of a driving ban, they gave him three penalty points, fined £76 and ordered him to pay £130 prosecution costs and a £30 statutory surcharge.
