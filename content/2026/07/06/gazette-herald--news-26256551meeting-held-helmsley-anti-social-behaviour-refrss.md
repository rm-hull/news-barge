A PUBLIC meeting is being held this week following a number of instances of anti-social behaviour in a North Yorkshire town.

Local residents are invited to the meeting in Helmsley which will be attended by representatives from a number of public agencies.

Concerns have been raised by people living in the town recently following the incidents of anti-social behaviour and an alleged arson.

Following a recent gathering with local people in Helmsley, Kevin Hollinrake MP wrote to North Yorkshire Council, North Yorkshire Police and other public bodies to request that they attend a meeting with the community.

The meeting has now been confirmed for Friday, 10th July, at 10am at Helmsley Town Hall.

Councillor George Jabbour, who represents Helmsley on North Yorkshire Council, said: “Anyone who would like to raise a local matter or concern is urged to join the meeting to communicate any issues they have.

“There is no need to book a place in advance. However, we are expecting a large number of residents to be present.

“Therefore, we cannot guarantee that the room will have enough seats to accommodate everyone.

“People will be allocated a place on a first-come, first-served basis.”
