# 'Inspirational' new Agri-Innovation Village will be unveiled at Great Yorkshire Show in Harrogate

## A brand new new hydrogen-powered Agri-Innovation Village will showcase the digital and technological advances that can help the farming industry become more resilient at the Great Yorkshire Show in Harrogate.

With energy security, climate volatility and farming profitability among the era-defining challenges for today’s farm businesses, the Agri-Innovation Village at the Great Yorkshire Showground will showcase the digital and technological advances that can help the industry become more resilient.

Hosted by the Farmer Scientist Network at the Yorkshire Agricultural Society and sponsored by the York and North Yorkshire Combined Authority and Greene Tree, visitors are invited to drop in to the Agri-Innovation Village throughout this year’s show.

Dr Dave George, Chair of the Farmer Scientist Network, said: “There is no better platform than the Great Yorkshire Show to inspire farmers and the public about the exciting ways in which agricultural challenges are being met by innovative technology, solutions that can make farming more efficient and cost productive.

"It is a great feat to have the Agri-Innovation Village powered by hydrogen.

"It shows the potential for using this technology to tackle energy security, which is such a topical issue."

Hydrogen fuel has the potential to transform sustainable farming.

By using renewable energy for electrolysis, farms can produce green ammonia for fertiliser and use hydrogen fuel cells for tractors and equipment, reducing reliance on fossil fuels.

While the technology is in its infancy, the Agri-Innovation Village in this year’s Great Yorkshire Show in Harrogate will demonstrate what is possible, with the area’s electricity generated by a hydrogen unit supplied by TCP Group.

The Agri-Innovation Village will have displays and demonstrations bringing the latest research and technology, including a JCB electric-powered backhoe and hydrogen-powered vehicles from Energy Oasis.

A series of talks will explore the future of farming, and innovative solutions will be celebrated when the GYS Innovation Awards are presented on the Tuesday at 2pm.

The area will host summer socials with the Farmer Scientist Network on Tuesday at 4pm and the Women In Farming Network on Thursday at 3pm to bring members of the farming community together.
