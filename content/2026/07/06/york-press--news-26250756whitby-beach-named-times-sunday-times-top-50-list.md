THREE North Yorkshire beaches have been named among the best in the North.

In The Times and The Sunday Times’ 50 Best UK Beaches, Boggle Hole, Whitby Sands, and Marske Sands all secured spots on the prestigious list.

The annual guide, approaching its 20th edition, claims that it remains the only nationwide survey in which every listed beach is personally inspected within the previous eight weeks.

  


Each location is assessed against a 12-point checklist that covers water quality, cleanliness, accessibility, parking, lifeguard services, and facilities.

Chris Haslam, chief travel writer at The Times and The Sunday Times, said: "I’m just back from my 18th annual circuit of the British and Northern Irish coasts and I’ve never seen our seaside look more beautiful."

Beachgoers generally praise Whitby’s sweeping sands, framed by the pier and the shadow of the famous abbey above, and offering one of the most striking seafronts on the Yorkshire coast.

Tucked between rugged cliffs and rock pools, Boggle Hole has a quieter, more secret beauty than its better‑known neighbours along the bay although it can be accessed from a steep descent from the famous Cleveland Way, now part of the new King Charles III coastal path around England.

Low Newton-by-the-Sea in Northumberland was named the best beach in the North of England.

Other northern beaches making the list included Tynemouth Longsands, Roker, and Seahouses North.

Mr Haslam described the appeal of Low Newton-by-the-Sea.

He said: "The birders were all of a flutter when I arrived at Low Newton, a field of gold at the top end of Embleton Bay.

"Four Temmink’s stints en route to Siberia had been spotted treating the beach like a Welcome Break service station.

"A week later, a rosy starling, endemic to central Asia and an American black tern arrived, and then orcas were seen breaching in the bay.

"All were attracted, no doubt, by the gentle beauty of a Northumbrian beach that offers not only top-class wildlife watching but also, in the CAMRA award-winning, folk music-loving Ship Inn, arguably the nation’s best seaside boozer."

Mr Haslam added: "We’re now heading into the third heatwave of what we might look back upon as a summer to match 1976, so it would be a shame to waste those sunny days sitting in airports, queuing at immigration machines and fighting for sun loungers.

"You can do that next year.

"This year, drop your towel on a UK beach."

This summer, the guide introduced a new criterion focused on community engagement, recognising the importance of local involvement in transforming beaches into welcoming, year-round destinations.

For the first time, it also included beaches from the Outer Hebrides, the Isles of Scilly, and the Isle of Wight, following reader requests.
