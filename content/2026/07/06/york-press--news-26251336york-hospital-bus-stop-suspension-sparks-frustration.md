**York Hospital bus-stop suspension sparks frustration**

ON Wednesday I joined the queue of other hospital visitors waiting for the number 6 bus.

After half an hour it became obvious that there was not going to be a bus – in spite of the electronic board saying there would be.

We ended up walking to Clarence Street.

The number 6 bus came – via Haxby Road and not Wigginton Road.

It would have been bad enough at any point to have a bus service disappear, but surely outside the hospital it's not unreasonable to have a sign saying stop suspended?

I have, over the last year or so encountered this situation, more than once.

Is it no-one's remit to put up a sign saying 'stop suspended'?

If not, then it's time it was; and those responsible made to honour that commitment.

  *Brian Ledger,*

  *Copmanthorpe,*

  *York*
