A person was taken to hospital after a 'medical incident' on Sunday (July 5) evening that saw the air ambulance attend the scene.

Emergency services were called to Redcar Lane in Redcar, north of Whitby on the coast, at about 6.45pm, following reports of a medical incident.

The North East Ambulance Service (NEAS) and the Great North Air Ambulance (GNAAS) both attended the scene.

An air ambulance landed near the incident, and one person was taken to hospital via road ambulance.

A spokesperson for the Great North Air Ambulance said: "Our critical care team was activated at 6.43pm to reports of a medical incident in Redcar.

"We had two paramedics and a doctor on board our aircraft and they arrived on scene at 6.49pm.

"Our team worked alongside the North East Ambulance Service (NEAS) to assess and treat a patient.

"The patient was taken to hospital by a NEAS road crew, accompanied by our team."
