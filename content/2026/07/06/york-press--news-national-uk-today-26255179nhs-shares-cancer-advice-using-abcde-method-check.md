The NHS has told people to watch out for certain symptoms of skin cancer this summer using the ABCDE method.

Melanoma skin cancer is the fifth most common cancer in the UK and is the 19th most common cause of cancer death, according to Cancer Research UK.

Ultraviolet (UV) light is the most common cause of melanoma, and it comes from the sun and is used in sunbeds.

Melanoma is more common in older people, but young people are also at risk.


## What is the ABCDE method to check for skin cancer?

The NHS has taken to social media to share an easy method to check new and existing moles for signs of skin cancer.

Skin cancer is easier to treat the earlier it is found, so it is important to keep checking moles, especially in the summer.

The social media post read: “Do you know the ABCDE checklist?

"It could help you spot the warning signs of melanoma – use this quick list to check your moles.

"A new mole or a change in an existing mole may be signs of melanoma.

"If you spot any changes that you think could be cancer, contact your GP.

"Melanoma is a type of skin cancer that can spread to other areas of the body.

"To find out more, search 'NHS melanoma skin cancer'."

Using the ABCDE method, you should check if your mole is asymmetrical and has an uneven shape.


You should also check if the border is ragged and if the mole is different colours.

You also need to check if the diameter is bigger than six millimetres and if the mole is evolving, so changing in size, shape or colour.

The NHS says you should book an appointment with your GP if the mole has changed, is painful or itchy, is inflamed, bleeding or crusty, or you have a dark area under a nail that has not been caused by an injury.

You can lower your chances of getting skin cancer by using sunscreen and reapplying it regularly, staying out of the sun during the hottest parts of the day, and keeping your skin covered.
