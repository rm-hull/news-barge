# Invitation to night of 'wonderfully elegant French music' as Harrogate orchestra promises a memorable summer event

## A Harrogate orchestra with a long and distinguished history is inviting music lovers to enjoy its summer concert this weekend.

Taking place at St Mark's Church on Saturday, July 11, through its long history Harrogate Philharmonic Orchestra has been under the baton of many highly reputed regular and guest conductors including John Forsyth, Nick Meredith, Eno Koco, Phil Wilby, John Anderson, George Kennaway and the composer Arthur Butterworth.

A chamber orchestra of about 32 regular players, many of whom are professional and trained musicians, Harrogate Philharmonic Orchestra gives three concerts each year and is renowned for diverse and challenging repertoires.

The programme for this Saturday’s summer concert brings together some wonderfully elegant French music alongside a symphony that many audience members may be hearing for the first time.

The evening will begin with Emmanuel Chabrier's Suite Pastorale, a bright and cheerful work full of character and charm.

Claude Debussy's Petite Suite will follow, with its graceful melodies and colourful musical landscapes that have made it a favourite with audiences for generations.

In the second half, the Philharmonic will perform Louise Farrenc's Symphony No. 1.

Although Farrenc was one of the most respected musicians of her day, her music disappeared from concert programmes for decades.

Today her work is enjoying a well-deserved revival.

Her First Symphony is full of memorable melodies, energy and imagination, standing alongside the great Romantic symphonies of the 19th century.

Harrogate Philharmonic Orchestra Summer Concert, Saturday, July 11, 7.30pm, St Mark’s Church, Harrogate.
