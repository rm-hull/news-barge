Pizza Express reportedly launched an internal inquiry into whether Andrew Mountbatten-Windsor visited its Woking restaurant after his claims in an interview with the BBC’s *Newsnight*.

In the 2019 *Newsnight* interview, the former Duke of York said he had been at the Surrey restaurant on 10 March 2001 – the day he was alleged to have slept with Virginia Giuffre, one of Jeffrey Epstein's victims.

Ms Giuffre had alleged that on that date, when she was 17, Mr Mountbatten-Windsor had dined and danced with her, before having sex with her at Ghislaine Maxwell’s house in Belgravia, London.

Mr Mountbatten-Windsor told *Newsnight*: “I was with the children and I’d taken [Princess] Beatrice to a Pizza Express in Woking for a party at, I suppose, sort of 4pm or 5pm in the afternoon.

“And then because the Duchess [of York] was away, we have a simple rule in the family that when one is away, the other one is there.”

When asked how he remembers the event with such clarity, he replied: “Because going to Pizza Express in Woking is an unusual thing for me to do, a very unusual thing for me to do.”

![Emily Maitlis interviewed Andrew Mountbatten-Windsor for Newsnight in 2019](https://static.independent.co.uk/2025/10/31/9/35/Emily-Maitlis-Prince-Andrew-the-Epstein-Scandal-The-Newsnight-Interview-BBC-News.png)

The BBC has now reported that in 2019, Pizza Express’s senior management examined the plausibility of Mr Mountbatten-Windsor's claim, viewing it as a matter of public interest.

However, the pizza chain’s internal inquiry found no evidence that he had or had not been there, the BBC reported.

The Pizza Express internal inquiry comprised looking for records from that time – but could not find them – as well as attempting to talk to past members of staff and management, according to the BBC.

The manager of the Woking branch in 2001 had since left the business and could not be spoken to, it added.

Ms Giuffre, who died by suicide last year, alleged she was forced to have sex three times with Mr Mountbatten-Windsor after she was trafficked by Epstein.

The former prince has consistently denied the allegations and any wrongdoing.

He has also denied that he met Ms Giuffre at Maxwell’s Belgravia house.

*The Independent* attempted to reach representatives of Mr Mountbatten-Windsor for comment, and also approached Pizza Express for comment.
