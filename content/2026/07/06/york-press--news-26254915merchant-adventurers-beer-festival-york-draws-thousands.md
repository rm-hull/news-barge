## Thousands enjoy major beer festival in historic York setting

Jake Quirke, Steve Quirke and Richard Wickham always enjoy the festival *(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Thousands enjoyed a major charity beer festival in the city centre on Friday and Saturday.

More than 90 ales were available in the magnificent setting of the medieval Merchant Adventurers’ Hall.

In addition, there were food stalls, live music from the YO1 stage, plus rum tastings and cheese and beer tastings.

Organised by the Merchant Adventurers’ , the festival is a major fundraiser for it, with around £25,000 expected to be raised to fund its educational events.

The popular event, staged for its fourth consecutive year, was officially opened on Friday by the civic party.

Lee Grabham of Brew York, who founded the festival, said on Saturday the festival was going really well.

  Festival founder Lee Grabham was talking about pairing cheese and beer 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

He told the Press: “We made some tweaks this year to make it even better. We added another bar. We have expanded the cash bar. We have QR codes for people to see the beers on offer.

“There are also more attendees this year.”

Lee had just given a presentation on pairing beer and cheese.

He advised: “For me, the best pairing is always a dark beer with a blue cheese. Ideally, a sweet, dark beer to go with the salinity of the blue.”

Wayne Chadwick, managing director of YO1 Radio, said: “This is the fourth event. It’s grown year-on-year. We have been fortunate to be involved from the beginning when it was relatively small and raised £10,000.

  Wayne Chadwick, Jayne Knowles and Sean Knowles of YO1 Radio 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

“This weekend is the busiest beer festival they have ever had. The weather has held fair and we are surrounded by lots of wonderful people. We feel proud to be involved.”

Chris Houston, managing director of headline sponsor Tadweld, said he was proud his Tadcaster company is headline sponsor this year.

He told the Press: “The festival has raised more than £100,000 over the years for youth across York. This is something we value at Tadweld.

  Chris Houston of Tadweld and friends 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

“The beer is delicious and it’s fantastic to support local craft breweries. The music is also fantastic.

Steve Quirke of Copmanthorpe told the Press: “It’s a great venue. The beers are at a great price. I have been coming here year after year since it started. It’s the best yet.”

  Volunteers are ready to serve the best real ale 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Son Jake Quirke said: “There is always an excellent choice of beers. It always makes for a great afternoon.”

Friend Richard Wickham of Copmanthorpe added: “I particularly enjoy sampling the different beers. The live music is always good.”
