# Fast growing Harrogate company to move into 'fantastic' offices in town centre

## A fast-growing Harrogate company is the latest business to take up a property let in one of the town’s most historic office buildings.

Strategic property and construction advisors, Fox Lloyd Jones (FLJ) has announced that is has completed the letting of 2 Victoria Avenue, Harrogate, to education technology business PASS, acting on behalf of landlord High Point Estates Ltd.

PASS is a fast-growing education technology company headquartered in Harrogate, developing digital products and services that support schools, colleges, training providers and learners across the UK, through its two brands Pass and Pass Learn.

Oliver Naylor, co-founder of PASS, said: “Having worked with Harry, Bethan and others across the Fox Lloyd Jones team, we were quickly able to evaluate the commercial aspects of the deal and gain a good understanding of how the space could work for us.”

Originally known as Belvedere House, 2 Victoria Avenue is a Grade II listed Gothic Revival building built in 1861 for banker John Smith.

Designed by architects Perkin and Backhouse, it served as the original gateway property to the Victorian-era Victoria Park Estate development.

After Smith's passing in 1866, the villa was occupied by notable figures like George E. Donisthorpe and Edmund Faber.

In 1920, it was converted into a youth hostel by the Young Men's Christian Association.

It later became an art school, and then offices and has been grade II listed since 1975.

Following recent private equity investment, PASS has entered a new phase of growth, expanding both its product offering and its team.

The letting of 2 Victoria Avenue provides PASS with a new headquarters to support its continued expansion and future recruitment plans.

This famous property has attracted interest from occupiers seeking high-quality office accommodation in a central location, reflecting continued demand for well-presented workspace within the town centre.

Bethan Linehan, Senior Surveyor at Fox Lloyd Jones, said: “We’re pleased to have secured a successful outcome for High Point Estates, bringing a high-quality occupier into 2 Victoria Avenue.

“The letting demonstrates the continued demand for well-located office accommodation in Harrogate and the confidence that growing businesses have in the town.”
