# Great Yorkshire Show 2026 is delighted to showcase line dancing and a multi-award-winning choir

## Line dancing and a multi-award-winning choir will make for a show finale with a difference at this year’s Great Yorkshire Show.

On Friday, show visitors will be encouraged to get up on their feet for line dancing sessions with Boot Scootin’ Cow Girls from The Dance Studio Leeds during four performances throughout the day at the Sheep Shearing Stage, interspersed with three performances by Knaresborough-based Knot Another Choir.

The feelgood factor will be high as these two new additions to the Show’s line-up bring singing and dancing for everyone to enjoy on the final day of the 167th Great Yorkshire Show in Harrogate.

Rachel Coates, Show Director said: “The Great Yorkshire Show is proudly a celebration of farming, food and the countryside but it is also a great day out with first-class entertainment for all the family, and these new additions to the bill will bring a fantastic feelgood atmosphere to Friday at the Show.”

Leading the line for Boot Scootin’ Cow Girls from The Dance Studio Leeds will be Katie Geddes, who is keen to showcase the wellbeing and physical health benefits of line dancing by encouraging show visitors to join in and learn some steps to country music.

Katie Geddes said: “I came to the Show for the first time last year and I thought how cool would it be get everyone who comes to the Show involved in line dancing?

"It’s accessible, beginner-friendly and people can drop-in and have a go.

"It’s not about being perfect, it’s about coming together on-mass and creating that sense of community. It’s really good fun.”

Knot Another Choir, a registered charity, is a mixed community choir of champion pedigree, with singers aged between 17-87.

Led by Musical Director Craig Lees, the group has swept the board at competitions in 2026, picking up five trophies, including at Eskdale Festival in Whitby.

Julie Bradbury-Sharp, Trustee of Knot Another Choir said: “We’re very much a local choir with members all across the Harrogate area and beyond, and being Yorkshire people, being able to perform at the Great Yorkshire Show is a real honour. "
