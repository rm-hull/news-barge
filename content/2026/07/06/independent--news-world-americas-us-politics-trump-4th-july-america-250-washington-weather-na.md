President Donald Trump said he personally intervened to keep the National Mall’s Fourth of July celebration alive after severe weather triggered a mass evacuation and lengthy delays.

“When I heard that it was cancelled, I immediately overturned that decision,” Trump wrote Sunday on Truth Social. He hailed the evening — meant to commemorate America’s semiquintennial — as a “spectacular” success despite the interruptions.

The decision came after hours of chaos on the ground. As storms moved in Saturday, thousands of attendees were forced to flee the Mall and seek shelter in nearby buildings. Later, a reduced crowd filtered back through security checkpoints, gathering to watch Trump deliver a campaign-style speech followed by a massive, late-night fireworks display.

Behind the scenes, multiple officials had called for the event to be scrapped altogether.

A senior White House official told *The Washington Post***that**“all the entities involved” had proposed canceling the celebration after the weather prompted a mass evacuation. “When POTUS heard this, he told all involved to invite everyone back in and the speech would take place, even if it meant waiting until 2 a.m.,” the official added.

![President Donald Trump said he 'immediately overturned' a decision to cancel the July 4 celebration on the National Mall due to severe weather](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/06/18/21/GettyImages-2284712612.jpeg)

The deliberations reflected both safety concerns and logistical strain. The event, designated a National Special Security Event, required extensive coordination across federal and local agencies, and the evacuation of such a large crowd complicated any effort to resume programming.

As severe thunderstorms swept across the East Coast, a number of other cities also took a cautious approach. Philadelphia, New York and Boston all shifted their July 4 programming or temporarily issued evacuation orders.

On the ground in Washington, the day had already been testing attendees before the storms arrived.

Triple-digit heat bore down on crowds lining up along the National Mall, where security checkpoints and metal barricades created long, slow-moving lines. Inside the secured perimeter, dozens of people were taken to hospitals, some for heat-related issues as attendees waited for festivities to begin.

By early evening, the Mall had filled with thousands of attendees, many decked out in red, white and blue. Trump later said 422,000 people had gathered by 7 p.m., though that figure has not been confirmed or independently corroborated.

![A White House official said that ‘all the entities involved’ had proposed canceling the celebration after the weather prompted a mass evacuation](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/06/18/23/GettyImages-2284692889.jpeg)

![Many attendees clustered near barricades in the rain as officers encouraged people to disperse and take shelter](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/06/18/38/GettyImages-2284694527.jpeg)

But, as the sky grew dark and lightning flashed, law enforcement began urging people to leave, escalating their warnings as conditions worsened.

The evacuation quickly became congested and confusing.

Crowds backed up at exits, with some attendees unsure where to go and others reluctant to leave after waiting for hours in the heat, the *Post* reported. “Show is over. Please keep moving,” shouted one officer. One viral video on social media showed a brawl breaking out near a checkpoint as frustrated attendees caught in the rain reached breaking point.

Many would-be celebrants sought shelter in nearby buildings, some of which quickly filled up and reached capacity. On social media, organizers instructed people to move to other sites, including the Jefferson Memorial and the Ronald Reagan Building.

A spokesperson for the Secret Service insisted that such hiccups were bound to happen, telling the *Post*: “There’s never an event when you have to move more than 100,000 people on short notice, that it doesn’t cause some type of bump in the road.”

The White House referred *The Independent* to Trump’s post when asked for comment. 

![President Donald Trump ultimately took the stage around 11 pm, delivering a 40-minute campaign-style address, where he railed against communism and promoted the SAVE America Act](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/06/18/16/GettyImages-2284699201-(1).jpeg)

Around 9 p.m., Trump posted on Truth Social that the show would go on.

“Storms bring luck to whatever the occasion. They also make events a little bit more exciting! We will wait it out,” the 80-year-old president wrote. “I don’t care if it’s 2:00 O’Clock in the morning, or in one hour from now.”

“I’m not going to let some rain stop our 250th,” he added. “I’m leaving the White House soon. God Bless America!”

After much of the earlier programming was scrapped, the president took the stage shortly after 11 p.m., later noting that less than half of the original crowd had returned.

His roughly 40-minute speech took on the tone of a campaign rally, as he praised his administration’s military actions in Venezuela and Iran, denounced communism and urged passage of the SAVE America Act, a GOP-backed voting bill. He also paid tribute to military veterans and welcomed NASA astronauts to the stage.

The night concluded with a large-scale fireworks display over the Mall, described by the president as the “best fireworks show, EVER.”
