A James Street building would be converted to house eight padel courts if plans lodged with York Council are approved.

Northminster Developments Ltd’s application stated participation in padel was soaring nationally and attracting many people new to active sport.

They added there was significant demand for facilities for the sport in York, according to a survey done by its governing body the Lawn Tennis Association (LTA).

The plans would see the building, the southern part of which was previously used by neighbouring First Buses for storage and driver training, converted to house the courts.

Changing rooms and other facilities would also be included as part of the development.

The courts would be open from 6am to 11pm and up to 18 people would be employed as management, reception, cleaning and coaching staff.

There would be 38 parking spaces onsite including two accessible bays and five with electric vehicle charging points.

Padel is played on an enclosed court about a third of the size those used for tennis with balls played off glass walls similar to squash.

The LTA stated it is easy to learn and sociable, contributing to its growing popularity.

Plans for the James Street site stated more than 400,000 adults played padel at least once in 2024, up from 129,000 the previous year according to LTA figures.

They added the sport’s governing body estimated demand in York could sustain 16 padel facilities, with the city currently home to seven.

Plans stated: “Padel is one of the fastest growing sports in this country and is embracing many players new to active sport.

“The LTA demand analysis indicates that there is very significant demand to play padel in the York area.

“The LTA describes the active efforts to promote broader participation and a more diverse padel playing community, providing the combination of sport and social activity.

“Padel’s social appeal supports health and wellbeing objectives, making it a valuable asset for the public health agenda and for community sports providers.”
