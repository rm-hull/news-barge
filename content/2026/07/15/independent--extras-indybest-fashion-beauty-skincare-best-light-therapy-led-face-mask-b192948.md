LED face masks have become one of the most talked-about beauty devices in recent years, promising clinic-rivalling skincare from the comfort of your sofa. While IPL machines use light and sometimes lasers to tackle unwanted hair, LED technology and red light therapy focuses on improving the appearance of the skin itself, making it increasingly popular to curb the effects of ageing and environmental aggressors.

With lots of hearsay on the matter, deciphering truth from fiction can feel like a full-time job – but the science is becoming more convincing.* In a 2023 clinical study in the Skin Research & Technology journal involving 20 participants, one specific LED mask (the Skin Light Dior × Lucibel device) was shown to improve multiple signs of skin ageing when used consistently. After just one month, researchers recorded a 15.6 per cent reduction in crow's feet depth, a 26.4 per cent increase in dermal density and a 28.5 per cent decrease in pore diameter, with further improvements seen after months two and three.

If that doesn’t convince you, Dr Barbara Kubicka, doctor and member of the British College of Aesthetic Medicine, concurs that LED masks are “effective in improving skin texture, reducing inflammation and boosting radiance”. Likewise, GP dermatologist Dr Nyla Raja affirms that “red light is an anti-ageing treatment used to promote collagen production and to enhance skin elasticity”, while “blue light targets excess sebum in the dermis, removing acne-causing bacteria”.

Alongside eye creams and face massage tools, these high-tech devices are now a mainstay for many skincare buffs. So, to find out which ones are actually worth the investment, I – alongside a duo of experienced IndyBest testers – have spent two years putting the biggest names in the category through their paces. Here’s what I found.
