A team made waves at this year’s York Rotary Dragon Boat Challenge with their fun-filled Super Mario theme.

Super SASH Bros, representing youth homelessness charity SASH (Safe and Sound Homes), took to the River Ouse in colourful costume at the July 12 event.

The crew included charity supporters from all walks of life, including a mix of volunteers, fundraisers, a current service user, and a young person who previously received support from SASH.

They secured victory in two out of three qualifying heats but narrowly missed a spot in the final.

Team captain Ollie, who was supported by SASH for 18 months, said: "The atmosphere on the day was incredible.

"We all threw ourselves into it, from the costumes to the racing, and everyone gave 100 per cent.

"Winning two races was a great achievement, but the best part was representing SASH and showing what can be achieved when people come together to support young people."

Mr Ollie described the day as a chance to "give something back" to the charity that helped him.

York’s annual Dragon Boat Challenge is organised by York Rotary and raises thousands for good causes each year, bringing together teams from across the city for a day of friendly competition on the river.
