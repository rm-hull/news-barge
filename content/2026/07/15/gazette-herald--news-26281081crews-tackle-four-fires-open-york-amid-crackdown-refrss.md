Firefighters have tackled four fires in the open overnight after the emergency services launched a crackdown on arsons in the city.

Crews were called to Kingsway North in Clifton shortly before 6pm on Tuesday (July 14) to a report of a fire in the open measuring 5m by 5m.

The fire was extinguished by the Acomb crew and no further action was taken, North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service said.

Later on Tuesday, firefighters were called to another fire in the open in Clifton Backies shortly before 7.45pm which the fire service said is believed to have been started deliberately.

The fire was extinguished by the Acomb crew and no further action was required, the fire service said.

Just over an hour later, firefighters were called to a fire in the open in Torridon Place, Woodthorpe, shortly after 9pm.

The firefighters used knapsack sprayers and a bucket to extinguish the blaze, the fire service said.

Ten minutes after the fire in Torridon Place was reported, another crew was called to a campfire left unattended in Eastfield Avenue, Haxby.

The campfire was extinguished and no further action was required, the fire service said.

Earlier this month North Yorkshire Police and North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service announced a joint crackdown on arsons in York.

It came after more than 100 fires had been started deliberately in the past six months, the emergency services said.
