A York museum has shown its support for England as the Three Lions prepare to face Argentina for a place in the World Cup final.

York Castle Museum has flown the Three Lions flag ahead of the game on Wednesday night (July 15).

The museum said it has also set up a stand in its Swinging Sixties exhibition to summon the “winning spirit of the 19966”.

“We are excited to see how England performs in the semi-final tonight, flying the three lions over the York Castle Museum to show support for the team is a lovely way of engaging with the community around us in York and encouraging the team spirit,” said a spokesperson for York Museums trust, which runs York Castle Museum.

England fans said it is time for “revenge for the Hand of God” as supporters backed the Three Lions to reach the World Cup final if they can stop Argentina captain Lionel Messi.

Thousands of England supporters have descended on Atlanta ahead of Wednesday’s semi-final.

England manager Thomas Tuchel said his side were ready to face an Argentina team “fuelled by history” as they bid to reach a first men’s World Cup final since 1966.
