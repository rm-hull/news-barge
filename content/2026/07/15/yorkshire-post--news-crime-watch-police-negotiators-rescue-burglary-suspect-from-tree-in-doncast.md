# Watch: Police negotiators rescue burglary suspect from tree in Doncaster

## Bodycam footage shows the moment specialist police negotiators spent more than two hours persuading a burglary suspect to climb down from a tree following a break-in at a Doncaster business.

A 999 call was made at 12.09am on Thursday (9 Ju) and they alerted police to a burglary in progress at a premises in Doncaster city centre.

After gathering information about the suspect, including the clothes he was wearing and items he was carrying, response officers arrived at the scene and approached him.

The suspect then fled from officers into a yard and up a tree.

When he refused to leave the tree, two specially trained negotiators were called in the middle of the night to communicate with him and try to get him to safety.

Negotiators work together to build a rapport with people in crisis and persuade them to come to safety.

After over two hours of communicating with the suspect, he was brought to safety and was uninjured.

He was arrested and has since been charged with burglary and other theft offences allegedly committed earlier this month.

Doncaster Response Chief Inspector Gareth Hyde said: "It is estimated that £400 worth of damage was caused to the business as a result of the break-in and I am pleased a suspect is due to appear before the courts in relation to this crime.

"Teamwork in policing is crucial, and this was a fantastic example of it. From the accuracy of the information relayed to response officers on the ground by the call handler to the diligent work of our negotiators who were called in the middle of the night to ensure the suspect could be safely taken to custody.

"The response was rapid and focused, and negotiators worked calmly and expertly to defuse and de-escalate the situation, resulting in this incident being brought to a peaceful and safe conclusion."
