A charity ball has raised more than £19,000 in support of a North Yorkshire charity.

The Rainbow Ball 2026, organised by The Rainbow Centre in Scarborough, brought in a total of £19,250 to help continue the organisation’s work.

Held at Scarborough Spa, the event was attended by nearly 200 guests.

A fundraising officer at The Rainbow Centre said: "The Rainbow Ball is such a huge event in our calendar and a lot of time and effort goes into making the night such a fantastic success.

"It is incredibly encouraging to be supported by so many businesses and individuals within our community who continue to step up for The Rainbow Centre and the Scarborough community as a whole.

"Thank you to you all.

"We really appreciate it."

Hosted by radio presenter Paddy Billington and Nicky Priestley, the evening featured a performance by Funky Tuesday Choir and closed with live music from The Huge Party Band.

The Rainbow Centre extended its thanks to Scarborough Spa and all sponsors, with special mention to First Locate, part of The Rainbow 100 Club, who also sponsored The Rainbow Ball.

Proceeds from the ball will help continue the organisation’s work supporting those who need it most in the community.
