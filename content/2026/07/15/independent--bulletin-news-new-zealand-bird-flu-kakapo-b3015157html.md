# NZ rushes to vaccinate beloved native parrots as bird flu reaches shores

![The kākāpō, the world’s only flightless parrot, is endemic to New Zealand](https://static.independent.co.uk/s3fs-public/thumbnails/image/2014/03/10/13/kakapov2.jpg)

- New Zealand has confirmed its first case of the deadly H5N1 bird flu, found in a migratory brown skua near Wellington.
- This detection follows similar cases in neighbouring Australia and raises concerns for New Zealand's uniquely vulnerable native wildlife, many of which are flightless or ground-nesting.
- While there is no evidence of mass mortality or poultry infection yet, health officials are closely monitoring the situation and advising the public not to handle sick birds.
- A vaccination programme has begun for 300 core breeding birds from five of the country's most endangered species, including takahē and kākāpō.
- Despite preparations, experts express worry that the virus could push some critically endangered species to extinction if it spreads rapidly.
