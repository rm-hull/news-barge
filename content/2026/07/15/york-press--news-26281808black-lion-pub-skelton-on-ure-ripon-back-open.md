## Delight as North Yorkshire villagers reopen community-owned pub

The Black Lion at Skelton-on-Ure is back open again *(Image: Black Lion)*

Villagers have welcomed the return of their community-owned pub.

The Black Lion in Skelton-on-Ure, between Boroughbridge and Ripon, re-opened last week after two months of closure.

This followed the departure of its former tenants in May, who had run the 'pub and hub' to much acclaim since last October.

The Black Lion is community-owned and operated through a Community Benefit Society.

The group raised funds and secured Asset of Community Value status before purchasing the building.

More than 18 months of renovation work followed before the pub first reopened in October 2025.

Residents shared their delight at seeing the pub return to life.

  The villagers are running the pub themselves until new tenants are found 

*(Image: Black Lion)*

One resident said: "The volunteers have worked so hard to save the Black Lion, so seeing it open again is incredibly rewarding.

"The atmosphere is warm and welcoming, the staff couldn't be friendlier, and it's wonderful to see the pub busy once more with local people and visitors.

"It's fantastic to have the Black Lion back doing what it does best – bringing people together."

The pub hopes to attract a long-term tenant but it will continue operating with the support of its former bar team until then.

It has once again become a gathering place for community groups—including the village darts team and the Fireside Readers Book Club.

  The beer garden has proven popular in recent fine weather 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

A member of the book club said: "The Fireside Readers Book Club is absolutely delighted to have the lovely pub open once again.

"We’ve missed its warm atmosphere, friendly faces and the cosy corners that make our book chats so special."

The Black Lion has also reopened its popular café, serving homemade cakes, coffee, tea and light snacks.

For visitors exploring the area, the pub's caravan site also opens this weekend, offering five hard-standing pitches with electric hook-up for campervans and small touring caravans.

It is ideally situated for those visiting Newby Hall and Gardens, spending a day in Ripon, or exploring the surrounding countryside.

With its spacious beer garden and welcoming atmosphere, the Black Lion says it is once again at the heart of the village, offering "genuine Yorkshire hospitality" to all.
