A NEW festival which aims to bring together people from across Ryedale is taking place this weekend.

Ryedale Gateway Festival is a free, day-long celebration of live acts, DJs, music, arts, culture, and community, taking place at venues across Malton and Norton on Saturday, July 18.

From 10am to 6pm, the festival will fill shops, community spaces, pubs, bars and venues with something for everyone of all ages.

Designed with an “all welcome” ethos, organisers say the festival is a chance for families, neighbours and visitors to come together, enjoy local talent rooted in North Yorkshire.

The primary focus for will be the main arena in Malton Market Place, where two stages will host continuous live music from midday until 6pm, with covers and original songs from some of the area’s best local artists.

A short walk away, Talbot Yard will offer a more relaxed corner of the festival, where visitors can settle in for intimate acoustic performances while sampling local food and drink.

The Market Place running order will feature:

• Phil Hooley from 12pm to 12.20pm,

• Wolds End from 12.20pm to 12.50pm,

• Riff’n’Rye from 12.50pm to 1.10pm,

• Zak Parlby Jazz from 1.10pm to 1.40pm,

• Charley Daykin and AVA from 1.40pm to 2pm

• B-Minuses from 2pm to 2.30pm

• Nick Thompson from 2.30pm to 2.50pm

• Alex Hamilton from 2.50pm to 3.35pm

• Trinity Connected from 3.35pm to 3.55pm

• GhostShip from 3.55pm to 4.25pm

• The FillyBuskers from 4.25pm to 4.45pm

• Dan Webster from 4.45pm to 5.30pm

• Graeme Hargreaves from 5.30pm to 6pm.

As part of the festival, Skatefest will take place at the skatepark in Norton.

Ryan Swain, organiser of Skatefest, said: “Skatefest is about far more than skateboarding. It’s about giving young people opportunities, bringing communities together and showing what can happen when local organisations work together with a shared vision. Whether you’re stepping on a skateboard for the very first time or you’ve been skating for years, this event is for everyone. We’re incredibly proud to work alongside Sam, Norton Town Council, Mayor Di Keal, Claire, Gateway Fest, You Will Be CIC and all of our volunteers to make this one of the biggest free community events in North Yorkshire. We can’t wait to welcome everyone for an unforgettable day.”

Also taking place is Showcase '26 performed by dancers from Kirkham Henry Performing Arts at Norton College on Saturday and Sunday, while Sustainability Saturday will take over the Palace Cinema in Malton for a showing of the National Emergency Briefing film by Chris Packham.

The festival is being co-ordinated by You Will Be C.I.C., a Malton-based community interest company that supports local culture, accessible art spaces and creative community projects and has been made possible through £46,900 in funding from David Skaith, Mayor of York and North Yorkshire, and the York and North Yorkshire Combined Authority, with part of the grant supporting the creation of the Ryedale Gateway Festival.

Hermione Norris from You Will Be C.I.C. said: “We are really excited about the festival and our aim is to grow it into a yearly event, bigger and better each time. We want it to bring people into Malton and Norton, support the towns’ mix of shops and bars, and feel like a true community gathering.

“Thanks to the support from the Mayor and the Combined Authority, we’ve been able to build an event that is completely free to enter and deeply rooted in our local neighbourhood.

“Whether you are enjoying the live music in the Market Place, relaxing to acoustic sets in Talbot Yard, or checking out the other brilliant events like Skatefest at the skatepark, the Kirkham Henry Gala at Norton College or the other events around the town, everyone is welcome.”

For more information go to www.youwillbe.org.uk or follow You Will Be C.I.C. on social media.
