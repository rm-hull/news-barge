# Ripon Cathedral to celebrate its founding Patron Saint with remarkable 1,350 year legacy

## Ripon Cathedral has been a sacred place of worship and service for more than 1,350 years with a world class choral tradition at its heart.

But October will be a particularly special time at this medieval marvel as it celebrates Saint Wilfrid, the cathedral’s founding patron saint – who built the church on the site in 672.

Saint Wilfrid (634–709 AD) was a highly influential Northumbrian bishop, abbot, and missionary who left a legacy which has lasted.

He famously founded the monastery at Ripon in 672 AD and became a pivotal figure in English history by successfully advocating for Roman church customs over Celtic traditions at the Synod of Whitby in 664 AD.

From elegant dining in a star-lit nave to family friendly events, the Saint Wilfrid programme will showcase cathedral life in all its fullness.

On Friday, October 9, the public will get the opportunity to experience an unforgettable evening in the 15th Century nave of Ripon Cathedral with live music and dancing in the unique atmosphere of this sacred space, stunningly illuminated for the occasion.

The evening will include a sparkling reception and a delicious three-course dinner with money raised helping fund the cathedral’s mission, music, heritage conservation and education programme.

The beautiful service of choral evensong on Saturday, October 10 and the Patronal Eucharist (Service of Holy Communion) on Sunday, October 11 will both see a choir of more than 100 voices as its alumni gather to celebrate St Wilfrid.

On Friday, October 23, Ripon Cathedral will play host to two Silent Discos.

Set against the backdrop of centuries of history, guests can choose from multiple music channels while experiencing the cathedral in a very different way.

Rounding off St Wilfrid’s Month will be an evening of live music as The Rozzers, Europe’s most authentic tribute to Sting and The Police take to the stage.

Proceeds from these events help Ripon Cathedral’s mission to serve the people and communities of North Yorkshire and the Diocese of Leeds and the region.
