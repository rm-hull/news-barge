A MAN has been arrested following an early morning police chase through gardens in a York suburb.

North Yorkshire Police says its officers called to Rawcliffe at around 4.30am yesterday (July 14) after reports of a man acting suspiciously in gardens.

After searching the area, a man matching the description was spotted on Rawcliffe Avenue and ran off, pursued by officers.

The suspect, a 45-year-old man, was found and detained with the help of a police dog.

A spokesperson for the force said: "He was searched, and a small bag of white powder was found in his pocket.

"Officers then searched gardens nearby, and located a rucksack containing tools, a balaclava and gloves. A shed was also found with a snapped lock."

The man was arrested on suspicion of possession of Class A drugs, going equipped to steal, and burglary and was brought into custody for questioning.

He was been released on bail and enquiries remain ongoing.
