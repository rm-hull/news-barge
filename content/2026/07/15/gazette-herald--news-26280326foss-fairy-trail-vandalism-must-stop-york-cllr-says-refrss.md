Vandalism at a much-loved York community trail “must stop”, the area’s ward councillor has said.

Cllr Bob Webb said it was “a shame that a small number of individuals choose to spoil” the Foss Fairy Trail, after the area was vandalised in recent weeks.

The route, a free trail for families along the banks of the River Foss across from Huntington Road, has been targeted by vandals since it was set up around five years ago.

Tracy Ostle, who maintains the trail in her spare time, has appealed for help to repair ‘fairy houses’ on the trail which she said have been set alight and thrown into the river.

She also said that without help to repair the damage, “there will be no fairy trail” as the damage is too severe to fix by herself.

  Tracy Ostle at the Foss Fairy Trail which has been targeted by vandals since it was set up in York five years ago 

*(Image: Dylan Connell)*

Cllr Webb praised Ms Ostle for her work to create and maintain the trail, as he called for an end to the vandalism.

“Heworth Ward Councillors have supported local resident Tracy and her work on the Foss Fairy Trail since it started, both by volunteering to tidy the trail and by providing ward funding to purchase equipment,” he said.

  Logs from the Foss Fairy Trail have been thrown into the River Foss 

*(Image: Supplied)*

“The Foss Fairy Trail is a highly valued community asset used by hundred and hundreds of people across our city.

“By providing a free, family-friendly activity, Tracy has created a community asset that we all need to support and protect.

“It’s a shame that a small number of individuals choose to spoil it for everyone in our community, and this must stop.”

Sergeant Ben Ambler, from North Yorkshire Police, has called the damage “deeply disappointing”, and urged anyone with information about those responsible to come forward.

He said North Yorkshire Police is “committed to working with the Foss Fairy Trail to identify those responsible and ensure they face the consequences of their actions”.

“The damage caused at this much-loved community attraction is deeply disappointing,” he added.

  Sergeant Ben Ambler, from North Yorkshire Police 

*(Image: Dylan Connell)*

Sgt Ambler urged those with information that could help with the police investigation to come forward, saying: “Anyone with any information should please get in touch via our website, LiveChat or by calling 101.

“Alternatively, you can provide information anonymously via the independent charity Crimestoppers by calling 0800 555 111 or through their website.

“Please quote reference 12260131992 when providing any information.”
