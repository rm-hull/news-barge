# Hillsborough Law is an important step to preventing injustice being compounded

## The country’s conscience will be better as a result of the introduction of the Hillsborough Law. Introducing a duty of candour, it is an important step towards preventing injustice being compounded by further injustice.

The Hillsborough Law will undoubtedly go down as one of the few unqualified successes of Sir Keir Starmer’s premiership.

The law was a key promise in Labour’s 2024 manifesto, but has been delayed by wrangling over how the measures would apply to spies. Spies have been brought into the scope of the duty of candour, but with a “secure process” for disclosing information that could affect national security.

The real test of this law’s success was always going to be the reaction from the families of the victims of the Hillsborough Stadium disaster. And the fact that they have welcomed the law as presented to the Commons, shows that the Government has got it right.

Ultimately credit lies with the campaigners who have pushed hard for the Hillsborough Law. The Hillsborough families refused to accept the lies told by those in positions of power. Their tenacity has been inspiring. Victims of other injustices will hopefully not be put through the sort of injustice that they have suffered from for too long.

Their fight has been just as altruistic as it has been about getting another step closer to closure.

Hopefully, this will put an end to the scales of justice being weighted in the favour of the establishment.

For the victims of the Leeds maternity scandal, the introduction of the law could not be more timely. The law will be used to compel clinicians to give evidence. The last thing these families need is to face obstruction and obfuscation in their quest for the truth.
