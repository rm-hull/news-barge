# Farmers and land managers are key in fight against wildfires

## Rural communities will be tensely waiting for the heatwave to dissipate as the risk of wildfires continues to increase. The devastation brought upon rural communities by wildfires is not to be underestimated.

Yorkshire saw first-hand last year what the consequences are of carelessness during hot and dry spells that Britain is increasingly experiencing. The Langdale Moor wildfire left a trail of destruction in its wake. Wildfires threaten food security, the economic consequences of wildfires can reverberate for several years and the ecosystem can end up being irreparably damaged in some instances.

That is why it is important for everyone to do their bit to prevent wildfires from starting in the first place. Carelessness is the biggest danger during dry spells such as the one Britain is currently experiencing. There have been calls for disposable barbecues to be banned. Backing such a ban, the Mayor of York and North Yorkshire, David Skaith, says that they pose a risk.

People should be encouraged to get out and enjoy the countryside but these visits should be accompanied with a sense of responsibility.

As climate change increasingly takes effect, the Government must lean on the wealth of expertise that farmers and land managers have to counter dangers such as wildfires.

The fire on Langdale Moor last year showed that no one understands the countryside better than farmers as they joined firefighters on the frontline to create fire breaks and help douse flames. Managing land has never been more important and farmers understand this better than most.
