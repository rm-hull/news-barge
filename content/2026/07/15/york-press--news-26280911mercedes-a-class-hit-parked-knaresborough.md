POLICE have issued an update following a crash close to a pub in a North Yorkshire town.

The county's police force said it happened on Wednesday, July 1 around 1.55pm near the Half-Moon pub in Abbey Road, Knaresborough.

A grey and black Mercedes A-Class was hit while parked and the driver of the other vehicle failed to stop or report the incident.

  


A spokesman from North Yorkshire Police said: "We are appealing for witnesses and asking anyone who may have seen the incident, the vehicles involved, or who may have relevant CCTV, dashcam, or doorbell footage to get in touch.

"We would also like to hear from anyone who was in the area at the time and may have information that could assist our enquiries."

Officers are urging anyone with information that could help their investigation to contact them online in the first instance.

Information can be emailed to PC 319 Nabiel Galab at [nabiel.galab@northyorkshire.police.uk](mailto:nabiel.galab@northyorkshire.police.uk) or submitted via the North Yorkshire Police website.

Witnesses can use the "tell us about" section and select "an existing case or report."

Live chat is available on the website Monday to Friday, from 9am to 5pm.

Those unable to use online services can call 101.
