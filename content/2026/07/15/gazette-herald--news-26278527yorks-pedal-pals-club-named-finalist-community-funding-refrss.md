A new cycling club for children with special educational needs and disabilities is one of 12 regional finalists up for a community grant.

Pedal Pals Club, based in York, has been shortlisted for £3,000 funding through Caremark’s UK-wide Community Care Fund.

The club offers a supportive after-school environment where SEND children can learn to ride while building confidence, friendships, and independence.

If successful, the club will use the grant to purchase adapted starter bikes, enabling children of all abilities to take part in regular cycling sessions across York.

David Glover, CEO of Caremark, said: "Caremark’s work is built around helping people live well in the place they call home, but we know that a good quality of life is also shaped by the community around you.

"It is shaped by whether people feel safe, supported, included and connected.

"That is why we launched the Community Care Fund."

The Caremark Community Care Fund was created to recognise and support grassroots projects that help people feel connected and included in their communities.

The 12 finalists have been selected from more than 1,600 nominations, with three projects to receive funding based on a public vote.

Voting is open via Caremark’s Facebook page until September 1.
