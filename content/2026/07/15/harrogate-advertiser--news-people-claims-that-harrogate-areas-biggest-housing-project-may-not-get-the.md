# Claims that Harrogate area's biggest housing project 'may not get the schools and services promised'

## A Harrogate Green Party county councillor has raised fears that the controversial Maltkiln development may just lead to 3,000 new houses being built rather than a new community.

After much heated debate, the scheme by Wetherby-based property developer Caddick to create a ‘sustainable new village’ between Harrogate and York passed a major hurdle last August when it was approved by the Planning Inspectorate.

But councillors including Harrogate & Knaresborough Green Party's Coun Warneken, who represents the Ouseburn division at North Yorkshire Council, are now calling for firm guarantees that the infrastructure and services promised for what is the single largest allocation for housing in the Harrogate Local Plan will actually be delivered.

Putting pressure on North Yorkshire Council, Coun Warneken said: “Promises about community infrastructure must be kept.

"People understand that new homes are needed, but what they won't accept is getting the houses first and being told the schools, roads, sports facilities and other essential services might come later, if they come at all."

Centred around the existing Cattal railway station, as well as mixed-tenure homes, the new community is mean to have two primary schools, a mixed use local centre, and healthcare facilities and will take up to 30 years to develop.

The location for the new community at Maltkiln was chosen in 2018 by the-then Harrogate Borough Council in order to meet the national demand for new homes.

North Yorkshire Council voted in favour of adopting the Development Plan for the Maltkiln village scheme last November.

Concern has grown follow a meeting on Thursday, July 9 between developer Caddick, North Yorkshire Council, Kirk Hammerton Parish Council, Green Hammerton Parish Council, Whixley Parish Council and Hunsingore Parish Council.

During the meeting, developer Caddick said that five of the 12 planned phases of the new settlement, which would be constructed near the villages of Cattal, Whixley, Green Hammerton and Kirk Hammerton, were nothing to do with them because they are on land that the company does not own.

In effect, that means some of the key facilities, including the permanent sports hub and parts of the transport network, are planned on land outside the developer's control.

Kirk Hammerton Parish Councillor Alex Smith said: “The purpose of the Development Plan was to replace uncertainty with a clear and deliverable framework.

"Instead, important questions remain unanswered and new ones are emerging.

"The developer is seeking permission for the whole settlement while saying major parts of it are nothing to do with them.

"A school, sports hub or transport link shown on a masterplan is not the same as one that is controlled, funded and certain to be delivered.”

When Maltkiln was first approved as a site for development, the Government's planning inspector warned that there were already limits on how many homes the site could support while still delivering the high-quality community North Yorkshire Council wanted.

Since then, a large part of the land needed to deliver the original masterplan has become unavailable to the developer.

Coun Warneken said: "Residents accepted this development because they were promised a properly planned new community, not just houses - and they’ve spent years feeding into the overall plan.

"They're now being told that a significant part of that vision depends on land the developer doesn't control.”
