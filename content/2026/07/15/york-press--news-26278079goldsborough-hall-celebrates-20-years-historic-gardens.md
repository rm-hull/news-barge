## Royal-linked hall in Knaresborough opens gardens for anniversary celebration

Goldsborough Hall will reopen its gardens, marking 20 years since the estate began opening them *(Image: Goldsborough Hall)*

An estate with Royal links will soon reopen its historic gardens.

Goldsborough Hall will host the occasion, marking 20 years since the estate began opening its gardens for the National Garden Scheme.

The event, on Sunday, July 26, celebrates charitable garden visitation.

First opened by Princess Mary in 1928, the hall's garden tradition continues under the care of Mark and Clare Oglesby.

  The site was first opened by Princess Mary in 1928 

*(Image: Goldsborough Hall)*

The couple has dedicated the past two decades to reviving and enhancing the historic grounds.

A special exhibition titled A Garden Reopened 1928–2026 will showcase the gardens' transformation through archived and current photographs.

The exhibition will narrate the story of the site's extensive restoration.

Visitors will be able to explore herbaceous borders, rose gardens, a kitchen garden, woodland walks, and the historic Lime Tree Walk from 11am to 4pm.

An entrance fee of £7.50 applies to adults, while children under 12 and dogs on leads are welcome for free.
