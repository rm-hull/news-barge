More than £7,000 worth of fishing equipment has been stolen from a vehicle in York.

North Yorkshire Police said the equipment was stolen in Burton Stone Lane, Clifton, between Tuesday, July 7, and Wednesday, July 8.

The force said the stolen items include a “quantity of high-value fishing tackle and equipment used for angling”.

Officers are urging anyone with information about the theft to come forward.

“We are particularly keen to hear from anyone who may have relevant CCTV, dashcam, or doorbell footage that could assist our enquiries,” a police spokesperson said.

“We would also ask members of the public to remain vigilant for any fishing equipment being offered for sale online, through social media marketplaces, local selling groups, or at car boot sales.”

Anyone with information that could help police is asked to email [nabiel.galab@northyorkshire.police.uk](mailto:nabiel.galab@northyorkshire.police.uk) or phone 101 quoting reference 12260128382.
