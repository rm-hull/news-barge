A sea of wildflowers is delighting visitors this summer in a colourful celebration of nature and community in North Yorkshire.

The Wildflower Meadow at Spilmans in Sessay, near Thirsk, burst into bloom in July and will remain open until the end of August, offering a vibrant display of cornflowers, poppies, corn chamomile, and both red and white campion.

Tom Spilman, owner of the family-run farm, said: "We’ve had a brilliant start to the season with visitors really enjoying the beautiful meadow here at Church Farm."

More than 50,000 seeds were sown last autumn, ensuring a continuous display of colour throughout July and August.

The five-acre meadow sits beside the farm’s strawberry fields and has proven especially popular with photographers, content creators, families and couples alike.

Mr Spilman added: "The rich multi-coloured blooms against the bright blue summer skies are a stunning background for some Instagrammable pics."

Serving as both a visitor attraction and a haven for pollinators, the Wildflower Meadow is bursting with bees, butterflies and wildlife.

Entry to the meadow is included in tickets to the strawberry fields, open Wednesday to Sunday until the end of August, with prices starting at £2.50 for pre-booked visitors.

For more information, visit: www.spilmans.co.uk
