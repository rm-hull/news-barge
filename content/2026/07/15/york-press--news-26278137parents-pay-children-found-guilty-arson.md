### Parents should pay if children found guilty of arson

Hardly a week goes by without reports of a fire in and around York, usually with suspicion or undetermined causes.

With the extremely hot and dry period now affecting most of England (and all of Europe), acts of arson have taken on a more serious action.

An out-of-control fire is unpredictable and extremely dangerous to life and property, as the recent events in Almeria in Spain have highlighted the effects of uncontrollable fires.

With most schools closing for the summer holidays this week, perhaps it would be a time for schools to highlight the dangers relating to arson, (along with the dangers of drowning in cold water swimming).

And a warning to parents regarding the responsibilities for their children’s behaviour.

If a youngster is found responsible for arson or wilful damage, perhaps the parents should be expected to financially recompense for their children’s actions.

  **D M Deamer**

## Help a hedgehog

Ann Widdecombe was a passionate advocate for hedgehogs.

In the wake of her untimely death, those looking for a way to make a difference are encouraged to leave out a shallow dish of fresh water in their garden, create a hedgehog-sized gap in a fence, or let a patch of their garden grow wild.

Hedgehogs face mounting threats from habitat loss, busy roads, and dwindling sources of food and water, so even the smallest act of kindness can have a meaningful impact.

At a time when so many are grappling with what appears to be a senseless act of violence, showing consideration for vulnerable animals is one way to ensure that compassion, not cruelty, has the final word.

  **Elisa Allen, PETA Foundation**
