Two men have been arrested after three suspected stolen vehicles were recovered by police in an East Yorkshire town.

One vehicle was reported stolen in Bridlington in the early hours of Wednesday (July 15) after a resident saw someone in their garden, Humberside Police said.

The Kingston Close resident reported the intruder in their garden wearing what looked to be a balaclava before their vehicle was stolen around 2.15am, the force said.

A force spokesperson said the suspected stolen vehicle was later found by officers patrolling Bridlington but failed to stop for police on the B1259.

They said the vehicle proceeded to drive off road into a field near Langtoft after failing to stop for the officers.

Humberside Police’s dog unit found the vehicle abandoned with no one inside, and officers recovered it for forensic examination.

“Initial enquiries then quickly established this vehicle may have been connected to a possible convoy of vehicles travelling from the West Yorkshire area earlier in the night, including a suspected cloned vehicle which had been spotted in the area of Cherry Burton,” the Humberside Police spokesperson said.

They said officers intercepted the cloned vehicle which was also confirmed as stolen.

“Upon stopping, the driver reportedly blew 110 on a roadside breath test, and enquiries also established he was suspected of driving whist disqualified. The vehicle was also confirmed as stolen,” the police spokesperson said.

Meanwhile, another vehicle linked to the convoy was seen travelling west along the A166 before it came to a stop in Wetwang.

Humberside Police said the vehicle was also found to have been cloned and stolen.

“When the officers approached, they found a man inside the vehicle covered in mud, confirming their suspicions that it was potentially the same driver that fled into a field to evade officers earlier on,” the force’s spokesperson said.

They added: “All three stolen motor vehicles have since been recovered to be forensically examined before being returned to their rightful owners.”

Humberside Police said a 51-year-old man has been arrested on suspicion of drink driving, drug driving, disqualified driving, driving without insurance, two counts of theft of a motor vehicle, and burglary dwelling with intent to steal.

The force said a 26-year-old man has been arrested on suspicion of theft of motor vehicle, burglary dwelling with intent to steal, driving without insurance, driving without a licence, and on suspicion of drug driving.

Both men remained in custody on Wednesday morning assisting with the ongoing investigation, police said.

Anyone with information that could help the investigation is asked to contact Humberside Police on 101 or Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 quoting crime reference 2698518.
