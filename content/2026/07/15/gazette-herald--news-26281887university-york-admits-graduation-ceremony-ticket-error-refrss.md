THE University of York has admitted a system failure left many students without the ability to request extra graduation tickets for this year's ceremonies.

The issue, which arose from the introduction of a new booking system, meant some students may have missed the opportunity to secure additional guest spots for the upcoming ceremonies.

A spokesperson for the university said every graduating student is guaranteed two tickets for their ceremony, and this has not changed.

  


It is understood there is limited capacity at the campus graduation venue and where smaller ceremonies are planned, organisers can then offer additional free tickets capped at no more than two per student.

A spokesperson for The University of York said: "We are sorry for the new system error that affected the booking of any extra tickets.

"These additional tickets are never guaranteed because of limited seating in the ceremony hall, but we want to reassure families that no one will be turned away from our campus.

"We always have free, unticketed spaces with large screens right next to the main venues so additional family members can celebrate together and not miss a moment of the ceremony.

“We will also be releasing all remaining leftover tickets for main venues on the day of the ceremonies for in-person collection.

"We are grateful for our students' patience as we work to make graduation a special day for everyone.”

The University of York lists its next round of ceremonies from tomorrow to Tuesday, July 21.

An anonymous source who was familiar with the ticketing process said affected students lost the opportunity to obtain extra guest tickets because of the university's error and told The Press: "Graduation is a once-in-a-lifetime event for many students and their families.

"Many may have travelled significant distances, incurred travel costs, arranged annual leave or even made international travel arrangements."

"The proposed alternative is for excess family members to watch proceedings in an overflow livestream room rather than attending the graduation ceremony itself."
