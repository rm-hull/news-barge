A WOMAN has been arrested in connection with an incident where an "irritant" was allegedly thrown at a man and a woman who were sleeping rough in a North Yorkshire town.

It happened in the Ramshill Road area of Scarborough around 2.30am today (Wednesday, July 15).

The alleged victims were taken to hospital for treatment to facial injuries that are not thought to be serious, a North Yorkshire Police spokesperson said.

  


The arrested woman is currently in police custody.

North Yorkshire Police are continuing to search for other suspects and has launched an extensive investigation.

Officers are appealing for witnesses and information.

Anyone who can help is asked to contact police on 101, quoting reference 12260134286.

Information can also be given anonymously to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

A police spokesperson said: "Our thanks go to members of the public who assisted at the scene and to the local community for their patience while the road closures remain in place."

The A165 in Scarborough was closed in two areas and police cordons put up - Ramshill Road from the junction of St Martin's Place up to and including the junction with Royal Avenue, and Northway near the police station and the Stephen Joseph Theatre, although one lane was opened earlier this morning.

The two closures were approximately 800 metres apart from each other and cover one of the busiest main roads through the town.
