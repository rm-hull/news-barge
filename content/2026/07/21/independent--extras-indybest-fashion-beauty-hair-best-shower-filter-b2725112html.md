If your hair feels dry or rough, you might be surprised to learn that a shower filter could be the missing piece in your haircare routine – and prove even more beneficial than a transformative hair mask or bond repair treatment. Around 60 per cent of the UK has hard water, and while you might associate it with cloudy glasses and limescale-coated kettles, its mineral by-products can actually lurk in your shower and take a serious toll on your hair.

“Hard water contains high levels of dissolved minerals, primarily calcium and magnesium – and these minerals can have a significant impact on both skin and hair health,” explains Dr Anjali Mahto, consultant dermatologist and founder of Self London.

If your strands look lacklustre even after investing in a premium shampoo and conditioner duo, your water could be part of the problem. “Those who colour their hair may notice that their colour fades more quickly due to mineral build-up preventing proper pigment absorption,” adds Dr Mahto.

With more shower filters emerging on the market and an increasing number of brands promising to filter out these minerals before they reach your hair, I put a selection of the most popular shower filters to the test to find out whether they're a worthwhile investment or simply another bathroom gimmick.

It worked very well to combat the itchy, post-shaving dryness I often experience on my legs, and I found the haircare products I used, including shampoo, conditioner and masks, rinsed out much quicker, with a marked improvement overall in the condition of my hair. My hairdresser even commented on its condition, despite years of bleaching my hair.

The water pressure is high, but it uses less water, so overall it is a great energy-efficient option. It feels like an upgrade, it looks super chic and is very easy to clean, install and incorporate into your daily routine. It’s well worth the investment.
