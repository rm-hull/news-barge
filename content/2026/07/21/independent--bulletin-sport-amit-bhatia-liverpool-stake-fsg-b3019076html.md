A consortium led by British-Indian millionaire Amit Bhatia has expressed interest in acquiring a minority stake in Liverpool Football Club.

Bhatia is the son-in-law of billionaire Lakshimi Mittal, whose family recently acquired a controlling stake in the Indian Premier League team Rajasthan Royals.

Fenway Sports Group (FSG), the owners of Liverpool, confirmed they had been approached regarding the potential investment.

This development coincides with Bhatia's departure from the QPR board, concluding an 18-year involvement with the club.

Discussions are currently at an early stage, and no deal has been agreed, with any potential investment expected to be a minority stake similar to a previous deal with Dynasty.
