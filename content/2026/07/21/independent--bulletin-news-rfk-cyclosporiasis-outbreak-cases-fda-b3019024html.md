# RFK Jr says source of cyclosporiasis outbreak identified after recall

- Health and Human Services Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. stated that the U.S. cyclosporiasis outbreak is under control, with the source identified and a recall implemented, despite rising case numbers.
- More than 10,000 cases of cyclosporiasis have been reported across over 40 states, with Michigan accounting for the highest number of infections at 6,571.
- The Food and Drug Administration clarified that a “false positive” lab result for Taylor Farms lettuce does not alter the basis for their ongoing investigation or the voluntary recall.
- Cyclosporiasis infections are caused by the cyclospora parasite, leading to symptoms like explosive diarrhea, vomiting and fever, which can last for weeks.
- Symptoms typically appear one week after consuming contaminated food or water, and prevention involves avoiding contaminated sources, thoroughly cleaning and heating food to 158 degrees Fahrenheit.
