A £10 million scheme designed to help more York and North Yorkshire people enter the workforce is already making a difference.

The Get Britain Working Economic Inactivity Trailblazer programme, focused on York and North Yorkshire, has so far supported nearly 2,000 people in its first year, including those with long-term health conditions and other employment barriers.

David Skaith, Mayor of York and North Yorkshire, said: "Our trailblazing £10 million programme offers extra support to those who need it most, helping them overcome barriers, return to work, and transform their lives.

"We've supported thousands of Disabled people and those living with long-term health conditions or other complex needs, giving them the confidence they need to succeed.

"I'm proud of our progress, but we're just getting started."

In the first year, the programme reached 1,945 people, 1,561 of whom were not in employment.

Of these, 120 have since found jobs, 225 entered education or training, 234 began volunteering, 83 started work experience, and 25 moved into subsidised employment.

The scheme has also supported 261 employers.

Cllr Carl Les, leader of North Yorkshire Council, said: "Ensuring that our residents and businesses have the correct skills, support and jobs is vital to driving forward North Yorkshire’s economy.

"The first year of the Trailblazer project has been so important in helping to achieve this, with 17 projects being delivered across the county."

Support is provided through a range of services, including the Work and Skills Interchange, targeted youth programmes, and assistance for over-50s re-entering the workforce.

Employers can benefit from wage subsidies and the Employer Support Programme, which helps build more inclusive workplaces.

Cllr Bob Webb, deputy leader of City of York Council, said: "The continued funding will carry on the important work of powering the local economy and creating even more good jobs within our city.

"We’ll continue working with partners in York to pilot innovative new approaches and ideas, scale up what’s already working and was successful in the first year of the scheme."
