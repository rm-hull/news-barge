FIREFIGHTERS have been called to a North Yorkshire village after a fire spread into a roof void.

  



North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service said it was called to the village of Staxton near Scarborough at about 6.55pm yesterday (Monday, July 20).

Scarborough and Filey crews went to the scene after a tumble dryer caught fire.

A service spokesperson said: "Crews discovered the fire had spread into the roof above the room.

"Crews used two breathing apparatus and two hose reel jets to extinguish the fire and also a thermal imaging camera to check for further fire spread."
