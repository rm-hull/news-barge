THE resumption of an inquest into the death of a teenager who died in a York river a year ago has been announced.

Thomas George Bruce-Gregg, 19, died in the River Ouse on June 27, 2025.

North Yorkshire Police said his body was recovered just below the King's Arms pub in King's Staith around 8.30am that morning.

The force said it was called to the river at 2.48am and a multi-agency rescue operation followed involving the York Rescue Boat, North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service and the police helicopter.

Mr Bruce-Gregg was taken to the riverbank and his death was confirmed there.

As reported in The Press at the time, a police cordon was raised in Queen's Staith along with a significant emergency service presence in King's Staith.

An inquest into his death was opened and adjourned on Thursday, July 17, 2025.

The hearing is scheduled to resume at 11.30am on Thursday, August 6 at Northallerton Corner's Court.

Speaking at the time, North Yorkshire Police Chief Inspector Jon Aldred said: "My thoughts go out to the man's family at this very sad time.

"Our enquiries are continuing in order to establish the full circumstances."-

On June 27, 2025, police said they had arrested a 19-year-old woman seen entering the water with Mr Bruce-Gregg on suspicion of assault, and she was taken into custody.
