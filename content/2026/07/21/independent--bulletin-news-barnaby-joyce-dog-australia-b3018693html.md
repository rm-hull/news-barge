Moment Australian politician screams at his dog to 'sit down' on live TV

Australian politician Barnaby Joyce, a former deputy prime minister, was filmed shouting "sit down" at his dog during a live ABC News interview.

The incident occurred while Mr Joyce was being questioned by Sarah Ferguson about One Nation leader Pauline Hanson's meeting with British far-right agitator Tommy Robinson.

Mr Joyce apologised for the dog, which he claimed was barking incessantly under a deck, but later snapped at the interviewer when she suggested the dog was not a problem.

The clip quickly went viral, sparking debate online with some viewers criticising Mr Joyce's anger towards the animal, while others suggested he was attempting to deflect from difficult political questions.

Mr Joyce, who joined One Nation in 2025, was defending his party leader's decision to meet with Robinson, rejecting suggestions that she crossed a line.
