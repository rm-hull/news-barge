The countdown is on for the return of one of York's most popular outdoor summer events.

York Proms 2026, which has become a highlight of the city's summer social calendar, will take place this Sunday, July 26.

The event in York Museum Gardens will feature a mix of opera, West End songs, popular film pieces and classical favourites, as well as the traditional flag-waving Proms finale with fireworks.

  York Proms is one of the highlights of the city's summer series of events. 

*(Image: Submitted)*

It is expected to draw a large crowd, and guests are invited to bring picnics to the open-air, family-friendly concert.

Event organisers have formed a new partnership with York Against Cancer and are also inviting people who attend to pre-order luxury picnic hampers at £25 each, supporting the charity.

The hampers include a variety of locally-sourced items donated by York businesses.

The event celebrates community spirit, featuring an open-air concert filled with classical favourites, popular film pieces, and songs from famous shows, culminating in the traditional Proms finale with fireworks.

  Attendees can expect a mix of opera, show and film songs, and classical favourites along with the traditional fireworks finale. 

*(Image: Submitted)*

This year's concert features two soloists. Jennifer Coleman, a soprano with the Royal Opera, Covent Garden, will perform alongside Ashley Stilburn, the York-born performer who has gone on to become a leading man in London's West End. Together they will join the orchestra for what promises to be a memorable evening of entertainment.

  Jennifer Coleman, a soprano with the Royal Opera, Covent Garden, is one of the soloists at York Proms 2026. 

*(Image: Submitted)*

Rebecca Newman, who founded York Proms, said: "York Proms has always been about bringing people together to enjoy a fantastic evening of music while celebrating everything that's special about York.

"We're delighted to be partnering with York Against Cancer, giving our audience the opportunity to enjoy a wonderful picnic while supporting a charity that means so much to so many local families.

"We're also incredibly grateful to the businesses who have so generously donated items for the hampers."

  Soloist Ashley Stilburn is a York-born performer who has gone on to become a leading man in London's West End. 

*(Image: Submitted)*

York Against Cancer is known for its pioneering cancer research, helping to enhance patient treatment, and providing practical support for those living with cancer.

Claire Robinson, from York Against Cancer, said: "Every hamper purchased will help us continue supporting local people affected by cancer and funding projects that make a real difference across our region.

"We're incredibly grateful to York Proms and all the businesses involved for creating such a wonderful way for people to support the charity while enjoying a memorable evening."

  Countdown begins to this summer's York Proms 2026 with fireworks in Museum Gardens. 

*(Image: Submitted)*

Each hamper comes packed with a selection of treats, including a large sausage roll from Bar Hashery, fresh fruit and vegetables from Simon Baynes Fruit & Veg Supplier, KitKats donated by Nestlé, cream tea from the Dean Court Hotel, Harrogate Spring Water, and contributions from Tesco.

Techscend, an IT company based in York, sponsors this year’s event, contributing to the event's success and continuity.

Picnic hampers must be pre-ordered and can be collected at the Proms. Tickets for the event are currently available online, but will not be sold at the gates.

More information can be found on the York Proms official website.
