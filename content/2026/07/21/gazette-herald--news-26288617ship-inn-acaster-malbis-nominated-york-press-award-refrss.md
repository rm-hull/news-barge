A RIVERSIDE pub outside York is in the running for being named one of the best locals in 2026.

The Ship Inn in Acaster Malbis is one of ten venues shortlisted in a York Press reader competition.

Vicky and Phill Upton took over the pub in Hauling Lane less than two years ago and have already received amazing accolades in our best restaurants or gastropubs competition in April, where they were also shortlisted.

  The Ship Inn is in Hauling Lane, Acaster Malbis 

*(Image: Kevin Glenton)*

At that time Mrs Upton said: "It means a great deal to know that both the local community and visitors have recognised what we’re trying to create here at The Ship Inn."

Though the pub has a long history dating back to the 1700s, it reopened under the Uptons' ownership on August 1, 2024.

The pair have previously run Phill Ya Boots Café on Moorcroft Road, The Woodman Inn, and Nineteen Restaurant on Grape Lane.

The pub overlooks the River Ouse and is known for its cosy setting, friendly service, and traditional menu.

The food and accommodation have also both received glowing praise from visitors.

Recent TripAdvisor reviews praise the pub’s well-kept cask ales and drinks from Ossett Brewery, Timothy Taylor Landlord on tap, a strong lager selection, and a hand-picked wine list.

One customer who visited in December last year said: "What makes this place really special is the people.

"Thank you so much Vicky and Phill for a lovely stay and we will certainly be back!"

Local suppliers include butchers Sykes House Farm, R&J Yorkshire's Finest Farmers & Butchers Limited, Simon Baynes Wholesale fresh fruit and vegetables, and F.R. Fowler & Son.

  The outdoor seating at The Ship Inn provides a view of the nearby River Ouse 

*(Image: Kevin Glenton)*

Speaking in April Mrs Upton said: "Customers regularly comment on the cosy atmosphere, particularly in the colder months with our wood burning fires, as well as the friendly, attentive service that we are so proud of."

Since taking over, the couple spent more than three weeks refurbishing the pub before reopening.

Mr and Mrs Upton said they are proud of the loyal following they have built in a short time.

Mrs Upton said at the time of the first nomination: "One of the achievements we’re most proud of is the feedback we receive from our regulars and the local community.

"It’s a real privilege to hear, on a daily basis, how much people appreciate the care, effort and improvements we’ve made at The Ship Inn."

  *Voting slips for The York Press Best Pub 2026 are in copies of the newspaper up to and including Saturday, August 5 and must be posted off to the address provided to count.*

  *The winner will be announced shortly afterwards.*
