## Hundreds in 'fabulous attire' expected for 'extravagant' North Yorkshire town event

Steampunks during the Whitby Steampunk Weekend on Sunday (February 9) *(Image: Danny Lawson/PA Wire)*

A TIME-TRAVELLING celebration of style and imagination is set to return to the North Yorkshire coast.

Whitby Steampunk Weekend XX is set to take over Whitby Pavilion in West Cliff from July 24–26, promising a “splendid affair” filled with retro-futuristic fashion, Victorian-inspired curiosities, and creative storytelling.

Begun in 2017, the free, family-friendly event is considered by many to be one of the largest free Steampunk gatherings in the UK, regularly attracting thousands of visitors in February and July.

  Members of The Press Camera Club often attend Whitby Steampunk Weekend and capture the atmosphere 

*(Image: Garry Hornby)*

Whitby Steampunk Weekend’s photographic allure lies in its costumed crowds of brass and brocade set against Whitby’s gothic architecture and dramatic North Sea light, turning the town into a living Victorian‑futurist film set.

Members of The Press Camera Club are often seen at the event, along with national press agencies, to capture the atmosphere and colour.

  


Event organiser WSW said guests are encouraged to don their “fabulous attire” and immerse themselves in a weekend of talks, entertainment, and shopping.

Host Andy Dolan said: "Don't wait for all the gorgeous pictures to appear on your Facebook social media newsfeed and then wish you'd attended...make the effort and a day to remember, we look forward to welcoming you."

  WSW Events said grace, style and panache is the order of the day at Whitby Steampunk Weekend XX 

*(Image: Dave Dennis)*

Organisers promise a packed programme, including presentations, workshops, live entertainment, and a bustling retail emporium featuring around 70 traders.

The emporium will be open from 10am to 5pm on Saturday, July 25, and from 10am to 4pm on Sunday, July 26, with all daytime activities free to enter.

Talks include Whitby Ghosts and Legends, Vikings and Vampires, and a look at how Victorian society shaped modern funeral customs.

Other sessions will explore Viking mythology and its influence on Bram Stoker, as well as a presentation by Whitby Museum curators on their “curious, weird and wonderful” artefacts.

  Members of The Press Camera Club often attend Whitby Steampunk Weekend and capture the atmosphere - like this from February's XIX event 

*(Image: Garry Hornby)*

Elizabeth Cheyne, an expert on Lewis Carroll, will also make an appearance, promising a “rather special” announcement for Whitby in 2027.

Author Toby Virgo will present two new talks––one exploring a fateful night in 1895 between Oscar Wilde and Sir Henry Irving, and another examining the dramatic fallout between Sir Arthur Conan Doyle and Harry Houdini.

Musical performances across the weekend will include beat poetry act The Antipoet and outdoor entertainment from East Yorkshire’s Ravens Morris, who perform steampunk morris dancing.

Whitby Tillermen Friends, a traditional sea shanty group, will also perform.

Evening events kick off on Saturday, July 25, with a general knowledge quiz at Rusty Shears.

Entry to the quiz is free, with teams of four invited to compete for £10 per team.

All proceeds will go to Whitby and Scarborough Dog Rescue.

Later that evening, Whitby Pavilion Theatre will host The Duran Duran Experience and Ant Trouble, a tribute to Adam and The Ants.

Organisers said: "Grace, style and panache is the order of the day at the wonderful, whimsical world of Whitby Steampunk Weekend, where the past is always in the future.

"Family friendly, free entry and an absolute feast for the senses."

More information can be found at the WSW events Facebook page.
