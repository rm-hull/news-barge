## Andy Burnham must bring back death penalty to combat crime

Andy Burnham – Prime Minister! He loves his job, good; image good; charisma good; good speaker; from the North, good; has a plan, good; Manchester area much improved, very good.

I have a wish list. Improve social care for the ill, disabled, and elderly; enlarge UK defence capabilities; but my number one is reduce crime growth.

Crime in the UK is way out of control; the police need retraining; the laws need rewriting; there need to be meaningful deterrents; we need more prisons and probation staff; and there are so many crimes committed now that go unreported, not acted upon, considered as unimportant, almost non-crimes, and we need the reintroduction of the death penalty!

This must be done and implemented for more than the murder of a policeman or woman and being a traitor to Great Britain.

Anyone who commits murder, abuses or sexually attacks a child, or deliberately severely injures another person should be put to death.

Far too many villains in the UK have no fear of being caught or being severely dealt with.

Crimes are committed in so many different ways and adversely affect so many good decent folk in today’s Britain.

I HOPE our new Prime Minister agrees with me.

Yes, Andy Burnham offers HOPE, but how can he find the money and bring it to fruition?

  **David Quarrie, Lynden Way, Holgate, York**

## Starmer’s legacy

As he prepares to leave the UK to go and work in America, Sky News pundit Sir Trevor Phillips has pointed out to new Labour Leader Andy Burnham the electoral system must change now.

Sir Keir Starmer’s repeated attacks on the integrity of far-right politicians have not worked elsewhere in other democracies as a tactic, and Sir Trevor does not see why it could work here.

Sir Keir Starmer constantly mentioning his far right-wing opponents has only served to make them more famous.

Phillips also criticised Starmer for constantly moving to the centre ground and taking Lib Dem policies.

He categorised Starmer’s strategy as a complete flop – in the politest way possible.

He was at pains to point out Starmer won a huge House of Commons majority with only 33% of the popular vote, as his final parting word on Starmer.
