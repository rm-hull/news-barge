“If Malton isn’t the friendliest Roman Festival in the world, then it is certainly in the top one,” remarked bestselling author Terry Deary following his visit at the weekend.

Terry, who wrote the popular Horrible Histories series, was one of the special guests at Malton Roman Festival on Sunday, organised by Malton Museum.

Orchard Fields, the site of an old Roman fort, became Roman Britannia for the day as hundreds of people from across the country stepped back in time.

With an outstanding line-up, it was Malton Roman Festival’s biggest ever re-enactment village and living history area, where Legionnaires and Roman Britons alike shared their knowledge of life in Malton around 200AD.

The brand-new addition, The Northern Horse, joined the familiar faces of Legio VI Victrix, Eburakon, and more, with military displays tracing fascinating changes in equipment, special demonstrations from Roman cavalry, breathtaking recreations of Roman entertainment, and food delicacies of Ancient Rome.

Malton Museum was also delighted to welcome two acclaimed authors in the historical field as special guests, Terry Deary and Lindsey Davis.

Terry first appeared at Malton Roman Festival in 2024, and once again proved to be widely popular among children and adults alike as he discussed his much-loved series and latest book, Revolting: A Riotous History of Rebellions and Revolutions.

Renowned author of Roman fiction Lindsey, whose Marcus Didius Falco Mysteries are admired worldwide, was also welcomed for the first time.

New children’s activities included terracotta air-dry clay pot making and laurel wreath making, mosaic making, Roman helmet painting, and sorting archaeological finds.

Local researchers, academics, and archaeologists returned to share their knowledge, including MAP Archaeology, York Roman Bath Museum, York University, and the Yorkshire Archaeological & Historical Society.

Recognisable from documentaries and History Hit podcasts with Dan Snow, Dr Simon Elliot, historian, television favourite, and frequent visitor to Malton Museum, also appeared with new stories of ancient Rome and Roman Britain.

Malton Roman Festival co-ordinator Louise Lewis said: ‘I am so delighted that Malton Roman Festival V was enjoyed by so many people. The festival team works hard to make sure there is an element of interest to suit everyone so that the whole family can have a great day out together, learning about the Roman heritage on our doorstep.

“It takes a year of planning and a lot of work by a band of volunteers. We really couldn’t do it without them and I am so thankful to all of them for their generosity of time and energy to make sure the day went as planned.

“I am particularly thankful to both Lindsey Davis and Terry Deary who both travelled long distances to be with us - indeed Lindsey had the worst train journey ever but pushed through and still arrived to meet up with her fans. It was really great to see them both.”

Lindsey Davis said: ‘Well, Malton itself was great fun and thanks to all the people who diligently sat on hay bales waiting for me to appear after a nightmare of train delays that could have scuppered everything. We had a really nice event as the festival was being packed up around us. “

Eburakon Reenactment said on social media: ‘ We had a wonderful time once again at Malton Roman Festival! The day was packed with activities. Everything from spinning wool, reading fortunes, meeting the horses (and riders), easting sweet Libum cheesecake, and fashioning fine jewellery. A big thanks to Malton Museum for putting on this amazing show.’

Bistro Guy, a catering company based in York, said on social media: ‘One of Guy’s favourite events of the year… and once again, it didn’t disappoint!

What an incredible day at the Malton Roman Festival. A fantastic atmosphere, brilliant entertainment, and so many amazing people stopping by to enjoy our wood-fired sourdough pizzas.

A huge thank you to everyone who came along and supported us, and to Malton Museum for putting on another outstanding event. We can’t wait to be back next year!’

Yorkshire Archaeological & Historical society said on social media: ‘Thanks to everyone at the Museum, and all the volunteers, some of whom worked from 6am to 8pm to get everything right for we exhibitors and the public to enjoy it all. We at YAHS had a lovely time, met some lovely people, and look forward to welcoming some lovely new members!’’

The Northern Horse said: ‘A huge thank you to Malton Museum, all you amazing volunteers and superb groups, speakers and traders for a fantastic event!’

Malton Cllr Lindsay Burr said: “This festival is close to my heart as I have been involved with this event since its inception, and I am proud to have sponsored this event for many years. The day was fun and it was great to see all the children marching with the soldiers and chanting Roman battle cries.”
