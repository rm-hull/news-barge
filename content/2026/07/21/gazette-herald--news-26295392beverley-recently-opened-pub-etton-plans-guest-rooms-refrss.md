Search the Public Notice PortalTHE only pub in an East Yorkshire village could soon include accommodation adding "valuable facilities" for regional cyclists and walkers.

A planning application has been submitted by The Pub at Etton to the county council for four self-catering accommodation units with parking for four cars and a bicycle store on "brownfield site" directly behind the premises at 34 Main Street.

A similar application made in February of this year for land to the east of the pub was refused by the council planning officers who said plans for that "prominent and elevated" site would damage the appearance of the Etton Conservation area.

  


The proposal aims to develop a 790 square metre site to the north of the pub which reopened in April 2025 following refurbishment.

It has been closed since 2019, when it was known as The Light Dragoon.

The proposed development would support the pub by attracting overnight visitors, particularly walkers and cyclists exploring the Yorkshire Wolds and National Cycle Network routes, the plans said.

Planning documents state that "the pub operator has had many enquiries for accommodation" and that "the proposed accommodation would provide valuable facilities on this route and would provide significant support for the pub."

The planned scheme consists of a short terrace of self-catering units.

Each would include a double bedroom on the first floor and a ground floor kitchen and living area, allowing flexibility for overnight or longer stays.

The design also includes a grassed outdoor space for each unit.

Planning documents add that the new location is intended to address "site-specific heritage issues" identified in the earlier refused application.

The single-storey scheme is designed in traditional brick with clay pantiles, Yorkshire sliding sash windows, and barn-style oak composite doors.

The pub’s operators say the accommodation would "support the pub by diversifying the business, and by providing additional custom which will help to support its viability at a time when many pubs are struggling because of higher staff, utilities and other costs."

One accessible parking space would be provided for each unit, including electric vehicle charging. the plans say.

The application also includes a dedicated cycle store to serve visitors on the Yorkshire Wolds Cycle Route.

Popular local routes within five miles of Etton include the Hudson Way rail trail for traffic‑free walking and cycling between Market Weighton and the outskirts of Beverley, plus circular Wolds walks and quiet‑lane road cycling loops linking nearby villages such as Cherry Burton and South Dalton.

There are two National Cycle Network routes passing through Etton.

East Riding of Yorkshire Council said consultation on the planning application ends on Friday, August 7.
