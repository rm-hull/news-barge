A well-known country pub and hotel near Guisborough has gone up for sale.

The Cross Keys, on Middlesbrough Road in Upsall, is an old coaching inn dating back to the 19th century, built from stone with a traditional tiled roof.

It sits on the A171, the main road between Scarborough and Middlesbrough, and is close to the North York Moors National Park.

Inside, there's a bright-coloured bar and cosy restaurant with room for around 160 diners, along with a large kitchen and a beer cellar.

Upstairs, the pub has 20 en-suite bedrooms, including doubles, twins, and family rooms, plus a flat for a manager to live in.

The whole site, which dates back over 100 years, covers just over six-and-a-half acres.

It has parking for about 120 cars and a scenic outdoor seating area for another 120 people.

Altogether, the building measures nearly 15,000 sq ft.

"The property occupies a countryside setting, surrounded by open farmland, woodland and scattered residential properties while remaining accessible to nearby towns and surrounding villages," says Rightmove.

The pub is being sold outright. It is not a listed building and does not sit in a conservation area.

The sale is being handled by property agents Savills.

Anyone interested in buying it will need to book a viewing with Savills first.

It is listed on Rightmove, with a rateable value of £82,000.
