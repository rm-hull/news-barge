# Burnham announces first major policy with electricity bill tax cut

- Prime Minister Andy Burnham and Chancellor John Healey have announced the removal of VAT from domestic electricity bills.
- This tax cut, effective from October 1, aims to provide “breathing space” for households struggling with the cost of living.
- The change is estimated to reduce the annual Ofgem price cap by approximately £45, with companies expected to pass on the savings.
- Chancellor John Healey said the measure is funded by cancelling the Digital ID programme and will help reduce inflation.
- This announcement follows Mr Burnham's pledge for a “new political model and a new economic model” for Britain and a significant cabinet reshuffle.
