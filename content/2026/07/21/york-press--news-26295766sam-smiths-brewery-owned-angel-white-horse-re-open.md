## Sam Smith's 'flagship' pub to reopen, say reports, with recruitment underway

The Angel and White Horse in Tadcaster is said to be reopening next month. *(Image: Danny Law)*

The ‘flagship’ pub in the Sam Smith’s brewery empire looks to have gained a manager, with the Tadcaster-based venue set to open next month.

The Angel and White Horse in Bridge Street, situated right next to the Samuel Smith’s Old Brewery, has been closed for at least five years.

It was known as the brewery’s ‘tap’, with an extensive range of beers, the kegs of which were served from wooden casks. It also had a good reputation for food and was also noted for its wood panelled interior.

The pub’s back door led to a courtyard, which contains the stables that house the brewery’s shire horses that deliver ale locally.

Now, according to posts on social media, the pub, said to be a favourite of Sam Smith’s late chairman Humphrey Smith, who died last month, is recruiting for staff.

The Press understands a manager has been found, who will pick up the keys to the former Coaching Inn later this week in time for a reopening in August.

  The Old Brewery is situated next door to the Angel and White Horse. 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

A job advertisement posted on social media seeks part-time staff, who are asked to email [Manager.angelwhitehorse@gmail.com](mailto:Manager.angelwhitehorse@gmail.com)

Alas, as previously reported, Sam Smith’s Brewery currently has a policy of not talking to the media, making verification of pub re-openings difficult.

However, the placing of the advertisement and appeal for staff comes amid a renewed drive from the brewery to fill its empty pubs, said to number around half of the 300-plus estate.

Banners have been placed on a number of Sam Smith’s pubs in the area and other reopenings are taking place.

  The Angel and White Horse in Bridge Street, Tadcaster. 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

Tomorrow (Wednesday July 22) will see the re-opening of The Tankard Inn at Rufforth, west of York, which is the only pub in the village.

Its manager is Paula Dawson, who currently operates the award-winning Paula’s Kitchen café at the other side of the village, which will from now be run by her husband, as previously reported by The Press.

In York, the Wellington Inn in Alma Terrace, Fishergate, is said to be opening by the end of the month. Social media has reported a couple from Pontefract will be taking over.

Other Sam Smith’s pubs in the area are reported to have been visited by workmen carrying out repairs, but nothing further as to their re-opening has been confirmed.
