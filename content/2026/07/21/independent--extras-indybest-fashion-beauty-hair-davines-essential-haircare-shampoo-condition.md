Davines has long occupied a unique space in the haircare world, marrying salon-grade formulas with a strong commitment to sustainability.

This year, the Italian brand refreshed its cult essential haircare range – which boasts shampoos and conditioners, alongside a myriad of styling products – updating the packaging and further spotlighting the regenerative organic farming projects that sit at the heart of the collection.

Each shampoo, from minu to volu, is built around an ingredient grown by small-scale Italian producers, with the aim of supporting biodiversity while targeting specific hair concerns, such as colour fading, heat damage, flat roots and dehydration.

Of course, good intentions only get a brand so far. With price tags that sit somewhere between mid-range and aspirational, together with a devoted following, I wanted to find out whether the range could deliver on performance as well as credentials.

To put it through its paces, myself and IndyBest’s expert team of testers (spanning men and women with fine, thick, coloured, damaged, dry and oily hair types) spent weeks washing, styling and reviewing the shampoos in everyday life. From lather and cleansing power to hydration, shine and long-term results, this is how the Davines essential haircare formulas stacked up in real life.

I often struggle to find products that hydrate my dry hair without leaving it greasy and limp, so I was curious to see how Davines’s shampoo for dehydrated hair would fare. The brand says it delivers up to 11 times more hydrated hair, which I can't attest to, but my hair felt much softer and smoother from wash one.

![Davines momo shampoo best Davines shampoos IndyBest]([https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/20/13/06/Davines-momo-shampoo-best-Davines-shampoos-IndyBest.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/20/13/06/Davines-momo-shampoo-best-Davines-shampoos-IndyBest.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp) 608w)

The matching momo conditioner felt hydrating without adding extra weight, but the momo serum (£29.50, Davines.com) was the gamechanger. Applied to towel dried hair, it made my hair l far silkier – my hair drank it up, so it wasn’t greasy and flat like I worried it might be.

The shampoo takes a bit of work to lather up and as a gentle cleanser for dry hair it didn’t give my scalp the squeaky clean feeling I normally look for in a shampoo, though this is probably doing my hair good. Still, personally, I can see myself using a clarifying shampoo every once in a while. But I love the fresh scent that lingers on the hair for days.

My hair is annoyingly unruly: I have a lot of it, but strands are fine and frizz-prone. When it comes to shampoos and conditioners, I favour deeply hydrating formulas packed with smoothing proteins that work to seal my hair’s cuticle, lock out humidity and restore moisture. Davines’s love smoothing duo claims to do exactly that. My first impressions of the shampoo and conditioner duo was the delightful scent. A blend of citrus, florals and a hint of musk, it’s a sweet but not overpowering fragrance – and lingered long after application. Both products are a distinctive purple shade and work in tandem with the heat protectant milk (£31, Davines.com).

![davines love smoothing IndyBest]([https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/20/14/37/davines-love-smoothing-IndyBest.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/20/14/37/davines-love-smoothing-IndyBest.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp) 608w)

After two weeks of washing, styling and drying my hair using the three products, around three times a week, my hair has reaped the benefits. The shampoo and conditioner feel thorough on application, but wash out easily with rinsing (there’s no sticky residual feeling), with the conditioner feeling particularly nice and softening on my ends. The heat protectant helps to smooth fly aways and enhances shine, without making my hair feel heavy or lose its natural body. My frizz is noticeably calmed after each wash, with the shine-boost enhancing the look of my balayage. During the recent heatwave, the Davines trio was no match for drying in humid conditions – but this, alongside the premium price, is my own gripe. Even with double shampooing and a generous scoop out of the conditioner tub, the products haven’t depleted too much in two weeks of testing, either.

My hair is naturally full of beachy waves, which sounds ideal in theory, but between the constant frizz and years of colour damage, it’s also become prone to dryness and snapping. Davines’s nounou range is designed specifically for dry and chemically treated hair, so I was particularly interested to see how these products would perform. My first impressions were positive: the products have a rich, salon-quality texture and a strong but pleasant scent that lingers after washing.

![Davines nounou IndyBest]([https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/20/14/31/Davines-nounou-IndyBest.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/20/14/31/Davines-nounou-IndyBest.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp) 608w)

The shampoo was the standout product for me. It’s more hydrating than most shampoos I’ve tried, and after a few washes my hair already felt smoother and noticeably less brittle. I did need to use a little more product than usual to get a satisfying lather, but it left my hair feeling genuinely clean without that stripped or squeaky finish that often makes damaged hair feel worse.

The conditioner (£26, Davines.com) is extremely rich, so it’s best used sparingly, particularly if, like me, you have fine strands alongside a lot of texture. Used in moderation, though, it left the lengths feeling silky and deeply hydrated without weighing my hair down. The hair oil (£30, Davines.com) also impressed me, only a couple of drops are needed to smooth frizz and add shine, otherwise it can lean towards greasy. This does mean the small bottle will last a surprisingly long time, which, given the premium price, is a plus.

![Davines nounou IndyBest]([https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/20/14/06/Davines-nounou-IndyBest.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/20/14/06/Davines-nounou-IndyBest.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp) 608w)

Overall, my hair looked healthier, and my waves were more defined with far less frizz and flyaways. Most importantly, the deeply nourishing formulas meant it felt stronger and less prone to snapping when brushing or heat styling.

- **Best** Davines shampoo for removing build-up
- **Why we love it**- Effectively removes styling product, hard water and general build-up
 - Leaves hair feeling clean without feeling stripped or dry
 - Helps hair look smoother and less weighed down
 - Ideal weekly reset for bleached, heat-styled or product-heavy hair
 
- **Take note**- Herbal scent may not appeal to everyone initially

I recently cut my hair short after noticing some thinning, and because it's bleached and naturally prone to frizz, it takes a fair amount of styling to get it looking the way I like. Between daily heat styling to create waves, hairspray to tame baby hairs, and texture spray for that beachy finish, product build-up is inevitable. Living in a hard water area doesn't help either, as mineral deposits can accumulate on the hair over time, leaving it feeling dull, heavy and harder to manage. That's exactly why I was interested in trying the Davines solu shampoo, a clarifying product designed to remove residue and impurities without stripping the hair. It uses buckwheat extract, which is rich in antioxidants and claims to help cleanse while supporting hair health.

![Davines solu shampoo IndyBest]([https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/20/14/09/Davines-solu-shampoo-IndyBest.jpeg?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/20/14/09/Davines-solu-shampoo-IndyBest.jpeg?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp) 608w)

My first impression was the scent. It has a very herbal aroma that I wasn't completely sold on at first, but I've genuinely grown to love it – it feels like stepping into a natural spa. I've been testing it for several weeks, using it once a week alongside my usual Kérastase blond absolu shampoo as a deep-clean reset. Clarifying shampoos are best used occasionally to avoid over-cleansing, and once a week has worked perfectly for me.

It lathers beautifully, so you don't need much product, and even after the first wash my hair felt noticeably cleaner without feeling dry. In fact, it looked smoother and had more body, to the point that I've been able to skip texture spray on wash day. While solu doesn't have a matching conditioner, I paired it with the Davines dede conditioner. The tub packaging is a little inconvenient as you have to twist the lid off, but the product itself is excellent. It left my hair instantly silky and I could feel the difference as I rinsed it out. One tip: a little really does go a long way, especially on shorter hair. When I used too much, I found it harder to rinse completely.

![Davines solu shampoo and dede conditioner IndyBest]([https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/20/14/00/Davines-solu-shampoo-and-dede-conditioner-IndyBest.jpeg?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/20/14/00/Davines-solu-shampoo-and-dede-conditioner-IndyBest.jpeg?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp) 608w)

Overall, I'm incredibly impressed with solu shampoo and look forward to using it weekly for that fresh, deep-clean feeling that leaves my hair looking fuller, smoother and healthier.

Davines’s volu shampoo and conditioner are undoubtedly a good pick for fine hair types. If, like me, you’re someone who suffers an oily scalp and the limpness that accompanies it, you’ll enjoy the immediate lift this wash duo offers. After cleansing (twice) and conditioning, my blow-dried strands had more height at the root and felt thicker on the whole.

![davines volu before and after IndyBest]([https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/20/14/40/davines-volu-before-and-after-IndyBest.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp](https://static.independent.co.uk/2026/07/20/14/40/davines-volu-before-and-after-IndyBest.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp) 608w)

In my experience, volumising products often bulk out the hair at the expense of smoothness, and this was somewhat true of the volu duo. While my locks appeared slightly more frizzy through the mid-lengths and ends, the volu conditioner ensured they maintained an attractive shine – and the frizz was easily fixed with a light hair oil.

As a beauty expert, I’m well aware that a non-lathering shampoo is often the result of excess oil and build-up, and not the shampoo itself. That said, this shampoo proved particularly hard to lather and sometimes required a third cleanse. Given the 250ml size (versus competitors like Redken and Ouai, who offer 300ml bottles), this was slightly disappointing. Thankfully, the conditioner made up for it and I found my hair needed less than a shot’s worth of product to feel adequately nourished.
