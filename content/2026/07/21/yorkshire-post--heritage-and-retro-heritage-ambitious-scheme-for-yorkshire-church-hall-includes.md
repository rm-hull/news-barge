# Ambitious scheme for Yorkshire church hall includes community garden

## Ambitious plans to revitalise a church hall will see a new community garden and extra parking spaces created – if planners agree.

The parochial church council of the Ecclesiastical Parish of All Saints Church want to make improvements to the Sunday School building at the church in Savile Road, Elland.

In early May another application – to convert the building into four flats – was withdrawn.

The adjoining All Saints church itself is a Grade II*-listed building, consecrated in 1912 and with the Sunday School building started three years later.

![All Saints Church, Elland.](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOmZiYzViNDkxLThiYWYtNGQ1YS1hMzcyLTJmOGVlZGI0YTBkMjplNGJmZWFkMC1hNDRmLTRjMTUtYThlZi0wZWEzOWQ3NmJhZWE=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



It has had a history in the community ever since, being joined to an arched cloister connecting it with the church, with an attractive grass quadrangle and flower beds which was consecrated as a Garden of Repose.

The Sunday School was also the home of the local dramatic society and at one time it had been financially supported by the former West Riding County Council but this ceased in the 1950s.

Currently it is rented out to as a pre-school nursery, according to a supporting heritage statement with the latest application.

The statements explains the church wishes to maximise the potential of its assets in order to continue to serve the area, and to manage the magnificent building.

“The community aspect provided by the Sunday School also extends into the Canon Winter Centre – the basement part of the church which has been upgraded to form a well used community space with two meeting rooms, a kitchen and toilet facilities.

“Car parking has been extended to service the overall needs of the church.

“It is intended to upgrade as far as is practicable towards the efficient use of energy.

“To this end it is proposed to use the south facing roof for solar panels with battery storage in the low level store,” says the statement.

The seclusion of the Garden of Repose will be unaffected with the new communal garden to be formed at the west end of the building with additional parking provided at the east side of the grounds, it explains.

The full scope of proposals also includes replacing windows, removing or refurbishing dormers, putting new roof lights on the north-facing roof while the solar panels would be fixed to the south-facing roof, and replacing existing roof felting with red tiles.

New entrances, windows and access points to aspects of the building are also planned.
