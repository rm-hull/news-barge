Residents are facing “uncertainty” due to the disruption caused by a large water main bursting in York, the area’s ward councillors have said.

Leeman Road’s Labour ward councillors said they will be pressing officials to replace bus services and improve transport links for residents after Salisbury Terrace closed for work to repair the main.

The water pipe burst outside the former Jubilee pub burst on Sunday evening (July 19) resulting in the basement of the pub and a neighbouring home being flooded.

A hole has been left in the ground outside the pub with crews working overnight from Sunday and throughout Monday to repair the damage.

Yorkshire Water said the road closure in Salisbury Terrace was needed to repair the “significant burst on a 21-inch water main”, adding that it will “open the road as soon as it is safe to do so”.

Ward councillors Cllr Lucy Steels-Walshaw and Cllr Kallum Taylor said the closure of Salisbury Terrace has resulted in the number 10 and 59 bus services being diverted away from Leeman Road.

It means local residents are unable to get a direct bus into the city centre, they said.

The councillors said they are calling for a replacement bus service to serve St Peter’s Quarter.

They also said they will be asking City of York Council to bring forward maintenance work planned for next year to resurface the route to “avoid a double disruption” while Salisbury Terrace is closed.

## 'Unsettling time' for residents, cllr says

Cllr Taylor said it had been an “unsettling time” for residents in the area.

“The closure of a public road does create uncertainty, and I have asked council officers to look at whether changes to the existing one-way system could help residents going about their daily lives,” he said.

“We need to see a temporary traffic management plan that helps residents avoid having to add several miles to their journeys.

"Our priority is minimising the impact of this event on residents and getting back to normal as quickly as possible.”

Cllr Steels-Walshaw, a Leeman Road resident, added: “I know this incident has been difficult for many residents in Leeman Road and particularly in Balfour Street which is most affected.

“While I did ask Yorkshire Water for bottled water to be supplied to those affected, I’m pleased this wasn’t necessary due to the company acting quickly to restore water supplies.

“I’d encourage residents to contact me if they have any problems with their water supply.”

A Yorkshire Water spokesperson said on Monday: “Our teams responded very quickly to the significant burst on a 21-inch water main in York yesterday.

"Due to the size of the main there has been some water ingress at three properties, including the former pub and some residential properties, and damage to some vehicles in the area.

"Our customers teams were on site last night speaking directly with customers affected and they have returned today to continue speaking with anyone affected and will work with customers to understand any damage caused and how we can assist them in the short and longer-term.

“A case manager has been assigned to this as a single point of contact for affected customers to assist them with any claims or ongoing issues related to the burst.

“Repairs to the main are underway and to allow this to be done safely, a road closure and signed diversion is in place.

"Unfortunately, the burst caused significant damage to the road surface, which will take longer than usual to repair and reinstate.

"Our teams will be working as quickly as possible to carry out the reinstatement and will open the road as soon as it is safe to do so.

"We apologise for any inconvenience and thank customers for their patience while we complete the necessary repairs.”
