When it comes to fashion’s most recognisable shoe styles, few are as instantly distinctive as the Maison Margiela tabi. First introduced in 1988, the split-toe design has become a cult favourite thanks to its unusual silhouette and fashion-forward appeal, with the statement shape continuing to inspire new looks decades later.

While the original tabi shoes come with a luxury £820 price tag, the trend has opened the door for more affordable interpretations. If you’ve been tempted by the iconic tabi shoes but can’t quite justify dropping hundreds, there’s a more affordable way to try the trend. Split-toe shoes are now popping up everywhere, and this £98 pair from Matisse offers a similar look, but without quite so high a price tag.

Made from smooth leather, these flats are designed to be versatile enough for everything from workwear outfits to summer dressing. Whether you’re already a fan of the tabi trend or simply looking for a unique alternative to traditional ballet flats, this pair is an easy way to embrace the trend without spending designer prices.

The Matisse Mason split toe ballet glats are made from smooth leather and feature a distinctive split-toe design inspired by the iconic Tabi silhouette. The ballet flat shape keeps them classic and wearable, while the unusual toe detail adds a fashion-forward twist. They have a leather upper, faux-leather lining and a padded lining for comfort.

They’re available in two colourways: a classic black or red for a pop of brightness.
