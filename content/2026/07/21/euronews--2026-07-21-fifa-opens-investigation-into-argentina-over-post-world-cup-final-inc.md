## Football's governing body has appointed a prosecutor to assess the clashes between Argentine and Spanish players at the full-time whistle at the Metlife Stadium as well as the banner held by players after the semi-final with England.

FIFA has opened a disciplinary investigation into the Argentina national team over the incidents that occurred after the World Cup final, in which Spain were crowned champions. The body's disciplinary committee has appointed a disciplinary and ethics prosecutor to analyse **the referees' reports and the images of the match** to determine whether sanctions should be imposed on players or members of the Albiceleste coaching staff.

Among the incidents under investigation is **Leandro Paredes'** conduct**at the end of the match**. According to the footage, the midfielder was involved in a confrontation with several Spanish players, in which he grabbed** Eric García**by the neck and pushed** Gavi** to the floor during La Roja's title celebrations.

FIFA will also review the actions of **Nahuel Molina**, accused of striking** Rodri**, as well as the alleged involvement of Argentina's assistant coach,** Roberto Ayala**, over an apparent push on** Dani Olmo**. It will likewise examine the behaviour of several Argentina players during the trophy presentation ceremony, when some of them** turned their backs** as Spain prepared to lift the World Cup.

Argentina are already under investigation following the semi-final against England over possible breaches of FIFA regulations on political messages. In that match, **Giovani Lo Celso** displayed a banner bearing the slogan "Las Malvinas son argentinas" (The Malvinas are Argentine), a gesture that has already been criticised by the UK government.

Once all the evidence has been reviewed, **FIFA will decide whether to open individual disciplinary cases** and what sanctions could be imposed on those involved.
