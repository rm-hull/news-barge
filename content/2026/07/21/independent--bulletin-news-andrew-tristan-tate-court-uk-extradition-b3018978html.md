# Tate brothers to fight extradition to UK, says lawyer

![](https://cdn.jwplayer.com/v2/media/rhBdPm9g/poster.jpg?width=720)

- Andrew and Tristan Tate were arrested in Miami and are fighting extradition to the UK, where they face charges of rape and sex trafficking.
- The charges in the UK relate to allegations from multiple complainants, including rape, assault, trafficking, and offences concerning indecent images of a child, spanning from 2010 to 2017.
- Their lawyer, Joseph McBride, stated that the brothers are innocent and that their arrest and the extradition attempt are politically motivated.
- A US district judge will decide whether the Tates meet the conditions for extradition, with the next hearing set for 27 July.
- The brothers have consistently denied all allegations of abuse and human trafficking, including previous charges in Romania which have not progressed due to legal issues.
