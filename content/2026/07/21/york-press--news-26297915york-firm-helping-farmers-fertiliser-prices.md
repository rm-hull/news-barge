## York firm helps farmers cut fertiliser use by a quarter

High Yeilding - North Yorkshire farmer Derek Cornforth now uses Encera after trailing it at his farm in 2025. *(Image: Pic supplied)*

Farmers could soon slash their fertiliser use by up to a quarter, thanks to a York company

Azotic Technologies claims its bacteria-based product, Encera, improves nitrogen efficiency, helping farmers reduce reliance on traditional fertilisers.

The product is drawing increased interest following recent disruptions in global fertiliser markets, which have been linked to instability in the Middle East and the temporary closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a key route for energy supplies.

Sander Pabbruwee, CEO at Azotic, said: "Farmers are under real pressure to produce the same yields with less fertiliser.

"Our technology helps them do exactly that, by enabling crops to access nitrogen naturally from within the plant itself.

"Most nitrogen fertilisers are produced using energy-intensive processes that rely heavily on natural gas.

  Plant power: Encera is spread onto crops 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

"When disruption hits, prices rise quickly and farmers feel it immediately.

"What we’re hearing from farmers and growers is clear, they are looking for more consistent and predictable ways to manage inputs.

"By improving nitrogen efficiency, they can protect both their margins and their long-term resilience."

Encera relies on a naturally occurring bacterium that enables crops to access nitrogen internally throughout the growing season.

This process, which Azotic describes as Systemic Nitrogen Fixation, reduces overall fertiliser requirements, while supporting crop performance.

  Microscopic marvel- the bacteria behind Encera 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

Originally discovered in Brazilian sugarcane, the bacterium has been developed over 20 years of research.

The technology is suitable for a range of crops, including wheat, maize, oilseed rape and potatoes.

Derek Cornforth, a North Yorkshire farmer who trialled Encera on his land, said: "The results spoke for themselves.

"With fertiliser prices where they are, anything that helps reduce what I need to buy while still delivering a good crop is a real advantage."

Azotic believes that the long-term effects of current supply chain issues will continue to shape global fertiliser prices and availability, fuelling ongoing demand for alternative solutions.

The company says farmers are increasingly looking to reduce reliance on conventional fertilisers, improve efficiency, and build resilience into their production systems.

  Success story- Derek Cornforth and his ProCam agronomist Danny Hatchett 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

Headquartered at Hassacar Close, Four Lane Ends, just off the A1079 Hull Road, in York, Azotic Technologies also operates in North America and reports rising interest from farmers both locally and abroad.

Encera is supported by more than two decades of research.

Azotic believes its product could play a key role in helping farmers reduce reliance on traditional nitrogen fertilisers, manage input costs and improve resilience amid ongoing market instability.

More information about Azotic Technologies and Encera is available at www.azotic.com .
