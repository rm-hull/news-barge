Sweet treats will be up for grabs as part of a city centre treasure hunt.

To mark Yorkshire Day, Bettys is hosting a Fat Rascal token hunt in York on Friday, August 1, inviting visitors to join a playful search across the city.

A total of 125 wooden Rascal tokens will be hidden at notable locations throughout York.

Carol Hanson, manager of Bettys in York, said: "Yorkshire Day is the perfect moment to celebrate a little mischief, a lot of local pride and the unmistakable smile of the Fat Rascal.

"We hope families, visitors, and York residents enjoy following the clues and discovering Rascals across the city."

Participants can collect a map from the Bettys-branded pop-up stand in St Helen’s Square, follow the clues, and exchange any wooden Rascal they find for a real Fat Rascal in Bettys York.

The treasure hunt is part of wider Yorkshire Day celebrations at other Bettys locations in Ilkley, Northallerton and Harrogate, including Bettys at RHS Harlow Carr Gardens.

First introduced more than 40 years ago, the Fat Rascal has become one of Bettys’ most recognisable and well-loved bakery treats.

The pastry is a "plump, fruity scone decorated with a cherry and almond grin".
