## Two million people took to the streets of Madrid to welcome home their World Cup heroes last night. In the UK, Andy Burnham delivered his first address to the nation as Prime Minister, while in Germany Chancellor Friedrich Merz has signalled a government reshuffle.

## On today's show:

Spanish football journalists **Jorge López-Torrecilla** and**Pablo Montaño** join us to discuss Spain's World Cup success and why the team is cementing their place as a dominant force in world football as the country celebrated the title last night in Madrid.

Euronews' **Sonja Issel** reports on a potential Germany’s government reshuffle as Chancellor Friedrich Merz seeks to fill the vacancy left by his centre-right CDU party’s parliamentary leader Jens Spahn.

Euronews' Mared Gwyn speaks to **Professor Tony Travers** of the London School of Economics about the UK’s new prime minister, Andy Burnham, and the first government appointments.

Euronews' **Zoltán Siposhegyi** reports from Budapest on Hungary’s new president after Prime Minister Péter Magyar publicly called on chess icon Judit Polgár to become the country's next president, a request she politely declined. An interim president is now in post.
