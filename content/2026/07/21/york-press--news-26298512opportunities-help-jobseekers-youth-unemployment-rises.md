## Work experience pays as claimant count shows sharp rise in those aged 18-24

York Jobcentre has a range of initiatives to help get people into work, with it this month highlighting the importance of work experience. *(Image: NQ)*

Work experience is opening doors to employment and brighter futures, according to York Jobcentre.

Opportunities are being created through a York scheme for jobseekers to develop skills, gain confidence and take steps towards sustainable work, supported by placements in retail, warehousing and visitor attractions.

Sarah Lyus and Kelley Marriage, recruitment consultants at York Jobcentre, said: "Work experience is one of the most effective ways of helping people gain the skills, confidence and workplace understanding they need to progress into employment.

"We have seen first-hand the positive impact these opportunities can have on our customers, with many developing valuable skills and, in some cases, moving directly into work.

"We are keen to work with more local employers who can offer placements within their businesses.

"By supporting work experience opportunities, employers can play a vital role in developing the workforce of the future while making a real difference in their local community."

One participant recently secured a permanent job at B&M following a month-long placement organised through the Jobcentre.

The jobseeker said: "I've had one (unsuccessful) interview since February, and there were 60 applicants for that role.

"My work experience at B&M enabled me to demonstrate my skills, which resulted in them offering me a permanent job.

"I am absolutely delighted."

Placements are available across a wide range of sectors, including retail roles with B&M, warehouse experience at Barnardo’s, and customer service positions at York’s Cold War Bunker.

Work experience helps participants develop essential employability skills such as teamwork, communication, and time management, while building a better understanding of workplace expectations.

These placements not only boost confidence but also allow individuals to explore different career options before committing to further training or employment, the jobcentre adds.

Employers interested in offering placements and supporting jobseekers are encouraged to contact York Jobcentre’s Employer Engagement Team at [york.employerengagementteam@dwp.gov.uk.](mailto:york.employerengagementteam@dwp.gov.uk.)

The comments come as youth unemployment has risen sharply across the region over the past year.

Figures from the Department of Work and Pensions reports York having a claimant count in June of 2480, a decrease of 50 or 2 per cent on a year ago.

However, for those aged 18-24, the claimant count increased 75 or 16 per cent over the same period.

In Ryedale, the claimant count over the past year has increased 9 per cent or 55 to 645. For 18-24s, there was an increase of ten, or 15 per cent to 75.

In Selby, the claimant count increase by 20 or 2 per cent to 1,160. For 18-24s, the increase was of 10 per cent, or 20, to 238.

Nationally, the UK unemployment rate was unchanged at 4.9% in the three months to May 2026, unexpectedly defying expectations that it would increase to 5.0%.

The number of unemployed people fell by 17 thousand to 1.760 million, due to a drop in the number of people unemployed for 6-12 months and for more than 12 months.
