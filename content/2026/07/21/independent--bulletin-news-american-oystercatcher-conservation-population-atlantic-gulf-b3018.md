Extensive conservation efforts have led to a significant recovery of the American oystercatcher population along the Atlantic and Gulf coasts.

A decade-long, $10 million initiative involving 35 organizations across 16 states focused on protecting nesting habitat, educating beachgoers, and reducing threats from predators and human disturbance.

Between 2008 and 2023, the species experienced a 45% population increase, marking a conservation success story amidst declines in most other North American shorebird populations.

Tailored local conservation plans, developed by the American Oystercatcher Working Group, addressed specific drivers of decline such as low chick survival and recreational disturbance.

Despite the rebound, climate change remains the greatest long-term threat, and recent changes to Endangered Species Act regulations are raising concerns about future habitat protection.
