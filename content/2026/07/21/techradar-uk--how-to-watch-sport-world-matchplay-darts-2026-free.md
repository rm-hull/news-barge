World Matchplay Darts 2026 is well underway at Blackpool's Winter Gardens, with Luke Littler aiming to add yet more silverware to his trophy cabinet as he looks to retain the crown he won for the first time 12 months ago.

World No. 1 Littler headlines the bill as the top 16 players in the PDC World Rankings and the top 16 in the PDC ProTour Order of Merit do battle across nine days in one of the eight major events on the calendar. The 19-year-old, who beat James Wade in the 2025 final, is already one step closer to adding a second crown after seeing off Niko Springer 10-6 in the first round.

However, there has already been a shock in the opening round, with Littler's closest rival, Luke Humphries, defeated at the first hurdle. The Leeds-born star lost 10-7 to Cameron Menzies.

Littler will still face plenty of competition, though, with the likes of Michael van Gerwen, Gian van Veen, Gerwyn Price and Jonny Clayton all eager to upset the 19-year-old.
