Many Ukrainian refugee children are rapidly losing their mother tongue as they adapt to life in the UK.

Four years after the Russian invasion of Ukraine, an estimated 34,000 children are among 230,000 Ukrainian refugees living in the UK.

A new study by the University of York has found that, after just 12 months in English-speaking schools, many children are losing fluency in Ukrainian—even struggling to remember basic words.

Professor Monika Schmid, from the University of York’s Department of Language and Linguistics, said: "We wanted to know whether there would also be such a rapid process of decline in these Ukrainian refugee children, most of whom are in close and regular contact with family and friends, often including a parent, back in Ukraine."

The study warns that loss of language could make it difficult for children to communicate with relatives in Ukraine and hinder their ability to return and help rebuild the country after the war.

Professor Schmid added: "Four years into the war in Ukraine, it is now urgent that children are supported in maintaining their native language alongside English."

The research is now in its second phase, examining changes in language skills over the past two years.
