A Grammy-nominated singer is set to play two sell-out shows in Scarborough this week after "phenomenal demand" saw a second date added to his North Yorkshire stop.

Teddy Swims will take to the stage at Scarborough Open Air Theatre Wednesday, July 22 and Thursday, July 23, as part of the TK Maxx summer series, with both nights now at capacity.

The second show was announced after a rush for pre-sale tickets for the first date, highlighting the scale of demand for the Atlanta-born artist’s UK and Ireland tour.

  Teddy Swims, Grammy Nominee, set for two Scarborough shows (Image Claire Marie Vogel). 

*(Image: (Image Claire Marie Vogel).)*

Swims, who first built a following on YouTube, has since become an international chart-topper with a string of major hits.

His breakthrough track, 'Lose Control,' reached number one on the Billboard Hot 100, became the longest-charting hit in the chart’s history, and has racked up more than four billion streams worldwide.

The song featured on his debut album, 'I’ve Tried Everything But Therapy (Part 1),' released in 2024, which helped propel him to global success.

He has since followed it with 'Part 2' and a complete edition featuring collaborations with artists including GIVĒON, Muni Long, and Coco Jones.

He will be joined in Scarborough by his band, Freak Freely, with support from New Zealand-Australian singer and multi-instrumentalist, Jordan Rakei.

Speaking previously, Julian Murray, venue programmer for Cuffe and Taylor, said: "We are once again delighted to be bringing globally celebrated talent to the Yorkshire coast.

"Teddy Swims has enjoyed an incredible rise in the last few years and is rightly one of the most talked about performers.

"He’s a vocal powerhouse who is universally adored around the world and this will undoubtedly be a stunning night here at Scarborough Open Air Theatre."

Swims’ success has earned him a Grammy nomination for Best New Artist and multiple BET Award nominations, alongside a string of international honours.

  The double date adds to a busy summer at the open-air venue, which has already welcomed **Rick Astley**,** Madness**, Paul Weller, The Kooks, Skunk Anansie, Garbage, Anastacia, Heather Small, Pete Tong, Richard Ashcroft, Billy Ocean, Bowling for Soup, James Arthur, David Gray, Alanis Morissette, Michael Bublé, Deacon Blue, and Bastille.
