An animal sanctuary in York is appealing for the public's help to raise £2 million to transform its ageing facilities.

York Animal Home is aiming to upgrade its site to secure its future caring for vulnerable animals across York, Harrogate, and the surrounding areas.

The charity has set a target of raising £2 million for the initial phase of a redevelopment project that will create a modern cattery, specialist puppy facilities, and a new community welfare hub at the site in Landing Lane, York.

  York Animal Home has launched a public appeal to raise funds to redevelop and modernise its ageing facilities in Landing Lane. 

*(Image: York Animal Home)*

The first phase is part of a broader £5 million plan to completely overhaul the site over the coming years, with a target completion date for phase one of June 2027.

The appeal begins with £1.2 million already secured, including a significant £100,000 contribution from Pets at Home, leaving £800,000 still needed to complete the first phase.

  York Animal Home launches £2m appeal to secure future - Pictured, an artist's impression of the new-look York Animal Home in Landing Lane. 

*(Image: York Animal Home)*

While the home proudly operates under the RSPCA banner, it is an independently-funded local charity.

It relies on the generosity of supporters, businesses, charitable trusts, and foundations to fund its work caring for vulnerable animals across York, Harrogate, and the surrounding region.

  An artist's impression of the new-look York Animal Home in Landing Lane. 

*(Image: York Animal Home)*

However, the charity's facilities are now struggling to meet modern welfare expectations.

As construction has already started, obtaining the remaining funds is crucial to ensuring the project stays on schedule and to deliver the new facilities by June 2027.

For nearly half a century, York Animal Home has been a refuge for vulnerable animals who have been abandoned, neglected, mistreated, or left without other options.

  The refurbished York Animal Home will include a modern cattery. 

*(Image: York Animal Home)*

Annually, the home provides veterinary care, rehabilitation, and the chance for hundreds of dogs, cats, and small animals to find a loving new home.

Some of the accommodation for animals is already unusable, the puppy facilities can accommodate only a fraction of the animals they were originally designed for, and the ageing buildings are becoming increasingly difficult to maintain.

Without significant redevelopment, the charity faces growing challenges in continuing to provide the level of care that vulnerable animals deserve.

  The charity has been caring for vulnerable animals for almost 50 years. 

*(Image: York Animal Home)*

Lucy Lowthian, Capital Campaign Lead for York Animal Home, said: "Every animal that arrives at York Animal Home deserves a safe place to recover, heal and begin a new chapter.

"For almost 50 years we've been providing that second chance, but our facilities are no longer fit for the future.

"This redevelopment is about far more than buildings. It's about ensuring vulnerable animals across York, Harrogate and the surrounding region continue to receive the care they need for generations to come."

The wider redevelopment will ultimately create modern dog rehabilitation facilities and kennels, improved veterinary and adoption spaces, and sustainable buildings designed to reduce operating costs and support the charity's long-term future.

York Animal Home is encouraging individuals, businesses, charitable trusts, and community groups to get involved in the campaign.

  Supporters can contribute through one-off donations, regular giving, corporate partnerships and sponsorship, major gifts, trust and foundation support, legacy pledges, and through fundraising events and community initiatives.
