CITY of York Council announced a public transport update following a road closure caused by a sinkhole opening in the road of a York suburb.

A shuttle bus service began at 6.30am this morning (Tuesday, July 21) for residents affected by the void which opened in Salisbury Terrace, in the Leeman Road area of the city after a water main burst on Sunday evening (July 19).

  


The council said the bus has been laid on with the support of Yorkshire Water.

A City of York Council spokesperson said: "The bus will run a circular route from York Station via Leeman Rd, Park St, Garfield Terrace, Livingstone St, Salisbury Terrace, Water End, Poppleton Rd, Holgate Rd, Blossom St and back to York Station, approximately every 30 mins.

"The bus will stop at all stops on Park Street, Cinder Street, Livingstone St and Salisbury Road (heading towards Water End), and the Seldon Road stop near Poppleton Road Primary School.

"Then it will run express to Blossom Street and on to the Station where if needed passengers will be able to connect with other services."

The council said the last bus will leave the railway station at 10.30pm.

Residents who have waste collection scheduled for this week will receive a letter from the council with updated collection information.

As reported by The Press, crews have been working since Sunday to repair the damage after Yorkshire Water said the 21-inch water main burst was "significant" and the leak was "stopped promptly", adding it would "open the road as soon as it is safe to do so."

Salisbury Terrace residents described how their street "turned into a river", and a former pub and one home were flooded.

First Bus runs service numbers 59 and 10L along Salisbury Terrace.

The Press has contacted the company for information about potential planned diversions.
