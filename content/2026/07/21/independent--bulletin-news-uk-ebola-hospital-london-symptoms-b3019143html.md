# Brit in London hospital after ‘potential Ebola exposure in DR Congo’ – what we know

- A humanitarian worker is being monitored in a London hospital following potential exposure to Ebola in the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC).
- The individual, who was supporting the Ebola outbreak response in the DRC, is not displaying symptoms but was evacuated as a precautionary measure.
- They are being assessed and will isolate for the potential incubation period of two to 21 days.
- The UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA) states there are no confirmed Ebola cases in the UK, and the risk to the general public remains low.
- The current Bundibugyo variation of Ebola in the DRC outbreak has no vaccine or specific treatment, though human trials for a new jab are expected soon.
