## North Yorkshire venue launches weekday weddings to meet rising demand

Deighton Lodge is now offering weddings on weekdays *(Image: Image copyrighted to DS Wedding Photography: www.dsweddingphotography.com)*

A wedding venue near York has launched midweek ceremonies to meet growing demand.

Deighton Lodge, an independent wedding venue set on three acres of farmland is now offering weekday weddings in response to changing preferences among couples who want more flexible and affordable options.

The venue, founded in 2016 by businesswoman Carla Mitchell, has traditionally hosted ceremonies at weekends but will now accommodate weddings from Sunday to Thursday.

Ms Mitchell said: "When I look back at planning my own wedding, I resonate with many of the couples who visit us and the emotional weight that can come with securing weekend dates.

"Midweek weddings are something I’ve discussed with couples before, but because Deighton Lodge also operates as a guest house, I needed to ensure the logistics were better balanced, so the two sides of the business didn’t overlap."

  Deighton Lodge near York from above 

*(Image: Image copyrighted to DS Wedding Photography: www.dsweddingphotography.com)*

Ms Mitchell said: "This has now been achieved, which means we can offer midweek weddings and provide greater ceremony availability during peak season."

The decision follows research from the Bank of England indicating a rising trend in couples choosing weekday celebrations.

Deighton Lodge, which offers both indoor and outdoor ceremony spaces, is designed to be a peaceful and relaxed venue.

Ms Mitchell, who is also a trained yoga teacher, said: "Practising yoga has become my ‘go to’ for any worry and has helped me find inner peace.

"I believe it has helped me to better shape Deighton Lodge into a venue that feels peaceful, relaxed and full of character.

  Carla Mitchell, owner of Deighton Lodge 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

"Creating meaningful and stress-free wedding celebrations is incredibly important to me, and I feel we’ve got the ambiance of the space just right."

The venue is managed by a team of four, including newly appointed general manager Natasha Douglas, who previously served as head of housekeeping for five years.

Ms Mitchell said: "I feel very lucky to have Natasha as our new General Manager.

"She knows the logistics of Deighton Lodge incredibly well, and I’m confident she will help couples feel more at ease during the hustle and bustle of wedding planning."

Deighton Lodge features a large barn with space for up to 200 guests, a seven-bedroom farmhouse, manicured gardens, and multiple ceremony spaces.

More details are available at www.deightonlodge.com.
