# New signing Jack Butland believes unity ‘humongous’ at promoted Hull City

## Having played in the Championship and the Premier League, Jack Butland says unity will be the biggest thing Hull City need to make the step up next season, and he senses a dressing room which can deliver it.

Character and team spirit were arguably the Tigers' biggest strengths as they unexpectedly won last season's Championship play-offs but with owner/chairman Acun Ilicali targeting 14 new players in the transfer window, there is no guarantee it will still be intact for a season in one of the world's most competitive domestic leagues.

As the first signing of the window, experienced goalkeeper Butland is determined to do his best to preserve and even improve on those qualities.

"It's humongous," commented Butland, who made his England debut as a teenager but has played his most recent football in Scotland with Rangers. "It's the only way you can be successful, in my opinion, if you've got a group.

![AIMING HIGH: Goalkeeper Jack Butland  Picture: Andrew Milligan/PA](https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOjYzMzY3Mzc5LTM0YzQtNDU5OC1iNWJkLWQ3OTVjZmJlY2ZhYTozYmIzYjYxMS1iZmFlLTQzMmYtYWNjNS1iM2NjMDVlZGRjMWE=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



"And I'm not just talking about the 11 on the pitch. It's the group that surrounds that, that are all fighting for the same thing, being unselfish and working for the group.

"I've got an absolute desperation to play but if that's not the case, and there's been times where I've not been that guy, I'll be the biggest supporter of whoever is playing. That's me as a person.

"I'll encourage that if I have to out of the group. I don't think I need to, but I'll encourage that of everybody else if that's the case. It's only a group that can be successful.

"You can't have some wanting it and some wanting a way out and some not pulling in the same direction. You can't have that. And that's not something I think we're going to struggle with.

"I've always been a winner. I've always been someone that wants to perform at my best all the time. I've always been someone that demands of other people.

"I think when you're younger, you're probably a little bit more ruthless at times and you're probably a little bit less worried about how and when you say things.

"As you get older, you start to learn and realise when the right time is to say it, who it is that you're dealing with and how to speak to them. When you're young and you're just all guns blazing, you're not necessarily thinking about how you're saying it or who you're saying it to.

"As you get older, you start to see the game differently. You get to see people differently.

"You understand people as you get older. You understand whether someone needs an arm around them, whether someone needs an instruction or whether someone does need a good shout or whatever."

Despite his experience and his early signing, there is some doubt over whether Butland will be Hull's number one, with the club hoping to bring in Greece Konstantinos Tzolakis to have genuine competition at goalkeeper.

"Ultimately, the quality and the standard of the player goes up," explained the 33-year-old, who played in the Championship with Birmingham City, Leeds United, Barnsley and Derby County, the Premier League with Crystal Palace and both divisions with Stoke City.

"It's less intense in terms of your schedule, so you're going to have a lot more time on the training pitch and the build-up to games. There's a lot more time to prepare. They tend to be a little bit more tactical affairs and really rely on individual talent and quality.

"The Championship can be at times a bit of a boxing match and whoever wants it most on the day can sort of come out on top, whereas the Premier League is a little bit more tactical than that, a little bit more decisive in terms of the quality and what you bring on the day. And you've got to be at your very, very top level because you're going to come up against some top players. So the demands are slightly different.

"The way that you approach games is slightly different, but it's the best league in the world and it's a great league to play in.

"You can say the speed, you can say the style (are different for foreign players), but nowadays there's so many teams in the league that play the way that it's played in Spain. Teams like Man City and teams like that that play like your Spanish teams and you've got teams that have got the classical English style.

"You've got some that are a little bit more defensive.

"There's still a lot of challenges for them and no two games are the same. It's how you prepare. It's how you set up as a group yourself.

"It's just a challenge, 38 games to give it your best."
