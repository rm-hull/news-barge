A village near York is set to see the return of its only pub tomorrow.

Paula Watson will be re-opening the Tankard Inn at Rufforth at 12-noon on Wednesday.

The much-missed pub, owned by Tadcaster-based Sam Smith’s brewery, has been closed since September 2023.

Paul, who currently run’s the Paula’s Kitchen café at the other side of the village, said: “We are getting there.”

“We are just waiting for the benches coming for outside. It’s looking good. We have had the beers delivered, everything. I just want to go (and open the pub) now.”

Earlier, electricians are on site to change the pub’s lighting and “fix a few faults.”

It has also had a new kitchen put in.

Rufforth residents are excited about the return of The Tankard.

Paula continued: “They are all looking forward to it. Hopefully, I will get a lot of support from them from what they are saying.”

Already, the bellringers from the village have said they will come for a drink after their sessions.

Paula added: “I think we will do all right. I just want to be open. We are on track and raring to go.”

Initially, The Tankard will be a drink’s only pub but after several weeks, Paula plans to offer food, starting with Sunday roast.

As reported by the Press previously, Paula’s Kitchen in Mill Lane, which won York Press Best Sandwich Bar earlier this year, will continue to operate, but run by Paula’s husband.
