A MUCH-LOVED York teacher is retiring after 35 years of service.

Christine Wise, who has spent her entire career working in Catholic education, is stepping down from her role at St Aelred’s RC School in Tang Hall.

  


Head teacher Claire Hughes said: "Mrs Wise has dedicated more than 35 years to education, and her impact on St Aelred's has been truly immeasurable.

"As both a former pupil and a much-loved member of staff, she embodies everything our school stands for.

"Her unwavering faith, kindness, humility and dedication have shaped generations of children and inspired all members of the St Aelred's team throughout her career.

"Christine has always placed Christ at the heart of school life, nurturing the spiritual development of our children with compassion, wisdom and dedication."

Ms Hughes said Mrs Wise’s influence extended far beyond teaching, having led the school’s chaplaincy and liturgies and offered encouragement to everyone around her.

She also highlighted Mrs Wise’s service to the wider community as a devoted member of the parish at St Aelred’s, describing her as someone who served with generosity and faith.

Ms Hughes said: "Helen simply said, 'I'll just let God know.'

"For me, those few words perfectly capture Mrs Wise’s greatest gift.

"She has helped generations of children understand that faith is something they can carry with them every day, knowing that God is always there in both the big moments and the small ones."

Having attended St Aelred’s as a pupil herself, Christine returned to the school as a teacher during the early years of her career.

After a career that included a number of leadership roles across Catholic schools, she came back to St Aelred’s seven years ago to complete her teaching journey.

Ms Hughes said: "It is a truly special way to bring an extraordinary career to a close.

"As Christine herself says, 'It was obviously meant to be.'

"On behalf of everyone at St Aelred's, I would like to thank Mrs Wise for her extraordinary service and wish her a long, happy and well-deserved retirement.

"Although she is leaving the classroom, she will always remain a treasured part of our St Aelred's family."

She is also a devoted and active member of the parish community at St Aelred's.
