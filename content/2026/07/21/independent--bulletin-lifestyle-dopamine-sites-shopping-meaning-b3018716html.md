A new viral trend involves dopamine apps or sites where users simulate ordering food, shopping, or other activities without making actual purchases.

Examples include FoodNeverComes for fake food delivery, Dopamine Shop for virtual shopping, and No Smoke Zone for simulated smoking, all designed to provide a “dopamine hit” from the anticipation and selection process.

While these apps offer a sense of gratification without real-world cost, they raise concerns about potential ”gateway behaviours”.

Past research on social casino games indicates that simulated activities can lead users to engage in real-money gambling or similar behaviours.

Additionally, these free, ad-supported platforms collect user data through cookies, which can be used by marketers to deliver more targeted advertising for real products.
