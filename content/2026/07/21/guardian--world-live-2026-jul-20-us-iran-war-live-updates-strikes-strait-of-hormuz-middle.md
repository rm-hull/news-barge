Yemen's Houthis declare naval blockade against Saudi Arabia

Yemen’s Iran-backed Houthi rebels have announced a maritime embargo of Saudi Arabia.

The rebels “declare a maritime embargo against the criminal Saudi enemy, based on the equation of ‘an eye for an eye’ effective immediately upon the issuance of this statement,” military spokesperson Yahya Saree said.

The territory controlled by the Houthis in Yemen sits alongside the critical Bab al-Mandab strait, which connects the Red Sea to the Gulf of Aden and the Arabian Sea, and is vulnerable to Houthi attacks by drones missiles and small boats. The Red Sea route is responsible for about 15% of global naval shipping trade.

The decision by the Houthis could put new pressure on Saudi Arabia as it is using its East-West Pipeline to try to get oil out of the kingdom as the strait of Hormuz remains in the effective control of Iran.

Smoke rises after airstrikes on Sana’a international airport on 13 July 2026. Photograph: Xinhua/Shutterstock

Tensions between the Houthis and Saudi Arabia were inflamed earlier this month after Yemeni government planes, supported by Saudi Arabia, bombed the Houthi-controlled Sana’a airport in protest at Iranian efforts to send a plane to the city containing a Houthi delegation returning from the funeral of the Iranian supreme leader Ali Khamenei. The Houthis responded by targeting Saudi Arabia’s Abha international airport.

The Houthi militant group controls Sana’a, while Yemen’s internationally-recognised government, backed by Saudi Arabia, is based in the southern Yemeni city of Aden. For over a decade, aircraft entering Yemeni airspace have needed permission from the Saudi-led coalition first.

US issues 'worldwide caution' alert for American citizens

The US has issued a “worldwide caution” to American citizens amid the heightened tensions in the Middle East and the “potential for unforeseen escalation”.

The alert for US citizens abroad from the state department’s TravelGov, posted on social media, says:

U.S. diplomatic facilities, including outside the Middle East, have been targeted. Iran and groups supportive of Iran may target other U.S. interests overseas or at locations associated with the United States and Americans throughout the world.

Crew abandon targeted tanker and on lifeboat in strait of Hormuz – report

Iran’s claim on stopping two oil tankers in the strait of Hormuz comes as a British maritime agency says the crew of a tanker that was reportedly struck earlier had abandoned the vessel and were now in a lifeboat.

The United Kingdom Maritime Trade Operations agency gave the update – citing the tanker’s company security officer – in a report posted on X after earlier saying the tanker was reportedly struck by an unknown projectile eight nautical miles north-east of Limah, Oman.

Iran says blasts as it stopped two tankers crossing Hormuz strait

Two oil tankers caught fire after explosions while attempting to transit the southern route of the strait of Hormuz, Iran’s Revolutionary Guard has said.

It added in the statement on Tuesday that the strait would remain closed as long as the US military actions in the region continued.

However, the US military said after its 10th consecutive night of strikes on Iran that commercial vessel transits continue through the vital energy route and that US forces would “hold Iran accountable for unwarranted aggression toward civilian mariners”.

The Guard’s statement, carried by state news agency Irna, said:

Two non-compliant oil tankers attempting to pass through the unsafe southern route of the Strait of Hormuz were stopped after explosions caused extensive fires aboard them.

US says latest strikes hit Iran sites including military command centres

The US military says it has completed its latest round of strikes against Iran and asserts that transits through the strait of Hormuz “continue”.

Central Command (Centcom) said in a statement posted on X:

U.S. forces struck Iranian military command centers, maritime capabilities, missile and drone launch sites, and air defense systems to degrade Iran’s ability to continue attacking commercial vessels flowing through the Strait of Hormuz.

Commercial vessel transits through the vital international maritime corridor continue. Since early May, CENTCOM forces have helped facilitate the transit of approximately 900 commercial vessels and 450 million barrels of crude oil.

An image from video released by the US military showing American forces launching strikes against Iran. Photograph: US Central Command (Centcom)/AFP/Getty Images

Centcom also said:

American forces remain postured and prepared to hold Iran accountable for unwarranted aggression toward civilian mariners seeking to freely and openly transit the strait.

The latest strikes were completed at 9pm ET (1am GMT Tuesday), it said.

Oil prices ease after hitting highest level in over a month

Oil prices have softened as markets weigh reports of mediation efforts between the US and Iran against the fresh strikes between the two and threats of a naval blockade of Saudi Arabia by Yemen’s Houthis.

Brent crude futures eased 35 cents, or 0.4%, to $88.87 a barrel by 0052 GMT on Tuesday, while US West Texas Intermediate crude for September delivery was steady at $82.47 a barrel, Reuters is reporting.

Both contracts were trading below their highest levels in more than a month hit in the previous session.

The chief market analyst at KCM Trade, Tim Waterer, said:

The threats of a naval blockade on Saudi Arabia by the Houthis are significant because they raise the risk of disruption to another major oil exporter.

The latest US-Iran hostilities are likely to cast a shadow over a diplomatic gathering this week in south-east Asia – a region that relies heavily on Middle East oil and gas and was severely hit by the global supply and price shocks the war triggered.

The Middle East crisis is expected to feature prominently in the talks of foreign ministers from the 11-member Association of Southeast Asian Nations in Manila, said a spokesperson for host the Philippines.

“We’re looking at several occasions where they can raise this very, very critical topic,” Dominic Xavier Imperial said, quoted by Reuters.

The import-dependent Philippines declared a national energy emergency in March and has been forced to cast an ever-wider net for fuel, including purchases of oil from Russia, whose foreign minister Sergei Lavrov is to arrive on Tuesday, AFP reports.

US secretary of state Marco Rubio landed in Manila on Tuesday morning as the rekindled war with Iran places traditional American allies under economic strain.

Strikes reported in Iran as US launches 10th round of attacks

Here’s more on the US military’s 10th consecutive round of strikes on Iran to force the reopening of the strait of Hormuz.

William Christou reports that earlier on Monday, Iran’s president said the country was in a “full-scale war” after the US expanded its aerial campaign and Tehran struck Washington’s Gulf allies, even as diplomats said talks to de-escalate were continuing.

Iranian media reported strikes around Tabriz, believed to hold underground missile bases, as well as Bushehr, home to the country’s only operational civilian nuclear power plant.

The US military said it had struck “military command centres, air defence and coastal surveillance sites, maritime capabilities, missile and drone launch sites and communications networks”.
