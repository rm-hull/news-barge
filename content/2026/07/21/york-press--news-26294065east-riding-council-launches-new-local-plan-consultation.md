Search the Public Notice PortalEast Riding of Yorkshire Council has begun work on its latest local plan, giving people the chance to have their say.

The project is launching with a public scoping consultation open until Monday 31 August 2026.

The consultation invites feedback on planning issues, national policy alignment, and stakeholder engagement methods.

Residents and stakeholders can take part in the consultation at www.eastriding.gov.uk/localplan.

Councillor Nick Coultish, cabinet member for planning, said: "The local plan plays a key role in shaping developments in our region, and we highly encourage residents to share their views throughout the process."

The new plan is prompted by the Government’s introduction of a 30-month streamlined local plan system.

It will replace the 2025 Local Plan Update once adopted.

This scoping consultation will then inform the later stages of the plan-making process, which will need to identify the scale of development that will come forward over a 15-year period and specific sites where new development will be supported.

The council is also seeking input on how best to engage with stakeholders throughout the process.

The updated plan will guide planning decisions, allocate land for new development, and inform investment across the East Riding.
