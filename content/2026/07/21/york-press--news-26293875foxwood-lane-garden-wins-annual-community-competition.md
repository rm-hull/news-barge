A York garden bursting with colour and community charm has taken home the top prize in a neighbourhood competition.

Mr Russell Stone of Foxwood Lane has been named winner of the 2026 Garden of the Year contest, organised by the Foxwood Residents Association with sponsorship from Indigo Greens estate agents.

His garden, which features Begonias, Lobella, Petunias, Impatiens, Marigolds, evergreen shrubs, and a gnome, was described by judges as "bringing a burst of colour and character to Foxwood Lane".

Simon Cartwright, from Indigo Greens, said: "A well-kept garden doesn’t just lift a street — it lifts everyone’s spirits.

"Colourful front gardens like Russell’s make Foxwood feel even more welcoming.

"We’d like to thank all the entrants for their hard work and look forward to seeing even more colour next summer."

Mr Stone received a trophy and a £100 cash prize for his efforts.

Shirley Gumley, chair of the Foxwood Residents Association, said: "We’ve had a record number of entries this year, which shows how much pride people take in their neighbourhood.

"Foxwood’s community spirit is truly in full bloom."

The long-running competition forms part of wider efforts by the association to brighten the area, including the distribution of wildflower seeds and the sowing of new patches in hedgerows.
