A much-loved floral event is marking its 50th anniversary in North Yorkshire.

The Harrogate Autumn Flower Show, taking place at Newby Hall & Gardens, will celebrate five decades of horticultural excellence this September.

Founded in 1975 by the North of England Horticultural Society, the show began as the Great Autumn Flower Show and first took place in 1976 in Harrogate’s Valley Gardens.

Martin Fish, a former show director and NEHS president, said: “Harrogate Autumn Flower Show holds so many fond memories for me, from my first visit as a young gardener in the early 1980s to later becoming President of the NEHS and then Show Director.

"It is a real honour to be part of an event that represents the culmination of months, and sometimes years, of dedicated growing.

"There is a wonderful harvest festival feel about the show, with beautiful blooms, immaculate fruit and vegetables and specialist nursery displays.

"Now at Newby Hall, visitors also get to enjoy the gardens as they enter their beautiful autumn phase.”

The event quickly grew in popularity, moving to the Exhibition Halls in 1983, then the Great Yorkshire Showground in 1995, before relocating to Newby Hall & Gardens in 2021.

At the heart of the show are its historic and competitive displays, including long-running partnerships with the National Dahlia Society and National Chrysanthemum Society.

Ian Ledger, secretary of the National Dahlia Society, said: "The idea of bringing multiple gardening societies together under one umbrella show was brilliant.

"The Great Autumn Flower Show created a unique opportunity to celebrate specialist horticulture together, and it remains an incredibly important event for growers.

"From a dahlia exhibitor’s perspective, it is the best dahlia show in the country, and it is a privilege to display and judge these wonderful flowers alongside other specialist societies."

The event is equally renowned for its fruit and vegetable competitions, such as the National Onion Championship, which began in 1983 and has produced several world record-breaking onions.

Visitors can also admire the perfectly formed produce of the National Vegetable Society, highlighting the skill and dedication of master growers.

This year’s show will include the return of its signature competitions, with hopes high for another record-breaking onion.

Food will also be a highlight, with chef Stephanie Moon preparing 1970s-inspired seasonal dishes in one of Newby Hall’s kitchen garden areas.

A floral art installation by designer Jonathan Moseley, titled ‘Grain & Grandeur…The Harvest Ball,’ will be on display within the principal rooms of Newby Hall.

The event also offers family-friendly features, including the Little Nature Rangers Zone, birds of prey demonstrations, face painting, and floral crafts.

The Harrogate Autumn Flower Show takes place from September 18 to 20 at Newby Hall & Gardens.

Tickets are available via www.flowershow.org.uk or on the gate.
