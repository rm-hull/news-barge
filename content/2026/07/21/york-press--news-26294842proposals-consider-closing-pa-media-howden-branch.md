A trade union has condemned proposals to shut a news agency branch, saying it has helped train and support generations of journalists.

PA Media Group is considering closing its long-standing office in Howden, East Yorkshire, as part of plans to relocate its northern hub to Leeds.

The move follows a review of the group’s office spaces, focusing on future business needs and long-term strategy.

  


But the National Union of Journalists (NUJ) have said it is concerned for the branch’s more than 250 employees, who would be expected to leave Howden – home to PA since 2002.

A spokesperson said: “For more than 20 years, PA staff have chosen to build careers and homes in the market town of Howden.

“Many have expressed deep sorrow at the prospect of leaving the area and are concerned at the potential impact that shutting the office might have on the local community.

“Since the consultation was announced last week, PA chapel representatives have been on site liaising with journalists who are affected by the proposal, which could put staff at potential risk of redundancy if they chose not to relocate in the event that the Howden office closes.”

A PA Media Group spokesperson said the company would be engaging in a “meaningful” consultation process – and said no final decision had been made.

A statement read: “We will be ensuring that colleagues and their representatives have the opportunity to provide feedback before any outcomes are confirmed.

“This proposal follows a company-wide review of the property portfolio, including how our office space is used by colleagues, the needs of our business and how our workplaces can best support the Group’s long-term strategy.”

Chris Morley, NUJ Northern & Midlands senior organiser, said: “Although many employees at Howden felt for some time the hub’s days might be numbered, such a significant announcement about complete closure of the site inevitably is the source of much concern.

“A generation of journalists working for the Press Association have built their lives around this site and to end that is always going to be painful for many.

“The plans to make those roles in the page production unit redundant is a particularly difficult one for those in skilled jobs looking to the future but in a declining print market.

He added that the NUJ will be “working hard” to achieve the “best possible outcomes” for members affected.

He said: “It is essential that the company finds a way to retain and accommodate as many staff as possible to maintain the journalistic quality and experience that made the company’s name.”
