Iran on Monday vowed to continue striking American targets “for years” if required as diplomacy slipped further out of view after a ninth consecutive day of strikes on the country.

The Islamic Revolutionary Guards Corps (IRGC) pushed back on Donald Trump’s recent claims that Iran is running out of munitions, as the regime continues to pound US facilities in neighbouring countries.

“[He] should know that, by God's grace, even if this war lasts for several years, until the very last day, our missiles and drones will rain down on American criminals,” the statement said.

Meanwhile, an American official familiar with internal discussions told the *Washington Post* that the US is still “planning for a wider war” by rushing military aircraft to the region - but could face its own shortages.

The official, speaking on condition of anonymity, warned that US operations would be limited by low stockpiles of air defences and long-range munitions, however.

## Oil prices dip as mediation efforts offset US-Iran strikes

Oil prices eased this morning as markets weighed reports of mediation efforts between the US and Iran against an exchange of fresh attacks between the two and threats of a naval blockade of Saudi Arabia by Yemen's Houthis.

Brent crude futures eased 35 cents, or 0.4 per cent, to $88.87 per barrel, while US West Texas Intermediate crude for September delivery was steady at $82.47 a barrel.

Both contracts were trading below their highest levels in more than a month hit in the previous session.

## Crew abandons ship as tanker hit by projectile in Strait of Hormuz

A tanker in the Strait of Hormuz reported being struck by an unknown projectile, forcing its crew to abandon the vessel and board a lifeboat, the United Kingdom Maritime Trade Operations agency (UKMTO) said this morning.

UKMTO said it had received multiple reports that a tanker had broadcast over VHF Channel 16 that it had been hit by an unknown projectile. The incident was reported eight nautical miles east of Oman's Limah and authorities were investigating.

UKMTO said the tanker's company security officer had reported that the crew had abandoned the vessel and were aboard a lifeboat and no environmental impact had been reported.

Separately, Iran's Revolutionary Guard said this morning that two oil tankers had caught fire after explosions while attempting to transit the southern route of the strait.

The Guard said rescue teams were removing the crews from the area but did not identify the vessels.

## Diplomatic efforts made to restore ceasefire

A senior Iranian official said on Monday that Tehran had received a proposal from mediators for a 10-day ceasefire in efforts to salvage the interim deal, intended to pave the way to a lasting agreement to end the war that began on 28 February with US-Israeli attacks on Iran.

Neither the ministry nor the official who spoke to Reuters gave any details on the putative ceasefire discussions.

Two Pakistani government sources said separately that Iranian Interior Minister Eskandar Momeni had asked Pakistan to resume its mediation role in the conflict. He later arrived in Islamabad for further talks.

The diplomatic push followed another night of US strikes on Iranian cities and attacks by Iran's Revolutionary Guards on US military assets across the region. U.S. Central Command said Monday afternoon US time that it had begun another round of strikes on Iran.

President Donald Trump, under mounting political pressure at home over rising gasoline prices since the war began and Iran effectively closed the vital Strait of Hormuz, defended the latest strikes on Iran as honouring the deaths of American service members killed in recent Iranian attacks.

## Iran war is America's leader popular since polls began

Net approval for Donald Trump’s decision to attack Iran is now as low as -30 per cent among Americans, according to recent polling.

Among Democrats, approval of the conflict now sits at -84%. Independents sit at -52%, while MAGA Republicans buck the trend with +72%, per *The Economist*.

More than half only approve “somewhat” of the handling of the war, while non-MAGA Republicans have turned from supporting the war on balance (+26% in April to -25% now), per Washington Post/Ipsos.

## Recap: Pentagon withheld dozens of US military injuries during Iran war - report

Dozens of US service members were injured in a fresh Iranian attack on Friday, several US officials have said, despite no announcement from the Pentagon.

The attacks injured US forces and damaged helicopters, they told the New York Times on condition of anonymity.

Those strikes were not disclosed by the Pentagon, however, which an official said is not required to release information about injured service members.

The NYT cast the revelation as the latest example of friction between the Pentagon’s insistence on security versus the government’s obligation to be transparent with the public about its war conduct.

The UK government is currently advising against all but essential travel to that area.

Jordan, Bahrain and Kuwait have reported intercepting Iranian drones and missiles as attacks across the region have intensified.

At least two US service members were killed in action in Jordan while defending against Iranian ballistic missile and drone attacks.

## Video appears to show Iranian missile attack on airbase which killed two US service members

Footage appears to show the moment that Iranian missiles hit an airbase in Jordan on Friday (17 July), killing two US service members.

Another service member is missing, whilst four others were medically evacuated to hospitals in Jordan and have since been discharged.

The two US deaths were the first to occur following the collapse of a ceasefire that was agreed upon last month.

On Monday (20 July), the US military said it completed its ninth consecutive evening of airstrikes against Iran.

At least 15 US service members have now died during the five-month-long war with Tehran, including six people who were killed when two refuelling jets supporting US military operations crashed in Iraq.
