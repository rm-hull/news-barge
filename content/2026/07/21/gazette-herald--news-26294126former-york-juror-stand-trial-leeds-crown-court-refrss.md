A North Yorkshire businessman has denied committing an offence while sitting as a juror on a trial at York Crown Court.

David Whan, 60, of Old Barber, Harrogate, is alleged to have told fellow jurors information relating to the case they were sitting on that was not given in the courtroom. The offence allegedly occurred in 2024.

He denied the charge of disclosing information to other jurors when he appeared before Leeds Crown Court as a defendant.

Judge Neil Clark ordered that Whan’s trial start on August 26 next year. It is expected to last for two days and will be held at Leeds Crown Court.

Whan was released on conditional bail until then.
