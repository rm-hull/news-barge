# Trump reveals what he told Canadian PM at World Cup final

- President Donald Trump confronted Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney at the World Cup Final, asserting that smoke from Canadian wildfires was "poisoning" US air and demanding action to stop the fires.
- Trump suggested Canada should pay damages or face tariffs for the environmental impact, later reiterating on Truth Social that Canada was responsible for "Willful Negligence" in forest management.
- The wildfires, which started on July 6 from lightning strikes, have burned over 68,956 acres, causing harmful air pollution to drift into northeastern U.S .cities, leading to business disruptions and health issues.
- Carney, in turn, had previously urged the Trump administration to take the climate crisis more seriously, stating that climate change is everyone's responsibility, including the U.S.
- Despite the leaders' exchange, no tensions were evident between them at the game, though Trump was booed by the crowd and an Argentinian player declined to shake his hand during the medal ceremony.
