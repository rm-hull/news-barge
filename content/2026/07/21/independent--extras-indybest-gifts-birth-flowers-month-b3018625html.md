Like birthstones, the idea of birth flowers may have been around for a long time, but lately it’s been having a bit of a moment, especially when it comes to gift-giving.

Long before personality quizzes and astrology memes took over the internet, each month of the year became linked to its own specific bloom. From the loyal carnation of January to March’s classic signal of spring, the daffodil, knowing someone’s birth flower can help you choose a more meaningful bouquet.

Each flower carries its own symbolism: carnations represent devotion, roses are tied to love and admiration, and lilies often signal celebration and purity, so if you love the idea of matching a person to the flower, you’ll be creating incredibly special gifts.

To make things easy, Interflora’s birthday flowers collection takes birth flowers as a starting point for expertly handcrafted bouquets created using fresh seasonal blooms. The online florist details all the flowers associated with each month, and recommends choosing based on your loved one’s birth month:

- **January birth flower:** Carnations
- **February birth flower:** Violet
- **March birth flower:** Daffodils
- **April birth flower:** Daisy
- **May birth flower:** Lily
- **June birth flower:** Rose
- **July birth flower:** Delphinium
- **August birth flower:** Gladioli
- **September birth flower:** Aster
- **October birth flower:** Marigold
- **November birth flower:** Chrysanthemum
- **December birth flower:** Holly

There’s also a zodiac angle, with star signs paired to floral styles that reflect personality traits. For example, for a Sagittarius, Interflora recommends carnations, and for a Pisces, it suggests water lilies. Whether you pick a bouquet based on a loved one’s birth month or star sign, it’s a great way of gifting something that feels more personal than a standard arrangement.
