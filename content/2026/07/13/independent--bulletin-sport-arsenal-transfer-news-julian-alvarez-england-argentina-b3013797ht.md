# Arsenal step up pursuit of Argentina star set to face England at the World Cup

![](https://cdn.jwplayer.com/v2/media/wb4wj3d9/poster.jpg?width=720)

- Arsenal are actively pursuing a deal to sign Atletico Madrid's Julian Alvarez before the pre-season, with the 26-year-old striker being Mikel Arteta's primary attacking target.
- Alvarez scored a superb curling goal, an extra-time stunner for Argentina in their 3-1 victory over Switzerland in their World Cup quarter-final on Saturday and is due to face England on Wednesday.
- Despite Alvarez's initial preference to remain in Spain with either Barcelona or Real Madrid, Atletico Madrid are insistent on not selling him to their rivals.
- A significant hurdle in the transfer is the valuation, as Atletico are demanding over £100m, while Arsenal are unwilling to exceed £90m for the player.
- The World Cup has complicated and slowed down transfer negotiations, with Arsenal's sporting director, Andrea Berta, involved in discussions with his former club.
