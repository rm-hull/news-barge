A NUMBER of deliberate fires were set across York yesterday evening (Sunday, July 12), with firefighters responding to at least three reports of blazes in parks and on grassland.

The incidents began at 6.25pm on Foxwood Lane, where a crew from Acomb found three separate grass fires that were believed to have been started by youths.

These were extinguished using buckets of water and beaters, the service said, and police were informed.

  


At 7.29pm, crews were called to a park in Thanet Road, where around six square metres of grassland had been set alight.

Firefighters used a hose reel jet to put out the flames and youths were thought to be responsible and police were brought in.

A short time later, at 7.46pm, another report of youths setting fires in a park in Bellhouse Way was received and police were notified.

No action was needed as the fire was already out when crews arrived.

At 8.53pm, a tree was found on fire in a park on Leeman Road and was extinguished by firefighters.
