A bistro found in the centre of York has been named the best restaurant in Yorkshire and the North East by a prestigious guide.

The Good Food Guide has released its list of Britain’s 100 Best Local Restaurants for 2026 in partnership with Square.

The list features restaurants from all over the UK, including the Black Wheat Club in York, which managed to take tenth place.

The modern bistro is known for its seasonal cooking and small-batch bakes to create a "relaxed" dining experience.


## What is the Black Wheat Club in York like?

The bistro’s website reads: "Our brunch menu highlights fresh, locally sourced ingredients, served alongside house-baked breads, pastries, and speciality coffee.

"In the evening, we offer a changing selection of seasonal sharing plates that are bold, playful, and built to surprise.

"The wine list is ever-evolving, approachable and full of gems.

"We pour wines from winemakers who share our philosophy, both in craft and care.

"Everything on your plate at Black Wheat Club is made in-house.

"From bread and ferments to sausages.

"Our menus evolves constantly, driven by the freshest seasonal ingredients we can source.

"We work closely with local producers and farmers who share our commitment to quality and craft."

The restaurant offers a breakfast and lunch menu, a variety of plates on its dinner menu, a great selection of wines, and a takeaway deli menu to enjoy its dishes at home.

The breakfast menu features options including classic smoked salmon poached eggs to more inventive choices like the salt beef poached eggs that is topped things like sauerkraut and cucumber pickles.

You can pick a range of dinner plates, including the BWC lamb sausages, grilled chicken thighs with oyster mushrooms and summer cabbage, and the BWC potatoes, to create your own meal.


For those who love sweet treats, you could try one of their homemade pastries alongside a coffee or matcha drink.

On Good Food Guide’s list, the Head of Reviews & Listings at the Good Food Guide, Chloë Hamilton, said: "In the face of adversity, creativity has flourished in unusual and unlikely places.

"It has been hugely rewarding to see how these tiny operations are offering outstanding quality in a way that so clearly strikes a chord with the local crowd.

"Arrive with an open mind and a good appetite and you’ll be brilliantly rewarded."
