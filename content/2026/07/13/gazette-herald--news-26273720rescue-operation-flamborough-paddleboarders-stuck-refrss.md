PADDLEBOARDERS and a dog have been rescued after getting into difficulty in rocks off the East Yorkshire coast.

Bridlington Coastguard Rescue Team was called to North Landing in Flamborough, around midday yesterday (Sunday, July 12), where a woman had suffered a serious injury to her lower leg as the tide rose.

A tethered rescue operation saw an officer enter the water to reach the group and the injured woman and her dog were floated to safety under the supervision of the emergency crew.

  



The other individual was guided back across the rocks to the beach.

The injured woman received immediate first aid from Coastguard Rescue Officers onshore.

She was then transferred to a Yorkshire Ambulance Service crew for more medical attention.

A spokesperson for Bridlington Coastguard Rescue Team said: "Today’s incident serves as a timely reminder that whilst our coastline can appear calm and inviting, conditions can change quickly and seemingly accessible areas can become hazardous.

"Always wear appropriate clothing and safety equipment, carefully consider whether your chosen activity is suitable for the conditions, and ensure you have a reliable means of calling for help."
