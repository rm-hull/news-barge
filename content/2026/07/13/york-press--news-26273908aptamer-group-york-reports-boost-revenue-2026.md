York-based life sciences company Aptamer Group, known for its Optimer binders, expects to report revenue of approximately £1.5 million for the year ending June 30 2026 (FY2026), an increase of 25 per cent on last year.

Dr Arron Tolley, chief executive, said: "We are pleased to report strong revenue growth for FY2026, reflecting continued commercial momentum and successful delivery across the business.

"The increase in revenues, alongside the expansion of our pipeline to £4.8 million, demonstrates growing demand for our Optimer® platform and our ability to convert opportunities into contracted programmes."

Optimer binders consist of artificial strands of DNA that target molecules such as proteins, toxins or cells. Often called "chemical antibodies", they serve as synthetic, highly adaptable alternatives to traditional antibodies.

Aptamer Group's order book for FY2027 currently stands at £600,000, while its overall sales pipeline has grown by 55 per cent to £4.8 million.

The company also generated its first licensing revenue during the year, following agreements with Twist Bioscience and Alphazyme.

Dr Tolley said: "A key strategic milestone was achieved in the generation of our first licensing revenues following agreements signed in December 2025.

"Together with encouraging partner feedback and ongoing commercial discussions, this reinforces our confidence in the long-term potential of our higher-margin licensing strategy."

Operational highlights for the year included the delivery of Optimers to a global life sciences conglomerate for customer validation.

If successful, Aptamer will receive a two per cent royalty on sales of immunohistochemistry and diagnostic assay kits using the technology.

Other projects include ongoing collaborations with top pharmaceutical companies, work with Invizius, and the completion of binder selection for Metir plc’s Cryptosporidium programme.

The company also advanced a food fortification rapid test project, with binders due to be delivered to Imperial College London for development into a lateral flow device in July 2026.

Dr Tolley said: "With a strong sales pipeline, increasing commercial traction and an emerging licensing revenue stream, we believe the Group is well positioned to continue its progress into FY2027 and beyond."
