# Great Yorkshire Show sold out as Harrogate prepares to welcome 150,000 visitors



## The countdown is on to the opening of the 167th Great Yorkshire Show, with organisers putting the finishing touches to the Harrogate showground ahead of tomorrow’s launch.

Around 150,000 visitors are expected to attend the four-day event, which runs from Tuesday to Friday, with all tickets now sold out.

Livestock remains at the heart of the show, with thousands of animals on display, including national shows for Charolais cattle and Zwartbles sheep, alongside competitions featuring some of the UK's leading breeders.

Among those taking part is Georgia Bowman, who will compete in Thursday's Dairy Young Handlers competition with her calf, Parkhouse Foggathorpe. The Bowman family, who have bred Dairy Shorthorns in Weardale since 1812, will be showing six home-bred cattle throughout the week.



Visitors will also be able to see one of the show's biggest attractions – the New Holland CR11 combine harvester, believed to be the world's largest, which is on display on the President's Lawn.



The expanded Agri-Innovation Village will showcase the latest developments in farming technology, including a modular biomethane refuelling station, a New Holland biomethane-powered tractor and Toyota's hydrogen-powered Hilux prototype, making its debut at a UK agricultural show.

Elsewhere, Walworth Birds of Prey will make its final appearance at the Great Yorkshire Show before retiring from the agricultural show circuit. The team will give twice-daily displays in the Countryside Arena throughout the week.



Show Director Rachel Coates said: "The show is the highlight of the year for many of our exhibitors and visitors, and I am very excited about sharing the wealth of farming, food and countryside features we have on offer.

"We are indebted to the whole team who come together to help deliver a fantastic day out for our visitors."

Celebrity guests appearing on the Ariat GYS Stage include Kelvin and Liz Fletcher on Tuesday, Peter Wright from *The Yorkshire Vet* on Wednesday, Jules Hudson on Thursday and Harriet Cowan from*Clarkson's Farm* on Friday.

Free shuttle buses will run every 10 minutes between Harrogate Bus Station and the White Gate entrance throughout the show, with services starting at 7.20am each day.
