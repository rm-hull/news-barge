# At least 27 killed in Bangkok blaze that ripped through popular bar

- A devastating fire at the Rong Beer Na Lat Phrao bar in Bangkok, Thailand, early on Monday resulted in 27 fatalities and 63 injuries.
- Initial investigations suggest the blaze was caused by an electrical short circuit in an air-conditioning unit, starting near the stage and spreading rapidly.
- Obstructed emergency exits, blocked by beer crates and a table, significantly hampered patrons' escape, trapping many inside the venue.
- Thai Prime Minister Anutin Charnvirakul has ordered a full investigation into the "national tragedy," with authorities working to identify all victims and offering compensation.
- This incident marks another in a series of deadly fires in Thailand over the past three decades, highlighting ongoing safety concerns.
