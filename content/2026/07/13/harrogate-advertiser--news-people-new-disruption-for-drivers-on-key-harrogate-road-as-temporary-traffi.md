# New disruption for drivers on key Harrogate road as temporary traffic lights return 'at wrong time'

## Harrogate motorists are experiencing delays on a major road just in time for the Great Yorkshire Show.

After 18 months of roadworks, road closures and temporary traffic controls on and off across Harrogate, the latest disruption is taking place the A59 on Knaresborough Road towards Harrogate District Hospital end.

Temporary traffic lights were installed on Saturday to enable Yorkshire Water to carry out essential utility repair and maintenance works.

The disruption on Knaresborough Road is, coincidentally, scheduled to carry on until 6pm on July 17.

There have already been concerns over the traffic situation for this year’s Great Yorkshire Show after news of another delay to the £82.5 million project to reroute the A59 route at Kex Gill.

The new 2.5-mile stretch of road, which bypasses the landslip-prone section between Skipton and Blubberhouses, had been due to open last week.

Construction began in 2023 and has been described by North Yorkshire Council as its most ambitious highways project to date due to the complexity of the ground conditions.

The latest delay on completion is expected to create problems for drivers coming from Skipton for the show.

Harrogate drivers have become accustomed to disruption on the A59 between Harrogate and Knaresborough.

In fact, there are more than one set of roadworks right now.

North Yorkshire Council is currently undertaking essential highway repairs and maintenance works at another small section of Knaresborough Road.

Those are scheduled to end at 4pm on July 14.

The area’s long-running transport headaches during an era of new housing developments have been raised several times by Harrogate and Knaresborough MP Tom Gordon who has labelling the management of roadworks "disastrous".

Earlier this year, North Yorkshire Council won permission from the Department of Transport to introduce a lane rental scheme to charge utility companies for carrying out roadworks on key roads during the busiest times of the day.

Under the scheme, companies would incur daily fees of up to £2,500 for undertaking maintenance work at peak times.
