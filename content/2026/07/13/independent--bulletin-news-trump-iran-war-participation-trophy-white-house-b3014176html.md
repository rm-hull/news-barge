# White House reacts to ‘Iran War Participation Trophy’ on National Mall mocking Trump

- A giant satirical “participation trophy” statue appeared on the National Mall in Washington, D.C., on Monday, mocking the Trump administration's continued stalemate in the Iran war.
- The statue's plaque “awards President Donald J. Trump this participation trophy for his enthusiastic involvement in the Iran War,” suggesting participation without a clear victory.
- The artwork was created by Secret Handshake, an anonymous group of artists known for installing similar prank statues, including one depicting Trump and Jeffrey Epstein.
- White House spokeswoman Anna Kelly criticized the artists as “untalented” and asserted that the U.S. military achieved all its objectives during Operation Epic Fury.
- The trophy plays on President Trump's known affinity for awards, while his administration continues to claim victory in Iran despite a collapsed peace deal and plans to reimpose a blockade on Iranian ports.
