## Top York venue gets update on food hygiene after recent one-star rating

Agility Parks at Nether Poppleton is back to five stars for food hygiene following a recent re-inspection. *(Image: Agility Parks)*

The boss of a popular leisure facility which scored one-star for food hygiene has urged other businesses to “respond swiftly, transparently and thoroughly” should it happen to them.

Giuseppe Caringella, general manager of Agility Parks at Nether Poppleton, gave the advice this week after his venue gained a five-star rating for food hygiene following a re-inspection on June 23.

It followed City of York Counill awarding just one star, meaning major improvement necessary, during a previous inspection on March 9.

City of York Council inspectors found the venue had a broken boiler, and though it was fixed, council officials felt the time the centre relied on boiling water on a cooker was too long, leading to the prospect of possible action on future visits.

A copy of the inspectors' report also said several items of food were found to be up to three days past their use-by dates. The inspectors called for better cleaning of surfaces, including floors and doors. The food hygiene awareness among staff was branded ‘inadequate'.

Soon after the Press reported the incident, Agility Parks explained to the Press the actions it had taken, stressing that at no time, were any customers at risk.

Now, Guiseppe Caringella says he is extremely pleased Agility Parks has the five star rating it has always had previously, following the re-inspection.

He explained: “It reflects the significant investment of time and resources our team put in to uphold our standards, and we regard it as validation of that work.

“Following the initial rating, we acted without delay. The hot water boiler replacement was already booked in prior to the visit, and within a few days was installed.

“We subsequently implemented a state of the art food labelling system, substantially enhanced our food safety management systems, and ensured all staff underwent full retraining to align with the required standards.”

Guiseppe continued: “The majority of our customer base remained loyal. We attribute this to the transparency of our response and the speed with which demonstrable improvements were made, which gave customers confidence that the matter was being addressed properly.”

He added: “We would encourage any business facing a similar situation to respond swiftly, transparently, and thoroughly. We are grateful for the continued support of our customers and team throughout this process, and for the council's recognition of the improvements we have made.

As previously reported, Agility Parks opened around six years ago as a fitness park with two Ninja Warrior obstacle courses for children of five years up to adults.

With more than 100 linear meters of obstacle course and more than 30 obstacles the park says there is something to challenge all ages and abilities.

The venue has received great reviews, with Google awarding 4.6 stars out of five, based on 347 reviews.

TripAdvisor awards 4.5 stars out of five, based on 54 reviews. TripAdvisor notes the venue has a large café and bar on two floors catering to a wide range of parties, functions, events and group sessions.

The Shack Café offers jalapeños cheese dogs, salads, loaded fries and stone baked pizzas, plus real espresso coffee. A new cafe bar has also just opened serving a range of beers wines and non alcoholic drinks, the reviews website also said.
