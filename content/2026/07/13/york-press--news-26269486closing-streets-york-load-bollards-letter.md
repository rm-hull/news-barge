**'Why closing these streets in York is a load of - bollards'**

CAN anyone from City of York Council please explain how closing one or two roads to ease congestion can possibly cost £2.35 million?

I can understand that the registration system is a costly piece of kit but where is rest of the money being spent?

How much does a signpost cost?

I have a suggestion for the council ...bollards.

  *Joanne Ellis,*

  *Charlton Street,*

  *York*

... AFTER reading repeatedly the proposed traffic restrictions involving George Hudson Street there appears to be a lack of clarity.

How will Blue Badge holders vehicles be identified ?

The Blue Badge is assigned to the individual regardless of which car the person is travelling in.

If a Blue Badge holder were to arrange a taxi which is not permitted to use the route to the station how would this be identified?

None of this makes sense unless it’s a person manning the route and checking your badge!

  *Ruth Clarke,*

  *Heworth,*

  *York*

  


  **MPs should have experience of the real world**

NOT many voters within the electorate will have bothered to investigate how many MPs, particularly from the Labour Party, have experience of life outside the "bubble" of politics, unfortunately it is a significant number.

To avoid being ruled by these professional politicians, a law should be introduced which states "no person can stand in a Parliamentary election without having at least seven years employment experience in work not connected with politics".

Also, the stampede by Labour MPs to nominate someone to be Prime Minister, whose manifesto is completely unknown, suggests to the electorate at large these sheep like members are interested in one thing only - themselves, resulting in "the cremation of democracy" taking place in Westminster on July 20th the service to be conducted by A Burnham Esq.
