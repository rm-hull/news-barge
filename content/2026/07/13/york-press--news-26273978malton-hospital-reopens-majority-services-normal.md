## Majority of services at Malton Hospital are now operating as normal

York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust has said the majority of services at Malton Hospital  are now operating as normal. *(Image: Freelancer)*

A NORTH Yorkshire hospital has reopened this morning (Monday).

York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust has said the majority of services at Malton Hospital are now operating as normal.

  A spokesperson said: "We're pleased to confirm that, from today, Monday 13 July, the majority of services at Malton Hospital will be operating as normal.

  "If you have an appointment, please attend as planned unless you have been contacted directly and advised otherwise.

  "Our Urgent Treatment Centre is now also open, as well as blood taking services.

  "Thank you to our colleagues, partner organisations, emergency services and the local community for your incredible support over the past few days."

Firefighters had worked through the night to extinguish the fire that started at around 11.30am on Wednesday and ripped through the hospital's Springwood unit.

  


Fire investigation teams have since been on site to establish the cause of the blaze.

Fifteen patients, along with staff, were evacuated from the hospital and taken to nearby Malton School.

A spokesperson for North Yorkshire Fire and Rescue said: "Crews worked through the night on Wednesday to extinguish the fire and checked for hotspots. "The cause of the fire is still under investigation at this time."
