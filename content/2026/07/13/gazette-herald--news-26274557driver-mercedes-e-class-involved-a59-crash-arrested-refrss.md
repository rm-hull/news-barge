THE DRIVER of a car involved in a crash on a main road in North Yorkshire has been arrested.

The incident took place on the A59 in Kirk Hammerton at around 3.40pm on Saturday (July 11).

The crash involved a dark-coloured Mercedes E-Class, a blue Alpine and a combine harvester – all of which were travelling in the direction of York.

  


The driver of the Mercedes, a man in his 30s from Bradford, was arrested on suspicion of drug driving and taken to hospital for treatment, North Yorkshire Police confirmed.

He has since been discharged from hospital.

A police spokesperson said: “The road was closed for several hours while we investigated the scene and to allow the vehicles involved to be recovered.

“We’re appealing for any witnesses to the collision, specifically anyone who may have seen the vehicles prior to the collision, or anyone with relevant dashcam footage, especially in regard to any overtaking manoeuvres of the Mercedes to contact us.”

If you have information that you believe could assist the investigation, please contact police in the first instance by emailing [gary.dukes@northyorkshire.police.uk](mailto:gary.dukes@northyorkshire.police.uk) or visiting the force’s website, selecting ‘Tell us about’ and then choosing ‘An existing case or report’.

Live chat is available on the North Yorkshire Police website from 9am to 5pm, Monday to Friday.

If you are unable to use these options, please call North Yorkshire Police on 101.

Please quote reference 12660131498 when providing information.
