A POLICE-led operation to tackle youth anti-social behaviour in a York suburb has delivered a three-month reduction of reported incidents in the area.

Recordings of anti-social behaviour have fallen by 73 per cent between April and June this year in the Derwenthorpe area, according to North Yorkshire Police figures.

  



A force spokesperson said over the past few months officers and local partners have been working together to address the issues.

The spokesperson thanked the local community for all its help and support.
