Revised plans for window graphics on Rialto House, in Fishergate, have been approved by York Council after ones put up when it was built were refused retrospective permission.

Operators Fusion Students’ plans stated new graphics would feature the company’s branding and wayfinding labelling directing halls residents to communal areas.

Council planning officers stated the simple, subtle design of the graphics were in keeping with the building and would not have a negative effect on the area.

The application to put up the graphics in 14 windows on the ground floor of the side of the building facing Fishergate follows the retrospective planning ruling in April.

Previous graphics featured pictures of people and rooms and measured 2.2m-high and 1.7m-wide.

They covered the windows of the halls’ ground floor laundrette, common room and gym and were designed to give students using them privacy.

Graphics were put up during the construction of the building which was completed in summer 2025 ahead of students moving in for the academic year beginning last September.

But four objections were lodged claiming the graphics were excessive, inappropriate and not in keeping with the wider area.

Council planning officers ruled the graphics were large, dominant and had a negative effect on the building and the surrounding streetscape.

Officers stated: “The proposed window graphics are considered to constitute unnecessary clutter and have a significant adverse visual impact on the amenity of the location, by virtue of their scale, position and number.”

Fusion Students lodged revised plans for graphics in May in response to the council’s decision.

Planning officers stated they recognised the graphics were needed on the ground floor for privacy.

They added the designs no longer dominated the windows.

Officers stated: “As the branding would be far more subtle than the previous application, its dominance in the streetscene would be reduced, and it would attract less attention.

“As the proposed signage would be of a simple visual design and non-illuminated, it is not considered that the scale, height, and design of the signage would have a negative impact on highway users or have an adverse impact on pedestrian safety.”

Fusion Students’ original application to demolish existing buildings on the site, on the corner of Fishergate and Blue Bridge Lane, was approved in 2021.

Plans to install signs without lighting featuring the company’s name on the side of the building were approved in 2025.

Fusion Students operates accommodation in Leeds, Liverpool, London, Manchester, Nottingham and York.
