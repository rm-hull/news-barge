Senator Lindsey Graham, a close congressional ally of President Donald Trump and a vocal advocate for a more aggressive U.S. foreign policy, has died. His office announced preliminary medical examiner findings indicating a tear in his aorta.

This tear, an aortic dissection in the inner wall, was linked to the hardening of Graham's arteries. An official cause of death will be disclosed following toxicological and microscopic testing.

It has been reported that paramedics were called to Graham’s D.C. home after receiving reports of someone suffering from severe chest pains. Dr. Kendra J. Grubb, a heart surgeon formerly at Emory University, described the pain as being similar to a “knife to the heart.”

Dr. Jason Lee, the chief of vascular surgery at Stanford Medicine, told *The San Francisco Chronicle***that this occurs when the three-layer lining of the aorta separates, disrupting blood flow to organs. It can also cause the aorta to rupture. 

The aorta is the main artery that sends blood from the heart to the rest of the body.

Lee said that the condition is common among men in their 60s and 70s who work in stressful environments. Intense exercise is among the triggers that can lead to the rupture.

Younger people can also suffer from a tear, but they are generally genetically predisposed to the condition.

According to the John Ritter Foundation for Aortic Health, around 20,000 people in the U.S. suffer a tear in any given year.

Heart surgeon Dr. Michael Mack told *The New York Times* that the tear is fatal in 50 percent of cases. 

“An aortic dissection is a life-threatening condition that requires rapid diagnosis, as emergent surgery can be necessary. Subsequent decision-making is based on the location of the tear in the aorta and any associated poor blood flow to organs,” Dr. Robert Aru of Thomas Jefferson University told ABC News.

Aru echoed Dr. Grubb’s description of the pain, calling it “the worst chest or back pain of their life that is similar to a ripping or stabbing sensation.”

The condition is often misdiagnosed as a heart attack. Symptoms include shortness of breath, fainting, difficulty walking, sudden loss of vision and severe chest pain.

Earlier this year, the condition claimed the life of Graham’s fellow Republican lawmaker Rep. Doug LaMalfa of Ohio.
