# UK agrees deal to make travel to major European nation easier

- Britain has agreed its "largest ever services deal" with Switzerland, aiming to ease travel for professionals and holidaymakers.
- The agreement will allow UK citizens to use Swiss e-gates at Zurich Airport by the end of 2026, with Geneva and Basel to follow.
- Roaming charges for mobile phones will be scrapped for travellers between the UK and Switzerland.
- The existing UK-Swiss services mobility agreement, which allows business people to supply services for up to 90 days without a work permit, has been made permanent.
- The government estimates the new free trade agreement will unlock an additional £5.2 billion annually in UK services exports to Switzerland.
