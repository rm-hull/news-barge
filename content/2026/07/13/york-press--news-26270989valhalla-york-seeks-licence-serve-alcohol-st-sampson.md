### What is the Public Notice Portal?

The Public Notice Portal carries statutory public notices published in local newspapers and is the fastest and most effective way of finding out what is happening in YOUR neighbourhood.

Search the Public Notice PortalA city centre community hub is set to become a licensed venue.

Valhalla York Ltd applied for a premises licence on July 2, for St Sampson Community Centre, situated on Church Street.

The proposed licence includes the sale of alcohol between 11am and 11pm every day.

The community centre's courtyard and garden are also included in the premises for the proposed licence.

The public can submit written representations regarding the application.

Residents can view the licensing application register by contacting the City of York Council Licensing Team at Hazel Court, EcoDepot, James Street.

These must be sent to the City of York Council Licensing Team, Hazel Court, EcoDepot, or via email to [licensing@york.gov.uk.](mailto:licensing@york.gov.uk.)

The deadline for submitting representations is July 30 - the 28-day period starting from the day after the application was made.

It is crucial to note that making a false statement in connection with a premises licence application is an offence.

Anyone found guilty of this offence could face an unlimited fine.
