# Ukraine war briefing: Three killed in Ukrainian drone attack on Moscow

- President Zelensky announced that officials responsible for allowing a weapons warehouse in a residential area near Kyiv, which led to 10 deaths after a Russian strike, have been identified and will face accountability.
- Western allies are meeting in Paris to bolster Ukraine's air defence capabilities, including sourcing Patriot interceptors and developing new systems, amidst critical munition shortages.
- Ukrainian drone attacks resulted in at least three deaths and five injuries in the Moscow Region, with 81 drones downed, according to governor Andrei Vorobyov.
- Russian spies, previously expelled from Western countries, are reportedly operating in Japan to acquire technology for Russia's war effort, according to *The New York Times,* with Ukraine estimating 90 per cent of Russian missiles and drones contain Japanese components.
- Five people were killed and 30 injured in a Russian glide bomb attack on Sumy, while Ukrainian Prime Minister Yulia Svyrydenko resigned as President Zelensky announced a government reshuffle.
