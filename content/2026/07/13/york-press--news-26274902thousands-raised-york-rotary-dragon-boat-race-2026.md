## PICTURES - York Rotary Dragon Boat Race raises thousands for charities

York St John University team as the YSJ Paddle Scholars *(Image: Newsquest)>*

More than £100,000 has been raised for charity through the annual York Rotary Dragon Boat Race.

Hundreds of spectators lined the banks of the River Ouse on Sunday (July 12) to cheer on 36 dragon boat teams competing in the event.

This year's race marked the 24th edition of the popular fundraiser - which has generated more than £1.7 million for thousands of charitable causes since it began.

  


The competitors, many of whom sported fancy dress, raced from Scarborough Bridge to York Rowing Club, starting at 10am.

  The Parky Paddlers raising vital funds for the Parkinsons UK York branch 

*(Image: Newsquest)*

Charity stalls, food vendors and local representatives lined Dame Judi Dench Walk to support the event.

Racing teams included those from Survive – the event’s nominated charity for 2026 – A Shining Light, York & Scarborough Hospitals Charity, North Yorkshire Police/Fire and York St John University.

  More than £100,000 was raised for numerous charities on the day 

*(Image: Newsquest)*

Speaking about the event, York Rotary representative David Fotheringham, said: “We’ve been very lucky with some wonderful weather today, and it’s great to see everyone out supporting the teams.

“I’d like to thank our kind volunteers today – 70 of whom are from York Rotary.

“We raised £114,000 last year, and this year we hope to generate around £120,000.”

  The Ware & Kay Paddle Pirates raised money for mental health charity The Little Push 4 Good with their Dragon Boat Race 

*(Image: Newsquest)*

Two-thirds of the amount raised by each team goes to the group’s chosen charity.

Sponsors of the event this year were Simpson, Clifton Park Hospital and Portakabin.

Teams awarded accolades will formally receive them during a ceremony later this year by York Rotary.

Awards include the Challenge Trophy and other prestigious awards, including Fastest Charity Team, Commercial Team and the Best Dressed Team.
