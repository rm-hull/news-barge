A new pop-up play area is set to open in the city centre next week.

The Tempest, a free attraction brought to the city by York BID, will open on Parliament Street on Tuesday, July 21.

Carl Alsop, operations manager at York BID, said they were incredibly exciting to open the play area.

York and North Yorkshire Mayor David Skaith said the attraction would be a family-friendly space that would bring local history to life and encourage people to visit the city centre.

The play area will be open daily from 9am to 6pm throughout the summer.

It features climbing and balancing equipment, sensory play features, bridges, interactive elements, and accessible play equipment, all designed to introduce children to Anderson’s life and work.

The Tempest includes sensory panels, mirrored elements, a wheelchair-friendly rumble strip, and spaces for both social and independent play.

Seating is provided around the perimeter for families.

It is inspired by York-born scientist and volcanologist Tempest Anderson and designed to engage children through play while bringing York’s heritage to life.

The project has been delivered by York BID in partnership with Playscheme, Make It York, Thor's, and York Civic Trust.

Funding has come from David Skaith, Mayor of York and North Yorkshire, through the York & North Yorkshire Combined Authority’s Vibrant & Sustainable High Streets Fund.

BID Operations Manager Mr Alsop said a survey of local people last year showed families were looking for free, high-quality activities when they visit the city centre.

The manager said: "It's a really distinctive summer addition to York - combining play with local history, creating something that's fun, educational and completely unique to the city."

Mayor Mr Skaith said the project was one of 66 supported across the region aiming to transform high streets into vibrant destinations.

Mr Skaith said: "Our high streets are where our communities come together.

"That’s why I’m backing creative ideas from the people who know their area best with my High Street Fund.

"By supporting The Tempest Play Area, we're helping to create a family-friendly space that brings local history to life and encourages more people to visit the city centre.

York Civic Trust has worked with the project team to develop the educational elements, helping bring Tempest Anderson's story to life for a new generation.

The space has been designed to be safe, accessible and welcoming for all visitors.

Daily inspections, CCTV monitoring, and regular maintenance will help ensure a secure and welcoming environment.
