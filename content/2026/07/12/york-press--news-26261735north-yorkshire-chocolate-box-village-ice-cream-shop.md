If you are looking for a tiny village to escape to for a day in North Yorkshire, you could take a trip out to Thornton-le-Dale.

The village is filled with picture-perfect thatched cottages and is often referred to as the Jewel of the Moors.

While Thornton-le-Dale is quite small with a population of around 2,000, there are still lots of things to see and do.

Around the village pond, there is a nature trail where you can spend some time spotting the local wildlife and relaxing.


## What else can I do in Thornton-le-Dale?

If you fancy enjoying your free time in a café, you might want to head to Balderson’s Welcome Café, which serves savoury breakfasts and lunch options as well as ice cream that Tripadvisor reviews say is "gorgeous."

The village is also home to a few traditional pubs where you can enjoy a pint, such as the New Inn that is "well worth a visit" and the Buck.

If you are looking for somewhere to have a sit-down meal, the Brandysnap Bistro offers "fantastic" food with a "cosy atmosphere."

The Chocolate Factory is described as a "must-visit" where you can pick up fresh handmade chocolates, chocolate bars, and chocolate figures made from pure Belgian chocolate to take home with you.


For those who love looking through old antiques for hidden treasure, you could spend some time going around Gray’s Emporium for vintage glassware, paintings, prints, and collectables.

The village’s information point has a leaflet with two village walks in it and lots of background information about the area’s history.

When walking around Thornton-le-Dale, you should make sure you look out for the Beck Isle Thatched Cottage on Pickering Road, which is a Grade II listed building and one of the most photographed cottages in Yorkshire.
