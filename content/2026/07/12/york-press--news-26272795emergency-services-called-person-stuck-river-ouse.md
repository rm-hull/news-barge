Emergency services were called to a person stuck in a York river.

Fire crews from York and Acomb, along with specialist rescue resources from Malton and Ripon and a rescue boat from Selby, were joined by North Yorkshire Police, York Rescue Boat and paramedics in Castlegate at 1.36am on Sunday (July 12).

The person was reported to be in waist deep water and unable to get out.

  


Officers from North Yorkshire Police secured the individual with a throw line and firefighters used a triple extension ladder to safely remove them from the water.

A North Yorkshire Fore and Rescue Service spokesperson said: “The individual was left in the care of police and the ambulance service.”
