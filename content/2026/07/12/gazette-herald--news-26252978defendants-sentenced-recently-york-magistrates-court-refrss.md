Robert Wharton, 21, of no fixed address, pleaded guilty to causing actual bodily harm to a man in York and three offences breaching a non-molestation order in Cawood and Selby.

He was given an 18-month community order with 18 days’ rehabilitative activities and 200 hours unpaid work and ordered to pay £200 to the man.

Christopher Austwick, 49, of Station Road, Hensall, pleaded guilty to driving whilst disqualified and without insurance on Bawtry Road, Selby.

He was given a 12-month community order with 20 days’ rehabilitative activities, banned for 45 months, fined £120 and ordered to pay £85 prosecution costs.

Ty Adaway, 21, of Brook Street, Selby, pleaded guilty to breaching a sexual harm prevention order by having unsupervised contact with a child.

He was given a 12-month community order with six months’ alcohol treatment and 16 days’ rehabilitative activities and ordered to pay £85 prosecution costs.

William Haylett, of Fulford Road, Scarborough, pleaded guilty to using threatening words or behaviour at Scarborough Town Hall, driving whilst disqualified in Scarborough, failure to provide a sample of blood when suspected of drink or drug driving, possession of pregabalin and cannabis and failure to attend court.

He was given a 12-month community order with 20 days’ rehabilitative activities and 180 hours’ unpaid work, banned from driving for two years and ordered to pay £85 prosecution costs.
