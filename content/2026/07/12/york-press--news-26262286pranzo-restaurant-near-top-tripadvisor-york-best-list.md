## York restaurant marks first year as it eyes TripAdvisor top ten rank out of 800

Pranzo staff when the restaurant opened a year ago *(Image: Pic supplied)*

A York restaurant, which opened a year ago, is confident it will make the TripAdvisor top ten, out of the 800 or so restaurants in the city.

Pranzo York opened in Church Street in late July in Church Street, where Yo! Sushi used to be.

The restaurant is noted for its homemade pasta inspired by the ‘Nonna’ or Nana of restaurant owner Marco Greco.

The York-branch is the fourth in a small family-owned chain, adding to Harrogate, Horsforth and Ilkley.

With a 4.9 out of 5* rating from 498 TripAdvisor reviews, this summer the Italian restaurant in York is aiming for a top ten spot on the UK’s favourite eating out search engine.

  The interior of Pranzo in York 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

Currently ranked 15th out of 797 restaurants in York, based on 505 reviews, they are almost there. Google awards 4.8 stars out of five, based on 342 reviews.

This compares with the 4.8 stars on TripAdvisor for the Pranzo restaurants in Harrogate, Ilkley and Horsforth. This rating ranks Harrogate fifth out of 295 Harrogate restaurants, Ilkley first out of 87 Ilkley restaurants and Horsforth third out of 33 Horsforth restaurants.

Operations director Stephen Peace said: “York has a vibrant dining scene with 800 restaurants. There are some great places to eat.”

  Stephen Peace says Pranzo York has had a 'good' year in line with forecasts. 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

“The people of York are loving what we have to offer. No one else in the city is doing homemade pasta, and our unique dishes from the Calabria region are proving popular.”

Pranzo says it leaves no stone unturned in their investment in achieving excellence.

“We want the whole experience to be incredible. Obviously, the food takes centre stage, but to us, customer service, our booking systems, cleanliness and every aspect of every visit, even before someone has stepped into the restaurant, needs to be faultless.”

  Pizza at Pranzo 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

To ensure excellence, one area of investment for Pranzo continues to be bi-monthly Mystery Diner visits.

During these visits, the restaurant is under a microscope. From initial exterior impressions to allergen advice, the ordering process, dish presentation and the healthiness of plants, nothing is missed. Already scoring above the industry average, Pranzo says it is using the results to continue driving standards.

  Nduja Paccheri 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

This is why Stephen says business at the York restaurant has been ‘good’ and in line with company forecasts.

However, with York having so many restaurants, diners are demanding more choice, not just at Pranzo, he says.

“Our home-made pasta is our USP but we have given the customer more choice. We have more salads, steak and fish in York. We have just started Roman-style rectangular pizzas.”

Stephen continued: “We’re constantly trying to evolve our offering. When it comes to produce, the fresher the better, which means keeping it as local as possible.

  Pranzo founder Marco Greco in York 

*(Image: Darren Greenwood)*

“Our region has some incredible food producers, and all our beef comes from Yorkshire farms. Our wine supplier is also based in North Yorkshire and sources unique wines from family-run vineyards in Italy.”

However, he adds the weather is having a much bigger impact than it used to have, something Stephen also says is affecting other hospitality venues.

“Sunny days bring in far more people than they used to and far less when the weather is bad. We have to capitalise more on the good days and try harder when its bad.”
