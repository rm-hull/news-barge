Search the Public Notice PortalA major resurfacing project is set to cause overnight closures on a key stretch of the A1(M).

It will be shut between 8pm and 6am and affect the northbound carriageway from Junction 50's exit slip road to Junction 52's entry slip road.

The work is scheduled to begin on Wednesday, July 15, 2026, and is expected to last for one month.

Alternative routes will be available and clearly marked at all times during the closures.

The northbound carriageway, from 1,600 metres south of the exit slip road at Junction 50 to 200 metres north of the entry slip road at Junction 52, will have a temporary speed restriction of 50mph, enforced 24 hours a day.

This restriction also applies to the exit and entry slip roads at Junctions 50, 51, and 52.

Traffic signs will indicate the extent of the restrictions.

The restrictions will not apply to emergency service vehicles or special forces vehicles.

The prohibitions will also not apply to emergency service vehicles or vehicles being used for the works, winter maintenance, or traffic officer purposes.

The order, which comes into effect on July 14, 2026, has a maximum duration of 18 months.

Naveed Ahmed, from National Highways, said the works are expected to be completed within the one-month timeframe - until August 15.

However, the 18-month order allows flexibility for any unforeseen circumstances that may delay the works.

The public is advised to plan journeys accordingly and allow extra time for travel during this period.

For further information, contact National Highways' Customer Contact Centre on 0300 123 5000.
