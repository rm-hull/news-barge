# Jannik Sinner repeats as Wimbledon champion after defeating Alexander Zverev in 4 sets

- Jannik Sinner successfully defended his Wimbledon title, securing his fifth Grand Slam victory by defeating Alexander Zverev.
- The world number one triumphed in a tight four-set match, winning 6-7 7-6 6-3 6-4, which lasted three hours and 46 minutes.
- The final featured only two breaks of serve, both going Sinner's way, as he denied Zverev back-to-back Grand Slam titles.
- This victory marks Sinner's tenth consecutive win against Zverev, following his semi-final triumph over Novak Djokovic.
- Sinner expressed satisfaction with the high level of play from both competitors in what he described as an 'amazing final'.
