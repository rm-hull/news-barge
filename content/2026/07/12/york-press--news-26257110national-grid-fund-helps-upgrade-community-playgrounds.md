## Nearly £500,000 awarded to improve playgrounds and outdoor gyms in Yorkshire

Monk Fryston and Hillam Community Association *(Image: National Grid)*

Playgrounds and outdoor gyms are getting a boost thanks to nearly £500,000 of new funding.

The grants come from National Grid’s Local Community Fund, part of the Yorkshire GREEN project.

A £400 million upgrade of Yorkshire’s high-voltage electricity network will deliver long-term infrastructure improvements alongside immediate support for community improvements in East Riding and North Yorkshire.

  Wilberfoss, Kexby and Newton Playing Fields 

*(Image: National Grid)*

Ray Newton, playground project implementation manager and former chair and trustee of the Monk Fryston and Hillam Community Association, said: "Our playground has been supported by the local community for many years, providing a safe and well-used space for children and families.

"Funding from the Yorkshire GREEN project at Rawfield Lane has helped us refurbish and upgrade the facilities for current and future generations."

  The Monk Fryston & Hillam Community Association has received almost £20,000 to restore and expand its playground 

*(Image: National Grid)*

The Monk Fryston & Hillam Community Association has received almost £20,000 to restore and expand its playground.

Other recipients include the Wilberfoss, Kexby and Newton Playing Fields Association, which received £8,000 to install an outdoor gym for people aged 12 and over.

Paul Gibney, vice chairman of the association, said: "The grant has made a real difference, allowing us to invest in an outdoor gym that will expand our facilities and create new opportunities for people who may not typically take part in sport."
