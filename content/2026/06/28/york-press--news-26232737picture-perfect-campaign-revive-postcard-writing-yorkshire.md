Tourism businesses across North Yorkshire are being invited to participate in a free summer visitor marketing campaign that will tap into the power of nostalgia and revive the once staple holiday activity - sending postcards.

The Picture Perfect campaign is the brainchild of tourism marketing specialists Susan Briggs and Amanda Brown who champion businesses in the Yorkshire Dales and North York Moors through The Tourism Network business support agency.

They’re encouraging businesses such as holiday cottages, hotels, B&Bs, attractions, galleries, pubs, restaurants, shops and tearooms to help people fall back in love with postcard writing as an antidote to being on screen.

A high proportion of visitors already say they’ll recommend the area to friends and family, it’s hoped postcards will act as a further trigger to do so.

Susan said: “There’s something incredibly meaningful and heartfelt about receiving a handwritten card which can’t be replicated in a digital message or being tagged in a social media post.

“We’re seeing more and more visitors wanting to take time off-screen. The Yorkshire Dales and North York Moors are perfect places for encouraging people to slow down and escape their busy lives; discovering, or rediscovering the joy of writing a handwritten note offers another way of deliberately disengaging from technology.

“Businesses will benefit as visitors will feel more engaged with the place they’re visiting and we hope they’ll enjoy the novelty of sending a postcard.”

There’s no charge for businesses to participate: they simply need to sign up at www.tourismnetwork.co.uk/postcard
