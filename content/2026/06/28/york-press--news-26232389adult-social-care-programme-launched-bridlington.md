A new programme is opening pathways into careers in adult social care in Bridlington.

The Widening Access Demonstrator (WAD) scheme is being delivered through a partnership between East Riding of Yorkshire Council and the NHS Humber and North Yorkshire Integrated Care Board (ICB).

Councillor Jonathan Owen, cabinet member for adults, health and care at East Riding of Yorkshire Council, said: "The Widening Access Demonstrator (WAD) programme is a fantastic opportunity for Bridlington, helping people of all ages discover the wide range of rewarding careers available in adult social care.

"By working closely with our partners, we are creating clear routes into employment through training, experience, and tailored support."

The programme features a SEA (sessions, experience, application) approach, offering interactive learning, work placements, and practical help with CVs, applications, and interviews.

A recruitment fair will take place on Wednesday, July 8, from 10am to 12pm at the Crown Community Building on Quay Road in Bridlington.

Tina Jenkinson, head of the Breakthrough Programme for Work, Health and Skills at the ICB, said: "We are delighted to be working with East Riding of Yorkshire Council to bring the Widening Access Demonstrator programme to Bridlington.

"This programme will help build a skilled local workforce while supporting residents to access meaningful employment opportunities."
