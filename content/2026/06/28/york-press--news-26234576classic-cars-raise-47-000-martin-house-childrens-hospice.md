Dozens of classic cars have taken part in a charity Mad Hatters Classic Car Run to support a children's hospice near Tadcaster.

The vehicles raised a total of £47,000 for Martin House Children’s Hospice during the car run, which began at Rudding Park in Harrogate and passed through Boston Spa before continuing to Harome.

Becky Leighton, relationship and philanthropy manager at Martin House, said: "We’re incredibly grateful to everyone who took part in this year’s Mad Hatters event – from our drivers and passengers, to our amazing venues, and headline sponsor Rothschild & Co Wealth Management.

  Mad Hatters Classic Car Run in aid of Martin House Children's Hospice 

*(Image: Bruce Fitzgerald)*

"It was a brilliant day, with lots of fun for all involved.

"Crucially, we also raised vital funds which will allow us to continue to be there for families when they need us most."

Participants enjoyed a Michelin-starred lunch at The Star Inn before taking part in a fundraising auction led by TV antique expert Angus Ashworth.

  Mad Hatters Classic Car Run in aid of Martin House Children's Hospice 

*(Image: Bruce Fitzgerald)*

The final leg of the route took drivers to Grantley Hall near Ripon for afternoon tea and an awards ceremony.

The event included a variety of prize categories, from best-sounding engine to most-loved British car.

The best-dressed Mad Hatter award went to the Minions.

Funds raised from the event will help Martin House continue to provide its specialist palliative care to children, young people and their families across North, West, and East Yorkshire.

  Mad Hatters Classic Car Run in aid of Martin House Children's Hospice 

*(Image: Bruce Fitzgerald)*

The hospice supports more than 550 children and young people with life-shortening conditions each year, as well as around 200 bereaved family members.

Annual running costs exceed £13 million, with most of the funding coming from voluntary donations and fundraising activities.

Ms Leighton also praised the dedication of long-standing supporters.

  Mad Hatters Classic Car Run in aid of Martin House Children's Hospice

She said: "Finally, this event simply wouldn’t be possible without the vision and passion of our amazing supporters Jonathan Sands OBE and Will Linley.

"Their dedication, generosity, and tireless commitment are instrumental in bringing the day together and making it such a resounding success."

For more information or to support the charity, visit martinhouse.org.uk.
