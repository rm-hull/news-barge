A chamber music duo will make its UK debut in York.

Duo Léger, comprising Germany-based opera singer Shawn Mlynek and Belgian-based harpist Eleonora Congiu, is set to perform at Holy Trinity Goodramgate on July 30th.

The duo, founded in 2021, has gained attention across Europe and the US, praised for its "harmonious musical collaboration."

Mr Mlynek said: "I visited York last summer as a tourist and immediately loved the city.

"Between the architecture and history, the city had a character that was very special to me.

"I also found the people of York to be very friendly, open, and cultured, and was able to enjoy the arts scene of the city, including the National Center for Early Music and the art museums.

"While in York, I coincidentally happened to walk by Holy Trinity Goodramgate on the evening they were presenting a touring production of Shakespeare's The Tempest.

"After looking around the church and hearing the acoustics, I knew it would be a perfect venue for Duo Léger."

The duo's programme for its York debut will include a variety of pieces, from Renaissance songs and Italian Baroque selections to Czech folk songs, and 50s and 60s pop songs reimagined for tenor and harp.
