Traffic on a major road near the York Outer Ring Road is being sent on a detour of more than 40 miles, all day.

The A59 closed at 7am between the Red Lion at Poppleton and a point west of the roads leading to Hessay. It will remain closed until 5pm this afternoon while the road is resurfaced.

The official diversion route takes traffic west along the A59 to the A1(M) north to its junction with the A168 at Dishforth, north-east to the Thirsk bypass, south on the A19 to the Shipton Road junction of the York Outer Ring Road and then west along the ring road to the Poppleton roundabout.

City of York Council, which is carrying out the work, says that although there are alternative routes available, the official route has to be able to handle all the kind of traffic that usually uses the A59 including large heavy lorries.

Cars could use the B1224 York to Wetherby Road to avoid the closure, among other routes.

Cyclists are being diverted along Marston Lane to the B1224 near Rufforth where they can take the off-road cycle path to Acomb.

Poppleton Park and Ride is open but only for vehicles approaching along the York Outer Ring Road, not the A59.
