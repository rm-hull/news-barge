## Tributes paid to York businessman who trained apprentices and travelled the world

Don Oldfield *(Image: Provided)*

TRIBUTES have been paid to a York businessman and dad who worked right up until the end of his life.

Donald Anthony Oldfield, known as Don, died peacefully surrounded by family at the age of 86 in his home in Elvington, York, after a battle with bowel cancer.

He started his career in 1955 as a 15-year-old apprentice engineer at Sheppee Engineering, an industrial engineering firm in York which makes high-speed glass container machinery.

Within four years, Don advanced from the shop floor to the drawing office, excelling as a draftsman with his initials -"Drawn by DAO" - still found on some glass industry drawings still in use today.

As the glass industry switched from mechanical controls to computer automation, Don thrived and became a sales and technical manager.

This role took him all over the world, initially to Brazil in 1974, followed by trips to America, Africa, and the Far East - the latter of which started a lifelong love for the region.

His technical expertise and global outreach eventually paved the way for his entry into Sheppee Engineering's boardroom as technical director and by 1985, Don was appointed joint managing director.

Under his leadership, the company took on nearly 150 employees and a turnover of between £3–5 million.

Alongside his professional achievements, Don valued his training on the job, so when Vickers Instruments, which provided off-the-job training for apprentices, closed, he worked with other local employers and the council to establish a new training hub.

This still exists as the Derwent Training facility and continues to train students to this day.

After the 1992 recession, Don was left unemployed for the first time in his career and co-founded Mechtronics in Melbourne near York, servicing existing glass industry orders.

In 1994, he started trading under his own name at D A Oldfield Ltd in Dunnington, York and as the business grew, he relocated it within Elvington, along with his son, Garry Oldfield.

Adapting with the times, Don picked up computer-aided design (CAD) and continued his biannual technical sales trips to the Philippines until the onset of the Covid-19 pandemic.

His career spanned 71 years, right until his passing, and his family's business will continue to play a significant role in the global glass industry.

Outside of work, Don held a second-dan black belt in judo from the Old Priory in York and held lifelong passion for Far Eastern culture and food.

Don's funeral is on 1st July at 12.30pm at Octon Crematorium, with the family requesting that donations in Don's memory be made to St Leonard’s Hospice, York.

Flowers and donations may be sent care of J. Rymer Funeral Directors.
