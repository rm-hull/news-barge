## REVIEW: Richard Ashcroft at Scarborough Open Air Theatre

Richard Ashcroft at Scarborough Open Air Theatre *(Image: Dave Lawrence)*

It was welcome return to the area for Richard Ashcroft at a baking hot Scarborough Open Air Theatre on Saturday. His last outdoor performance in the region was a show in Dalby Forest two years ago.

Saturday was the opening date of a series of outdoor summer shows for the former frontman of The Verve who spent much of 2025 supporting Oasis on their reunion tour.

And, despite Ashcroft having only completed a UK arena tour two months ago that visited Leeds and Newcastle plenty of fans were happy to come out and see him again.

  Richard Ashcroft at Scarborough Open Air Theatre 

*(Image: Dave Lawrence)*

The singer has a new album out titled Lovin' You but instead the Scarborough audience were treated to set that was nostalgic in nature and full of classic songs.

Ashcroft, still with something of a shamanic aura to him swaggered on stage at 8.15pm - in good time to ensure everyone had chance to catch the England football match later that evening.

  Richard Ashcroft at Scarborough Open Air Theatre 

*(Image: Dave Lawrence)*

The show was book-ended by Verve songs. Ashcroft, playing acoustic opened with Weeping Willow before dedicating the second number, Sonnet to the late artist David Hockney. He closed eighty minutes later with an extended, epic version of Bitter Sweet Symphony.

At first glance the setlist appeared on the short side but this was mainly due to Ashcroft giving some of the songs time to breath and develop through some extended instrumental passages and audience singalongs.

One example was the twelve minute version of Music is Power, before which the Wigan-born Ashcroft good naturedly responded to the audience chants of “Yorkshire” saying “I’ve played all around the world, and I like it when people are proud of where they come from. That’s why I’ll be selling Uncle Joe’s Mint Balls and some pork pies later.”

Introducing the sublime A Song For the Lovers, Ashcroft said “This is for any young songwriters. I was banned from Music GCSE and called the cancer of the class.”

  Richard Ashcroft at Scarborough Open Air Theatre 

*(Image: Dave Lawrence)*

With one of the UK’s best-selling albums on his CV and a couple of Ivor Novello awards he’s not done too badly.

The Drugs Don’t Work and Lucky Man closed the main set before an encore of C’mon People and Bitter Sweet Symphony ended a magnificent performance.

  Richard Ashcroft at Scarborough Open Air Theatre 

*(Image: Dave Lawrence)*

It is always a joy to hear Ashcroft performing the Verve material in particular. These are songs that have stood the test of time for almost thirty years and he steadfastly performs them with a pride and passion you might be forgiven for thinking they had only been written a few weeks ago.

Earlier, Leeds indie-band Apollo Junction – who performed at the Isle of Wight festival last weekend – made a welcome return to the venue delivering a set that included Are You Happy? and had singer Jamie Williamson in the crowd dancing with audience members.

  Tom Meighan at Scarborough Open Air Theatre 

*(Image: Dave Lawrence)*

Former Kasabian frontman Tom Meighan then performed a lively set that that featured several bangers to excite the audience. Unsurprisingly, Kasabian favourites like Empire, Shoot the Runner, Club Foot, L.S.F. (Lost Souls Forever) and final number Fire were rapturously received.
