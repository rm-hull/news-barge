The 2026/27 Small Grants Scheme has been launched to help organisations deliver activities, services, events and improvements that strengthen communities, enhance local facilities and improve quality of life in Harrogate.

The funding is available through two separate grant programmes. A General Grants Fund of £24,045 will support community projects, activities and initiatives, while a further £50,000 Infrastructure and Capital Grants Fund is available for projects that improve community facilities or help Harrogate respond to development and population growth.

Importantly, the £50,000 Infrastructure and Capital Grants Fund comes from developer contributions linked to new housing developments in the Harrogate area. These funds are specifically intended to help communities meet the increased demand on local facilities and services that can arise from growth and development.

Mayor of Harrogate, Councillor Viv Poskitt, said:

“Community organisations play a vital role in supporting residents, creating opportunities and bringing people together. Through this grants programme, Harrogate Town Council is investing £74,000 in projects that will make a real difference to communities across the town.”

“We are particularly pleased that a significant proportion of this funding has been secured through developer contributions, ensuring that investment linked to new development can be directed back into our communities. We encourage eligible organisations to explore the opportunities available and submit applications for projects that will deliver lasting benefits for Harrogate residents.”

## What Funding Is Available?

### General Grants Fund – £24,045

Funding is available for community projects, activities, services and initiatives that benefit Harrogate residents. This could include events, activities, community support projects, small equipment purchases or initiatives that improve wellbeing, inclusion and community cohesion.

### Infrastructure and Capital Grants Fund – £50,000

Funding is available for infrastructure, capital and community facility projects. Applicants will need to demonstrate how their project helps Harrogate respond to development, growth or increased demand on local facilities and services.

Individual grants will normally be awarded up to a maximum of £5,000, although larger awards may be considered in exceptional circumstances.

## Who Can Apply?

Applications are welcomed from:

- Charities
- Community groups
- Voluntary organisations
- Not-for-profit organisations
- Other properly constituted organisations operating for community benefit

Projects should normally benefit residents within the Harrogate Town Council area.

## Key Dates

- Applications open: Now
- Application deadline: 15 September 2026
- Applications considered by the Finance and General Purposes Committee: 11 November 2026
- Applicants notified of decisions: By 30 November 2026

Successful applicants will normally be expected to spend grant funding within two years of receiving an award.

## How to Apply

Full guidance, eligibility criteria and application forms are available on the Town Council’s grants page.
