## Coastal café to be demolished after nearly 50 years

Sandsend Café, in Sandsend, near Whitby, is set to be demolished and rebuilt into a fresh venue for customers *(Image: Google Maps)*

A MUCH-LOVED café which has been serving customers for almost five decades is expected to be demolished in North Yorkshire.

Sandsend Café, in Sandsend, near Whitby, has been open since 1978, but will be demolished in its current form to make way for the company’s new venue.

The café is set to be rebuilt with a new first floor, with additional space to improve its kitchen space, install new staff facilities and an office, and increase customer seating.

  


The plans received consent from North Yorkshire Council’s planning department on Tuesday (June 16).

A timescale for the project has not been publicly disclosed yet.

  Sandsend Café has been serving customers since 1978 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Council)*

Speaking on behalf of the company in plans submitted to the council, Saddington Taylor Planning said the ageing building had suffered damage following a vehicle crash in May 2024.

They added: “Given the construction method used for the building which utilises interlocking timber logs, a Building Surveyor has indicated that the only way to rectify the area of damage would be to take the entire structure down and rebuild it.”

After the replacement café is built, more customers will be able to sit and enjoy sea views year-round - thanks to the structure’s glazed roof.

The café will accommodate 140 seats, 50 of which will be inside, and a further 30 (covered) and 60 (open) in the area outside.

The business will continue to sell beach toys and trinkets and cater for takeaway services.

The site does not have car park facilities, but the developer said that most customers make their way to the café on foot through the village.
