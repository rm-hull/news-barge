We spend roughly a third of our lives asleep, so getting your sleep setup right really does matter. A pillow plays an important role in your sleep. It “can influence comfort and how supported your body feels during the night”, helping your “head and neck stay aligned so you are not distracted by aches or tension”, says Stephanie Romiszewski, a sleep psychologist.

When choosing one, it’s important to consider the loft (the height of the pillow). It’s recommended that side sleepers usually need a higher loft to properly fill the gap between the shoulder and mattress, while front sleepers are better suited to a flatter option. Back sleepers tend to do best with a medium loft, which balances support and comfort.

To help find the best pillow for every type of sleeper, I’ve tested a wide range of pillows to separate the genuinely effective ones from the rest and find those that improve sleep quality.

After weeks of testing, Simba’s hybrid pillow emerged as the most adaptable, consistently comfortable pillow on test. What makes it a winner is its “nanocube” fill – instead of vague “adjustable” claims, you can genuinely fine-tune loft and firmness to your liking, by adding or removing the small foam cubes.

![indybest best pillows review Simba hybrid pillow]([https://static.independent.co.uk/2025/08/21/10/20/pillow-1-copy.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp](https://static.independent.co.uk/2025/08/21/10/20/pillow-1-copy.png?quality=75&width=608&auto=webp) 608w)

You can then store the overflow in the pouch provided. As a side-sleeper, my sweet spot came after I removed a couple of handfuls, which eliminated the slight neck stiffness I’ve experienced when testing denser pillows.

The cooling promise is legitimate, providing a subtle, instant chill as air is kept moving through the mesh perimeter. I found that I flipped it less on warm nights, and it stayed a comfortable temperature whether put in a cotton case or used as is. So much so, it also took the top spot in my review of the best cooling pillows.

The quality is also thoughtful, with smooth fabric on one side and cool tech on the other. The cover is also fully machine-washable and comes out good as new. The only downsides are the price, and it needs regular fluffing to avoid feeling a little segmented.

Support is the main selling point. On top of that, though the marketing makes a lot of this pillow being ‘natural’, it’s made of bamboo-derived viscose, not fibres of bamboo. It’s an excellent pillow, but it’s worth bearing in mind that “bamboo” is ultimately a synthetic fabric in this context.
