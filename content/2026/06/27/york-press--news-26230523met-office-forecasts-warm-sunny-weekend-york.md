The Met Office has forecast a warm and sunny weekend for York.

Saturday will start clear and sunny, with temperatures rising from 16°C to 19°C by late morning.

The afternoon will remain sunny, with temperatures climbing to a high of 27°C.

The chance of rain is very low, starting at under 10 per cent and rising to around 30 per cent by late afternoon.

A pleasant breeze of 8–13 mph is expected.

The evening will see warm sunny intervals, becoming partly cloudy later.

Temperatures will remain warm, staying at 27°C at 6pm and around 21°C by 11pm.

The chance of rain will be between 10 and 20 per cent.

Sunday morning will start with sunny intervals, turning mostly cloudy later.

Temperatures will be between 19°C and 20°C, and the chance of rain will be between 10 and 30 per cent.

The afternoon will brighten up with sunny intervals, and temperatures will be around 21°C.

The chance of rain will drop to less than 10 per cent, and below 5 per cent later.

The evening will be sunny, then clear overnight.

Temperatures will cool from 20°C to 14°C, with the chance of rain less than 5 per cent.

Overall, the weekend is expected to be dry with plenty of sunshine, making it a great time to be outdoors.
