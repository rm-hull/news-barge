## York village footpath set to close for over a year over landslip danger

The Public Right of Way leading from the Dumcombe Lane track to a section of Centenary Way in Strensall will be closed for ongoing works after a landslip for 15 months *(Image: Newsquest)*

A PUBLIC footpath in a York village is set to close for over a year due to risks posed by a landslip.

Pedestrians will not be able to walk along the public right of way leading from the Dumcombe Lane track to a section of Centenary Way in Strensall from Tuesday (June 30) to Friday (December 31) 2027.

City of York Council spokesperson said: “This is to ensure the safety of route users.

“An alternative route for diverted pedestrians will not be signed during this period, but traffic signs/barriers will be in place to indicate how much of the path will be closed.

“For further information please contact: [highway.regulation@york.gov.uk.](mailto:highway.regulation@york.gov.uk.)”
