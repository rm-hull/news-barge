## York centenarian named after Flemish war nurse who helped father celebrates 100 years

Zelie celebrated her special milestone with her family and friends at Heworth Golf Club *(Image: Supplied)*

A YORK centenarian has celebrated a special milestone with over fifty members of her family and friends in the suburb she's lived in throughout her life.

Zelie Caffrey from Heworth turned 100 on Wednesday, June 24 and marked the occasion with an afternoon tea celebration at Heworth Golf Club in Muncastergate on Saturday.

She was joined by her children, many of her grandchildren and great-grandchildren some of whom had travelled from Bahrain, her sister, and also family and friends from near and far.

  A special message from The King and Queen featured alongside the rest of the celebratory message 

*(Image: Supplied)*

A phone call also came in from New Zealand on the morning of the celebration which her daughter Trisha said was a wonderful way to start the day.

Zelie also invited neighbours, family and friends to her house on Wednesday, June 24.

A birthday book was opened and invited guests wrote about Zelie's long friendships, her interesting stories and all the memories of her kindness and love.

Catherine Zelie Wright (known as Zelie) was born at home in Stockton Lane on June 24, 1926 to Ellen (nee Craven) and Edward Wright.

She was names after a Flemish nurse who had nursed her father back to health during the First World War.

  Zelie pictured in her teenage years 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Her father owned Wright's Pram Shop in Market Street with his two brothers, which also sold gramophones and records and was latterly a Millets and is now a branch of GO Outdoors.

She has a younger sister Adrienne Josephine, born March 14, 1929.

Zelie started at Eboracum School in Chestnut Avenue, Heworth in 1933 and then Queen Anne School for girls in in 1936.

In 1944 she went to teacher training college in Sheffield and her first teaching job was at Park Grove Junior School starting in 1946.

During this time, she met her future husband Kenneth Caffrey at a dance in the De Grey Rooms York, whose building yard was in the vicinity of Park Grove School.

They were married at Holy Trinity Church Heworth on August 8, 1953 and moved into a house in Heworth built by T & M Caffrey builders where Zelie still lives today.

  Zelie and Ken after their marriage at Holy Trinity Church, Heworth on August 8, 1953 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Zelie & Ken had four children - Ann, Peter, Michael and Trisha, and were married for 33 years.

As a family they attended St Cuthberts Church in Peasholme Green before moving to St Michael Le Belfrey under the leadership of David Watson.

Trisha said Zelie’s faith is important to her & she still attends a local church whenever she is able

The family enjoyed many walking holidays together Zelie also taught at Carr Lane School and Tang Hall Infant School from where she retired in 1986.

  Zelie's great-grandchildren joined in the celebration with over 50 others in Heworth 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Following Ken's death, Zelie spent much of her retirement regularly travelling to Bahrain to visit family and also accompanied family and friends to many other parts of the world and the UK.

She was an active member of U3A - a UK-wide movement of locally-run interest groups that provide a wide range of opportunities to come together - and enjoyed participating in many of their activities - where she remains a member to this day, Trisha added.

Zelie has always enjoyed being with her family & friends & whenever possible she still enjoys their company today.
