# 'Amazingly civilised' Deer Shed Festival will return with cream of current indie and alternative scene

## Deer Shed Festival may be one of Britain’s greatest independent music festivals but it’s also highly regarded for a lot more including its stunning spoken word and literary programme.

Since it was first launched at beautiful Baldersby Park near Thirsk in 2010 by local couple Oliver Jones and Kate Webster, this award-winning outdoors event has built up an incredibly strong reputation, once being named in the eight best UK summer festivals for families by National Geographic magazine.

This year’s Deer Shed Festival, which will run from July 24-26, will feature the cream of the current indie and alternative scene in the most civilised of set-ups with great food and drink.

The acts include Sleaford Mods, Say She She, Everything Everything, The Twilight Sad, Jasmine 4.T, Fat Dog, Bar Italia, DEADLETTER, DJ Rory Hoy and much more.

Equally impressive is the Literature & Spoken Word line-up whose headline event will be Sathnam Sanghera in conversation with Dave Simpson talking about his book Tonight The Music Seems So Loud – The Meaning of George Michael.

Other literary events will include Sheffield-based writer Daniel Dylan Wray in conversation about his new book Groovy, Laid Back and Nasty: A History of Independent Music in Sheffield.

Plus Scottish fuzzed out folk five-piece Brògeal will be special guests for the live recording of the 101 Part Time Jobs Podcast.

Meanwhile, the Do I Detect A Problem? Podcast will be joined by special guest Mez Sanders-Green, frontperson of Hull-based alt heroes LIFE.
