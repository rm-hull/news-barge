## Women at War summer programme launches at museum near Malton

Eden Camp’s Women at War programme honours the contributions of women who kept Britain running during the Second World War *(Image: Eden Camp)*

A summer programme near Malton is celebrating the bravery and resilience of women during wartime.

Running from July 17 to August 31, Eden Camp’s Women at War programme honours the often-overlooked contributions of women who kept Britain running during the Second World War.

The immersive experience features hands-on workshops and themed activities designed to educate, inspire, and entertain visitors of all ages.

Nick Hill, museum manager at Eden Camp, said: "We’re so pleased to be implementing the Government’s VAT reduction scheme in full and passing those savings directly on to visitors, helping more families enjoy a day out.

  The immersive experience features hands-on workshops and themed activities 

*(Image: Eden Camp)*

"Our Women at War programme is full of hands-on and interactive activities that bring history to life for all ages, and we can’t wait to welcome families and history enthusiasts alike to experience it."

As part of the Great British Summer Savings VAT reduction scheme, families can benefit from reduced ticket prices.

Advance purchase tickets start at £14.75 for adults, £13 for children, and family tickets are available from £26.25.

Highlights of the programme include a 'Poo Clues Trail,' wartime street games, a cow milking experience, and the return of Eden Camp’s Living History Weekend.

To find out more about Eden Camp and to book tickets, visit [https://edencamp.co.uk/](https://edencamp.co.uk/)
