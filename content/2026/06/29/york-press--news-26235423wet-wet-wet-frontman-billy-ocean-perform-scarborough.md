## Two members of 80s pop royalty perform in one night at top North Yorkshire venue

Fans in Scarborough were treated to an unforgettable night of hit-filled anthems, organisers said 

TWO members of 80s pop-royalty performed on stage at a top North Yorkshire venue last night (Sunday, June 28).

Billy Ocean brought a night of hits to Scarborough Open Air Theatre, following an earlier singalong-filled set from former Wet Wet Wet frontman Marti Pellow.

Grammy-winning singer Billy Ocean performed classics from his 50-year career, starting with 'One World' and including favourites like 'Love Really Hurts Without You,' 'Suddenly,' and 'Loverboy.'

  Former Wet Wet Wet frontman Marti Pellow took to the stage in Scarborough to perform a string of singalong hits 

*(Image: Cuffe and Taylor)*

  


A standout moment was his 1977 hit 'Red Light Spells Danger,' which energised the crowd, according to onlookers.

The show continued with 'The Colour of Love,' a cover of 'No Woman, No Cry,' and more favourites like 'Caribbean Queen.'

  Billy Ocean performed a string of classics from a 50-year career at Scarborough Open Air Theatre last night (Sunday, June 28) 

*(Image: Cuffe and Taylor)*

Earlier in the night, fans enjoyed Marti Pellow performing a cavalcade of hits such as 'Wishing I Was Lucky,' 'Sweet Little Mystery,' and 'Love Is All Around.'

Scarborough's Open Air performances continue with 13 acts lined up for July.
