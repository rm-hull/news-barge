## Yorkshire woman shaves head after losing partner and friend to same cancer

Clair and Iain *(Image: Provided)*

A YORKSHIRE woman has shaved her head after losing her partner and a close family friend to the same rare form of brain cancer.

Clair Wiggins, who is 47 and a charity fundraiser from Boston Spa, has launched a new campaign for Blood Cancer UK after her partner Iain and family friend Bruce both passed away from AML (Acute myeloid leukaemia) earlier this year.

AML is a fast-growing blood cancer that starts in the bone marrow and affects white blood cells that have not reached full maturity yet.

Clair said that she was with her partner Iain for 17 years and that he had always been fit and healthy – even acting as her carer over the years.

She said: “The first time we met was a chance encounter that felt like fate, and grew into a deep, loving partnership that I cherished every day. We shared so many happy times, laughed together, and supported each other through everything.

“He did a lot in the community for people who were struggling like picking up gardening jobs for all of our elderly neighbours.

  Clair and Iain when she first shaved her head 

*(Image: Provided)*

“The way it all started was that he collapsed one day. We all thought he was having a heart attack and he was rushed to Harrogate's hospital, where he had to be given five pints of blood.

“The doctors told us that he only had three quarters of the blood people normally have in their body.”

After this, he remained in hospital for 48 hours before his care was transferred to Leeds and five days later, he was diagnosed with AML where he was told that he had five different mutations to battle against.

  Clair ahead of her big shave 

*(Image: Provided)*

She added: “Iain had to have 18 teeth removed prior to the start of his chemo, which was a really big thing for him as he was terrified of the dentist.

“After his first round of chemotherapy, we were told that he beat 25 per cent of the cancer, but between round one and two, he had a gastric bleed that meant the cancer progressed.

"He was told that he was terminal a week before his 54th birthday and later, he had a cough which ended up developing into pneumonia.”

Iain died in hospital on March 19, receiving treatment in his final days alongside family friend Bruce, who suffered from the mutations and required platelet and blood transfusions after his chemotherapy failed.

Clair said: “Bruce was a kind, generous man who touched so many lives in our community, and his loss has been felt deeply by everyone who knew him.

“Losing both Iain and Bruce to AML in such a short time has been heartbreaking and it made me determined to turn my grief into something meaningful to honour them both and help others facing this terrible illness.

“I decided to shave my head bald on my birthday, June 14, to honour them.

"I’ve actually shaved my head before – Iain did it for me – but I’m still recovering from sepsis, so I wanted to do something that would be fairly easy for me whilst still giving back.”
