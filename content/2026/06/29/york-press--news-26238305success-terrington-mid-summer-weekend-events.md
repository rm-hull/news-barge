## Mid-summer event in North Yorkshire village a success

Jane Burley (Janet's daughter), Sue John (Chair of the Terrington Choir Committee), Janet Goodwill (Choir President) and Kerr Wilson (Terrington Choir Musical Director)

A very successful mid-summer weekend of flowers, song and scarecrows was recently held in Terrington.

Following the theme of ‘The Magic of Musicals’, members of Malton and District Flower Club created beautiful floral displays in the Church. Inspirations were taken from Musicals across the decades, from the 1950s to recent times.

Terrington Choir celebrated its 30th anniversary as part of the Terrington Flower Festival with a concert in All Saint’s Church on Saturday, June 20.

The church was filled with an audience from the village and surrounding area who had come along to listen to the choir take a journey through some of the most celebrated musicals in theatre history.

A spokesperson for the choir said: "Amid beautiful floral displays, each themed on a different musical, the choir began with a selection from the much-loved musicals The Sound of Music, Sweet Charity and The Lion King.

"A moving tribute was made to the late John Goodwill who first suggested forming the choir in 1996 and in recognition of this, his wife Janet was honoured to accept an invitation to become the first President of Terrington Choir.

"The tribute was followed by a delightful duet to start ABBA’s Thank You For The Music – a particular favourite of John’s.

"The second half of the concert took the audience through some of Andrew Lloyd Webber’s most memorable and loved songs from Cats, Aspects of Love and Phantom of the Opera.

"A talented young pianist made a guest appearance, performing a piano solo from the movie Project Hail Mary and a piano duet by Debussy with the choir accompanist.

"There was humour during the concert too with a lively rendition of The Lonely Goatherd, complete with yodelling, and a trio of ‘bears’ singing The Bare Necessities!

"The beautiful summer’s evening of music closed with the audience singing along to You’ll Never Walk Alone and Climb Every Mountain."

Meanwhile throughout the weekend Scarecrows had been appearing in the village. Sunday saw an organised Scarecrow Trail of the 43 exhibits. From traditional scarecrows to a Scary Shark ‘Jaws’ the variety was immense. This proved to be a very popular event with villagers and visitors alike.

Fundraising over the weekend was for Terrington Church and the Village Playground Project.
