# Harrogate man jailed after drunken fight on plane preparing for take-off at Leeds Bradford Airport

## A man from Harrogate has been jailed after a drunken fight broke out on a plane preparing to take off from Leeds Bradford Airport.

Nathan Cooper, 44, of Dene Park, was sentenced to 15 months in prison at Leeds Crown Court on Wednesday, June 24 after pleading guilty to affray and being drunk on board an aircraft.

The incident happened on July 24 last year as passengers were preparing to depart on a flight to Chania in Crete.

West Yorkshire Police said Cooper started fighting with another passenger in the rear galley while the aircraft was taxiing towards the runway, causing alarm among cabin crew and fellow passengers.

![Nathan Cooper, 44, of Dene Park in Harrogate, has been sentenced to 15 months in prison after pleading guilty to affray and being drunk on board an aircraft](https://www.harrogateadvertiser.co.uk/webimg/b25lY21zOmJjMGVkM2NkLTAwZjktNGM1ZS05Y2ExLWJlYmEyNzFlYzUyMjpiYTllMmNiZC1jYmRiLTQyZGYtYTg1OS02Yzg2NzBjYWVkNDI=.jpg?crop=3:2,smart&trim=&width=640&quality=65)



When officers attended the aircraft, the altercation was still ongoing.

During his arrest, Cooper punched one police officer and bit another.

A bottle of vodka, which was mostly empty, was later found at Cooper's seat.

The sentencing comes as police issue a warning to holidaymakers ahead of the busy summer travel season, urging passengers to think carefully about the consequences of drunken, disorderly and violent behaviour on flights.

Inspector Kevin Lowther, of West Yorkshire Police, said: "There may have been a heatwave in the UK this week, but we know lots of people will have holidays planned to usually hotter climes in the coming months.

"Sadly, a few people's selfish actions have the power to derail the travel plans of every other passenger on their flight.

"Cabin staff should also not have to deal with such threatening and frightening behaviour in their workplace.

"We need people to take some personal responsibility and think how they would feel if the shoe was on the other foot and they were in a confined space, potentially with young children, being put in fear by someone shouting or behaving violently.

"Our attending officers in this incident were also met with violence and this kind of behaviour will not be tolerated.

"I hope the fact that Cooper has been sent to prison sends a message to others about the repercussions of drunk and violent behaviour on aeroplanes."
