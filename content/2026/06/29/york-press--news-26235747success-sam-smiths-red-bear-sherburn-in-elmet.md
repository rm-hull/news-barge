## 'Lovely' North Yorkshire Sam Smith's pub built in 1768 thrives under new management

The Red Bear's 'dream team' *(Image: Pic supplied)*

A Sam Smith’s owned pub is thriving in a fast-growing North Yorkshire town.

Since Sarah Edwards and partner Steven Burton took over the Red Bear in Sherburn-in-Elmet a year ago this week, the management couple have earned rave reviews for their ales, food and service on social media.

Previously, the couple ran the Red Lion at Conisbrough, near Doncaster.

Sarah says they were attracted to the Red Bear after Tadcaster-based Sam Smith’s rung them to say the previous management couple were leaving and could they look after the pub, which was built in 1768.

Sarah told the Press: “We were only going to look after it 4-6 weeks but we decided to stay as it’s such a lovely pub.”

In the past decade or so, The Red Bear received a major renovation taking several years, which was completed in 2022.

  Sarah and Edwards and Steven Burton, enjoyed success in the Sherburn Carnival for their window displays. 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

Since taking the reins, the couple have introduced a quiz night, as a fundrasier for McMillan. The pub also offers bingo as well, plus board games and scrabble mornings, and American game nights.

The Red Bear has also just started offering breakfasts on a Saturday morning, which can come with a pint, if you wish. The move means the Red Bear now opens at 10am on Fridays and Saturdays at 10am, instead of 11am.

  The exterior of The Red Bear in Sherburn-in-Elmet town centre 

*(Image: Red Bear, Facebook)*

In addition, The Red Bear also offers a range of traditional pub classics foodwise, along with Sunday lunch.

Sarah said: “Food is doing brilliantly. Steve has introduced a chicken curry, which is doing good and will be sent to the other (Sam Smith's) pubs.”

As for future ideas and changes, Sarah says it is a matter of what customers are seeking.

In May, the Red Bear took part in the Sherburn-in-Elmet Gala, which this year was celebrating in its 50th year. The pub won gold for its decorated windows and other displays, which included former carnival queens over the years.

  The Red Bear in Sherburn-in-Elmet was built in 1768 

*(Image: Facebook, Red Bear)*

The Red Bear also has function rooms for birthday parties, wakes and engagements and other events.

Now, Sam’s Smith’s pubs are famous for having no music and not allowing customers to use their digital devices such as phones, but the Red Bear remains a popular venue for such gatherings.

Sarah explained: “It’s not about the noise. It’s about the get together with family and friends.”

  Part of the pub interior 

*(Image: Red Bear, Facebook)*

She also says other customers come into the Red Bear because there is no music and “they like that.”

Sarah added: “We are happy working for Sam Smith’s and it’s doing very well.”

And it certainly seems a recipe for success.

  Fish and chips have proven to be hugely popular 

*(Image: Red Bear, Facebook)*

Google awards The Red Bear 4.4 stars out of five, based on 163 reviews.

TripAdvisor awards 4.9 stars out of five based on 11 reviews, ranking it sixth out of 15 Sherburn-in-Elmet restaurants.

  Some of the highly-rated food at The Red Bear 

*(Image: Pic supplied)*

The latest TripAdvisor review awarded five stars, calling the venue ‘excellent.’

The review added: “Excellent food all freshly prepared. Desserts were large portions, two of us shared! Bar staff were very friendly and the waiting staff were very polite, attentive and competent. Our second visit but not our last. Will be back to try Sunday lunch.”
