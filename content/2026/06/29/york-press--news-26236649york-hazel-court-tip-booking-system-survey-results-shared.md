## How York residents really feel about new York tip booking system

A majority of survey participants said that they were "very satisfied" with their appointment at Hazel Court and had experienced shorter wait times *(Image: City of York Council)*

A SURVEY has revealed how users feel about a new public booking system at York’s main tip.

More than 82 per cent of respondents to a City of York Council survey said they were ‘very satisfied’ with their appointment at Hazel Court Household Waste and Recycling Centre (HWRC).

Launched on February 23, the Click Before You Tip system was introduced to tackle queues at the James Street site, which the council said had resulted in heavy congestion that was harming businesses in and around the area.

The new rule allows residents up to four 10-minute visits a week while businesses can book two 15-minute slots a day to dispose of their waste.

Four months on from the change, 83 per cent of survey participants said the move has resulted in shorter wait times, with 346 people adding that they were able to book a suitable date and time to use the tip.

  The Click Before You Tip system was introduced to tackle queues at the James Street site - which the majority of respondents said had eased since the changes were launched in February 

*(Image: Supplied)*

But fewer respondents said they were satisfied with the overall booking system.

Among the criticism, some said that selecting an appointment online proved difficult.

One respondent said: “The booking system is very poor and needs a total redesign.

“Currently, you have to read a lot of terms and conditions and input a lot of personal information before you even get to see what booking slots are available.”

One person said that the booking page was “not intuitive” at all, with another adding: “I actually work for the council and work on the computer all the time and I found it hard to work out”.

Speaking about this, executive member for environment, Cllr Jenny Kent, said: “It’s great to see that a significant majority of residents using the booking system reporting shorter waits and positive experiences on site.

“In addition to high satisfaction rates with the booking process, this shows the trial is working well for residents, helping us manage peak demand and keeping traffic moving around Hazel Court.

“We also know this is a significant change and it’s important we listen to feedback and continue to develop the system.”

Once booked in, more than 300 people said they were satisfied with their appointment at Hazel Court.

One respondent said: “Turning up and getting into the site was easier.

“The staff were all very helpful and accommodating, however the website let it down and should be easier.”

Two more respondents said that despite being “fairly satisfied” with their experience, it would be made “easier” if the council website would recognise them through their car registration plate as repeat users of the tip - ensuring that they didn’t have to answer the same “tiring” admin questions each time.

Ian Hoult, Head of Environmental Services at City of York Council, said: “We’re pleased to see a strong majority of residents are seeing shorter wait times and a positive experience once on site.

“At the same time, we recognise that the booking system itself needs to be as simple, flexible and accessible as possible, and we are continuing to refine it based on feedback.”

Cllr Kent added: “Ultimately, this is about making sure York’s recycling facilities work efficiently for residents, businesses and the wider road network.

“We’ll continue to review all the data – including usage and waste volumes over time – before making any long term decisions.”
