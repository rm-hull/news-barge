A MAIN road in York is closed this morning after a water pipe burst.

Yorkshire Water said a burst main in Wigginton Road outside York Hospital has forced the closure of one lane whilst work is carried out on the repair.

A spokesperson for York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust said the hospital remains accessible, but patients and visitors are advised to plan ahead and allow extra time for their journey.

A member of staff at the hospital said the road is closed near to the bridge before Crichton Avenue.

  


He added that the Yorkshire Water closure meant traffic can leave York in a northerly direction but vehicles were prohibited from coming into the city from that way.

He said he saw water gushing out onto Wigginton Road and pooled at one of the hospital entrances, with pedestrians walking around it and vehicles slowing on both sides of the road.
