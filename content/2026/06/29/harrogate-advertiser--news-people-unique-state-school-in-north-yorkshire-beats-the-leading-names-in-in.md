# 'Unique' state school in North Yorkshire beats the leading names in independent sector

## A state school in North Yorkshire which first opened nearly 500 years ago has been named the North of England Grammar School of the Year.

Ripon Grammar School has won the prestigious accolade in the LUXlife Private Education Awards alongside some of the UK’s leading independent schools and private education providers .

The award reinforces the state boarding school’s reputation for combining exceptional academic outcomes with an inclusive boarding experience, offering families the benefits of a world-class education within the state sector.

Headmaster Jonathan Webb said: “We are incredibly proud to receive this award.

"It is particularly meaningful because it recognises that a state school can deliver an educational and boarding experience that rivals the very best in the independent sector.

“Our boarding community enriches the whole school and creates opportunities for students from a wide range of backgrounds to thrive.”

This major award is a notable achievement for Ripon Grammar School, whose current foundation dates from 1555 when Queen Mary and King Philip signed its charter.

The school’s combination of exceptional academic standards, outstanding pastoral care and affordable boarding enables it to compete with - and stand out among - private education providers.

Although a state-funded grammar school, its successful boarding provision means it occupies a unique position in the education sector, being one of the few UK grammar schools to offer boarding and welcomes students from across the UK and overseas.

Ripon Grammar School, which has also been named by The Sunday Times as The Top State School in the North for the last 12 years, was first taken under local authority control In the late 19th century.

Located on the west side of Ripon, it amalgamated with Ripon Girls’ High School in the 1960s.

The LUXlife Private Education Awards were first launched in 2021 and have recognised organisations demonstrating excellence in teaching, innovation, leadership and student support across the global private education sector.

wards coordinator Melissa Bramall said: “We’re proud to recognise the commitment of such compassionate, empathetic, and caring individuals who form the integral teams within the private education market around the globe."
