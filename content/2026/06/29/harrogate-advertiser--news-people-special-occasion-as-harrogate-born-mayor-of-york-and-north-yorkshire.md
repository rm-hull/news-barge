# 'Special occasion' as Harrogate-born Mayor of York and North Yorkshire returns to his former school

## The Harrogate-born man elected to serve as the first-ever Mayor of York and North Yorkshire is to return to his old school next week in what will be a “special occasion”.

David Skaith, who was born in Harrogate, is to visit Rossett School to officially launch its sponsored walk in support of a charity founded and led by former Rossett students.

Mark Bulmer, Assistant Headteacher at Rossett School, said: "It is a particular honour to welcome David Skaith, Mayor of York and North Yorkshire, to officially open the walk.

"As a former Rossett student, his presence makes the occasion even more special and we are delighted to welcome him back to school."

The Labour Mayor, who grew up in Harrogate where his family still lives and his brother serves as a police officer, will see students and colleagues from Rossett School tackle a 12km walk through Harrogate, including Harlow Carr and Valley Gardens.

Taking place on Friday, July 10, organisers aim to raise £12,000 through the event with the chosen charity being The Sibling Group, a Harrogate charity founded and led by former Rossett students.

The charity supports children and young people who have a disabled sibling, helping them build friendships, access support and connect with others who share similar experiences.

Funds raised from the walk will also support Rossett School's minibus appeal.

Mark Bulmer said: "The Rossett Sponsored Walk is one of the highlights of our school calendar and reflects the strong sense of community here.

"It is about far more than raising money, it is an opportunity for students and colleagues to come together, support one another and live out the values that define our school.

"We are especially proud to be raising funds for The Sibling Group, whose work makes a meaningful difference to the lives of so many young people, while also supporting our minibus appeal."

Students and colleagues are being encouraged to embrace the Rossett Red theme by wearing red clothing and accessories.

The walk will be marshalled by Rossett colleagues and volunteers, helping to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience.
