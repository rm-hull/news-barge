## Thriving sports and social club near York goes to market for £99,500 leasehold

The bar area *(Image: Ernest Wilson)*

A thriving sports and social club near York has gone on the market.

Ernest Wilson is seeking £99,500 for the leasehold of the Copmanthorpe Sports and Social Club, which reports an annual revenue of £442,000.

The club is an independent commercial enterprise, which forms part of the Copmanthorpe Recreation Centre, which is owned by a charitable trust.

Sales particulars say the venue occupies an extensive site on Barons Crescent, has plentiful parking, and receives much footfall from the sporting and community activities taking place across the site.

The agents continued: “The business itself operates as a highly successful sports bar, events venue and community hub, achieving an impressive average weekly turnover in the region of £8,000 to £9,000, underpinned by a combination of bar sales, sporting usage and function hire.

“It enjoys strong profit margins and a loyal customer base, reflecting its established reputation within the village and surrounding area. Additional income is derived from a room within the premises which is currently sub-let, providing a steady rental return of £250 per calendar month.”

The venue has a main bar, toilets, seating areas, games room, small kitchen, changing rooms and other facilities to create a major sports and social destination.

A ‘standout feature’, it continued, is an indoor sports hall, which is also used as a function suite for events including parties, celebrations, conferences and large scale social functions, making it “one of the key function venues within the locality.”

*(Image: Ernest Wilson)*

Outside a covered verandah provides outdoor seating, which is popular during the warmer months and during spoerts events.

Ernest Wilson also said: “The business is widely regarded as one of the most versatile leisure venues in the area, offering a mix of everyday bar trade, organised sports usage, and a busy calendar of functions and events.

“Since recent investment and improvements, it has continued to grow its reputation as a vibrant, family-friendly venue that caters for everything from children’s parties to large celebrations and live entertainment.”

The agents added “despite its already strong performance” there remains scope for further development, most notably through a higher food offering and more private functions and events.

“Its combination of location, scale, versatility and community importance ensures that it will continue to thrive under enthusiastic new ownership, while still offering ample opportunity for further growth.”
