# Magnificent Grade I listed venue in Harrogate to host 'joyful' choir this summer

## A Harrogate group of singers with a pedigree stretching back more than 50 years is gearing up for its “uplifting” summer concert.

Vocalis, a mixed-voice choir which was founded in 1974 as Harrogate Chamber Singers, will present a programme “filled with joyful, uplifting and deeply moving choral music".

Talking place on Sunday, July 12, the event will enhanced by its setting inside the beautiful acoustics of the magnificent St Wilfrid’s Church, Harrogate’s only Grade I listed building.

Under the direction of Musical Director James Savage-Hanford, the choir will present a rich selection of American spirituals, folk songs and timeless favourites from the Great American Songbook.

The programme features a number of well-known spirituals and Gospel songs, including Michael Tippett’s powerful set of Five Spirituals from A Child of Our Time - Steal Away; Nobody Knows the Trouble I See; Go Down, Moses; O, By and By and Deep River. You’ll also hear Moses Hogan’s thrilling arrangement of The Battle of Jericho.

The concert will continue with a set of Appalachian folk music, including a beautifully crafted arrangement of Shenandoah and the traditional Shaker tune Simple Gifts.

To bring the afternoon to a warm and memorable close, Vocalis will send you on your way with a selection of beloved Great American Songbook classics such as George Gershwin’s Summertime, Cole Porter’s Let’s Do It, and Ain’t Misbehavin’ by Thomas “Fats” Waller and Harry Brooks.

Tickets are also available from Vocalis members and on the door.
