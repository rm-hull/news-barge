TRAVELLERS who pitched up at a much-loved playing field in York have been ordered to leave.

City of York Council confirmed that the Travellers, who are camped at Ethel Ward Playing Field in Haxby, were visited and served a Direction to Leave Notice by Neighbourhood Enforcement Officers on Monday (June 29).

The Travellers arrived at the playing field on Friday (June 26).

The group has until 2pm on Wednesday (July 1) to leave the area, the council has confirmed.

  


A council spokesperson said: “If this is breached then Neighbourhood Enforcement Officers will apply to the Magistrate’s Court for Direction to Leave Orders.”

As The Press reported, Haxby Town Council said it was working with City of York Council and North Yorkshire Police to move the Travellers on as “quickly as possible”.

In a statement on Monday (June 29) a Haxby Town Council spokesperson said: “We are aware that there have been a number of incidents on Ethel Ward.

“We advise all to report any incident to the police as soon as possible.”
