## Former York warehouse transformed into £600,000 townhouse

Former warehouse converted into a four-bedroom £600,000 townhouse in Sandringham Street *(Image: Ashtons)*

A former warehouse in York has been transformed into a four-bedroom townhouse.

The property, in Sandringham Street, was once used by the York Beer and Wine Company.

The building is now a four-bedroom home with an industrial-inspired design.

On sale at £600,000, the property sits in a riverside city location and is described as being arranged over three floors.

  Open-plan living area with glazed openings to the front and rear 

*(Image: Ashtons)*

According to information provided on the listing, the building, previously used as a warehouse, has been reimagined by a developer who is described as award-winning.

The listing states that the conversion has focused on an industrial-inspired design and a contemporary look throughout.

The accommodation features an open-plan living environment on the ground floor.

  Bespoke kitchen within the open-plan ground floor layout 

*(Image: Ashtons)*

The developer has included large glazed openings to the front and rear, which the listing says are intended to create a flow of space and allow for natural light.

Both front and rear courtyard gardens are included with the property.

Accommodation comprises four bedrooms, with one principal room offering an en-suite shower room.

  En-suite shower room serving the principal bedroom 

*(Image: Ashtons)*

The listing states that a house bathroom is also present.

Internally, the refurbishment is described in the listing as being completed to a high standard, with new plumbing, wiring, and plasterwork.

Newly installed windows, a bespoke kitchen, and new bathrooms are included.

The home is advertised as freehold and comes with a 10-year build warranty.

  Front and rear courtyard gardens with outdoor space 

*(Image: Ashtons)*

The location is noted to be close to riverside and park walks, Bishopthorpe Road, York city centre, and the railway station, as well as a range of shops, cafés, and restaurants.

At a reported 1,354 sq. ft, the listed cost equates to £443 per square foot.

The property is said to offer immediate move-in potential as the first owner of the conversion.
