## Swimmers raise more than £1,900 for York and Scarborough Hospitals Charity

A group of swimmers has raised more than £1,900 to support the York & Scarborough Hospitals Charity *(Image: York & Scarborough Hospitals Charity)*

A group of swimmers has raised more than £1,900 to support patient care and staff wellbeing.

The funds were raised for the York & Scarborough Hospitals Charity by three swimmers who took part in the one-mile Great North Swim in Lake Windermere, with the money going towards improvements at York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust.

Natalie Marriott, Events Fundraiser for York & Scarborough Hospitals Charity, said: "We’re incredibly proud of our swimmers for taking on the Great North Swim this year.

  The group took part in the one-mile Great North Swim in Lake Windermere 

*(Image: York & Scarborough Hospitals Charity)*

"Completing a one-mile open water swim is a huge achievement, and to raise such a fantastic amount on top of that is incredible.

"Their support will make a real difference to patients, families and staff across our hospitals."

The money raised will help fund enhancements to hospital environments and services beyond what is provided by NHS funding.

The Great North Swim is an open water event held in the Lake District.
