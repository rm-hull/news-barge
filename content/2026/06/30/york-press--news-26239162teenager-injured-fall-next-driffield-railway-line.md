A teenager has been airlifted to hospital after a fall next to a railway line in East Yorkshire.

Emergency services were called to the scene on the railway line between Driffield and Nafferton on Monday afternoon (June 29).

Yorkshire Ambulance Service said its crews were called for the teenager who was left injured after falling in Driffield just before 4pm.

“One patient was conveyed by air to hospital,” an ambulance service spokesperson said.

Train lines next to the scene in Driffield were closed during the incident and reopened at around 5.45pm.

Northern said trains between Hull and Scarborough faced delays and cancellations until 6pm on Monday due to the incident.
