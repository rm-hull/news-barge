## Concern after Travellers pitch up at York playing field

Haxby Ward Councillor Andrew Hollyer said he had received "numerous" concerned reports from residents near to Ethel Ward Playing Field *(Image: Cllr Andrew Hollyer)*

A LOCAL councillor has voiced his concerns about a community playing field after Travellers moved onto the site four days ago.

Cllr Andrew Hollyer said he had received “numerous" concerned reports from residents after Travellers pitched up at Ethel Ward Playing Field in Haxby on Friday (June 26).

As reported by The Press, members of the travelling community at the site were served with a Direction to Leave Notice by Neighbourhood Enforcement Officers on Monday (June 29).

  


The group has until 2pm on Wednesday (July 1) to leave the area, City of York Council has confirmed.

  The Travellers were served with a Direction to Leave Notice by Neighbourhood Enforcement Officers on June 29 

*(Image: Cllr Andrew Hollyer)*

Cllr Hollyer said: “When I arrived at Ethel Ward on Tuesday morning, I saw about 16 caravans, and 13 cars and vehicles pitched at the site.

“The Travellers arrived on Friday, and the council was quick and effective with their order response.

“It is tricky, however, when nothing can be done over the weekend and swift action isn’t possible.”

Speaking about the Direction to Leave Order, a City of York Council spokesperson said: “If this is breached then Neighbourhood Enforcement Officers will apply to the Magistrate’s Court for Direction to Leave Orders.”

  “Ethel Ward is a site that is particularly vulnerable to these types of incidents" 

*(Image: Cllr Andrew Hollyer)*

The Haxby Ward councillor explained: “Ethel Ward is a site that is particularly vulnerable to these types of incidents.

“It is open and accessible on all sides. It is very hard and costly to put physical measures in place to gate it.”

Haxby Town Council said it was working with City of York Council and North Yorkshire Police to move the Travellers on as “quickly as possible”.

A spokesperson added on June 29: “We are aware that there have been a number of incidents on Ethel Ward.

“We advise all to report any incident to the police as soon as possible.”
