Search the Public Notice PortalYork Council is introducing a series of new 'no waiting at any time' restrictions across the city.

The changes, effective from June 29, 2026, will affect numerous streets and areas, aiming to improve traffic flow and safety around the city.

The decision follows consultations with the police and is being implemented under the Road Traffic Regulation Act, 1984.

The council has released a comprehensive list of streets and areas to be affected by the new restrictions.

These include parts of Albemarle Road, Amberley Street, Barkston Avenue, Barlow Street, and Bede Avenue, among others.

The changes mean that vehicles will not be allowed to wait at any time in these specified areas, affecting both residents and visitors.

This is part of a wider effort to manage parking in York more effectively, helping to reduce congestion and improve safety for pedestrians and cyclists.

The restrictions will also be implemented in parts of Copmanthorpe Lane, Bishopthorpe, Clifton Without, Church Lane, Elvington, Fulford, and Haxby.

In Huntington, parts of Avon Drive, Linden Close, and Strensall Road will also see the introduction of 'no waiting at any time' restrictions.

The same will apply to parts of Moor Lane in Haxby/Wigginton, Whitby Avenue in Heworth without, Rawcliffe, Kirklands in Strensall, and several streets in Wigginton.

A 'no waiting Monday-Friday 8.30am-9.30am & 3pm-4pm' restriction will be introduced on Station Road, Haxby.

The council is also revoking the Multiple Occupancy Permit and the Multiple Occupancy Discount Permit from the list of available permit types within the Residents Parking Scheme.

The new restrictions mean that any vehicle parked in these areas at any time will be subject to penalties.

The council has urged residents and visitors to familiarise themselves with the new restrictions and make necessary arrangements.

The full list of affected streets and areas can be inspected at the Reception, West Offices, Station Rise, York, during normal business hours.

The council has also stated that any person who wishes to question the validity of the Order or any of its provisions on the grounds that it is not within the powers of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984 as amended or that a requirement of any regulation thereunder has not been complied with may, within six weeks from the date of making the Order, make an application for that purpose to the High Court.

The new restrictions are part of the council's ongoing efforts to manage parking more effectively and ensure the safety of all road users in York.

The council has stated that these new restrictions are necessary to ensure the smooth flow of traffic, improve road safety, and maintain access for emergency vehicles.

The changes are expected to reduce congestion and improve the overall environment for both residents and visitors to the city.

The council has stressed the importance of compliance with the new restrictions and has warned that any vehicle found parked in these areas at any time will be subject to penalties.

Motorists are being urged to pay close attention to road signage and markings to avoid inadvertently parking in a restricted area.

The council has also made it clear that these new restrictions will be enforced rigorously to ensure compliance.

The introduction of these new restrictions comes as part of a wider effort by the City of York Council to improve the overall quality of life in the city by reducing congestion and improving road safety.

The council has stated that it will continue to monitor the situation and make further changes as necessary to ensure that the city remains a safe and pleasant place to live, work, and visit.

The council has also sought to reassure residents and visitors that it is committed to ensuring that York remains an accessible and welcoming city for all.

The introduction of these new restrictions is just one part of a broader strategy to achieve this goal.

The council has indicated that it will continue to work closely with the police and other stakeholders to ensure the successful implementation of these new measures.
