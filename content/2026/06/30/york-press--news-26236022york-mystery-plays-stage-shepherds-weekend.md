A puppet goat, sheep, and a pop-up angel are all set to take centre stage in York this weekend.

They will appear in a retelling of The Shepherds for the York Mystery Plays, which is being staged by the York Cordwainers and directed by Pip Cook.

For Ms Cook, a recent graduate of the Bristol Old Vic Theatre School, the production marks her directorial debut.

Ms Cook said: "It’s been a very joyous collaborative experience, and I am grateful to everyone for going along with my crazy ideas.

"From sheep puppets to a pop-up angel, everyone just took it in their stride."

She has teamed up with actors from Papermouse Theatre to bring new energy and creativity to the performance.

  Joy Cullwick, Master of the York Company of Cordwainers 

*(Image: Supplied)*

Joy Cullwick, master of the Cordwainers, said: "I believe the play will be really first class and am very excited to be part of it.

"Pip Cook, the director and Papermouse Theatre, are so creative and talented.

"The production is engaging, funny, and moving – you will laugh, and you will cry.

"There’s lots of wonderful music and even a goat puppet – very appropriate for the Cordwainers as we have a goat on our company badge."

The York Mystery Plays are produced by the York Festival Trust and will be performed on Sunday, 5 July.

Ten plays will be staged on traditional pageant wagons, which will travel through the city and stop at four locations to perform.
