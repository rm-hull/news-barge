## One lane of busy main road in York closed for second morning

The hole is next to Wigginton Terrace in Wigginton Road *(Image: Kevin Glenton)*

A BUSY main road in a York traffic hotspot is down to one lane again this morning.

The Press has been along to Wigginton Road where the York-bound lane was closed at 7am (Tuesday, June 30).

  


  The burst water pipe affected traffic outside York Hospital in Wigginton Road yesterday morning (Monday, June 28) 

*(Image: North Yorkshire Weather Updates)*

As reported yesterday, a fractured water pipe caused the closure of one lane as Yorkshire Water began repairs very close to one of the entrances of York Hospital, almost opposite Faversham Crescent.

The footpath on the Wigginton Terrace side of Wigginton Road is also closed around the scene.

Road closed signs are up at either end of the affected area from the junction with Crichton Avenue up to Wigginton Terrace and diversion signs are also in place to move traffic away from the closed area where there is a deep hole in the road surface.

  Yorkshire Water said it needs to assess any damage to the road and plan for it to be resurfaced 

*(Image: Kevin Glenton)*

Yesterday, the ambulance service said emergency vehicles were accessing the hospital from another route and York and Scarborough Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust said the site remains accessible but patients and visitors were advised to plan ahead.

The road was dug up following the burst water main yesterday and inspection works from Yorkshire Water started after 10am.

A spokesperson for the utility company said the repair was completed at 5pm yesterday and it needed to assess any damage to the road and plan for resurfacing.

The presence of high-voltage cables needed removing which meant contractors had to dig by hand yesterday.

As reported yesterday, Wigginton Road has been the site of multiple water pipe fractures in the last few years.
