## More than 1,000 children sing at York Schools Choral Festival

More than 1,000 children took part in the York Schools Choral Festival 2026 *(Image: York Music Hub)*

More than 1,000 children came together for a joyful choral celebration in York.

The young performers took part in the York Schools Choral Festival 2026, now in its 14th year, representing 32 choirs from across the region.

The event was held at the Sir Jack Lyons Concert Hall at the University of York and was funded by York Music Hub and in partnership with the University of York.

The festival has steadily grown over the years, expanding from a two-day event to four days in 2026.

  The festival has steadily grown over the years, expanding from a two-day event to four days 

*(Image: York Music Hub)*

Molly Newton, who co-leads the festival alongside fellow music teacher and community musician Buffy Breakwell, said: "This year has been beyond all our expectations with the festival growing from two to four days and now involving over 1,000 children from across the city.

"Those who took part have once again gone beyond all our expectations in terms of their enthusiasm and commitment."

Secondary schools represented included Vale of York Academy, York High School, Joseph Rowntree School, Huntington School, Archbishop Holgate’s School, Barlby High School, and Malton School Choir.

  The event is now in its 14th year, representing 32 choirs from across the region 

*(Image: York Music Hub)*

All Saints Lower School Choir had intended to take part but had to withdraw due to an unexpected school closure on the day of their performance.

Each day of the festival began with a group warm-up, followed by school-specific workshops and guided rehearsals led by professional musicians.

Pupils then performed a prepared piece in an afternoon showcase before joining together in a combined choir finale to parents and carers.

  The event was held at the Sir Jack Lyons Concert Hall at the University of York 

*(Image: York Music Hub)*

Ms Newton added: "The songs were exceptional from all schools involved and there was a huge variety of styles and genres.

"We all joined together at the end of each day to perform "Go the Distance" and also had fabulous inspirational performances from young singers Jasmine Lowe and Benedict Barrington Lightfoot-Loftus, as well as from Holly Gowen Jing-Ye Fang, two up-and-coming classical singers studying for master's degrees in Vocal Studies at the University of York."

She also thanked the many individuals and organisations who made the festival possible.

Ms Newton said: "Once again we owe a huge thank you to our workshops leaders, and to York Music Hub for supporting the event.

"We are indebted to the University of York music department and, in particular, Kath Watson and Faye Robinson for facilitating the festival.

"Additionally, we had invaluable assistance from five Year 13 students from Archbishop Holgate's School who volunteered all week to facilitate the event."

They are invaluably assisted by Caroline Smith, who is their administrator and Joanne Yau, manager of York Music Hub, who coordinates and organises all the workshops.
