A long-standing advocate of disability arts in York has been appointed executive director of a charity.

Tim Wheeler has taken on the role at York-based Accessible Arts & Media (AAM), bringing more than 35 years of experience in arts, culture, and disability sectors.

He will help to shape the organisation’s future following the retirement of co-founder Rose Kent in 2025.

Mr Wheeler said: "I’m thrilled to be joining Accessible Arts & Media and excited to be supporting the organisation over the next 18 months as it strengthens its structure, operating model, and future direction.

"AAM has a committed team, a strong track record and deep roots in York, and I feel privileged to be part of its next chapter."

He has worked extensively across community engagement and organisational development.

Mr Wheeler co-founded Mind the Gap, one of Europe’s leading learning disability theatre companies, and led the Arts Council England partnership programme All In, helping cultural organisations build access and involvement into their everyday practice.

Lucy Galliard, chair of trustees at AAM, said: "We’re thrilled to welcome Tim to Accessible Arts & Media, and deeply value the knowledge, experience and expertise he brings to the role of Executive Director."
