A York-based cycling company’s event sponsorship has taken on personal significance this year.

Jorvik Tricycles is the lead sponsor of the Yorkshire Walk for Parkinson's UK, taking place at Leeds’ Temple Newsam on Sunday, July 19.

The walk is raising funds for the charity’s Parkinson’s Nurse Appeal, and sponsorship is especially meaningful to Jorvik Tricycles’ founder James Walker, whose father’s Parkinson’s diagnosis inspired the business more than a decade ago.

  Jorvik Tricycles’ founder James Walker's father was diagnosed with Parkinson’s more than a decade ago 

*(Image: Jorvik Tricycles)*

Mr Walker said: "Parkinson’s UK has always been central to the Jorvik Tricycles story, and being able to contribute to the charity’s work in this way has been really special."

Participants can choose between a 6.1-mile or accessible 1.1-mile route.

Attendees will also be able to test and explore tricycles from the Jorvik range through 1-2-1 demonstrations.

Jorvik Tricycles was founded in 2014 and has since grown into a multi-million-pound retailer serving over 37,000 customers globally.
