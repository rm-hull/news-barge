## York households could face higher council tax bills- how to avoid them

People receiving single person's council tax discounts could lose them if they do not respond to council checks in time *(Image: Agency)*

SOME York households could be hit with higher council tax bills under new checks being performed.

City of York Council is set to begin regularly reviewing 25 per cent single person council tax discounts and checking which households are still eligible for them.

Letters are set to be sent to those getting discounts and households could lose them if they do not confirm they are eligible in time.

Cllr Katie Lomas, the council’s Labour finance spokesperson, said making the checks meant officials would be able to make changes quickly if necessary and avoid debt problems.

Checks set to start in July will see households in receipt of single person’s discounts receive a letter asking them to confirm their eligibility within two weeks.

If they do not respond they will get another reminder letter with a new deadline.

Their 25 per cent discount would be removed if they do not respond and a new council tax bill would be issued.

Instalments which need to be paid would be spread over the current financial year.

Bills for households who do respond saying their circumstances have changed will have their bills adjusted accordingly with any instalments spread over the rest of the financial year.

  Cllr Katie Lomas, Labour Finance Executive Member on City of York Council 

*(Image: City of York Council)*

Further checks may take place to check that information held on households is accurate and up-to-date and changes made and action taken where appropriate.

Council finance executive member Cllr Lomas said support was available for anyone worried about money.

The executive member said: “We know that circumstances can change so we need to check in with people receiving a discount.

“We also know that the cost of living crisis is making bills tricky for many and we do offer support.

“Following this review, we will change payment arrangements for those who are no longer eligible for the single person’s discount.

“Making these checks means we can change payments quickly when needed and help to avoid debt problems.”
