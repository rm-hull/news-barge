A music service is celebrating three decades of inspiring young talent.

East Riding Music Service, which began with a single orchestra, now supports 13 ensembles and delivers weekly lessons to more than 5,000 pupils.

A festival at Bridlington Spa on Sunday 21 June featured 185 musicians of all ages.

Councillor Victoria Aitken, Cabinet Member for Children, Families and Education, said: "East Riding’s Music Service has grown over the years to provide a diverse music offer to people of all ages, abilities, and musical tastes.

"The service enables children to learn instruments in school, offers opportunities to play as part of ensembles and to perform in front of audiences.

"The diversity of opportunities has grown, as has the locations that groups play at – and the service continues to grow.

"It’s fantastic to see children inspired by their musical involvement and to hear senior music members deliver fantastic performances."

Events earlier in the month included The Big Sing and The Little Sing, with more than 2,200 children singing for audiences totalling around 3,000 people.

The service’s ERock musicians also performed at the Polar Bear Music Club in Hull and at the Humber Music Hub stage during Beverley Folk Festival as part of the anniversary celebrations.
