Religious literacy is essential for community cohesion, according to the Archbishop of York.

The Most Reverend Stephen Cottrell spoke during his keynote speech at the Religion Media Festival at Methodist Central Hall in Westminster.

He spoke on the role of faith in society and the importance of religious representation in the media.

The Archbishop said: "Representation in the media matters because faith matters, because values matter, but also to demystify and familiarise those who have no religious affiliation, so that everyone, of whatever faith or none, can see themselves represented and that faith can be understood, represented and explained."

He warned that religious illiteracy in the media contributes to misunderstanding and misrepresentation. This can "lead to fear of people ‘not like us’ and can contribute to the false narratives of dread and hatred", he said.

Mr Cottrell also emphasised that trust in the media has fallen, with only 30 per cent of UK adults expressing confidence in news reporting—a 20-point drop over the past decade.

He argued that public service broadcasting must give greater priority to religious content.

A panel discussion followed his address.

It was chaired by journalist Roger Bolton and included participants from the media sector and higher education.
