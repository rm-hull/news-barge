Martin Lewis has issued what he calls an "important warning" for millions of adults aged between 18 and 39, urging them to put just £1 into a Lifetime ISA (LISA) while they still can.

The Money Saving Expert founder says the simple £1 move could protect access to one of the UK's most generous savings products if the Government presses ahead with plans to replace it.

Speaking on his latest podcast, Martin explained that opening a Lifetime ISA now – even with the minimum amount – could allow savers to continue using the account for years to come, even if it closes to new applicants.

He said: "If you do not have a Lifetime ISA, get a pound in one now."

## Why Martin Lewis says £1 could make a big difference

A Lifetime ISA lets eligible adults save up to £4,000 a year, with the Government adding a 25% bonus worth up to £1,000 annually.

The money can be used either towards buying a first home that meets the scheme rules or withdrawn from age 60 for retirement.

However, Martin warned that the Government is currently consulting on replacing the Lifetime ISA with a new First Time Buyer ISA.

If that happens, new applicants would no longer be able to open a LISA.

His advice is to open one now, even if it's only funded with £1.


Martin explained that anyone who already has a Lifetime ISA open would likely be allowed to continue paying into it under the current proposals, meaning they could still use it for retirement savings in future if their circumstances change.

He said that while a workplace pension is usually a better option for retirement, a Lifetime ISA can still be valuable for some people, including self-employed basic-rate taxpayers or those no longer paying into a workplace pension.

"We don't know what you'll need in 10 years' time," he said. "Putting a pound in now means you'll have the facility to use it."

## ISA shake-up could change how millions save

Martin's warning comes as wider ISA reforms are expected to reshape how many people save and invest.

Under plans expected to take effect from April 2027, cash held inside Stocks and Shares ISAs could become subject to tax on interest, ending the current position where ISA savings are generally sheltered from tax.

The reforms are also expected to reduce the annual Cash ISA allowance for many savers while encouraging more people to invest rather than hold cash.

Adam Nasli, Head Broker Analyst at BrokerChooser , says people still have time to prepare but shouldn't leave reviewing their savings until the last minute.

He said: "With the changes not taking effect until April 2027, people still have time to review how their money is structured before any potential tax implications come into force.

"Rather than waiting for the deadline, use this transition period to take stock of how your cash is currently allocated within ISA wrappers."

Nasli said the changes are likely to encourage savers to think more carefully about how much they keep in cash versus investments, but warned the added complexity could confuse many people.

"ISAs have always been popular because they're simple," he said. "If the new rules become difficult to understand, there's a risk some people could end up disengaging from investing altogether."

## Should you open a Lifetime ISA now?

Martin Lewis' message isn't that everyone should immediately start paying thousands into a Lifetime ISA.

Instead, his advice is about preserving the option while it's still available.

For anyone aged 18 to 39 who may want to use a Lifetime ISA in future, opening one with just £1 now could mean keeping access to the account even if the rules change in the years ahead.
