## Yorkshire Ambulance Service celebrates milestone anniversary of emergency care

Yorkshire Ambulance Service recently marked 20 years of emergency care, support, and impact across the region *(Image: Supplied)*

Yorkshire Ambulance Service is marking 20 years of emergency care, support, and impact across the region.

Formed on July 1, 2006 through the merger of several regional ambulance services, the trust has since handled more than 18 million 999 calls and nearly 20 million non-emergency patient journeys.

Peter Reading, chief executive of Yorkshire Ambulance Service, said: "Reaching our 20th anniversary is a proud moment and a chance to recognise the difference our people have made to millions of patients in communities across Yorkshire and the Humber."

The trust now employs around 7,700 full and part-time staff—more than double its original workforce—and in the past year alone, it responded to 1.25 million 999 calls.

Alongside emergency care, the Yorkshire Ambulance Service Charity is running a fundraising campaign throughout the remainder of 2026 to mark the milestone.

Carey Taylor, head of the YAS Charity, said: "By supporting the YAS Charity, you are not only showing your appreciation for our life-saving staff and volunteers, but you are also being a life-saver yourself by helping to fund vital equipment and training which can make all the difference in a medical emergency."

The charity also provides CPR training to thousands of people across the region each year.
