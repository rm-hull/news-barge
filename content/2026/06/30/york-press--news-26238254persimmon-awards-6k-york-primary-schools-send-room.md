A special school project in York has received a huge funding boost to help create a more inclusive learning environment.

Our Lady Queen of Martyrs Catholic Primary School has been awarded a £6,000 donation from Persimmon’s Community Champions initiative, which will be used to refurbish the school’s sensory room for pupils with special education needs and disabilities (SEND).

Councillor Claire Douglas, Leader of City of York Council, said: "It was lovely to visit Our Lady Queen of Martyrs and have a tour with Assistant Head Hannah Elsworth and Jane Blackwood from the board of governors.

"A big thank you to the children, staff, governors and parents and carers for being so welcoming and a big thanks to Persimmon and their contribution to our community."

The funding was secured after the school’s governors applied to the initiative, which supports schools, charities, community groups, and sports clubs.

Kate Starkie, deputy managing director of Persimmon Yorkshire, said: "It was fantastic to be given a tour of the school and see first-hand the plans for its new SEND space.

"We’re always delighted to help schools, charities and local community groups looking for funding where we can, particularly in our home city of York."

For more information about Persimmon’s Community Champions initiative, visit: [https://www.persimmonhomes.com/community-champions](https://www.persimmonhomes.com/community-champions)
