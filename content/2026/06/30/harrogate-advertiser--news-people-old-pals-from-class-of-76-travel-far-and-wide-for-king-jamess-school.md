# Old pals from Class of 76 'travel far and wide' for King James’s School reunion in Knaresborough

## A memorable school reunion in Knaresborough of the class of 1976 was so successful that some of the former pupils are planning to return and live again in the town after all these years.

Old school pals travelled from far and wide for the King James's School reunion last weekend to celebrate half a century since most of them had left the school to start their careers.

Taking place at the Mitre Hotel in Knaresborough, more than 50 former students turned out for the event, some of whom had started their schooling together at Manor Road Infants School before moving onto the then Castle Yard Junior School and then finally the then newly-opened King James’s School in 1971, following the changing of the school's status from Grammar School to a Comprehensive.

Dubbed the class of 76, a number of those attending had not seen many of their former class mates for decades, with reminiscing by some going on until closing time.

One of the attendees was Philip Allott, who like many of the others attended all three local schools.

"It was great to catch-up with former school friends and to learn about some of the great and varied things people have done since leaving school,” said Mr Allott.

"It was also fun to be reminded about some of the things people had done together at school, which due to the passing of time had been long forgotten.

"While many of those who were at school have stayed relatively local, others have lived and travelled far and wide, with some of those who attended now planning to return and live again in Knaresborough, which was also great to hear."

Founded in 1616 by Royal Charter, King James’s School celebrated its 400th anniversary back in 2016.

Former student Ian Bell. who was instrumental along with others in making the King James's School reunion happen, was delighted with the attendance and there is talk on making a meet up an annual event.

Future reunion plans will be put on Facebook or you can send a message to Ian Bell by using Facebook Messenger.
