A POLICE officer has appeared before York Magistrates Court charged with rape and other sexual offences against a child.

Detective Constable Dan Livesley is currently suspended from duty by his force, West Yorkshire Police.

He faces one allegation he raped a girl and three that he sexually assaulted a girl. The offences are alleged to have happened some years ago when he was off duty.

Livesley, 48, of Bradford, was not asked to enter a plea to the charges because rape can only be tried by a judge and jury.

He was sent to York Crown Court where he will appear for a plea and trial preparation hearing on July 27. He will then be expected to enter his pleas.

The police officer was released on bail with a condition that he have no contact with children. Before being suspended, he was based in the Bradford district of West Yorkshire Police.
