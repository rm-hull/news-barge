Police officers have been awarded for risking their lives while trying to save a driver who was killed after crashing onto a railway line in North Yorkshire.

PC Mike Halsted, a roads policing officer with North Yorkshire Police, was first on the scene at the crash off York Place, in Harrogate, on the afternoon of July 18 last year.

He found the car, a silver VW Golf driven by a man in his 70s, at the bottom of a steep bank, having come to rest upside down on the railway line.

Firearms officers PC Alex McGee and PC Anthony Grant-Marston were nearby and attended the scene, as well as firefighters.

Network Rail warned that it was unsafe to enter the tracks, but the officers decided to access the line to help the driver, according to the North Yorkshire Police Federation.

The driver was removed from the car and carried up the steep embankment to the road by officers and firefighters, where emergency treatment was provided before ambulance and air ambulance crews took over.

Despite the efforts of everyone involved, the driver died.

The federation said PC Halsted was given a Bravery Award for his actions in trying to save the driver.

PC McGee and PC Grant-Marston were also recognised for their actions.

PC Halsted dedicated his award to his wife, Nici, and their children, Georgia, 16, and Harry, 12.

Recalling arriving at the scene of the fatal crash, he said: “I can remember the specific transmission and just the feeling of thinking ‘surely not’.

“Then you get there and realise it is exactly as reported. There was just chaos, really.

“Because of the shift I was working that day, I wasn’t doing my normal duty, but I was the closest to it.

“You go from thinking one thing to dealing with something completely different.”

PC Halsted said he was “incredibly shocked” to be awarded for his actions, adding: “There are hundreds of cops in our force and thousands across the country who have faced life-threatening situations and not had any recognition.”

"I still struggle with the bravery element because I know loads of cops who have been to horrendous jobs and never had any recognition,” he continued.

“A good friend of mine went into a burning building to drag somebody out after a car had gone through the side of his house and there was no recognition for that.”

Rob Bowles, North Yorkshire Police Federation chair, said the officers’ “selfless actions were in the best traditions of British policing”.

He added: “They put their lives at risk, going onto the railway tracks, to try to save the life of a member of the public. They are a credit to North Yorkshire Police.”

## 'I am immensely proud of their actions,' says chief constable

North Yorkshire Police’s chief constable, Tim Forber, praised the three officers for their actions.

“The actions of PC Mike Halstead, PC Alex McGee and PC Anthony Grant-Marston exemplify the very best of policing in our country,” he said.

“They showed remarkable courage, professionalism, and dedication in what was undoubtedly a dangerous and unpredictable situation.

“Their willingness to put themselves in harm's way to help someone in need reflects the highest standard of our service to the community and the ideals of our force as a whole.

“I am immensely proud of their actions and delighted to see their bravery recognised through this nomination.”
