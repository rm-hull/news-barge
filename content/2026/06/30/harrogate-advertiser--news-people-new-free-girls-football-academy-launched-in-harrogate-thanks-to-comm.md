# New free girls football academy launched in Harrogate thanks to community funding

## Young female footballers in Harrogate are being given the opportunity to develop their skills, build confidence and make new friends through a free football academy.

The Sporting Influence Foundation CIC has launched a weekly Girls' Football Academy at Ashville Sports Centre, with sessions running during term time until Easter 2027.

The initiative has been designed to provide a welcoming, girls-only environment where around 50 young players can improve their footballing ability while boosting their physical fitness, resilience and self-confidence.

A £1,950 grant from the Banks Group's Banks Community Fund has funded the cost of kits for the coming year, ensuring there are no financial barriers preventing girls from taking part.

Each participant will receive their own branded kit, helping to create a strong sense of belonging and team identity.

The Foundation also hopes to support girls who wish to continue playing by helping them access opportunities with local grassroots football clubs.

Founded in 2020, the Sporting Influence Foundation grew out of Harrogate firm Sporting Influence, which uses sport to develop social skills in schools across the district and delivers activity camps during the school holidays.

The charity works to create opportunities for children and young people facing barriers to sport, encouraging participation in physical activity while promoting healthier and happier lifestyles.

Chris Doey, Director at Sporting Influence Foundation, said: “We believe every child deserves the chance to experience the joy, confidence and well-being that sport can bring and run a wide range of different activities to help provide these opportunities.

“The Girls’ Football Academy aims to improve confidence, physical literacy, resilience and teamwork in an inclusive, welcoming environment and to support players’ long-term engagement in football by building links with local clubs that they can go on to join if they want to.

“We’ve got 22 girls taking part in the summer term sessions, with more spaces opening up in the autumn, and we’ve seen an immediate impact on the skills that they’re developing and, more importantly, in the new friendships that they take with them off it.

“Giving them their own kit makes a real difference in building team identity and making everyone feel like they’re part of something special.

“The support we’ve had from the Banks Group for this project has removed the financial barrier to participation that might have stopped some of our players taking part and we’re really grateful for their support.”

Kate Culverhouse, Community Relations Manager at Banks Group, added: “The Sporting Influence Foundation opens up so many opportunities for local young people that might not otherwise have come their way.

“This is a brilliant chance for local girls to give football a try while also making new friendships, developing their fitness and building their self-confidence.”
