A school reunion brought together old friends and classmates in Knaresborough after five decades apart.

Former students from the class of 1976 at King James’s School in Knaresborough gathered at the Mitre Hotel on Saturday, June 27, to mark 50 years since leaving school and starting their careers.

More than 50 people attended the reunion, some of whom began their education together at Manor Road Infants School before moving on to Castle Yard Junior School and then King James’s School, which became a comprehensive in 1971.

Philip Allott, one of the attendees, said: "It was great to catch up with former school friends and to learn about some of the great and varied things people have done since leaving school.

"It was also fun to be reminded about some of the things people had done together, which due to the passing of time had been forgotten."

Mr Allott said that while many former classmates have stayed in the area, others have travelled far and wide, with some now planning to return to Knaresborough.

The event was organised in part by former student Ian Bell, who said he was pleased with the turnout.

There is now talk of making the gathering an annual tradition.
