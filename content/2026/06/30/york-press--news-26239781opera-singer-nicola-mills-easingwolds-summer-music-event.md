## Music fans gather as ‘down to earth diva’ entertains crowd with opera and classics

Opera singer Nicola Mills entertains an al fresco audience in Easingwold Market Place as part of the town's Summer of Music programme. *(Image: Submitted)*

An opera singer and 'down to earth diva' wowed crowds in a North Yorkshire market town with a powerful open-air performance.

Nicola Mills returned to Easingwold’s Market Place to support its summer series and drew a strong audience last Saturday despite temperatures approaching 30°C, for an afternoon blending classical pieces with well-known favourites.

The event formed part of the town’s free Summer of Music programme, which aims to showcase a wide mix of genres. Following a rock ‘n’ roll set the previous weekend, this performance offered a more classical tone, with Nicola reprising a well-received appearance from last year.

Performing under the heat, she joked about needing longer breaks between songs, but delivered a confident and engaging set that reflected her “opera for the people” ethos.

  Opera singer Nicola Mills at Easingwold summer music event. 

*(Image: Submitted)*

Between songs, Nicola shared stories from her career, which has taken her from her upbringing in Oldham to performing with Glyndebourne and opera companies across Europe, before settling in Yorkshire.

While living in Belgium, Nicola started her own venture, calling herself ‘The Down to Earth Diva’ which then became ‘Opera for the People’.

Her programme for Easingwold ranged from Puccini to musical theatre, including songs associated with Julie Andrews such as selections from Mary Poppins and The Sound of Music.

One of the standout moments came with A Spoonful of Sugar while other highlights included Summertime, Imagine, and interpretations of songs by Elvis Presley and Cat Stevens, before closing with Barcelona by Freddie Mercury.

As reported, Easingwold’s town centre's live music series is a home-grown initiative that started life as a way to attract visitors at the weekend when a lot of businesses had been shutting up shop after the morning's trade.

  Town councillor Frank Johnston-Banks previously told The Press that before 2020, much of the town centre had closed by lunchtime and "was literally a ghost town".

  

  Now crowds gather with spectators enjoying the music while enjoying refreshments from local pubs and cafes and lingering in the town.

The series continues this Saturday with the return of Kaminari Taiko drummers, promising a high-energy display of rhythm and performance.
