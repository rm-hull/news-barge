## Balaclava-clad hammer-wielding robber terrorised staff in two York shops

Serial shop robber Thomas Hugh Robinson *(Image: North Yorkshire Police)*

A BALACLAVA-CLAD robber wielding a hammer forced a shop assistant to empty the tills at a city convenience store, York Crown Court heard.

It was the second time Thomas Hugh Robinson, 28, had disguised himself and terrorised shop staff in York to hand over money.

In a personal statement, his latest victim said he had been so terrified by the robber’s actions, he still cannot do night shifts and is constantly on the alert while at work.

But, York Crown Court heard, as the robber left, the employee was already contacting his colleagues and within an houir Robinson had been arrested.

Kelly Clarke, prosecuting, said Robinson dragged the employee into the cashier’s booth at the Co-op shop in Campleshon Road, South Bank, shortly before 8pm on February 8, and made him empty one till.

After leaving the booth and looking at the cash the robber came back to the employee and told him: “Open the other one, this isn’t enough.”

The employee then opened the other till and handed over more money.

Robinson had previously been jailed for robbing a Sainsbury’s employee and threatening to kill him.

  The Sainsbury's store at the junction of Bishopthorpe Road and Scarcroft Road raided by Thomas Hugh Robinson in 2022 

*(Image: Google Street View)*

At the time of the Co-op raid was on a suspended prison sentence for causing grievous bodily harm imposed by the Recorder of York, Judge Sean Morris, just one month earlier.

He was also on bail for shoplifting and on police bail suspected of carrying out a different robbery, which he was not charged with.

His barrister Eddison Flint said he committed crimes to fund his drug habit.

Judge Morris told Robinson: “We meet again.

“I took a chance with you and you throw it back in my face.

“The hard-working people who run corner shops and supermarkets, who work in them are sick to death of being preyed on by drug addicts.

“It is time for you to learn a lesson.”

He jailed Robinson for five years and two months, including 20 of the 24 months he had previously supported.

It was the second time he had jailed Robinson for robbery. In 2022, he had locked him up for 16 months for threatening to shoot a Sainsbury's employee in the city.

Robinson, of Coggan Close, South Bank, pleaded guilty to robbing the Co-op employee.

  The Co-op store off Campleshon Road, South Bank, raided by Thomas Hugh Robinson 

*(Image: Google Street View)*

The victim said: “It has impacted me a lot more than I thought it would. I believe I will remember what happened, and the feelings I had, for the rest of my life.”

Mr Flint said Robinson had a “degree of remorse” over what he did in the Co-op store.

He had initially tried to deal with his drug habit after the suspended sentence was imposed, but the “pull of Class A drugs for people like Thomas Robinson becomes harder and harder”.

He also had mental health issues and doctors were assessing whether he had attention deficit and hyperactivity disorder.

The judge said he had heard all about Robinson at the January hearing.
