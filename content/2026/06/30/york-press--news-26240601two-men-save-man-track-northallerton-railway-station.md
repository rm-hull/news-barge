TWO men risked their lives to rescue a vulnerable man from a railway track in North Yorkshire, prompting a police appeal for their actions to be recognised.

The bystanders narrowly escaped an oncoming train as they managed to save the man, who was drunk and had climbed onto the tracks at Northallerton railway station.

North Yorkshire Police want to formally recognise and praise the life-savers' actions and are now trying to trace one of the two Good Samaritans who had to leave the scene when his train arrived.

A force spokesperson said: "On Friday, May 29, at about 10pm, we were called to reports that a heavily intoxicated and vulnerable man had climbed down onto the tracks.

"As we made our way to the scene, two brave members of the public managed to get him to safety - narrowly missing an oncoming train, and no doubt preventing a tragic outcome.

"The man was subsequently arrested and safeguarded by the police."

People - including train drivers - have taken to social media to praise the 'selfless heroes' for their caring actions in preventing a tragedy.

The spokesperson added: "We have identified one of the members of the public who assisted, a young man. We are now seeking details of the second person: a man who had to catch a train while our officers were still dealing with the incident. He is believed to have travelled home to Dubai."

If you were involved in this incident, or know the man who was, please get in touch with PC 103 James Crawford - email [james.crawford@northyorkshire.police.uk](mailto:james.crawford@northyorkshire.police.uk) or call North Yorkshire Police on 101, quoting reference 12260098013.
