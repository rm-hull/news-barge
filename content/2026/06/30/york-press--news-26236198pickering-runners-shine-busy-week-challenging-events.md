The Helmsley Triathlon and Duathlon saw competitors from Pickering Running Club tackle challenging courses with impressive results.

The triathlon, based at the open-air swimming pool, consisted of an 800m pool swim, a gruelling 40k bike ride, and a 10k run.

Mike Richmond completed the event in 2 hours 28 minutes, placing him 7th out of 66 finishers and 2nd in the M50 category.

Kelly Richmond was the 2nd female finisher with a time of 2 hours 41 minutes.

Sue Slack, with a time of 3 hours 34 minutes, came 2nd in the F60 category, while Rachel Morley finished in 3 hours 44 minutes.

The duathlon, which was an 800m pool swim followed by a 10k run, saw 32 competitors.

Charles Corbett finished in 1 hour 8 minutes, placing him 1st in the M40 category.

Shirley Tyrrell, with a time of 1 hour 34 minutes, was 2nd in the F60 category, and Sally Brown's time of 1 hour 37 minutes placed her 3rd in the F60 category.

Emily Schofield completed the duathlon in 1 hour 49 minutes.

Anna Bailey took part in the Lamplighter 20, a race from sunset to sunrise, which started at 9.30pm.

The route was actually 24 miles with over 2,600 feet of climb over open moorland and forest trails.

Anna finished well before sunrise with a time of 4 hours 41 minutes, placing her 2nd female and 3rd overall.

Chloe Riley and her father, Steve, joined 133 runners tackling the Crackpots Trail Run.

The 7-mile race, with 5 water crossings, was part of the Kirkby Malzeard summer fete.

Chloe and Steve crossed the finish line in 73 minutes and 1 second.

They then enjoyed the atmosphere with live music, food, and the vibrant village festival.

Andrew Bates participated in the Sloth Run 5k in Las Vegas.

Despite starting at 7.30am, the temperature was already 30°C and rising.

Andrew had signed up for the 10k but switched to the 5k due to the heat and was relieved to reach the finish line in 29 minutes 48 seconds.

The Bugthorpe 10k, the fifth race in the York & District Road Race League, took place on Tuesday evening.

Running in nearly 30°C with high humidity and an undulating course with nearly 800 feet of climb, this was a tough challenge for the runners.

Only 299 runners braved the extreme conditions compared to 459 in the last race.

The race organisers and volunteers did an excellent job ensuring all runners crossed the line safely by providing an extra water station and plenty of encouragement.

The first to finish for PRC was Simon Jones in 42 minutes 2 seconds, placing him 2nd in the M55 category and 34th out of 299.

Kelly Richmond was 2nd in the U35 category with a time of 43 minutes 45 seconds.

Mike Richmond was not far behind in 44 minutes 17 seconds, followed by Richard Robinson in 44 minutes 34 seconds, Johnny Everitt in 48 minutes 52 seconds, Paul Hickman in 51 minutes 23 seconds, Kirsty Brown in 51 minutes 44 seconds, Ben Paviour in 52 minutes 55 seconds, and Dave Owuor in 53 minutes 20 seconds.

Paul Robinson's time of 59 minutes 33 seconds placed him 2nd in the M70 category.

Heather Riley finished in 79 minutes 28 seconds.
