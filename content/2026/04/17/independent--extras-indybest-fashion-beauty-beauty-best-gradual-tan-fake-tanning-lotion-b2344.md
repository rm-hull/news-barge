Whether you fake tan all-year-round or bronze up before a summer holiday, the best gradual tans achieve a natural, sun-kissed finish. While mousses and mists tend to steal the spotlight, lotion and cream formulas are an underrated addition to your beauty arsenal – and arguably the easiest way to achieve a soft, believable bronze.

As a seasoned fake tanner, I’ve tried countless formulas over the last decade, from quick-absorbing lotions and creams to rich butters and lightweight serums. More gentle on the skin than traditional tanning methods (farewell, tiger-bread dryness), these hybrid formulas make tricky areas such as elbows, knees and ankles far easier to manage, too.

Thanks to the inclusion of skin-conditioning ingredients, these formulas don’t just add colour, they leave skin feeling hydrated and smooth for days after application. Applied just like a regular body lotion, they’re far more forgiving than instant tans, making streaks less likely even when using bare hands (just don’t forget to wash them afterwards to avoid those tell-tale Wotsit fingers).

Perhaps best of all, gradual tanners allow you to achieve the right level of bronze for you. Reapply as often as you like to build up your glow, stopping once you’ve reached your ideal shade – and don’t forget about about the face, of course. “For the best results, apply your tan an hour after showering so skin is hydrated,” Jules Von Hep, celebrity fake tanning artist, advises. “When applying, start from the ankles and work your way up, massaging the skin as you go,” Von Hep says. “Imagine you’re frosting a cake, spreading the tan onto the skin.”

“As a rule whatever you apply on each arm double or triple for the leg,” he adds. Armed with this expert advise, I’ve tested the best gradual fake tans for 2026, from bargain buys to luxury lotions.

I doubt I’m alone when I admit that Garnier’s summer body gradual moisturiser was my first foray into the world of fake tan many moons ago – and it’s testament to the formula that it’s had enduring popularity. The comforting, apricot, summer-esque scent instantly takes me back to my teenage years.

Setting you back just £5 for a 400ml bottle (more than double the size of most gradual tanners on the market, and 90 per cent cheaper than Gatineau’s below), it’s the best budget gradual tan I’ve tested and it yields impressive results. If you like an understated tan, or want to prolong a deeper real – or fake – tan, Garnier’s bargain bottle can’t be faulted. Note that it isn’t a long-lasting fake tan, so you’ll need to apply it multiple times a week.

Living up to its tanning and toning promises, Skin & Tan’s wonder gradual serum helps to achieve a silky smooth, natural-looking tan with minimal fuss. Combining bodycare with self-tan, the formula works to hydrate, firm and moisturise skin thanks to glycerine, soothing aloe vera and antioxidants.

I love how quick-drying the serum is, and that it gives you a bronzed finish instantly – within just five hours, I had a streak-free, sunless tan that made my legs and arms look far more toned, too. It’s a great choice for applying before an evening out, owing to the instant tan element, but Skin & Tan’s lightweight formula is also one of the best for topping up an existing layer of fake tan for a longer-lasting glow. The fake tan remains on skin for around four days, so it isn’t the most long-lasting.
