Harrogate is a town known for its heritage, green spaces and strong sense of community. We’re recruiting a dedicated Events & Culture Lead to shape and deliver civic and community events that reflect Harrogate’s identity and what residents value.

This is a formative role with the opportunity to build a coherent events programme for the Council, working closely with partners, community groups and cultural organisations. You’ll bring creativity, structure and strong project management to a developing area of work, ensuring events are safe, inclusive and well‑managed.

You’ll also play a key role in strengthening relationships across Harrogate’s cultural landscape, enabling collaboration, supporting community‑led ideas and helping to deliver activity that feels distinctly Harrogate.

**What we’re looking for**

We’re seeking someone who can bring confidence, creativity and calm leadership to a varied and community‑focused role. You will have:

- Proven experience designing and delivering public events.
- Strong partnership and stakeholder engagement skills.
- Experience managing budgets and preparing reports.
- Understanding of safeguarding, EDI and Health & Safety.
- Excellent communication and interpersonal skills.
- Strong organisational and project‑management ability.
- Proficiency with digital tools and social media.
- IOSH Managing Safely (or willingness to undertake) and DBS clearance (or willingness to undergo a check)

**Why Apply?**

This is a varied and rewarding role for someone who wants to make a real difference in Harrogate. You’ll join a supportive team that embraces fresh ideas and play a key part in shaping the Council’s future through events and community engagement.

**The Role**

Full-time (37 hours per week, flexible evenings/weekends)

 Salary: SCP 24–28 (£35,412 – £39,152 per annum) + attractive Local Government Pension Scheme

 Location: Harrogate HG1 5LT

**How to apply**

Review the candidate pack, then complete the application form and email it to [recruitment@chrgs.co.uk.](mailto:recruitment@chrgs.co.uk.)
